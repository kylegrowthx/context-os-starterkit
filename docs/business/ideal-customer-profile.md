## TL;DR

- Best-fit customers are B2B technology companies (SaaS, AI/ML, DevTools, Cloud, Cybersecurity) that are already investing in content or know they need to.
- Company stage: Series A through public. They need enough budget and content volume to justify our engagement model.
- The single biggest predictor of success: a champion who cares about content as a growth lever, has authority to make decisions, and moves fast.
- We struggle with early-stage companies without product-market fit, committee decision-making, and companies that view content as a cost center.
- ICP discipline is how we win. Say no fast so you can say yes with force.

## The Point

Not every company that wants to work with you should.

When the fit is wrong, everyone loses. The client doesn't get results because their organization isn't set up to benefit from what we do. We burn delivery capacity on accounts that churn. Editors get demoralized producing work that never gets approved. EMs spend time firefighting instead of driving outcomes.

Bad-fit clients cost more than no clients. They consume resources that could serve great-fit clients, and they damage morale and reputation on the way out.

## The Short Version

**We work best with:**

- B2B technology companies (SaaS, AI/ML, DevTools, Cloud Infrastructure, Cybersecurity)
- Series A through public companies with marketing budgets that support $15K-$25K/mo retainers
- Companies already investing in content or with clear intent to start (they see content as a growth lever, not an expense)
- A single decision-maker or champion who can approve strategy, content, and budget without committee consensus
- Organizations that move fast on approvals and feedback (24-48 hour turnaround, not weeks)

**We struggle with:**

- Early-stage companies without product-market fit (they don't know what to say yet because they don't know who they serve)
- Committee decision-making where every piece of content needs 5+ approvals
- Companies that view content as a cost center and measure success by word count, not business outcomes
- Highly regulated industries where compliance review adds weeks to publishing cycles (healthcare, financial services with strict compliance)

## Why These Criteria Matter

### B2B technology focus

Our AI workflows, playbooks, and editorial expertise are built for B2B tech. We understand the buyer journey, the competitive dynamics, and the content formats that drive pipeline in SaaS, DevTools, AI/ML, and adjacent verticals. When we work outside this focus, our systems are less effective and our team has to rebuild context from scratch. That kills the efficiency advantage that makes our model work.

### Company stage and budget

Strategy Sprints and Growth Execution require meaningful investment ($15K-$25K/mo). Companies below Series A rarely have the budget, the content volume, or the organizational maturity to benefit. They're still figuring out positioning and product-market fit. Our model works best when the company knows what they sell and who they sell it to. We help them say it better and at scale.

### Content as a growth lever

Companies that already believe in content (or have concrete evidence they need it) convert faster and retain longer. They understand the compounding nature of organic content. Companies we have to convince that content matters often churn early because they expect immediate, linear ROI.

### Decision-making speed

Our 8-week sprint cadence requires fast approvals. When feedback takes two weeks, the sprint stalls and results suffer. The best clients have one champion who can greenlight strategy and content without routing through layers of review.

### Willingness to invest in AI-era growth

The companies that get the most value from GrowthX understand that AI has changed discovery. They recognize that showing up in AI answers (not just Google results) requires a fundamentally different approach to content. Companies still optimizing purely for traditional SEO are a step behind but can be good fits if they're willing to evolve.

## The Scoring System

We evaluate prospects across three stages. Each stage has qualification criteria that must be met before moving forward.

| Stage | Max Points | Minimum to Proceed |
|-------|------------|-------------------|
| Fit Score (ICP alignment) | 30 | 20 |
| Engagement Readiness | 20 | 14 |
| Commercial Viability | 15 | 10 |

**Final score determines the path:**

| Score | Decision |
|-------|----------|
| 55-65 (ideal fit) | Fast-track to Strategy Sprint. Priority onboarding. |
| 45-54 (good fit) | Proceed with standard qualification. Strategy call to confirm alignment. |
| 35-44 (borderline) | Proceed with caution. Address gaps before committing resources. May require custom scoping. |
| Below 35 (not ready) | Decline or defer. Recommend they come back when conditions change. |

### Fit Score breakdown (30 points max)

- Industry match (B2B tech vertical): 0-10
- Company stage and budget capacity: 0-8
- Content maturity (existing content program or clear intent): 0-7
- AI/digital growth awareness: 0-5

### Engagement Readiness breakdown (20 points max)

- Champion identified with decision authority: 0-8
- Approval speed (can they turn around feedback in 24-48 hours?): 0-5
- Technical access available (CMS, analytics, search console): 0-4
- Stakeholder alignment (key people agree on goals): 0-3

### Commercial Viability breakdown (15 points max)

- Budget confirmed for $15K-$25K/mo range: 0-6
- Contract timeline realistic (not "we need results in 2 weeks"): 0-5
- Expansion potential (additional services, products, or verticals): 0-4

## Red Flags

Walk away if you see:

- **"We need to run this by the board/committee first"** — Signals slow decision-making and diffuse accountability. Every approval will take weeks.
- **"Can you just write 20 blog posts a month?"** — Treats content as a commodity. They want output volume, not outcomes. They'll measure us on word count, not business impact.
- **"We tried content marketing and it didn't work"** — Could signal a bad past experience that makes them skeptical and micromanaging. Dig deeper: if they blame the strategy rather than the execution partner, it might still be a fit. If they don't believe in content, it's not.
- **"Our legal team needs to review everything before publishing"** — Multi-week compliance cycles break the sprint cadence. Some review is fine; a 3-week legal queue kills momentum.
- **"We're not sure who our audience is yet"** — They haven't achieved product-market fit. We help companies say the right thing at scale. We can't help them figure out what to say in the first place.

Our best customers share a few things:

ICP discipline is how we win. Say no fast so you can say yes with force.
