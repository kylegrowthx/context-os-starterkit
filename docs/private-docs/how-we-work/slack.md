# How We Use Slack

## Overview

Creating a work environment where people can do their best work is the primary job of our management team, and communication is fundamental for a successful remote culture. Slack is our "office," so we take this part of our tooling very seriously.

**We try to keep the number of channels to a minimum.** If you are going to create a channel, read this doc & try to follow the same patterns.

---

## Slack Access for Freelancers & External Contractors/Consultants

- Freelancers and External Contractors/Consultants may only be added to Slack as Guests
  - They may be single-channel or multi-channel guests.
  - Note: Multi-channel guests are also considered paid user licenses.
- Freelancers and External Contractors can only be added to internal channels, not external/shared channels with customers using external email addresses.
  - If they need to be added to "External" or "Customer" channels, they must be issued GrowthX email addresses.
  - In order to get GrowthX email addresses, appropriate agreements must be put into place.
- Please reach out to #b-it-and-tools to request access for them.
- Note: This does not impact employees or contractors added via Deel, who are always given GrowthX email addresses & Slack membership.

## Slack vs Linear

We are in the process of rolling out Linear as our ticketing & project management system for the entire organization. The way to think about these two tools is to think about importance & expectations.

Slack is for either URGENT needs that can't wait, or for things that can be missed. If you need something from somebody, then create a Linear ticket and assign it.

- **Urgent**: DMs & cell-phone - ideal for when you need an answer ASAP
- **Important**: Linear ticket (optionally followed by an @ mention on channel message)
- **Non-important**: Slack channels - best for back-and-forth, idea exchanges, etc. If a real request or need comes out of it, create a Linear Ticket.

---

## Avoid DMs at All Costs

DMs should only be used for urgent communication. They reduce visibility and transparency, and disrupt people's work with notifications. Instead, use the appropriate channels for communication—we have general channels, functional area channels, and project channels available.

---

## Slack Organization Split by Delivery & Build Areas

Our company has two distinct areas that are equally important:

1. **Delivery area**: every person working with our clients (Directors, Content Managers, Managing Editors, SEO Experts, Freelancers, etc)
2. **Build area**: everyone that isn't allocated to our clients (Sales, Biz Ops, Engineering & Product)

These two areas are represented in our Slack channels based on the prefix:
- `d-` for Delivery
- `b-` for Build
- `all-` for everyone

Channel prefixes:
- `d-ext-` are the channels with clients
- `d-int-` are the internal discussion channels for that team working with that client
- `ext-` (without the d-) for partners, sales leads, and others
- `-proj-` are temporary project work that are constantly archived (usually tied to a Linear project)
- `-alert` are usually for automations

## Geekbot: Working in the Open

We embrace "working in the open" by sharing our progress and learnings regularly. This promotes collaboration and prevents duplicate work.

Daily Geekbot check-ins are key to our workflow. These brief updates reduce meetings, increase transparency, and help teams stay aligned.

Consistent participation is required - missing updates for more than a day will prompt follow-up from your manager. These check-ins help build an efficient and connected team.

## How We Organize Our Channels

### Delivery

- **d-general [public]**: Delivery team general channel, a place to chat about anything, ask questions, etc.
- **d-announcements [public]**: This is the Delivery team's inbox for important updates, changes, etc.
- **d-directors [private]**: Cross-pod learnings, challenges, concerns, tactics and strategy, ideas, etc.
- **d-pod-xyz [public]**: Day-to-day operations of the pod as a place for shared learning between accounts
- **d-onboarding [public]**: Questions, ideas, and feedback for everyone onboarding on the Delivery team
- **d-int-client-xyz**: Internal discussion channels for specific client teams
- **d-ext-client-xyz**: External communication with the client
- **d-help-writers-and-editors**: The place to recruit freelancer writers & editors

### Build

- **b-announcements**: Subscribe to notifications
- **b-onboarding**: Everyone not in delivery
- **b-epd**: Engineering, Product, Design (Daily Standup)
- **b-epd-activity-feed**: Linear + Github, etc
- **b-epd-alerts**: Errors, warnings, etc
- **b-epd-requests-and-feedback**: Requests and feedback for EPD
- **b-epd-tech-watercooler**: Engineering news, tech talk, AI news
- **b-ops-general**: Operations (Daily Standup)
- **b-finance**: Finance discussions
- **b-legal**: Legal discussions
- **b-hiring**: Hiring discussions
- **b-gtm-sales**: Go-to-market and sales
- **b-it-and-cybersec**: IT and cybersecurity

### All

- **all-general**: Bi-weekly check-in
- **all-announcements**: Company-wide announcements
- **all-watercooler**: Casual conversations
- **all-kudos**: Recognition and appreciation

### Leadership

- **#leaders-board [private]**: Founders + Investors
- **#leaders [private]**: VPs, Heads, CXX - Anything high-level / strategy / tactics
- **#office-(name)**: Manager + CoS and assistants, etc - Task + Delegation space

---

## Suggested Organization

You can organize the section of your Slack however you want, but consider organizing by:
- Priority channels at the top
- Client channels grouped together
- Build/Delivery separation
- Muted channels for reference
