# Fiscal Plan 2025

## Background and Context

We've built incredible momentum this quarter. We are projected to end Q1 at $6.2M in ARR (up from $2.9M in Q4), and we already have $6.5M in booked ARR with kickoffs scheduled through April 7th. We are currently closing additional deals for kickoffs through the end of April, which will put us at approximately $7.3M to $8.1M in ARR.

### Go-to-Market Process

- Prospects book time on our calendar through our website
  - Based on urgency and budget, either Marcel or another VP-level strategist conducts the initial meeting
- After the first meeting, we either disqualify the prospect or create an opportunity and follow our standard playbook
  - Add to Slack channel, send proposal to reserve kickoff date, and follow up with meeting recording
- Most opportunities then require either a few follow-up questions or one additional 30-minute meeting. For strategic or enterprise clients, we sometimes prepare a more detailed proposal or audit. While this typically takes 1–2 hours, it saves time during implementation.

While this process is lean and MVP, it's proving effective. We want to refine it while preserving the "magic."

While building capacity, we're currently limiting ourselves to 2 kickoffs per week (excluding U.S. holiday weeks). We plan to increase this to 3 kickoffs per week on average by the end of April.

### Key Observations

**Gross Margin:** Our gross margin is intentionally going down this year. That is primarily due to our model of hiring managing directors (MDs) and experts who serve as both sales, marketing, product development, and client delivery. We are accounting for 100% of their costs as COGS but we could change that. If you were to remove the MDs costs from COGS, our margins would jump from 59% to 74% for the year, ending Q4 at 81%.

**MRR per Headcount Cost:** We propose tracking MRR per headcount cost as a key metric of leverage. We want to ensure our entire business model continues to gain efficiency over time. We're focusing on dollars of recurring revenue per dollar of headcount cost rather than headcount alone.

**Future Revenue Streams:** We are not yet ready to forecast additional revenue streams from our platform, API usage, self-serve software, or incremental services. While we expect to see new revenue streams this year, focusing on forecasting them now would distract from our critical execution path.

## Burn Forecast

We are projecting to burn $6.7M this year while adding ~$20M in net new ARR. Our growth efficiency (Net New ARR Growth / Net Cash Burn) would be approximately 3, which is well above best-in-class.

About $1.8M of that would be for bonuses and performance based comp based on the company achieving revenue targets.

The strategy here is to be aggressive and around the time we hit $20m+ in ARR be ready to raise a favorable series B to enable us to consider even more aggressive expansion and R&D.

Our strategy relies on the fact that most of our burn comes from variable expenses that we can readily control. For example, we could achieve positive cash flow in Q4 by foregoing the company bonus, canceling the offsite, and reducing variable marketing spend. This puts us in a strong "default alive" position at any time we want to.
