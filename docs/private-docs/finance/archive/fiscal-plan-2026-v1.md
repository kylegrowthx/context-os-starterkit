## The Goal

Grow from $12.1M to $33.8M ARR while burning at most $7.6M.

That's 2.8x growth in 12 months. We did 9x in 2025. This is the next chapter.

---

## Why We Believe

2025 taught us three things. First, the Strategy Sprint model works. We went from idea to $12M ARR in 12 months because the sprint creates urgency, demonstrates value fast, and converts at 70%+. Second, customers who succeed with us stay. Our best accounts are expanding, referring others, and asking for more. Third, we have a real product in CheckThat. It's not vaporware. Thousands of people are already using it before we've spent a dollar on marketing.

2026 is different because we're not figuring it out anymore. We're running a proven playbook at scale. The unknown isn't whether our model works. It's whether we can build the systems, hire the leaders, and maintain quality while tripling volume.

This will be hard. 140 kickoffs means roughly 3 per week, every week, for 52 weeks. 80% renewal means keeping 50+ customers happy enough to re-sign. 75% gross margin means running delivery with discipline we haven't yet demonstrated. None of this is guaranteed.

But here's the thing: we've done harder. Going from $0 to $12M with no playbook was harder than running a playbook we've proven. The question isn't whether we can do this. It's whether we'll stay focused enough to do it.

## What Could Go Wrong

These are the risks that could derail the plan. Naming them creates accountability.

| Risk | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|--------|------------|
| Renewal rate comes in at 70% instead of 80% | Lose $1.2M in retained ARR. Miss target by ~$3M. | Weekly health reviews. Early renewal conversations. Fix at-risk accounts before they churn. |
| VP Sales hire takes longer than Q1 | Kickoffs stall at 2/week. Miss pipeline targets. | CEO stays hands-on in sales. Parallel search with multiple recruiters. Consider interim leader. |
| CheckThat launch doesn't generate expected pipeline | Miss $4.2M software ARR. Slower services lead flow. | Services business hits $29M regardless. CheckThat is upside, not the plan. |
| Delivery can't scale to 3+ kickoffs/week | Quality drops. Customers churn. Reputation damage. | Hire ahead of demand. Build playbooks. Invest in tooling. Monitor quality metrics weekly. |
| Key talent leaves | Institutional knowledge loss. Delivery gaps. | Competitive comp. Clear growth paths. Stay close to high performers. |

We don't control whether these happen. We control whether we see them coming and respond fast.

**Delivery:** You are the product. Every kickoff that converts, every customer that renews, every expansion that closes traces back to the work you do. Quality drives renewals. Speed drives capacity. **Everyone:** The plan works if we all execute. No single function can carry this. Sales needs delivery to convert. Delivery needs product to scale. Product needs customer feedback to prioritize. We're building something together. Act like it.

## The Math

### The ARR Waterfall

This is how we get from $12.1M to $33.8M:

```
Starting ARR (Jan 2026):          $12.1M
─────────────────────────────────────────
+ New ARR from kickoffs:          +$22.8M   (140 × 80% × $204K)
- Churn (20% of starting):        -$2.4M    (Risk: every 1% worse = $120K lost)
+ Expansion revenue:              +$2.0M    (Existing customers buying more)
- Churn on new ARR (partial yr):  -$0.9M    (Contracts signed later churn less)
+ CheckThat ARR:                  +$4.2M    (Upside, not required for base plan)
─────────────────────────────────────────
Ending ARR (Dec 2026):            $33.8M
```

The two biggest levers: **kickoffs** (volume) and **churn** (retention). Everything else is secondary.

### GrowthX Engine

Strategy Sprints are 8 weeks. They convert at 80% into 12-month Growth Execution contracts. That's when they become ARR.

| Input | 2025 Actual | 2026 Target |
|-------|-------------|-------------|
| Kickoffs | 74 | 140 |
| Sprint Conversion | 70% | 80% |
| ACV | $204K | $204K |
| Renewal Rate | TBD | 80% |

At 140 kickoffs × 80% conversion × $204K ACV = ~$23M in new ARR.
Starting ARR of $12.1M × 80% retention = ~$9.7M retained.
Plus ~$2M in expansion revenue.

That gets us to ~$29M from GrowthX alone. Add CheckThat and we hit $33.8M.

### CheckThat Engine

## The Bottom Line

We 9x'd ARR in 2025 from $1.34M to $12.1M. That was the "figure it out" phase. We found product-market fit. We proved the model. We built a team that can deliver.

2026 is the "scale it" phase. The playbook exists. Now we run it 140 times without breaking.

Here's what we're NOT doing this year:
- We're not chasing new verticals. B2B tech is our market.
- We're not building features that don't serve delivery or retention.
- We're not hiring ahead of demand. Efficiency matters.
- We're not treating CheckThat as optional. It's the bet that changes our trajectory.

Here's what we ARE doing:
- We're keeping customers. 80% renewal or bust.
- We're scaling kickoffs. 140 is the number.
- We're building systems. What works for 62 customers must work for 110.
- We're proving CheckThat. Software revenue changes everything.

The math works. The risks are known. The plan is clear.

Now we execute. Not perfectly. Relentlessly.

Every week, we ask: Are we on track? If not, what's blocking us? If so, what's next?

That's how we get to $33.8M. That's how we build something that lasts.
