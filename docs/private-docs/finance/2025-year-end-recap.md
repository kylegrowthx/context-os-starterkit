## Looking Ahead

We continue to be focused on two major priorities:

1. Make our core offering more predictable, scalable, and sticky
2. Turn CheckThat into a growth engine and data moat to win the AEO category

### Priority 1: Make Our Core Offering Predictable, Scalable, and Sticky

To get there, we're focusing on platform, people, and process.

**Platform:** Shipping the closed loop in OS (our platform) with Pages, Opportunities, and Assignments. The system will know your content, track performance, surface what to create or optimize, and turn opportunities into executed work. On track for end of January.

**Process:** Hyper-focused on setting new customers up for success. Pilots should feel like an extension of the sales process. Build trust earlier. More quality checks. Crystal clear on what we're working towards together.

**People:** Reorganized the entire org around one clear owner per account. No ambiguity on who's accountable. Deeper focus on outcomes, not just content quality. And we're hiring leaders. Too many doers running around, not enough managers.

### Priority 2: CheckThat as Growth Engine and Data Moat

The play: build the open AI visibility index for B2B. Make category data public. Track how AI answers buyer questions over time. Historical data can't be recreated. That's the data moat.

The Crunchbase/G2 playbook: public data → search rankings → traffic → more data → better rankings → more traffic. Three strategic benefits: high-margin revenue, lead funnel into OS, and intelligence feeding back into our platform.

We're moving fast:
- Launched the site with over 6,000 brand pages
- Pages getting indexed and already generating organic traffic
- Full product built end-to-end, already collecting data
- Sign-up, onboarding, and billing shipping in the next 2 weeks

We'll share more at the next board meeting.
