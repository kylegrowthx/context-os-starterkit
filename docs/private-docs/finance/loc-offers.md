## GrowthX Suggested Requests for JPM

### 1. Increase Initial Availability / Adjust Expansion Trigger

**Primary ask:**
- Increase day-one availability to ~$7M (vs. $5M proposed)

**Fallback / alternative:**
- Replace the Series B milestone for the second $5M tranche with a revenue-based milestone
  - Example: $30M ARR unlocks the additional $5M

**Rationale:**
- Aligns credit expansion with operating performance rather than financing timing
- Reduces dependency on capital markets while still de-risking JPM

### 2. Extend Interest-Only Period / Reduce Amortization Drag

**Primary ask:**
- Interest-only for the full term with bullet repayment at maturity (vs. 30-month amortization)

**Fallback / structured compromise:**
- Interest-only for full term if total amount drawn ≤ $3–5M
- Amortization applies only above that threshold

**Rationale:**
- Preserves cash during scaling phase
- Matches risk profile for smaller draws
- Brings JPM closer to alternate banks cash-flow flexibility

### 3. Equity / Warrants

**Primary ask:**
- No equity or warrants (vs. proposed 15 bps, 10-year exercise)

**Trade-off position:**
- Willing to concede warrants *if* JPM agrees to improvements in (1) availability and (2) interest-only structure

**Rationale:**
- Equity should compensate for real economic concessions
- Prefer to pay for flexibility with dilution rather than cash flow

### 4. Fundraising & Equity Issuance Consent (Clarifying Ask)

**Ask:**
- Explicit confirmation that equity financings, SAFEs, and internal corporate restructures do not require lender consent

**Rationale:**
- Prevents unintended lender veto over fundraising
- Preserves strategic flexibility
- Low-friction clarification that avoids future execution risk

## Response Email Template

Thanks again for sharing the draft terms - we've reviewed internally and with our Board, and wanted to follow up with a few items we'd love to discuss to see if we can get to a structure that works well for both sides.

**1. Facility Size & Initial Availability**

We'd like to explore increasing the total facility closer to $15M, with $10M available at close. This is more in line with other bank proposals we received over the holidays and would give us confidence the facility can serve as a true strategic buffer, even if we don't expect to draw the full amount near term.

**2. Series B Requirement / Expansion Mechanics**

Rather than tying the second $5M tranche to a Series B by a specific date, we're hoping to reframe this around operating performance.

Would you be open to unlocking the second tranche upon reaching a revenue-based milestone (e.g., ~$30M ARR), with definitions mutually agreed? This would align expansion with business fundamentals rather than capital markets timing.

**3. Interest & Amortization Structure**

We'd prefer an interest-only structure for the full term with a bullet repayment at maturity, rather than a 30-month amortization. This preserves cash during scaling phases and brings the JPM proposal closer to other offers we've seen that are more cash-flow flexible.

**4. Reporting**

We're not able to share board materials outside of the board itself, but we're very comfortable providing our quarterly financials, which are the same financial package we share with the board. Can you confirm that works for JPM?

**5. Equity / Warrants**

At this time, we're not able to obtain approval to issue equity or warrants to banking partners as part of the facility. It would be best to focus the economics on pricing and structure instead.

**6. Prepayment Flexibility**

Can you confirm there is no prepayment penalty if we draw on the line and later repay early?

**7. Fundraising & Corporate Actions**

Finally, can you confirm that customary equity financings (including SAFEs) would not require explicit lender consent?

We're very interested in moving forward with JPM and think these adjustments would get us to a structure that's durable and mutually attractive. Happy to walk through any of this live.
