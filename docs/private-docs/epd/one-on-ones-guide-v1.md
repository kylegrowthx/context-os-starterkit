## How to Prepare for Your 1:1s

This guide will help you get the most out of your 1:1 meetings with Leo and Daniel (I, Daniel, will read Leo's meetings and his notes).

---

## Why These Meetings Exist

These 1:1s are about **two-way context sharing** — not checking if you're working. We know you are.

The challenge we're solving is simple: our team is mostly very senior and everyone is quite capable, but we're spread across multiple lanes and projects. You lack context on what's happening elsewhere, and we will often lack context on the details of what you're building. That gap can create misalignment.

These meetings exist so we can:

- **Learn from you** — understand how things are getting built, in detail
- **Share context back** — what's happening in other lanes, why priorities are shifting, what's coming next
- **Align across teams** — so everyone has the full picture, not just their piece

The more we understand your work and what you are focused on, the better we can connect the dots across the organization.

---

## Before Every 1:1

Spend 5-10 minutes before the meeting thinking through:

### 1. What you're working on

- What did you ship or make progress on since last time?
- Is there something you'd like to walk through or demo?  
  (Highly recommend setting up Geekbot MCP to get reports on your own work ahead)

### 2. Blockers or anything that was not super clear?

- Is anything slowing you down?
- Are you waiting on a decision, clarification, or someone else?
- Is there something you're unsure about?

### 3. Direction and priorities

- Do you understand why your current tasks matter?
- Are you confident you're working on the right thing?
- Any concerns about the direction of your project or lane?

### 4. Ideas or suggestions

- Is there something you think we should be doing differently?
- Any improvements you've noticed we could make?
- Any product / project ideas?

### 5. Anything else

- Is there something on your mind you want to discuss?
- Any feedback for how things are going?

---

## What to Bring

A simple agenda is enough. It can be as short as:

```
- Walk through the new [feature] I shipped
- Blocker: waiting on design for [X]
- Question: not sure if we should prioritize [Y] or [Z]
```

You don't need a formal document — just a few bullet points so we can dive in.

---

## Meeting Types

### Leo's 1:1s (Every 2-3 weeks, ~30 min)

**Focus:** Understanding your work, surfacing challenges, sharing context

Leo will want to learn about what you're building and understand the challenges you're facing. These are good opportunities to:

- Walk him through something you built
- Raise blockers or confusion
- Share concerns about direction
- Ask questions about other projects or priorities

### Daniel's 1:1s (Monthly, ~45 min)

**Focus:** Same as Leo's but we can also cover feedback, performance, bigger picture

---

## Tips

- **Go deep.** We want to understand how things actually work, not just that they're "done." Walk us through the details.
- **Share what you're learning.** Technical challenges, tradeoffs you made, things that surprised you — this is valuable context.
- **Ask about other lanes.** If you're curious what's happening elsewhere, ask. That's what these meetings are for.
- **Raise misalignment early.** If something feels off or you're not sure why a decision was made, bring it up. Small misalignments are easy to fix.
