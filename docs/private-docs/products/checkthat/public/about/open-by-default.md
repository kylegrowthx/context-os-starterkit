# Open by Default

We made a deliberate choice when building CheckThat: make the data public.

Not some of it. The core index. Categories. Brands. How AI answers buyer questions. Visibility rankings. All of it. Free to browse. No signup required.

This wasn't an accident. It's the foundation of how we think the product should work.

---

## The Case for Openness

Right now, hundreds of companies are independently tracking how AI answers questions in their category. They each pay a tool. They each guess which prompts matter. They each build their own incomplete picture of the same landscape.

None of that work benefits anyone else.

This is like every company in a city building their own private map of the streets. Expensive. Duplicative. And every map is missing pieces that other companies have already found.

The alternative is a shared map. One that everyone can see. One that gets better as more people contribute to it. One where history is preserved and can't be erased.

That's what we're building.

---

## Why This Works

Public data creates a cycle that closed dashboards can't replicate.

When the index is open, people use it. They explore categories. They look up brands. They share pages with colleagues. That usage drives attention. Attention drives signups. Signups generate more tracking data. More data makes the index richer. A richer index attracts more people.

Closed dashboards don't have this cycle. Their data sits behind a login, useful only to the company that paid for it. No compounding. No network effects. No shared benefit.

We chose the model that compounds.

---

## What "Open" Means (and What It Doesn't)

**Open means:**

- Anyone can browse our category index and see which brands AI recommends
- Brand pages with research and AI visibility data are publicly accessible
- Our shared prompt library tracks questions across the entire B2B software landscape
- Historical trends are visible without a subscription
- AI answer pages show how different engines respond to buyer questions

**Open doesn't mean:**

- We're not giving away the personalized experience. Your brand's custom tracking, detailed insights, and competitive benchmarking are private to your workspace.
- We're not exposing raw data without context. Every public page has editorial depth. Human review. Real research. Not just scraped outputs.

The line is simple: shared intelligence is public. Your brand's specific analysis is private.

---

## The Historical Argument

There's a practical reason openness matters that goes beyond philosophy.

AI response data has a unique property: it can't be backfilled. If no one tracked what ChatGPT said about expense management tools in January 2026, that data is gone. There's no way to go back and retrieve it.

Every day we track is a day of history that can't be recreated. Six months from now, the companies that started tracking today will have six months of trend data. Companies that start then will have zero.

This is true for the shared index and for individual brands. The earlier the data exists, the more valuable it becomes. Open data means that history starts building for everyone from day one, not just for the companies that happened to pay early.

---

## What We Believe

We believe the best infrastructure is shared. The best data is cumulative. The best products give value before they ask for payment.

We believe that making AI visibility data public is not a risk. It's the only approach that creates a dataset worth building on.

We believe that companies should compete on how they use insights, not on whether they have access to them.

That's why we're open by default. Not as a marketing tactic. As a building principle.
