# The Shift

Search has flipped.

For twenty years, B2B buyers followed the same pattern. They searched Google. They clicked links. They read websites. They filled out forms. They talked to sales.

That pattern is breaking.

---

## Buyers Ask AI First

Today, a growing number of B2B buyers start their research inside AI. They open ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, or Google AI Mode and ask:

> "What's the best expense management tool for a 200-person finance team?"

> "Compare Ramp and Brex for a Series A startup."

> "Which CRM has the best Slack integration for sales teams under 50 people?"

They get conversational answers. Recommendations. Comparisons. Pros and cons. Sometimes with sources, sometimes without.

They make shortlists before they ever visit a vendor's website.

---

## This Isn't Coming. It's Here.

ChatGPT has over 800 million weekly active users. Google now shows AI-generated answers in a quarter of all searches. Claude is growing faster than ChatGPT did at the same stage. Perplexity has become the default research tool for millions of knowledge workers.

These aren't niche tools anymore. They're where decisions start.

And AI-generated answers convert differently than traditional search results. When a buyer asks AI for a recommendation and gets a clear answer, they act on it. The trust is higher. The intent is sharper. The path from question to shortlist is shorter.

---

## The Fragmentation Problem

Here's what makes this harder: every AI engine answers differently.

Ask ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, and Google AI the same question about expense management tools. You'll get four different answers. Different brands mentioned. Different rankings. Different reasoning. Different sources cited.

A brand that's the top recommendation in ChatGPT might not appear at all in Claude. A brand that Perplexity cites with links might get a passing mention in Google AI with no attribution.

There's no single "AI ranking" to optimize for. There are multiple, shifting, opaque systems that each form their own view of your market.

---

## What This Means for Brands

Every B2B brand now has a shadow reputation. AI engines tell a story about your company every time a buyer asks a question. That story is being written right now, with or without your input.

If you're visible, positioned well, and accurately described, AI becomes your most powerful distribution channel. Buyers arrive pre-sold. Sales cycles shorten. The demand is already qualified.

If you're invisible, misrepresented, or positioned as an afterthought, buyers never find you. They don't know you exist. You lose deals you never knew were happening.

This is the biggest shift in B2B discovery since Google. And most companies haven't started paying attention.
