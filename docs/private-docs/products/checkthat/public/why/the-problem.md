# The Problem

Brands can't answer three basic questions about AI.

---

## The Three Questions

**1. Do we show up?**

When a buyer asks AI "what's the best project management tool for remote teams," does your brand appear in the answer? Across which engines? For which types of questions?

Most companies don't know. They've never checked. They're guessing.

**2. How do we show up?**

When AI does mention you, what does it say? Are you the top recommendation or a footnote? Are you described accurately? Does AI know your latest pricing, your newest features, your actual positioning?

Wrong information in an AI answer is worse than no mention at all. Buyers trust what AI tells them.

**3. What should we do about it?**

Which buyer questions matter most in your category? Where are your competitors winning? What content would actually change how AI talks about you? What should you prioritize this quarter?

This is where every existing approach falls apart.

---

## The Current Approach Is Broken

The tools in market all follow the same playbook.

**Step 1: Pay upfront.** Before you learn anything about your visibility, you commit to a subscription. You don't know if the tool is useful yet. You pay anyway.

**Step 2: Guess which prompts matter.** The tool gives you an empty dashboard and asks: what questions do you want to track? You brainstorm 50 to 100 prompts based on instinct. Maybe you ask your marketing team. Maybe you look at what competitors might be tracking. You're guessing.

**Step 3: Wait.** Data trickles in over days or weeks. You start seeing AI responses to your prompts. Thousands of them.

**Step 4: Get a data dump.** You now have a dashboard full of charts showing which prompts mention you and which don't. Raw AI responses. Mention counts. Maybe a summary.

What you don't have: any idea whether you tracked the right questions. Any context on what buyers in your category actually ask. Any direction on what to do next.

You get data. You don't get direction.

---

## The Deeper Problem

There's something more fundamentally broken than the tools themselves.

Every company tracking AI visibility is doing the same research alone. Brand A tracks how AI answers questions in their category. Brand B does the same thing. Brand C does it too. They all pay separately. They all guess separately. They all learn separately.

None of that research becomes shared knowledge. How AI sees the expense management category, or the CRM category, or any category, stays locked inside private dashboards. There's no public benchmark. No shared understanding. No way to see the full picture without building it yourself from scratch.

This is wasteful. It's like every company building their own map of the same territory, never sharing what they find.

---

## What Brands Actually Need

The answer isn't more dashboards. It's not more data.

Brands need three things:

**Context before commitment.** See how AI views your category before you pay for anything. Understand the landscape. Know which questions buyers ask. Then decide what to track.

**Direction, not data dumps.** Don't tell me AI mentioned my brand 47 times this week. Tell me which questions matter most, where I'm winning, where I'm invisible, and what to do about it.

**Shared intelligence.** AI visibility data should be public and cumulative. When one company learns something about how AI sees a category, everyone in that category should benefit. The data gets better as more people contribute.

That's what we built.
