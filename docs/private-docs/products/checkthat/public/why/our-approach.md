# Our Approach

We're building the open AI visibility index for B2B.

That one sentence explains everything. The rest of this page explains why it works.

---

## The Model

Think about Crunchbase for startup funding. G2 for software reviews. SimilarWeb for web traffic. These companies became the source of truth for their domains by doing one counterintuitive thing: making the data public.

Crunchbase didn't gate funding data behind a paywall. They published it. Millions of people used it, shared it, contributed to it. That created a dataset no one else could replicate. Then they built premium tools on top of it.

G2 did the same with reviews. SimilarWeb did it with traffic data.

The pattern is the same every time: public data builds trust. Trust drives adoption. Adoption creates better data. Better data attracts more users. The cycle compounds.

We're applying this pattern to AI visibility.

---

## How It Works

We define B2B software categories. We map the buyer questions that matter in each category. We track how AI engines answer those questions daily, across ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, Google AI, and Gemini.

Then we make it all public. No signup required.

Anyone can browse any category. See which brands AI recommends. Understand how the landscape shifts over time. That data is free, open, and growing.

When a brand wants to go deeper, they add their own tracking. Custom prompts. Detailed insights. Competitive benchmarking. That's where the premium experience starts.

Every brand that tracks their visibility contributes data back to the shared index. The index gets richer. The benchmarks get more accurate. The category views get more complete. Everyone benefits.

---

## Why Buyer Intent

Most AI visibility tools start with the brand. They ask: "Where does our brand show up?"

We start with the buyer. We ask: "What are buyers actually asking?"

This changes everything.

When you start with the brand, you track mentions. You count how many times AI says your name. That's backward-looking and self-referential. It tells you where you are but not where the opportunity is.

When you start with the buyer, you track intent. You understand which questions drive decisions in your category. You see which brands AI recommends for each type of buyer, each use case, each stage of research. You find the gaps where buyers are asking questions and no one is a clear answer.

That's where the real opportunity lives. Not in tracking your name. In understanding the conversation.

---

## Why Open

Making data public isn't charity. It's strategy.

When AI visibility data lives behind closed dashboards, every company builds the same map alone. That's slow, expensive, and incomplete. No single company can map an entire category as well as everyone in the category can map it together.

When the data is public, it compounds. More brands tracked means fuller categories. More questions mapped means better benchmarks. More history means clearer trends.

The companies that join early benefit most. Not because they pay more. Because their data has been compounding longer. Historical AI response data can't be backfilled. Every day of tracking creates something that didn't exist before and can't be recreated later.

We're not building another AEO dashboard. We're building the shared infrastructure for understanding how AI sees B2B markets.

Open data. Buyer intent. Historical depth. Human editorial quality.

That's the approach. Everything we build supports it.
