# Track Your Brand

The open index shows you the landscape. Tracking your brand shows you your position in it.

---

## How It Works

You add your brand to CheckThat. From that moment, all the historical data from the shared index flows into your dashboard. You don't start from zero.

If your brand already appears in categories we track, you immediately see: which buyer questions mention you, how you're positioned relative to competitors, and how your visibility has changed over time.

Then you go deeper.

---

## Custom Prompts

The shared prompt library tracks 100,000+ buyer questions across the entire B2B software landscape. That's your starting point. Most of the questions that matter in your category are already being tracked.

But every brand has unique angles. Your specific use cases. Your actual buyer personas. The competitors you care about most. The way your customers describe the problems you solve.

Custom prompts let you track the questions that matter specifically to you. Write prompts that reflect how your buyers actually research and evaluate software. We review them, activate them, and track them across AI engines alongside everything else.

The shared library gives you breadth. Custom prompts give you depth. Together, they give you a complete picture of how AI sees your brand in context.

---

## Multi-Engine Tracking

Every prompt gets tracked across ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, Google AI, and Gemini.

This matters because each engine answers differently. You might be the top recommendation in ChatGPT and invisible in Claude. You might get cited with links in Perplexity and mentioned without attribution in Google AI.

Your dashboard shows how each engine talks about you. Where you're strong. Where you're absent. How those positions shift over time.

---

## Insights, Not Just Data

Tracking AI responses is useful. Understanding what they mean is more useful. Knowing what to do about it is what actually matters.

CheckThat answers the three questions:

**Where do you show up?** Across which engines, for which buyer questions, in which categories. Not a vanity metric. A map of your actual visibility.

**How are you positioned?** Are you the first recommendation or the last? Are you described accurately? Are you positioned the way you want to be? How does your positioning compare to competitors in the same category?

**What should you do next?** Which gaps matter most? Where are competitors winning that you could challenge? Which buyer questions in your category are underserved? What content, messaging, or positioning changes would have the biggest impact?

The goal is direction. Not just charts that tell you where you stand, but a clear view of where to go.

---

## Competitive Benchmarking

Your visibility only means something in context.

CheckThat tracks your competitors in the same categories, for the same buyer questions, across the same engines. You see how you compare. Where you lead. Where they lead. How the competitive landscape shifts week over week.

This isn't abstract market research. It's your category, your competitors, your buyer questions. Updated continuously.
