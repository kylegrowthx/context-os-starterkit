# The Open Index

CheckThat is an index, not a dashboard.

That distinction matters. Dashboards are private. You log in, you see your data, you log out. No one else benefits from your presence on the platform.

An index is shared. It's a public resource that gets more valuable as more people use it and contribute to it. Crunchbase did this for startup funding. G2 did it for software reviews. We're doing it for AI visibility.

---

## What the Index Contains

We track how AI engines answer B2B buyer questions. The index organizes that data into a browsable, interconnected structure.

**1,740 B2B software categories.** Every meaningful software market where buyers ask AI for recommendations. Expense management. CRM. Project management. AI agents. Low-code platforms. Each category maps the competitive landscape as AI sees it.

**5,828+ brands.** Companies, products, and tools tracked across the index. For each brand, we build deep context: what they do, who they serve, how they're positioned, what AI says about them across engines.

**100,000+ buyer prompts.** Questions that reflect how real buyers research and evaluate software. Built with human editorial review. Not keyword variations or generic queries. Real buyer questions.

**2.6M+ AI responses.** Tracked daily across ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, Google AI, and Gemini. Which brands get mentioned. How they're described. What sources AI cites. How answers shift over time.

---

## Why It's Public

Every piece of data in the shared index is free to browse. No login. No paywall. No gated content.

We made this choice for a practical reason: public data compounds in ways private data can't.

When a CMO at a Series B startup browses the expense management category, they see the full landscape. They don't need to commit to a tool first. They don't need to guess which prompts to track. The data already exists. They can evaluate whether deeper tracking is worth it based on real information, not a sales pitch.

When that CMO decides to track their brand, their data feeds back into the shared index. The category gets richer. The benchmarks get more precise. Everyone who browses that category in the future benefits.

The longer the index exists, the more valuable it becomes. Historical AI response data can't be backfilled. The data we tracked last month is data that will never exist anywhere else.

---

## The Editorial Layer

This isn't a scraping operation.

Every brand in the index gets genuine research. Product details. Positioning. Competitive context. Buyer personas. We don't just pull what's on a company's website. We build real context that helps AI tracking produce meaningful results.

Every prompt in the shared library goes through human editorial review. We don't auto-generate thousands of generic questions. We study how buyers actually research software and craft prompts that reflect real decision-making behavior.

Every answer page includes our own editorial take, not just what AI says. We create comprehensive answers to buyer questions, then layer in data on how AI engines respond to those same questions.

This combination of editorial depth and AI tracking data is what makes the index valuable. It's not thin. It's not auto-generated. It's researched, reviewed, and continuously improved.

---

## How It Grows

The index is designed to compound.

More brands tracked means fuller categories. Fuller categories mean better benchmarks. Better benchmarks attract more attention. More attention drives more signups. More signups generate more tracking. The cycle repeats.

This only works because the core data is public. If we gated everything behind a paywall, there would be no cycle. Just a product with a sales funnel.

We chose the approach that creates lasting, compounding value for everyone who participates.
