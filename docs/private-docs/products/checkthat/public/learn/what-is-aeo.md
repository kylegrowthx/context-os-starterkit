# What is AEO?

Answer Engine Optimization is the practice of improving how your brand appears when AI engines answer questions about your market.

That's it. No mystery. No buzzword fog.

If SEO is about ranking in search results, AEO is about being recommended in AI answers.

---

## The Difference from SEO

SEO and AEO share a goal: make your brand visible when buyers are looking. But the mechanics are different.

**SEO operates on links.** You publish a page. Search engines crawl it. They rank it based on relevance, authority, and technical quality. A buyer searches, sees a list of links, and clicks one. You win by being the best link.

**AEO operates on answers.** A buyer asks an AI engine a question. The engine synthesizes an answer from everything it knows: web content, reviews, forums, social media, databases. It recommends specific brands. The buyer reads the answer and acts on it. There's no link to click. There's a recommendation to follow.

In SEO, you compete for position in a list. In AEO, you compete to be the recommendation in a conversation.

---

## Why B2B Is Different

AEO matters for every type of business, but it hits B2B differently.

B2B buyers don't impulse-buy. They research. They compare. They evaluate. They ask detailed questions with specific constraints: budget, team size, industry, integration requirements, compliance needs.

These are exactly the kinds of questions AI engines are built to answer well. A buyer asking "what's the best CRM for a 50-person real estate team with a $500/month budget" gets a much better answer from AI than from a Google search results page full of generic listicles.

The more specific the question, the better AI performs relative to traditional search. B2B buying is full of specific questions. That's why AI is reshaping B2B discovery faster than consumer discovery.

---

## How It Works in Practice

Here's what a B2B buying journey looks like when AI is involved.

A VP of Marketing at a growing fintech company needs to evaluate expense management tools. In the past, she'd search Google, click through ten websites, fill out three demo forms, and spend weeks comparing options.

Now she opens ChatGPT and types:

> "I'm the VP of Marketing at a 200-person fintech. We need an expense management tool that integrates with NetSuite, handles international currencies, and costs under $15 per employee per month. What should I consider?"

She gets an answer. Specific brands named. Pros and cons for each. Pricing context. Integration details. She follows up:

> "Compare Ramp and Brex for my use case."

Another answer. More detailed. She asks about implementation timelines, customer support quality, hidden fees. Each answer gets more specific.

By the time she talks to a sales rep, she's already made her shortlist. She's already formed opinions about each vendor. The AI conversation shaped her decision before any human salesperson got involved.

For the brands that appeared in those answers, AI just generated a warm, qualified lead. For the brands that didn't, that deal never existed.

---

## Why Most Companies Aren't Ready

AEO is new enough that most B2B companies haven't thought seriously about it.

They track their Google rankings. They know their domain authority. They measure organic traffic. But they have no idea what ChatGPT says when a buyer asks about their category.

The ones who have started looking often use tools that give them data without direction. They track whether they're mentioned but don't understand why. They have dashboards but no strategy.

AEO isn't just a new metric to track. It requires understanding how AI forms its recommendations, what signals drive visibility, and how buyer questions actually shape the conversation. It's a different way of thinking about brand visibility.

---

## The Bottom Line

AEO is the practice of making sure AI engines accurately and favorably represent your brand when buyers ask questions.

It matters because AI is becoming the first stop in B2B research. Not the future. Now.

The brands that understand this early will build visibility that compounds. The brands that wait will wonder why their pipeline dried up.
