# Our Methodology

Transparency builds trust. Here's how we approach data quality at CheckThat.

---

## How We Define Categories

We map B2B software into categories based on how buyers think about their options. Not how analysts organize reports. Not how vendors describe themselves.

Our category definitions are human-driven. We research each market, identify the meaningful segments, and define categories that match real buying behavior. Some map neatly to established categories. Others reflect emerging markets where the boundaries are still forming.

Each category gets editorial review. We verify that the brands assigned to it actually compete there. We check that the category name matches how buyers describe their needs. We update categories as markets evolve.

Currently: 1,740 B2B software categories mapped and maintained.

---

## How We Research Brands

Every brand in the index gets real research. Not scraped metadata. Not auto-generated profiles.

We build context on each brand: what products they offer, who they serve, how they're positioned, who their competitors are, and what their buyers care about. This research draws from public sources: company websites, review platforms, funding databases, news coverage, and community discussions.

This context isn't just for the public index pages. It informs how we write prompts, how we interpret AI responses, and how we generate insights. The deeper our understanding of a brand, the more useful the tracking data becomes.

---

## How We Build the Prompt Library

The shared prompt library contains 100,000+ buyer questions, and it's growing.

Every prompt goes through human editorial review before it enters the library. We don't auto-generate prompts from keywords or use templated variations of generic queries.

Our process:

1. We study how buyers actually research and evaluate software. What do they ask on Reddit? What questions come up in sales calls? How do they describe their needs when talking to AI?
2. We write prompts that reflect those real behaviors. Specific. Contextual. Reflective of actual decision-making.
3. We review each prompt for quality: Does this sound like something a real buyer would ask? Does it reflect genuine intent? Would tracking this produce actionable data?
4. We map prompts to the relevant categories and brands, so the tracking data is contextualized from the start.

The result is a library that tracks what buyers actually ask, not what we think they might ask.

---

## How We Track AI Responses

We monitor multiple AI engines daily: ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, Google AI, and Gemini.

For each prompt, we capture the full AI response. Then we extract structured data: which brands were mentioned, how they were described, what position they appeared in, what sources the AI cited, and the overall sentiment of the recommendation.

We track this consistently over time. Daily tracking means we can show how AI answers change week over week, month over month. Which brands are gaining visibility. Which are losing it. How competitive landscapes shift.

This historical data is one of our most valuable assets. AI responses from three months ago don't exist anywhere else. That data can't be recreated. It can only be captured in real time.

---

## How We Ensure Quality

Scale without quality is noise. We invest in editorial depth to make sure the index is genuinely useful.

**For the public index:** Brand pages, category pages, and answer pages all receive editorial attention. We don't publish thin, auto-generated content. Every public page adds real value for the reader.

**For the prompt library:** Human review on every prompt. No prompt enters the shared library without being verified for realism, intent, and quality.

**For AI tracking:** We validate that our tracking accurately captures what AI engines say. We monitor for inconsistencies and correct them. We test across engines regularly to ensure our data reflects reality.

**For insights:** The recommendations we surface are based on real patterns in the data, not algorithmic guesses. When we say "you're invisible for this type of buyer question," we've verified that signal across multiple data points.

---

## What We're Building Toward

The index is a living system. It gets better over time.

More brands tracked means fuller categories. More prompts tracked means better coverage of buyer behavior. More history means clearer trends. More users means more feedback that improves quality.

We publish our methodology because we believe the best way to earn trust is to show how the work gets done. Not in exhaustive technical detail, but with enough transparency that you understand the principles behind the data.
