# How We Measure AI Visibility

Most AI visibility tools show you what AI said. We measure whether AI got it right.

That distinction changes everything about how tracking works, what data you get, and what you can actually do with it.

---

## The Idea Behind the Approach

Brand research has solved this problem for decades. Companies like Nielsen and Kantar don't just ask "do consumers know your brand?" They measure awareness, perception, accuracy, and the impact of campaigns on all three. They compare what consumers believe against what's actually true. They track it over time.

We apply the same logic to AI engines.

Instead of surveying consumers, we survey ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, Gemini, and Google AI. Instead of asking people "name three CRM tools," we ask AI engines the same buyer questions your customers are already asking. Instead of measuring ad recall, we measure whether AI recalls your brand when a buyer needs it.

The result is structured AI brand intelligence. Not a dashboard of raw responses. Not a list of mentions. A clear picture of how AI sees your brand, how accurate that picture is, and whether it's getting better or worse.

## Three Layers

Everything in CheckThat rests on three layers. Each one depends on the one before it.

### 1. Context

Context is the source of truth. It's what IS true about your brand, your market, and your buyers before we ask AI anything.

**Brand context** captures who you are: your products, positioning, pricing, differentiators, and competitive set. This is the answer key. When we measure AI later, we compare what AI says against what you've told us is true.

**Market context** captures the landscape: your category, all the players in it, how the market is structured, what's changing. We need to understand the full picture, not just one brand in isolation.

**Buyer context** captures how people in your category actually research and buy. Their roles, their criteria, their questions, their decision process. This is what tells us which questions matter.

Without context, AI responses are just text. With context, they become measurable signals.

### 2. Instruments

Instruments are the questions we use to survey AI engines. They're not random. They're designed to reveal specific things about how AI perceives your market.

We classify every question across multiple dimensions: what market it addresses, what the buyer is trying to accomplish, what type of question it is, who's asking, and what we're trying to measure.

The shared prompt library contains 100,000+ questions built from real buyer behavior. Sales call transcripts. Reddit discussions. G2 reviews. The actual language buyers use when they're evaluating software.

When you add custom prompts, you're adding depth to that foundation. Every prompt inherits the same classification system, so your data is structured and comparable from the start.

### 3. Measurement

Measurement is what we learn from the survey. Not the raw responses. The intelligence extracted from them.

This is where CheckThat differs from every other tool in the category. We don't just show you what AI said. We measure four specific things.

## What We Measure

### Recall

Does AI remember your brand?

This is the most fundamental question. If AI doesn't mention you when a buyer asks about your category, nothing else matters.

We test this the same way brand researchers test unaided awareness. We ask AI category questions and best-of questions without mentioning any specific brand. Then we check: did your brand appear? How often? In what position? Across which engines?

Recall rate tells you what percentage of relevant buyer questions surface your brand. Recall position tells you whether you're the top recommendation or an afterthought. Cross-engine recall tells you whether your visibility is broad or limited to one platform.

### Sentiment

How does AI describe your brand?

Not just positive or negative. The full picture. What attributes does AI associate with you? What story does it tell? Does it recommend you enthusiastically or with caveats? How does it describe you compared to competitors?

Sentiment reveals your brand perception in AI. It's the equivalent of a brand perception survey, except the respondent is the engine that's shaping buyer opinions every day.

### Alignment

Does AI's description match reality?

This is the metric no other tool measures. You define what's true about your brand. Your features. Your pricing. Your positioning. Your differentiators. Then we compare that against what AI actually says.

Is AI describing your product accurately? Does it have your current pricing? Does it understand what makes you different? Is it telling the story you want told?

Alignment turns brand context into a scoring function. It answers the question brands actually care about: is AI getting us right?

### Lift

Is it getting better?

Lift measures the impact of your actions over time. You publish a new comparison page. You get a G2 review surge. You launch a PR campaign. Lift tells you whether those actions changed how AI talks about you.

It works like a brand lift study. Track the same questions before and after. Measure the change in recall, sentiment, and alignment. Correlate with the actions you took. Know what worked.

---

## How These Connect

The four metrics work together. Recall without alignment means AI knows your name but gets the details wrong. Alignment without recall means AI is accurate when asked directly but doesn't mention you when buyers ask category questions. Sentiment without alignment means AI speaks positively about you but describes a different product than the one you actually sell.

You need all four. And you need them tracked over time, across engines, compared against competitors.

That's what the dashboard shows. Not a wall of AI responses. A structured view of how AI perceives your brand, where the gaps are, and what's changing.
