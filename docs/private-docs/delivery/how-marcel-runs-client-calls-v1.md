# How Marcel Runs Client Calls

A playbook for engagement managers. Based on the Knox partnership call (Feb 5, 2026).

---

## The Big Idea

Marcel doesn't sell. He thinks out loud with the client about their business. By the end, the client is convincing themselves to work with us.

The call feels like a conversation with a brilliant friend who happens to know a lot about their exact problem. Not a pitch. Not a demo. A shared exploration.

---

## The Playbook

### 1. Open warm, not corporate

Marcel spends the first few minutes being a human. Weather, travel, where they're based. It's fast but it's real.

He doesn't say "thanks for taking the time" or "I'd love to walk you through our capabilities." He says "hopefully it's not too freezing" and "makes you feel better, it's an awful 63 degrees and sunny here."

**Do this:** Start with something personal and light. Match their energy. If they're high-energy, meet them. If they're chill, be chill. Don't rush into slides.

**Don't do this:** Jump straight to "so tell me about your goals" or "let me share my screen."

---

### 2. Build credibility through shared context, not credentials

Marcel doesn't say "I have 15 years of experience in content marketing." He tells a story about working at IBM's security team and convincing them to publish zero-day vulnerability findings. Then he connects it directly to what the client is doing.

He mentions HashiCorp because the client's first customer forked Terraform. He mentions Scale AI because FedRamp was a big milestone there too. Every reference is relevant to their world.

**Do this:** Find the overlap between your experience and their business. Tell a 30-second story that makes them think "oh, this person actually gets our world."

**Don't do this:** List your resume. Name-drop without context. Talk about GrowthX's capabilities in the abstract.

---

### 3. Listen for the gold, then get visibly excited

When Irina described the government procurement data opportunity (RFPs, contracts, usaspending.gov), Marcel literally said "oh my God" and "I'm smiling because I'm so excited."

This isn't fake enthusiasm. It's genuine pattern recognition. He saw something that mapped to what worked for other clients and got fired up.

**Do this:** When the client describes something with massive potential, react. Say "that's a gold mine" or "this is as juicy as it gets." Let them feel your energy. They need to know you're not just going through the motions.

**Don't do this:** Stay neutral and analytical the whole call. Clients hire partners who give a damn.

---

### 4. Show, don't tell

Marcel screen-shared three times on this call:
- The Airbyte connector workflow (to show how agentic workflows work)
- The Biologic research supervisor workflow (to show editorial rigor)
- A client's organic growth chart (to show real results)

He didn't describe capabilities. He showed running systems with real outputs.

**Do this:** Have 2-3 relevant examples ready to screen share. Pick ones that connect to the client's situation. Show the actual workflows, the actual dashboards, the actual results.

**Don't do this:** Talk about what we "can" do in theory. No one cares about theory.

---

### 5. Diagnose their real constraint

Marcel identified Knox's bottleneck in one sentence: "Your limiting factor today in your ability to grow an organic compounding engine is your domain authority."

He didn't just diagnose. He explained why. Early site, no authority, Google won't even give you signals to learn from. Then he gave a metaphor: "You have to plant more quality seeds, but your seed strength is weaker because your domain doesn't have any authority."

**Do this:** Name the one thing holding them back. Make it concrete. Give them a mental model for understanding it.

**Don't do this:** List 10 things you could improve. That overwhelms. Find the bottleneck.

---

### 6. Give real advice on the call (don't hold back)

Marcel gave Knox actionable advice they could use whether they hired us or not:
- "Don't waste any opportunity on a press release without getting a link back to your site"
- "If you can get government links, that's like 100x"
- "Having procore.com link to you will be insane"

He gave away the thinking. This is counterintuitive but it works. The more you help on the call, the more they trust you to help after.

**Do this:** Give 2-3 pieces of advice they can act on immediately. Be specific. Name the sites, the strategies, the opportunities.

**Don't do this:** Say "we'll figure that out during onboarding" or hold back insights to create artificial scarcity.

---

### 7. Connect their problem to client proof

Marcel referenced five client examples on this 30-minute call:
- **IBM Security Intelligence** (link magnets from zero-day vulnerabilities)
- **Ramp** (vendor library that attracted a dedicated data science team)
- **Airbyte** (connector agents workflow)
- **Biologic** (medical compliance content with research supervisor)
- **Datagrid/Procore** (domain authority growth through strategic linking)

Every example was selected because it mapped to something Knox needed. Not random case studies. Relevant parallels.

**Do this:** Before the call, pick 3-5 client examples that connect to their specific situation. Have them ready to reference naturally (not as a slide deck).

**Don't do this:** Use the same case studies for every client. Match the proof to the problem.

---

### 8. Set honest expectations

Marcel said: "I don't want to BS you and be like, hey, it's going to be a magic button and you call it a day. We're going to need to decompose what quality means to your audience in layers."

And: "It might not get to 100% because that's nearly impossible all the time."

This builds trust faster than any promise. Clients know when you're overselling. Honesty is the shortcut.

**Do this:** Name what will be hard. Name what won't be instant. Then explain why it's still worth it. "It's not overnight, but we have the engine."

**Don't do this:** Promise fast results to close the deal. That debt comes due in week 3.

---

### 9. Map a clear starting point and a horizon

Marcel laid out a simple sequence: "Let's start with the editorial because you got to set some foundation. And then later on, as we prove ourselves first, then we can layer some programmatic things."

He didn't dump a 12-week plan. He gave them a logical starting point and a vision of where it goes.

**Do this:** Give them a sentence-level version of the roadmap. Foundation first, then scale. Simple, logical, and they can see the path.

**Don't do this:** Overwhelm with a detailed project plan on the first call. Save that for onboarding.

---

### 10. Close with humility and genuine excitement

Marcel ended with: "Thank you so much for even giving us a shot and trusting us. It's really incredible business you all are building and so excited to somehow help a little bit along the way."

Not "we're the best agency for this." Not "let's get the contract signed." Just gratitude and respect for what they're building.

**Do this:** Acknowledge what they've built. Express genuine gratitude. Make them feel like a partner, not a customer.

**Don't do this:** Hard close. Ask about budget. Push for next steps aggressively.

## The Patterns

| What Marcel Does | Why It Works |
|---|---|
| Tells stories instead of listing features | Stories are memorable. Features aren't. |
| Gets visibly excited about their business | People hire people who care. |
| Diagnoses the bottleneck, not the symptoms | Shows strategic thinking, not just execution skill. |
| Gives away advice for free | Builds trust. If the free stuff is this good, the paid stuff must be better. |
| Shows running systems, not slides | Proof beats promises. |
| Names what will be hard | Honesty creates trust faster than optimism. |
| Connects every example to their situation | Relevance beats impressiveness. |
| Closes with humility | Confidence without arrogance. |

---

## Before Your Next Client Call

Prep checklist:
- [ ] Research their business for 15 minutes. Know their domain, their competitors, their market.
- [ ] Pick 3-5 client examples that map to their specific situation.
- [ ] Have 2-3 things ready to screen share (workflows, dashboards, results).
- [ ] Identify what you think their bottleneck is. Be ready to name it.
- [ ] Prepare 2-3 pieces of advice you'd give them for free.
- [ ] Know their domain authority, current organic presence, and competitive landscape.

---

## The Bottom Line

Marcel's superpower is making the client feel like they just had the best conversation of their week. Not because he was charming (he is), but because he genuinely engaged with their business, showed them things that made them think, and gave them value before asking for anything in return.

The framework is simple: Be human. Know their world. Get excited about the opportunity. Show proof. Be honest about what's hard. Give away the thinking. Let the work speak for itself.
