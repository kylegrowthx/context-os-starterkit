## The opportunity is massive

Content is the atomic unit of marketing. Everything in marketing is technically content. If we get good at this, we earn the right to expand from some marketing jobs to be done to all of them.

We're solving a pain I've lived as a marketing leader for 15+ years:
- Content drives organic growth and compounds better than any other channel
- Doing it well at scale is way harder than it looks
- There are no tools that actually help
- Agencies are expensive and don't get it, freelancers are hit or miss
- AI alone produces slop that requires constant babysitting and comes with no strategy

To put this in perspective: there are 32,000 content agencies. If the average one makes a conservative $1M per year, that's $32B in annual revenue up for grabs.

No matter how you slice it, content is an insanely massive opportunity.

That's why we've never been demand constrained, despite not putting serious effort into sales and marketing beyond community and events.

Our challenge is simple: **How do we build leverage so we can serve more customers successfully?**

---

## Are we an agency?

The short answer is no. We're building a platform, but we realized there's no path to success without meeting the market where it is. That means customers need three things:
- Strategy
- AI workflows and tooling
- Human review and last-mile execution

So we run a forward-deployed delivery model. We position as an agency because it's the mental model customers understand.

Another way to frame it: **We're an AI-powered content agency with a platform operated by forward-deployed strategists and experts.**

Here's what matters though. We don't actually like services. We're not doing this because it's easier or because we prefer it. The opposite, in fact. Services are hard. Our goal isn't to think like a service business.

If we're doing something painfully manual and time-consuming, and weeks later you're still doing it the same way with no path to change and no additional leverage, we're doing it wrong. Simple as that.

Put differently: **every week serving the same customer should get easier, better, faster.**

That's what I mean by **leverage**.

## How we're building the business

We landed our first customers in April 2024 while I was still a CMO. By the time I quit, we had crossed $1M in annual revenue. We ran the company profitably until we raised our Series A ($12.5M), which was the first money we ever took.

Point is: we know how to turn a profit. But there's a real tension here. We have a massive opportunity in front of us, and winning it requires aggressive investment. At the same time, we can't become dependent on VCs to survive.

That's a tricky line. Once you raise money, many investors want you to keep burning so you keep needing them. We're playing a different game. We manage our financials so that people costs roughly match monthly revenue. This means we can get back to cashflow positive within a month or two if we need to.

Right now we have about $9M in the bank as of end of September 2025, and we're back to breakeven on a cashflow basis this month. We're burning very little given how fast we're growing.

To be clear: our goal is not to be profitable right now. There's too much to build and too much market to win. But we're building from a position of strength, not dependency.

**Fiscally conservative in structure, aggressive in execution.**

## The output numbers we care about

These numbers must hold true for long-term sustainability and to remain highly fundable if we choose to raise again:

- **GRR - 85%+** (retain $225K+ in MRR of the $265K up for renewal in Q4+Q1)
- **Gross Margin - 70% → 72%** (maintain and slightly improve)
- **NRR - 110%+** (add $60K in MRR upsells)
- **Burn - Maintain 18+ months of runway** (never dip below $6M in cash)
- **ARR/headcount** - Less critical but we can't just throw bodies at problems. This shows we're building leverage over time, not losing it.

Our goal: end 2025 at $15M in ARR (5x YoY) and end Q1 at $20M+ (4x YoY).

To be clear, growth isn't more important than the metrics above. But not growing isn't an option either. Our top priorities: retain and expand existing customers, land the right ICP customers, and prove scalability.

## What we need to optimize for

Our true north is simple:

> **Make every customer so successful that losing us would be their biggest business risk, and do it in a way that continuously improves with every output.**

Put differently: **we succeed only when customers consider us the best hire they ever made, worth 10x what they pay, and indispensable.**

Everything we do should drive one or both of these:
- **Retention** - Number of logos we keep (GRR) and our ability to deliver more value over time (NRR).
- **Leverage** - Scaling capacity without just throwing more people at it. Making it easier and better to serve the next customer, consistently.

Hit these and everything else follows. Everyone should be obsessed with these two vectors.

Getting there requires people, process, and tech.

Tech and AI give us the most leverage but take the longest to get right. Hiring more people has diminishing returns. Process is how we work, but enforcing and teaching it is hard.

### The controllable inputs

Here's what we can actually control. These five inputs drive retention and leverage:

- **Quality** - How good our work is
- **Output** - How much work we ship (published)
- **Performance** - How that work generates results customers care about (AI visibility, traffic, conversions)
- **Relationship** - How happy and satisfied customers are (would they buy again?)
- **Leverage / Capacity** - How many customers can we successfully serve with existing team?

Every decision, every process, every feature we build should move at least one of these forward. If it doesn't, we shouldn't be doing it.

## Top priorities: 4 workstreams that determine if we win

Everything we do ladders up to these four core workstreams. Each one is critical.

### Workstream 1: Retention

**Goal:** Make renewals inevitable through systematic value delivery

Every customer should feel like losing us would be a major set back. That doesn't happen by accident. It requires deliberate systems that consistently prove value. We need to deliver work that compounds, track what matters to clients, and make sure every workspace stays healthy and productive. When clients see continuous results and feel like we're always finding new opportunities for them, leaving becomes unthinkable.

### Workstream 2: Capacity & Leverage

**Goal:** Build systems that enable 10x scale without linear headcount growth

We can't just add more people to serve more customers. We need leverage. AI features that handle the heavy lifting, expert marketplaces that scale quality, and automation that makes onboarding and production faster. The goal is simple: one person should be able to manage many more clients than they can today, and the work should get easier over time, not harder.

### Workstream 3: Win AEO Opportunity

**Goal:** Own the AI visibility category before competitors define it

AI search is exploding. Answer Engine Optimization is the wedge. We need to own this space now, before others set the narrative. This means building the systems that identify opportunities, the workflows that capitalize on them, and the proof points that make us the obvious choice. This is our entry point to become the default platform for AI-powered growth.

### Workstream 4: Run the Business

**Goal:** Maintain operational excellence while scaling

This is where everything comes together. People, process, and tech.

- **People:** Who we hire, how we enable them, what they prioritize. The right talent in the right roles with clear focus.
- **Process:** How we work, deliver value, help customers win, and operate day to day. Systems that don't require heroics to function.
- **Tech:** What we build, how we drive adoption, how we distribute it. Building the right things and making sure they actually get used.

Without this foundation, the other three workstreams fall apart. Growth without operational discipline is just chaos with a bigger budget.

The opportunity is clear. The challenge is execution.

We have demand. We have proven unit economics. We have a model that works. Now we need to build the systems that let us scale without breaking what's already working.

These four workstreams aren't abstract goals. They're the daily work. Retention keeps the business healthy. Capacity lets us grow. AEO gives us a category to own. Running the business well keeps all of it from falling apart.

We're playing to win a massive market. But winning requires discipline, focus, and relentless execution on the fundamentals.
