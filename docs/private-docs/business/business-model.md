## Why This Model Exists

Every company that wants to grow organically faces the same bad options:

- **Agencies** — Expensive, slow, disconnected from your reality. Inconsistent quality.
- **Freelancers** — Hit or miss. Hard to find, manage, or retain anyone good.
- **In-house teams** — Costly to build, takes forever to hire, gets bogged down by meetings.
- **AI tools** — Cheap and scalable, but your team still does all the work. Strategy, setup, fixing outputs.

None of these solve the real problem: producing great content at scale, fast, without burning out your team or your budget.

**Our model combines:**

- Strategic leadership (think fractional CMO / Head of Content)
- Dedicated team (editors, SEO, production, design)
- AI workflows that do the heavy lifting
- Platform and tooling integrated end-to-end
- Forward-deployed engineers for customization

That's the hybrid. Human expertise where it matters. AI scale where it doesn't. One system that gets better over time.

## The Business Model

We sell **Strategy Sprints** (8-week paid engagements) that convert into **12-month contracts**.

This is how we de-risk the sale. Selling a $200k+ annual contract without the customer ever working with you first is hard. Sales cycles get long. Trust is low. Strategy Sprints solve this.

By the time we hand them off to Growth Execution, the relationship is de-risked. We know them. They know us. The work is already flowing.

## The Unit Economics

Here's what drives our revenue:

| Metric | What It Means |
|--------|---------------|
| **ACV (Annual Contract Value)** | What customers pay per year. We target $200k+ deals. |
| **Strategy Sprint Conversion** | Percentage of Sprints that convert to 12-month contracts. Target is 75-80%+. |
| **Monthly Churn** | Customers we lose each month. Every point of churn compounds against us. |
| **Monthly Expansion** | Additional revenue from existing customers. Upsells, added workstreams. |
| **Gross Margin** | Revenue minus cost of delivery. Target is 70%+ and improving. |

---

## The ARR Math

The math is simple. The execution is hard.

**Inputs:**
- How many kickoffs per week
- Conversion rate from Sprint to 12-month contract
- Average contract value
- Churn rate

**The formula:**

```
(Kickoffs per year) × (Conversion rate) × (ACV) = Net new ARR
```

Subtract churn. Add expansion. That's your growth.

### The Levers We Have

There are only a few ways to grow faster:

1. **More kickoffs** — Do more Strategy Sprints
2. **Better conversion** — Convert more Sprints to contracts
3. **Higher ACV** — Charge more per customer
4. **Lower churn** — Keep customers longer
5. **More expansion** — Grow existing accounts

Each lever has different effort and impact. Conversion improvements are incremental. Kickoff capacity is a multiplier but requires hiring and ramp time. Churn reduction compounds over time.

---

## Why Churn Matters So Much

Churn is the silent killer.

At 4% monthly churn, you lose ~40% of customers per year. To grow, you need to replace 40% just to stay flat. Everything above that is actual growth.

At 2% monthly churn, you lose ~22% of customers per year. Much more efficient. Renewals become the foundation, not the fight.

Small changes in churn have massive long-term impact. This is why retention is never not a priority.

## What It Takes to Scale

Every input has a constraint. Every constraint has a ramp time.

| Input | What It Requires |
|-------|------------------|
| **Sales pipeline** | Enough qualified opportunities to convert into kickoffs |
| **Sprint team capacity** | People to run Strategy Sprints (strategists, production, FDEs) |
| **Pod capacity** | Teams to absorb converted customers into Growth Execution |

To do more kickoffs, you need more Sprint capacity. To absorb more conversions, you need more pods. Each hire takes time to ramp. Everything compounds on everything else.

**This is why scaling a services business is hard.** You can't just flip a switch. You have to build capacity ahead of demand, hire the right people, train them, and maintain quality while growing.

## Gross Margins

Our target is 70%+ gross margins. Here's what drives them:

**What's in COGS (Cost of Goods Sold):**
- Delivery team compensation (EMs, Editors, Production)
- AI and compute costs
- Platform costs
- Contractor and freelancer costs

**What improves margins:**
- Platform leverage (AI doing more, humans doing less)
- System improvements (every week gets easier)
- Better onboarding (faster ramp to productivity)
- Higher ACV (same delivery cost, more revenue)

**What hurts margins:**
- Over-servicing (doing more than contracted)
- Churn (losing revenue, keeping costs)
- Quality issues (rework, escalations)
- Slow ramp (new hires not productive yet)

## Why the Model Scales

We have structural advantages that compound:

**1. AI does more over time**

The goal is for AI workflows to handle the majority of ongoing production work. Humans focus on strategy, calibration, and quality. As workflows improve, the same team can serve more customers at higher quality.

**2. Global talent**

International hiring gives us better cost structure and access to talent most companies can't reach. We can build world-class teams at a fraction of the cost of US-only hiring.

**3. Low sales costs**

Our leaders promote the business and close deals. Word of mouth and inbound drive most pipeline. We don't need a massive sales org to grow.

**4. We use our own product**

GrowthX runs on GrowthX. Our platform, our workflows, our content engine. We're our own best case study.

**Where this goes:**

As AI workflows mature and platform leverage kicks in, gross margins can expand significantly. The model gets more efficient over time, not less. That's the whole point.

## How Strategy Sprints De-Risk Everything

Strategy Sprints aren't just good for customers. They're good for us.

**For customers:**
- Lower commitment to start
- Prove value before signing 12 months
- Build trust with the team

**For us:**
- Paid discovery (we get paid to learn their business)
- Qualification (bad fits don't convert, and that's fine)
- Higher conversion (trust is built before the big ask)
- Faster sales cycle (8 weeks vs months of negotiation)

A 75% conversion rate on paid Sprints is far better than a 30% close rate on cold 12-month contracts. The Sprint model is a structural advantage.
