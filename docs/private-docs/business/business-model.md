## Why This Model Exists

Most companies face two bad options for content marketing:

**Agencies:** Expensive, slow, and hard to scale. You get expertise but at a premium, with no compounding.

**Tools:** Scalable and affordable but your team does all the work. The tool doesn't do the job. It just creates a different job.

Both fall short. Companies need outcomes, not more tools or more headcount.

**Our model combines:**

- **Service delivery** that produces actual outcomes, not just strategy decks
- **AI-powered workflows** that handle the repetitive, scalable work
- **Expert oversight** that ensures quality and strategic alignment
- **Continuous improvement** where every engagement makes the system smarter

This is service-as-software. The best of both worlds without the downsides of either.

## The Business Model

We sell 8-week Strategy Sprints as the entry point. These are paid engagements ($15K-$25K/mo) that de-risk the sale for both sides. The client gets to see our work before committing long-term. We get to prove our value before asking for a bigger commitment.

Strategy Sprints convert into ongoing Growth Execution contracts. Growth Execution is where the real value compounds: monthly retainers, pod-based delivery, and results that improve over time as our AI learns each client's context.

On top of services, we have SaaS products that generate recurring subscription revenue:
- **CheckThat** (freemium AI visibility monitoring) generates leads and expands into paid tiers
- **ContentOS** (content operations platform) is the core production engine
- **ExpertLayer** (expert marketplace) provides the human quality layer
- **Output** (open-source AI framework) builds technical credibility and long-term infrastructure

## The Revenue Math

Revenue growth is a function of five inputs, each one a lever you can pull.

**Inputs:**
- Number of new Strategy Sprint kickoffs per month
- Sprint-to-Growth Execution conversion rate
- Average monthly contract value
- Monthly churn rate (clients lost)
- Expansion revenue from existing clients (upsells, new services, product subscriptions)

**The formula:**

```
MRR Growth = (New Kickoffs × Win Rate × Conversion Rate × ACV) - (Existing MRR × Churn Rate) + Expansion Revenue
```

### Growth Levers

There are only a few ways to grow faster:

1. **More kickoffs** — Increase sales pipeline and sprint capacity. Constrained by team ramp time (~2 months per new sprint team member).
2. **Better conversion** — Improve the sprint experience so more clients convert to long-term. The sprint is the product demo.
3. **Higher ACV** — Sell larger scopes, more content pillars, or bundle product subscriptions with services.
4. **Lower churn** — Retain clients longer. Compounding results make this easier over time. Every month a client stays, the ROI improves.
5. **More expansion** — Upsell existing clients into additional services, new content clusters, or product subscriptions (CheckThat, ContentOS).

## Why Churn Matters

Churn is the silent killer of recurring revenue businesses.

At 4% monthly churn, you lose ~40% of your customer base annually. That means nearly half your revenue needs to be replaced every year just to stay flat. At 2% monthly, you lose ~22%. At 1%, ~11%.

The math is unforgiving. High churn turns growth into a treadmill.

Our model fights churn structurally:
- **Embedding moat:** As our AI adapts to each client's voice and context, switching costs increase. Leaving means losing all that accumulated intelligence.
- **Compounding results:** Content compounds over time. Month 6 is better than month 1. Clients who stay see accelerating returns.
- **Relationship depth:** Engagement managers become trusted advisors, not just vendors. The relationship itself becomes the product.

## Gross Margins

Target gross margin: 70%+. COGS includes delivery team compensation (EMs, editors, specialists), AI compute costs, and shared resource allocation.

**What improves margins:**
- AI leverage: more output per person-hour as workflows improve
- Cross-client knowledge compounding: what we learn from one client benefits all
- System improvements: every week serving the same client should get easier. If it doesn't, something is broken upstream.

**What hurts margins:**
- Custom work that doesn't scale or feed back into the system
- High editor-to-client ratios due to quality issues or complex industries
- Churn that forces constant onboarding of new clients (onboarding is the most expensive phase)

## Why the Model Scales

Four structural advantages:

**1. AI leverage compounds.** Each project teaches our workflows something new. The marginal cost of serving the next client drops while quality stays high or improves.

**2. Knowledge network effects.** What we learn from one client's industry improves outcomes for all clients in adjacent spaces. The more clients we serve, the smarter the system gets.

**3. Pod-based delivery.** Pods (EM + editors + shared specialists) are modular. Adding capacity means adding pods, not rebuilding infrastructure.

**4. Product revenue layer.** SaaS products (CheckThat, ContentOS) generate recurring revenue with near-zero marginal cost per user. Products also serve as lead generation for services.

The three-part engine (Services to Technology, Technology to Marketplace, Marketplace to Services) creates compounding loops. Each turn of the flywheel makes the next turn easier.
