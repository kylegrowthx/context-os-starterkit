## The Point

Not every company that wants to work with us should.

We charge $200k+/year. We require a real champion. We need companies that can move fast and trust us to do our job. When the fit is wrong, everyone loses. The customer is frustrated. The team is burned out. The work suffers. We churn them anyway.

The ICP exists to protect both sides.

---

## The Short Version

**We work best with:**

- B2B tech companies
- $10M+ raised or 500+ employees
- Growing (recent raise or 20%+ headcount growth)
- Domain authority 40+ (established web presence)
- Publishing content already (they believe in it)
- Single decision-maker who gets content marketing
- Fast approval process (days, not weeks)
- Standard B2B SaaS (not highly regulated or deeply technical)

**We struggle with:**

- Early-stage companies still figuring out positioning
- Committee decision-making
- Micromanagers who don't trust the process
- Highly regulated industries (legal, medical, finance compliance)
- Companies that expect instant results
- No budget authority on the call

---

## Why These Criteria Matter

### Company Stage

We need companies with money and stability.

$10M+ raised or 500+ employees means they can afford us, they're not going to disappear, and they have real marketing budgets. Early-stage companies churn. They run out of money. They pivot. They're not ready for a $200k/year commitment.

### Domain Authority

Higher DA means their content will actually rank.

If their domain authority is below 20, we're fighting uphill. Everything takes longer. Results are harder to show. They get frustrated. We get frustrated. DA 40+ means we can move faster and prove value sooner.

### Content Track Record

Companies already publishing content believe in it.

If they've never done content marketing, we have to sell them on the concept AND execute the work. That's two jobs. Companies publishing 2+/week or with 500+ pages already understand that content matters. They just need help doing it better.

### Growth Trajectory

Growing companies invest. Flat companies cut.

Recent raise or 20%+ headcount growth means they're in expansion mode. They have budget. They're thinking offense, not defense. Flat or declining companies are looking to cut costs, not add a $200k vendor.

### Champion Quality

This is the single biggest predictor of success.

We need one person with authority who understands content marketing and can make decisions in days. Not a committee. Not someone who needs 5 approvals. Not a micromanager who wants to control every word.

When we have a strong champion, everything works. When we don't, nothing does.

### Approval Process

Speed matters more than people think.

If it takes 2+ weeks to approve a piece of content, we can't iterate. We can't learn. We can't show results. The engagement stalls. Everyone gets frustrated.

Fast approvals (1-3 days) means we can ship, learn, and improve. That's how we prove value.

### Execution Complexity

We're great at B2B SaaS content. We're not great at everything.

Highly technical content (deep engineering, scientific research) requires specialized knowledge we may not have. Heavily regulated industries (legal, medical, financial compliance) require review processes that slow everything down.

Standard B2B SaaS is our wheelhouse. We can stretch into adjacent areas, but the further we go, the harder it gets.

## The Scoring System

We use a 100-point qualification framework across three stages:

| Stage | Max Points | Minimum to Proceed |
|-------|------------|-------------------|
| Account Potential | 25 | 13 |
| Engagement Score | 25 | 13 |
| Partnership Fit | 50 | — |

**Final score determines the path:**

| Score | Decision |
|-------|----------|
| 85-100 | Ideal client. Move fast. Assign best resources. |
| 70-84 | Good client. Standard process. Watch red flags. |
| 55-69 | Borderline. Default to CheckThat + Community. |
| Below 55 | Not ready. No exceptions. |

If they don't hit 70+, they go to CheckThat and AI Led Growth. That's not a rejection. It's the right fit for where they are.

## Red Flags

Walk away or route to CheckThat if you see:

- **"We need results in 30 days"** → Unrealistic expectations
- **"Let me check with the team"** on everything → No real authority
- **"We've tried content before and it didn't work"** → Skepticism baked in
- **Multiple stakeholders who disagree** → Internal conflict we can't solve
- **No existing content or very low DA** → Uphill battle from day one
- **Highly regulated industry** → Approval hell
- **Shopping multiple agencies** → Price-focused, not partnership-focused

## The Bottom Line

Our best customers share a few things:

1. They have money and are growing
2. They already believe in content
3. They have one champion who can decide
4. They move fast
5. They trust us to do our job

When we get this right, they expand scope, refer others, and renew without question.

When we get it wrong, we spend 6 months fighting for a relationship that was never going to work.

ICP discipline is how we win. Say no fast so you can say yes with force.
