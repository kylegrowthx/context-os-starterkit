## Overview

A customer moves from prospect to long-term partner through a deliberate sequence: qualification, a paid 8-week Strategy Sprint that proves value, conversion to ongoing Growth Execution, and expansion as results compound. The sprint is the product demo. The results are the sales pitch for the long-term contract. Every phase builds trust that makes the next phase possible.

```
Prospect → Qualified Lead → Week 00 Prep → Strategy Sprint (8 weeks) → Growth Execution (pods) → Renewal & Expansion
```

---

## Phase 1: Prospecting and Qualification

**Goal:** Identify companies that match our ICP and confirm mutual fit before investing resources.

**Duration:** 2-6 weeks

**Key activities:**
- Inbound leads through thought leadership, LinkedIn presence, CheckThat reports, and referrals
- ICP scoring against fit, engagement readiness, and commercial viability criteria
- Discovery call to understand their content challenges, current approach, goals, and decision-making process
- Strategy call to present our model and confirm alignment

**Exit criteria:** Prospect scores 45+ on ICP scorecard, champion identified, budget confirmed, contract signed for Strategy Sprint.

**Owner:** Sales team

---

## Phase 2: Week 00 Prep and Strategy Sprint (8 weeks)

**Goal:** Prove our value, build the content engine foundation, and earn the long-term contract.

**Duration:** ~9 weeks (1 week prep + 8-week sprint)

**Key activities:**
- **Week 00:** Sales handoff, deep-dive research on client's business/product/audience/competitors, workspace setup, initial strategy hypothesis drafted
- **Weeks 1-2:** Kickoff, stakeholder interviews, content audit, "What We Heard" document, artifact creation (Writing Guidelines, Company Context, Personas, Competitors, Calibration Guide)
- **Weeks 3-4:** Content quality rubric, editorial workflow establishment, first sample publications through the pipeline, approval bottleneck removal
- **Weeks 5-6:** Momentum building, early performance signals, ROI analysis, Week 6 circuit breaker decision (proceed or not)
- **Weeks 7-8:** Scale up production if proceeding, document processes, lock in Growth Execution contract. Or wrap up cleanly if not continuing.

**Exit criteria:** Week 6 decision made. If proceeding: Growth Execution contract signed, pod assigned, handoff brief created. If not: clean wrap-up with documented learnings and 90-day roadmap delivered.

**Owner:** Engagement Manager (with sprint team support)

---

## Phase 3: Growth Execution Onboarding

**Goal:** Smoothly transition from sprint to ongoing pod-based delivery without losing momentum.

**Duration:** 2-4 weeks (overlaps with end of sprint)

**Key activities:**
- Pod assignment (EM + editors + shared specialists)
- Director alignment on account strategy and content roadmap
- Dashboard setup for performance tracking and reporting
- Publishing cadence established (editors producing content weeks ahead, never scrambling week-of)
- LLM/AI visibility tracking configured

**Exit criteria:** Pod fully operational, publishing rhythm proven sustainable, performance data flowing, client confirms satisfaction with transition.

**Owner:** Engagement Manager (with pod team)

---

## Phase 4: Active Growth Execution

**Goal:** Drive measurable content-led growth. Every month should produce better results than the last.

**Duration:** Ongoing (monthly retainers, quarterly reviews)

**Key activities:**
- Weekly client calls following the Weekly Call Playbook (prep, accountability, performance review, strategy recommendations, next week alignment)
- Content production at cadence: strategy, creation, optimization, publishing
- Monthly health scoring across five dimensions (Output, Quality, Performance, Relationship, Strategy)
- Quarterly business reviews with comprehensive performance analysis
- Proactive strategy recommendations based on competitive landscape, AI visibility trends, and performance data

**Success metrics:** Content driving qualified leads and conversions from organic channels. Rankings improving. AI citation/visibility growing. Client expanding scope or referring peers. Health scores consistently at "Strong" or "Healthy" tiers.

**Owner:** Engagement Manager (accountable for renewals and expansion)

---

## Phase 5: Renewal, Expansion, and Advocacy

**Goal:** Retain clients as long-term partners, expand scope, and turn successful clients into referral sources.

**Key activities:**
- Renewal conversations seeded by compounding results (content ROI improves over time, making renewal an easy decision)
- Expansion opportunities: additional content clusters, new verticals, higher volume, product upsells (CheckThat Pro, ContentOS), new content types
- Account growth planning: identify next growth levers for the client's business
- Referral cultivation: satisfied clients become our strongest sales channel (word of mouth has driven nearly all GrowthX growth)

**Owner:** Engagement Manager (with leadership support for large expansions)

## Handoff Points

| From | To | What Gets Transferred | How |
|------|----|-----------------------|-----|
| Sales | Strategy Sprint team | Signed contract, discovery call notes, sales call recordings, ICP scorecard, champion contact info | Week 00 sales handoff meeting + internal prep doc |
| Strategy Sprint team | Growth Execution pod | Handoff brief (all artifacts, strategy docs, client preferences, stakeholder map, calibration guide), content calendar, dashboard setup, relationship context | End of sprint handoff meeting + director alignment session |

## Lifecycle Metrics

| Metric | Target | Why It Matters |
|--------|--------|----------------|
| Sprint-to-Growth Execution conversion rate | [To be confirmed] | The sprint is the product demo. Low conversion means the sprint experience isn't proving value. |
| Monthly client retention rate | 96%+ (less than 4% monthly churn) | Churn kills compounding. Every month a client stays, ROI improves for both sides. |
| Net revenue retention | 110%+ | Expansion revenue from existing clients should exceed losses from churn. |
| Time from signed contract to first published content | Within first 3 weeks of sprint | Speed to quality seeds. Fast delivery builds trust and proves we execute. |
| Client health score (average across portfolio) | 18+ (Healthy tier) | Leading indicator of retention. Scores below 15 signal churn risk. |
