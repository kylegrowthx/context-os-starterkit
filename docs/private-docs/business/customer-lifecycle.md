## Overview

Every customer goes through three phases:

| Phase | Duration | Goal |
|-------|----------|------|
| **Sales** | ~6 weeks | Qualify fit |
| **Strategy Sprint** | 8 weeks | Build trust, calibrate, set up for success |
| **Growth Execution** | 12+ months | Drive compounding growth |

The Strategy Sprint is where trust is built or broken. Growth Execution is where we prove compounding value over time.

---

## Phase 1: Sales Process

We have three layers of qualification before someone becomes a customer.

We're filtering for fit. Not every company is right for us. Not every deal is worth taking.

**The goal:** Make sure we can actually help them before we take their money.

### What We're Looking For

- B2B tech-enabled company
- Pressure to grow
- Found product-market fit
- Scaling
- Sustainable business model
- Established website
- Budget for $200k+/year engagement
- Right stakeholder involvement

### Sales Metrics

- 33% win rate
- ~6 week sales cycle
- Booking kickoffs 4-6 weeks out

## Phase 2: Strategy Sprint

This is where trust is built or broken. The Sprint is an extension of sales. We're still proving ourselves.

**Duration:** 8 weeks

**Conversion target:** 80%+ into 12-month Growth Execution contracts

### The Rule

**There can be no trust-busters in the Strategy Sprint.**

Move fast. Follow through. Attention to detail. Quality. Go above and beyond. Impress the hell out of them.

### What Happens in a Strategy Sprint

Four workstreams run in parallel:

#### 1. Context Engineering

We become experts in their business as fast as humanly possible.

- Research the company, product, market, competitors, audience
- Interview stakeholders
- Map internal dynamics and pressures
- Understand their metrics and what they're held against
- Identify challenges the business is experiencing

**The goal:** Seek to understand. Connect the dots. Give them insights on their own business that they don't have.

#### 2. Strategy Development

Turn context into a plan.

- Prioritize goals and target personas
- Find and cluster opportunities
- Define what content to create and in what order
- Identify signals to validate as quickly as possible

**The goal:** A clear strategy they buy into, with prioritized opportunities and a path to test what works.

#### 3. Calibration

Get aligned on quality and execution.

- Get their definition of "done" and "quality"
- Understand how they want to sound
- Learn their product deeply
- Identify no-go's, gotchas, patterns that work
- Decompose research requirements (sources they care about, what's important)

**The goal:** Build trust so we can execute without micromanagement. If we let garbage hit them, they lose trust and we're in trouble.

**Quality has layers:**
1. Context (did we get their business right?)
2. Voice (do we sound like them?)
3. Product knowledge (do we understand what they sell?)
4. Style (is the approach right?)
5. Research (are the sources credible?)
6. Technical nuance (are the details accurate?)

#### 4. Baseline Establishment

You can't prove value without a baseline.

- Audit existing content (what they have, quality assessment)
- Snapshot SEO (rankings, traffic, keywords, technical issues)
- Snapshot AI visibility (citations, mentions, competitor positioning)
- Connect analytics so we can measure what works

**The goal:** Know where they started so we can show where we took them.

### Building Trust and Confidence

They need to buy into the big picture strategy. They need early signals we're heading in the right direction.

**Trust signals come from:**

1. **How we operate** - Are we organized? Proactive? On top of things?
2. **How we communicate** - Over-communication builds trust. No surprises.
3. **Execution quality** - Output that won't ruin their reputation.
4. **Early performance signals** - Published? Indexed? Impressions? Clicks?

Not full ROI. Just early signs it's working.

### Setting Up for ROI

They need to see growth. Growth means conversions and transactions. But you can't start there.

**The outcome chain:**

1. Right strategy → Are we targeting the right opportunities?
2. Consistent quality content → Are we publishing regularly?
3. Indexed → Is Google/LLMs even seeing this? (If not, zero visibility)
4. Impressions and citations → Showing up in search and AI answers?
5. Clicks and traffic → Are people visiting?
6. Conversions → Are they taking action?

**Authority matters:**
- High authority brands → Faster visibility, can be more targeted
- Low authority brands → Need higher volume, go more long-tail to get signals

### The Conversion Question

Traffic is worthless if it doesn't convert. There are only a few things it can do:
- Sign up to talk to sales
- Sign up for the product
- Drive a transaction
- Engage with an offer

**We are not in the conversion rate optimization business.** We build the engine that drives high-quality traffic. But we need to help them think about conversion, because if they can't convert, they won't renew.

**Questions to ask at the start:**
- What traffic converts today?
- How do they convert?
- What are the paths to conversion?
- How do you measure it?

If they don't know, they need to fix that. Otherwise they can't prove the value of any initiative.

### What Good Looks Like

When we do the Strategy Sprint right:
- They want to double their scope
- They love working with us
- They tell everyone about us
- (See: Lovable, Ramp, Webflow)

### What Bad Looks Like

When we don't:
- Babysitting
- Disappointment
- They're paying a lot and they're not impressed
- Conversations about output counts instead of outcomes
- That's how we lose

## Phase 3: Growth Execution

After the Strategy Sprint converts, the customer moves into a 12-month contract.

They're handed off to a steady-state pod. The relationship is established. The context is built. The calibration is done.

Now we execute. Consistently. At scale. Driving compounding growth.

### What Growth Execution Is Really About

It's not "here's 5 articles this week, here's the next 5."

It's continuously pushing the frontier of what's possible for them.

- Building leverage
- Picking the right shots on goal
- Paying attention to signals
- Doubling down on what works

### Every Page Is an Asset

Assets need to be nurtured and improved over time.

**Our systems need to surface:**
- Pages that are decaying
- Pages that are improving
- Pages that are trending
- Critical issues to fix
- New opportunities to create
- Cross-linking opportunities
- Pages that need more media or images
- Broken links
- Fact-checking needs
- Thin content that needs enrichment

### Proactive, Not Reactive

**The goal:** They never flag an issue for us. We flag it first. We fix it proactively.

If a customer tells us about a problem we didn't catch, we failed.

### Every Week Is an Opportunity

- Build trust
- Sell the work we're doing and the results we're generating
- Talk about signals (good or bad) and what we're doing about them
- Get prioritization input
- Understand what's happening in their business
- Build relationships with new stakeholders

### The Wrong Conversation

"Can you do 6 articles instead of 5?"

That means we've lost the plot. We're not selling growth anymore. We're selling output. That's a bad sign.

### The Right Conversation

"Here's what's working. Here's what we're doubling down on. Here's the opportunity we see next. Here's how the system is getting smarter."

That's the conversation that leads to expansion and referrals.
