# Time Off Policy

## Practical Side: How Time Off Works at GrowthX

At GrowthX, taking time off is about the **time you need**, not about adhering to a fixed **quota**.

- **Unlimited, Need-Based Time Off**: We don't have a strict limit or 'quota' for holidays at GrowthX. Instead, we trust you to take the time off that you need to recharge, focus on your life, and maintain a healthy work-life balance. We don't keep track of your holidays, nor do we 'pay out' unused time off at any point.

- **Guideline**: As a general rule of thumb, ~4 weeks of time off annually is a healthy range. Less than that may not give you the proper rest you need, while more than that might need some checking in with your lead to ensure balance with your team.

- **National Holidays**: We generally adhere to commonly recognized national holidays, which amounts to around 8 days a year. However, we understand that people have different traditions and holidays they value. Whether it's Eid al-Fitr or Christmas, you're free to decide if you want to work or take the day off. We don't dictate your preferences.
  - For our international team, please feel free to take your own country's national holidays.
  - For our US-team, we observe federal holidays.

- **Team Agreement**: Always discuss your time off with your team and manager. We are all in this together, and clear communication helps maintain trust and efficiency. If you are taking a day or two off, give at least 2 weeks notice. If you are taking 1+ weeks off, 4+ weeks notice is requested.

- **Calendar Updates**: Make sure you add your time off to both your personal calendar and invite your team/pod/manager to the calendar invite, so everyone stays in the loop (also add team@growthxlabs.com to the calendar invite).

- **Slack status**: Please update your Slack profile with a 🌴 to indicate you are on PTO. Bonus points if you add a status for the dates you'll be out for visibility e.g. "OOO until 4/3"

- **Life Events**: Life is unpredictable, and sometimes things come up. Whether it's a sickness, a family emergency, or a personal health day, communicate openly with your lead about what you need. For example: "Hey team, I'll be out for the next few days. I had something come up with my family, and I'll be out of pocket until next week."

Above all, **enjoy** your time. Whether it's getting a haircut or going to the dentist during typical work hours, we trust you to make the best choices for yourself and GrowthX.

---

## Mutual Expectations: Making It Work

- **Flexibility Goes Both Ways**: The flexibility we offer must be reciprocated with responsibility and respect towards the team. Balance is key.
- **Take Enough Time Off**: Since there's no payout for unused days, make sure to take the time you need. We want you to be well-rested and at your best.
- **Clear Communication**: Agree on your dates with your team and make sure it's reflected in the shared calendar so everyone is aware of each other's availability.

## The Philosophy Behind Our Time Off Policy

At GrowthX, we believe that traditional rules and quotas around time off are limited. We operate from the standpoint of what you **need** rather than what's allocated.

### Work Hard, Rest Well

We work hard, and we need adequate rest to keep our minds and bodies functioning at their best. Sometimes we work harder and need more rest, and sometimes we work less and need less. It's about listening to yourself and responding appropriately.

We also recognize that we have something called **life**, which inevitably presents moments when work has to take a backseat. Whether that's a family emergency or an unexpected opportunity, GrowthX wants you to have the freedom to prioritize what's important.

Maintaining **solid time off** practices is crucial for sustainable work. Our approach is as simple as an on-off switch: give it your all when working, and take adequate time to fully recharge when you're off.

### Not About Hours, About Outcomes

The concept of work is not always measured in time (like 40 hours a week). Most of the people at GrowthX don't keep strict track of hours because time alone is a limited way to measure impact. Instead, we focus on outcomes and trust that people will balance their workloads effectively.

This is why **we don't keep track of holidays** in a strict sense. We expect our people to take responsibility for both their contribution to GrowthX and their personal well-being. This is not a matter of entitlement but rather a moral obligation to yourself, your loved ones, and the company.

---

## Closing Thoughts

We aim to create an environment where **work-life harmony** is a reality. Taking enough rest is just as crucial as putting in the effort when you're working. The GrowthX approach to time off is designed to encourage you to take responsibility, find your balance, and ensure you're always at your best—both for yourself and for the company.
