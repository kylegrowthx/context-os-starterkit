# Client ops team lead

## TL;DR

- The team lead combines hands-on IC work (60–70%) with team enablement and leadership (30–40%).
- Responsible for recruiting, coaching, special projects, and team coordination.
- Works closely with the product/project manager on cycle planning, capacity, and escalations.
- Pragmatism over perfection — ship working solutions, iterate based on real needs.

## What this role does

The client ops team lead bridges individual contribution with team leadership. You spend the majority of your time doing high-value engineering work — special projects, complex implementations, proof of concepts. The rest goes to making the team better — coaching, recruiting, unblocking, and maintaining quality standards.

You lead the [client ops engineering team](/epd/client-ops-engineering) — the forward-deployed engineers who embed with delivery pods.

## Team structure

| Role | Focus |
|------|-------|
| **Team lead (you)** | Recruiting, coaching, special projects, team coordination, stakeholder support |
| **Product/project manager** | Triage, prioritization, cycle planning, client coordination |
| **Senior engineers** | Complex technical projects, code reviews |
| **Forward-deployed engineers (L1 & L2)** | Standard client implementations and workflow builds |

## Key responsibilities

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Enablement and coaching">
    - **Onboard new team members** — Guide new hires through the platform, processes, and client workflows
    - **Knowledge transfer** — Create and maintain documentation for common implementation patterns
    - **Skills development** — Help junior engineers level up in AI workflows, CMS integrations, and client communication
    - **Interview support** — Participate in candidate interviews to assess fit for forward-deploy roles
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Team support and coordination">
    - Work closely with the PM on cycle planning, capacity allocation, client escalations, process improvements, and quality standards
    - Unblock junior engineers when they get stuck on technical or client issues
    - Identify blockers in daily standups and coordinate solutions
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Special projects">
    - High-stakes or complex client accounts alongside senior engineers
    - Novel AI workflow or framework architecture alongside infra and future ML/AI teams
    - Proof of concepts — build and test new workflow patterns, new APIs
    - Client strategy sprints — support pre-engagement discovery to determine what's possible
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Quality and process">
    - Establish and enforce quality standards for client implementations
    - Identify bottlenecks and inefficiencies in team workflows
    - Assist with scoping and technical feasibility for new client opportunities
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Collaboration model

**With the PM:** The PM handles tickets, sprint planning, prioritization, and client pipeline reviews. You complement with technical guidance on scoping and feasibility, team capacity planning, capability assessment, and strategic problem-solving for complex clients.

**With senior engineers:** Code review pipeline, special projects collaboration, and technical escalation support.

**With junior FDEs:** First line of support for questions and guidance. Quality review before work goes to clients. Hands-on mentoring through pair programming or workflow building sessions.

## Time allocation

- **30–40%** on team lead work — monitor standups, Linear board, deal desk support, recruiting, training material
- **60–70%** on focused IC work on whatever is most valuable

## Accountabilities

| What you own | How it's measured |
|-------------|-------------------|
| Team capability | Engineer skill growth, onboarding speed, quality consistency |
| Recruiting pipeline | Hiring velocity, candidate quality, team growth |
| Special project delivery | Successful completion of high-stakes implementations |
| Team health | Retention, engagement, blockers resolved quickly |

## What great looks like

- The team is growing in capability — engineers handle increasingly complex work independently
- New hires ramp quickly because onboarding and documentation are excellent
- Special projects ship successfully and become patterns others can follow
- You stay connected to the technical reality by doing real work, not just managing
- The team trusts you because you lead by doing, not just directing

## Working principles

1. **Pragmatism over perfection** — Ship working solutions, iterate based on real client needs
2. **Enable** — Help the team grow their capabilities. Flag performance issues early.
3. **Horizontal support** — Help across deal desk, strategy, delivery, hiring, and coaching
4. **Lead by doing** — Maintain hands-on work to stay connected to team reality

## Key relationships

| Who | How you work together |
|-----|----------------------|
| Product/project manager | Close partnership on planning, prioritization, and escalations |
| Senior engineers | Technical collaboration on complex projects and code reviews |
| Forward-deployed engineers | Coaching, mentoring, unblocking, quality review |
| Engagement managers | Understand delivery needs, support technical scoping |
| Engineering leadership | Team growth planning, hiring strategy, career development |
