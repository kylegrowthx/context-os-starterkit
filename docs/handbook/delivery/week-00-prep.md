# Week 00 — before kickoff

Before each new engagement, read this guide. It outlines how we prepare to hit the ground running at kickoff.

<Note>
**Mission:** Thoroughly research the client's business, product, audience, and competitive environment so you can walk into the kickoff meeting (Week 1) ultra-prepared — and instantly earn trust.
</Note>

## The prep process

<Steps>
  <Step title="Gather context from sales and prior conversations">
    ### Sales handoff notes
    - Request recordings or notes from sales calls
    - Identify client's top priorities and any special stakeholder preferences
    - Note any immediate deliverables the client expects post-signing

    ### Existing client documents
    - Gather brand decks, style guides, pitch materials, or any existing content audits
    - If missing, politely request from your internal team or the client's point of contact

    ### Consolidate in a prep doc
    - Create a document titled "Week 00 Research Overview"
    - Organize findings under **Company Background**, **Product/Service**, **Audience & Goals**, **Competitors**, **Brand Voice**

    <Tip>
    Summarize major themes under a "What stands out" bullet list for each category. This lets you spot crucial insights at a glance.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Deep-dive company and product understanding">
    ### Website and collateral scan
    - Study the client's homepage, about, product pages, solution briefs, and case studies
    - Ask yourself: "Which USPs do they emphasize? Who is the main audience?"

    ### Product hands-on
    - Sign up for trials or watch demos if available
    - Look at G2/Capterra reviews to see how real users describe the product's pros and cons

    ### Capture positioning and brand voice
    - Jot down key marketing phrases they use
    - Determine if they're more technical/formal or casual/friendly

    <Tip>
    If the product is complex, create a "Product 101" summary for your editorial team: key features, target use cases, brand spin.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Persona and ICP exploration">
    ### Confirm ideal customer profiles
    - Identify main user/buyer personas: job roles, challenges, typical buying journey
    - Document 2-3 core pain points or "jobs-to-be-done" for each persona

    ### Use jobs-to-be-done
    - For each persona, list the "job" they want done, the main frustrations, and how the client's product addresses it
    - This step directly inspires future content topics (guides, how-tos)

    ### Industry familiarity
    - If the industry is heavily regulated (healthcare/finance), note relevant compliance issues
    - Recognize sub-vertical nuances if the client caters to multiple verticals

    <Tip>
    If the client lacks formal persona docs, your Week 00 interviews are essential to build the missing details.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Competitor and domain research">
    ### Direct and indirect competitors
    - Focus on top 3-5 direct competitors the client regularly faces
    - Also note "mindshare" competitors like big players or alternative approaches

    ### Competitive content strategy
    - Review competitor blogs, resource hubs, or major site sections
    - Note recurring topics, top-performing articles, and any visible content gaps

    ### Opportunities and differentiators
    - List competitor oversights or angles the client could own

    <Tip>
    Use SEO tools (SEMrush, Ahrefs) to find competitor "Top Pages" and see which ones rank highest for relevant keywords.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Domain and topical research">
    ### Industry publications and influencers
    - Identify 2-3 leading industry sites, magazines, or podcasts
    - Skim their top-shared content for potential content ideas or angles

    ### Keyword brainstorm
    - Start a simple list of ~15-20 relevant keywords (refine later in the official content strategy)
    - Include short phrases ("marketing automation") and long-tail queries ("how to set up email drip campaigns")

    ### Authoritative sources
    - Note official reports or recognized experts you can cite
    - This helps create credible content that stands out
  </Step>

  <Step title="Content and strategy audit (if existing content)">
    ### Review current content
    - Skim the 5-10 latest blog posts or major resources
    - Assess their tone, structure, and performance if analytics are available

    ### Identify quick wins
    - If something ranks #11 and just needs on-page tweaks to push top 5, note it
    - Mark underperforming or outdated pieces for future refresh

    ### Assess visuals and formatting
    - Are they using images, bullet lists, subheadings effectively?
    - If it's a text wall, you can plan to improve readability in future content
  </Step>

  <Step title="Draft initial content strategy hypothesis">
    ### Synthesize research
    - Combine everything: ICP pains, competitor gaps, domain topics
    - Propose 3-5 **content pillars** addressing audience needs and brand differentiators

    ### Create a first-year roadmap
    - Suggest a rough plan for 6-12 months or at least next 2 quarters
    - Include possible content types (blogs, guides, case studies, videos)

    ### Plan for kickoff discussion
    - Prepare a bullet list of questions to confirm with the client at kickoff

    <Tip>
    Keep your hypothesis flexible. The client may reveal new angles at kickoff that shift your strategy.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Set up deliverables and client touchpoints">
    ### Internal research doc
    - Store all your notes on brand, product, audience, competitors, existing content in one accessible place

    ### Slack and workspace setup
    - Invite all client stakeholders to Slack
    - Create a workspace with categories: Strategy, Competitors, Editorial Calendar, etc.

    ### Welcome message and roadmap overview
    - Send a short message explaining the engagement plan
    - Show the client you have a methodical approach from day one

    ### Request additional materials
    - Ask if they have any overlooked docs, competitor intel, or brand style references
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Week 00 deliverables

- **Internal prep doc:** Summaries of brand background, ICP, competitor analysis, and initial content strategy notes
- **Workspace setup:** Proper channels and pages so the client and team can collaborate
- **Welcome message:** Outlining the engagement journey and the major milestones they can expect
- **Initial strategy hypothesis:** A short internal doc explaining possible pillars, content angles, and next steps (to be refined at kickoff)

## Week 00 objectives

- **Gain a comprehensive understanding** of the client's domain, product, and target personas
- **Identify key competitor insights** to pinpoint gaps or unique opportunities for future content
- **Draft a hypothetical content roadmap** aligned with discovered ICP pains and brand goals
- **Organize everything** (Slack, workspace) to be fully ready for a seamless Week 1 kickoff

## Client checklist

- [ ] Share existing materials: brand decks, style guides, product sheets, competitor intelligence
- [ ] Provide access to relevant platforms (analytics, prior content docs, etc.)
- [ ] Confirm kickoff meeting date and time
- [ ] Clarify stakeholders: who from their team should be in Slack channels and the workspace
- [ ] Highlight immediate priorities: pressing product launches or specific topics to tackle first

<Info>
Finishing Week 00 with a solid grasp of the client's ecosystem sets the tone for a high-impact Week 1 kickoff. The more thorough and organized you are now, the more confidence the client will have in your ability to deliver results fast while upholding the highest quality standards.
</Info>
