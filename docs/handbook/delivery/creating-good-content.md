# Creating good content

At GrowthX, we promise clients AI + **experts** in the loop.

Our benchmark for quality shouldn't be "it's good enough for AI." We are the experts and we are responsible for elevating content quality.

We aim to create **good content** that avoids what's become known as "AI slop" — and plain bad writing.

## What is good content?

Good content is comprehensive, accurate, and helpful to its target audience. Google's own studies confirm this. Content quality and relevance is the single most correlated ranking factor across every major study (see the [SEO ranking factors guide](/guides/marketing/seo-ranking-factors#part-3-content-quality--e-e-a-t) for the evidence). Use the [content scoring framework](/guides/marketing/seo-operating-guide#content-scoring-framework) to evaluate every piece before publishing. It uses principles of good writing:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="No repetition">
    **Good:** "A topic cluster (or content cluster) is a group of interconnected, thematically related pages on a website. Each cluster includes one pillar page, which is the main page that provides a broad overview of a particular topic. And multiple subpages (also called cluster pages), which cover associated subtopics. All of these pages are connected with internal links."
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="No fluff or jargon">
    **Good:** "AI excels at identifying patterns and connections. Use it to find new leads who are very similar to your current best customers (and therefore more likely to buy). His method is to have AI review data from your customer CRM to find not-so-obvious similarities they share. Then, you can apply those characteristics to refine your targeting."
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Avoiding the should-how fallacy">
    **Good:** "Qualify HARO respondents by including prerequisites, like industry, job title, or years of experience, in each question."

    Not: "Be very specific when you put out HARO requests to ensure you reach relevant contacts."

    Tell people **how**, not just that they **should**.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Providing valuable and expert insights">
    **Good:** "People often send ad clicks straight to a product page or their homepage. While this can work, a dedicated landing page is often more effective."

    Add real examples, real data, real experience. Generic advice is worthless.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Being specific and actionable">
    **Good:** "In Site Explorer, search for your website. Then, use the Competitors filter to add competitors to compare against. If your performance goes down as theirs goes up, it's likely they're taking your traffic."

    Name the tool. Describe the steps. Show the outcome.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Being easy to understand">
    Keep each sentence under 16-25 words unless absolutely necessary. Share only one or two ideas per sentence. If a reader has to re-read it, it's too complex.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Directing readers to reputable resources">
    If you're listing a stat or research, find the original report where it appears. **Do not** link to aggregator posts like "30+ Statistics About Content Marketing." If you can't find the original source for a stat, remove it.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Using BLUF (Bottom Line Up Front)">
    Place the most important information first — right after the heading. Don't make readers wade through context before getting the answer. The inverted pyramid is perfectly suited for the web.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Good structure

Good content answers the search intent and is structured coherently:

- The structure and angle of the article is helpful to the reader searching a specific keyword
- Every H2 is directly related to the H1/Title
- Every H3 is related to or answers a question posed by the H2

Since we're an agency, good content must also:
- Follow the client's guidelines
- Avoid mentioning their competitors (unless absolutely necessary)
- Describe the client's products and services accurately
- Include quality internal and external links

## What is AI slop?

AI slop is poor-quality, AI-generated content that seemingly provides a lot of information but delivers little value.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Repeating the same points with slight wording variations">
    **Bad:** "An effective content strategy ensures your message reaches the right audience. With the right approach to content, you can effectively communicate your message to your ideal audience."

    Same idea twice with different words. Cut one.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Fluffy, meaningless filler content">
    **Bad:** "In today's fast-paced digital landscape, leveraging innovative solutions is the key to success for businesses of all sizes."

    **Bad:** "Content is a powerful tool for businesses today. When used well, it can have a significant impact."

    Says nothing. Remove entirely or replace with something specific.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Lack of depth or unique insights">
    **Bad:** "Using social media can help increase your brand awareness."

    Everyone knows this. Add the *how*, the *why*, or the *evidence*.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Awkward, overly formal, or robotic tone">
    **Bad:** "Organizations must leverage multi-channel frameworks to drive scalable outcomes in the digital ecosystem."

    This is jargon soup. Write like a human.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Long, wordy sentences or repetitive structure">
    **Bad:** "When creating content, it is important to ensure that the content you create is relevant to the audience you are targeting."

    Too many words for a simple idea. Tighten it.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Jargon and buzzwords">
    **Bad:** "Drive engagement by leveraging omnichannel touchpoints across the customer journey."

    If you wouldn't say it out loud to a colleague, don't write it.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Overly formatted content">
    The article might be filled with bullet points or have meaningless H3s/H4s with thin content. Format should serve the reader, not pad the page.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## How to create good content with AI

<Note>
You need to familiarize yourself with the client's product, audience, and industry before creating or editing a piece of content. You can't create good content without this step.
</Note>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Look up the primary keyword">
    Go to the SERP analysis section in your SEO tool.

    - Understand what kind of content ranks on page one — is it a listicle or a guide? What are the meta title/description patterns?
    - Check the search intent. If the SERP is filled with landing pages, a blog post may not rank.
    - Use location-specific SERP information. If your client targets Canada, don't use US search data.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Keep the target audience in mind">
    There are two target audiences: your client's and the keyword's. They should align.

    - A keyword can be relevant for a broader audience, but it should include your client's target audience.
    - When in doubt, google the keyword and check the SERP. It will reveal who the target audience is.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Create a good outline">
    - A good outline answers all the questions a reader could have about the topic, structured logically with each H2 answering a question related to the H1.
    - Sections of the outline should not overlap. If one section covers benefits, don't cover pros elsewhere.
    - Watch out for "What is [main topic]?" subheadings — in most cases, answer this in the introduction.
    - Ask: where does the client fit into this story? Find the H3 where you can naturally weave in their product as a solution.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Review the first draft">
    - Read through and remove redundant information, AI fluff, and other issues.
    - You can use AI as your editing assistant — prompt it to find writing issues in a specific section and fix them. (See [prompt design fundamentals](/guides/ai/prompt-design-fundamentals) for techniques that improve AI output quality.)
    - Edit out difficult-to-parse sentences. Watch for:
      - "To do X, you need to do Y" → Better: "Do X by doing Y"
      - Long run-on sentences with multiple clauses
      - Filler words like "that," "can," "help," and "which" that can often be removed
  </Step>

  <Step title="Loop in your managing editor">
    Not sure about a specific edit? Tag them directly in the doc to avoid going back and forth in Slack.
  </Step>
</Steps>
