# Director

## TL;DR

- Directors are senior strategists and marketing experts. They provide leverage — the experienced bench that gets pulled in for hard problems.
- Shared resource deployed across engagements as needed.
- Focus areas: complex strategy, escalation support, quality oversight, training and mentorship.
- They amplify the capacity of EMs and production teams without being embedded in every account.

## What this role does

Directors are the senior bench. When an engagement manager hits a complex strategic challenge, when a client escalation needs experienced hands, when the team needs training on a new capability — that's when directors get involved.

You're not embedded in a single account. You work across engagements, bringing deep marketing expertise and pattern recognition to the hardest problems. Your value comes from making other people more effective.

## Key responsibilities

- **Complex strategy** — Help EMs develop sophisticated content strategies for high-value or challenging accounts. Provide the strategic depth that junior team members are still building.
- **Escalation support** — Step in when client relationships need senior attention. Resolve complex issues, rebuild confidence, redirect engagements that are drifting.
- **Quality oversight** — Review work across accounts for strategic alignment and quality. Spot patterns that indicate systemic problems.
- **Training and mentorship** — Develop EMs and editors. Share frameworks, review approaches, build the team's strategic capability.
- **Cross-account insights** — Identify patterns across engagements. What's working in one account might transform another.

## Accountabilities

| What you own | How it's measured |
|-------------|-------------------|
| Strategic quality | Quality of strategies across accounts you support |
| Escalation resolution | Speed and effectiveness of escalation outcomes |
| Team development | Growth of EMs and editors you mentor |
| Cross-account leverage | Insights that improve multiple engagements |

## What great looks like

- EMs proactively seek your input because it makes their strategies better
- Escalations get resolved quickly and leave the client relationship stronger
- The team's strategic capability grows measurably over time
- You spot systemic issues from cross-account patterns and fix them at the root
- You can be deployed to any account and add value immediately because you've seen the patterns before

## How deployment works

Directors are a shared resource. You're not assigned to a fixed set of accounts. Instead:

- **Scheduled involvement** — Regular check-ins with accounts that benefit from senior strategy input
- **On-demand deployment** — EMs pull you in for specific challenges, escalations, or deep dives
- **Training blocks** — Dedicated time for team development, workshops, and mentoring sessions
- **Strategic reviews** — Periodic reviews of account strategies across the portfolio

## Key relationships

| Who | How you work together |
|-----|----------------------|
| Engagement managers | Your primary partners. You provide leverage on their hardest problems. |
| Chief Content Officer | Aligned on quality standards. You enforce quality; CCO sets the bar. |
| Editors | You help them develop strategic thinking, not just production skills. |
| Sales | Support pre-engagement strategy discussions for complex prospects. |

## Career path

Director is a senior role. Growth paths include:

- **VP / Head of Delivery** — Lead the delivery function, own capacity planning and team strategy
- **Chief Content Officer** — Own quality standards across all teams
- **Specialization** — Become the definitive expert in a vertical, channel, or methodology
