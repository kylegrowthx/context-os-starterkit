# SEO specialist

## TL;DR

- SEO specialists are technical SEO experts. Shared resource deployed across accounts as needed.
- Handle audits, technical SEO analysis, search performance deep dives, and strategic recommendations.
- Not embedded in every account — deployed where SEO expertise drives the most impact.
- Work closely with EMs and editors to translate technical insights into actionable content strategy.

## What this role does

SEO specialists bring deep technical expertise that most EMs and editors don't have. You're the person who digs into crawl data, analyzes site architecture, interprets search console anomalies, and turns technical findings into strategic recommendations.

You're a [shared resource](/company/organization#the-two-operating-models) — deployed across accounts based on need. Some accounts need you weekly. Others need you quarterly for audits. The engagement manager pulls you in when technical SEO expertise is required.

## Key responsibilities

- **Technical SEO audits** — Crawl analysis, site architecture review, indexation issues, Core Web Vitals, structured data. The full technical health check.
- **Search performance analysis** — Deep dives into search console data, ranking trends, traffic patterns. Identify what's working and what's declining.
- **Strategic recommendations** — Translate technical findings into clear, actionable advice that EMs and editors can execute.
- **Competitive SEO analysis** — Analyze competitor search strategies, backlink profiles, content gaps, and ranking patterns.
- **Content optimization guidance** — Keyword targeting, on-page optimization, internal linking strategy. Help editors produce content that ranks.
- **Monitoring and alerting** — Set up tracking for key metrics and alert the team to significant changes.

## Accountabilities

| What you own | How it's measured |
|-------------|-------------------|
| Technical SEO health | Audit scores, crawl efficiency, indexation rates |
| Search performance insights | Quality and actionability of analysis and recommendations |
| Optimization impact | Ranking improvements, traffic growth from SEO initiatives |

## What great looks like

- Your audits uncover issues the team didn't know existed — and come with clear fix priorities
- Recommendations are actionable — editors and EMs can execute without needing a translator
- You spot trends early — ranking declines, algorithm shifts, competitive moves
- Technical SEO never becomes the bottleneck — sites are healthy, indexed, and optimized
- You share cross-account patterns that improve everyone's SEO approach

## How deployment works

- **Scheduled audits** — Regular technical audits for accounts on a cadence (quarterly or as needed)
- **On-demand analysis** — EMs pull you in for specific issues, deep dives, or strategic questions
- **Proactive monitoring** — Flag significant changes across accounts you're tracking
- **Team training** — Help editors and EMs build foundational SEO knowledge so they need you less for routine questions

## Tools you'll use

- **Google Search Console** — Core search performance data
- **SEMrush / Ahrefs** — Keyword research, competitive analysis, backlink audits
- **Screaming Frog / Sitebulb** — Technical crawl analysis
- **Google Analytics** — Traffic analysis and conversion tracking
- **CheckThat** — AI visibility monitoring and analysis

## Key relationships

| Who | How you work together |
|-----|----------------------|
| Engagement managers | They pull you in for specific accounts. You deliver audits and recommendations. |
| Editors | You guide their content optimization. They execute your keyword and on-page recommendations. |
| Forward-deployed engineers | Technical implementations — structured data, site speed fixes, indexation issues. |
| Directors | Strategic SEO conversations for complex or high-value accounts. |
