# Editor

## TL;DR

- Editors produce the content that drives client results. They're the production backbone of every pod.
- Operate AI workflows, calibrate outputs to brand voice, and maintain quality standards.
- Production should run weeks ahead. If you're scrambling week-of, something is broken.
- Feed improvements back into the system — every edit should make the AI better.

## What this role does

Editors turn strategy into published content. You operate the AI workflows that handle 80–90% of the heavy lifting, then apply the human expertise that makes the output great — brand voice calibration, quality control, strategic alignment, and contextual nuance.

You work within a [delivery pod](/delivery/teams-and-operations#pods-growth-execution), reporting to an engagement manager. Your output directly drives client results — search rankings, traffic, leads.

## Key responsibilities

- **Content production** — Produce content using AI workflows and human expertise. Blog posts, guides, thought leadership, whatever the strategy calls for.
- **Brand voice calibration** — Ensure every piece sounds like the client, not like a generic AI. Apply the voice guidelines and calibration docs created during the sprint.
- **Quality control** — Review AI outputs critically. Catch factual errors, tone mismatches, structural problems. Apply the [content quality standards](/delivery/creating-good-content).
- **Production pacing** — Maintain a buffer. Produce content weeks ahead of schedule, not week-of. If you're behind, escalate immediately.
- **Workflow improvement** — Every edit you make is a signal. Feed recurring issues back into AI workflow configuration. If you're making the same fix repeatedly, it's a pipeline problem.
- **Platform operation** — Use ContentOS effectively. Manage the editorial calendar, publishing pipeline, and content status tracking.

## Accountabilities

| What you own | How it's measured |
|-------------|-------------------|
| Content quality | Revision rates, editorial standards compliance |
| Production rhythm | Content delivered on schedule, buffer maintained |
| AI workflow effectiveness | Reduction in edits over time, zero-edit output rate |
| Publishing pipeline | Content moves through stages without bottlenecks |

## What great looks like

- Content is weeks ahead of schedule — never scrambling
- AI outputs require minimal editing because you've calibrated the workflows well
- The client's brand voice is consistent across all content
- You identify pipeline problems and escalate them before they become emergencies
- Every week, the same types of content take less effort because you're improving the system
- Content performs — it ranks, it drives traffic, it converts

## A typical week

- **Monday:** Review production calendar, check AI workflow outputs from weekend runs, prioritize editing queue
- **Tuesday–Thursday:** Edit and refine AI outputs, calibrate workflows, publish content, collaborate with EM on strategy adjustments
- **Friday:** Review content performance, feed learnings back into workflows, prepare next week's pipeline

## Tools you'll use

- **ContentOS** — Primary workspace for content operations
- **AI workflows** — The automation layer that produces first drafts and optimized content
- **CMS** (client's) — Publishing platform for final content
- **Google Docs / Notion** — Collaboration and review
- **SEO tools** — Keyword research, content optimization, performance tracking

## Key relationships

| Who | How you work together |
|-----|----------------------|
| Engagement manager | Your pod lead. Sets strategy and priorities. Reviews quality. |
| Other editors | Peer review, knowledge sharing, workflow tips |
| Forward-deployed engineers | Fix workflow issues, improve AI pipeline configuration |
| Directors | Senior support for complex editorial challenges |
| Chief Content Officer | Quality standards, training, editorial oversight |

## Career path

- **Senior editor** — Take on more complex accounts, mentor junior editors, lead editorial quality initiatives
- **Engagement manager** — Transition to owning client relationships with a strategic, full-stack role
- **Editorial director** — Focus on quality standards, training programs, and editorial strategy across accounts
