# Chief Content Officer

## TL;DR

- The CCO oversees content quality across all teams and engagements.
- Sets quality standards, manages editorial oversight, runs training programs.
- Escalation point for quality issues that directors and EMs can't resolve.
- Ensures the quality bar stays high as we scale — so scale doesn't mean declining standards.

## What this role does

The Chief Content Officer is the quality bar for GrowthX. You set the standard for what great content looks like, ensure every team meets it, and build the systems and training that make quality sustainable at scale.

This isn't a production role — you don't write content day-to-day. You architect the quality systems, train the people, and intervene when something threatens the standard.

## Key responsibilities

- **Quality standards** — Define and maintain the content quality bar across all engagements. What constitutes great content at GrowthX? You decide.
- **Editorial oversight** — Review content quality across teams. Spot trends, identify systemic issues, ensure consistency.
- **Training programs** — Build and run training for editors and EMs. New hire calibration, ongoing development, skill-specific workshops.
- **Escalation resolution** — Final escalation point for quality issues. When directors and EMs can't resolve a quality problem, it comes to you.
- **Process improvement** — Work with EPD to improve AI workflows based on quality signals. Every quality issue is an opportunity to make the system better.
- **Voice and style** — Maintain the frameworks for brand voice calibration. Ensure our process for capturing and replicating client voice is effective.

## Accountabilities

| What you own | How it's measured |
|-------------|-------------------|
| Quality standards | Consistency and level of content across all accounts |
| Editorial team capability | Editor performance, training completion, skill growth |
| Quality escalation | Speed and effectiveness of quality issue resolution |
| System quality | AI workflow quality improvements driven by your insights |

## What great looks like

- Quality stays consistent or improves as we scale — adding accounts doesn't mean declining standards
- Editors know exactly what "great" means because you've defined it clearly and trained them on it
- Quality issues are caught before they reach clients, not after
- AI workflows get better over time because quality signals feed back into the system
- New editors ramp to production quality faster because training programs are effective

## Quality escalation path

```
Editor catches issue
    → EM reviews and addresses
        → Director supports if needed
            → CCO resolves if unresolved
```

Most quality issues should be handled at the editor or EM level. If they're reaching you regularly, it signals a training or process gap that needs systemic fixing.

## Key relationships

| Who | How you work together |
|-----|----------------------|
| Directors | Aligned on quality expectations. Directors enforce the standards you set. |
| Engagement managers | You train and calibrate their quality instincts. They escalate when needed. |
| Editors | You set the bar they aim for. Training programs, calibration guides, quality reviews. |
| Forward-deployed engineers | Quality signals become workflow improvements. You provide the "what," they build the "how." |

## Career path

CCO is a C-level role. Growth paths include:

- **VP of Content** at a larger organization
- **Expanded scope** — Take on strategy, brand, and marketing leadership beyond content
- **Advisory** — Serve as a fractional CCO for multiple organizations
