# Engagement manager

## TL;DR

- EMs own the client relationship end-to-end. They're accountable for conversion, renewals, and expansion.
- Part customer success, part strategist, part growth marketer, part editor. The EM is the driver.
- Customers should view their EM as the best hire they've ever made.
- EMs manage a book of business and lead their delivery pod.

## What this role does

The engagement manager is the most important role in delivery. You own everything about the client relationship — strategy, communication, outcomes, and satisfaction. You're the person the customer calls when something matters, and the person who makes sure problems get solved before they escalate.

You lead a [delivery pod](/delivery/teams-and-operations#pods-growth-execution). You manage editors, pull in shared resources when needed, and ensure the pod delivers results that compound over time.

## Key responsibilities

- **Own the client relationship** — You're the single point of accountability. Strategy, communication, and outcomes all run through you.
- **Drive content strategy** — Align content pillars with business goals. Ensure every piece serves a strategic purpose.
- **Manage the pod** — Lead editors, coordinate with directors and specialists, maintain production rhythm.
- **Ensure conversion and retention** — In Strategy Sprint, you drive conversion to Growth Execution. In Growth Execution, you drive renewals and expansion.
- **Quality oversight** — You're the last line before work reaches the customer. If quality slips, it's on you to catch it.
- **Build the system** — Every week should get easier. Feed improvements back into AI workflows and processes. Escalate pipeline problems, don't just fix symptoms.
- **Stakeholder management** — Build relationships across the customer's organization. Proactive, not reactive.

## Accountabilities

| What you own | How it's measured |
|-------------|-------------------|
| Strategy Sprint conversion | % of sprints converting to Growth Execution |
| Growth Execution renewals | Renewal rate and expansion revenue |
| Client satisfaction | NPS, qualitative feedback, relationship depth |
| Production quality | Content performance, revision rates, on-time delivery |
| System improvement | Reduction in manual work, fewer recurring issues |

## What great looks like

- Customers view you as the best hire they've ever made
- Proactive, not reactive — you anticipate problems and solve them before the customer notices
- You build relationships across the customer's organization, not just with your primary contact
- You drive outcomes, not just manage output
- Your pod runs smoothly — editors know what to produce, shared resources are pulled in at the right time
- Every week serving the same client gets easier because you're building leverage

## A typical week

- **Monday:** Review upcoming deliverables, check production pipeline, plan the week with your pod
- **Tuesday–Thursday:** Client calls, content reviews, strategy sessions, stakeholder updates. Pull in directors or engineers for specific needs.
- **Friday:** Review metrics, plan next week, feed improvements back into the system

The exact rhythm depends on your book of business. Some accounts need daily attention. Others are running smoothly with weekly check-ins.

## Tools you'll use

- **ContentOS** — Content operations platform for managing the production pipeline
- **Slack** — Primary communication with clients and internal teams
- **Notion** — Documentation, project management, client workspaces
- **Google Search Console / Analytics** — Performance monitoring
- **SEMrush / Ahrefs** — SEO analysis and competitive research

## Key relationships

| Who | How you work together |
|-----|----------------------|
| Editors | Direct reports in your pod. You set direction, they produce. You review quality. |
| Directors | Senior bench you pull in for complex strategy, escalations, or training needs |
| Forward-deployed engineers | Technical co-pilots for AI workflow setup and customization |
| SEO specialists | Shared resource for technical SEO audits and analysis |
| Chief Content Officer | Quality escalation path. Sets standards you enforce. |
| Sales | Receive handoffs for new engagements. Your delivery drives their pipeline through referrals. |

## Career path

EM is already a senior role. Growth paths include:

- **Director** — Move to the senior bench, handling complex strategy across multiple accounts and training the next generation of EMs
- **Leadership** — Take on team leadership, capacity planning, and organizational responsibilities
- **Specialization** — Deepen expertise in a vertical or capability (SEO, AEO, specific industries)
