# Time off

At GrowthX, taking time off is about the **time you need**, not a fixed quota.

---

## How it works

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Unlimited, need-based time off">
    We don't have a strict limit or quota for holidays. We trust you to take the time off you need to recharge, focus on your life, and maintain a healthy work-life balance. We don't track your holidays, and we don't pay out unused time off.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How much is right?">
    As a general guideline, around 4 weeks of time off annually is a healthy range. Less than that may not give you proper rest. More than that might need a check-in with your lead to ensure balance with your team.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="National holidays">
    We generally observe commonly recognized national holidays (around 8 days a year). But we understand people have different traditions and holidays they value. Whether it's Eid al-Fitr or Christmas, you're free to decide if you want to work or take the day off.

    - **International team** — Take your own country's national holidays.
    - **US team** — We observe federal holidays.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Requesting time off">
    Always discuss your time off with your team and manager. Clear communication helps maintain trust and efficiency.

    - **A day or two off** — Give at least 2 weeks notice.
    - **One week or more** — Give at least 4 weeks notice.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Calendar and Slack updates">
    Make sure you:
    - Add your time off to your personal calendar
    - Invite your team, pod, and manager to the calendar event
    - Update your Slack status with a palm tree emoji to indicate you're on PTO (bonus points for adding dates, e.g. "OOO until 4/3")
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Life events">
    Life is unpredictable. Whether it's a sickness, a family emergency, or a personal health day, communicate openly with your lead about what you need.

    For example: *"Hey team, I'll be out for the next few days. Something came up with my family, and I'll be out of pocket until next week."*
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

Above all, **enjoy** your time. Whether it's getting a haircut or going to the dentist during typical work hours, we trust you to make the best choices for yourself and GrowthX.

---

## Mutual expectations

- **Flexibility goes both ways.** The flexibility we offer must be reciprocated with responsibility and respect toward the team. Balance is key.
- **Take enough time off.** Since there's no payout for unused days, make sure to take the time you need. We want you well-rested and at your best.
- **Clear communication.** Agree on dates with your team and make sure it's reflected in the shared calendar so everyone is aware.

---

## The philosophy

At GrowthX, we believe traditional rules and quotas around time off are limited. We operate from the standpoint of what you **need** rather than what's allocated.

### Work hard, rest well

We work hard, and we need adequate rest to keep our minds and bodies functioning at their best. Sometimes we work harder and need more rest. Sometimes we work less and need less. Listen to yourself and respond appropriately.

We also recognize that life presents moments when work has to take a backseat. Whether it's a family emergency or an unexpected opportunity, you should have the freedom to prioritize what's important.

Maintaining solid time off practices is crucial for sustainable work. Give it your all when working. Take adequate time to fully recharge when you're off.

### Not about hours, about outcomes

Work is not always measured in time. Most people at GrowthX don't keep strict track of hours because time alone is a limited way to measure impact. We focus on outcomes and trust that people will balance their workloads effectively.

This is why we don't track holidays in a strict sense. We expect our people to take responsibility for both their contribution to GrowthX and their personal well-being. This is not entitlement — it's a moral obligation to yourself, your loved ones, and the company.

---

## Closing thoughts

We aim to create an environment where work-life harmony is a reality. Taking enough rest is just as crucial as putting in the effort when you're working. Our approach to time off is designed to encourage you to take responsibility, find your balance, and ensure you're always at your best — both for yourself and for the company.
