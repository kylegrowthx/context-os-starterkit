# Delivery

## TL;DR

- Delivery is the core of GrowthX. It's where we produce outcomes for customers on an ongoing basis.
- Organized in pods: each pod has an engagement manager (lead), editors (production), and access to shared specialists.
- Shared resources — directors, forward-deployed engineers, SEO specialists, design/dev — are deployed where needed, not embedded in every account.
- The system mindset: every week serving the same client should get easier.

## Purpose

Delivery exists to run Growth Execution — our ongoing client engagements. After a customer converts from a [Strategy Sprint](/company/strategy-sprint), they're assigned to a pod. That pod owns the relationship, drives outcomes, and improves the system over time.

This is the revenue engine. Most of GrowthX's team works in delivery. Most of our value is created here.

## Pod structure

Each pod is a small, autonomous team responsible for a set of client accounts.

```
Pod
├── Engagement Manager (lead)
├── Editor(s) (production)
└── Shared teams (as needed)
    ├── Directors
    ├── Forward-deployed engineers
    ├── SEO specialists
    └── Design / developers
```

**Engagement managers** lead the pod. They own the client relationships, drive strategy, and are accountable for renewals and expansion. They manage a book of business that varies in size and complexity.

**Editors** produce the work — content that drives results. They operate the AI workflows, calibrate outputs to brand voice, and maintain production rhythm weeks ahead of schedule.

See [teams and operations](/delivery/teams-and-operations) for the full operational playbook — capacity planning, the accountability model, and the system mindset.

## Shared resources

Directors, forward-deployed engineers, SEO specialists, and design/dev are shared across pods. They get deployed where the need is greatest — not permanently assigned to individual accounts.

| Role | What they do | How they're deployed |
|------|-------------|---------------------|
| Directors | Complex strategy, escalations, quality oversight, training | As needed — the senior bench for hard problems |
| Forward-deployed engineers | AI workflows, workspace setup, technical implementation | As needed — see [client ops engineering](/epd/client-ops-engineering) |
| SEO specialists | Technical SEO audits, search performance analysis | As needed per account |
| Design / developers | Web work, design support, special initiatives | As needed per account |

## The human-AI model

Delivery combines human expertise with AI workflows. AI handles 80–90% of the production heavy lifting. Humans do the strategic thinking, quality control, and customer-specific calibration that AI can't handle yet.

Three human roles are critical:
- **Process architects** — systematize complex work into repeatable workflows
- **Input calibrators** — extract real requirements from customer conversations
- **Production refiners** — quality control and strategic guidance throughout production

See [human-AI collaboration](/delivery/human-ai-collaboration) for the full framework.

## How delivery connects to other functions

**Strategy Sprint → Delivery:** Successful sprints convert to Growth Execution. The sprint builds the foundation — AI workflows, brand voice, production rhythms — that the pod inherits and scales.

**Delivery → Sales:** Great delivery creates referrals. Happy customers are the primary pipeline source. The better delivery performs, the easier sales becomes.

**EPD → Delivery:** Forward-deployed engineers from EPD embed with pods. Platform improvements (ContentOS, AI workflows) directly increase delivery capacity and quality.
