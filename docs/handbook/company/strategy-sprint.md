# Strategy Sprint

## TL;DR

- Strategy Sprint is an 8-week engagement for new customers. It proves the model works before they commit long-term.
- The sprint team runs the engagement: engagement manager, editors, with shared specialist support as needed.
- Week 6 is the circuit breaker — a clear decision point on whether to continue.
- Success means conversion to Growth Execution (our ongoing delivery model).

## Purpose

Strategy Sprint exists to prove value fast. New customers don't sign a multi-year contract on faith. They sign up for 8 weeks, and we show them what's possible.

The sprint has two jobs:
1. **Deliver real results** — ranked content, traffic growth, quality outputs the customer can see and measure.
2. **Build the system** — set up AI workflows, calibrate brand voice, establish production rhythms that scale into ongoing delivery.

If we do both well, the customer converts to Growth Execution. If we don't, we part ways with clear documentation of what was learned.

## How the sprint works

The 8-week plan is the operational backbone. Each week has a mission, a risk it targets, and clear deliverables. See the [full 8-week plan](/delivery/8-week-plan) for week-by-week detail.

**Key milestones:**

| Week | Focus |
|------|-------|
| 1 | Kickoff — rapid context absorption, stakeholder alignment |
| 2 | Foundation — strategy framework, brand voice, quality standards |
| 3 | Priorities — content quality rubric, editorial workflow |
| 4 | Definition of done — full publishing pipeline tested end-to-end |
| 5 | Rhythm — sustainable production cadence, measurement live |
| 6 | Decision point — proceed or not, based on data |
| 7–8 | Scale up or wind down based on Week 6 decision |

## Team structure

The sprint team is lean but senior. Speed matters.

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|---------------|
| **Engagement manager** | Owns the client relationship. Drives strategy, manages stakeholders, accountable for conversion. |
| **Editors** | Produce content. Calibrate to brand voice. Build the production rhythm. |
| **Directors** | Pulled in for complex strategy, escalations, or quality issues. Shared resource. |
| **Forward-deployed engineers** | Set up AI workflows, configure workspaces, customize pipelines. Shared resource. |

**Capacity:** ~4 FTEs per 1 new kickoff per week. Scaling to more kickoffs requires proportional team growth with a ~2 month ramp.

## Pre-sprint preparation

Before Week 1, the engagement manager runs [Week 00 prep](/delivery/week-00-prep) — gathering context from the sales handoff, researching the client's business, setting up workspaces, and preparing to walk into kickoff fully briefed.

The quality of Week 00 prep directly determines how fast the sprint produces value.

## The Week 6 decision

Week 6 is the circuit breaker. It's a structured evaluation:

- **Performance data** — Is content ranking? Is traffic growing? Are quality metrics improving?
- **System health** — Are AI workflows calibrated? Is the production rhythm sustainable?
- **Customer alignment** — Does the customer see value? Are they engaged?
- **Team fit** — Is the working relationship productive?

If the answer to all four is yes, the customer converts to Growth Execution and gets assigned to a [delivery pod](/delivery/teams-and-operations#pods-growth-execution).

If not, we wind down with full documentation and strategic recommendations.

## How Strategy Sprint connects to other functions

**Sales → Strategy Sprint:** Every new deal enters as a sprint. Sales provides the handoff context. The quality of that handoff determines sprint velocity.

**Strategy Sprint → Delivery:** Successful sprints convert to Growth Execution. The sprint team builds the foundation that the pod inherits — AI workflows, brand voice calibration, production rhythms.

**EPD → Strategy Sprint:** Forward-deployed engineers set up the technical infrastructure during the sprint. Better AI workflows and platform tools make sprints faster and more effective.

## What good looks like

- The customer sees tangible results within the first 4 weeks
- Brand voice and AI workflows are calibrated by Week 3
- Publishing rhythm is proven sustainable by Week 5
- Week 6 decision is clear and data-driven — no ambiguity
- Conversion rate to Growth Execution is high because we deliver real value
- Handoff to the delivery pod is seamless — no context lost
