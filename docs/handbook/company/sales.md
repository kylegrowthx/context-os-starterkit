# Sales

## TL;DR

- Sales qualifies and closes new business. The goal is signed Strategy Sprint engagements.
- Word of mouth drives most of our pipeline. Great delivery is our best sales tool.
- ~33% win rate with a 6-week average sales cycle.
- Sales hands off to Strategy Sprint at signing — with full context so the team hits the ground running.

## Purpose

Sales exists to fill the top of the funnel and convert qualified prospects into Strategy Sprint engagements. We're not selling software licenses or retainers. We're selling a system that delivers outcomes — and we need to qualify for fit before committing.

## What we sell

We sell **service-as-software**: AI-powered content marketing that delivers outcomes, not tools. Customers get a full content operation — strategy, production, optimization, distribution — powered by AI workflows and guided by human experts.

The entry point is always a [Strategy Sprint](/company/strategy-sprint) — an 8-week engagement that proves the model works for their business before they commit to ongoing delivery.

## How pipeline works

Most of our business comes from word of mouth and referrals. When we do great work, customers tell other companies. That compounding trust is our primary growth engine.

**Pipeline sources:**
- Customer referrals and word of mouth
- Inbound from content and thought leadership
- Strategic outreach to ideal-fit companies
- Conference and event connections

**Qualification criteria:**
- B2B tech company (our sweet spot)
- Content marketing is a priority, not an afterthought
- Willingness to invest in the engagement ($200K+/year)
- Executive sponsorship — someone senior cares about making this work
- Realistic expectations about timelines and outcomes

## The sales cycle

| Stage | What happens | Duration |
|-------|-------------|----------|
| Discovery | Understand the prospect's business, goals, and pain points | 1–2 weeks |
| Qualification | Assess fit against our criteria — can we actually help? | 1 week |
| Proposal | Present the engagement model, scope, and pricing | 1 week |
| Decision | Stakeholder alignment, contract review, close | 1–2 weeks |

**Average cycle:** ~6 weeks from first conversation to signed engagement.

**Win rate:** ~33%. We'd rather disqualify early than take on engagements where we can't deliver great results.

## The handoff

When a deal closes, sales hands off to the Strategy Sprint team with full context:

- Sales call recordings and notes
- Client priorities, stakeholder preferences, and any specific expectations
- Brand materials, existing content, and competitive context gathered during discovery
- Any red flags or special considerations

The [Week 00 prep](/delivery/week-00-prep) process picks up from here. The engagement manager uses this handoff to walk into kickoff ultra-prepared.

## How sales connects to other functions

**Sales → Strategy Sprint:** Every closed deal becomes a Strategy Sprint. The quality of the sales handoff directly affects how fast the team ramps.

**Delivery → Sales:** Great delivery creates referrals. Happy customers are our best salespeople. The [brand moat](/company/vision-and-strategy#1-brand-moat) compounds over time.

**EPD → Sales:** Better platform capabilities expand what we can sell. New features and AI workflows unlock new client segments.

## Capacity constraints

Sales pipeline feeds everything downstream. The math:

- ~33% win rate means we need 3x pipeline to hit targets
- Each new engagement needs sprint team capacity (4 FTEs per kickoff/week)
- Building capacity takes ~2 months of ramp time

Selling faster than we can deliver is worse than selling slower. See [teams and operations](/delivery/teams-and-operations#how-capacity-works) for the full capacity model.

## What good looks like

- Prospects feel like they're talking to a trusted advisor, not a salesperson
- Qualification is honest — we say no to bad fits
- Handoffs are thorough — the sprint team never walks in blind
- Pipeline is predictable — no feast-or-famine cycles
- Customers who sign stay and expand (that's the real proof sales did their job)
