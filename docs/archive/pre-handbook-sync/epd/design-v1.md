## Principles

1. **Good design drives outcomes**  
   Design is not decoration. It is a tool for impact. What we make must work and resonate—how we got there matters less than what it changes.

2. **Good design begins with systems**  
   Surfaces shift. Systems scale. Structure makes quality repeatable and intent legible across every touchpoint.

3. **Good design embraces chaos**  
   Growth is unpredictable. Design lives inside that mess. The work is to stay calm, stay clear, and shape coherence from change.

4. **Good design thrives on constraints**  
   Constraints are not limits but launchpads. They focus creativity and create room for others to do their best work.

5. **Good design is high-velocity *and* high-fidelity**  
   We move at speed without cutting corners. Every detail matters. Velocity and refinement are not in conflict—they are the standard.

## Roles

We have two types of design roles at GrowthX. While primarily contributing individually, role scope can grow into a player/coach rubric.

### Marketing Designer

Marketing designers at GrowthX operate primarily within the **Deliver** function, focusing on client-facing brand execution. They work on high-priority accounts to produce landing pages, creative assets, motion, and visual systems. In addition to hands-on execution, they review work from content managers, provide creative direction, and ensure visual consistency across deliverables. Marketing designers also collaborate with the **Build** function to identify and implement workflow improvements, helping scale design quality and reduce repetition across the Deliver org. Strong taste, speed, and attention to detail are expected.

### Design Engineer

Design Engineers at GrowthX are embedded in the **Build** function. They blend product design, engineering, and product management to build fully functional interfaces working with other engineers. Their responsibilities include building reusable components, refining interaction patterns, ensuring performance and accessibility, and translating prototypes into production-ready code. They also collaborate with marketing designers to ship internal tools and design workflows that improve velocity and quality across the Deliver organization. Precision, ownership, and systems thinking are core to the role.

## Operations

### Rituals

We're deliberately meeting-light to prioritize focus time. As we grow, we'll implement more synchronicity. Primarily use project and company-wide channels for communication. For design-only feedback, use #x-design.

### Process

You were hired because we trust you to run a world-class design process. From a project management perspective, these are our general expectations.

1. **Prepare:** Define your success metric (eg. 1000 Threads mentions in 1 week), and fire up a Slack channel ([dept]-proj-[area]-[name], eg. "b-proj-epd-seoworkflows").

2. **Kick-off:** Lead by default, be a good host to your team, make your shareholders feel included, define individual contributions, share responsibility where appropriate.

3. **Iterate:** Prioritize frequent feedback loops, trust your gut if there's no bandwidth for review.

4. **Ship:** It's not real until it's public or usable.

5. **Measure:** Check in and report back on your initial success metric.
