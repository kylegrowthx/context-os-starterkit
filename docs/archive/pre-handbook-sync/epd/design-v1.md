# Design

Design principles, roles, operations, and tool stack across product, brand, and GTM.

> The quality of design is a shared responsibility. This document helps designers ship great work and guides team members working with design.

## Design at GrowthX

We delight customers, guide teams toward confident decisions, and inspire through refined craft and visionary execution.

We sit at the intersection of product, research, engineering, brand, and go-to-market — ensuring a coherent and elegant experience from first interaction to consistent usage.

We turn ambiguity into tangible outcomes at all levels of the organization.

## Principles

1. **Good design drives outcomes** — Design is not decoration. It's a tool for impact. What we make must work and resonate — how we got there matters less than what it changes.
2. **Good design begins with systems** — Surfaces shift. Systems scale. Structure makes quality repeatable and intent legible across every touchpoint.
3. **Good design embraces chaos** — Growth is unpredictable. Design lives inside that mess. The work is to stay calm, stay clear, and shape coherence from change.
4. **Good design thrives on constraints** — Constraints are not limits but launchpads. They focus creativity and create room for others to do their best work.
5. **Good design is high-velocity *and* high-fidelity** — We move at speed without cutting corners. Every detail matters. Velocity and refinement are not in conflict — they are the standard.

## Areas of responsibility

### Product

We're building a range of products, services, and technologies to serve our acute and longer-term needs. The primary environment is Atlas — an environment to architect marketing content at scale through a range of features across different product surfaces.

Atlas uses Shadcn components and minimal styling to validate functionality quickly. The UI is still in flux as we iterate rapidly.

### Brand

**GrowthX.ai** — The current brand was developed in 2024 with an external agency. A brand refresh is in progress.

**The Output** — Our podcast/newsletter/publication (GrowthX media). Its brand identity is loosely based on GrowthX's brand pillars.

### GTM

We support several GTM-led projects that overlap delivery and product, including Codelibrary.ai and CheckThat.ai.

## Roles

We have two types of design roles at GrowthX. While primarily contributing individually, role scope can grow into a player/coach rubric.

### Marketing designer

Marketing designers operate primarily within the **delivery** function, focusing on client-facing brand execution. They work on high-priority accounts to produce landing pages, creative assets, motion, and visual systems. In addition to hands-on execution, they review work from content managers, provide creative direction, and ensure visual consistency across deliverables. They also collaborate with the **product** function to identify and implement workflow improvements. Strong taste, speed, and attention to detail are expected.

### Design engineer

Design engineers are embedded in the **product** function. They blend product design, engineering, and product management to build fully functional interfaces working with other engineers. Responsibilities include building reusable components, refining interaction patterns, ensuring performance and accessibility, and translating prototypes into production-ready code. They also collaborate with marketing designers to ship internal tools and design workflows. Precision, ownership, and systems thinking are core to the role.

## Operations

### Rituals

We're deliberately meeting-light to prioritize focus time. As we grow, we'll add more synchronous touchpoints. Use project and company-wide channels for communication. For design-only feedback, use the design channel.

### Process

You were hired because we trust you to run a world-class design process. From a project management perspective, these are the general expectations:

1. **Prepare** — Define your success metric (e.g., 1000 Threads mentions in 1 week) and set up a project Slack channel.
2. **Kick off** — Lead by default. Be a good host to your team, make stakeholders feel included, define individual contributions, and share responsibility where appropriate.
3. **Iterate** — Prioritize frequent feedback loops. Trust your gut if there's no bandwidth for review.
4. **Ship** — It's not real until it's public or usable.
5. **Measure** — Check in and report back on your initial success metric.
