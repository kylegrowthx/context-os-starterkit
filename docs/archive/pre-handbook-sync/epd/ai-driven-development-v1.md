## Intro: AI Driven vs AI Assisted vs Vibe Coding

We are at an inflection point where we should all strive to be truly AI native, and I see a big delta in productivity when we learn to use AI not just as a coding assistant, but as a strategic partner early in the process.

For a startup like us, speed is everything. We need to ship the right features before our window closes, and we need code that is as bug-free as possible and somewhat easy to extend/iterate on. There's a difference between a mild productivity boost and a 3-10x multiplier when it comes to how you use AI in the process.

I've spent the past three years using AI mostly focused on the planning phase and less on auto-complete - that's where most of the benefits are. That used to take a lot of work to set things up, but now the tools are finally catching up to make this workflow easy and expected from everyone.

This weekend, while working on CheckThat I decided to record myself building a large feature end-to-end, from Rails and React to the AI workflows.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Use AI in the planning phase** — This is where most of the productivity gains come from, not just autocomplete
2. **AI as strategic partner** — Think of AI as a collaborator who helps you design the solution, not just implement it
3. **3-10x multiplier** — The difference between AI-assisted and AI-driven development is significant
4. **End-to-end workflow** — Plan → Review → Execute across the full stack (Rails, React, AI workflows)
