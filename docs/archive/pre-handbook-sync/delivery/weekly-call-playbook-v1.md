# Weekly Customer Call Playbook — Engagement Managers

This is your go-to reference before, during, and after every weekly customer call. It covers all customer stages: onboarding (Strategy Sprint), active execution, and at-risk accounts.

---

## Before the Call (15–20 min prep)

**Know where the customer is in their journey.** The call structure shifts depending on whether they're in a Strategy Sprint, Growth Execution, or flagged as at-risk.

### Prep Checklist

- Review last week's action items — what got done, what didn't, and why
- Check publishing schedule: are we ahead, on track, or behind?
- Pull performance data: impressions, clicks, signups, any notable wins
- Scan Slack/email for anything the client raised between calls
- Identify one proactive insight or recommendation to bring (a content opportunity, a competitor move, a performance trend)
- If Strategy Sprint: confirm which week you're in and what deliverables are due
- If at-risk: review the health scores and pinpoint the dimension(s) dragging

**The goal of prep is to walk in with a point of view, not just an update.**

---

## Call Structure

### 1. Open Warm (2 min)

Start personal. Ask about their week, reference something real — a product launch they just did, a tweet you saw, the city they're in. Don't open with "thanks for your time" or a slide deck.

The relationship is the product. Treat every call like you're catching up with someone you respect, not running a status meeting.

### 2. Recap and Accountability (5 min)

Walk through last week's action items. Be direct about what shipped and what didn't. If something slipped on your side, name it and explain what you're doing about it. If something slipped on their side, ask about it without being passive-aggressive.

This is where trust compounds or erodes. Consistent follow-through on small things builds the credibility that carries you through hard conversations later.

### 3. Performance and Results (5–10 min)

Share what's working and what isn't. Lead with outcomes, not output counts. "We published 4 pieces" is less useful than "the competitive comparison guide is already ranking page 2 for a term with 2,400 monthly searches — here's what we're doing to push it to page 1."

Frame results against the goals you set together. If you're in the Strategy Sprint, this section is lighter and more about early signals. If you're in Growth Execution, this is the core of the call.

**What to cover:**

- Content published vs. plan
- Traffic, ranking, and conversion trends
- Top-performing pieces and why they're working
- Underperformers and your hypothesis on what to change
- Any AI visibility or LLM citation data worth noting

### 4. Strategy and Recommendations (5–10 min)

This is where you earn your seat. Don't just report — advise. Bring one or two things they didn't ask for: a content gap you spotted, a competitor publishing aggressively in a cluster you own, a keyword opportunity that maps to their sales motion.

**For Strategy Sprint customers:** This section is about alignment. Are the pillars right? Is the voice calibration landing? Are we removing publishing bottlenecks?

**For Growth Execution customers:** This is about optimization and expansion. What should we double down on? What should we stop doing? Where's the next growth lever?

**For at-risk customers:** This is about recovery. Name the problem directly. Bring a concrete plan, not just reassurance. "I know quality hasn't been where it needs to be. Here's what we've changed in the last week and here's how we're measuring whether it's working."

### 5. Align on Next Week (3–5 min)

Be specific about what's shipping next week, who owns what, and when they should expect deliverables. If you need something from them (approvals, access, feedback), ask for a specific deadline.

Don't leave the call with vague next steps. Every action item should have an owner and a date.

### 6. Close with Energy (1–2 min)

End on something forward-looking. Reference a result you're excited about, a piece that's about to go live, or a milestone that's coming up. The last thing they hear should make them feel good about working with you.

## Adjustments by Customer Stage

### Strategy Sprint (Weeks 1–8)

The Sprint is where trust is built or broken. Conversion target is 75–80%+ into 12-month contracts, so every interaction matters.

**Call cadence:** Weekly, with async updates every 2 days minimum.

**Focus areas by week:**

- **Weeks 1–2:** Context gathering and artifact sign-offs. You're proving you can learn their business fast. Bring the "What We Heard" doc, confirm you're building the right artifacts (Writing Guidelines, Company Context, Personas, Competitors, Calibration Guide).
- **Weeks 3–4:** Priorities and workflow. Show them the content quality rubric, align on editorial workflow, eliminate approval bottlenecks. First sample publications should be going through the pipeline.
- **Weeks 5–6:** Momentum and the circuit breaker. Performance review, early results, ROI signals. Week 6 is the honest conversation: are we both seeing enough here to continue? If yes, start talking about the 12-month engagement. If no, be honest about why.
- **Weeks 7–8:** Scale or wrap. Either ramp up production and lock in the contract, or document what you learned and close cleanly.

**Trust-busters to avoid during Sprint:**

- Going dark between calls
- Delivering artifacts late without a heads-up
- Publishing content that doesn't match their voice
- Surprising them with bad news instead of flagging it early

### Growth Execution (Month 3+)

You've earned the contract. Now you need to keep earning it every month. Target: 96%+ monthly retention.

**Call cadence:** Weekly, with quarterly business reviews.

**Focus areas:**

- Shift from "are we set up right?" to "are we driving results?"
- Every call should reference measurable outcomes
- Start planting seeds for expansion (new content types, new clusters, higher volume)
- Monitor the five health dimensions every month; don't wait for the QBR to flag issues

**Leading indicators that things are going well:**

- Client shares the content internally or on social
- They bring new stakeholders to calls
- They ask about expanding scope
- Response times stay fast
- They refer other companies

### At-Risk Accounts

An account is at-risk when any health dimension hits a 2, or the total score drops below 15.

**Leading signals to watch for:**

- Communication quality declining (shorter replies, less engagement in calls)
- Responsiveness dropping (approvals taking longer, calls getting rescheduled)
- Revision requests increasing
- Questions about cost relative to output
- Missing calls or sending a junior person instead of the champion

**How to run the call when a customer is at-risk:**

1. **Acknowledge the issue directly.** Don't dance around it. "I want to be upfront — I know the last few weeks haven't been at the level you expect, and I want to talk about what we're doing about it."
2. **Diagnose the root cause.** Is it output volume? Quality? Results? Relationship? Strategy alignment? Name it.
3. **Present a concrete recovery plan.** Not "we'll do better" — specific changes with timelines. "We've reassigned the editorial lead, here are three pieces in review right now that reflect the new standard, and I'll send you a revised content calendar by Friday."
4. **Set a check-in cadence.** Tighten the feedback loop. If weekly isn't enough, go to twice a week until confidence is restored.
5. **Escalate internally.** Pull in your director or senior leadership. Don't try to save a struggling account alone.

## After the Call (10 min)

- Send a follow-up within 2 hours summarizing key decisions, action items (with owners and dates), and what's coming next week
- Update the client health scores if anything shifted
- Log any expansion or churn signals
- Flag anything that needs escalation to your director
- Update the content calendar if priorities changed

---

## The Five Health Dimensions (Score 1–5 Each)

Use these to assess every account monthly. Be honest with yourself — inflated scores help no one.

1. **Output** — Are we publishing on schedule? Are we ahead or scrambling?
2. **Quality** — Would this content build or damage their reputation? Is it accurate, on-voice, and genuinely useful?
3. **Performance** — Is the content driving growth? Are results trending up or flat?
4. **Relationship** — Do they enjoy working with us? Are they expanding scope, referring others, or pulling away?
5. **Strategy** — Are they bought into the direction? Is the executive sponsor still aligned?

**Health tiers:**

- **Strong** (20+ total, no dimension below 4): Referenceable, likely to renew and expand
- **Healthy** (18+ total, no dimension below 3): Solid, monitor quarterly
- **At Risk** (15+ total or any dimension at 2): Needs active intervention this week
- **Critical** (below 15 or any dimension at 1): Escalate immediately, high churn risk

## Principles to Internalize

**Lead with outcomes, not output.** Nobody cares how many articles you published. They care whether the content is driving signups, pipeline, or brand authority.

**Be proactive, not reactive.** The best EMs bring insights their clients didn't ask for. You should know their competitive landscape, their content gaps, and their next opportunity before they do.

**Name problems early.** A small issue raised proactively builds trust. The same issue discovered by the client erodes it. Bad news doesn't age well.

**The relationship is the product.** Your strategic judgment, your responsiveness, your ability to make their life easier — that's what they're paying for. The content is the vehicle; you are the driver.

**Every week should get easier.** If you're solving the same problems repeatedly, something is broken upstream. Fix the system, not just the symptom.
