## 8-Week Content Foundation Plan

### Speed to Quality Seeds

**You can have the best strategy in the world, but if you can't act on it, what good does it do?**

This plan prioritizes speed of execution over perfect planning. We move fast to plant the right quality seeds, nurture them, and learn from the process. The goal isn't to have the perfect content strategy—it's to build a content engine that ships consistently and improves rapidly.

---

## Targeting Risk at Every Step

This plan targets one specific risk: **the risk of not shipping quality content consistently**. We've seen too many content strategies die in planning committees or get bogged down in endless revisions.

**We reduce risk in three ways:**

1. **Shaping Risk Reduction:** We solve open questions about audience, messaging, and quality before committing to production timelines. No rabbit holes or tangled dependencies make it into the work.

2. **Planning Risk Reduction:** Each week is capped with clear boundaries. If something isn't working by Week 6, we have a natural circuit breaker to prevent endless investment in approaches that need rethinking.

3. **Building Risk Reduction:** We integrate content creation with performance measurement from day one. Instead of building disconnected pieces hoping they'll work together, we create end-to-end content systems early and iterate based on real signals.

---

## The Right Level of Abstraction

This plan is shaped at the right level—not too vague ("increase organic traffic") and not too concrete (specifying exact article topics). Each week defines clear boundaries and outcomes while leaving room for the team to solve tactical details as we learn what works.

**Target:** Build a content engine that drives [X qualified leads/conversions] from organic channels per month within 6 months.

---

## Week 1: Kickoff and Learn Quickly

**Mission:** Rapidly absorb everything about the client's business, audience, and competitive landscape.

**Risk We're Targeting:** The risk of building content for the wrong audience or solving the wrong problems.

**What:** Deep-dive stakeholder interviews, product demos, competitive content analysis, audience pain point mapping, existing content audit.

**Why It Matters:** Fast learning prevents false starts. We sequence learning from most unknown (audience needs) to least worrisome (content formats) to integrate insights as quickly as possible.

**Success:** Complete business context documented, key stakeholders aligned, content gaps identified, no major unknowns remaining.

### Deliverables

1. **Access to Systems:** Google Search Console, Google Analytics, CMS access and walkthrough
2. *'What We Heard'* document is sent <24 hours after kick-off
3. All key artifacts generated and enriched with *'What We Heard'* + any other context the company shares post kick-off
4. **Deep Dives Scheduled:**
   - Product Deep Dive (1hr)
   - Expert Deep Dive (1hr)
   - Customer/User Deep Dive: This can either consist of user interviews, meeting with the head of User Research, getting access to any docs or databases the company has on users, personas, pain points, etc (1hr)
5. **Recurring Weekly Meeting Scheduled**
6. Send comprehensive follow-up of semi-shaped lanes, with an emphasis on the client reviewing 'What We Heard' and other key artifacts
7. **Golden Rule:** The client should not go 2 days without getting a progress update from our team in the first week

---

## Week 2: Set the Foundation, Align on Direction

**Mission:** Establish the strategic framework that will guide all future content decisions.

**Risk We're Targeting:** The risk of scattered content efforts that don't build toward meaningful business outcomes.

**What:** Define 3-5 content pillars targeting primary buyer personas, create brand voice guidelines, establish quality standards, set up tracking infrastructure.

**Why It Matters:** We solve strategic alignment questions before committing to production. Clear pillars ensure every piece builds toward topical authority without rabbit holes or tangled dependencies.

**Success:** Content strategy approved, brand guidelines finalized, measurement systems operational, team aligned on direction.

### Deliverables

1. Sign-off on all key artifacts from client (ideally this takes place a-sync or live on the first weekly sync meeting). Fully enriched with the most up to date client information:
   - Writing Guidelines
   - Company Context
   - Audience Personas
   - Direct and Audience Competitors
   - Calibration Guide
   - Examples of Good
   - Product Specific Artifact (Feature Matrix)
2. Finish all deep-dives
3. First weekly check in meeting: Use this to unblock anything from week 1, review 'What We Hear' and get feedback on most important articles and research completed since kick-off
4. Sample Publication/article sent
5. ContentOS fully fleshed out with topical clusters and recommended assignments by EOW

## Week 3: Set Priorities, Define Great

**Mission:** Establish what excellent content looks like for the target audience and prioritize the highest-impact opportunities.

**Risk We're Targeting:** The risk of producing content that doesn't meet audience needs or business quality standards.

**What:** Create content quality rubric, develop templates for different content types, prioritize topics based on audience need + business impact, establish editorial workflow.

**Why It Matters:** We define excellence and remove workflow obstacles before committing to volume production. This prevents quality degradation and publishing bottlenecks as we scale.

**Success:** Quality standards documented, content templates created, priority topics ranked and approved, publishing workflow tested end-to-end.

### Deliverables

*To be defined based on client context*

---

## Week 4: Set Definition of Done, Remove Publishing Blocks

**Mission:** Eliminate all friction from content creation to publication.

**Risk We're Targeting:** The risk of content getting stuck in approval cycles or publishing bottlenecks.

**What:** Map complete editorial workflow, identify bottlenecks in approval/publishing process, create content brief templates, establish stakeholder review cycles, test full publication pipeline.

**Why It Matters:** Publishing friction kills momentum. We build one meaningful piece of content end-to-end to test the entire system and remove obstacles before committing to higher volume.

**Success:** End-to-end publishing workflow documented and tested, approval bottlenecks eliminated, first calibration piece published, team confident in production rhythm.

### Deliverables

1. Looker Dashboard Set Up
2. Decide and align on which POD this account will be passed onto
3. Create a hand-off brief for the director and POD

---

## Week 5: Build Rhythm, Start Measuring

**Mission:** Establish sustainable content production cadence while implementing measurement systems.

**Risk We're Targeting:** The risk of unsustainable production or publishing content without learning from performance.

**What:** Publish 2-3 pieces targeting primary audience pain points, implement daily performance monitoring, create weekly learning review process, establish content optimization protocols.

**Why It Matters:** We integrate content creation with performance measurement from day one. Instead of hoping disconnected pieces work together, we sequence from most uncertain (audience response) to least worrisome (content formats) and learn what works immediately.

**Success:** Publishing rhythm proven sustainable, performance data flowing, optimization process operational, team learning from real signals.

### Deliverables

1. Set up LLM tracking (Looker + scrunch)
2. ME, CM, and Lead spend an hour to align the new director on it

---

## Week 6: Align on Big Picture (Proceed or Not)

**Mission:** Evaluate progress against goals and decide whether to continue the engagement.

**Risk We're Targeting:** The risk of continuing to invest time and resources in an approach that isn't driving results.

**What:** Comprehensive performance review, ROI analysis of content-to-lead pipeline, stakeholder feedback collection, assessment of team fit and content quality, decision on engagement continuation.

**Why It Matters:** This is our circuit breaker. Six weeks provides enough data to evaluate whether the content approach is driving toward conversion goals. If something isn't working, we have a natural stopping point to prevent endless investment in concepts that need rethinking.

**Success:** Performance data analyzed, stakeholder alignment on results, clear decision on engagement continuation, no ambiguity about next steps.

---

## Week 7: Ramp Up or Ramp Down

**Mission:** Scale successful approaches or wind down engagement based on Week 6 decision.

**Risk We're Targeting:** The risk of drift—either failing to capitalize on what's working or continuing what isn't.

**If Ramping Up:** Expand content production, implement advanced distribution strategies, optimize high-performing pieces, begin conversion funnel development.

**If Ramping Down:** Document lessons learned, transfer knowledge to internal team, complete final content optimizations, provide strategic recommendations.

**Why It Matters:** Clear scaling decisions prevent waste. We either double down on proven approaches or maximize value in the remaining time. No middle ground that leads to mediocre results.

**Success:** Either scaled content engine operational with measurable improvement, or comprehensive handoff completed with clear next steps.

---

## Week 8: Document and Wrap Up or Scale Up

**Mission:** Finalize engagement with complete documentation or establish foundation for continued scaling.

**Risk We're Targeting:** The risk of losing momentum or failing to transfer learnings effectively.

**If Wrapping Up:** Deliver comprehensive playbook, complete final performance analysis, transfer all assets and knowledge, provide 90-day strategic roadmap.

**If Scaling Up:** Document proven processes, create team expansion plan, establish advanced measurement systems, launch next-phase content initiatives.

**Why It Matters:** Proper documentation ensures learning transfers and momentum continues. Whether transitioning internally or scaling externally, the content engine must survive beyond the engagement.

**Success:** Complete engagement documentation delivered with clear success metrics, or scaling framework established and operational with proven growth trajectory.

---

## The Two-Track System

**Shaping Track:** Each week we analyze performance data and stakeholder feedback to shape the following week's approach. This happens in parallel with execution.

**Building Track:** Each week we execute the shaped work while gathering signals for the shaping track. Clear separation prevents analysis paralysis.

**Week 6 Decision Point:** The two-track system culminates in a clear decision based on measurable progress toward conversion goals.

---

## Core Properties of This Plan

**Property 1: It's Rough**
Tactical details are left open for the team to solve based on real-world constraints discovered during execution.

**Property 2: It's Solved**
Each week has clear outcomes that build toward the conversion goal. The path from current state to decision point is mapped out.

**Property 3: It's Bounded**
Each week has specific scope and time constraints. The Week 6 decision point provides a natural boundary for commitment.

## Customization Guidelines

### Adapting the Target

Replace "[X qualified leads/conversions]" with client-specific metrics:

- B2B SaaS: "500 qualified demo requests"
- E-commerce: "1000 email subscribers with 15% purchase rate"
- Service business: "50 consultation bookings"

### Adapting Content Focus

Adjust content pillars based on industry:

- **B2B Tech:** Product education, use cases, integration guides
- **Professional Services:** Thought leadership, case studies, process guides
- **E-commerce:** Product comparisons, buying guides, lifestyle content
- **Healthcare:** Educational content, compliance guides, patient resources

### Adapting Audience Targeting

Modify persona focus based on buyer characteristics:

- **Enterprise:** IT decision makers, procurement, end users
- **SMB:** Business owners, department heads, individual contributors
- **Consumer:** Demographics, psychographics, behavioral segments

### Adapting Success Metrics

Align measurement to business model:

- **Lead Generation:** Form fills, demo requests, consultation bookings
- **E-commerce:** Email signups, product page visits, purchase conversions
- **SaaS:** Free trial signups, feature adoption, upgrade conversions
- **Content/Media:** Subscriber growth, engagement rates, premium conversions

### Adapting Timeline

Modify based on client complexity and resources:

- **Complex Enterprise:** Extend to 10-12 weeks for additional stakeholder alignment
- **Fast-Moving Startup:** Compress to 6 weeks with daily check-ins
- **Seasonal Business:** Align timeline with business cycles and peak seasons

This template provides a proven framework while maintaining flexibility for diverse client needs and industries.
