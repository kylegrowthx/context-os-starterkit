- Three teams: Sales, Strategy Sprint, Delivery (pods).
- EMs own the client relationship end-to-end. They're accountable for conversion, renewals, and expansion.
- Editors should be producing content weeks ahead, not scrambling week-of.

## Overview

Our delivery model is unique. We have both the tech (our platform) and forward-deployed people who do the work alongside our customers.

On the supply side, we need:
- The right people
- The right process
- Enough capacity
- Trained and ramped up
- Delivering and exceeding expectations

## The Teams

Three main groups:

| Team | Focus | Phase |
|------|-------|-------|
| **Sales** | Qualify and close new business | Pre-Sprint |
| **Strategy Sprint** | Run 8-week sprints, convert to Growth Execution | Sprint |
| **Delivery** | Run Growth Execution in pods | Ongoing |

Different functions exist within each. Some roles span multiple phases. Some are dedicated to one.

## Core Roles

### Engagement Managers (EMs)

EMs are ultimately responsible for the end-to-end client relationship.

**They're part:**
- Customer success
- Strategist
- Growth marketer
- Sales
- Editor

That's a lot. But EMs are the drivers. They should be customer-first and customer-last for everything related to their engagement.

**Accountabilities:**

| Phase | EM Responsibility |
|-------|-------------------|
| Strategy Sprint | Successful conversion into Growth Execution |
| Growth Execution | Renewals and expansion |

**Scope:**
- Strategy Sprint: Pre-kickoff through handoff
- Growth Execution: Ongoing relationship, escalations, shared team coordination

**What good looks like:**
- Customers view them as the best hire they've ever made
- Proactive, not reactive
- Building relationships across the customer's organization
- Driving outcomes, not just managing output

---

### Production and Editorial (Editors)

Production and editorial are responsible for outputs and quality. The work that drives results.

**These roles are less customer-facing unless:**
- They're in a Strategy Sprint (more hands-on)
- There's an escalation
- Deep dives are needed to address issues

**Pacing:**

Editors should be producing content **weeks ahead**. Never producing content the week it's due.

If you're scrambling week-of, something is broken.

**The System Mindset:**

Our goal is to build a system. Production teams should approach their work as **finding failure modes in the pipelines and systematically fixing them**.

One-off last-mile execution is sometimes necessary. But the goal is not to do the same edits over and over again.

**The goal is to:**
- Check
- Give feedback
- Label
- Calibrate
- Improve the next iteration

**Every week serving the same client should get easier.** More leverage. Less effort for the same output.

If that's not happening, we're doing something wrong.

**When it is happening:**
- We can deliver more
- We can achieve more results
- We can focus on different tasks
- We can refresh, enrich, and optimize existing content
- We can find new opportunities to deliver value

## Forward-Deployed Teams (Shared Model)

These specialists are deployed where needed. Not dedicated to one client. Shared across engagements.

| Role | Function | Deployment |
|------|----------|------------|
| Directors | Strategy, escalations, quality | As needed |
| FDEs | AI workflows, technical setup | As needed |
| SEO Specialists | Audits, technical SEO | As needed |
| Design/Dev | Web work, implementation | As needed |

We identify where these specialists are needed and deploy them. They're a shared resource, not embedded in every pod.

## Pods (Growth Execution)

Once a customer converts from Strategy Sprint to Growth Execution, they're assigned to a pod.

### Pod Structure

```
Pod
├── Engagement Manager (lead)
├── Editor(s) (production)
└── Shared teams (as needed)
    ├── Directors
    ├── FDEs
    ├── SEO
    └── Design/Dev
```

**EMs manage a book of business** that varies in size and complexity.

**They hold the relationships.** They're responsible for escalations and pulling in help from shared teams when needed.

### Quality Escalation Path

If there are quality issues on the editorial side:

1. EM flags it
2. Chief Content Officer oversees quality across all teams
3. Editorial Directors help address specific issues
4. Editors are trained on platform operation and content production

## The System Mindset

This is critical. Read it twice.

**We are not a content factory where humans do the same manual work every week. We are building a system.**

### What This Means in Practice

- Every edit you make should feed back into the system
- Every failure mode you find should get fixed upstream
- Every week should be easier than the last for the same client
- If you're doing the same fix repeatedly, escalate it. That's a pipeline problem, not a content problem.

### The Goal

Production partners check, label, calibrate, and improve. They don't rewrite the same things over and over.

### When We Get This Right

- Capacity increases without adding headcount
- Quality stays consistent or improves
- Clients get more value
- Margins improve

### When We Get This Wrong

- Every client feels like starting from scratch
- Editors burn out
- Quality is inconsistent
- We can't scale

## How Capacity Works

To do more kickoffs, we need to scale every input. Each has a ratio and ramp time.

| Input | Ratio | Ramp Time |
|-------|-------|-----------|
| Sprint team capacity | 4 FTEs per 1 kickoff/week | 2 months |
| Forward-deployed experts | 2 FDEs per 2 kickoffs/week | Shared |
| Pod capacity | 1 pod per 6 monthly conversions | 2 months |
| Sales pipeline | 33% win rate, 6-week cycle | — |

**Every input has a constraint. Every constraint has a ramp time. That's why scaling is hard.**

### The Math to Scale from 2 to 4 Kickoffs/Week

| Input | Current (2/week) | Target (4/week) | Gap |
|-------|------------------|-----------------|-----|
| Sprint FTEs | 8 | 16 | +8 hires |
| FDEs | 2 | 4 | +2 hires |
| Pods | ~1/month | ~2/month | +1 pod |
| Pipeline | 18 opps/week | 36 opps/week | 2x pipeline |

Hiring takes time. Ramping takes time. Everything compounds on everything else.

## Summary

Our delivery model combines:
- **Platform** (AI workflows, ContentOS)
- **Forward-deployed people** (EMs, Editors, Specialists)
- **Shared resources** (Directors, FDEs, SEO, Design)
- **System thinking** (every week gets easier)

The goal is leverage. Not headcount. Every week serving the same customer should get better, faster, easier.

When we do this right, we can scale without linear hiring. When we don't, we're just another agency.
