At GrowthX, we promise clients AI + **experts** in the loop.

We need to keep this in mind whenever we're creating content.

Our benchmark for quality shouldn't be "it's good enough for AI." We are the experts and we are responsible for elevating the content quality.

We should aim to create **good content** that avoids using markers of what's become known as "**AI slop**." (And plain bad writing.)

---

## What is Good Content?

Good content is comprehensive, accurate, and helpful to its target audience. It uses principles of good writing that include:

### No Repetition
- 👍 *A topic cluster (or content cluster) is a group of interconnected, thematically related pages on a website. Each cluster includes one pillar page, which is the main page that provides a broad overview of a particular topic. And multiple subpages (also called cluster pages), which cover associated subtopics. All of these pages are connected with internal links.*

### No Fluff or Jargon
- 👍 *"AI excels at identifying patterns and connections. Use it to find new leads who are very similar to your current best customers (and therefore more likely to buy). Levente Kótka, CEO and founder of InnoBotZ, briefly mentioned this idea in a LinkedIn post. His method is to have AI review data from your customer CRM to find not-so-obvious similarities they share. Then, you can apply those characteristics to refine your targeting."*

### Avoiding the Should-How Fallacy
- 👍 "Qualify HARO respondents by including prerequisites, like industry, job title, or years of experience, in each question." instead of "Be very specific when you put out HARO requests to ensure you reach relevant contacts."

### Providing Valuable/Expert Insights
- 👍 *"People often send ad clicks straight to a product page or their homepage. While this can work, a dedicated landing page is often more effective. For example, Mike created this page, which was worth reading and had a catchy title."*

### Being Specific and Actionable
- 👍 *"Sometimes, traffic losses are due to up-and-coming or aggressive competitors stealing your SEO traffic. In Site Explorer, search for your website. Then, use the Competitors filter to add competitors to compare against. If your performance goes down as theirs goes up, it's likely they're taking your traffic."*

### Being Easy to Understand
- 👍 *"Similar to using smaller words, you should also aim to shorten your sentences. Imagine if I wrote something that your brain had to continue to read, with multiple commas, multiple ideas, and varying concepts, all in one huge run-on sentence... Makes things hard to follow, right? Instead, keep each sentence under 16–25 words unless absolutely necessary, and only share one or two ideas per sentence."*

### Directing Readers to Reputable Resources
- 👍 If you're listing a stat or a piece of research, find the original report where it appears, such as the "2024 State of Content Marketing by Y Company." **Do not** link to sources such as "30+ Statistics About Content Marketing". Those are not original sources. If you can't find a source for a stat, then it's best to remove it.

### Using BLUF or the Inverted Pyramid Principle
Places the most important information first, e.g. after an H2 or H3.

- 👍 *"**H2:** Benefits of the Inverted-Pyramid Style for Web Writing. **Opening paragraph:** The inverted pyramid is perfectly suited for the web - on any screen size. We know that users don't read carefully online. They have little patience for content that doesn't engage them. Users scroll, but only when they think that the content they want or need will appear on that page. The inverted pyramid style addresses all of these aspects of user behavior."*

## Good Structure

**Good content answers the search intent and is structured in a coherent way. This means that:**

- The structure and the angle of the article is helpful to the reader googling a specific keyword
- Every H2 is directly related to the H1/Title
- Every H3 is related to/answers a question posed by the H2

Since we're an agency, good content must also:
- Follow the client's guidelines
- Avoid mentioning their competitors (unless absolutely necessary)
- Describe the client's products/services accurately
- Include quality internal and external links

## What is AI Slop?

AI slop is poor-quality, AI-generated content that seemingly provides a lot of information. But little of it is valuable.

The signs of AI slop include:

### Repeating the Same Points with Slight Wording Variations
- ❌ *"An effective content strategy ensures your message reaches the right audience. With the right approach to content, you can effectively communicate your message to your ideal audience."*

### Fluffy, Meaningless Filler Content
- ❌ *"In today's fast-paced digital landscape, leveraging innovative solutions is the key to success for businesses of all sizes."*
- ❌ *"Content is a powerful tool for businesses today. When used well, it can have a significant impact."*

### Lack of Depth or Unique Insights
- ❌ *"Using social media can help increase your brand awareness."*

### Awkward, Overly Formal, or Robotic Tone
- ❌ *"Organizations must leverage multi-channel frameworks to drive scalable outcomes in the digital ecosystem."*

### Long and Wordy Sentences, or Same Sentence Structure Over and Over
- ❌ *"When creating content, it is important to ensure that the content you create is relevant to the audience you are targeting."*
- ❌ *"To create good content, you need to consider your audience, ensuring the content is relevant to them. The content should be tailored to their pain points, increasing the likelihood of engagement."*

### Using Jargon or Buzzwords
- ❌ *"Drive engagement by leveraging omnichannel touchpoints across the customer journey."*

### Overly Formatted Content
E.g., the article might be filled with bullet points or have meaningless H3s/H4s with thin content.

## How to Create Good Content with AI

> **Note:** You need to familiarize yourself with the client's product, audience, and industry before creating/editing a piece of content. It's impossible to create good content without this step.

When you need to generate or edit an AI article, follow these steps:

### 1. Look Up the Primary Keyword in Semrush

Go to the SERP analysis section.

- This step is important for us to understand what kind of content ranks on page one. Is it a listicle or a guide? What are the meta title/meta description patterns?
- It also reveals the search intent. Although tools like Semrush and Ahrefs can also identify intent, it helps to double-check. If the SERP is filled with landing pages, perhaps a blog post doesn't have a high chance of ranking.
- For example, you google the keyword "AI marketing campaigns". The SERP reveals that the top-ranking content heavily focuses on real examples of AI campaigns. So our content should also incorporate them in some way.
- Use location-specific SERP information. E.g. if your client targets Canada, don't use US search data.

### 2. Keep the Target Audience in Mind

There are two types of target audiences here: your client's and the keyword's. They should align.

- A keyword can be relevant for a broader audience, but even then it should include your client's target audience.
- For example, if your client targets B2B customers in the financial sector, you can't target a keyword such as "how to report credit card fraud" because this keyword is relevant for consumers, not businesses.
- When in doubt, always google the keyword and check the SERP. It will always reveal who the target audience is.

### 3. Create a Good Outline

- A good outline answers all the questions a reader could have regarding the target topic. It is structured logically, with each H2 answering a question related to the H1.
- The sections of the outline do not overlap. If one section talks about the benefits, we shouldn't talk about pros in another section.
- Watch out for "What is [main topic]?" subheadings. In most cases, we can answer this in the introduction. You don't need such sections *unless* your article is super top-of-the-funnel.
- The outline covers only the sections that are relevant to a specific topic.
- Ask: where does the client fit into this story? There might be an H3 where you can naturally weave in your client's product as a solution.

### 4. Review the First Draft

When you generate the first draft:

- Read through it and remove redundant information, AI fluff, and make other edits as needed.
- You can use AI to be your editing assistant. E.g. prompt ChatGPT or Claude to find writing issues in a specific section and fix them.
- Edit out sentences that are difficult to parse—especially for scanners. Look out for these difficult-to-read sentence structures:
  - "To do X, you need to do Y." → Better: "Do X by doing Y"
  - Long run-on sentences with multiple clauses
  - Filler words like "that," "can," "help," and "which" that can often be removed

### 5. Loop In Your Managing Editor

Not sure about a specific edit? Tag them directly in the doc to avoid going back and forth in Slack.
