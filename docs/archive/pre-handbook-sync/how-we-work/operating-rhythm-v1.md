# Operating Rhythm

## The point

Systems beat heroics. This is how we run the company: what meetings happen, when, who owns them, and what they produce.

Follow this rhythm and nothing falls through the cracks. Skip it and chaos compounds.

## The stack

**Daily standups** — Pod-level execution check-ins owned by Pod Leads.

**Leadership L10** — Weekly 90-min session to surface issues and unblock decisions. Owned by COO.

**CEO update** — Weekly written pulse on priorities, metrics, and wins. Owned by CEO.

**Business review** — Monthly performance vs. targets. Owned by COO + Finance.

**Quarterly planning** — Set Rocks, refresh OKRs, allocate resources. Owned by CEO + Leadership.

**Annual planning** — Vision, strategy, and major bets. Owned by CEO + Board.

## Weekly: the heartbeat

### Leadership L10 (90 minutes, same day every week)

This is the forcing function. Miss it and problems compound silently.

**Agenda:**

| Section | Time | What happens |
|---------|------|-------------|
| Check-in | 5 min | Personal good news, transition in |
| Scorecard | 5 min | Review off-track numbers only |
| Rocks | 5 min | On track / off track status |
| Headlines | 5 min | Notable wins, escalations, departures |
| To-do review | 5 min | Done / not done from last week |
| **IDS** | **60 min** | Identify, Discuss, Solve top issues |
| Wrap | 5 min | Recap to-dos, rate meeting 1-10 |

> **Note:** Same day, same time, no exceptions. Start and end on time. If you rate the meeting below 8, say why.

**Rules:**
- 6-7 people max (functional leads)
- No explanations for missed to-dos — just "done" or "not done"
- Issues List updated async throughout the week

**Owner:** COO runs the meeting. Chief of Staff documents decisions and to-dos.

### CEO weekly update (written, Monday morning)

500-800 words covering four sections:

1. **What I'm thinking about** — 1-3 topics with why they matter
2. **How we're doing** — Company metrics, honest assessment
3. **Wins** — Team accomplishments worth celebrating
4. **Question** — Something to drive engagement

Prepare Friday afternoon. Send Monday 9am. Always end with a question.

## Monthly: the review

### Business review (90 minutes, first week of month)

Exception-based. Don't review every metric — focus on variance.

**Meeting format:**
- Metric owners present variance and root cause
- "I don't know, investigating" is acceptable. "It's fine" when it's red is not
- No strategy discussions — tactical only
- Well-run reviews finish in 45 minutes

**Output:** Action items with owners and deadlines. Update Issues List for L10.

## Quarterly: the reset

### Quarterly planning (half-day, last week of quarter)

This is where you set Rocks and refresh direction.

**Pre-work (due 5 days before):**
- Each function submits draft Rocks (3-5 per team)
- Finance provides rolling forecast
- COO provides capacity analysis

**Agenda:**

| Block | Time | Purpose |
|-------|------|---------|
| Review prior quarter | 60 min | Rock completion rate, wins, misses, lessons |
| Company OKR check | 30 min | Are we on track? Adjust if needed |
| Capacity and constraints | 30 min | Hiring plan, pod capacity, bottlenecks |
| Set company Rocks | 60 min | 3-5 company-level priorities for next 90 days |
| Cascade to functions | 60 min | Align team Rocks to company Rocks |

**Rock requirements:**
- Specific deliverable
- Single owner
- Measurable success criteria
- Achievable in 90 days
- Clear connection to company goals

> **Tip:** Target 80%+ Rock completion rate per quarter. If you're hitting 100%, goals are too easy.

## Annual: the big picture

### Annual planning (full day + follow-ups, Q4)

**Outputs:**
- Company OKRs for the year
- Budget and hiring plan
- 3-year picture refresh
- Major strategic bets

1. **CEO drafts vision and priorities** — Set the direction before gathering input.
2. **Leadership offsite** — Pressure-test and align on strategy.
3. **Cascade to teams** — Translate company goals into team-level plans.
4. **Board review and approval** — Validate direction with governance.
5. **Company all-hands** — Communicate the plan to everyone.

## Decision-making: who decides what

Default framework: **DACI**

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|----------------|
| **Driver** | Keeps it moving, ensures decision by deadline |
| **Approver** | Makes final call (ONE person) |
| **Contributors** | Provide input, no vote |
| **Informed** | Need to know, no input |

> **Note:** **Speed check:** Most decisions are two-way doors — reversible. Don't treat them like one-way doors. If you can reverse it cheaply, move fast.
>
> **Deadlock breaker:** "Disagree and commit." Voice your disagreement with data. Then commit fully.

## Communication channels

| Channel | Purpose | Response time |
|---------|---------|---------------|
| Notion/Wiki | Decisions, policies, processes | Reference |
| Slack | Quick coordination, social, urgent | Same day |
| Email | External, formal records | 24 hours |
| Loom/Video | Async walkthroughs, context | 24-48 hours |
| Meetings | Relationship building, complex discussion | Scheduled |

> **Tip:** Writing helps everyone. Speaking only helps who's in the room.

## Pod-level operating rhythm

Each delivery pod runs its own micro-rhythm:

| Meeting | Cadence | Duration | Purpose |
|---------|---------|----------|---------|
| Daily standup | Daily | 15 min | Blockers, priorities, handoffs |
| Pod retro | Bi-weekly | 30 min | What's working, what's not, improve |
| Client sync | Weekly | 30 min | Status, feedback, upcoming |

### Customer health tracking

Each account is scored weekly on 5 dimensions (1-5 points each, 25 total):

| Dimension | Question |
|-----------|----------|
| Output | Are we doing the work? |
| Quality | Is the work good? |
| Performance | Is it working? |
| Relationship | Do they love us? |
| Strategy | Are we aligned? |

Accounts are placed in health tiers:

| Tier | Criteria |
|------|----------|
| Strong | 20+ total AND no dimension below 4 |
| Healthy | 18+ total AND no dimension below 3 |
| At Risk | 15+ total OR any dimension at 2 |
| Critical | Below 15 total OR any dimension at 1 |

**Healthy ARR %** = weighted sum of account ARR by tier / total ARR. Target: 80%.

## The scorecard

Track weekly. Review variance in L10. Deep-dive monthly.

**Leading indicators** (what you can influence):
- Kickoffs scheduled
- Pipeline coverage ratio
- Healthy ARR %
- Qualified opportunities created
- Rock completion rate

**Lagging indicators** (what happened):
- ARR
- Sprint conversion rate
- Gross margin
- Net Revenue Retention
- Churn

> **Tip:** Balance: 60% leading, 40% lagging. If all your metrics are lagging, you're flying blind.

## Getting started

1. **Month 1: Foundation** — Lock in weekly L10 day/time. Publish scorecard with owners. Start CEO weekly update cadence. Document current Rocks.
2. **Month 2: Expansion** — Run first monthly Business Review. Roll out DACI for cross-functional decisions. Establish pod-level standups.
3. **Month 3: Full rhythm** — Run first quarterly planning session. Set next-quarter Rocks with success criteria. Review and adjust scorecard metrics.

## The bottom line

The operating rhythm is the company's metabolism. Run it consistently and problems surface early, decisions get made, and execution compounds.

Skip it and you're flying blind, reacting to fires, wondering why things fell through the cracks.

Same day, same time, every week. That's the discipline that scales.
