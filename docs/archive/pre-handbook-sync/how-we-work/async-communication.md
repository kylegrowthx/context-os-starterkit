# Asynchronous Communication

This document serves as a guide to how we communicate at GrowthX. Since GrowthX is a globally distributed team, we rely on both sync and async, and each serves a different role.

---

## Definitions

- **Sync Communication**: Communication happening in real-time, like meetings, Slack DMs & huddles, or calls. This is best used for immediate, real-time problem-solving, such as 1-1s, urgent issues, and retrospectives.
- **Async Communication**: Communication that doesn't require an immediate response, allowing recipients to engage when it suits them. Ideal for things that require deep thinking, announcements OR requests that a person needs to see.
- **Async by Default**: A mindset that prefers async methods first. Ask yourself: "Does this need to be a meeting?" If it can be handled async, choose that route.

> **AVOID SLACK DMs:** We consider Slack DMs sync – use them for urgent needs, for when you are blocked and need someone's attention fast.

---

## Benefits of Async by Default

1. **Deep, Focused Work**: Fewer interruptions mean more opportunities to focus and achieve "flow".
2. **Faster Delivery**: Async allows people to contribute according to their schedules, optimizing workflow around the clock.
3. **Flexibility and Lower Stress**: Team members have control over their own schedules, leading to a more balanced work life.
4. **Inclusion**: Async ensures that work isn't bound by time zones, making it easier for everyone to contribute.
5. **Knowledge Retention**: Async encourages thorough documentation, making knowledge more accessible.

## How We Communicate

### We Assume Good Intent

Assuming good intent means believing that your colleagues are not trying to harm or ignore you with their communication. Instead of assuming the worst from a short message or reaction, we interpret it positively. This promotes constructive and collaborative communication, reducing conflicts and misunderstandings.

You send a detailed message to a colleague, and they reply with a 👍.
- ❌ **Bad Intent**: You might think they are being dismissive or not paying attention.
- ✅ **Good Intent**: They are using emojis to acknowledge your message and indicate they are on it.

### We Self-Serve Before Reaching Out

Before asking for help, take the time to look through existing resources such as the knowledge base, Notion, or even previous Slack conversations. Self-serving helps reduce noise and ensures we don't repeat questions that have already been answered.

### We Use Slack Over Email

Slack is the main tool for internal communication at GrowthX, while email is reserved for external parties.

**Why Slack?**
- **Transparency**: Conversations are easy to search, and everyone can follow along.
- **Security**: Slack is less susceptible to phishing compared to email.
- **Open Conversations**: Public channels make it easy to add someone to ongoing discussions, ensuring full context is available.

### We Default to Public Communication

We communicate in public channels whenever possible to maintain transparency, avoid silos, and encourage collaboration.

**When to Use Private Channels or DMs**
- Sensitive or confidential discussions.
- Personal or HR-related conversations.

### We Keep Conversations Close to the Work

Reply in the same place where the discussion started, using threads or comments to keep context intact. Avoid spreading one topic over multiple channels or tools.

- **Slack**: Use threads to keep conversations organized.
- **Notion**: Comment directly on relevant pages to keep the context in one place.

### We Use Functional Emojis

Functional emojis are a shared language at GrowthX:
- **👍** = Agree/approved.
- **👀** = I'm reviewing.
- **✅** = Completed.
- **🙏** = Thank you.

### We Set Expectations for Responses

- **Timely Acknowledgment**: Use emojis like 👀 to indicate you have seen a message.
- **Response Expectations**: Generally, reply within one working day unless marked as urgent. Communicate when you plan to provide a detailed response.

### Manage Notifications

Use Slack's **Do Not Disturb (DND)** to minimize interruptions during focus times or outside of work hours. Schedule messages so they reach the recipient at a reasonable hour without disturbing them off-hours.

## When to Use Sync Communication

Some situations call for synchronous communication:
- **1:1 Meetings**: For personal topics or feedback.
- **Project Kickoffs**: To align expectations quickly.
- **Social Interaction**: Virtual coffee chats or onboarding.
- **Urgent issues**: Something is on fire, or you need immediate help.

---

## Avoid Sync for These Scenarios

- **Status Updates**: Use Notion or Slack posts.
- **Simple Decisions**: Use Slack messages or comments.
- **Report Creation**: Use written formats shared asynchronously.

---

## What Async Looks Like in Practice

- **Public Communication**: Share non-sensitive information in open Slack channels or in Notion.
- **Document Everything**: Project decisions, status updates, and FAQs should be documented in Notion for easy reference.
- **Async Meetings**: Prepare a Notion document and invite others to collaborate asynchronously before scheduling a meeting.
- **Over-Communicate**: Be proactive and share updates frequently.
- **Default to Action**: Execute tasks even if all details aren't perfect, and adapt as needed.
- **Recording and Agendas**: Always have an agenda for recurring meetings and record them for those unable to attend.

---

## Slack Etiquette for Async Work

- **Channel Guidelines**: Follow key channels, such as #general and your team's channel. Reply when mentioned directly.
- **Assume Offline**: Don't depend on the "green dot". Assume people are offline and provide all necessary information in your initial message.
- **Use Tags**: Label messages clearly:
  - **✅ ANSWERED**
  - **❓ QUESTION**
  - **[DECISION] Important decision needing input**
- **Closing Threads**: Mark a thread complete with a ✅ emoji to indicate closure.
- **Ad-Hoc Syncs**: After three back-and-forth async messages, consider a quick sync (e.g., Slack huddle). Summarize outcomes in writing.

---

## Async Tools & Tips

- **DND Mode**: Set regular DND times that align with your work hours to manage notifications and keep your focus.
- **Calendar Integration**: Use tools like Clockwise to automate focus time and update Slack statuses.
- **Organize Slack**: Use Slack's starring or custom sections feature to keep important channels easily accessible.
- **Focus Time**: Use the "🤫 Focus Time" Slack status to signal deep work periods. Coworkers will know not to expect an immediate response unless it's urgent.
- **Sync and Async Balance**: Default to async, but use sync when it adds a unique value. Aim to strike a balance that enhances productivity and collaboration.

By embracing asynchronous communication, GrowthX ensures every team member can work at their best, uninterrupted by constant notifications, while maintaining full transparency and efficiency in collaboration. Let's work together—mindfully and asynchronously!
