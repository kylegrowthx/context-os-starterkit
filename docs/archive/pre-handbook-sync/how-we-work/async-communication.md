# Async Communication

GrowthX is a globally distributed team. We rely on both sync and async communication, and each serves a different role.

## Definitions

- **Sync communication**: Real-time interactions like meetings, Slack DMs, huddles, or calls. Best for immediate problem-solving, 1-1s, urgent issues, and retrospectives.
- **Async communication**: Messages that don't require an immediate response, letting recipients engage when it suits them. Ideal for deep thinking, announcements, or requests someone needs to see.
- **Async by default**: A mindset that prefers async first. Ask yourself: "Does this need to be a meeting?" If it can be handled async, choose that route.

> **Warning:** **Avoid Slack DMs.** We treat DMs as sync — use them only for urgent needs when you're blocked and need someone's attention fast.

## Why async by default

**Deep, focused work** — Fewer interruptions mean more time in flow state.

**Faster delivery** — People contribute on their own schedules, keeping work moving around the clock.

**Flexibility** — Team members control their schedules, leading to better work-life balance.

**Inclusion** — Work isn't bound by time zones — everyone can contribute equally.

**Knowledge retention** — Async encourages thorough documentation, making knowledge more accessible.

## Assess your async acumen

Not everyone is an expert in async right away. Here's a progression:

**Level 1 — Foundations:**
- Assume good intent and communicate with a positive tone
- Self-serve solutions before reaching out — search docs and prior discussions first
- Use Slack for internal communication; email for external
- Default to public channels for transparency
- Use functional emojis for simple reactions (👍, 👀, ✅)
- Acknowledge messages when pinged directly

**Level 2 — Building skills:**
- Keep conversations in one place, closest to the work (threads, comments)
- Communicate clearly with short sentences and minimal jargon
- Format for readability: bullets, headings, clear punctuation
- Use @mentions thoughtfully to avoid unnecessary notifications

**Level 3 — Advanced:**
- Create inclusive communication suitable for a global audience
- Lead with the important points, then provide context and detail
- Over-communicate to answer potential questions before they arise

**Level 4 — Mastery:**
- Use concise videos for sharing complex information visually
- Store important communication where it's easily accessible

## How we communicate

### Assume good intent

When a colleague replies with a 👍, don't assume they're being dismissive. They're acknowledging your message and indicating they're on it. This mindset promotes constructive collaboration and reduces misunderstandings.

### Self-serve before reaching out

Before asking for help, search existing resources — the knowledge base, Notion, previous Slack conversations. This reduces noise and avoids repeating questions that have already been answered.

### Slack over email

Slack is our primary internal communication tool. Email is reserved for external parties.

**Why Slack?**
- **Transparency**: Conversations are searchable and everyone can follow along
- **Security**: Less susceptible to phishing than email
- **Open conversations**: Public channels make it easy to add context for anyone joining later

### Default to public communication

Communicate in public channels whenever possible. Use private channels or DMs only for sensitive, confidential, or HR-related conversations.

### Keep conversations close to the work

Reply where the discussion started. Use threads in Slack, comments in Notion. Don't spread one topic across multiple channels or tools.

### Functional emojis

These are a shared language at GrowthX:
- **👍** = Agree / approved
- **👀** = I'm reviewing
- **✅** = Completed
- **🙏** = Thank you

### Set expectations for responses

- Use emojis like 👀 to indicate you've seen a message
- Reply within one working day unless marked urgent
- Communicate when you plan to provide a detailed response

### Manage notifications

Use Slack's **Do Not Disturb** to minimize interruptions during focus time or outside work hours. Schedule messages so they reach the recipient at a reasonable hour.

## When to use sync

Some situations call for real-time communication:
- **1:1 meetings** — Personal topics or feedback
- **Project kickoffs** — Align expectations quickly
- **Social interaction** — Virtual coffee chats or onboarding
- **Urgent issues** — Something is on fire and you need immediate help

> **Note:** **Avoid sync for:** Status updates (use Notion or Slack posts), simple decisions (use Slack or comments), and report creation (use written formats shared async).

## Async in practice

- **Share openly**: Post non-sensitive information in public Slack channels or Notion
- **Document everything**: Capture project decisions, status updates, and FAQs in Notion
- **Async meetings**: Prepare a Notion doc and invite collaboration before scheduling a sync
- **Over-communicate**: Share updates proactively and frequently
- **Default to action**: Execute even if all details aren't perfect — adapt as you go
- **Record and agenda**: Always have an agenda for recurring meetings and record them for those who can't attend

## Slack etiquette

- **Follow key channels**: Subscribe to #general and your team's channel. Reply when mentioned directly
- **Assume offline**: Don't rely on the green dot. Provide all necessary information in your initial message
- **Label messages clearly**: Use tags like ✅ ANSWERED, ❓ QUESTION, or [DECISION] for important requests
- **Close threads**: Mark threads complete with a ✅ to indicate closure
- **Know when to sync**: After three back-and-forth async messages, hop on a quick huddle — then summarize outcomes in writing

## Tools and tips

> **Tip:**
> - **DND mode**: Set regular Do Not Disturb times aligned with your work hours
> - **Calendar integration**: Use tools like Clockwise to automate focus time and update Slack status
> - **Organize Slack**: Star important channels or use custom sections
> - **Focus time status**: Use the Focus Time Slack status to signal deep work periods
> - **Balance**: Default to async, but use sync when it adds unique value
