## Overview

Our sales motion is primarily inbound and referral-driven. Word of mouth has driven nearly all our growth. The typical deal is a paid 8-week Strategy Sprint ($15K-$25K/mo) that serves as both the entry point and the product demo for a long-term Growth Execution contract. Average sales cycle is ~6 weeks from qualified lead to signed sprint. Win rate is approximately 33% of qualified pipeline.

We don't do long, complex enterprise sales cycles. The sprint de-risks the decision for both sides: the client sees our work before committing long-term, and we confirm fit before investing delivery resources.

## Sales Stages

| Stage | Activities | Exit Criteria | Typical Duration |
|-------|-----------|---------------|-----------------|
| Lead | Inbound inquiry, referral, or CheckThat-generated lead. Initial outreach and scheduling. | Discovery call scheduled | 1-2 weeks |
| Discovery | Discovery call to understand their content challenges, current approach, goals, team, decision-making process. ICP scoring. | ICP score 45+, champion identified, mutual fit confirmed | 1 week |
| Strategy Call | Present our model (service-as-software), share relevant case studies, outline what the 8-week sprint looks like and what they can expect. Address objections. | Client agrees to move forward, requests proposal | 1 week |
| Proposal | Send scoping document with sprint outline, pricing ($15K-$25K/mo), deliverables, and timeline. Customize based on discovery findings. | Proposal reviewed, questions answered | 1 week |
| Negotiation | Handle final questions, scope adjustments, legal/procurement review. Keep momentum. | Contract signed, start date set | 1-2 weeks |
| Closed Won | Contract executed. Trigger Week 00 prep process. Handoff to delivery. | Sales handoff complete, sprint team assigned | — |

## Qualification

We use the ICP criteria in [ideal-customer-profile.md](../business/ideal-customer-profile.md) to qualify prospects.

**Quick qualification checklist:**
- [ ] B2B technology company (SaaS, AI/ML, DevTools, Cloud, Cybersecurity)
- [ ] Series A or later with budget capacity for $15K-$25K/mo
- [ ] Champion identified who has decision authority and moves fast on approvals
- [ ] Content is seen as a growth lever, not a cost center
- [ ] No multi-week compliance/legal review process that would break sprint cadence

## Discovery Call Structure

1. **Open with context** (2 min) — Reference what you know about their company. Show you did your homework. Don't start with a pitch.
2. **Understand their situation** (15 min) — What's their current content approach? What's working and what isn't? What are they trying to achieve in the next 6-12 months? Who makes decisions on content and marketing? What have they tried before? Why now?
3. **Introduce the model** (10 min) — Explain service-as-software at a high level. The sprint as an entry point. How results compound over time. Use their language, not ours. Frame everything against their specific problems.
4. **Close for next steps** (3 min) — If the fit is clear, propose a strategy call to go deeper. If it's borderline, be honest about concerns and whether it makes sense to continue. If it's not a fit, say so directly and explain why. Better to lose a deal cleanly than waste both sides' time.

## Pricing

**Strategy Sprint:** $15K-$25K/mo for 8 weeks. Price varies based on scope (number of content pillars, volume, complexity of industry, technical requirements).

**Growth Execution:** Ongoing monthly retainer at similar or higher rate. Pricing adjusts based on content volume, number of clusters, and additional services.

**Product subscriptions:** CheckThat and ContentOS are priced separately as SaaS subscriptions. Often bundled with Growth Execution contracts for expanded value.

Pricing is value-based, not hourly. We price against the outcome delivered, not the time spent. Custom quotes for every engagement based on discovery findings.

## Handoff to Delivery

When a deal closes:

1. **Sales handoff meeting:** Sales briefs the assigned Engagement Manager on everything learned during the sales process: client priorities, stakeholder dynamics, special requests, any concerns raised, champion relationship context, and all call recordings/notes.
2. **Week 00 prep kicks off:** EM begins pre-kickoff preparation: deep-dive research on the client's business, product, audience, competitors. Workspace setup. Strategy hypothesis drafted. (See Week 00 Prep guide for full details.)
3. **Client welcome:** Send welcome message outlining the engagement plan, major milestones, and what the client can expect in Week 1. Request access to systems (Google Search Console, Google Analytics, CMS) and any existing materials (brand decks, style guides, competitor intel).

See the Week 00 Prep guide and 8-Week Plan for the full onboarding and delivery process.
