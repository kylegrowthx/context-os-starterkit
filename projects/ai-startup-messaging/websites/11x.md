# 11x — Website Messaging Research

## Headline / Hero Messaging

**Primary headline:** "Digital workers, Human results"

**Supporting copy:** 11x positions as the company building AI "digital workers" that replace entire job functions — starting with the AI SDR named "Alice." Their tagline and brand name reference the aspiration to make workers 11x more productive.

## Positioning

**Category:** AI Digital Workers / AI SDR

**Market frame:** 11x doesn't position as a "tool" or "platform." They position as a provider of digital workers — AI agents that replace headcount, not augment it. This is a deliberate choice: they're selling the replacement of a role (SDR), not a productivity boost for existing SDRs.

**Competitive alternatives:** Human SDRs, other AI SDR platforms (AiSDR, Ava by Artisan), outbound platforms with AI features (Outreach, SalesLoft), Clay + automation stack.

**Unique attributes:**
- "Digital workers" framing (not tools, not assistants)
- Alice: Named AI SDR agent with a personality and brand
- Replacement positioning (replaces SDRs, doesn't assist them)
- "Killing traditional software" narrative
- End-to-end SDR workflow (research, outreach, follow-up, booking)

**Value themes:**
- Replacement: Digital workers replace headcount (not just augment)
- Results: "Human results" from digital workers
- Cost: Fraction of the cost of a human SDR
- Scale: Digital workers don't sleep, don't churn, don't need training
- Bold vision: "Killing traditional software"

## Key Messaging Pillars

1. **"Digital workers, Human results"** — The core promise. You get the quality of a human SDR without the cost, management overhead, and scaling constraints.

2. **"Killing traditional software"** — Provocative narrative about the future of SaaS. 11x believes AI agents (digital workers) replace software tools entirely. You don't need an outbound platform if you have a digital worker who does outbound.

3. **"Alice"** — Named AI agent. Personification makes the AI feel real, creates brand stickiness, and makes the "digital worker" concept tangible.

4. **Replacement, not augmentation** — Bold positioning choice. Most AI companies say "augment humans." 11x says "replace the function." Higher stakes, higher reward messaging.

## Social Proof / Credibility

- $50M Series B at $350M valuation
- Led by Benchmark
- Alice AI SDR deployed at scale
- Aggressive growth in AI SDR market
- Media coverage for provocative positioning

## Brand Voice & Tone

- Bold and provocative (deliberately controversial)
- Future-oriented (painting a picture of a world without traditional software)
- Personified AI (Alice has a name, personality, brand)
- Startup energy (move fast, think big)
- Direct and unapologetic about replacement narrative

## Narrative Arc

1. **Problem:** SDRs are expensive, hard to hire, hard to train, and hard to retain. The traditional SDR model doesn't scale.
2. **Insight:** What if the SDR function could be performed by a digital worker? Not a tool that helps SDRs, but a worker that IS the SDR.
3. **Category:** AI Digital Workers — autonomous AI agents that perform entire job functions
4. **Product:** Alice — the first digital SDR. She researches, writes, follows up, and books meetings.
5. **Vision:** "Killing traditional software" — digital workers replace entire software categories
6. **Bold claim:** The future isn't better tools. It's workers that don't need tools.

## What's Working (Initial PMM Assessment)

- **"Digital workers" framing is bold and differentiated.** While competitors say "AI-powered" or "AI-assisted," 11x says "digital workers." The language creates a new mental model.
- **Alice as a named agent** is a smart branding move. It makes the abstract tangible and creates word-of-mouth ("Have you tried Alice?").
- **Replacement positioning** is risky but attention-getting. In a market where everyone says "augment," saying "replace" stands out.
- **"Killing traditional software"** is a visionary narrative that attracts investors and press.

## What's Missing / Gaps

- Replacement narrative alienates potential buyers (SDR managers, VP Sales who manage SDR teams)
- Controversy around AI replacing jobs could create negative brand associations
- $350M valuation for a Series B creates high expectations
- Alice's actual performance vs. human SDRs is debated in the market
- "Killing traditional software" is provocative but hard to prove
