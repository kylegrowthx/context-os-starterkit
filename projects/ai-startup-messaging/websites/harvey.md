# Harvey — Website Messaging Research

## Headline / Hero Messaging

**Primary headline:** "Professional Class AI"

**Supporting copy:** Harvey positions as the AI platform for elite professional services — not just legal, but expanding into professional knowledge work broadly. The messaging emphasizes that Harvey is built specifically for the complexity and rigor of professional work.

## Positioning

**Category:** Professional Class AI (category creation)

**Market frame:** Harvey doesn't position as "legal AI" anymore — they've graduated to "Professional Class AI." This is a deliberate category expansion play. They started with law (hardest vertical to crack due to accuracy requirements) and now use that credibility to expand into adjacent professional services.

**Competitive alternatives:** Thomson Reuters AI (Westlaw), LexisNexis AI, Paxton AI (smaller), general-purpose AI (ChatGPT, Claude for legal), in-house legal tech teams at BigLaw firms.

**Unique attributes:**
- "Professional Class" positioning (not just legal, not general-purpose)
- 8+ hours saved per week per lawyer (claimed)
- $8B valuation (signals market confidence)
- Built for the complexity and rigor professional work demands
- Custom-trained models for professional domains
- Enterprise-grade security and compliance

**Value themes:**
- Prestige: "Professional Class" = premium, elite, trustworthy
- Productivity: 8+ hours saved per week per lawyer
- Precision: Built for domains where errors have real consequences
- Expansion: Legal-first, but applicable to all professional services

## Key Messaging Pillars

1. **"Professional Class AI"** — Category creation at its finest. Not "legal AI" (too narrow), not "enterprise AI" (too generic). "Professional Class" implies a tier of quality — like business class on a flight.

2. **"8+ hours saved per week per lawyer"** — Quantified productivity gain that's specific enough to be credible and large enough to be compelling.

3. **"Built for domains where errors have consequences"** — Implicit differentiation from general-purpose AI. Harvey understands that professional work has stakes.

4. **Expansion narrative** — Started in legal → expanding to professional services broadly. The narrative is: we cracked the hardest vertical first, now we're expanding.

## Social Proof / Credibility

- $8B valuation (one of the highest in AI startups)
- $150M Series E
- Used by top-tier law firms (Allen & Overy was an early marquee customer)
- Backed by Sequoia, Google Ventures, OpenAI
- 8+ hours saved per week per lawyer

## Brand Voice & Tone

- Premium and prestigious (matches the professional audience)
- Confident without being brash
- Enterprise-quality language (security, compliance, rigor)
- Category-defining (educating the market on what "Professional Class AI" means)
- Aspirational (this is the future of professional work)

## Narrative Arc

1. **Problem:** AI promises to transform knowledge work, but general-purpose AI isn't built for the precision and stakes of professional services
2. **Insight:** Professional work is different — errors have consequences, context is complex, trust is non-negotiable
3. **Proof:** Harvey started with law (the hardest professional domain) and succeeded where others failed
4. **Category:** Professional Class AI — a new category for AI built to professional standards
5. **Expansion:** What works for lawyers works for accountants, consultants, and all professional services
6. **Vision:** Harvey is the AI platform for the professional class

## Notable Quotes / Phrases

- "Professional Class AI"
- "8+ hours saved per week per lawyer"
- "Built for domains where errors have consequences"
- "Enterprise-grade AI for professional services"

## What's Working (Initial PMM Assessment)

- **"Professional Class AI" is masterful category design.** It elevates Harvey above both "legal AI" tools and general-purpose "enterprise AI." The naming implies a quality tier.
- **Legal-first credibility** creates a natural expansion story. If you can build for lawyers, you can build for any professional.
- **Prestige positioning** matches the buyer (BigLaw, top firms) and their self-image.
- **$8B valuation** itself becomes a messaging asset — signals that the market agrees.

## What's Missing / Gaps

- Premium positioning may limit mid-market adoption (contrast with Paxton AI's accessibility play)
- "Professional Class" is aspirational but vague — what specifically makes it "professional class" vs. other enterprise AI?
- Expansion beyond legal is still early — risk of spreading thin
- Limited public pricing transparency
