# Warmly — Website Messaging Research

## Headline / Hero Messaging

**Primary headline:** "Autonomous Revenue Agents for B2B GTM"

**Supporting copy:** Warmly positions as the autonomous revenue orchestration platform that identifies, engages, and converts website visitors using AI agents — specifically two named agents and a "context graph" that powers intelligent engagement.

## Positioning

**Category:** Autonomous Revenue Orchestration / Website Visitor Intelligence

**Market frame:** Warmly started in website visitor identification (de-anonymizing website traffic) and has evolved into "autonomous revenue agents." The progression is: identify who's on your website → understand their intent → engage them automatically → convert them. The "autonomous" framing means the AI agents handle the entire workflow without human intervention.

**Competitive alternatives:** Website visitor identification tools (Clearbit Reveal, Lead Forensics, RB2B), chatbots (Drift, Intercom), sales engagement platforms, intent data providers.

**Unique attributes:**
- Two named AI agents working in tandem
- Context graph architecture connecting all buyer signals
- Autonomous engagement (no human in the loop for initial outreach)
- Website visitor identification + intent signals + engagement in one platform
- Small but efficient team (capital efficient)

**Value themes:**
- Autonomy: Revenue agents work 24/7 without human intervention
- Intelligence: Context graph provides complete buyer understanding
- Speed: Engage visitors while they're still on your site
- Efficiency: Replace multiple tools (identification + intent + engagement) with one platform

## Key Messaging Pillars

1. **"Autonomous Revenue Agents"** — The core promise. Not chatbots (reactive), not automation (rules-based), but autonomous agents that proactively engage buyers.

2. **"Context Graph"** — Technical differentiator shared with Conversion.ai's approach. A unified data layer that gives agents complete context about every visitor.

3. **Two-agent architecture** — Interesting structural choice. Two specialized agents working together rather than one general-purpose agent.

4. **Website visitor intelligence → Revenue orchestration** — Evolution narrative. Started with identification, now orchestrates the entire revenue workflow.

## Social Proof / Credibility

- $6M Series A+ (capital efficient)
- Growing in B2B website visitor intelligence space
- Active in GTM/sales tech community
- Strong content marketing and thought leadership presence

## Brand Voice & Tone

- Energetic and startup-y
- Community-driven (active in GTM communities)
- Technical but accessible
- Agent-forward (the AI agents are the product heroes)
- Growth-oriented (speaks to revenue teams)

## Narrative Arc

1. **Problem:** B2B websites get traffic, but most visitors leave anonymous. Sales teams can't follow up on what they can't see.
2. **Evolution:** First-gen tools identified visitors. That helped, but it just created more work for sales teams.
3. **Insight:** Identification without action is just more data. You need agents that identify, understand, and engage — autonomously.
4. **Solution:** Warmly's two AI agents + context graph identify visitors, understand their intent, and engage them in real-time.
5. **Outcome:** Autonomous revenue orchestration — your website becomes a revenue engine, not just a brochure.

## What's Working (Initial PMM Assessment)

- **Evolution from identification to orchestration** is a smart narrative arc
- **"Autonomous" positioning** differentiates from chatbot and basic automation tools
- **Context graph** adds technical credibility
- **Capital efficiency** ($6M raise, lean team) is a positive signal for the GTM community

## What's Missing / Gaps

- Smaller raise ($6M) vs. competitors may signal limited resources
- "Autonomous Revenue Agents" is similar to language used by several other companies
- Two-agent architecture needs more explanation — why two? What does each do?
- Limited brand recognition outside the GTM community
- The messaging borrows heavily from broader AI agent trends — harder to own
