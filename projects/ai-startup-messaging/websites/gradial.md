# Gradial — Website Messaging Research

## Headline / Hero Messaging

**Primary headline:** "Marketing at the Speed of Thought"

**Supporting copy:** Gradial positions as the agentic marketing automation platform that uses AI agents to execute marketing workflows with 99.9% accuracy at 80% time savings.

## Positioning

**Category:** Agentic Marketing Automation

**Market frame:** Gradial occupies the emerging "agentic" marketing space — AI agents that don't just assist marketers but autonomously execute marketing tasks. The "speed of thought" framing positions marketing execution as the bottleneck, and AI agents as the solution.

**Competitive alternatives:** Traditional marketing automation (HubSpot, Marketo, Pardot), point solutions for specific marketing tasks, marketing ops teams doing manual work.

**Unique attributes:**
- AI agents that autonomously execute marketing workflows
- 99.9% accuracy claim (quality at speed)
- 80% time savings (efficiency gain)
- Agentic architecture (agents do the work, marketers direct)
- Multi-channel marketing execution

**Value themes:**
- Speed: Execute marketing at the speed of thought, not the speed of process
- Accuracy: 99.9% — AI agents don't make the mistakes humans do at scale
- Efficiency: 80% time savings frees marketers for strategy
- Autonomy: Agents execute, marketers manage

## Key Messaging Pillars

1. **"Marketing at the Speed of Thought"** — Aspirational headline. The bottleneck in marketing isn't ideas, it's execution. Gradial removes the execution bottleneck.

2. **"Agentic Marketing Automation"** — Category claim. Not just automation (rules-based), but agentic (AI-driven, autonomous). Evolution beyond traditional martech.

3. **"99.9% accuracy"** — Precision claim that addresses the #1 objection to AI agents: can I trust them? This says yes.

4. **"80% time savings"** — Quantified efficiency gain. Concrete and compelling for marketing ops buyers.

## Social Proof / Credibility

- $35M Series B
- Strong accuracy and efficiency metrics
- Growing in B2B marketing automation space

## Brand Voice & Tone

- Aspirational but grounded (big vision + specific metrics)
- Agent-forward (the AI agent is the hero, not the marketer)
- Performance-oriented (accuracy and speed metrics prominent)
- Modern and bold

## Narrative Arc

1. **Problem:** Marketing teams are drowning in execution work — campaigns, emails, content, reporting
2. **Insight:** The bottleneck isn't strategy or creativity. It's the manual execution that sits between idea and market.
3. **Category:** Agentic marketing automation — AI agents that autonomously execute, not just automate
4. **Solution:** Gradial deploys AI agents that execute marketing workflows with 99.9% accuracy
5. **Outcome:** Marketing at the speed of thought — 80% time savings, teams focus on strategy

## What's Working (Initial PMM Assessment)

- **"Speed of Thought" is memorable** and taps into a real pain point — execution bottlenecks
- **Dual metric (99.9% accuracy + 80% time savings)** addresses both quality and efficiency concerns
- **"Agentic" positioning** is timely and differentiated from legacy "automation"

## What's Missing / Gaps

- "Agentic" is becoming an overused term in AI — risk of blending into noise
- Less clear what specific marketing workflows the agents handle
- Limited brand recognition vs. established marketing automation players
- The accuracy claim (99.9%) needs more context — 99.9% of what, exactly?
