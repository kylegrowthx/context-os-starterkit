# V7 (Actively-Inspired) — Improvement Notes

Comparing Version 7 against Actively.ai's homepage and messaging analysis to identify what's missing, what's weak, and what to sharpen.

---

## 1. What Actively.ai's homepage does well that V7 doesn't capture

**Category invention.** Actively coins "GTM Superintelligence" — a term that doesn't exist anywhere else. It creates a tier above "AI agents" and "sales automation." V7 uses "Growth Intelligence," which is descriptive but generic. It names a capability, not a category. Nobody's going to say "we need Growth Intelligence" the way they might say "we need GTM Superintelligence." The category-creation move is Actively's strongest play and V7 doesn't attempt it.

**The "System of" framing layer.** Actively adds "System of Intelligence for Enterprise-Scale GTM" between the hero and the product sections. This borrows enterprise infrastructure vocabulary (System of Record → System of Engagement → System of Intelligence) and positions the product as the next logical layer in the stack. V7 has no equivalent framing layer. It jumps from the hero tagline straight into the process section.

**One-line process steps.** Actively's Deep Reasoning section uses single-sentence descriptions for each step:
- "Aggregates data across your CRM, past interactions, signals, and the web"
- "Builds a detailed profile of every account — needs, decision makers, likely buying criteria, and more"

V7's process steps are 2-3 sentences each. They explain more but land with less force. The Actively approach treats each step as a punch, not a paragraph.

**Customer quotes that lead with ROI, not features.** Actively uses two customer quotes and both open with ROI:
- "The ROI of Actively AI for outbound-driven revenue organizations is undeniable."
- "The ROI we've seen makes Actively AI a no-brainer for any GTM org chasing ambitious revenue goals."

V7 has zero customer voice. The "numbers" section shows metrics but they float without attribution or narrative context. Numbers without a customer saying "this happened to us" are claims, not proof.

**The "Actions" output section.** Actively has a dedicated section called "Tells your team what to do and say next" — it shows what the system actually produces for humans. V7 never clearly says "here's what you get." The 4-step process describes how GrowthX thinks but doesn't show what it hands to the client.

**Enterprise trust layer.** SOC2 Type II, GDPR, CCPA badges. V7 has no trust signals — no security badges, no client logos, no compliance markers.

**Restrained CTA.** "Try Actively and see for yourself" — low-pressure, implies the product speaks for itself. V7's "Get an audit" is directionally fine but wraps it in "We'll show you what GrowthX intelligence looks like built around your business," which is wordy and generic.

## 2. Weak spots in V7

**The hero doesn't invent a category.**
> "Growth Intelligence for Website-Driven Companies."

"Growth Intelligence" sounds like a dashboard feature, not a category. "Website-Driven Companies" is a descriptor, not an aspiration. Compare to "GTM Superintelligence for the Enterprise" — that line makes buyers self-select upward. V7's hero makes buyers think "is that me?" instead of "I want that."

**The sub-hero tries to do too much.**
> "A custom growth model — built on your data, your market, and your competitive landscape. Not generic AI. Intelligence that compounds because it learns your business."

Three separate ideas in three sentences: (1) custom model, (2) anti-generic positioning, (3) compounding intelligence. Actively's sub-hero does one thing: explain the mechanism. Pick one idea and land it.

**The process steps are overwritten.**
> "**2. Understands** — Maps buyer intent across three audiences: the human buyer, the AI agent influencing the buyer, and the training bot shaping the AI agent. Identifies where you win, where you're invisible, and where competitors own the conversation."

The three-audience insight is V7's single most original idea — but it's buried as the second sentence in Step 2 of a process section. This deserves its own section, not a subordinate clause.

**"Per-website growth agents" is a feature dump.**
> "Each client gets: A custom growth model trained on their specific market... Dedicated AI agents for content production... Continuous intelligence... Human calibration from named strategists..."

Bullet lists of capabilities read like a feature comparison chart, not a story. Actively avoids bullet lists entirely. Every section is narrative.

**"The numbers" section lacks context.**
> "**2-4x** organic traffic growth within 12 months"
> "**80-90%** of content production handled by AI"

These numbers are disconnected from each other and from any customer story. Actively anchors every metric inside a customer quote. V7 presents numbers as standalone facts — they feel like marketing claims rather than evidence.

**"The result:" line is defensive.**
> "The result: growth that's specific to you, not generic 'best practices' applied at scale."

Defining yourself by what you're not. Actively does this once ("delivering results that generic AI simply can't match") then moves on. V7 returns to the "we're not generic" well three times across the page. Once is positioning. Three times is insecurity.

**The CTA is vague.**
> "See your custom growth model. Get an audit. We'll show you what GrowthX intelligence looks like built around your business."

"See your custom growth model" promises something abstract. "Get an audit" is concrete but buried. "We'll show you what GrowthX intelligence looks like built around your business" is 14 words saying nothing specific.

## 3. Structural improvements

**Add a system-framing layer after the hero.** Between the hero and "How GrowthX thinks," add a one-line positioning statement that anchors the product in a system vocabulary. Something equivalent to Actively's "System of Intelligence for Enterprise-Scale GTM." For GrowthX, this could be "The growth operating system for B2B" or similar — a phrase that frames the product as infrastructure, not a service.

**Promote the three-audience insight to its own section.** The human buyer / AI agent / training bot framework is V7's most differentiated idea. It's currently a clause inside Step 2. Give it a full section with a header like "Three audiences. One strategy." or "Who your content actually reaches." This is the equivalent of Actively's "Custom Model — Trained to think like your best sellers" — a section that exists solely to land one differentiator.

**Compress the process steps to one line each.** Cut each step from 2-3 sentences to one. The reader should be able to scan all four steps in 5 seconds. Current version requires 30+ seconds of reading. Model:
- Current: "Ingests your site architecture, content library, search performance, AI visibility, and competitive landscape. Builds a complete growth graph."
- Target: "Ingests your site, search performance, AI visibility, and competitive landscape into a complete growth graph."

**Add a "What you get" output section.** After the process section, add a section equivalent to Actively's "Actions — Tells your team what to do and say next." For GrowthX: what does the client actually receive? Strategy document? Monthly content? Performance dashboard? Weekly recommendations? Name the outputs.

**Replace "The numbers" with proof stories.** Replace the standalone metrics section with 1-2 customer proof blocks. Each block: a customer quote (leading with ROI or outcome) plus the relevant metric anchored to that customer's result. If real quotes aren't available yet, restructure the metrics as "Client X saw 2-4x traffic growth" rather than "2-4x organic traffic growth" floating in space.

**Add a trust layer before the CTA.** Client logos, any compliance/security markers, or a "Trusted by" line. Even just logos. V7 currently goes from metrics straight to CTA with no trust buffer.

**Sharpen the CTA to one action, one sentence.** "Get your growth audit" is enough. Drop the explanatory sentence entirely. Actively's "Try Actively and see for yourself" is 7 words.

## 4. Language/tone improvements

**Stop hedging with "not."** V7 says "Not generic AI" (sub-hero), "not generic 'best practices'" (per-website section), and "GrowthX doesn't run one algorithm for everyone" (per-website opener). Three negations across one page. Replace with positive claims. Instead of "Not generic AI," say what it IS. Actively does this: "The only solution that builds a custom model tailored to your business."

**Cut circular phrasing.**
> "Growth accelerates because the intelligence accelerates."

This says the same word twice. Replace with a concrete mechanism: what actually accelerates? Content output? Keyword coverage? Pipeline?

**Match Actively's declaration energy.** V7 explains. Actively declares. Compare:
- V7: "Most growth tools run the same playbook for every company. GrowthX builds a model around yours."
- Actively: "Actively AI is the only solution that builds a custom model tailored to your business, products, and GTM strategy."

The V7 version starts by talking about other companies. The Actively version starts by talking about itself. Lead with the claim, not the contrast.

**Tighten the verbs.** V7 uses soft verbs: "gets," "feeds back," "builds on." Actively uses: "trains," "deploys," "synthesize," "convert." Upgrade to verbs that imply engineering precision, not organic process.

**Drop "the system learns."** Step 4 opens with "The system learns." This is generic AI-product language — every AI product claims to learn. Replace with what specifically improves: "Strategies sharpen against real performance data" or "Each quarter's model outperforms the last."

## 5. What's working and should be kept

**The 4-step process structure.** Researches → Understands → Optimizes → Compounds is a strong arc. The verb choices are good — they show progression from input to intelligence to action to improvement. This mirrors Actively's Researches → Understands → Reasons → Improves without copying it. Keep the structure; compress the descriptions.

**The three-audience framework.** "The human buyer, the AI agent influencing the buyer, and the training bot shaping the AI agent." This is the single most original idea in V7 and has no equivalent on Actively's page or most competitor pages. It deserves MORE prominence, not less.

**The custom model concept.** "A custom growth model — built on your data, your market, and your competitive landscape" is the right move. It mirrors Actively's strongest differentiator. The idea is solid; the execution needs sharpening.

**"Human calibration from named strategists."** This is a genuine differentiator — Actively is pure software, GrowthX has humans in the loop. The phrase "named strategists who understand the model and can override it" is specific and trustworthy. Keep and promote this.

**The "compounding" metaphor.** "Intelligence that compounds because it learns your business" is a strong concept — it borrows from finance vocabulary (compound interest) and applies it to growth. The metaphor works. It just needs to be deployed once, clearly, not repeated across multiple sections.

**CheckThat numbers.** "172 B2B categories tracked" and "5,800+ brands benchmarked" — these are specific, proprietary, and verifiable. They signal real infrastructure. Keep them, but anchor them in a proof section rather than a floating metrics list.

## 6. Top 5 highest-impact changes

### 1. Invent a category in the hero

**Current:** "Growth Intelligence for Website-Driven Companies."

**Problem:** Descriptive, not inventive. Doesn't create a new tier.

**Replace with something that coins a term.** Candidates:
- "Growth Superintelligence for B2B."
- "The Growth Intelligence Platform."
- "Compound Growth Intelligence."

The exact term needs workshopping, but the move is clear: name something that doesn't exist yet and own it. Actively's entire page authority flows from "GTM Superintelligence." V7 needs its own coined category.

### 2. Give the three-audience framework its own section

**Current location:** Buried in Step 2 of the process section:
> "Maps buyer intent across three audiences: the human buyer, the AI agent influencing the buyer, and the training bot shaping the AI agent."

**Move to:** A standalone section between the process and the proof, with a header and a 2-3 line explanation. This is V7's most differentiated insight and most people will miss it at its current depth.

**Draft:**
> **Three audiences. One growth model.**
>
> Your content doesn't just reach buyers anymore. It reaches the AI agents recommending solutions and the training bots shaping those agents. GrowthX is the only growth system that optimizes for all three simultaneously.

### 3. Compress process steps to one line each

**Current (Step 1):**
> "**1. Researches** — Ingests your site architecture, content library, search performance, AI visibility, and competitive landscape. Builds a complete growth graph."

**Replace with:**
> "**1. Researches** — Ingests your site, content, search performance, AI visibility, and competitive landscape into one growth graph."

Do this for all four steps. The section should scan in 10 seconds, not 30.

### 4. Add customer proof that leads with ROI

**Current:** The "numbers" section presents metrics without attribution or narrative.

**Replace with 1-2 proof blocks:**
> **[Client Name], [Role]**
> "Quote that leads with outcome or ROI metric."
> — Result: 2.8x organic traffic in 9 months / 85% of content produced by AI

If real quotes aren't available, restructure as: "Webflow saw X. Abnormal saw Y." Attributed results beat unattributed claims.

### 5. Add a "What you get" output section

**Currently missing.** V7 explains how GrowthX thinks but never says what it delivers.

**Add after the process section:**
> **What GrowthX delivers.**
>
> A custom growth model. Monthly strategy calibration. AI-produced content at scale. Performance scoring for every page. Competitive intelligence updated weekly. A named strategist who knows your model and can override it.

This is the equivalent of Actively's "Actions — Tells your team what to do and say next." The buyer needs to know what lands in their inbox.
