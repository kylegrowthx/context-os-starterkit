# Research Notes

## Research Method

Direct website scraping (Jina Reader API) and browser access were both blocked by network restrictions. Pivoted to web search-based research using parallel Agent subagents. All data sourced from search results, press releases, TechCrunch/Bloomberg coverage, funding announcements, and community discussions.

## Company Discovery Process

Searched for: AI startup funding rounds (Series A/B/C) announced between May 2025 and March 2026 in domains: sales AI, marketing AI, GTM platforms, website analytics, legal AI, customer research, brand research, vertical AI.

Started with 20+ candidates, narrowed to 11 based on:
- Domain relevance (sales, marketing, GTM, analytics, legal, vertical AI)
- Funding round significance (Series A minimum $6M)
- Messaging clarity (enough public positioning data to analyze)
- Category representation (wanted coverage across all target domains)

## Companies Considered But Not Included

- Jasper AI (content AI — too narrow, also name confusion with old Conversion.ai)
- Writer (enterprise content AI — less GTM-focused)
- Gong (revenue intelligence — earlier stage funding, not Series A/B/C in window)
- Various smaller AI SDR tools (too early, limited public messaging)
- Numerous vertical AI companies outside target domains

## Key Research Themes Discovered

1. **Category creation is the dominant strategy** — 8 of 11 companies attempting it
2. **"Agent" vs. "Platform" positioning split** — market bifurcating
3. **Trust architecture is critical** — specific metrics + third-party validation work best
4. **Headline type matters** — outcome > vision > capability
5. **Naming correlates with memorability** — short, ownable names win
6. **Valuation correlates with category ownership** — top 3 all created categories

## Data Gaps

- Pricing data limited for most companies
- Customer case studies not always publicly available
- Some companies (Gradial, Conversion.ai) have limited press coverage
- Warmly's smaller raise means less public information
