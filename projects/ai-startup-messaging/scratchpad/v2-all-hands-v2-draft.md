Here's what's happening right now.

Buyers don't browse ten blue links anymore. They ask an AI for the answer. ChatGPT went from 300 million to 800 million weekly active users in under a year. AI Overviews now show up in 30%+ of Google queries. 78% of enterprises are already using AI in at least one function.

If you're not in the AI answer, you don't exist.

That's not a tagline. That's the reality for every B2B company on the planet right now. Three audiences matter for every business: the buyer trying to purchase your product, the AI agent influencing that buyer's decision, and the training bot shaping the AI agent's knowledge. Miss any one of those three and you have a gap that gets wider every month. Your website used to be important. Now it's the primary interface between your company and every one of those audiences.

Content is the atomic unit of marketing. It always has been. But the job of creating it, managing it, and making it work has completely changed. The volume is staggering. The decay is faster than anyone expected. And 94% of CMOs are planning to increase their AI visibility investments this year because they can see what's coming.

So here's the opportunity. And I want you to feel the size of this.

The outsourced content marketing services market is $107 billion, growing 13%+ a year. The SEO market — which is going through its biggest shift since PageRank — is $75 billion. The AI agents market is projected at $52 billion by 2030 with a 46% compound annual growth rate. That's a combined market of over $230 billion going through a once-in-a-generation disruption. All at the same time.

And this isn't theoretical. Harvey AI built a $3 billion company on the same model we're building — service-as-software — in legal. Mercor hit $2 billion in recruiting on the same playbook. Harvey crossed $100 million ARR in roughly three years. The model works. It's proven. It's happening right now across industries. And nobody has claimed this market in B2B growth yet.

Here's the challenge. Companies trying to solve this today have two options, and both are broken.

Option one: hire an agency. They bring expertise, but they're expensive, they're slow, and they're hard to scale. 60% of marketing leaders decreased agency spend because of AI last year — not because the work went away, but because the model stopped making sense.

Option two: buy tools. They're scalable and affordable, but your team does all the work. Strategy, execution, troubleshooting, iteration. The tool doesn't do the job. It creates a different job. And you still need the same number of people — they're just toggling between more dashboards.

Tools don't build houses. People do.

Neither option delivers outcomes. Agencies deliver effort. Tools deliver features. Neither delivers growth that builds on itself.

So here's the thing. We built a third way.

Service-as-software. AI handles the scale. Experts ensure the quality and strategic alignment. The system gets smarter with every output. That's what GrowthX is. The operating system for website-driven growth.

And here's why the business model is so powerful: we run at 70%+ gross margins. Traditional agencies sit at 30-40%. We're hitting software-like margins while delivering services-level outcomes. That's the direct result of having AI do 80-90% of production while humans handle the 10-20% that machines can't — strategy, editorial judgment, calibration.

Start with great, then make it efficient. Never the other way around.

Now let me walk you through what we actually built. Because this isn't four separate products sitting next to each other. This is a flywheel. Each part feeds the next.

**CheckThat** is the intelligence layer. The open AI visibility index. 172 categories. 5,800+ brands. 2.6 million+ AI responses analyzed. Any company can check their visibility score for free. They see how AI talks about them — and how AI talks about their competitors. Over $200 million in VC has gone to 24+ AEO startups in the last 18 months. The category is real and accelerating. And CheckThat is our top of funnel. A company discovers their AI visibility gap. That's a GrowthX conversation.

**ContentOS** is the execution layer. The all-batteries-included platform that makes the old agency model obsolete. Strategy to production to optimization — at scale. This is where AI handles the volume and humans handle the judgment. One system that manages hundreds of pages without hundreds of people. Nobody wants more content. They want visibility, traffic, conversions, revenue. That's what ContentOS delivers.

**Output.ai** is the workflow layer. Open-source framework for agentic workflows. This powers everything under the hood — content production, optimization, audits, monitoring. We're not building AI tools in a lab. We're running agentic workflows in production for dozens of clients every day. That feedback loop is something most framework companies will never have.

**ExpertLayer** is the quality layer. The marketplace that ensures every output meets the bar. You can't delegate to AI what you can't delegate to a human. The experts in this network are what make the difference between AI-generated content that reads like AI-generated content and work that actually builds trust with buyers.

Here's how the flywheel works. A company discovers CheckThat. They see their AI visibility score. They realize they have a problem. That's a ContentOS conversation. ContentOS runs the operation. Output.ai powers the workflows. ExpertLayer ensures the quality. And everything that happens — every client, every page, every workflow — makes CheckThat smarter. Which attracts the next company.

Each layer feeds the next. The whole thing accelerates.

So why do we win? Because we have four moats. And they all reinforce each other.

**Brand moat.** Trust compounds. When we do great work, customers tell everyone. They become our sales team. Word of mouth has driven nearly all our growth. That's not an accident. It's the direct result of being the best hire our customers ever made.

**Scale moat.** Unit economics improve over time. Each project teaches our AI something new. Each client makes the system smarter. The marginal cost of serving the next customer drops while quality stays high or improves.

**Embedding moat.** Switching costs increase over time. As our AI adapts to a customer's voice, context, and processes, leaving becomes expensive. They'd lose all that accumulated intelligence. They'd have to start over. That's a moat.

**Network moat.** Knowledge builds across clients. What we learn from one engagement improves outcomes for all engagements. Cross-client insights feed our AI. Our AI attracts better experts. Better experts produce better insights. The flywheel spins.

That's why when you're onboarding a customer, the goal isn't to have them sit on the platform and read five Google Docs a week. The goal is to build them a growth engine they can't live without. A Strategy Sprint — eight weeks, decision at Week 6, data-driven proof before they commit long-term. And once they see the system work, they don't leave. Because leaving means losing everything the system learned about their brand, their market, their competitors.

We're not selling a content shop. We're selling a compounding growth engine.

So why does this moment matter so much?

There's a 2-3 year window. Right now, AI is good enough to augment human expertise but not good enough to replace it. That's exactly when service-as-software companies build dominant positions — before the market commoditizes.

Look at the speed. ChatGPT's user base nearly tripled in a year. AI Overviews jumped from 6.5% to 30%+ of Google queries. 83% of B2B marketing decision-makers expect budgets to grow this year. $2.52 trillion in global AI spending in 2026.

The companies that build dominant positions in the next 2-3 years will own their categories. The ones that wait will spend the next decade trying to catch up. That window is open right now. And we're running through it.

Here's what I want everyone in this room to take away.

Every role at GrowthX connects to this. If you're editing content, you're building the execution layer. If you're writing code, you're building the infrastructure. If you're closing deals, you're expanding the ecosystem. If you're managing a client, you're feeding the flywheel.

We're not three startups. We're one company with three ways to win — CheckThat, ContentOS, and Output.ai. The operating system gets smarter with every client, every page, every workflow.

We're early in a market that's accelerating faster than anyone predicted. The opportunity is massive. The model is proven. The window is open. And everyone in this room is building a piece of it.

Let's go.
