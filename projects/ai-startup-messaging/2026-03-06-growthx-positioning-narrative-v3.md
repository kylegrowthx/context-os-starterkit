## Goal

Reposition GrowthX and simplify our positioning and messaging so it's a genuine no-brainer for buyers.

We don't want to be seen as an agency, a SaaS tool, or a hard-to-use platform. We want this to feel like we understand their pains — because Marcel lived through it for 15 years as a CMO building growth for some of the fastest companies in the world.

**This homepage is successful if, within seconds, our audience understands:**

1. **Why we exist**
2. **The pain we're solving**
3. **Why it matters**

**The tone:**

This shouldn't feel like jargon, buzzwords, or clever-sounding filler. But it also can't come across as unsophisticated. It needs to hit you — *these people really know their shit, and their experience runs deep.*

Thoughtful. Direct. Built from lived experience. Not trying to sound smart. Actually smart.

## Section 2: The Problem

**Section headline:**

> Your website is your most valuable growth asset.
> Most companies manage it with a spreadsheet and a prayer.

**Three problem cards:**

### Blind Portfolio

You have hundreds of pages. Some capture 80% of their potential. Some capture 5%. Your agency can't tell you which is which. By the time they review last quarter's numbers, the traffic is already gone.

### Disconnected Stack

GA4. Search Console. Ahrefs. Clearscope. A CMS. A project tracker. An agency. A freelancer. You're stitching together a growth strategy from parts that were never designed to work together.

### Quarterly Reset

New brief. New onboarding call. New strategist who needs to "get up to speed." Nothing your agency learned last quarter carries into this one. Meanwhile, AI search changed everything and they haven't noticed.

**Design note:** Three cards, side by side. Short, punchy, personal. The buyer should recognize their own situation in at least two of three.

**One-liner under each column:**

**Design note:** Horizontal grid with icons, similar to the Reevo "Find / Connect / Sell / Manage" pattern. Each column header is a single word. Sub-items are scannable labels, not sentences. The one-liners sit below the grid as optional supporting text.

## Section 4: What You Get (The Four Pillars)

**Section headline:**

> Manage every page. Find every opportunity. Create at scale. See everything.

### Page Portfolio

Every page on your site — crawled, classified, and scored against your custom taxonomy. Connected to GA4, Search Console, and AI visibility data. You see what's performing, what's declining, and what's missing.

Every page scored against its potential. How much traffic could it capture? How much is it actually capturing?

### Opportunities

Research agents surface gaps you'd never find manually. Persona topics, competitor gaps, article gaps, top player discovery. Each opportunity lands with keyword data, SERP analysis, AI visibility potential, relevance scoring, and recommended content type.

### Creation Pipeline

Accepted opportunity flows through Brief → Outline → Article → Review → Published. Each step generated by a dedicated agent. Reviewed by a human. Every workspace has its own writing agent calibrated to your brand voice. It improves with feedback. 18+ content types.

### Portfolio Snapshots

High-level reports covering portfolio health, competitive landscape, content performance, and next steps. Portfolio Audit, Competitor Landscape, Opportunities Map, Performance, Keyword Movements, Search Visibility, Quality, Competitive Shifts, ROI Summary. Full audit on day one. Any snapshot on demand. Plus a weekly digest: what our operators spotted, acted on, and what needs your attention.

**Design note:** Four cards or expandable sections. Each should feel like a product feature page, not a paragraph. Lead with the outcome, then the mechanism. Consider showing product UI screenshots for each pillar.

## Section 9: Three Audiences

**Section headline:**

> Your brand now has three audiences. Most companies optimize for one.

**The Buyer** — The human researching, evaluating, and choosing your product. They still search Google. They still read content. But they also ask ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Claude before they visit your site.

**The AI Agent** — The model answering the buyer's question. It decides which brands to recommend, which sources to cite, which answers to surface. If the AI agent doesn't know you, the buyer never hears your name.

**The Training Bot** — The crawler shaping what the AI agent knows tomorrow. It reads your website, indexes your content, and builds the knowledge base the agent draws from. Your content is the raw material of AI perception.

GrowthX optimizes for all three. Search rankings, AI recommendations, and the training data that feeds them.

**Design note:** Three vertical cards or a diagram showing the relationship. This section educates — it gives the buyer a frame they didn't have before. It should feel like insight, not a sales pitch.

## Open Questions

- **Section order:** Should "Three Audiences" come earlier (after the problem, before how it works) to set the frame? Or does it work better as a late-page insight that deepens conviction?
- **Section 4 vs Section 3:** The four pillars (Section 4) overlap with the process grid (Section 3). Could combine into one section or make Section 3 the overview and Section 4 an expandable deep-dive.
- **Pricing:** Not on the homepage yet. Strategy says $10k/mo starting. When it appears, it should sit near Section 6 (What It Replaces) to anchor against the $30-90k alternative.
- **Video / demo:** Should the hero include a product walkthrough or keep it static UI?
- **ContentOS naming:** The homepage currently says "GrowthX." The strategy doc names the product "ContentOS." Decide whether ContentOS appears on the homepage or stays internal.
