##### Interventional Pain Management

Dr. Aditya Patel
================

Dr. Patel’s patients appreciate his thoroughness, the time he spends with them, and his ability to provide them with the information they need when they need it.

Dr. Patel earned his bachelor’s degree in physiology and neuroscience from the University of California, San Diego, followed by his medical degree with high honors from St. George’s University School of Medicine in St. George’s, Grenada.

Dr. Patel completed his residency in anesthesiology at the Cooperman Barnabas Medical Center in Livingston, New Jersey, where he was chief resident during his final year. During his residency, Dr. Patel developed a particular interest in interventional pain medicine and regional anesthesia. He had the opportunity to pursue his interests when he attended the Icahn School of Medicine at Mount Sinai, New York City, for his postgraduate fellowship in interventional pain management.

After finishing his fellowship training, Dr. Patel became an anesthesiologist at New York City’s Mount Sinai Brooklyn Hospital before moving to private practice. Dr. Patel’s passions include office-based diagnostics and therapeutic interventions with multidisciplinary physical medicine treatments to achieve optimal results for his patients.

![Image 3: Dr. Aditya Patel](https://s43932.pcdn.co/wp-content/uploads/sites/224/2024/02/Dr.-Aditya-Patel-photo.jpg.optimal.jpg)

### The Importance of Continuing Education

Dr. Patel is continually seeking new treatment options for his patients, such as the use of biologics, like platelet-rich plasma (PRP), to reduce pain and restore function. He has also undergone specialized training in the most advanced, up-to-date, and minimally invasive treatments. Dr. Patel also has expertise in the field of Neuromodulation, specifically Spinal Cord Stimulation (SCS) and Peripheral Nerve Stimulation (PNS) to treat a multitude of chronic pain conditions.
