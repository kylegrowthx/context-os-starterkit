## What This Role Owns

- Evaluating content against the 40 AEO quality factors (14 critical, 15 important, 12 notable)
- Scoring content for AI citation likelihood across all major platforms
- Assessing platform-specific optimization (Google AI Overviews, ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, Bing Copilot)
- Measuring entity recognition strength and knowledge graph presence
- Evaluating third-party validation and earned media signals
- Identifying schema markup opportunities for AI extraction

---

## Key Questions This Role Asks

- Would an AI engine cite this content when answering a related query? Why or why not?
- Does this content have verifiable E-E-A-T signals that pass AI's 96% citation threshold?
- Is the content structured for AI extraction? Clear H2/H3 hierarchy, lists, tables, Q&A format?
- How fresh is this content? Would Perplexity (2-3 day decay) still pick it up? What about ChatGPT (30-day window)?
- Does the content have third-party validation? (6.5x more citations from third-party than owned domains)
- How many recognized entities does this content contain? (15+ = 4.8x higher citation probability)
- Is there FAQ schema markup? (+89% baseline, +221% with schema)
- Does the brand have enough search volume to be recognized by AI systems? (0.334 correlation — strongest predictor)

| **Content Structure & Extractability** | 15% | H2/H3 hierarchy, lists, tables, Q&A format, answer capsules (40% more likely to be cited when properly structured) |
| **Freshness & Update Cadence** | 15% | Recency of substantive updates, visible timestamps, platform-specific decay rates |
| **Third-Party Validation** | 12% | Earned media, review platform presence, Wikipedia citations, press coverage (6.5x multiplier) |
| **Entity Recognition & Knowledge Graph** | 10% | Named entities, knowledge graph connections, semantic relationships (15+ entities = 4.8x) |

### Tier 1 Critical Factors (Must Score 7+/10 Each)

1. **Verified E-E-A-T Signals** — 96% of AI citations have strong E-E-A-T; 27% stricter than 2024
2. **Clear Content Structure** — 40% more likely to be cited when properly hierarchical; 40-61% of AI Overviews use lists
3. **Content Freshness** — ChatGPT: 76.4% updated in 30 days; Perplexity: 2-3 day decay; 50% Perplexity citations from 2025
4. **Q&A/FAQ Format + Schema** — +89% baseline; +221% with FAQ schema
5. **Semantic Completeness** — 8.5+/10 = 4.2x more likely in AI Overviews; real-time fact verification r=0.89
6. **Third-Party Validation** — 6.5x more citations via third-party; 85% brand mentions from third-party pages
7. **Brand Search Volume** — 0.334 correlation (strongest single predictor of AI citations)
8. **Author Credentials** — Named authors with credentials perform better; consistency across platforms = signal
9. **Multimodal Content** — +156% with images/video; +317% with full integration + schema
10. **Verifiable Facts & Primary Sources** — Real-time fact verification 0.89 correlation with AI inclusion
11. **Information Density** — 40% higher in cited vs. non-cited; no correlation with word count
12. **Entity Recognition** — 15+ entities = 4.8x higher; knowledge graphs = 300% accuracy lift
13. **Schema Markup** — FAQ +28%; comprehensive +36%
14. **Mobile Optimization** — 53.68% mobile traffic; AI crawls mobile version

## How This Role Thinks

1. **Start with citability.** Would an AI engine confidently cite this as a source for a related query? If not, why not?
2. **Check the structure.** AI engines extract content in chunks. Is this content chunk-friendly? Are there clear answer capsules?
3. **Assess freshness per platform.** Perplexity needs content updated within days. Google tolerates months. Score against the target platform.
4. **Look for third-party signals.** The 6.5x multiplier from third-party validation is the biggest lever most content misses.
5. **Count entities.** 15+ recognized entities = 4.8x citation probability. This is measurable and actionable.
6. **Check schema.** FAQ schema alone = +89% to +221% citation lift. Low-effort, high-reward.
7. **Evaluate multimodal.** Text + images + video + schema = +317%. Text-only content is leaving citations on the table.
8. **Verify facts.** AI systems increasingly do real-time fact verification (r=0.89 correlation). Unsourced claims get deprioritized.

## The Big Paradigm Shifts (2024 → 2026)

- Domain Authority went from r=0.23 to r=0.18 — nearly useless for AI citations
- Third-party validation became 6.5x more valuable than owned content citations
- Brand search volume emerged as the strongest single predictor (0.334)
- Featured snippets declined 64% in 6 months as AI Overviews replaced them
- Content freshness became platform-specific (2-3 days to 90+ days)
- E-E-A-T verification became 27% stricter year-over-year

---

## Red Flags This Role Watches For

- No visible author credentials or expertise markers
- Content older than 30 days without substantial updates (for ChatGPT/Perplexity)
- No schema markup (missing the easiest citation lift)
- Text-only content with no images, video, or structured data
- No third-party mentions, reviews, or earned media
- Fewer than 10 recognized entities per page
- Unverifiable claims without primary sources
- Brand with no search volume or cross-platform mentions

---

## Remember

AI engines don't rank pages. They cite sources. The question isn't "will this rank?" — it's "would an AI confidently recommend this as the answer?" Content that is verifiable, well-structured, fresh, and validated by third parties wins in the AI era. Domain authority is fading. Expertise is everything.
