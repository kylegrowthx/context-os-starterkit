### 5. Topical Authority & Content Clustering
**Confidence Level**: Very High (Multiple studies with quantified results)

**Quantified Evidence**:
- Topical authority analysis of 250,000+ search results: topical authority strongest on-page ranking factor
- Pillar-cluster architecture results:
  - 40% increase in organic traffic vs. non-clustered strategies
  - 63% more keyword rankings within 90 days
  - 8-point average domain authority increase
  - B2B SaaS: median increase of 8.1 DA (Study of 50 sites)

**Structure Requirements**:
- Pillar page: 3,000-5,000 words, comprehensive topic overview
- Cluster pages: 8-15 subtopic pages, 1,000-2,500 words each
- Internal linking: Pillar links to all clusters, clusters link to pillar and related clusters
- Topic coherence: All content within cluster thematically related

**Why It Works**:
- Shows Google comprehensive topic coverage
- Signals genuine expertise in niche
- Creates semantic web of related concepts
- Improves content discoverability

### 8. Content Freshness (When Query Deserves It)
**Confidence Level**: High (Selective application, query-dependent)

**Quantified Evidence**:
- Content updated within 30 days: 3.2x more AI citations than content older than 90 days (ALM Corp)
- Query Deserves Freshness (QDF) algorithm detects trending topics through:
  - News sites actively writing about topic
  - Blog posts covering topic frequently
  - Search volume spiking
- 50% of Perplexity citations from 2025 alone; 44% from 2025 specifically (recent preference in AI)
- Websites maintaining 9+ new posts monthly: 20.1% organic traffic uplift (3.6x stronger than low frequency)

**When Freshness Matters**:
- Breaking news and current events
- Trending topics with spike in search volume
- Topics with regulatory/law changes
- Tech/software product updates
- Market trend shifts
- Annual statistics (e.g., "best tools 2026")

**When Freshness Doesn't Matter**:
- Evergreen how-to content (if method hasn't changed)
- Historical information
- Timeless advice
- Topics with stable information

**Important Caution**:
- Never update publish date without making substantial changes
- Google detects and ignores superficial "timestamp padding"

## TIER 2: IMPORTANT CONTENT QUALITY FACTORS

### 9. Core Web Vitals & Page Experience
**Confidence Level**: High (Confirmed ranking factors, quantified impact)

**Quantified Evidence**:
- Moz study: Page experience signals = 28% of ranking weight
- Pages at position 1: 10% more likely to pass Core Web Vitals than position 9
- When content quality is similar, better Core Web Vitals = higher rankings
- December 2025 tightening: Pages with INP > 200ms showing "ranking tax" in mobile search
- Performance-to-business impact: 0.1 second delay = 8.4% conversion rate increase (Deloitte/Google)

**The Three Core Web Vitals**:
1. **Largest Contentful Paint (LCP)**: ≤2.5 seconds
   - Measures main content loading speed
   - 67% of websites had fast LCP in June 2025
   - Replaced First Contentful Paint (FCP) as key metric

2. **Interaction to Next Paint (INP)**: ≤200 milliseconds
   - Measures page responsiveness to user actions
   - Replaced First Input Delay (FID) in 2024
   - Most critical tightening in Dec 2025 update
   - Measures full visual response time, not just delay

3. **Cumulative Layout Shift (CLS)**: ≤0.1
   - Measures visual stability
   - No elements unexpectedly shifting
   - Particularly important on mobile

**Why CWV Matters**:
- Direct user experience signal
- Mobile users expect instant response
- Poor experience = higher bounce rate = ranking demotion via Navboost

**How to Improve**:
- Image optimization and next-gen formats
- Code splitting and lazy loading
- Server-side rendering or edge caching
- CSS optimization
- Remove render-blocking resources

### 10. User Engagement & Navboost Signals
**Confidence Level**: High (Google leak + expert analysis)

**Quantified Evidence**:
- Navboost uses 13 months of user interaction data to refine rankings
- Formula: Relevance × Quality × Popularity = Rankings
- "Good clicks": User finds what they need
- "Bad clicks": Pogo-sticking (return to SERP immediately) signals dissatisfaction
- Longest clicks: Extended engagement on page signals high satisfaction

**Signals That Matter**:
- Click-through rate (CTR): Does SERP snippet attract clicks?
- Dwell time: How long does user stay?
- Bounce rate: Does user return to SERP?
- Pogo-sticking: Is user dissatisfied?
- Engagement depth: Scrolling, reading time, interaction

**Challenges**:
- Position bias: Higher rankings naturally get more clicks
- Google acknowledges this and uses de-biasing techniques across engines
- Low engagement doesn't always mean low quality (might mean high answer clarity)

**Optimization**:
- Write compelling meta descriptions to improve CTR
- Front-load value to reduce bounce
- Use internal links and related content to extend dwell
- Clear formatting for easy scanning

### 13. Internal Linking & Content Structure
**Confidence Level**: High (Expert consensus + technical SEO authority)

**Quantified Evidence**:
- Every major ranking boost in 2024-2025 came from reorganizing internal structure
- Best practice: 5-10 internal links per 2,000 words (one link per 200-300 words)
- TOC-linked headings boost indexation by 18% (Screaming Frog 2025)
- Contextual links in surrounding text more valuable than sidebar links
- Semantic web of connected ideas preferred over isolated articles

**Anchor Text Best Practices**:
- Exact-match anchors: Limited use (too much looks spammy)
- Semantic anchors: Natural variation that matches search intent
- Branded anchors: 30% of profile
- Partial match anchors: 20% of profile
- Exact match anchors: Small fraction (5-10%)

**Internal Linking Strategy**:
- Pillar-cluster model with clear hierarchy
- Link from high-authority pages to target pages
- Use descriptive, relevant anchor text
- Include table of contents with anchor links
- Connect related content laterally
- Avoid over-linking (excessive = devaluation)

## CORRELATION VS. CAUSATION NOTES

**Important Distinction**:
Several factors listed above correlate with rankings but may not directly cause rankings:

1. **Backlinks**: Correlate strongly but probably aren't "weighted" as directly as once thought. They seem to serve as authority validation combined with content signals.

2. **Domain Authority**: Correlates with rankings but can be overcome by topical authority. New sites can rank in niches they specialize in.

3. **Dwell Time**: Correlates with rankings via Navboost system but can't be directly gamed (it's user behavior).

4. **Content Length**: No direct correlation per Backlinko, but longer content often means more comprehensive (which does correlate).

**Primary Causation Factors**:
- Information relevance to query
- Information completeness
- User satisfaction (inferred from engagement)
- Website authority and trust
- Content recency (when applicable)
