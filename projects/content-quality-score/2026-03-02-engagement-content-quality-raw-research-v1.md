## ENGAGEMENT METRICS & CORE BEHAVIORS

### Time on Page & Scroll Depth
- **Optimal Combined Metrics**: 40-50% scroll depth + 2-3 minutes time on page = 70-90% positive audience sentiment
- **Knotch Study**: Analysis of 15,000+ content pages confirmed combined metric approach
- **Critical Insight**: Neither scroll depth NOR time on page alone predicts high engagement; combined effect is transformative
- **Benchmark**: 50%+ scroll depth with 2+ minutes indicates genuine visitor interest

### Bounce Rate Reduction
- **2-second load time**: 9% bounce rate
- **5-second load time**: 38% bounce rate
- **3-second threshold**: 53% of visitors abandon sites taking longer than 3 seconds
- **Conversion impact**: 1-second delay = 7% conversion reduction
- **Mobile-specific**: 53% abandon mobile if page load exceeds 3 seconds

### Engagement Reliability (2025 Addition)
- **New metric**: Google's 2025 Engagement Reliability measures how consistently interactive elements work across devices/conditions
- **Importance**: Adds consistency dimension to user satisfaction metrics

## READABILITY METRICS

### Flesch-Kincaid Readability Scores
- **Grade 6-7**: Highest social media shares + backlinks
- **General audience optimal**: 8th-grade reading level (age 13-14)
- **AI automation**: 2025 - AI writing assistants now optimize readability in real-time

### Line Spacing Impact
- **100% to 120% increase**: 20% improvement in reading accuracy + 30% reduction in eye strain during prolonged reading
- **Leading (line height)**: Critical for eye's ability to locate next line start

### Font Selection
- **Sans-serif (Arial, Verdana)**: Superior for digital + dyslexic readers vs serif/decorative
- **Character spacing**: Must be sufficiently small to form visual word units, sufficiently large to avoid blurring

## BEHAVIORAL PSYCHOLOGY

### Information Gap Theory (Loewenstein 1994)
- **Definition**: Curiosity = cognitive-induced deprivation from knowledge/understanding gap
- **Motivation**: Deprivation creates aversive psychological state; information-seeking resolves it

### Two Types of Curiosity
- **Diversive**: Interest in exploring unfamiliar topics + learning something new
- **Specific**: Desire to reduce uncertainty + resolve gaps in understanding

### Practical Applications
- **Education**: Teachers use information gaps for participation + critical thinking
- **Marketing**: Create intrigue + consumer engagement through knowledge gaps
- **Media**: Journalists leverage gaps to capture + maintain audience attention
