**Candidate takeaway:** You're not inheriting a broken marketing function. You're building one from scratch — with better raw materials than most VPs ever get.

Content:

- What exists: 130+ handbook docs, ROI frameworks, CheckThat data (172 categories, 5,800+ brands, 2.6M+ AI responses), proof points (IBM $17M, Augment Code)
- What doesn't exist: inbound pipeline, CheckThat GTM, marketing team, systematic demand generation

### 6. Challenges

**Context:** GrowthX grew 9x in 2025 but growth exposed structural gaps. Board docs surface recurring themes: retention is unproven at scale (first real renewal season in 2026, lost $2.7M Surge account), delivery doesn't scale linearly (diminishing returns from headcount), pipeline is 100% word-of-mouth with no inbound system, competitors are raising large rounds and getting aggressive, and the company doesn't show up in AI answers for its own category.
**Candidate takeaway:** These aren't hidden problems — leadership talks about them openly at the board level. The VP of Marketing directly owns or influences most of them.

Content:
- Retention: first real renewal season, Q1 projected 70-80%, lost Surge ($2.7M), expansion muscle is weak, engagement management team is green
- Pipeline: 100% WoM/inbound to date, pipeline generation slowed in Q4, only 1 AE until recently, booking 4-6 weeks out — demand constraint approaching
- Market positioning: 163+ AEO tools, competitors raising $19-58M (Profound, Scrunch, Peec), GrowthX doesn't show up in AI answers for its own category, messaging too technical for non-AI-native buyers

### 10. Assignment

**What:** A focused exercise to see how you think about positioning and narrative.
**Context:** GrowthX's current messaging is too technical for non-AI-native buyers. Candidates who've talked to us have flagged AI buzzword fatigue. The challenge: simplify the story without dumbing it down. One candidate suggested "portfolio manager for your website" — that's the level of clarity we're looking for.
**Candidate takeaway:** This isn't a test with a right answer. It's a window into how you think about positioning, simplification, and buyer empathy.

Content:
- The problem: current messaging over-indexes on AI/technical language, buyers glaze over
- The deliverable: a repositioned narrative for GrowthX services (1-2 pages) — who we are, what we do, why it matters, in language a non-technical VP of Marketing would forward to their CEO
- Evaluation criteria: clarity of thinking, buyer empathy, narrative simplification, strategic grounding
