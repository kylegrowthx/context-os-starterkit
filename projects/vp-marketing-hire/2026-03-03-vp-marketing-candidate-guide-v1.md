## What GrowthX actually is

GrowthX is a B2B content marketing company that runs two engines: a services business (the revenue engine) and a software product called CheckThat (the strategic bet).

**The services business** delivers AI-powered content marketing to B2B tech companies. We don't publish articles. We build compounding growth engines on clients' websites — content that ranks in organic search, gets cited by AI models (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, Claude), and drives brand search. One investment, three return channels, each amplifying the others.

**CheckThat** is an AI visibility index — think "surveys for AI engines" the way brands survey consumers. It measures how AI models perceive, recommend, and rank brands across four scores: Presence (are you showing up?), Reputation (what does the world think?), Perception (what story does AI tell?), and Influence (how much can you change it?). Freemium product designed to become the industry standard for AI visibility measurement. Already tracking 172 B2B software categories, 5,800+ brands, and 2.6M+ AI responses.

The thesis connecting both: *If you're not in the AI answer, you don't exist.* AI is eating the middle of the marketing funnel. 89% of B2B buyers use AI for research. AI referral traffic is growing 527% year-over-year. The companies that show up in AI-generated answers win. The companies that don't become invisible.

## The market thesis

GrowthX sits at the intersection of three converging trends:

1. **AI is restructuring how buyers find information.** Search is no longer just Google. It's ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, Claude. Buyers ask AI for recommendations before they ever visit a website. Content cited by AI models converts at 5x the rate of organic search traffic (14.2% vs. 2.8%).

2. **The services market is massive and underserved.** Content marketing services is a $100B+ market. Content marketing software is a $5B market. Most companies need someone to do the work, not another tool to learn. GrowthX leads with services to build trust and data, then wraps technology around the relationship.

3. **Human-AI collaboration is the real moat.** Fully AI-generated content averages position 16.8 in search vs. position 5.2 for human-edited content. We start with great people, make them incredibly productive with AI workflows (80-90% of the heavy lifting), and use humans for the strategic, creative, and quality work AI can't do yet. Then we encode that expertise into software.

**Four moats we're building:** Brand (thought leadership, CheckThat as category creator), Scale (AI workflows that compound), Embedding (clients depend on our platform and expertise), Network (data from every engagement makes the system smarter for everyone).

## How the business works

### The customer journey

**Sales → Strategy Sprint → Growth Execution**

**Sales** is primarily word-of-mouth. ~33% win rate, 6-week sales cycle. We qualify for $200K+/year B2B tech engagements. Executive sponsorship required. The pipeline is relationship-driven, not inbound-heavy. That's both a strength (high trust, organic advocacy) and a constraint (hard to scale). Building inbound is one of the biggest opportunities for this role.

**Strategy Sprint** is an 8-week prove-it-or-leave-it engagement. New customers start here. Weeks 1-4: build the content strategy, calibrate voice, start publishing. Week 5-6: first performance data. Week 6 is the circuit breaker — an honest, data-driven conversation about whether to continue. Weeks 7-8: transition to Growth Execution if both sides say yes.

**Growth Execution** is the long-term engagement. Compounding happens here. Monthly cohorts of content stack on previous cohorts. Cost per acquisition drops every month while paid channels move in the opposite direction.

### The delivery model

Delivery runs in **pods**: one Engagement Manager (EM) leading the client relationship, editors producing content, and shared specialists (Directors, Forward-Deployed Engineers, SEO specialists, Design/Dev) pulled in as needed.

**The EM is the most important role in delivery.** They own everything — strategy, communication, outcomes, satisfaction. The goal: customers view their EM as the best hire they've ever made. EMs manage a book of business and are accountable for Sprint conversion, renewals, and expansion.

**Capacity math:** 4 FTEs per 1 new kickoff/week. ~2 month ramp time for new hires. Selling faster than we can deliver is worse than selling slower.

### Revenue model and financial posture

Services is the core revenue today. The product ecosystem is being built to create a technology moat and eventually become a significant revenue stream.

GrowthX operates with financial discipline. The 0.33 burn multiple isn't just a metric — it's a philosophy. Grow profitably. Stay in control. This isn't a VC-funded blitz-scale company burning cash on growth. The VP of Marketing needs to build a capital-efficient growth engine that produces demonstrable returns on every dollar invested.

## The ROI story — how we prove value

This is critical for a VP of Marketing to internalize. It's the same story you'll tell externally about GrowthX and the same framework clients use to justify their investment.

### The compounding thesis

**Organic is compounding. Paid is diminishing.** Over any 12+ month horizon, organic wins on total ROI. Every month adds a new content cohort stacking on all previous cohorts. The cost per visit drops every month. Paid channels do the opposite — saturating audiences, rising CPCs, declining conversion rates.

The analogy we use: organic is building a house (equity accumulates). Paid is renting (zero residual value when you stop paying).

### Four components of the ROI model

1. **Refresh** — Improving existing content. Fastest path to results. Like renovating rooms in a house you already own.
2. **New content** — Cohort maturation. Zero traffic month one, 25% of peak month two, 50% month three, peak by month four. The compounding comes from stacking cohorts.
3. **AEO multiplier** — The same content created for organic search is cited by AI models. AI referral visitors convert at 5x organic (14.2% vs. 2.8%). This turns a "good investment" into a "no-brainer."
4. **Brand lift** — Brand search is the highest-converting traffic at every company. Every touchpoint (search or AI) has a probability of generating a brand search later. Brand search volume predicts AI visibility (0.334 correlation). Self-reinforcing loop.

### Four measurement tiers

We use Amazon's "working backwards" approach — controllable inputs, not outputs:

- **Tier 1: Activity** — How many did we do? Articles published, pages refreshed, newsletters sent.
- **Tier 2: Reach** — Search (impressions → clicks) + AI (citations → referral visits).
- **Tier 3: Engagement** — Time on page, bounce rate, scroll depth. Is the content delivering on its promise?
- **Tier 4: Conversion** — Deeper into site, micro-offer signup, product signup. AI referral conversion tracked separately.

We focus on Tier 1-2 early, shift to Tier 3-4 as engagements mature.

## Culture and how we work

### Seven values

1. **Communicate with clarity** — Be direct. Say the hard thing kindly but clearly.
2. **Relentless excellence** — Raise the bar. Don't accept "good enough."
3. **Never stop learning** — Curiosity is a competitive advantage.
4. **Move fast, stay steady** — Speed without panic. Urgency with composure.
5. **Lead with empathy** — Care about people. Understand before judging.
6. **Own the outcome** — Don't pass the buck. Take responsibility for results.
7. **Master the process first** — Understand how things work before trying to improve them.

### Async-first, documentation-heavy

GrowthX is fully remote and international. Async communication is the default. Slack is primary. Notion is the single source of truth. The handbook you're reading is the shared brain.

The operating rhythm: weekly L10 meetings (EOS-style), CEO weekly update (written, 500-800 words), monthly business review, quarterly planning with Rocks/OKRs. DACI framework for decisions (Driver, Approver, Contributors, Informed). Most decisions are two-way doors — move fast.

### The system mindset

This is the cultural throughline that matters most: **every week serving the same client should get easier.** We're not a content factory where humans repeat manual work. We're building a system. Every edit feeds back into the system. Every failure mode gets fixed upstream. If you're doing the same fix repeatedly, that's a pipeline problem, not a content problem. Escalate it.

### The writing standard

GrowthX has strong opinions on writing. This matters for a VP of Marketing more than anyone else in the company.

**The voice:** Write like a smart friend explaining something important over coffee. Direct, clear, real.

**Style anchors:** Paul Graham (conversational precision), Naval Ravikant (compressed wisdom), Derek Sivers (pithy contrarian), Morgan Housel (storytelling clarity), Hemingway (raw simplicity).

**Anti-patterns:** AI slop (repetitive, fluffy, jargon-heavy), should-how fallacy (telling people what to do without showing how), burying the lead, overly formatted content. The BLUF principle (Bottom Line Up Front) is core — lead with the point, then decompose.

**Quality checks:** First-sentence test (is the point in sentence one?), read-aloud test (would you say this to a friend?), cut test (can you remove 20%?), Graham test (would Paul Graham write it this way?).

If you write in corporate-speak, it won't survive here.

## What this role needs to do

### The "practice what we preach" imperative

This is the single most important thing. GrowthX tells clients to build compounding content engines. The VP of Marketing's first job is to do exactly that for GrowthX itself.

If GrowthX can't do for itself what it does for clients, the credibility gap will erode the brand. Conversely, if the GrowthX website, content, and AI visibility are best-in-class, every client conversation starts with proof.

### Six specific mandates

**1. Build the GrowthX brand as the authority on AI-era content marketing.**

The market is shifting from SEO to AEO. GrowthX is positioned to own this narrative. CheckThat gives us proprietary data nobody else has. The handbook gives us depth of thinking nobody else publishes. This is a thought leadership goldmine waiting to be externalized.

**2. Drive CheckThat adoption through category creation.**

CheckThat isn't competing with 163 AEO dashboards. It's creating a new category: the open AI visibility index. Category creation requires defining the problem before pitching the solution. "If you're not in the AI answer, you don't exist" is the framing. The goal is making CheckThat the standard way companies measure AI visibility.

**3. Build a real inbound pipeline.**

Sales today is mostly word-of-mouth. That's a strength (high trust) and a constraint (hard to scale). The VP of Marketing needs to build the content engine, the demand generation system, and the brand presence that creates inbound qualified leads at scale — without killing the word-of-mouth channel that already works.

**4. Own the AEO narrative.**

GrowthX has the research, the data, and the framework (Presence, Reputation, Perception, Influence) to define how the industry thinks about AI visibility. This is a category-creation moment. The company that defines the vocabulary wins the category.

**5. Build LinkedIn and thought leadership at scale.**

The writing style guide is mapped. The LinkedIn engagement research is deep — hook psychology, post structures, algorithm dynamics. Marcel's personal brand is a strategic asset. The content machine for thought leadership needs a leader to execute it consistently.

**6. Connect marketing to the product flywheel.**

CheckThat generates data. ContentOS operationalizes it. Marketing should amplify the insights from both — turning proprietary data into content that attracts prospects, educates the market, and drives product adoption. The flywheel only works if marketing connects the pieces.

## Key frameworks to know

These frameworks appear throughout the handbook and in client conversations:

- **Building vs. renting** — Organic is equity. Paid is rent. The analogy used in every sales conversation.
- **The compounding curve** — Backloaded returns. Months 1-3 are planting. Months 6-12+ are harvest.
- **Three return channels** — Every piece of content earns in organic search, AI citations, and brand search simultaneously.
- **Four measurement tiers** — Activity → Reach → Engagement → Conversion.
- **Quality (Q\*) and Popularity (P\*)** — Google's two master ranking signals confirmed under oath.
- **The system mindset** — Every week gets easier. Fix upstream, not downstream.
- **Human-AI three roles** — Process Architects (systematize), Input Calibrators (extract strategy), Production Refiners (quality control).
- **The four-layer SEO stack** — Technical → Content → Authority → Visibility. Fix from the foundation up.
- **Six AEO prompt categories** — Comparison, Alternatives, Best-of, Reviews, Pricing, Features. How buyers use AI during evaluation.
- **Category design** — Category kings capture 76% of category economics. CheckThat is category design in action.
- **Demand creation vs. demand capture** — 95% of the market isn't in-market. Brand building creates future demand. Don't fall into the lead treadmill.
- **Max MRR** — Revenue ceiling = New MRR / Cancellation Rate. Small churn improvements compound dramatically.
