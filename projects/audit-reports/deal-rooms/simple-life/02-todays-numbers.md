## The baseline

Simple's branded search moat is strong. The homepage alone drives $39K in traffic value. But the content engine is producing at a fraction of its potential.

Ruled.me, a single-author keto site with comparable domain authority, generates 52,700 monthly visits from content pages. Diet Doctor generates 130,800. Simple's blog is at 5,800.

### What the top content pages look like today

| # | Page | Traffic | % of Total |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Homepage | 20,946 | 72% |
| 2 | Keto diet meal plan for beginners | 789 | 2.7% |
| 3 | Dry fasting | 466 | 1.6% |
| 4 | How much weight can you lose with IF | 453 | 1.6% |
| 5 | Does chair yoga really work | 348 | 1.2% |

The blog's top page drives 789 visits. Competitor blogs regularly hit 5,000–40,000 from single pages. The "does X break a fast" cluster, Simple's strongest play, has ~12 pages. It should have 40+.

### The gap

Simple has ~50 content pages generating ~5,800 visits. That's 166 visits per page. Competitors with similar domain authority average 264–502 visits per page. The topics are proven. Simple just hasn't built the pages.

### Why AI matters here

Simple currently has **zero AI referral traffic** because the site blocks AI indexation. Intermittent fasting is one of the most heavily queried health categories in AI. Simple doesn't show up in any of those responses.

AI referral traffic converts at 2x the rate of organic search. Unblocking AI indexation alone could be worth 150–250 incremental purchases per month by month 12.
