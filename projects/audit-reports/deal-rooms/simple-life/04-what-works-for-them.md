## What drives traffic for competitors

Three content types drive the most traffic. Simple doesn't have any of them.

### 1. Calculators and tools

Ruled.me's keto calculator drives 8,299 visits/month. That's 15.5% of their entire site from one page. Nutrisense's blood sugar chart drives 41,018. Perfect Keto's macro calculator drives 3,400. Single pages outperforming Simple's entire blog.

Calculators work because people come with a specific question, spend time getting their answer, and leave with a reason to download the app. AI engines cite them heavily.

Simple has zero calculator or tool pages.

### 2. Template content: "does X break a fast"

The "break a fast" question is the most proven template in the IF space. Every competitor has some version of it. Zero gets ~1,775/month from 5 pages. Fastic gets ~2,553 from 5 pages. Simple has 12 pages in this cluster but averages only ~100 visits each.

KD is consistently low (5–15) and there are 75+ searchable variants. Simple already ranks #1 for "does heavy cream break a fast" and "does AG1 break a fast." The authority is there. It's a volume play.

### 3. Food content: lists, plans, recipes

Diet Doctor, Perfect Keto, and Ruled.me all outperform Simple by 2–5x with food/recipe/diet-plan content. Simple's highest-traffic blog post is "keto diet meal plan for beginners" at 789 visits. The cluster has 10–50x room to grow.

Food lists are evergreen, shareable, and link naturally to dozens of related pages. They build topical authority fast.
