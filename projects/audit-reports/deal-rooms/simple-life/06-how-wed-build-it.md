## The growth model

Every piece of content generates value across three channels at once.

### Three return channels from one investment

1. **Organic search traffic.** Content ranks on Google. Impressions, clicks, visits. Each monthly cohort matures over 4 months and compounds on every previous cohort.

2. **AI citations.** The same content AI engines cite when someone asks "what's the best intermittent fasting app?" AI referral traffic converts at 2x the rate of organic search. The visitor arrives pre-qualified and endorsed.

3. **Brand lift.** Every visitor who encounters Simple, whether from search or AI, has a probability of searching for Simple later. Brand search converts at 1.56% visit-to-purchase vs. 0.68% for organic content. Brand search volume also predicts AI visibility, so this creates a loop.

### Content maturation curve

New pages don't produce traffic on day one. The ramp:

| Months After Publish | % of Peak Traffic |
|---|---|
| Month 1 | 0% (indexing) |
| Month 2 | 25% (initial rankings) |
| Month 3 | 50% (rankings improving) |
| Month 4+ | 100% (steady state) |

Every month adds a new cohort on top of all previous ones. Month 6 isn't 6x month 1. It's more, because all cohorts are stacking.

## How the engagement works

### 8-week Strategy Sprint

Every engagement starts with an 8-week sprint. We prove value in 6 weeks or address what's not working.

- **Weeks 1–2.** Deep-dive on Simple's business, audience, and competitors. Gather context fast.
- **Weeks 3–5.** First content cohort ships. Calculators, top "break a fast" pages, and pillar science content go live. Early indexation and impression signals.
- **Week 6. Circuit breaker.** Are we both seeing enough to continue? If pages are indexing, impressions building, and early AI signals appearing, we scale. If not, we stop and figure out why.
- **Weeks 7–8.** Transition into Growth Execution. Set production cadence, team structure, and reporting.

### Growth Execution pod

After the sprint converts, a dedicated pod takes over:
- **Engagement Manager.** Owns the relationship and strategic direction.
- **Editors.** Content quality, brand voice, editorial calendar.
- **SEO Specialist.** Technical SEO, schema, internal linking.
- **Designer / Developer.** Calculator UIs, infographics, visual assets.
- **Shared specialists.** AEO optimization, analytics, QA.

### Human-AI production model

AI handles 80–90% of production volume: research, first drafts, SEO optimization, structured data. Humans handle strategy, voice calibration, quality control, and the editorial calls that determine whether content ranks or doesn't.

Fully AI-generated content averages position 16.8 in search. Human-edited content averages position 5.2.
