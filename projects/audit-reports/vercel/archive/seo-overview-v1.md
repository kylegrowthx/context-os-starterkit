## Summary & highest-leverage fixes

### What's working

- **Rank momentum is strong.** From 25,232 to 13,550 in 12 months. Traffic up 83% YoY and 32% over 6 months. The growth is real and sustained.
- **Documentation as SEO engine.** 115 docs pages contributing 10.1% of traffic. Several docs pages rank for generic developer queries (cron jobs, serverless functions), acting as top-of-funnel discovery.
- **Templates section captures framework queries.** 57 pages ranking for "nextjs templates," "react template," "saas boilerplate" — these are high-intent developer queries at the evaluation stage.
- **Product pages rank for category terms.** The `/ai` and `/ai-gateway` pages capture "ai gateway" (position 1) and related AI infrastructure queries.
- **Keyword velocity is explosive.** 23,096 new keywords in the last month (54% of inventory). The domain continues to expand its reach.
- **High traffic value per visit.** $677K value / 168K visits = ~$4.03 per visit, reflecting enterprise-tier keyword value.

### What's broken

- **Blog is dramatically underperforming.** 51 pages generating only 2.1% of traffic. Nearly all blog traffic comes from product announcements (v0, SDKs), not SEO-targeted content. For a domain with Vercel's authority, the blog should be driving 10–20% of traffic.
- **Homepage traffic concentration at 61%.** For a domain ranking 13,550 with 76K keywords, this is too high. Healthy mature sites have homepage share below 30%. This signals that non-branded content isn't pulling its weight.
- **95% of keywords are below position 10.** With 42,442 keywords tracked by DataForSEO, only 2,109 are in the top 10. There's a massive mid-position inventory (30–70) that could be promoted with targeted optimization.
- **No comparison or alternative pages.** "Vercel vs Netlify," "Vercel vs AWS Amplify," "Vercel vs Railway" — these high-intent queries are being captured by third-party sites, not Vercel. The single `/kb/guide/migrate-to-vercel-from-netlify` page ranks but is a migration guide, not a comparison.
- **Community forum generates noise.** 36 community pages in the sample, but they rank for largely irrelevant queries ("snaptroid," "nsfw," "peardeck"). Community content is diluting topical authority without contributing meaningful traffic.
- **KB articles (`/kb/`) are underpowered.** 34 pages generating 0.6% of traffic. These troubleshooting and how-to guides could rank much higher with better internal linking and keyword optimization.

### Prioritized fix recommendations

1. **Launch a programmatic content initiative for the blog.** Target non-branded developer queries: "what is serverless," "how to deploy a React app," "next.js vs remix," "best practices for edge computing." Even 20–30 high-quality tutorial/comparison posts could add 10–20K monthly visits.

2. **Build comparison and alternative pages.** Create `/vs/netlify`, `/vs/aws-amplify`, `/vs/cloudflare-pages`, `/vs/railway`. These bottom-of-funnel pages capture buyers actively evaluating deployment platforms.

3. **Optimize the position 11–30 keyword cluster.** 5,011 keywords sit in positions 11–30. Identify pages ranking for these terms and run targeted on-page optimization (title tags, H1s, content depth) to push them into the top 10. Even a 10% promotion rate would add ~500 top-10 keywords.

4. **Cross-link docs ↔ blog ↔ templates.** Internal linking between these three content sections is likely weak. Docs pages should link to relevant blog posts and templates; blog posts should link to docs; templates should link to both. This distributes homepage authority to content pages.

5. **Prune or noindex community noise.** Community pages ranking for "snaptroid," "nsfw," "peardeck" and similar off-topic queries are topical pollution. Consider noindexing low-quality community threads or implementing stricter community moderation signals.

6. **Refresh and expand KB articles.** The `/kb/` section has good bones (troubleshooting, CORS guides, migration guides) but ranks poorly. Merge thin KB articles, add depth, improve titles, and build internal links from docs.

7. **Expand the templates section strategically.** Templates rank for framework queries at scale. Adding templates for trending categories (AI chatbots, SaaS starters, e-commerce, dashboards) with SEO-optimized landing pages would capture evaluation-stage traffic.
