Vercel's organic story over the last 6 months: **traffic up 32% with essentially flat keywords.** The domain gained ~41,000 monthly visits without materially expanding its keyword footprint. Existing pages climbed into better positions rather than new content being published.

The full-index pull reveals a critical detail invisible in sampled data: Vercel has **4,742 indexed pages** but only **244 generate any traffic** (5.1%). The remaining 94.9% is index bloat — pages Google crawls and indexes but delivers zero organic visitors to.

The 12-month picture is dramatic: traffic up 83% (91,747 → 168,294) and rank improved from 25,232 to 13,550.

## Summary & highest-leverage fixes

### What's working

- **Rank momentum is strong.** From 25,232 to 13,550 in 12 months. Traffic up 83% YoY and 32% over 6 months.
- **Documentation as SEO engine.** 679 docs pages contributing 9.9% of traffic. Several rank for generic developer queries.
- **Templates capture framework queries.** 465 pages targeting "nextjs templates," "react template," "saas boilerplate" — high-intent developer queries.
- **Knowledge Base launched successfully.** 301 pages indexed, 24 with traffic, entire section is new. Growth trajectory is positive.
- **Keyword velocity is explosive.** 23,096 new keywords in the last month (54% of inventory).
- **High traffic value per visit.** $677K value / 168K visits = ~$4.03 per visit.

### What's broken

- **94.9% of indexed pages generate zero traffic.** 4,498 of 4,742 pages are dead weight. This is a significant crawl budget and quality signal problem.
- **Blog is catastrophically bloated.** 379 pages, 25 with traffic (6.6%). 354 dead blog posts dilute topical authority without contributing value. Blog drives only 2.1% of traffic despite being the primary "content marketing" vehicle.
- **Homepage traffic concentration at 60.7%.** For a domain ranked 13,550 with 76K keywords, this is far too high. Healthy mature sites target <30%.
- **Community is pure bloat.** 1,778 indexed pages, 17 with traffic, 99% zero-traffic rate. Traffic collapsed 75% in 6 months. Community content ranks for irrelevant queries.
- **Templates section declining.** -34.9% traffic despite adding 73 new pages. 435 of 465 templates have no traffic.
- **95% of keywords below position 10.** Massive mid-position inventory (30–70) that could be promoted with targeted optimization.
- **No comparison or alternative pages.** "Vercel vs Netlify" captured only by a KB migration guide, not a purpose-built comparison.
- **Content page trajectory is bimodal.** Pages are either surging (new) or in freefall (old). Zero pages are stable. No evergreen content strategy.

### Prioritized fix recommendations

1. **Index hygiene — noindex dead content.** Noindex the 1,761 zero-traffic community pages, 403 dead changelog entries, 435 dead templates, and 354 dead blog posts. This alone removes ~2,950 pages from the index and improves crawl efficiency. Start with community and changelog (lowest risk), then evaluate blog and templates.

2. **Launch evergreen content program.** The blog's product-announcement model creates a spike-and-decay pattern. Build 20–30 evergreen tutorial and comparison posts targeting non-branded queries: "what is serverless," "how to deploy a React app," "next.js vs remix," "best practices for edge computing."

3. **Build comparison and alternative pages.** Create `/vs/netlify`, `/vs/aws-amplify`, `/vs/cloudflare-pages`, `/vs/railway`. These bottom-of-funnel pages capture buyers evaluating deployment platforms.

4. **Optimize position 11–30 keywords.** 5,011 keywords in positions 11–30. Identify the pages ranking for these and run targeted on-page optimization. Even a 10% promotion rate adds ~500 top-10 keywords.

5. **Cross-link docs, blog, templates, and KB.** Internal linking between these sections distributes homepage authority to content pages. Docs pages should link to relevant blog posts and templates; blog posts should link to docs.

6. **Consolidate and refresh KB articles.** 277 of 301 KB pages have no traffic. Merge thin articles, improve titles, and build internal links from docs to promote discovery.

7. **Prune or noindex community.** Community pages ranking for off-topic queries dilute topical authority. Noindex low-quality threads or implement robots.txt disallow for the community subdomain.
