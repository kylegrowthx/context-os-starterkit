# Flodesk AEO Audit Report

*Analysis Date: March 1, 2026*
*Data source: CheckThat AI Visibility Index (production)*
*Tracking period: December 18, 2025 – March 1, 2026 (74 days)*
*Prompts tracked: 201 evaluation-stage, unbranded buyer queries*
*AI engines: ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, Gemini, Google AI Overviews*
*Competitors tracked: 28*

---

## Executive Summary

Flodesk has a serious AI visibility problem. Across 201 evaluation-stage buyer prompts tracked daily over 74 days, Flodesk ranks **17th out of 29 brands** with an average visibility score of **3.51 out of 100**. That's 17.6x below the category leader Klaviyo (61.74) and 12.9x below Mailchimp (45.31).

When AI engines answer the questions buyers actually ask — "What's the best email marketing tool for small businesses?", "Which email platform has the best design tools?", "What are good Mailchimp alternatives?" — Flodesk barely registers. The brand appears in only ~3.5% of tracked prompts, and when it does appear, it averages position 9.5 (middle of the pack or lower).

The trend is worsening. Visibility declined 20.4% from January (3.88) to February (3.09). Flodesk's own domain is cited by AI engines approximately 24 times across 73 days of tracking — less than once every three days — while competitor domains like mailchimp.com, klaviyo.com, and mailerlite.com appear in AI responses every single day.

**The bottom line:** In an era where AI engines increasingly mediate buyer decisions, Flodesk is nearly invisible. The company's strong product, design-first positioning, and $36M ARR are not translating into AI awareness. This report details why, and what can be done.

## 5. Root Cause Analysis

### 5.1 Why AI Engines Don't Recommend Flodesk

Based on the data, five root causes explain Flodesk's low AI visibility:

**1. Weak source footprint.** AI engines build recommendations from cited sources. Flodesk's domain is cited ~24 times over 73 days. Competitors' domains are cited daily. The content that AI engines crawl and reference — listicles, comparison pages, review sites, educational content — either doesn't feature Flodesk or features it too minimally to influence the model.

**2. Missing from category-defining listicles.** The most-cited URLs are "best email marketing" roundups from third-party sites (emailtooltester, techradar, moosend, marketermilk, etc.). If Flodesk isn't prominently featured on these pages, AI engines won't include it in their recommendations.

**3. Low informational content volume.** Flodesk's cited pages are almost exclusively comparison/alternatives posts and listicles from its own blog. There's no evidence of product documentation, educational content, or category-defining thought leadership being cited. Competitors like HubSpot have both blog.hubspot.com and knowledge.hubspot.com cited daily.

**4. Narrow content positioning.** The Flodesk URLs that do get cited are all comparison-style content ("X alternatives"). This tells AI engines that Flodesk competes in a specific niche but doesn't establish it as a category authority. Meanwhile, competitors produce content across the full buyer journey.

**5. Third-party presence gap.** AI engines heavily cite G2, Reddit, emailtooltester, and TechRadar. Flodesk's presence on these platforms may be insufficient (few reviews, few mentions in roundups, limited community discussion) compared to competitors.

### 5.2 What Flodesk Does Well in AI

Despite low visibility, there are positive signals:

- **Zero negative sentiment.** No AI engine characterizes Flodesk negatively. The brand narrative, when it appears, is clean.
- **86.5% positive mentions.** AI correctly understands Flodesk's design-first value proposition and ease of use.
- **109 rank-1 mentions.** There are specific prompt angles where Flodesk wins — likely design-focused, creator-focused, or flat-rate-pricing-focused queries. These represent content opportunities.
- **Comparison content works.** The pages AI does cite from Flodesk are comparison-style posts. This content type has traction and can be scaled.

## 6. Recommendations

### 6.1 Immediate (0–30 days)

**1. Audit and improve third-party listicle presence.**
Identify the top 20 most-cited URLs in AI responses (see raw data dump). For each one, check if Flodesk is featured. Where it's missing, pursue inclusion through outreach, updated profiles, or sponsored placements. Priority targets:
- emailtooltester.com/en/blog/free-email-marketing-services/
- moosend.com/blog/best-email-marketing-services/
- marketermilk.com/blog/best-newsletter-platforms
- techradar.com/best/best-email-marketing-software
- seedprod.com/best-email-newsletter-services/

**2. Strengthen G2 and review platform presence.**
G2.com is cited by AI engines every single day. Review volume, rating, and category rankings on G2 directly influence AI recommendations. Prioritize G2 review generation campaigns.

**3. Create Reddit presence.**
Reddit is cited daily by AI engines. Authentic, helpful discussions about Flodesk in subreddits like r/emailmarketing, r/smallbusiness, r/ecommerce can influence AI perception.

### 6.2 Short-term (30–90 days)

**4. Scale comparison and alternatives content.**
Flodesk's comparison posts are the only owned content AI cites. Double down:
- Create "[Competitor] vs Flodesk" pages for every Tier 1 and Tier 2 competitor
- Create "Best [use case] email marketing tools" content targeting the exact prompt angles tracked
- Target the 201 specific prompts being tracked with dedicated content

**5. Build category-defining educational content.**
HubSpot's knowledge base and blog are cited daily because they answer fundamental questions: "What is email marketing?", "How to write a newsletter", "Email automation best practices." Flodesk needs a content hub that answers the same questions with a design-first perspective.

**6. Optimize product and feature pages for AI crawlability.**
Ensure flodesk.com product pages, pricing page, and feature pages are structured in a way AI engines can extract and cite: clear headings, factual claims, comparison-friendly formatting, structured data markup.

### 6.3 Medium-term (90–180 days)

**7. Develop a thought leadership corpus.**
The brands with highest AI visibility (Klaviyo, HubSpot) have extensive educational content libraries. Flodesk should build a resource center covering email marketing strategy, design best practices, automation playbooks, and creator economy insights.

**8. Pursue media and analyst coverage.**
Businessinsider.com, entrepreneur.com, and globenewswire.com are all cited daily. Press coverage, contributed articles, and analyst reports that mention Flodesk will directly feed AI training data and citation pools.

**9. Monitor and iterate.**
Use the 74-day baseline established here to measure progress. Track:
- Visibility score trend (target: 10+ within 90 days, 20+ within 180 days)
- Average rank improvement (target: below 7.0)
- Own-domain citation rate increase
- Third-party citation source diversification

### C. Methodology

This audit uses data from CheckThat's AI Visibility Index, which measures brand presence across AI engines (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, Gemini, Google AI Overviews) using evaluation-stage, unbranded buyer prompts. The methodology:

1. **Prompts** are classified by buyer stage, intent, and question type. Only evaluation-stage, unbranded prompts count toward visibility scores.
2. **Probes** capture each AI engine's response to each prompt, daily.
3. **Mentions** are extracted from probe text, scored for sentiment, and ranked by position.
4. **Citations** (URLs and domains) are extracted and classified by source type.
5. **Snapshots** aggregate daily signals per brand into scores including visibility, share of voice, sentiment, citation rate, and citation share.
6. **Visibility Score** (0–100) is the primary metric, calculated from presence rate (70%), stability, position, source control, and engine coverage.
