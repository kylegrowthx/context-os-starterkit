## Content health

### Content pages: None

giga.ai has **zero content pages**. There is no blog, resource center, documentation hub, help center, or educational content of any kind. This means:

- **No content trajectory analysis possible** — there's nothing to categorize into freefall/declining/stable/growing/surging
- **No SEO moat** — all traffic is branded, which means it evaporates if brand awareness stalls
- **No keyword defense** — competitors with content pages will dominate every non-branded query in the AI customer support space
- **No link magnet** — no content exists for third parties to link to or reference

With only 8 pages and traffic overwhelmingly concentrated on the homepage (96%), the per-page metrics reflect brand strength, not SEO performance. There is no keyword diversification — if branded search volume dips, traffic craters.

## Summary & highest-leverage fixes

### What's working

- **Strong branded keyword capture.** "Giga," "giga ai," "giga ml," and variants all resolve to the homepage at position 1. Brand recognition from YC, the DoorDash partnership, and Series A coverage is translating directly to search queries.
- **Rapid domain authority accretion.** Going from non-existent to rank ~457K in 4 months shows the brand is gaining traction in Google's index.
- **High traffic value efficiency.** $42,968 in estimated traffic value from 3,162 visits means Google assigns ~$13.59 per visit — these are high-intent queries.
- **Keyword momentum is strongly positive.** 140 new keywords in the last month, only 24 declining. The domain is still in launch-phase growth.

### What's broken

- **Zero content strategy.** No blog, no resources, no documentation, no help center. This is the single biggest gap. Every competitor with a content engine (e.g., Intercom, Zendesk, Five9) owns thousands of non-branded keywords that giga.ai will never rank for.
- **100% branded traffic dependency.** If brand awareness plateaus (post-fundraise news cycle), traffic will flatline. There is no organic discovery mechanism for users who don't already know the brand.
- **No link magnet.** Without content, there's nothing to earn backlinks. The domain's authority will stall unless propped up by press cycles.
- **Site is too thin for technical SEO value.** With 8 pages, there's no internal linking structure, no topical authority signals, and no URL depth for Google to crawl.
- **Careers page ranking for irrelevant keywords.** giga.ai/careers ranks for "equinox gym," "ping pong san francisco," and other unrelated terms — likely caused by thin page content or Google hallucinating associations from co-founder bios.

### Prioritized fix recommendations

1. **Launch a blog / resource center.** Even 10–15 high-quality articles targeting "AI call center," "AI customer support," "call deflection," "voice AI for enterprise," and similar terms would dramatically expand keyword footprint. These are the queries where giga.ai currently ranks 30–60+ with zero dedicated pages.

2. **Create a documentation / developer hub.** Product-focused documentation (API docs, integration guides, use case walkthroughs) builds SEO authority and serves as a link magnet for technical audiences.

3. **Publish case studies as standalone pages.** The DoorDash case study exists but has zero traffic. Expanding it into a detailed, keyword-optimized page (+ additional case studies) would create credible, linkable assets.

4. **Build comparison and category pages.** "Giga vs. Intercom," "AI voice agents vs. IVR," "best AI customer support platforms" — these bottom-of-funnel pages capture buyers actively evaluating solutions.

5. **Fix careers page relevance.** Add structured content about engineering culture, roles, and the team to prevent Google from associating the page with random gym and restaurant keywords.
