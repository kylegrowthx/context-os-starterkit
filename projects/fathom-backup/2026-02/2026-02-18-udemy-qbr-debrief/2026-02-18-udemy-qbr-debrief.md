## Context

Udemy is a renewal risk at approximately 50% confidence. The engagement has involved strategy sprints, content roadmap planning, and ongoing article production, but Steph (likely procurement or strategy lead) is questioning whether the agency's investment is justified when her internal team could handle basic article generation. This concern surfaced in the QBR despite positive feedback on execution, signaling a shift from capability questions to strategic value questions. Additional context: Jennifer (who brought us in) has left, and the organization underwent an acquisition, both of which have created leadership shifts and may be driving renewed scrutiny of agency spend.

The internal team at GrowthX recognizes this is not a simple execution problem but a communication and positioning problem. Steph wants to see that GrowthX is driving strategy (speed of learning, multi-channel thinking) rather than just producing output. The team also noted that Udemy's broader approach (campaign-focused reporting rather than channel-specific metrics) makes it harder to tell a traditional SEO ROI story, which complicates value demonstration. The strategy side of the work—keyword universe research, pillar mapping, campaign strategy—is less visible in the day-to-day engagement manager interactions.
