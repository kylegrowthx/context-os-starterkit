## Context

GrowthX is implementing three interconnected process changes to improve content quality, team accountability, and client outcomes. First, technical SEO audits are now mandatory best practices for all clients regardless of contract language—these prevent technical debt (broken links, poor site structure) from undermining content ROI. Second, the team is shifting to a "micro-team" model where Content Managers (EMs) and Marketing Experts (MEs) co-create content strategy in Week 2, with EMs preparing data and both roles brainstorming to refine strategy before the client-facing presentation. This increases ME workload but improves content quality by front-loading expert review and feedback. Third, the calibration article has moved from Week 1 to Week 2 to allow more time for client iteration, de-risking the entire sprint pipeline. Alongside these changes, a custom Notion tool is launching next week to replace the Kanban board, providing dual views (client and personal task lists) and integrated client health signals (Green/Yellow/Red flags) that both EMs and MEs are responsible for reporting.

## Key Topics

### New Strategic Processes

  - **Technical SEO Audits**
      - **Decision:** Now a mandatory best practice for all clients.
      - **Rationale:** Prevents technical debt (e.g., broken links, poor site structure) from undermining content performance.
      - **Action:** William will confirm Talal has bandwidth to perform these audits for all new clients.
  - **ME/EM Collaboration on Content Strategy**
      - **Change:** MEs and EMs will now build the content strategy together in Week 2.
      - **Goal:** Foster a "micro-team" partnership, giving MEs more strategic input and providing EMs with an expert review to "poke holes" in the plan before client presentation.
      - **Process:**
          - EMs prepare initial data (e.g., competitor CSVs, cluster analysis).
          - EMs and MEs hold a 1-hour brainstorm call to finalize the strategy.
          - EMs record a Loom presentation for team review, with MEs available to answer questions.
          - EMs remain the sole client-facing presenter.
      - **Workload:** Acknowledged as more work for MEs, but framed as an investment that improves content quality and reduces rework.
  - **Calibration Article Moved to Week 2**
      - **Change:** The calibration article is now due by the end of Week 2.
      - **Rationale:** Provides more time for client feedback, de-risking the production pipeline.
      - **Impact:** If clients are highly responsive, more articles may be published by the end of the sprint. This is a positive outcome, as the goal is to deliver maximum value and build a strong case for the growth phase.
  - **Project Management Tool**
      - **Change:** A custom Notion PM solution is being built to replace the current Kanban board.
      - **Launch:** Expected next week.
      - **Features:** Client and personal task views, client health monitoring, and dependency tracking.
  - **Escalation & Health Signals**
      - **Change:** New SOPs are being developed for escalation paths and defining Sprint Health Signals (Green, Yellow, Red).
      - **Goal:** Ensure clear, consistent reporting on client health, a shared ME/EM responsibility.
