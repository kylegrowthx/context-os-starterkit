## Context

GrowthX is an AI-native, high-growth company building production applications entirely on Anthropic's Claude models. The company has been an early adopter of Claude 4.5 beta access and is scaling rapidly, but this growth is creating friction: rigid usage caps on the current Teams plan trigger rate-limiting, forcing operational workarounds and creating unpredictable costs. The timing of this call is strategic—John Russell recently joined Anthropic's startup program after four years at Google Cloud, where he managed growth partnerships with pre-Series C companies. This gives him both the credibility and mandate to move fast.

GrowthX's ask is straightforward but layered: (1) immediate relief (monthly invoicing, higher limits), (2) a clear migration path to Enterprise (which replaces caps with transparent pay-as-you-go pricing), and (3) a long-term partnership framework (reserved capacity and volume discounts backed by a 12-month forecast). Leo Steffen, GrowthX's CEO, emphasized that the company uses Claude across every product and operation, making this not a nice-to-have but a foundational constraint on growth. Anthropic's interest in the partnership is equally explicit: they want feedback on Claude's competitive positioning (especially multimodality vs. GPT-4o) and leverage GrowthX's technical team for prompt engineering collaboration. The call closes with clear mutual commitment and a credible roadmap.
