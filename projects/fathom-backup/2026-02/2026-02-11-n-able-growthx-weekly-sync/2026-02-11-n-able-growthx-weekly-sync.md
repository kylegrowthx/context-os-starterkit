## Context

This is a mid-engagement partnership check-in between GrowthX (content marketing) and N-Able (endpoint management and cybersecurity). N-Able is executing a business pivot toward cyber resiliency, and the collaboration focuses on building topical authority in AdLumen (advanced threat detection) and security-adjacent content while establishing visibility in AI models (ChatGPT, Bing) through CheckThat. The conversation reveals tension between quality listicles as a necessary AI visibility strategy and internal N-Able skepticism about that format; GrowthX addressed this by positioning balanced, critical listicles as a differentiator rather than pure vendor bias. Editorial workflow confusion was resolved by clarifying the topic approval pipeline: ideas → considering (N-Able feedback) → approve to start (GrowthX production).

## Key Topics

### Content Strategy & Performance

  - **Current Mix:** Heavy focus on AdLumen (6 blogs) and full-stack topics (5 blogs) to support the company's cyber resiliency pivot.
  - **Future Focus:** Shift priority to Cove (backup/DR) over UEM.
      - **Rationale:** The market knows N-able for UEM but not for security or Cove. This shift aims to build awareness in new, strategic areas.
  - **Performance:** AdLumen content has lower traffic, which is expected for a new product cluster. Traffic is projected to grow as more content is published and market education increases.

### Editorial Process & Feedback

  - **MDR Topics:** Jen (Security PMM) provided feedback on some MDR topics.
      - **Action:** GrowthX will address feedback on specific topics and move all others to "Approve to Start" to begin production.
  - **Product Descriptions:** UEM team feedback requested more dynamic product descriptions.
      - **Problem:** Repetitive, generic descriptions (e.g., "N-Central... automation scripts") are used across all blogs.
      - **Solution:** Frame product references to the specific article's topic (e.g., network segmentation) for greater relevance.

### New Content Format: Listicles for AI Citation

  - **Problem:** ChatGPT frequently cites biased listicles from competitors (e.g., SentinelOne, Acronis) as authoritative sources for evaluation-level prompts.
  - **Rationale for Adoption:** This format is now a necessity to compete for AI citation, despite internal reservations.
  - **Key Finding:** Listicles with a balanced view (including drawbacks) are more likely to be cited. This provides a strategic angle for N-able to offer a valuable, critical perspective.

### AI Visibility Tool: "Check that"

  - **Purpose:** Track AI citations and prompt performance to demonstrate value to internal teams.
  - **Action:** N-able to review the tool's tracked prompts to ensure they are relevant and accurate for measuring success.

### Partnership Health Check

  - **N-able Feedback:** The partnership is meeting expectations.
  - **Primary Blocker:** Internal N-able processes and lack of focus, not GrowthX's performance.
  - **GrowthX Feedback:** Traffic is increasing immediately with each new blog, creating strong momentum.
