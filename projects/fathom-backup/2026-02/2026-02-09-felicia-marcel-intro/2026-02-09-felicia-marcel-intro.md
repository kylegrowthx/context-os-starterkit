  - **Present:** Now cashflow positive, Binti is reinvesting in growth, doubling its sales team and hiring BDRs.
  - **Rationale:** Early BDR success proves that driving more inbound traffic is a viable growth lever.

### GrowthX's Content Engine Model

- **Core Philosophy:** Build a content engine that becomes the definitive resource for a market, creating compounding returns.
- **Process:**
  1. **Strategy Sprint (Phase 1):** Define audience, goals, and content strategy.
  2. **Ongoing Execution (Phase 2):** GrowthX acts as a fractional team, publishing daily (new or refreshed content) to build "surface area."
- **Key Differentiator:** Optimizes for three audiences: human search, AI agents, and AI training bots.
- **Timeline:**
  - **Week 1:** V1 context artifacts
  - **Weeks 2–3:** Content strategy & roadmap
  - **Week 4:** First articles published
  - **End of 8 weeks:** 10–20+ articles published
- **ROI:** A long-term play; strong signal typically appears in 6 months.

### Binti's Content Opportunity

- **Current State:** Binti's site has 49 pages, with only ~11 blog posts and minimal organic traffic.
- **Target Audiences:**
  - **Primary:** State & county officials, agency leaders, social workers.
  - **Secondary:** Investors, government officials.
  - **Not Primary:** Foster/adoptive families (they are users, not direct buyers).
- **Competitive Landscape:**
  - **Accenture & Deloitte:** Build bespoke, often over-budget systems.
  - **Cardinality & Redmayne:** Offer narrow licensing software, not child welfare-specific.
- **Conclusion:** The market is wide open for Binti to become the dominant content authority.
