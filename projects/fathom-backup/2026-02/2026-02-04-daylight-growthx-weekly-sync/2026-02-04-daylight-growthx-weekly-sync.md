## Summary

GrowthX and Daylight aligned on a five-topic-cluster content strategy to drive inbound leads and LLM visibility targeting security experts and CISOs. ## Context

Daylight is a managed detection and response (MDR) platform with AI-driven security operations capabilities. GrowthX is running a content marketing engagement to build topical authority and organic visibility for Daylight, targeting CISOs and security engineers through educational and competitive positioning content. The engagement is driven by a "portfolio approach" that mixes bottom-of-funnel competitive content with thought leadership, positioning Daylight against competitors like Expel, Red Canary, ReliQuest, and Profit Security.

This meeting reviewed feedback on content strategy artifacts (personas, competitor analysis, and topic clusters) and established processes for tracking changes to those deliverables. A key concern from Daylight's side was ensuring that competitor metrics (especially escalation reduction rates) are accurate and differentiated from competitors' inflated claims. The team also identified opportunities in off-page SEO through Daylight's planned Reddit effort and "SOC Innovation Days" event series.

## Key Topics

### Process & Artifact Feedback

  - **Problem:** Maya's comments on strategy artifacts disappeared without a clear resolution process.
  - **Solution:** GrowthX will use version control and a change log to show how feedback is implemented.
  - **Content Metric Clarification:**
      - **Escalation Reduction:** Avoid using a single percentage (e.g., "90% reduction"). Competitors use inflated numbers (e.g., 99%) for "escalation rate" (investigations vs. customer team handoffs), which is a different metric and creates an unfair comparison.
      - **Integration Depth:** Avoid specific percentages (e.g., "90% coverage for WizDefend"). Coverage varies by tool, making a single number misleading.

### Competitor Landscape Correction

  - **Problem:** The competitor analysis was inaccurate, including non-competitors and miscategorizing others.
  - **Solution:** Maya will correct the list to ensure the content database is accurate.
  - **New Categories:**
      - **Premium MDR:** Expel, Red Canary, ReliQuest.
      - **AI SOC with services:** Profit Security.
  - **Prioritization:** Maya will tier competitors (Tier 1, 2, etc.) to focus content efforts.

### Content Strategy & Topic Clusters

  - **Goal:** Drive inbound leads and LLM visibility by targeting experts and CISOs.
  - **Approach:** A "portfolio approach" mixing educational and bottom-of-funnel content to build topical authority.
  - **Approved Clusters:**
    1.  **MDR & Threat Hunting:** Targets both ICPs; foundational.
    2.  **Competitive Comparisons:** Targets both ICPs; high-value "vs." and "best of" content.
    3.  **Modern Security Architecture:** Targets first-time buyers; fills a content gap for cloud-native security.
    4.  **Security Operations Transformation:** Targets both ICPs; frames Daylight's unique story.
  - **Cluster 5: Security Leadership & Governance**
      - **Maya's Concern:** This high-level topic may not drive direct solution-seeking traffic.
      - **George's Rationale:** Builds executive awareness and thought leadership.
      - **Decision:** Test both this cluster and a more tactical one (e.g., phishing) to see which resonates.
  - **Cluster 7:** Pruned from the initial list.

### Off-Page SEO & Technical Audit

  - **Daylight Activities:** Maya is launching a Reddit effort and an event series ("SOC Innovation Days") on a separate domain.
  - **GrowthX Support:** GrowthX will provide guidance on how these activities can support the overall SEO strategy.
  - **New Initiative:** GrowthX will conduct a technical SEO audit of Daylight's site to identify optimization opportunities.
