## Overview

- **Strategic Pivot to TTS:** Deepgram is shifting focus to Text-to-Speech (TTS) to compete with 11 Labs, driven by leadership concern over the 80/20 revenue split (STT vs. TTS).
- **Content Gap Identified:** A key content gap was found: Deepgram fails to rank for "11 Labs transcription alternative" because its competitive content is TTS-focused, not Speech-to-Text (STT).
- **Programmatic Language Pages:** Hebrew, Persian, and Urdu pages are live. Chinese (Mandarin/Cantonese) and Thai pages will be updated to Nova 3 for a March 11 launch, switching from keyword boosting to key term prompting.

---

## Key Topics

### Strategic Focus: Competing with 11 Labs

  - **Problem:** Leadership is concerned about Deepgram's TTS competitiveness against 11 Labs, despite STT being 80% of revenue.
  - **Goal:** Increase TTS market share and demonstrate clear wins in this area.
  - **Progress:** "11 Labs alternatives" content now drives a few dozen weekly site visitors, up from zero.
  - **Content Gap:** Deepgram does not rank for "11 Labs transcription alternative."
      - **Reason:** Competitive content is TTS-focused, not STT-focused.
      - **Action:** Create STT-focused competitive content to capture this search intent.
  - **New Content:** Jose is publishing three new 11 Labs articles (custom voices, multilingual, accents) over the next three days.

### Programmatic Language Page Updates

  - **Recent Launches:** Hebrew, Persian, and Urdu pages are now live.
      - **Fix:** Inconsistent "speech to text" hyphenation across all 50+ pages was corrected.
  - **Upcoming Updates (March 11 Launch):** Chinese (Mandarin/Cantonese) and Thai pages will be updated to Nova 3.
      - **Change:** Switch from keyword boosting to key term prompting.
      - **Note:** These are updates to existing Nova 2 pages, not new languages.
  - **Future Consideration:** The upcoming Flux Multilingual launch will require a separate strategy discussion.
