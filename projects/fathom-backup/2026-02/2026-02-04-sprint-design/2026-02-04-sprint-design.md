## Context

GrowthX's Sprint engagements are high-touch, 8-week content acceleration programs. To improve conversion rates and reduce surprise risks, the team identified gaps in three processes: health scoring, escalation protocols, and pre-kickoff setup. This meeting synthesized learnings from recent Sprints (Elementum, Ramp, CRV) and formalized a repeatable system. The Content OS—a platform that automates site crawling, persona generation, and content workflow—will underpin the new approach, enabling tighter timelines and clearer client visibility. This shift requires EMs to become strategic consultants rather than execution managers, supported by structured health tracking and clear escalation triggers.

## Key Discussion Highlights

### 1. Sprint Health Score Framework

The team established a biweekly health score system (Green/Yellow/Red) that varies by Sprint phase:

**Weeks 1-2 (Strategy & Kickoff)**
- **Green signals:** Strategy approved, content artifacts delivered, calibration article sent, proactive client engagement, clear stakeholders identified, all system access granted
- **Red flags:** Client unhappy with strategy, requesting scope changes, unresponsive, or unable to provide required materials
- **Qualitative factors:** Client showing active/proactive engagement (not just receiving), stakeholder clarity, alignment on jobs-to-be-done

**Weeks 3-4 (Ramp-up & Production)**
- **Green signals:** Content calendar approved, Content OS reviewed/approved with minimal feedback, calibration article delivered (generated, not necessarily approved), production ramped to 3 articles week 3 → 5 articles week 4
- **Red flags:** Content OS not approved, publishing bottlenecks, client feedback purely tactical/detail-oriented rather than strategic
- **Key milestone:** Calibration article delivered by end of week 2; customer sees content in CMS ready to publish by week 3
- **Team responsibility:** Weekly Slack updates minimum; track communications in Slack (not email/Google Docs); share strategic insights proactively

**Weeks 5-6 (Performance & Refinement)**
- **Green signals:** Content indexed and driving measurable impressions/clicks, feedback volume minimal, customer enthusiastic about calibration quality
- **Red flags:** High volume of edits (signals possible customer pivot in tone/messaging), lack of performance visibility shared with customer
- **Metrics:** Monitor comments-per-piece to quantify edit volume; use GSC/GA to show impact by taxonomy/topic

**Trigger overrides:** CEO/primary stakeholder disapproval or major scope creep overrides any green score.

### 2. CheckFat Positioning Strategy

CheckFat will be positioned as a free value-add during Sprints to accelerate ARR conversion:

- **Setup timing:** Configure CheckFat pre-kickoff so customer can see data immediately; Looker ticket filed in parallel
- **Presentation timing:** Show in week 2-4 (stagger with strategy → content calendar → CheckFat review → optionally Atlas)
- **Pricing approach:** Do NOT upsell CheckFat during Sprint. Instead, offer as data access (with contract amendment + revenue swap if customer requests direct access). Focus is on proving value, not incremental revenue.
- **Post-Sprint:** CheckFat becomes standard contract SKU. Upsells count toward NRR only after Sprint converts to ARR.
- **Rationale:** Upsells during Sprint risk conversion and distract from "prove yourself" phase. A dollar of SaaS revenue is valued ~10X higher than services revenue, so convert first → upsell later.

### 3. Content OS Demo & EM Role Transformation

Daniel demonstrated the Content OS platform:

**Core automation:**
- Crawls client website to auto-generate personas, taxonomy, tone/style profiles
- Identifies top-performing pages and research sources (deleted/confirmed by EM)
- Builds calibrated library of competitors, topics, CTAs

**Client & team experience:**
- Read-only annotation UI for customer feedback; system learns and recalibrates
- EM role shifts from tactical execution to strategic advisory: guide clients through OS, translate business goals into content actions
- Enables EMs to share proactive insights (e.g., "We found 26k keywords + 19 competitors; here are the opportunities")

**Timeline impact:** Could compress Sprints from 8 weeks to 3-4 weeks if fully operational by March.

### 4. Pre-Kickoff Process (14 days prior)

Not currently implemented; must be standardized:

- Competitive analysis
- Technical audit (site health, GSC/GA setup, CMS walkthrough)
- Initial context gathering and document review
- Create site health/tech audit checklist; build Loom guide for repeatability

**Value:** Reduces surprises, identifies missing access/broken assets, positions GrowthX as technical consultant.

### 5. Documentation & Knowledge Management

Decided to use **Notion** as source of truth (not Google Docs or Linear, due to integration limitations):

- Build custom Sprint task database (Leah Myers)
- Structure as: (1) **Journey** (high-level phases), (2) **Operator's Guide** (detailed actions/timelines), (3) **Troubleshooting Guide** (decision trees by trigger)
- Avoid "Bible" document overload; separate concerns by role/audience

### 6. Escalation & Management Protocols

**Established triggers for escalation:**
- Client unresponsive for >5 days
- Scope creep or major pivot in tone/messaging
- Approval cycles blocking publication (week 4+)
- Stakeholder/decision-maker absence or new gatekeepers introduced

**EM best practice:** One-on-one sidebars with stakeholders by week 4 to unblock bottlenecks, check alignment, ensure stakeholder clarity.
