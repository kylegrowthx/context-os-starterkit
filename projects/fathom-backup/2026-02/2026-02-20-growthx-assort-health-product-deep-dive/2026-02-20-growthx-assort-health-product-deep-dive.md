## Summary

Ray Mathew (VP Sales) from Assort Health walked GrowthX through their voice AI platform, positioning it as a labor-cost reduction engine addressing healthcare's "vicious cycle" of rising staff costs vs. flat reimbursements. The core differentiator is the "Assort Synapse" engine, which builds specialty-specific AI agents by ingesting unstructured data (EHRs, runbooks, VoIP logs) to handle complex patient access workflows that go far beyond simple call routing. The conversation surfaced critical sales dynamics: strong market validation but pricing and contract structure objections, plus post-sale alignment gaps that can undermine implementation if not addressed by executive sponsorship.

---

## Context

Assort Health competes in the voice AI / call center automation space, targeting mid-market to large provider groups (8 to 4,000+ providers per organization). Ray's sales team (6 BDRs, 8 AEs) brings inbound leads through events, outbound, and customer referrals. Ray focuses on provider groups (100-500 providers at distribution center) rather than health systems, though some large prospects span both. Assort Health is positioning against voice AI vendors like Relatient, Luma, and Transform9—not traditional VoIP providers—and claims a 3-month implementation advantage vs. competitors' 1-year free trials.

The core buyer motivation is labor reduction: healthcare providers face a "vicious cycle" of rising costs (staff, supplies) while reimbursement rates stay flat, forcing them to optimize with fewer staff. The solution's magic is handling the real-world complexity of patient access beyond simple call handling—navigating insurance rules (e.g., global periods that dictate provider roles), specialty-specific protocols, and unstructured data (EHR templates, runbooks, patient communication preferences). Ray emphasized that price and contract structure (competitors offer month-to-month; Assort Health does longer terms) are the main friction points, but the deeper post-sale issue is lack of executive alignment: customers who don't have clear C-suite buy-in can later scrutinize call transcripts and withdraw support if concerns surface.

## Key Topics

### Market Challenge: The "Vicious Cycle"

  - The pitch begins by framing the market challenge as a "vicious cycle" for healthcare providers:
      - Rising costs (labor, supplies) vs. flat reimbursements → Pressure to optimize with fewer staff.
      - This leads to patient dissatisfaction and operational strain.
  - The pitch is tailored to specific specialties (e.g., orthopedics, gastro) to resonate with buyers who "think they're special snowflakes."

### Solution: Handling Real-World Complexity

  - Assort Health's value is in handling the real-world complexity of patient access, which goes far beyond a simple AI call.
  - **Key Variables:**
      - **Patient:** Condition, insurance, communication preferences (e.g., language, digital literacy).
      - **Practice:** Specific scheduling templates, visit history, and care team structures.
      - **Insurance:** Complex rules like global periods, which dictate billing eligibility and care team assignments (e.g., MD vs. PA).

### Differentiator: The "Assort Synapse" Engine

  - The core technical advantage is the "Assort Synapse" engine, which builds bespoke AI agents by ingesting unstructured data.
  - **Data Sources:**
      - **EHR:** Scheduling templates, visit history, and non-obvious provider patterns.
      - **VoIP System:** Call recordings and line structures.
      - **Runbooks:** Specialty-specific "bibles" or "cheat sheets" containing protocols for eligibility, prior auths, and prep instructions.
  - This process allows for a bespoke agent build that handles complex workflows, which is the "reason to believe" that validates the product's claims.

### Demo & Product Overview

  - The demo has two parts:
      - **Voice Demo:** A live call simulation showcasing natural language processing and complex workflow navigation (e.g., confirming insurance, finding a provider).
      - **Backend Dashboard:** A leadership view for tracking performance (resolution rates, transfers) and a detailed call log for accountability.
  - **New Intake Product:** A feature for managing patient check-in journeys, including form collection and automated outreach.
  - **Competitive Landscape:**
      - Competes with voice AI vendors (e.g., Relatient, Luma, Transform9), not traditional VoIP providers (e.g., RingCentral).
      - Key advantage: A 3-month implementation period vs. competitors' 1-year free trials, enabling faster ROI.

### Sales & Onboarding Dynamics

  - **ICP:** Provider groups from 8 providers to 4,000 providers (MSOs).
  - **Lead Sources:** Events, outbound, and customer referrals.
  - **Sales Team:** 6 BDRs (5 in seat) and 8 AEs (6 active).
  - **Common Objections:**
      - **Price:** Handled with ROI calculators and case studies.
      - **Contract Structure:** Competitors offer month-to-month. Assort Health counters by framing these as unproven products and emphasizing its own track record.
  - **Onboarding Friction:**
      - Post-sales team needs better objection handling and framing skills.
      - Lacks explicit executive alignment post-sale, which can cause issues if stakeholders later scrutinize call transcripts.

### Messaging Guidance

  - **Use:** "Patient access," "patient engagement," "voice AI," "call center AI."
  - **Avoid:** "Agentic AI." The ICP is more familiar with "AI agents."
  - **Key Asset:** An EHR matrix document detailing compatible systems is critical for sales and could be a future content asset.
