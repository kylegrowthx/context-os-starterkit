## Summary

Two new client handoffs with distinct challenges: JAMA Software (technical manufacturing software) requires a two-part strategy to manage SME quality expectations through upfront communication and pipeline customization, while Assort Health (AI voice agents for healthcare) presents collaborative growth opportunity with low current traffic baseline. The team identified a systemic gap in pre-kickoff preparation (the 2-week window before each engagement) and committed to stabilizing existing processes before scaling.

---

## Context

JAMA Software presents the most immediate strategic challenge. Referred by XFunnel (which was acquired by HubSpot and can no longer serve clients), JAMA demands "technical content quality that won't embarrass" them—a requirement that conflicts with GrowthX's service model, which doesn't employ subject matter experts in every field. The client's internal team (led by Fonny in Amsterdam, with CRO Tom and Creative Manager Mario in the US) has 1,200 indexed pages but is seeing success primarily from basic, non-technical content like "What is the AEC industry?" This paradox is the key to the solution: shifting expectations from SME-level thought leadership to high-quality visibility-driving content that meets audience needs without requiring internal expertise we don't have.

Assort Health, by contrast, is an early-stage AI voice agent company in healthcare with strong funding, a highly engaged CEO, and collaborative culture. With minimal current traffic, they represent a lower-risk opportunity where 2x growth is realistic. The main enabler here is simplicity: one point of contact, clear alignment on goals, and no expertise gap concerns.

Both engagements expose a broader operational weakness: the 2-week pre-kickoff preparation window is systematically missed due to late client documentation and short-notice handoffs. This gap compounds the challenge with technical clients like JAMA. The team agreed that fixing this requires disciplined stabilization of current processes—not process expansion—and will become easier once hiring adds more Engagement Managers.

## Key Topics

### Process Improvement: Pre-Kickoff Prep

- The team identified a recurring gap: the 2-week pre-kickoff preparation window is consistently missed.
- **Root Cause:** Late client documentation and short-notice handoffs.
- **Solution:** Focus on stabilizing the current system before adding new steps.
    - **Goal:** Ensure handoffs occur at least 2 weeks before kickoff.
    - **Enabler:** The upcoming hiring of more Engagement Managers (EMs).

### Client Handoff: JAMA Software

- **Source:** Referral from XFunnel (acquired by HubSpot), which can no longer serve clients.
- **Primary Contact:** Fonny (Amsterdam-based), a results-focused "speed demon" with internal influence.
- **Key Stakeholders:**
    - **CRO (Tom):** Involved to connect content to sales pipeline.
    - **Creative Manager (Mario):** U.S.-based, manages writers.
- **Challenge: Technical Content Quality**
    - **Client Requirement:** "Technical content quality that won't embarrass JAMA."
    - **Risk:** Our standard workflow may not meet this SME-level depth, potentially leading to client dissatisfaction.
- **Mitigating Factors:**
    - **Current Content:** JAMA's top-performing articles are basic (e.g., "What is the AEC industry?"), suggesting low-hanging fruit for improvement.
    - **Focus:** 80% Top-of-Funnel (ToFu) content, which is less technical.
    - **Commitment:** Fonny views it as a minimum 1-year engagement, providing a long runway for results.

### Solution: Managing Technical Content Expectations

- The team defined a two-part strategy for clients with high technical demands.
- **External Communication (Sales & Kickoff):**
    - Set clear expectations: "We are not subject matter experts (SMEs) in your field."
    - Frame our role as driving visibility and traffic, not creating thought leadership.
    - Position the client's internal experts as the source for SME depth (e.g., voice notes).
- **Internal Execution (Pipeline Optimization):**
    - Involve a pipeline optimizer (e.g., Tammy) early (Week 2–3) to customize the workflow.
    - Train the pipeline on "good examples" provided by the client to align with their quality standards.
    - Create a dedicated "artifacts" folder with these examples for the pipeline to reference.

- **Profile:** SF-based AI voice agent company for healthcare.
- **Status:** Early-stage with high growth potential.
- **Key Details:**
    - Strong funding.
    - Highly engaged CEO.
    - Low current traffic → 2x growth is a reasonable goal.
    - Collaborative client.
