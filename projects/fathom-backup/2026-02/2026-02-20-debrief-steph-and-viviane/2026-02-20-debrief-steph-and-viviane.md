### Defining AI Screening Tiers

The team addressed a critical hiring framework ambiguity: William assessed Viviane as "highly AI native," while Bridget disagreed. This sparked conversation on what AI proficiency should mean for GrowthX hiring across all levels.

**Three-Tier Framework Established:**

- **AI Basic:** Uses LLMs for straightforward tasks (research, writing, summarization).
  - Differentiator: Uses ChatGPT as a search engine; no workflow optimization.
  - Nearly all current candidates fall here.

- **AI Curious:** Actively experiments with AI to solve problems; shows initiative beyond basic use.
  - Differentiator: Attempts to automate workflows, combine tools, or build simple systems—even if results are imperfect.
  - Shows mindset of exploration and problem-solving versus passive consumption.
  - Example: Attempted to extract and sync recruiting data from Ashby using AI, or built custom prompts to improve output quality.

- **AI Native:** Builds advanced, multi-tool AI workflows; understands context management, agent design, and tool composition.
  - Differentiator: Shipped products or systems using multiple AI models, custom GPTs, agents, or complex prompt chains.
  - Deep understanding of LLM capabilities and limitations.
  - Example: George's men's relationship app (built in 2 days, shipped to App Store, uses multiple models + agents + diary integration; already monetized).

**Debate & Resolution:**
- William's position: High daily ChatGPT usage ("every single day for almost everything") signals AI-enabled mindset; signals shift from pre-ChatGPT to post-ChatGPT workflow.
- Bridget's position: Daily ChatGPT use doesn't indicate AI skill—it's just using a search engine. Real signal is solving problems with AI, combining tools, or building workflows.
- George's insight: Key question surfaces the gap: "How do you get it to sound like you?" A "it just remembers how I sound" answer shows reliance on single-chat memory vs. understanding context bundling and reusability.
- **Resolution:** Team will use this framework in all debriefs, noting each candidate's tier. Not disqualifying for most roles (training is rapid), but essential calibration for high-leverage roles (e.g., Marcel's Chief of Staff opening requires AI Native).

**Rationale for Framework:**
- GrowthX is exceptionally far ahead on AI compared to most companies; internal standards are unusually high.
- Candidates must be at least AI Curious—baseline openness to problem-solving with AI—to avoid long onboarding drag.
- However, GrowthX's training infrastructure (shared Claude accounts with loaded context, pod structure, daily collaboration) enables rapid upskilling from Curious → Native.
- Current hiring gap: Must calibrate between Marcel's high bar and practical hiring velocity; this framework makes that trade-off explicit.
