## Key Topics

### The Problem: "Agents Gone Wild"

  - CIOs face chaos from hundreds or thousands of unmanaged agents, creating unreliable, inconsistent, and unscalable processes.
  - **Example:** An agent promising an unauthorized discount, leading to a legal loss for a Canadian airline.
  - **Core Misconception:** The belief that probabilistic agents can handle all enterprise workflows, including critical finance or HR processes where outcomes must be 100% reliable.

### Elementum's Solution: Deterministic Workflow Orchestration

  - **Core Principle:** Agents are a component of the workflow, not the workflow itself.
  - **Platform:** A deterministic workflow engine (4 years in development, 20k+ capabilities) orchestrates agents, rules, and humans.
  - **Future-Proofing:** The platform is open and model-agnostic, allowing customers to swap agents as AI evolves without rebuilding core business logic.
  - **Data Strategy:** Workflows run where the data lives (e.g., Snowflake), eliminating data movement, integration work, and compliance headaches.

### Target Audience & Sales Cycle

  - **Primary Buyer:** CIOs, driven by the need for a control plane for their agent landscape.
  - **Secondary Buyer:** Heads of AI.
  - **Influencer:** Chief Data Officers (CDOs), who value Elementum's data-at-rest approach.
  - **Sales Cycle:** 4-week pilot → 6–9 month enterprise sales cycle.

### Competitive Landscape & Messaging

  - **Legacy Vendors (ServiceNow, Pega):** AI is an afterthought; their engines are 20–30 years old and built for human-centric ticketing.
  - **Agentic Platforms (Anthropic, Microsoft):** Offer agent management but lack a deterministic workflow engine, requiring heavy custom coding to achieve enterprise reliability.
  - **Messaging Strategy:**
      - **Tone:** Confident and bold (like IBM), but not arrogant or attacking.
      - **Initial Focus:** Position Elementum as a way to make existing legacy systems better, not as a direct replacement.
      - **Long-Term Goal:** Plant a flag for the "SaaS-pocalypse" by showcasing real-world replacements (e.g., Sanofi replacing Coupa and Viva).

### Content & Customer Storytelling

  - **Top Use Cases:**
      - **ITSM:** Service requests, incident management.
      - **Procurement:** Purchase request/order management.
      - **CRM:** (3rd priority)
  - **Sanofi Case Study:** Saved $10M with software license management, leading to a larger CRM replacement project.

      - **Snowflake:** Use is less credible due to their investment in Elementum.
      - **Elevance Health:** Use is restricted due to a pending upsell.
