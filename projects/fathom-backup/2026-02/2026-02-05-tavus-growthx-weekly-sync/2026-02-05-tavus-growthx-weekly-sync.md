## Key Topics

### Competitor Analysis & Gaps

- **Initial Analysis:** Reviewed HeyGen, Synthesia, Soul Machines, and Anam.
    - **HeyGen & Synthesia:** Large keyword footprints, but much is irrelevant (e.g., deepfakes). The opportunity is to own real-time conversational training, a gap they don't address.
    - **Soul Machines:** Small footprint, but its relevance is now questionable due to a "notice of receivership" on its site.
    - **Anam:** A direct head-to-head competitor with a small footprint, presenting an immediate opportunity to own the space.
- **Required Revisions:**
    - **Add D-ID.com:** A critical keyword competitor with high search visibility, despite Tavus's view of its product quality. This is now a blocking item for strategy finalization.
    - **Investigate HeyGen's LiveAvatar.com:** Analyze this separate domain's strategy to inform a plan for poaching its upcoming renewals. William Leborgne will lead this analysis.
    - **Refine content focus:** Avoid static video topics. Instead, create real-time equivalents of enterprise use cases (e.g., e-learning) that competitors own.

### Proposed Content Clusters & Feedback

- **Cluster 1: Conversational AI Infrastructure**
    - **Goal:** Position Tavus as infrastructure, not a point solution.
    - **Feedback:** Good direction. Layer in key differentiators:
        - **Emotional Intelligence (EQ):** A core model strength that distinguishes Tavus from text-only AI.
        - **Audio-Only Mode:** Use this existing feature as a low-friction entry point for users searching for voice-only AI.
- **Cluster 2: Real-time Video Technology**
    - **Feedback:** Problematic. The term is too generic and conflicts with partners like Daily.co, diluting Tavus's focus.
    - **Action:** Reframe or deprioritize; too much positioning overlap with infrastructure partners.
- **Cluster 3: Category Creation (e.g., "Digital Humans")**
    - **Goal:** Define and own a new term to establish long-term market leadership.
    - **Feedback:** This is a top priority. The specific term is still undefined internally, but the cluster must be created.
    - **Action:** GrowthX will research potential terms (e.g., "AI humans") with leadership input to find a balance between future vision and current search volume. Tavus leadership must align on a single category term.
- **Cluster 4 & 5: Verticals (L&D, Sales Enablement, Recruitment, CX/CS)**
    - **Feedback:** The clusters are ~70% correct but need restructuring. Internal misalignment at Tavus mirrors GrowthX's research challenges.
    - **Action:** Split into distinct, programmatic verticals:
        - **L&D:** Focus on corporate training, language learning, and simulation. Includes employee onboarding use cases (e.g., AI instructor for training scenarios).
        - **Martech:** Target RevOps, AI SDRs/BDRs, sales agents, and sales coaching/role-play. Tavus sees this as a high-priority revenue vertical with AI vendor innovation cycles.
        - **CX/CS:** Focus on customer service training and employee onboarding. Specific examples: recruitment videos, customer service video, onboarding videos.
- **General Feedback:**
    - **"Proof" Content:** Prioritize case studies and programmatic use-case examples to educate the market on what's possible with real-time video. This is now the biggest content gap.
    - **Messaging Clarity:** Be explicit to avoid confusion with past "text-to-video" offerings, which carry SEO baggage.
    - **Programmatic Use-Case Examples:** Research programmatic targets like ElevenLabs and Vappy that have established search volume for Tavus to poach. This structure allows Tavus to say "you can use Tavus for X, Y, and Z" at scale.

### Strategic Discussion

- **Market Education as Primary Blocker:** Most buyers don't yet know they can build AI video experiences; Tavus's job is to hold their hand and introduce the category. This is different from competitors who assume category awareness.
- **Quick Revenue vs. Category Play:** Martech (RevOps, AI SDRs) is a quick-revenue vertical with existing buyer intent; category creation ("AI humans") is a 6-12 month bet that compounds defensively.
- **Internal Clarity Required at Tavus:** Several decisions require Tavus leadership input:
    1. Which category term to own (aligns with vision + has search volume).
    2. Exact scope and naming of each vertical (current feedback: L&D, Martech, CX/CS, possibly Simulation Training as a separate cluster).
    3. Validation of programmatic use-case targets (e.g., ElevenLabs, Vappy) for content partnerships.
