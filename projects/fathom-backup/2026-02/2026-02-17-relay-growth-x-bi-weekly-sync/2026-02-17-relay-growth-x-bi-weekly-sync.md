## Summary

Relay and GrowthX aligned on restructuring the blog around five core SMB categories plus dedicated Home Services and AB hubs, with plans to test vertical-depth content (e.g., HVAC accounting) across different trades. The team identified the critical attribution gap—organic traffic is flat despite rising AI-driven approvals—and will use GrowthX's AI visibility tool to connect content performance to conversions. Quick wins include refreshing legacy content (driving ~50 approvals/month), converting help center articles, and optimizing CTAs.

---

## Context

Relay is at an inflection point in its content strategy. For the first time, organic traffic has plateaued even as AI visibility improves—a signal that their content is being cited but not necessarily driving conversions. The challenge is proving ROI: they can see approvals rising from AI sources, but lack a clear path from content production to conversion. GrowthX is stepping in with two tools: (1) access to its AI visibility index to track prompts and citations, and (2) recommendations on content restructuring and optimization. Relay's team (Dave, Bethany, Gavin, Nick) is data-driven and process-focused. They track KPIs weekly and want precision on metrics before scaling investment. The conversation revealed three pain points: inconsistent SEO tool data (SEMrush vs. Ahrefs), unclear GA4 attribution, and a need to align editorial cadence (incremental volume growth) with pricing models.

## Key Topics

### The Attribution Gap

  - **Problem:** Relay cannot definitively link content performance to conversions, despite seeing AI-driven approvals rise.
  - **Context:** Organic traffic is flat for the first time, making it critical to prove content ROI.
  - **Data Challenges:**
      - **Tool Discrepancy:** SEMrush and Ahrefs data often conflict.
      - **GA4 Reporting:** Relay can share conversion percentages but needs guidance on building reports that isolate content impact.
  - **Solution:** GrowthX will provide access to its AI visibility tool to help track prompts and citations.

### New Content Strategy & Structure

  - Relay is restructuring its blog to improve content organization and SEO performance.
  - **New Structure:**
      - **5 Core SMB Categories:**
          - Cash Flow & Money Management
          - Business Banking
          - Starting & Running Your Business
          - Taxes & Accounting
          - Relay Features
      - **Dedicated Hubs:** Home Services and AB (account-based marketing).
  - **Rationale for "Starting & Running Your Business" Category:**
      - **Goal:** Build domain authority for LLMs.
      - **Method:** Publish foundational articles (e.g., "LLC vs. business license") to establish Relay as a trusted source.
      - **Significance:** This is a strategic SEO play, not a shift to targeting early-stage businesses.

### Home Services Hub Strategy

  - **Goal:** Get faster signal on content performance.
  - **Decision:** Focus on vertical depth over breadth, aligning with recent Google algorithm updates.
  - **Method:** Create deep, topic-based content (e.g., "HVAC accounting") that can be repurposed across different home services verticals (e.g., HVAC, plumbing).
  - **Next Step:** Relay and GrowthX will research and define the initial sub-topics for this approach.

### Editorial Plan & Quick Wins

  - **Editorial Mix:** 2 Home Services articles + 3 core SMB articles per week.
  - **Budget Flexibility:** Relay asked if article volume can be increased incrementally (e.g., 2–3 articles/month) instead of in 5-article bundles. GrowthX will investigate.
  - **High-Impact Opportunities:**
      - **Refresh Legacy Content:** Update high-performing articles (driving \~50 approvals/month) to deepen context and improve internal linking.
      - **Convert Help Center Articles:** Transform high-traffic support content into blog posts to capture organic search traffic.
      - **Optimize CTAs:** Request recommendations from GrowthX on improving inline and sticky CTAs.
