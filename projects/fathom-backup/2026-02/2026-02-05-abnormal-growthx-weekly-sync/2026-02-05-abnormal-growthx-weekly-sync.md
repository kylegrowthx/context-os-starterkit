## Summary

GrowthX and Abnormal pivoted the Education Hub from an external design engagement to an internal Abnormal build aligned with Web 3.0, with GrowthX providing best practices instead of wireframes. The team confirmed the content freeze is canceled, allowing continued publishing to avoid traffic dips, and aligned on a dual-lane strategy: 15 articles/week for broad, brand-agnostic education content with minimal review overhead, and 5 articles/week for campaign-aligned blog content prioritizing the Hub, AI Phishing Coach, and competitive positioning. ## Context

GrowthX and Abnormal have been collaborating on a content strategy to drive organic visibility and support product launches. A planned content freeze was intended to manage output while the Education Hub was being designed. However, Meaghan Winston announced mid-cycle that the Education Hub is now being built entirely in-house by Abnormal's team as part of a larger Web 3.0 initiative, requiring only strategic guidance from GrowthX rather than full design and development. This pivot changes the scope of GrowthX's engagement and eliminates the need to pause publishing.

January data showed strong performance from the repositioned content strategy (publishing and unpublishing decisions), particularly in core cybersecurity clusters. The team is using this momentum to launch a more aggressive forecast: a broad education lane targeting 6M+ monthly search volume in cybersecurity with 15 articles/week, paired with a narrower blog lane (5 articles/week) focused on high-intent, campaign-aligned content. The content strategy also addresses LLM visibility as a high-intent channel, with GrowthX's in-house CheckDot tool offering better ICP alignment than generic competitors like Scrunch.
