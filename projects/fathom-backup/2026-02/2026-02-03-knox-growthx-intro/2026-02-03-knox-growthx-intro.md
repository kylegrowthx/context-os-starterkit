## Key Topics

### The Problem: Zero AI Search Visibility

  - Knox's core service: Accelerating FedRAMP authorization (90 days, 90% less cost) for SaaS companies targeting the federal government.
  - **Goal:** Become synonymous with FedRAMP.
  - **Challenge:** Zero AI search visibility. A live ChatGPT demo showed Knox was completely omitted from vendor lists for the query "how do I get ramped."
  - **Opportunity:** The competitive landscape is weak. The primary competitor, Palantir, is not focused on this business group, leaving a wide-open field for Knox to dominate.

### The Solution: A Two-Part Content Engine

  - **Part 1: Foundational Content**
      - Build authority with editorial content on FedRAMP processes and benefits.
      - Focus on the core audience: CEOs, CROs, and Heads of Federal at SaaS companies ($50M–$1B revenue).
  - **Part 2: Programmatic Tools**
      - Create a tool to simplify finding government RFPs, a process that currently costs clients \~$30k via capture firms.
      - **Concept:** A search widget that pulls relevant contracts from public data (e.g., SAM.gov, USAspending.gov).
      - **Rationale:** This high-value tool would drive significant traffic and establish Knox as an indispensable partner for winning government business.

### The Partnership: GrowthX's AI Search Engine

  - GrowthX uses AI to accelerate content creation and achieve rapid search visibility.
  - **Process:**
    1.  **Contextual Artifacts:** Define brand voice and audience for AI workflows.
    2.  **Strategy:** Analyze keywords and competitor content to find "easy wins" and build authority via topic clusters.
    3.  **Execution:** Publish 5 articles per week, including programmatic content like directories and tools.
  - **Proof Points:**
      - **Augment Code:** Achieved near-GitHub visibility for key prompts.
      - **Ramp:** Built a vendor directory using internal spend data.
      - **Marcel (Founder):** Drove $17M revenue on \<$1M spend at IBM.
