## Key Topics

### Panther's New Content Strategy

- Shannon King (new Head of Marketing) outlined a two-pronged content strategy to position Panther as an AI SOC (Security Operations Center) thought leader.
- **Rationale:** Security is a "must-have" market (not nice-to-have), but highly commoditized. Differentiation depends on thought leadership and continuous education—competitors evolve constantly, and customers must stay current.
- **Two-Pronged Approach:**
    - **Layer 1 (Visionary):** High-level thought leadership from founder Jack—well-respected, proven leader in the space.
    - **Layer 2 (Technical):** Deep, practitioner-level content (tutorials, docs, comparisons, code examples) scaled by GrowthX.
- **Content Authenticity:** Must speak directly in the technical language of security engineers and developers. Shannon plans to hire a "security dev rel" to ensure this authenticity (developer turned marketer with technical credibility).
- **Past Agency Experience:** Shannon avoided agencies like Aerofs because their data-driven models felt reactive—insights came after market trends had already shifted. She wanted measurability (AI search citations share, competitive presence) but not legacy tools like Scrunch.

### GrowthX's AI-Powered Content Engine

- Tyler Pavlas (founding AE) presented GrowthX's "services as software" model, which combines AI automation with expert human oversight at every step.
- **Company Background:** Marcel (CEO, formerly at Deepgram) built a track record of AI-driven content engines. At Deepgram, he automated research, briefing, assignments, outlining, and drafting—results were impressive enough that he founded GrowthX to productize and scale this approach. Initially built on AirOps, now on custom internal platform to handle cost and complexity at scale.
- **Process Overview:**
    1. **Strategy Sprint (Phase 1):** Defines core artifacts to personalize content—ICP, writing guidelines, case studies, personas, industry context.
    2. **Keyword Analysis:** Identifies high-potential topics based on search volume, ranking difficulty, and competitor landscape.
    3. **AI Pipeline:** Generates content via agentic workflows—deep research, outlines, drafts, internal linking, fact-checking (passages broken down, deep research, suggested rewrites).
    4. **Expert Oversight:** Marketing leaders, writers, SEO specialists, and Chief Content Officer (former TechCrunch Editor-in-Chief for ~10 years) provide quality control at each step.
- **Platform Functionality:**
    - **Agentic Workflows:** Pipelines run in a loop until quality threshold is met (e.g., 32 minutes of deep research per article).
    - **Artifact Integration:** Editors inject personas, case studies, insights mid-stream to enrich content in real time.
    - **Automated Tasks:** Internal linking (cross-site), authoritative source verification, brand-compliant image generation and infographics.
    - **Flexibility:** Can scale from definitions and listicles (broad visibility) to deep technical guides, comparisons, glossaries, and custom programmatic use cases (e.g., Lovable templates library).
- **Example Success:** Augment Code went from zero AI search visibility to ranking ahead of Cursor (with GitHub behind it) in 6 months. Traffic grew 3x, paid ad spend dropped to near zero. GrowthX created technical guides, comparisons, and AI governance content with code snippets—all optimized for queries like "enterprise coding tools for context understanding."

### Measuring Success: AI Search & Pipeline Impact

- Shannon emphasized that success means engaging the *right* people at target accounts, not just maximizing traffic volume. CEOs and VCs want to know: "Are we in AI searches? What's our share? Our sentiment?"
- **AI Visibility Platform (Launching February 2026):**
    - Tracks Panther's performance in AI search results (ChatGPT, Claude, Google) for high-intent, ICP-specific queries.
    - **Metrics tracked:** Competitive presence (Are we in top 3 recommendations?), sentiment (positive/negative tone), citation share (% of responses mentioning Panther).
    - **Example queries:** "Splunk vs. Panther for AWS-heavy environments," "AI capabilities comparison of SIEM solutions," "AI SOC vendor selection guide."
    - **Seeded queries:** The platform was seeded with Panther-specific prompts a few days before the call; results refresh daily to track pickup by AI systems.
    - **Panther's baseline:** At call time, Panther was competing against mature vendors (Splunk, Datadog, SumoLogic) with years of content depth. Team advised focusing on competitive presence and sentiment, not absolute numbers.
    - **Access:** Panther will have direct access to the dashboard (vs. legacy Scrunch model where GrowthX reported on behalf of clients).
- **Pipeline Dashboard (In Development):**
    - Connects content performance directly to business outcomes: site clicks → solution page engagement → opportunities → revenue.
    - Aims to show which content types drive the right account visibility and which drive deal progression.
    - Team met with Panther's marketing ops to scope this integration.
