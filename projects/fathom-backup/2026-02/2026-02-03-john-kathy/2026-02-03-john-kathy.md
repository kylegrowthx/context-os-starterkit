  - A single, clear decision-maker is required to prevent review bottlenecks that could derail the 8-week sprint.

  - **Challenge:** John's team has multiple stakeholders (Chanel, James, Mariel) who have historically edited content, creating ambiguity.
  - **Immediate Hurdle:** Chanel's unavailability for two weeks in February for sales kickoffs (Spain, Vegas) requires a backup plan for approvals.

### Solution: Defined Roles & Workflow

  - **Primary Approver:** John will confirm with James and Chanel that Chanel will be the sole approver.
  - **Technical Review:** Daniel "Donny" Bagel (PMM, Data Security) will review the first \~8 articles for technical accuracy.
      - **Rationale:** Donny is the closest person on the team to a technical marketer and can catch inaccuracies before publication.
  - **Content Calendar Alignment:**
      - GrowthX requires access to Mariel's 2–3 month content calendar to prevent topic overlap.
      - **Proposed Division of Labor:**
          - **Mariel's Team:** Deep, proprietary content (e.g., "2026 HIPAA Guide") that leverages internal expertise.
          - **GrowthX:** Execution of the approved topic clusters.
  - **Feedback Protocol:**
      - **Method:** Use Google Docs comments/suggestions, not direct edits.
      - **Rationale:** This creates a clear, auditable trail of all changes and the reasoning behind them, which is essential for calibrating the content engine.
