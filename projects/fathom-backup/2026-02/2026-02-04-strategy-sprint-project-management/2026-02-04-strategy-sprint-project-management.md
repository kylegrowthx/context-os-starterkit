## Summary

GrowthX leadership defines new sprint operating standards: formalize EM/ME collaboration at strategy (W2) and performance review (W6), accelerate calibration to week two with RYG health flags, migrate sprint PM from Linear to a custom Notion database with HubSpot as the source of truth for client health, and establish clear PSEO pricing signals ($40k vs. $18k editorial) to eliminate mid-sprint ambiguity on scope and resourcing.

## Key Topics

### EM/ME Collaboration

  - **Problem:** EMs create content strategy in isolation, leading to tactical misalignment and missed opportunities for expert input from MEs.
  - **Solution:** Formalize EM/ME collaboration at two key points:
      - **W2: Strategy Development:** EM & ME partner on the content strategy.
          - **Rationale:** Ensures strategies are tactically feasible and grounded in client data (e.g., internal databases, building codes), preventing production issues.
      - **W6: Performance Review:** EM & ME review sprint performance (GA, GSC) against the W2 strategy.
          - **Outcome:** A data-backed summary for the W7 conversion meeting.
          - **Scope:** Must include an operational check of the content pipeline's performance.

### Calibration Process

  - **Problem:** The W3 calibration article process is a bottleneck, often taking 1–4 weeks, which delays the entire sprint.
  - **Solution:** Accelerate the process to create more buffer time.
      - **New Timeline:** Calibration article sent to client by end of W2.
      - **Health Flags:** A RYG system will track progress to enable proactive intervention.
          - **Green:** Feedback received by early W3.
          - **Yellow:** No feedback by end of W3.
          - **Red:** No feedback by W4.
      - **Topic Selection:** EMs will select a non-core, underperforming article to rewrite.
          - **Rationale:** This avoids client nitpicking on their main subject matter and provides a clear "before & after" comparison to demonstrate GrowthX's value.

### Project Management & Health Scoring

  - **Problem:** The current PM tool (Linear) is not being adopted, and there's no consistent, objective system for tracking sprint health.
  - **Solution 1: Notion for PM**
      - **Tool:** Move sprint PM to a custom Notion database built by Leah.
      - **Rationale:** Leverages an existing tool and allows for a tailored, user-friendly structure.
      - **System:** HubSpot remains the source of truth for client health; Notion will be for granular task management.
  - **Solution 2: Standardized Health Scoring**
      - **Method:** Adapt the pod team's 5-signal health score system for sprints.
      - **Rationale:** Provides a consistent, objective framework for tracking client health and forecasting conversion.
      - **Process:** Define clear criteria for each signal (1–5) and for the final RYG score.
      - **Contextual Override:** EMs must be trained to manually override the score based on external triggers (e.g., client acquisition, stakeholder changes).

### PSEO & Pricing Clarity

  - **Problem:** Ambiguity exists around delivering advanced PSEO services within the standard sprint.
  - **Solution:** Establish a clear process for PSEO-inclusive sprints.
      - **Trigger:** Higher pricing (e.g., $40k vs. $18k editorial) signals a PSEO-inclusive sprint.
      - **Action:** Teams must deliver the full scope (editorial + PSEO) from day one.
      - **Resource:** If the standard EM/ME pair is insufficient, additional resources must be assigned to ensure on-time delivery.
