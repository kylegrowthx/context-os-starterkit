## Context

Blaxel is a code-execution platform that allows developers to run code generated by LLMs. GrowthX and Blaxel have been in a 10-week content marketing engagement (concluding at the end of this week) to build organic visibility for Blaxel's value proposition. The partnership focuses on SEO-driven content that positions Blaxel within the AI/developer infrastructure space, particularly targeting communities using model hosting platforms who need a destination to run generated code. Paul Sinaï (Blaxel CEO) emphasized the need for more visionary, thought-leadership content to balance technical pieces, as AI alone cannot generate novel ideas about future infrastructure trends. The engagement transitions to Phase 2 with a new Engagement Manager joining, while the weekly cadence and content pipeline remain unchanged. Blaxel is exploring synergies between organic content and paid advertising campaigns.

## Key Topics

### Content Feedback & Strategy

  - **Comparison Articles (Moto, Daytona):**
      - Required significant revisions for accuracy (e.g., pricing) and editorial tone.
      - Now considered high-quality and nearly ready for publication.
      - Will serve as a baseline for the content workflow.
  - **LLM Hosting Articles:**
      - **Problem:** The content incorrectly positioned Blaxel as an LLM hosting provider, which is not a current feature.
      - **New Strategy:** Reposition Blaxel for *adjacent* use cases.
          - **Positioning:** Blaxel is the platform for running code *generated by* hosted LLMs.
          - **Goal:** Build awareness within the model hosting community.
  - **Visionary Content:**
      - **Request:** Paul requested more high-level, visionary content (e.g., "AI infrastructure in 5 years").
      - **Solution:** Blaxel will provide unique insights via audio recordings or sales call transcripts, as AI alone cannot generate novel ideas.
  - **Writing Guidelines:**
      - Updated to simplify voice and sentence structure based on Vikram's feedback.
      - Blaxel will review new drafts to confirm the change is effective.

### Performance & Impact

  - **Organic Search:**
      - Looker dashboard shows strong early results, on par with other GrowthX clients.
      - New content ranks on page 1–2, a positive signal of topic relevance and authority.
  - **LLM Visibility (Check That):**
      - The new Check That tool confirms content is being cited by LLMs.
      - **Example:** The "CodeSandbox alternative" post has been cited \>50 times.
      - **Significance:** This validates the content strategy's long-term impact on AI visibility.

### Process & Workflow

  - **Feedback Channel:**
      - **Decision:** Use Google Docs for all content feedback.
      - **Rationale:** Ensures immediate email notifications and avoids context-switching between tools.
  - **Author Attribution:**
      - **Decision:** Continue using real author names.
      - **Rationale:** Real authors with social media presence provide a stronger authority signal for SEO than generic personas.
  - **Artifact Maintenance:**
      - The AI pipeline's "artifacts" (knowledge base) are updated based on feedback and product changes.
      - **New Step:** A product messaging accuracy check will be added to the workflow to reduce revisions.
      - **Process:** Blaxel will share product change logs via Slack to keep artifacts current.

### Phase 2 Transition

  - **Timeline:** The 10-week initial phase concludes next week.
  - **Transition:** A new Engagement Manager will join the weekly calls to manage the long-term relationship.
  - **Continuity:** The content pipeline and weekly meeting cadence will remain unchanged.

### Future Growth Initiatives

  - **Programmatic Content:**
      - GrowthX proposed programmatic content (e.g., templates, calculators) to drive qualified traffic at scale.
      - **Blaxel's Concern:** Programmatic content that generates code could be high-maintenance.
      - **Alternative:** Blaxel is open to exploring independent side products (like Ramp's calculator library) that are not tightly coupled to the core product.
  - **Paid Ads:**
      - Blaxel asked how to articulate blog content with new paid ad campaigns.
      - **GrowthX's Role:** Use non-branded ad keywords for topic research to build a compounding organic foundation that supports paid efforts.
