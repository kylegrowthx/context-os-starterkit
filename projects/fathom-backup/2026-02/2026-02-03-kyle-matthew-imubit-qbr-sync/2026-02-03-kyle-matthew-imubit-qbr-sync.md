## Context

Matthew Alves-Hill joined the Imubit account in December and has been tasked with preparing a QBR for CRO Tim and other stakeholders. The program has published 200 articles generating ~150 clicks/week, representing 30% of Imubit's total website traffic. ## Key Topics

  - **Performance:** Matthew confirmed a recent upward trend, which he attributes to new keyword research and quick-win article refreshes.
  - **Content Audit:** A preliminary audit by Khalil confirms Matthew's suspicion of a "spray and pray" strategy in the initial 200 articles, resulting in duplicate content and poor keyword alignment.
  - **Metrics:**
      - 200 articles published → \~150 clicks/week.
      - This content drives \~30% of Imubit's total website clicks.

### New Strategy: Content Refresh

  - **Rationale:** The program has hit diminishing returns from new content. The solution is to refresh the existing library based on performance data and new business priorities.
  - **Framing:** Position this as a natural program evolution, not a negative. Kyle cited the Homebase case study, where a refresh-first strategy drove 2x growth.
  - **Client Alignment:** Imubit is already aligned on a refresh, driven by their own messaging shift from "closed loop AI as a solution" to "closed loop is a North Star."
  - **Dual Focus:** The strategy combines refresh with new content (60 assignments) to cover new verticals and messaging.

### Backlinking Performance

  - **Client Concern:** CRO Tim specifically requested a review of backlinking performance, which has not been reported on since the program began.
  - **Data Discrepancy:** Ahrefs shows a near 2x growth in referring domains, while SEMrush shows a flat trend.
  - **Action:** Matthew will use the Ahrefs data to tell a positive story, focusing on the growth in referring domains and Domain Authority. He is awaiting details from Jesus on which pages received links.

### Conversion & Attribution

  - **Client Gap:** Imubit lacks internal attribution tracking for organic pipeline, though they plan to build a dashboard and hire a paid agency in Q1.
  - **Framing:** Position low engagement as an opportunity to drive more conversions.
  - **Suggested Tactics:**
      - **Content Kits:** Bundle existing ungated assets (webinars, guides) into themed kits behind a form. This tactic has a high success rate.
      - **LLM Visibility:** Frame the refresh as an opportunity to optimize for LLM search, building on existing work (TLDRs, FAQs).
