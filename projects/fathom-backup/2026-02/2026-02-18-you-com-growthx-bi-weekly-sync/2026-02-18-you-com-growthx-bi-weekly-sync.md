## Key Topics

### Content Pipeline Blockers

  - **Primary Blocker:** A large content backlog is stalling publishing.
  - **Contributing Factors:**
      - **High Review Overhead:** Content requires extensive SME and design reviews, which are slow.
      - **Strategic Pivots:** Frequent internal strategy shifts (e.g., new competitor naming ban) invalidate written content, forcing rework.
      - **Irrelevant Backlog:** Much of the existing backlog is no longer relevant, making it inefficient to publish.
  - **Solution:** A new content hire will help manage the backlog.

### New Content Strategy & Focus

  - **Strategic Pivot:** The new focus is on top-of-funnel (ToFu) content for "AI laggards."
  - **Rationale:** This content type (e.g., "What is a web crawler?") requires minimal SME review, enabling faster publishing.
  - **Immediate Plan:**
      - **GrowthX:** Scope and build a content calendar for the new ToFu lane.
      - **You.com:** Provide a list of existing white papers to be repurposed into multiple blog posts.

### LLM Visibility & Check That

  - **Executive Priority:** Improving LLM visibility is a top executive concern.
  - **GrowthX's Role:** GrowthX will use the Check That platform to provide data-driven insights and recommendations to address this priority.
  - **Platform Demo:** Bailey demoed Check That, which tracks You.com's position, sentiment, and citations in LLMs.
  - **Key Insight:** The platform will provide data proving that comparison articles (which name competitors) are the most cited, giving Brooke leverage to challenge the naming ban.
