Brooke's new hire will help manage editorial volume, but the immediate need is to shift GrowthX's production toward lower-friction content (definitional, top-of-funnel pieces requiring minimal review) while avoiding the competitor naming trap through Check That data. Bailey introduced Check That as a tool to track You.com's position, sentiment, and citation share across LLMs—and to provide empirical proof that comparison articles (which name competitors by name) drive the most citations, giving Brooke leverage to challenge the naming ban with data.
