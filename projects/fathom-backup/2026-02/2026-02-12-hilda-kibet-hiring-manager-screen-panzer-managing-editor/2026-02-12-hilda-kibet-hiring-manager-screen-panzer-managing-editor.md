## Key Topics

### The Challenge: Scaling Content Production

  - **Problem:** Manual writing cannot scale to meet client demand.
  - **Solution:** AI pipelines automate the heavy lifting, enabling editors to manage high volume.
      - **Workflow:** Idea → Context → Deep Research → Draft → Post-processing (links, SEO, proofreading) → Publish.
  - **Human Role:** The editor's value is in providing high-quality feedback to refine the pipeline.
      - **Why:** Without a strong standard of "good," feedback is ineffective, and the pipeline cannot improve.

### The Solution: Providing the Right Context

  - **Core Principle:** High-quality AI output requires rich, specific context.
  - **Udemy Thought Experiment:** To generate a good 800-word blog post, Hilda Kibet identified key context needed:
      - Company identity (Udemy's mission)
      - Article goal/CTA
      - Style and tone
      - Relevant research/data
      - Existing content map (to avoid cannibalization)
      - **Crucial:** Audience persona (e.g., students vs. educators)
  - **Panzarino's Framework:** Our system uses three "contextual artifacts":
    1.  **Company Context:** About the client, products, and features.
    2.  **Audience Personas:** Who the content is for.
    3.  **Writing Guidelines:** How to write about the client.

### Candidate Experience & Alignment

      - **Process:** Built prompts from product/customer teams, then used Prompt Genie to refine them for the LLM.
  - **Alignment:** Hilda Kibet confirmed understanding the role's focus on AI pipeline operation, not manual writing.
