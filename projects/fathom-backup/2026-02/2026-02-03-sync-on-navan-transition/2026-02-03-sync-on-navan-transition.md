## Summary

Aida briefed Erik on the Navan account transition, covering the removal of legal review as a production bottleneck, the shift toward bottom-of-funnel comparison content, and new artifacts to support content production. Key focus areas include the AI cluster campaign and expense category keywords, with action items spanning legal compliance, client feedback, CheckThat access, and content quality improvements.

## Context

Navan, a B2B travel and expense management platform serving enterprise clients, is a GrowthX content marketing engagement focused on reducing paid search spend through organic content. The engagement started in January 2026 after a slow ramp-up due to Navan's internal complexity and legal review requirements. Aida Knezevic has been managing the relationship with Navan's content and SEO teams (Rich, Libby, Mehran) and decision-maker Nima. The sprint phase included Victor creating content and Sydney as editor. Early results show strong rankings due to Navan's high domain authority, and the removal of legal review unblocks faster content production moving into phase two.

## Relevance

- **Content Production:** New legal process bypasses review, enabling faster content scaling; requires substantiation links, internal comparison pages only, and specific disclaimer language.
- **SEO & Traffic:** Bottom-of-funnel comparison listicles are primary focus; AI cluster and expense category are priority targets; Navan's high domain authority driving page-one rankings.
- **Client Management:** Nima expects CheckThat access; content team (Rich/Libby) provides only line edits; proactive feedback solicitation needed to surface strategic concerns.
- **Measurement & Attribution:** Navan tracking content performance in Amplitude; first-touch vs. last-touch attribution model unclear; need to align on KPIs and early signals.
- **Internal Operations:** Victor's content has AI-isms and complex phrasing requiring Sydney's feedback; new German content marketer joining calls for U.S. content refresh support.
- **Competitive Context:** Brex and Ramp investing heavily in SEO; Navan avoiding association with smaller competitors (Engine); positioning as enterprise-only solution.

## Overview

- **New Legal Process:** Navan has approved a new process to bypass legal review, unblocking content production. It requires linking to internal comparison pages for competitor data and using a specific disclaimer.
- **Strategy Shift:** The focus is now on bottom-of-funnel (BoFu) content, especially comparison listicles, to directly support the client's primary goal of reducing paid search spend.
- **Content Priorities:** Current efforts target two key areas: the "AI" cluster to support a new campaign and the "Expense" category to build authority where Navan is weak.
- **Internal Tracking:** Navan's SEO lead, Mehran, is tracking content performance in Amplitude. Erik must understand their attribution model (e.g., first-touch vs. last-touch) to align on KPIs.

## Key Topics

### Client Strategy & Priorities

  - **Primary Goal:** Reduce paid search spend by capturing BoFu traffic with organic content.
  - **Content Focus:**
      - **BoFu:** Comparison listicles and vs. pages are the top priority.
      - **AI Cluster:** Supporting a new AI campaign with updated messaging.
      - **Expense Category:** Building authority with credit card-related keywords where Navan has a weak presence.
  - **Client Context:**
      - **Target Market:** Enterprise clients.
      - **Competitors:** Brex and Ramp are investing heavily in SEO; Navan avoids being seen in the same category as smaller players like Engine.
      - **Performance:** Early results show high average position rankings (often on page one) due to Navan's high domain authority.

### Content Production & Legal Process

  - **Legal Review:** Now bypassed, removing the primary production bottleneck.
  - **New Legal Requirements:**
      - Substantiate all facts with Google Doc links.
      - Do not link directly to competitor websites.
      - Use internal comparison pages as the source for competitor data.
      - Include the disclaimer: "Competitive data was collected and subject to change or update."
  - **Artifact Creation:** Create an artifact from Navan's comparison pages (e.g., screenshots) to serve as a pre-approved data source for content.
  - **Content Pipeline:**
      - **Cluster-Level Value Props/CTAs:** Programmatically inserts tailored CTAs based on content cluster.
      - **Statistics Artifact:** Populates articles with data from Navan's gated reports to build authority.

### Content Refresh Strategy

  - **Priority:** New content publication for the next month.
  - **Low Priority:** Glossary content refresh (AI-generated, awareness-focused).
  - **Future Focus:** Refreshing blog content.
      - **Data Source:** GSC clicks and GA4 demo events.
      - **Selection Criteria:** Commercial intent and significant traffic loss.
      - **Action:** Erik to update the refresh candidate list with new GSC data in \~1 month.

### Client Management & Feedback

  - **Feedback Style:** Navan's content team (Rich, Libby) provides line edits but rarely gives high-level strategic feedback.
  - **Action:** Erik must proactively solicit feedback to ensure content quality and alignment.
  - **Nima (Decision-Maker):**
      - **Expectation:** Expects free Check That access, as it was presented as part of the contract.
      - **Inquiry:** Asked about the search volume of Check That prompts; Erik should be prepared to explain the "black box" nature of LLMs and the strategy of targeting simple, BoFu queries.

### Internal Team & Content Quality

  - **Content Quality Issue:** Victor's content sometimes has "AI-isms" or overly complex phrasing.
  - **Action:** Erik to follow up with Sydney, the sprint editor, for specific feedback to improve Victor's writing before Sydney's editing role ends.
