Bailey (GrowthX) and Jason/Michael (Otto) aligned on content strategy for 2026: introduce Travel Managers as a new persona to support the Direct Travel TMC partnership, prioritize content around new product features (price protection, delegate access, Slack/Teams agents), and create deeper product demos with CTAs to drive signups. ## Context

Otto is launching a pilot program with four companies (10–3,000 travelers each, starting with 20 users) through Direct Travel, a well-established TMC partner. The partnership addresses a specific gap: employees at direct travel clients want a better experience than website self-serve or call-in, and travel managers (not end users) make the buying decision, focusing on cost savings (e.g., $70 call-in vs $20 online per transaction) and time savings. Content's role is not direct conversion but validation—prospects research Otto via Google, ChatGPT, and Claude after their TMC account manager pitches it, so Otto's presence in search and AI answers signals legitimacy.

GrowthX's content has successfully driven organic and assisted traffic, with the "travel disruption and optimization" pillar and comparison/list articles performing exceptionally well in AI visibility. However, Jason noted the content is starting to feel repetitive. The conversation centered on three levers: (1) introduce Travel Managers as a secondary persona with TMC-friendly messaging and refresh existing TMC-related posts to remove friction, (2) create feature-focused content around price protection and delegate access launching in coming weeks, and (3) test deeper product demos with screenshots/video and reusable visual CTAs (sticky right-column widgets) to move the funnel toward conversion.

## Key Topics

### 2026 Business Priorities

  - **Pilot Program:** A 20-person pilot is launching with four companies (10–3,000 travelers) to test new features.
  - **Product Roadmap:** Upcoming features create new content opportunities:
      - **Price Protection:** Automatically rebooks flights/hotels at lower prices.
      - **Delegate Access:** Enables EAs to manage travel for multiple executives.
      - **Teams/Slack Agents:** Allows direct interaction with Otto via chat apps.

### New Persona: Travel Managers

  - **Goal:** Support the Direct Travel TMC partnership by targeting Travel Managers.
  - **Rationale:** This content provides validation for TMC account managers presenting Otto to their clients.
      - **Why:** Prospects research Otto post-pitch (via Google, ChatGPT), and existing content builds trust and confirms Otto is a "real thing."
  - **Content Focus:** Educate on TMC-specific pain points and Otto's value, using their language.
      - **Key Metrics:** Cost savings (e.g., $70 call-in vs. $20 online transaction) and time savings.
  - **CTA:** A "get in touch" form, not a self-serve signup, to capture qualified leads for the sales team.

### Content Strategy & Performance

  - **Primary Goal:** Drive organic and assisted traffic leading to user signups and transactions.
  - **Performance:**
      - Traffic is trending up.
      - The "travel disruption and optimization" pillar performs well, aligning with core value prop.
      - Comparison/list articles are highly cited by AI search, boosting visibility.
  - **Challenge:** Content feels repetitive; new features offer a chance to create fresh, exciting topics.

### New Content Formats & CTAs

  - **Deeper Product Demos:** Create content showing "how Otto works" with screenshots and video.
      - **Why:** Better demonstrates value and moves beyond high-level mentions.
      - **Framing:** Connect features directly to solving user pain points.
  - **Reusable CTAs:** Test reusable, visual CTAs (e.g., sticky right-column widgets).
      - **Why:** Low-effort way to increase product engagement and track performance.
      - **Test:** Start with 3 CTAs to measure their effectiveness.
