### What Acceleration Looks Like

**Canva** went from 250 pages to 6,600 pages in a single year (2015). They 72x'd their search traffic by planting seeds aggressively. Today: 44,800+ pages, 280M monthly visitors, $100M+/month in organic value.

**GoodRx** built 30K pages around a structured database of entities (drugs, conditions, side effects). Repeatable templates + dense internal linking = 27M monthly visits worth $50M+/month.

**Square's "The Bottom Line"** operates as a cohesive publication with 6 topic hubs, multi-tag system, and consistent UX. Multiple discovery paths. Always know where you are.

<br>

## 4. The Solution: Tools + Indexes

### Tools Strategy

Internal teams have limited bandwidth—1 tool took 10 months. Engineering is ready for /tools to go live.

**Our approach:**
- 5 tools identified, ready to build
- Build in days what takes months internally
- One-up Ramp on every tool with PAQ content + fact-checking

**First 5 tools:** ChargeFinder, Expense Categories, Per Diem Calculator, Invoice Tool, +1 from competitive analysis

<br>

### Index/Directory Strategy

Directories are infinite and data-driven—quality is built into the structure.

| Tier | Focus | Examples |
|------|-------|----------|
| **1** | CFO Focus | CFO Directory for public companies. "Who is CFO of [Company]"—high volume, low competition. Direct ICP targeting. |
| **2** | Small Business | Directory by type & location. "Accountants in Austin." Infinite combinations. Capital One synergy. |
| **3** | Vertical & Salary | Salary guides by role/industry/geography. Vertical software rankings. |

**Execution clusters:** 50 pages (prove concept) → 500 pages (expand) → 3-5K pages (deploy) → Prune what doesn't work

<br>

### Hub & Spoke Architecture

Everything connects. User searches "Who is CFO of Capital One" → lands on CFO profile → links to CFO Salary Guide, Company Profile, Spend Trends article, Budget Calculator.

Every click keeps them in the Brex ecosystem. The more densely connected the web, the better everything performs.
