# Vercel Content Strategy: What We Have, What We're Building

**139,000+ keywords · 3 keyword tables · 5 topic clusters · 3 target personas**

---

## What's in the System Today

The Content OS Airtable holds Vercel's entire keyword universe across three tables:

**Editorial Keywords** (65,298 keywords): Core SEO terms spanning frontend development, deployment, frameworks, and platform features. Each tagged with search volume, keyword difficulty, CPC, and intent signals.

**AI Gateway Keywords** (70,961 keywords): The largest table. Covers AI model selection, routing, pricing, and infrastructure. This is where Vercel's fastest growth opportunity lives.

**Keywords Internal** (3,573 keywords): Vercel's own tracked terms from internal tooling. Smaller set, but high-signal because it reflects what the team already cares about ranking for.

Supporting tables track 6,824 external URLs for competitive benchmarking, plus structured tables for topic clusters, personas, assignments, target keywords, and content audit data.

---

## What We're Building From It

Three layers, each feeding the next:

### Layer 1: Topic Clusters

Five pillars that map to Vercel's product lines, competitive dynamics, and stakeholder priorities:

1. **AI Gateway & Model Traffic** (editorial): How to choose, route, and manage AI models. Thought leadership and strategy content, separate from the programmatic model pages.
2. **AI SDK & Agent Development**: Building AI-powered apps with Vercel's toolkit. Central to the 2026 growth narrative.
3. **Frontend Cloud & Platform**: The revenue foundation. Deployment, hosting, serverless, edge, performance. Composable commerce lives here too.
4. **Developer Ecosystem & Tooling**: Next.js, Turborepo, ShadCN/ui. Top-of-funnel acquisition through open-source.
5. **Competitor Comparisons & Alternatives**: Bottom-of-funnel, highest conversion value. "Vercel vs X" and migration content.

AI Gateway also runs as a separate programmatic workstream (own tab, own team, own codebase) for the ~170 template-driven model pages. That's a parallel effort, not part of these editorial clusters.

### Layer 2: Target Personas

Every keyword gets mapped to one of three personas, each representing a different stage in the buying journey:

- **Individual Developer**: Top-of-funnel. Learning frameworks, solving technical problems, evaluating tools.
- **Technical Decision Maker**: Mid-funnel. Comparing platforms, building the business case, evaluating architecture fit.
- **AI/ML Engineer**: Growth bet. Building with AI models, choosing infrastructure, making routing decisions.

### Layer 3: Prioritization Filters

Each keyword table gets custom columns that let us slice the data by what actually matters for prioritization:

- **Topic Cluster**: Which of the 5 pillars this keyword belongs to.
- **Target Persona**: Which buyer persona this keyword serves.
- **Publishing Surface**: Where this content would live (blog, /i section, stacks pages, AI gateway pages, KB).
- **Business Relevance**: How directly this keyword maps to Vercel's revenue and strategic priorities (High, Medium, Low).
- **Priority Tier**: The final call. Combines volume, difficulty, persona fit, and surface access into a simple High/Medium/Low/Skip ranking.

Custom views in Airtable then let us filter and sort across these dimensions: show me all high-priority AI SDK keywords for the Individual Developer persona that can publish on /i. That's how 139,000 keywords become a focused execution plan without needing a formula to tell us what matters.

---

## Why This Order Matters

We're not starting with "what keywords should we write about." We're starting with structure: clusters define the strategic pillars, personas define who we're writing for, and the filter columns let us sort by what actually drives impact.

The first keywords we target will be the ones sitting at the intersection of easiest difficulty, highest volume potential, and strongest cross-link advantage. Custom Airtable views make that intersection visible in one click.
