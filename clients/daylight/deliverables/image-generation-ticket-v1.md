# Daylight Blog Cover Images — New Concept & Template

**Client:** Daylight
**Priority:** High (blocking article publication)
**Source:** Feb 26 Daylight weekly sync — Maya Rotenberg (CMO)

---

## Context

We need to develop a scalable blog cover image concept for Daylight. The first batch of articles is ready to publish, but images are the blocker. Maya reviewed our initial image concepts and rejected them — they were generated based on the Daylight website/homepage style, which she considers wrong for blog imagery. She's asked for a fundamentally simpler approach.

Maya's design team is creating the first image (alert fatigue article) to unblock immediate publishing. We need to develop a repeatable template/concept for all future articles.

---

## Approved Direction: Tines Blog Style

**Reference:** https://tines.com/blog

Maya specifically approved this style. Key characteristics:
- Simple, clean background (gradient or solid color)
- Relevant imagery or icons layered on top that relate to the article topic
- Brand-aligned color palette
- Easy to produce variations at scale
- Each image feels purposeful — not decorative filler

The goal is a simple background that gives us freedom to place topic-relevant visuals on top. Maya's words: "I just want to make the background simpler so we can have more freedom to add in the relevant ones."

---

## What Maya Does NOT Want

These are explicit rejections from the call. Do not use any of these as a starting point:

1. **Do not use the Daylight website/homepage as visual inspiration.** Maya said: "You took it from the homepage, from the product page, and it is aligned with our style. I think it doesn't align with the blog image style."

2. **Do not reference the current Daylight blog images.** "Whatever we have now is not an example for anything." The existing blog images predate Maya and are not representative of the direction.

3. **No complex, abstract AI-generated imagery.** "I don't want complex images that have nothing to do with reality." and "We're trying too hard for something that doesn't mean anything."

4. **No realistic images.** "I don't need a realistic image."

5. **No unrelated icons or elements.** "I definitely don't agree with the second part. I don't want us to just put in icons that are not related. I want us to put there things that are related."

6. **No Daylight logo on the images.**

---

## What We Need to Deliver

1. **A reusable template concept** — simple branded background with space to layer topic-relevant imagery
2. **First image** — for the alert fatigue article (if Maya's team hasn't delivered theirs first)
3. **3-5 variations** — demonstrating the concept works across different article topics (MDR, threat hunting, AI SOC, etc.)

The concept must be scalable. We're producing 15 articles/month in steady state — the image team needs to be able to turn these around quickly without a custom design process each time.

## Brand Constraints

- Use Daylight's brand color palette (they have multiple colors available)
- Background should feel aligned with Daylight's brand without copying the website aesthetic
- Images should feel professional and relevant to cybersecurity/security operations

---
