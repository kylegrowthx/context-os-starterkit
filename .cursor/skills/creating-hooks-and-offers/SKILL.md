# Creating Hooks and Offers

All marketing projects start with the offer. Before creating content, campaigns, or funnels — define your offer first. Everything else is built to support it.

## Making Your Offer Stand Out

Ask yourself:
- **Can I visualize it?** If you can't see it in your mind, it won't be memorable
- **Can I falsify it?** Make claims that are objectively true or false — put your credibility on the line
- **Can nobody else say this?** Your offer should be uniquely ownable to your product/service

Frame your offer as an invitation or transformation, not a purchase. Consider never using the word "buy." Instead frame it as a donation, invitation, or opportunity. Maintain a single theme throughout the entire experience so it's memorable.

### Hook examples from successful launches:
- "We're giving away everything for free — just cover shipping"
- "Join us for [X days] of live training where we'll answer every question"
- "This isn't a purchase, it's an investment in your transformation"
- "Reserve your spot for exclusive access to [specific outcome]"

## Creating Your Offer

### Step 1: Write a Memo

Before building anything, write it down. This helps you see the holes in your thinking and clarify what you're actually selling. Write a letter to yourself or a business partner — don't start watching videos, looking at competitors' landing pages, or talking to AI first. Successful offers often require 10-15+ internal memos before finalizing. WAFM rule: Write a Freaking Memo before any meeting about your offer.

### Step 2: Frame the Transformation

Instead of saying "buy this," frame it as an invitation or transformation. Ask: What's the current attitude of the consumer, and what's the desired attitude? The job of marketing is to make selling easier. Pre-frame everything using content, ads, and communication to get people pre-interested, predisposed, and pre-motivated before you ever pitch. Create pain and show proof beforehand — don't tell them what it is first, build the desire first.

- **Current State → Desired State:** "From struggling with [problem] to mastering [solution] in [timeframe]"
- **Identity Shift:** "Stop being someone who [negative behavior], become someone who [positive identity]"
- **Pain to Pleasure:** "Eliminate [specific frustration] and finally experience [specific benefit]"

### Step 3: Make It Simple

If someone who's had two drinks in the back of an Uber can't understand your offer instantly, it's too complex. If people need 15 minutes to understand what you're selling, your offer isn't clear enough. Stop stacking features — people already expect the value stack and are "clenching their butt cheeks" waiting for the pitch. Do something different instead.

- "Get [specific result] in [timeframe] using [simple method]"
- "The [Number]-Step System to [Outcome]"
- "Learn how to [transform problem] without [common objection]"

### Step 4: Name Your System

Give your methodology a specific name. This makes it more valuable and memorable. Example: Instead of "mega open houses," call it the "4-1-2 Open House Formula" (4 days prior, 1 day of, 2 days after). Naming your system lets clients compare you to competitors who don't have a named methodology.

- **Number-Based:** "The 3-5-7 Method," "The 10X Framework," "4-Step Blueprint"
- **Acronym-Based:** "The SCALE System," "The RAPID Method," "The CLEAR Process"
- **Unique Descriptor:** "The Magnetic Offer Formula," "The Bulletproof Postcard System"

## Validation: Why, What, How, Now

When presenting your offer:
- **Why:** Why is this important right now?
- **What:** What is the 30,000-foot view of what this is?
- **How:** How are you going to actually do it?
- **Now:** What should they do next?

## Testing Your Offer

Test different variations and validate what resonates. Put out multiple versions and see which gets traction before investing heavily. If your ads aren't converting or people aren't signing up, the problem is usually that your offer isn't valuable enough — make the thing more valuable first before spending more on traffic.

Think in terms of volume — work backwards from your goal and ask what would make it unreasonable for you to fail:
- To get 100 webinar attendees → Need 10,000 leads
- To sell out 500-person event → Need 50,000 email touches and 200+ content pieces
- To generate 50 sales calls → Need 5,000 ad impressions with compelling hook

## High-Converting Hook Patterns

- "How [authority figure] generated [impressive result] in [short timeframe] using [unique method]"
- "The [counterintuitive approach] that [specific outcome] without [common struggle]"
- "Why [common belief] is killing your [desired outcome] (and what to do instead)"
- "[Number] ways to [achieve result] that [target audience] are using right now"
- "Stop [negative behavior]. Start [positive behavior]. Get [specific result]."
