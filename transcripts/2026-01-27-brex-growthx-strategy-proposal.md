## Summary

GrowthX presented a strategic proposal to help Brex achieve their 136% lead growth target. The meeting focused on three main areas: improving the overall web experience across Journal/Resources sections, launching new content clusters (tools and templates), and addressing editorial quality issues. Brex's acquisition by Capital One adds urgency to establishing defensible content assets. Key outcome: agreement to prioritize tools/templates launch, unify content experience across ~7,800 pages, and potentially add Pride Co site redesign as a separate workstream.

## Context

Brex has aggressive growth goals requiring 2-3x traffic increase. Their spend trends section has performed well, but Journal and Resources sections have declined in traffic and user experience quality. GrowthX is proposing a shift from "supercharging existing successes" to an aggressive growth mindset.

Brex was recently acquired by Capital One for ~$5 billion, creating uncertainty but also opportunity. Jon Kowieski leads SEO at Brex with a small team and limited engineering resources to build the tools he's prioritized for six months.

## Relevance to GrowthX

This meeting represents a pivotal moment in the Brex engagement. GrowthX needs to:

1. **Prove editorial quality** — Jon rated current content output at 6/10 and flagged hallucinations, repetitive citations, and unnatural chunking
2. **Earn trust to expand scope** — Tools and templates are the priority lane; success here unlocks more autonomy
3. **Demonstrate speed** — Jon values velocity over perfection; wants things "out the door"
4. **Unlock the Pride Co opportunity** — A secondary site that could become GrowthX's content playground

The feedback on content quality is direct: "If I'm picking it up in the editing process, it's just taking up my time." GrowthX must systematize Jon's quality standards or risk the engagement shrinking.

## Key Topics

### Traffic Analysis and Content Performance

- Spend trends section: 3x traffic growth since October 2024, consistent traffic per page
- Journal and Resources: declining traffic, poor user experience, inconsistent design
- Two pages (Vision Statement examples, Profit Margin) account for nearly 50% of traffic drop
- Pages lack basic elements: no author, no date, no table of contents, no CTAs, no cross-linking

### Web Experience Unification

- Proposal to create consistent patterns across ~7,800 pages (Journal, Resources, Spend Trends)
- Jon tried this initiative 8-9 months ago but got shut down by leadership
- Alex confirmed leadership barriers are no longer an issue
- Existing Figma designs may be available to accelerate execution

### New Content Clusters

- **Tools and Templates:** Priority launch, viewed as "playgrounds" for experimentation
- **Index/Directory:** Lists like "Top CFOs at Fortune 500 companies" — Jon likes the concept but warns about keyword cannibalization
- **Programmatic Content:** Goal is 20-30 pieces per week in sandbox environment for rapid learning

### Technical SEO Issues

- Missing canonical tags on Stacker articles causing ranking drops
- Some pages wiped from rankings due to this issue
- Product pages performing well; banking pages eliminated for certain keywords

### Editorial Quality Concerns

- Content rated 6/10 by Jon
- Specific issues: hallucinations, same source cited 8 times in one article, unnatural chunking
- Google algorithm update penalizing chunked content — Jon says "please do not chunk"
- Keyword cannibalization: team suggested keywords Brex already ranks for (sometimes position 3)

### Pride Co Opportunity

- Separate site in old CMS, 42 pages
- High priority for Jon — wants it turned into a content hub
- Has backlinks from TechCrunch (valuable for new site authority)
- Could be GrowthX's "playground" for content experimentation
- Risk: may be sold off if not acted on quickly

### Capital One Acquisition Impact

- Creates uncertainty about strategic direction
- Jon analyzing Capital One and Discover SEO (considers both poor)
- Thinking about how to integrate Capital One positively into content
- Company-wide meeting February 10-13; Jon unavailable that week
