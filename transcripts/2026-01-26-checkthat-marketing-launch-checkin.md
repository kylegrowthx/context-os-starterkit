## Context

## Key Topics

### Launch Strategy and Early Access
The team will offer early access to select community members, capping usage at 50 free prompts per account. Marcel plans to personally reach out with pre-created accounts, offering free months and 30-minute strategy sessions (valued at ~$1,000) to high-profile users. This approach balances cost control with high-value engagement to validate messaging before wider launch.

### Product Positioning
During a session with Quran, the concept of "buyer intent graph for AI" clicked as a positioning framework. This captures tracking 5,800 brands across 172 categories using 1.6 million AI responses monthly. Current homepage and marketing assets lack this clarity—"index" sounds cold, "graph" may be too technical. Marcel needs to finalize crisp positioning before Wednesday's video recording.

### Promotional Video
Video recording is scheduled for Wednesday, with a rough cut by end of Thursday and finalization on Friday. The video will open with user pain points (navigating 170+ AI tools) and frame the product's unique value. Jason secured an agency experienced with launches (worked with Gamma) to handle curated outreach and newsletter blasts.

### Organic Growth Challenges
Traffic has plateaued at ~150 clicks per week with impressions dropping by 1,000. Marcel's goal: 2,000 clicks in January, 5,000 the following month. At current pace, organic would deliver only 4 paying customers per year. Jason believes the issues are site newness signals and content quality—pages rank initially then drop off, indicating quality problems.

### Content Quality vs. Site Structure
Marcel pushed for more cross-linking between brand pages, alternatives, and comparisons. Currently, many mentions aren't clickable, category pages lack descriptions, and related brands don't link to each other. Jason ran an experiment rewriting 10 pricing pages—they immediately showed traffic recovery, validating the quality hypothesis. The debate: should alternatives pages be editorial listicles (better for SEO authority) or dynamic tables (better user experience)?

### Category Expansion
Current 178 categories feel "a couple orders of magnitude too small." Marcel has a list of 200 new subcategories to create, each with 10-20 relevant prompts. This would expand the monthly prompt index from 11,000 to 14-15,000—a manageable cost increase for significantly better user experience.

### Indexing Problems
Of 300+ pricing pages published, only 30 are indexed after weeks. This suggests something beyond typical SEO timing—possibly sitemap issues, backlink quality, or content quality signals. Indexly flagged backlink quality as a concern. Manual daily submissions to Indexly helped in early January (300 pages indexed in one week) but has slowed to 50 pages/week.

### Resource Constraints and Execution
Marcel expressed frustration that all three growth levers (pipeline, community, CheckThat) feel stalled. Jason acknowledged CheckThat needs more resources—Carrie is transitioning off, Talal is firefighting other issues, and Nika left. The team agreed to document concrete bottlenecks, resource needs, and weekly experiment plans with clear escalation paths.
