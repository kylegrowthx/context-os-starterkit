## Key Topics

### SEO Indexing Issues Across Accounts

Cresta has severe indexing problems. Only 3 of 10 new blogs have been indexed despite the site being established. Carly reviewed the robots.txt and found nothing blocking crawlers, but the configuration may waste crawl budget by indexing everything. Ada escalated to Talal but hasn't received a response. This is keeping her up at night—Cresta is in week 8 and needs to make a conversion decision.

Ramp faces similar issues (12 of 30 indexed) but the team identified the cause: a Cloudflare setting was blocking AI crawlers. They've fixed robots.txt and plan internal linking improvements. Client feedback remains positive but cautious.

### Client Value Proposition and Conversion

Ella raised a pattern emerging in renewals: clients ask "Why can't I use someone else for 50% of the cost?" The team identified three pillars of differentiation:
1. AI-powered scale with pipelines and tooling
2. Cross-client pattern recognition for proactive best practices
3. Future-forward marketing expertise

Warning signs to watch for: multiple external vendors appearing in client channels, CEO disengagement from calls, aggressive board pressure on financial results. When these appear, proactively reinforce GrowthX's differentiation.

### Brex Account Deep Dive

Brex is the most demanding account. John is a full-stack marketer who spends 30-40 minutes editing each piece—mostly on internal linking. Every internal link must support SEO with keyword-relevant anchor text and varied linking patterns. He flagged "chunking" (one paragraph per H3 section) as an obvious AI signal to avoid.

Ada is creating a case studies artifact from Brex's proprietary data to add information gain. The team discussed what would make Brex commit long-term: beyond quality, they need to position GrowthX as experts John can learn from, making him look smarter and more capable.

### Check That Platform Introduction

Jason Gong demoed Check That, the replacement for Scrunch. Key differentiators:
- Public index of 100,000+ prompts (competitors charge $500 for 200 prompts you pick yourself)
- Deep coupling with GrowthX's platform ecosystem (Atlas, content optimization)
- Built from actual GrowthX client work, not generic tooling

Three views: Overview (presence tracking), Prompts (custom + category prompts), Sources (cited content). Feedback channels: "Ask Check That" Slack channel for quick questions, Linear tickets for deeper UI improvements.

### Process Integration for Check That

The team discussed when to introduce Check That in the Sprint workflow:
- **For prospects:** Set up before contract signing (Tyler's job)
- **At kickoff:** Ensure client is properly categorized to seed relevant prompts
- **Week 1-2:** Add custom prompts based on kickoff insights
- **Week 2:** Show baseline with category-level data (early "wow moment")
- **Week 4:** Full presentation with customized prompt tracking

Ada will document her proposed process and stress test it with Jason. Marisol will integrate into Sprint documentation.
