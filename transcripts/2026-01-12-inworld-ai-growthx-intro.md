## Key Topics

### 1. Inworld's Marketing Gap

Inworld has a world-class technical product but underdeveloped GTM. Florin's bottlenecks:
- No marketing expertise internally to define what needs to be done
- No execution capacity even for things they do understand
- Can't wait for a VP of Marketing hire to start fixing the problem

### 2. GrowthX Model Overview

Marcel explained the growth engine approach:
- Map topics and questions the audience cares about
- Prioritize based on search volume, competition, and opportunity
- Execute daily with AI workflows + human editors
- Monitor signals and continuously optimize

The 8-week strategy sprint starts at $15k/month. Publishing begins by week 4, daily output by weeks 5-6. 80% of clients continue to 12-month contracts.

### 3. Competitive Analysis: 11 Labs

Marcel shared detailed analysis on 11 Labs' content strategy:
- Grew from 400 pages (Jan 2024) → 1,100 pages (Jan 2025) → 4,400 pages (now)
- 3M+ monthly visits
- Aggressive programmatic + editorial approach
- Target long-tail intents (e.g., "convert MP4 to MP3") to build domain authority
- Then leverage that authority to rank for high-intent enterprise keywords

### 4. Client Success Stories

**Augment Code:**
- Went from <1% to 17% AI visibility on category prompts
- Now nearly on par with Cursor for queries like "best coding tools for enterprises"
- Content GrowthX created is being cited in ChatGPT answers

**Lovable:**
- Templates library + weekly guides
- Hundreds of signups converted to paid plans
- Near ROI break-even by month 4
- Doubled scope this month

**VAPI:**
- Created use case pages programmatically (voice AI for wellness clinics, etc.)
- Hundreds of pages driving organic traffic

### 5. Distribution as a Moat

Marcel emphasized that in the voice AI space:
- APIs are too easy to swap—customers can switch anytime
- Brand and word of mouth alone won't win forever
- Sales-led only is expensive
- Distribution (organic content) is a sustainable moat alongside continued model improvement
