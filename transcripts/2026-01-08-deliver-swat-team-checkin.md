This creates a chokehold on growth. The team also needs better client value communication, publishing automation, and project management tooling.

## Context

Weekly check-in for the Deliver SWAT team focused on operational improvements across Sprint and Pod teams. The meeting addressed structural blockers preventing GrowthX from scaling toward its $50M revenue target.

## Relevance to GrowthX

This meeting directly addresses GrowthX's core operational challenges:
- Sprint team structure is blocking growth capacity
- Client renewals are at risk due to poor value communication
- Engineering resources need reallocation to automate publishing
- EM role definition needs clarity to improve client outcomes

## Overview

- **Team Restructuring:** Need to free Ada from content quality control to enable strategic focus and growth towards $50 million next year
- **Quality Oversight Role:** Proposed separate roles for strategic partnership and quality control to streamline processes and enhance editorial governance
- **Client Renewal Focus:** Improving renewals requires linking content to client goals and enhancing communication of value achieved
- **Publishing Automation:** Aiming to automate 70-80% of publishing tasks to reduce manual workload and improve scalability
- **Project Management Tools:** Evaluating best options (HubSpot, Vitally, or others) for team coordination, with Pot HQ implementation for immediate needs

## Key Topics

### Sprint Team Structure and Editorial Quality

Ada currently acts as both editorial gatekeeper and strategic EM, creating a systemic bottleneck. Matthew proposed documenting Ada's processes into runbooks and splitting her role into two: one focusing on strategic client partnership and one on granular quality control.

The team discussed creating a "team lead" quality ombudsman role on the Sprint team to oversee ME work. This person would identify issues as process problems, artifact problems, or engineering problems and escalate accordingly. Editorial expertise matters more than technical Atlas skills for this role.

### Renewals and Client Value Communication

Renewal risks arise because teams focus on "checking boxes" rather than linking content to client goals. The team needs programmatic client results reporting and frameworks for EMs to discuss value. Marcel's direct involvement drives success, but the broader team struggles to replicate this.

Matthew pushed for honest self-evaluation: What percentage of clients see material growth? The company does a lot, but is it actually working? Delivering value isn't enough—communicating that value clearly is critical to renewals.

### Publishing Automation

70-80% of publishing tasks could be automated through workflows connected to client CMSs. Suleiman is currently the single person handling all publishing and quality checks. Engineering resources need prioritization to build per-client automations.

### Project Management Tooling

The team evaluated options: HubSpot (accountability and account updates), Vitally (project management), Linear (currently used but not preferred), and Trello (William's personal choice). Leah will provide a Vitally recommendation by Monday. Pot HQ will roll out Tuesday as an interim solution.

### EM Role Evolution

The future EM profile may require different skills than current team members have. Directors were originally hired as senior EMs with marketing experience, but many lacked external client exposure. William represents the ideal EM profile. Carly (new hire with 10 years SEO and account management) will test whether this profile works.
