## Part 1: Foundations

### The 3-Second Rule

You have **3 seconds** to stop someone from scrolling. Your first 2 lines (what appears before "see more") determine whether your post lives or dies.

**Key stats:**
- 90% of your post's performance depends on your hook
- The first 60-90 minutes determine 70% of your reach
- Comments are weighted 8x more than likes by the algorithm

### Why Hooks Matter

On LinkedIn, you're competing with:
- Thousands of posts in your audience's feed
- Notifications, messages, and distractions
- The natural urge to keep scrolling

Your hook must create enough curiosity that clicking "see more" feels mandatory.

### The LinkedIn Algorithm (2024-2025)

**What gets rewarded:**
- **Dwell time** — How long users spend on your post (61+ seconds = 15.6% engagement rate vs 1.2% for 0-3 seconds)
- **Comments** — Real conversation, not emoji reactions
- **Saves** — 10x more valuable than likes
- **Native content** — Carousels, text, video outperform links by 1.9x

**What gets suppressed:**
- Engagement bait ("Like if you agree!")
- External links in the post body
- AI-generated content (30% less reach)
- Off-topic content (stay consistent for 90+ days)
- Posting about unrelated subjects

### The Psychology of Effective Hooks

Great hooks trigger one or more of these:

| Trigger | Why It Works | Example |
|---------|--------------|---------|
| **Curiosity gap** | Brain craves closure | "The advice everyone gives is wrong." |
| **Self-interest** | "What's in it for me?" | "3 mistakes costing you $10K/month" |
| **Emotion** | Bypasses rational filter | "I got fired 2 months after getting married." |
| **Specificity** | Concrete = credible | "How I went from $0 to $1M ARR in 18 months" |
| **Controversy** | Creates tension | "LinkedIn Premium is a waste of money." |
| **Social proof** | Others validate | "This post got me 4 investor intros in 48 hours." |

## Part 3: Post Structure Frameworks

### The SLAY Framework (Lara Acosta)

1. **S**tory — Share a personal insight or experience
2. **L**esson — Identify the problem clearly
3. **A**ctionable — Use lists for clarity and next steps
4. **Y**ou — Address the reader directly

### The 6-Part Post Structure

1. **Hook** — Stop the scroll (1 line)
2. **Re-hook** — Add curiosity with parentheses or clarification
3. **Lead** — Tell readers what they're learning
4. **Body** — Deliver the value
5. **Power ending** — 2-line summary for impact
6. **CTA** — Direct action or question

### The Transformation Framework

1. **Before** — Where you/they started (struggle)
2. **Turning point** — What changed
3. **After** — The result or lesson
4. **Takeaway** — What the reader can apply

### Jasmin Alić's 7 Rules

1. Always make hooks one-liners
2. First 3 lines should outline your entire post
3. Use numbered listicles (good for SEO)
4. Never start with "stop," "don't," or "quit"
5. Add signposting (social proof like follower counts)
6. Keep copy short and choppy
7. End with a question or P.S.

## Part 5: The Engagement Checklist

### Before You Post

- [ ] **Hook test:** Would someone click "see more" based on lines 1-2?
- [ ] **Specificity test:** Are there numbers, names, or concrete details?
- [ ] **Value test:** What does the reader gain by reading this?
- [ ] **Length test:** Is line 1 under 62 characters? Is the hook one line?
- [ ] **Avoid test:** No engagement bait, no external links in body, no AI-generated smell

### Post Structure

- [ ] Strong hook (first 1-2 lines)
- [ ] Clear value promise
- [ ] Scannable format (short paragraphs, lists, spacing)
- [ ] Specific examples or data
- [ ] CTA question at the end

### After Posting

- [ ] Respond to every comment in the first 60-90 minutes
- [ ] Don't edit the post (resets the algorithm)
- [ ] Save engagement data to track what works
- [ ] Note patterns in high-performing posts

## Part 6: Anti-Patterns (What to Avoid)

### Hook Killers

| Don't | Why | Instead |
|-------|-----|---------|
| Start with "I'm excited to announce..." | Boring, self-focused | Lead with the insight or result |
| Use vague openings | No curiosity hook | Be specific and concrete |
| Multi-line hooks | Harder to read, less punchy | Keep hooks to one line |
| Start with "Stop," "Don't," "Quit" | Negative frame loses readers | Reframe positively |
| External links in post | Algorithm suppresses | Put links in comments |

### Engagement Bait (Penalized by Algorithm)

- "Like if you agree!"
- "Comment YES below"
- "Drop a 🙌 if this resonates"
- "Share this with someone who needs it"
- Asking directly for engagement without offering value

### Content That Underperforms

- AI-generated posts (30% less reach)
- Off-topic content (stay consistent 90+ days)
- Single images (underperform text by 30% in 2026)
- Self-promotional content without insights
- Posts that don't invite discussion
