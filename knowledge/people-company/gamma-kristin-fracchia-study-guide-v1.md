## Part 1: Gamma — The Company

### The Origin (2020–2022)

Grant Lee was living in London in 2020, drowning in advisory work and slide decks. He reached out to two former Optimizely colleagues — James Fox and Jon Noronha — still based in San Francisco. All three agreed: the presentation format was broken.

They raised a $7M seed in October 2021 and spent three years building a PowerPoint alternative. By late 2022, the result was brutal: 60,000 users, cash burning fast, maybe 12 months of runway. An investor had called the idea "the dumbest idea he had ever heard."

The market had spoken. Nobody wanted another slide tool.

### The AI Pivot That Changed Everything (March 2023)

In March 2023, Gamma integrated generative AI. The timing was terrifying — two weeks before the biggest launch of their company, regional banks were collapsing. Grant was sitting in a two-bedroom apartment, "not knowing if we could even make payroll."

They launched anyway. The results were immediate and explosive: 3 million users in 3 months. 10 million within 9 months.

The product shifted from "alternative to PowerPoint" to "turn rough ideas into polished presentations in minutes." Gamma solved the first mile (idea to draft) and let users handle the last mile (editing and polishing). That removed the highest-friction moment entirely.

### How They Built It Differently

Gamma reached $100M ARR profitably with 50 people and $23M in initial funding. Here's what made it work:

**Tiny team, high leverage.** All 10 original employees are still at the company five years later. A 50-person team moved like a 200-person team because AI was their "human accelerant" — not longer hours, different workflows. They spent zero dollars on paid advertising.

**Design as moat.** One-third of the early team were designers. Good design created delight, delight created sharing, sharing created growth, growth created retention. Activation unlock + virality + taste was the real moat.

**Product-led virality.** Every Gamma deck was designed to be shared. Every shared deck had a "Made with Gamma" watermark. Each user created more users. The loop: freemium → instant output → emotional "wow" → habit → conversion.

**Multi-model orchestration.** Instead of building their own AI models, Gamma orchestrates best-in-class external models — Perplexity for outlines, Claude for reasoning, GPT-4 for visuals. When better models emerge, they swap in days while competitors spend months rebuilding.

**Experimentation DNA.** From their Optimizely roots, the team built a culture where everything is tested: models, prompt strategies, layout patterns, onboarding flows, upgrade nudges.

### Career Arc

Kristin's public profile indicates she started in academia — 15+ years teaching high school and university, plus independent college admissions counseling. She earned an excellence in teaching award from the University of California. Her free online content and YouTube videos on test prep and admissions reached 6 million+ views across 125+ countries. She moved into tech through education — with stints at Magoosh (content marketing), then at companies like HashiCorp and Chegg. She calls herself an "unabashed generalist and lifelong learner."

She joined Gamma around late 2024 as Head of Marketing. She runs ultramarathons (50–100 miles).

### What We Learned First-Hand (FTF Podcast, Feb 2026)

From the actual conversation, Kristin came across as:

**Deeply intellectually curious.** She thinks in first-principles terms about hiring, interviewing, and skill assessment. During the conversation, she articulated a philosophy of intellectual curiosity as the core trait she hires for — candidates who are comfortable with the unknown, have solved varied problems, and light up when faced with unexpected challenges. She and Marcel discussed building confidence in non-tech hires and preserving taste in the age of AI.

**A writer who discovered writing late.** She shared that she was "always really insecure about my writing" but learned at HashiCorp that writing was essential. Her reframe: "Writing is just my personal tool to organize my thoughts. If I can write and organize my thoughts, then my thoughts will be better." She now sees writing as a key assessment criterion for hiring — even for engineers.

**Passionate about AI education.** She raised a major point: "There's no AI university for knowledge workers. We're expecting hundreds of millions of people to completely retool themselves... and there's no curriculum for this." She feels an obligation to share how Gamma works internally to help fill that gap through community education and workshops.

**Values deep thinking over rehearsed confidence.** She dislikes the traditional "come in prepared, be confident" interview advice. She wants candidates who pause, think in first principles, and aren't afraid to say "I don't know." In her view, overconfident answers are risky when the world is changing daily — confidence without thinking is exactly what LLMs already do well.

### Current Focus at Gamma

Head of Marketing. Leads a 3-person, AI-native marketing team. Championed the "always on" creator strategy that became Gamma's primary growth engine alongside PLG.

Her current passions: AI education for knowledge workers, community workshops, and mentoring early career leaders. She's actively thinking about the AI literacy gap as a systemic problem.

## Part 3: The Marketing Playbook

This is the most tactically interesting section for a GrowthX conversation.

### The Creator Engine

Kristin built Gamma's creator marketing into a systematic, always-on program — not a campaign-based approach.

**Scale:** 6 agencies managing 150+ creators across TikTok, Instagram, LinkedIn, and YouTube. Daily content, not monthly bursts.

**Budget allocation:** 70% of creator spend goes to micro-influencers (10K–100K followers) for higher engagement rates. Remaining 30% reserved for mega-influencers during product launches.

**Onboarding:** In the early days, Grant Lee personally onboarded creators 1:1 on Zoom. This created deep relationships and authentic understanding of the product.

**Collaboration over transactions:** Top creators contribute product feedback and campaign ideas. Gamma runs surveys through their agencies to refine messaging. A key finding: technical AI specs underperform vs. outcome-focused storytelling.

Kristin's framing: "We've seen how powerful creator-led storytelling can be. When someone explains Gamma in their own voice — through their own use case — it clicks in a way no brand ad ever could."

**Results:** In Q2 2025 alone, 1.36M+ total impressions across 36 LinkedIn and newsletter posts. Strongest traction in AI, productivity, and solopreneur niches.

### The "Tiny Team" Thesis

Kristin's signature talk (given at CMO Alliance's AI for Marketers Summit, HubSpot's "Tiny Teams, Big Results" workshop, and the AIxMarketing Summit) makes the case that the fastest-growing AI companies — Cursor, Midjourney, Bolt, Lovable, Gamma — all achieved unprecedented growth with teams well under 50 people.

Her version: Gamma scaled to 50M+ users with a 3-person marketing team by being AI-native from day one. The team uses AI as a force multiplier across content creation, campaign management, and analytics — not as a cost-cutting tool but as a capability amplifier.

### PLG + Creator = The Full Loop

Gamma's growth engine is a two-part flywheel:

1. **Product-led virality** — Every shared deck is a marketing asset. "Made with Gamma" watermark creates discovery. Collaboration features drive internal team expansion.

2. **Creator-led awareness** — Creators demonstrate the product in context, driving new signups who then enter the PLG loop.

Neither piece works alone. PLG without awareness stalls at early adopters. Creator marketing without a viral product is a leaky bucket. Together, they compound.

## Part 6: First-Hand Insights (FTF Podcast, Feb 26, 2026)

> Source: [Full transcript](../../records/transcripts/2026-02-26-ftf-podcast-kristin-fracchia-transcript.md) | [Meeting notes](../../records/meetings/2026-02/2026-02-26-ftf-podcast-kristin-fracchia.md)

These insights come directly from a ~95 minute podcast conversation between Marcel and Kristin on Feb 26, 2026. They update, correct, and deepen the web-research-based sections above.

### Company Updates (Not in Public Sources)

**Team size clarification:** ~70 employees as of Feb 2026 (up from ~20 when Marcel joined in early 2025). The 1:1M user-to-employee ratio is a deliberate choice, not a constraint.

**Profitability positioning:** Marcel explicitly positions Gamma alongside Stripe as "the most stable companies in AI right now that are profitable." This is used in recruiting and brand messaging, not just investor relations.

**Series B campaign (Nov 2025) — full breakdown:** Four deliberate bets, all built on 12 months of always-on infrastructure:
1. Employee lens-signing video (pivoted to unicorn concept)
2. Inflatable unicorn office takeover (entire office in costumes; one of LinkedIn's most-viewed videos)
3. Rainn Wilson celebrity video (cold-emailed agent, negotiated via ChatGPT, 10M views)
4. Las Vegas Sphere advertising (hacked template-based pricing with custom motion design)

**World tour (March 2026):** Product launch March 17; user conferences in South Korea, London, São Paulo over 10 days. Driven by the insight that 80%+ of users (including paying) are outside the US.

**Claude MCP integration (Spring 2026):** Gamma will integrate with Claude via MCP connector — users can generate presentations from within Claude without context-switching. Marcel describes this as "a huge unlock."

**Remix / Templates:** Internal feature called "remix" (branded as "create from template") — generate presentation variations from existing templates. Speeds team-level content creation dramatically.

### Kristin's Marketing Philosophy (In Her Own Words)

**Zone defense, not man-to-man.** Three zones: brand (breaking through noise), product marketing (clarity on what you do), growth (reaching people). Pick the zone where you're strongest and be top 1% at it. You can't cover everything.

**Pick your one thing.** "Eighty ninety percent of your impact is gonna come from the one thing that only you can do really well right now." Gamma's spike: brand + creative (leveraging their design-first culture).

**Always-on infrastructure enables big moments.** The Series B campaign didn't start from scratch — it ramped up plays they'd been running all year. 300–350 creators/influencers per month as baseline; major launches just dial up.

**Marketing to AI and humans simultaneously.** Both skillsets matter. Team members spike toward one but understand both. The best leaders are "good enough" in both technical (AEO, LLMs) and creative (events, brand, taste).

### Hiring Insights

**Two profiles Kristin targets:**
1. Senior talent from bigger companies who are "done with the old way" — backed by side projects, AI tinkering, genuine enthusiasm for building differently
2. Non-tech transitioners with problem-solving aptitude and intellectual curiosity — assessed through curveball questions, not résumé credentials

**The unicorn whiteboard test:** Mid-interview, switch the scenario to something absurd ("unicorns are buying your product") — the candidates who light up and engage are the ones who'll thrive.

**Anti-framework stance:** "I'm pretty violently opposed to any frameworks right now. How can you have a framework that works two months later?"
