## The System

This is the operating manual for Marcel's calendar. Not theory — rules.

- **Morning person.** Peak cognitive output is 7–10am. That's when the best thinking happens. Protect it ruthlessly.
- **Energy over time.** Match work type to energy level. Don't fight biology.
- **Meetings are expensive.** A meeting dropped in the middle of focus time is the worst sin. Cluster them. Never scatter.
- **Don't mix cadences.** Internal and external meetings don't belong on the same day.
- **Recovery is load-bearing.** Close-out time, family time, and flex time aren't optional. They hold everything else up.

For the science behind these rules, see [CEO Schedule & Energy Optimization](ceo-schedule-energy-optimization-study-guide-v1.md). For EA/agent operational detail, see [EA Operating Guide](ea-operating-guide-study-guide-v1.md).

## Four Settings

The calendar changes based on where Marcel is. Each setting has different rules.

| Setting | Typical Days | What Works | What Doesn't |
|---------|-------------|------------|--------------|
| **Home** | Mon, Wed, Fri | Deep work, video calls, async, writing, building | In-person collaboration, whiteboarding |
| **Office** | Tue, Thu | In-person collab, whiteboarding, team energy, networking | Deep solo work (too many interruptions) |
| **Moving** | As needed | Audio calls, podcasts, voice memos, light processing | Deep work, video calls, anything needing a screen |
| **Traveling** | Varies | Events, client visits, in-person meetings | Normal schedule — traveling gets its own calendar |

**Default week:** Home Mon/Wed/Fri. Office Tue/Thu. Override only when there's a compelling reason for in-person on a different day.

**Moving** covers commuting to/from office, traveling to in-person meetings outside office, and local events. Use transit time for phone calls, audio content, and voice-to-text processing.

**Traveling** throws out the ideal week entirely. EA/agents build a trip-specific brief and calendar.

## The Rules

Non-negotiables. Agents and EA enforce these without asking.

1. **No meetings before 10am** (except Wednesday). Mornings are for peak work.
2. **No meetings after 3:30pm** (any day). Need 30 minutes to close out before family time.
3. **Family time: 4:30pm weekdays, 3:00pm Fridays.** Non-negotiable.
4. **Leave office by 3:45pm** on office days. No meetings past 3:30pm.
5. **Never split a focus block with a meeting.** If there's a 3-hour deep work block, don't drop a 30-min call in the middle. Move it to a meeting cluster or reschedule.
6. **Don't mix internal and external meetings** on the same day. One cadence per day.
7. **Every meeting has an agenda.** No agenda = declined.
8. **25/50-min defaults.** Not 30/60. Built-in buffer between meetings.
9. **Async first.** If it can be a doc, Slack thread, or Loom — it's not a meeting.
10. **Never cancel 1:1s.** Reschedule is fine. Canceling signals the person doesn't matter.
11. **Thursday is sacred.** CTO collaboration day, 10am–3:30pm. No external meetings. No exceptions.

## The Ideal Week

Three layers: day themes, daily time blocks, and which rhythm each day serves.

### Five Work Rhythms

| Rhythm | What it includes | Primary days |
|--------|-----------------|-------------|
| **Building** | Strategic thinking, system design, writing, product work, deep creation | Mon AM, Thu (with CTO), Tue AM |
| **Delivering** | Team alignment, 1:1s, operational meetings, leadership sync | Mon PM, Wed |
| **Selling** | Client calls, prospect meetings, pipeline, renewals | Scheduled into meeting windows |
| **Networking** | Lunches, coffees, relationship-building, industry connections | Tue lunch, events |
| **Promoting** | Content creation, newsletter, LinkedIn, thought leadership | Fri AM |

### Monday — Home — Building + Delivering

| Time | Block | What |
|------|-------|------|
| Before 7:00 | Exercise | Physical foundation |
| 7:00–10:00 | **Peak deep work** | Building: strategic thinking, system design, writing. No meetings. No interruptions. |
| 10:00–12:00 | Internal meetings | Delivering: team syncs, 1:1s, leadership alignment |
| 12:00–12:30 | Lunch | Real break |
| 12:30–3:30 | Internal meetings / deep work overflow | Delivering continued, or deep work spillover |
| 3:30–4:00 | Close out | Process notes, prep tomorrow, clear open loops |
| 4:30+ | Family time | |

Monday is internal day. No external meetings. Morning builds, afternoon aligns.

### Tuesday — Office — Networking + Flex

| Time | Block | What |
|------|-------|------|
| Before 7:00 | Exercise | Physical foundation |
| 7:00–10:00 | **Peak deep work** | Building: deep work before office energy kicks in. No meetings. |
| 10:00–12:00 | Flex / deep work overflow | Open block — selling calls, async catch-up, or continued building |
| 12:00–1:30 | Networking lunch | In-person: industry contacts, advisors, investors, interesting people |
| 1:30–3:30 | Flex | Selling, ad hoc meetings, office collaboration. Open by design. |
| 3:30–3:45 | Close out + leave office | Quick wrap, depart by 3:45 |
| 4:30+ | Family time | |

Tuesday is the social/external day. Office energy, networking lunch, flexible afternoon. The afternoon is deliberately unstructured — it's the release valve for the week.

### Wednesday — Home — Meeting Day

| Time | Block | What |
|------|-------|------|
| Before 7:00 | Exercise | Physical foundation |
| 7:00–8:00 | Quick prep | Review agendas, prep for the packed day |
| 8:00–3:30 | **Packed meetings** | The one day meetings can start before 10am. Internal meetings stacked tight. Video calls. Get all alignment done so other days stay clean. |
| 3:30–4:00 | Close out | Decompress, process action items |
| 4:30+ | Family time | |

Wednesday is the sacrifice day. Accept the meeting load here so it doesn't contaminate the other four days. Stack them tight, get it done, close clean. Internal cadence only.

### Thursday — Office — Building with CTO

| Time | Block | What |
|------|-------|------|
| Before 7:00 | Exercise | Physical foundation |
| 7:00–10:00 | **Peak deep work** | Solo building before CTO collab. Product thinking, system design, prep. |
| 10:00–3:30 | **CTO collaboration block** | In-office with Daniel. Product, architecture, strategy, technical decisions. Protected partnership block — no external meetings, no unrelated interruptions. |
| 3:30–3:45 | Close out + leave office | Wrap, depart by 3:45 |
| 4:30+ | Family time | |

Thursday is the maker day. Morning solo, afternoon paired. No external noise. No selling. No delivering. Just building.

### Friday — Flex — Review + Promoting

| Time | Block | What |
|------|-------|------|
| Before 7:00 | Exercise | Physical foundation |
| 7:00–12:00 | **Flex block** | Promoting: content, newsletter, LinkedIn. Or deep work overflow. Or learning. Most flexible block of the week. |
| 12:00–2:00 | Meeting block | The only meeting window on Friday. Pack anything that must happen here. |
| 2:00–3:00 | **Close out the week** | Weekly review, prep next week, clear open loops, reflect. |
| 3:00+ | **Family time (early)** | Friday family time starts at 3pm. |

Friday is the exhale. Flexible morning, contained meetings, deliberate close-out, early family time.

### The Math

- **15 hours/week of peak deep work** (7–10am Mon/Tue/Thu/Fri + Thu CTO collab)
- **Meetings contained to:** Mon 10am–3:30pm (internal), Wed 8am–3:30pm (packed), Fri noon–2pm, plus Tue flex
- **Family time protected** every single day
- **30-min close-out** built into every day

## Meeting Architecture

### Types and Defaults

| Type | Length | When | Prep required |
|------|--------|------|---------------|
| Internal 1:1 | 25 min | Mon or Wed | They own the agenda |
| Internal small group (2–5) | 25–50 min | Mon or Wed | Agenda in invite |
| Internal large (all-hands, reviews) | 30–50 min | Wed | Deck/agenda 24h before |
| External 1:1 (client, investor) | 50 min | Tue flex or Fri noon–2pm | Context brief 24h before |
| External small group | 50 min | Tue flex or Fri noon–2pm | Context brief 24h before |
| External large (presentations, events) | 60–90 min | Scheduled with buffer around it | Pre-read 48h before |
| Strategic/planning (internal) | 90 min | Mon PM or Wed | Pre-read 48h before |

### Clustering Rules

- Internal meetings cluster on **Monday PM** and **Wednesday**
- External meetings cluster on **Tuesday** (or into **Friday noon–2pm**)
- **Thursday** is CTO only — no other meetings
- Never mix internal and external on the same day

### 1:1 Structure

- 5 min: personal check-in
- 10 min: their top 2–3 items (they drive)
- 5 min: Marcel's items
- 5 min: action items and next steps

Don't waste 1:1 time on status updates. That's what Slack is for.

### Meeting Audit Question

For every recurring meeting: what decision or outcome does this produce? If you can't name one, the meeting probably shouldn't exist.

## How to Get Time with Marcel

Three access tiers.

### Immediate — Call or Text

- Client escalation that could cost an account
- Blocked decision that's time-sensitive
- Information that changes a decision already made
- Family (always)

### Scheduled — Slack DM, 24–48h

- Strategic question with an agenda
- Partner/vendor meeting with clear purpose
- Anything needing his input but not urgent

### Filtered — Redirect or Async

- Questions answerable with 10 minutes of research
- FYIs that don't need action
- Problems not yet thought through

### Channel Hierarchy

| Channel | Use for | Response time |
|---------|---------|---------------|
| Call/Text | True emergencies only | Immediate |
| Slack DM | Need input same day | Within hours |
| Slack Channel | Team coordination, FYI | Same day |
| Email | External, formal records | 24 hours |

### Scheduling a Meeting

External meetings go through Calendly: [calendly.com/growthxai/meet-marcel](https://calendly.com/growthxai/meet-marcel)

Agents and EA should send this link when scheduling external meetings on Marcel's behalf. Calendly enforces available windows automatically.

### How to Bring a Decision

Problem (with data) + options considered + your recommendation + what you need from him. He edits faster than he builds from scratch.

### What His Silence Means

Not approval. He's buried. Follow up.

## For EA and Agents

Operational protocols. This section is the foundation for agent skills and context instructions.

### Daily Protocols

**Morning brief (before 7am):**
- Today's calendar with prep status
- Overnight Slack/email summary by priority
- Open action items (overdue flagged)
- 3–5 day look-ahead

**Transition prompts (5 min before block changes):**
- "Next up: [Meeting/Task]. Context: [1–2 lines]. Start by: [specific first action]."

**Focus mode:**
- Auto-activate 7–10am on Mon/Tue/Thu/Fri
- Hold all non-emergencies, queue notifications for next available window
- On Wednesday, switch to meeting-support mode — pre-load context briefs for back-to-back calls

### Meeting Prep — 24h Before External Meetings

- Pull attendee profiles from CRM
- Gather last 3 interactions (emails, meeting notes, Slack)
- Check account status (renewal date, open issues, sentiment)
- Generate context brief: Subject / Why now / Background / Key data / Recommendation

### Meeting Follow-Up — Within 2 Hours

- Capture action items with owners and deadlines
- Send summary to all participants
- Update CRM and contact dossiers
- Schedule follow-up meetings
- Add commitments to tracking system

### Weekly Protocols

**Friday close-out audit:**
- Review next week against ideal week template
- Flag: meetings in peak block, missing buffers, internal/external mixed on same day, meetings without agendas, anything on Thursday outside CTO block, anything on Friday outside noon–2pm

**Adherence report:**
- Peak block protection rate (target: 100% Mon/Tue/Thu/Fri)
- Family time violations (target: 0)
- Meeting hours total (track, no hard cap — Wednesday will be heavy)
- Wednesday packing efficiency

### Circuit Breakers

1. **No meetings 7–10am** (except Wed) — auto-decline
2. **No meetings after 3:30pm** (any day) — auto-decline
3. **No meetings on Friday outside noon–2pm** — auto-decline
4. **Flag if internal + external meetings land on same day**
5. **Flag if 3+ consecutive weeks show evening work beyond 1 hour**
6. **At least one full week off per quarter**

### Setting-Aware Scheduling

- **Home days (Mon/Wed/Fri):** Default to video calls. No in-person scheduling.
- **Office days (Tue/Thu):** Can schedule in-person. Factor in commute time.
- **Moving:** Only phone-compatible meetings (audio, no screen share). Add travel buffer before and after.
- **Traveling:** Switch to trip calendar. Build trip brief per [EA Operating Guide](ea-operating-guide-study-guide-v1.md).

### Handling Stress Signals

When Marcel uses catastrophic language ("dead in the water," worst-case projections) — that's processing, not prediction. Respond with: facts (not interpretations), options (not just problems), quick turnaround on information requests. Don't add chaos with unprocessed updates.

## Quick Reference

**Do:**
- Send written context before meetings — a 2-min read saves a 20-min explanation
- Come with recommendations, not open questions
- Cluster meetings by type — internal OR external, not both
- Know your numbers cold
- Escalate early and specifically ("I need X by Thursday")
- Follow up if Marcel hasn't responded — silence is not approval
- Use 25/50-min meeting defaults

**Don't:**
- Schedule anything before 10am (except Wednesday)
- Schedule anything after 3:30pm (any day)
- Drop a meeting in the middle of a focus block
- Mix internal and external meetings on the same day
- Book anything on Thursday outside CTO collaboration
- Schedule meetings on Friday outside noon–2pm
- Assume family time is flexible — it isn't
- Say "it'll be fine" without evidence
