# AEO Prompt Tracking Playbook: Buyer Evaluation Stage

*A practical guide for marketing & growth teams building AI visibility into the evaluation stage of the B2B buyer journey.*

---

## Why This Matters Right Now

AI is eating the middle of the funnel. Up to 90% of B2B buyers now use generative AI during their purchase research (Forrester), and the evaluation stage — where buyers compare, shortlist, and narrow options — is seeing the most disruption. Buyers who used to spend weeks compiling vendor lists and reading whitepapers now prompt ChatGPT, Perplexity, or Gemini and build a shortlist in minutes.

If your brand doesn't show up in those AI responses, you're invisible at the exact moment evaluators build their shortlist. You're removed from consideration before a human salesperson can engage.

The fix: systematically track the prompts buyers actually use during evaluation, measure your visibility against competitors, and close the gap with targeted content.

---

## Part 1: How Buyers Use AI During Evaluation

Understanding what buyers actually *do* during evaluation shapes which prompts you need to track.

### What's Changed

- AI chat is now the **top source for building software shortlists** — buyers prompt "give me three CRM solutions for a hospital that work on iPads" and skip the multi-site research loop entirely.
- **84% of B2B buyers** use review platforms as a primary research method, with AI aggregating and sorting those results.
- Companies now need **58% less time** to make informed supplier decisions thanks to generative AI.
- Traffic from AI platforms (ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude, Perplexity, Grok) grew **527% YoY** in 2025.

### What Evaluation-Stage Queries Look Like

Buyers aren't typing keywords. They're asking contextual, high-intent questions:

> "What's the best logistics software that integrates with SAP for mid-market manufacturers?"

> "We're a mid-sized logistics firm looking for a secure CRM with offline capabilities and Salesforce integration"

> "Compare HubSpot vs Salesforce for a 50-person SaaS company"

These are the prompts you need to be tracking.

## Part 2: The Six Prompt Categories That Matter in Evaluation

Every prompt a buyer uses during evaluation falls into one of these six categories. Your tracking library should cover all of them.

### 1. Direct Comparison Prompts

The head-to-head matchups. These are the most common evaluation-stage prompts and the ones where positioning matters most.

**Pattern:** `[Brand A] vs [Brand B]` or `[Brand A] vs [Brand B] for [use case]`

**Examples to track:**
- `[Your Brand] vs [Top Competitor]`
- `[Your Brand] vs [Competitor] for [your strongest use case]`
- `[Competitor A] vs [Competitor B]` *(track even when you're not named — you want to break in)*
- `Compare top 5 [your category] tools`
- `[Your Brand] vs [Competitor] vs [Competitor]: which is best for [industry]?`

**Why it matters:** If AI recommends your competitor and doesn't mention you, you've lost the deal before it started.

---

### 2. "Alternatives To" Prompts

The most critical evaluation signal. Buyers asking for alternatives are actively looking to switch or expand their shortlist.

**Pattern:** `Alternatives to [Competitor]` or `[Competitor] replacement`

**Examples to track:**
- `Alternatives to [each major competitor]`
- `Best [competitor] alternatives`
- `What can I use instead of [competitor]?`
- `Open source alternatives to [commercial competitor]`
- `[Competitor] replacement for [specific use case]`

**Why it matters:** These are high-intent, high-conversion prompts. If you're not appearing as an alternative to your competitors, you're leaving pipeline on the table.

---

### 3. Category + Use Case Prompts

The "best of" queries. Buyers use these to build their initial shortlist, often without having a specific brand in mind yet.

**Pattern:** `Best [category] for [constraint]`

**Examples to track:**
- `Best [your category] for [each key industry you serve]`
- `Best [your category] for [company size: startups / mid-market / enterprise]`
- `Best [your category] that integrates with [major platform]`
- `Top [your category] tools for [specific team/role]`
- `Best [your category] with [key differentiating feature]`

**Why it matters:** This is where shortlists are born. Being named in a "best of" response establishes you as a legitimate contender.

---

### 4. Review & Reputation Prompts

Trust-building queries. Buyers use these to validate brands already on their shortlist.

**Pattern:** `[Brand] reviews` or `Is [Brand] good for [use case]?`

**Examples to track:**
- `[Your Brand] reviews`
- `[Your Brand] reviews [current year]`
- `Is [Your Brand] good for [key use case]?`
- `[Your Brand] pros and cons`
- `[Your Brand] customer complaints`
- `Top-rated [your category] software`

**Why it matters:** Negative or missing information here can knock you off a shortlist. You need to know what AI is saying about your reputation.

---

### 5. Pricing & Cost Prompts

Budget-stage evaluation. Buyers are comparing value, not just features.

**Pattern:** `[Brand] pricing` or `Best [category] under $X`

**Examples to track:**
- `[Your Brand] pricing`
- `[Your Brand] pricing vs [Competitor] pricing`
- `How much does [Your Brand] cost?`
- `Best [your category] under $[price point] per month`
- `[Your Brand] free tier / free trial`
- `Cost comparison: [Your Brand] vs [Competitor]`

**Why it matters:** If AI returns outdated or incorrect pricing, it could eliminate you. Track these to catch bad data early.

---

### 6. Feature & Integration Prompts

Capability-check queries. Buyers are validating that you can do what they need.

**Pattern:** `Does [Brand] have [feature]?` or `Which [category] has [capability]?`

**Examples to track:**
- `Does [Your Brand] have [your top 3-5 features]?`
- `Which [your category] has [your key differentiator]?`
- `[Your Brand] integrations`
- `Does [Your Brand] work with [major platforms your buyers use]?`
- `[Your Brand] API / [Your Brand] security / [Your Brand] compliance`

**Why it matters:** A "no" or incomplete answer here kills deals. You need to know if AI is accurately representing your capabilities.

## Part 3: Building Your Prompt Tracking Library

### Step 1: Build the Matrix

Organize prompts across three dimensions:

| Dimension | Examples |
|-----------|----------|
| **Prompt Category** | Comparison, Alternatives, Best-of, Reviews, Pricing, Features |
| **Buyer Persona** | VP Engineering, IT Director, Ops Manager, Founder, Procurement |
| **Industry / Use Case** | Healthcare, SaaS, Manufacturing, Financial Services, etc. |

Each cell in this matrix generates specific prompts. For example:

- **Category:** Best-of | **Persona:** VP Engineering | **Industry:** FinTech
  - → `Best observability platform for fintech engineering teams`
  - → `Top monitoring tools for financial services compliance`

### Step 2: Source Prompts From Real Buyer Behavior

Don't guess. Mine actual buyer language:

| Source | What to look for |
|--------|-----------------|
| **Sales call transcripts** | Questions prospects ask during discovery and evaluation |
| **Customer success tickets** | Post-sale questions that mirror pre-sale evaluation |
| **G2 / Capterra reviews** | How buyers describe your product and competitors |
| **LinkedIn discussions** | How industry professionals frame comparisons |
| **Reddit threads** | Raw, unfiltered buyer language (Reddit is cited in 40%+ of Perplexity responses) |
| **Your own search console data** | Query patterns from people already finding you |

### Step 3: Prioritize Using the Value/Effort Matrix

Not all prompts are equal. Score each prompt on two axes:

**Business Value (High/Low):**
- Does this prompt map to a real buying decision?
- Is there significant search volume or buyer intent behind it?
- Does winning this prompt influence pipeline?

**Content Effort (High/Low):**
- Do you already have content that could answer this?
- How much new content would you need to create?
- Can you realistically influence this prompt?

**Priority order:**
1. **Quick Wins** — High value, low effort (you have content, just need optimization)
2. **Strategic Bets** — High value, high effort (important but requires new content)
3. **Easy Adds** — Low value, low effort (nice-to-have, quick to implement)
4. **Deprioritize** — Low value, high effort (skip for now)

### Step 4: Set Your Starting Volume

| Team Size / Stage | Recommended Prompts | Notes |
|-------------------|--------------------|----|
| **Just starting out** | 50-100 | Focus on your top 2-3 competitors and primary use case |
| **Growing program** | 200-500 | Add personas, industries, and broader competitor set |
| **Enterprise / Mature** | 500+ | Full matrix coverage, multi-language, multi-region |

## Part 4: Tracking Cadence & Workflow

### Recommended Cadence

| Cadence | When to use |
|---------|-------------|
| **Weekly** | Standard for most teams. Frequent enough to catch trends, not so frequent you drown in data. Start here. |
| **Daily** | High-stakes launches, crisis monitoring, or fast-moving competitive situations. |
| **Bi-weekly** | Slower-moving categories where weekly is overkill. |
| **Monthly** | Long-term trend analysis layer on top of weekly tracking. |

**Key insight:** Only 30% of brands stayed visible from one AI answer to the next, and just 20% held presence across five consecutive runs. This volatility is why weekly tracking is the minimum.

### The Tracking Workflow

```
1. RUN PROMPTS
   Execute your prompt library across ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, Claude
   ↓
2. CAPTURE DATA
   For each prompt, record:
   • Were you mentioned? (yes/no)
   • Were you cited with a link? (yes/no)
   • Position in response (1st mentioned, 2nd, listed as alternative, etc.)
   • Sentiment (positive, neutral, negative)
   • Which competitors were mentioned?
   • What source was cited?
   ↓
3. SCORE & COMPARE
   Calculate:
   • Inclusion rate (% of prompts where you appear)
   • Share of Voice vs. competitors
   • Citation rate (mentions with links vs. without)
   • Sentiment breakdown
   ↓
4. IDENTIFY GAPS
   Flag prompts where:
   • You don't appear but competitors do
   • You appear but with negative sentiment
   • Incorrect information is being surfaced
   • You appeared last week but dropped off
   ↓
5. TAKE ACTION
   Route gaps to content team for:
   • New content creation
   • Existing content optimization
   • Technical/structural improvements
   • Third-party mention building
   ↓
6. VERIFY
   Re-run flagged prompts after content ships to measure impact
```

### Alert Thresholds

Set automated alerts for:
- Inclusion rate drops **>20%** week-over-week
- Sentiment turns **negative for 2 consecutive runs**
- **Incorrect pricing or product info** appears in any response
- A competitor **newly appears** on prompts where they weren't before

## Part 5: Metrics That Matter

| Metric | What It Measures | Target |
|--------|-----------------|--------|
| **Inclusion Rate** | % of tracked prompts where your brand appears | >60% of evaluation-stage prompts |
| **Citation Rate** | % of mentions that include a link to your content | Higher is better — citations drive traffic, mentions drive awareness |
| **Share of Voice** | Your mention frequency vs. competitors across the same prompts | Track relative trend, aim to lead in your core segments |
| **Position in Response** | Are you mentioned first, in a list, or as an afterthought? | First mention or featured recommendation |
| **Sentiment Score** | Positive / Neutral / Negative breakdown | >80% positive or neutral |
| **Accuracy Rate** | % of responses with correct product/pricing info | 100% target — flag and fix any inaccuracies immediately |
| **Stability Index** | How much your visibility fluctuates week-to-week | Lower volatility = stronger AI positioning |

### Content Formatting for AI Extraction

Structure your content so LLMs can easily extract and cite it:

- **Lead with the answer.** Put your key claim in the first 40-60 words of each section.
- **Use headers as questions.** Rewrite H2s/H3s to match how buyers actually prompt AI.
- **Make sections standalone.** Each section should make sense if pulled out of context — AI extracts passages, not full pages.
- **Use structured formats.** Comparison tables, pros/cons lists, and FAQ blocks are naturally extractable.
- **Implement schema markup.** Product schema, FAQ schema, comparison schema, and review schema. This is no longer optional.

### Authority Building for AI Visibility

What signals make LLMs cite your brand:

| Signal | Impact | Action |
|--------|--------|--------|
| **Brand search volume** | Strongest predictor of AI citations (0.334 correlation) | Invest in brand awareness campaigns |
| **Wikipedia presence** | 22% of LLM training data comes from Wikipedia | Ensure your Wikipedia page is accurate and current |
| **Third-party mentions** | 85% of brand mentions come from third-party pages | Pursue PR, guest posts, review site presence |
| **Reddit discussions** | Reddit is cited in 40%+ of Perplexity and many ChatGPT responses | Participate authentically in relevant subreddits |
| **Structured data** | 300% higher comprehension vs. unstructured content | Implement schema markup across your site |
| **Review platform presence** | G2, Capterra, TrustRadius feed directly into AI responses | Actively manage and grow your review profiles |

## Part 8: Quick-Start Checklist

Use this to get your first tracking cycle running within a week.

- [ ] **Pick your top 3 competitors** — these define your comparison and alternatives prompts
- [ ] **Identify your top 3 use cases / industries** — these define your "best of" prompts
- [ ] **Build 50 starter prompts** using the six categories above (aim for ~8 per category)
- [ ] **Run prompts manually** through ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Gemini (or use a tool)
- [ ] **Score each response:** Were you mentioned? Cited? What position? What sentiment? Who else appeared?
- [ ] **Calculate your baseline inclusion rate and Share of Voice**
- [ ] **Identify your 5 biggest gaps** — prompts where competitors appear and you don't
- [ ] **Create a content brief for each gap** — what content would you need to close it?
- [ ] **Set a weekly tracking cadence** — same day, same time, same process
- [ ] **Review and expand your prompt library monthly** — add new prompts based on what you learn
