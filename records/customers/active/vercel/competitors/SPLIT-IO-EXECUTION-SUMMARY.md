## Key Findings Summary

### Company Status

- **Founded:** 2015 (same year as Vercel)
- **Growth:** 45-50% YoY ARR (2021-2024)
- **Customer Base:** 500+ enterprises (Walmart, Uber, Target, Robinhood, Twilio, Zendesk, HubSpot)
- **Valuation:** ~$450-500M (Series E, 2022)
- **Status:** Acquired by Harness (July 2024, completed Q4 2024)

### Competitive Positioning

**Split.io is complementary, not direct competitor to Vercel:**
- Split.io dominates: Statistical A/B testing, advanced experimentation, multi-language SDKs, enterprise feature management
- Vercel dominates: Deployment integration, edge distribution, AI tooling, developer experience, speed/simplicity
- Market separation by buyer persona: Split.io = data analysts/product managers focused on experimentation; Vercel = developers/deployment teams

### Market Context (Harness Acquisition)

**Why Harness Acquired Split.io:**
1. Strategic consolidation of feature delivery capabilities
2. Competitive convergence in feature flags market
3. 500+ enterprise customer base valuable for cross-sell
4. Engineering talent in distributed SDKs and statistical testing

**Post-Acquisition Implications:**
1. Split.io becomes "Harness Feature Delivery Platform"
2. Integration with Harness Intelligence (analytics) and Continuous Delivery (pipeline automation)
3. Standalone Split.io product likely deprecated in 2-3 years
4. Pricing expected to consolidate into Harness bundles
5. Creates three-way market consolidation: LaunchDarkly vs Harness+Split vs Vercel+AWS

### Competitive Advantage Analysis

**Vercel's Structural Advantages Over Split.io:**
1. Deployment integration (git push to global production with flags)
2. Edge-first architecture (126 PoPs, 19 compute regions)
3. AI development tooling (v0, AI SDK, AI Gateway)
4. Financial resources ($863M raised vs $146M; $200M ARR vs $40-45M ARR)
5. Faster innovation velocity and market positioning
6. Broader developer ecosystem (40+ frameworks, marketplace)

**Split.io's Specialized Strength:**
1. Statistical A/B testing (Bayesian inference, power analysis, MAB, sample size calculation)
2. Multi-language SDK ecosystem (10+ vs Vercel's JavaScript focus)
3. Advanced audience management (rules engine, CDP integrations)
4. Enterprise feature management scale (proven at 10K+ flags)
5. Gartner Leader recognition in Feature Flags & Experimentation category
