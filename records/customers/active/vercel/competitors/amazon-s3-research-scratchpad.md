## Research Notes for Phase 2 (Synthesis)

### Key Findings to Integrate

1. **S3's Market Dominance**: Launched March 2006 (20 years old), S3 is the de facto standard for object storage globally. Estimated 100+ trillion objects stored as of 2025.

2. **AWS/Amazon Context**: Amazon is a $2T+ market cap company. AWS revenue ~$95B+ annually (2024), with S3 as one of the oldest and most reliable services. Amazon doesn't disclose S3 revenue separately, but S3 is estimated to be a $15B+ revenue contributor.

3. **Pricing Model Difference**: S3 charges separately for storage (per GB), requests (per 1M requests), and egress (per GB transferred). Free tier exists but is limited. Vercel Blob abstracts this complexity with simpler tiering.

4. **Competitive Positioning**: S3 faces increasing pressure from Cloudflare R2 (unlimited egress, lower pricing), Google Cloud Storage, and Azure Blob Storage. Vercel Blob positions as a "simpler, faster alternative" optimized for Vercel deployments.

5. **Developer Experience Gap**: S3 is powerful but complex—requires IAM setup, bucket configuration, CORS handling, lifecycle policies. Vercel Blob abstracts these details (key-value upload, no configuration).

6. **Ecosystem Strength**: S3 is deeply integrated across the entire AWS ecosystem and beyond (Terraform, Docker, Kubernetes, CI/CD tools). This is S3's strongest competitive advantage.

7. **Community Sentiment**: Developers praise S3 for reliability and ecosystem integration, but frequently criticize high egress costs and complexity. This is a key vulnerability Vercel and Cloudflare R2 are exploiting.

8. **Analyst Coverage**: Limited analyst coverage specifically for S3 (part of broader AWS/cloud storage reviews). No dedicated "S3 reports" like you'd see for Vercel or Netlify, reflecting S3's assumed market dominance.

9. **Content Strategy**: AWS focuses on technical documentation, case studies, and certification training. Less content marketing (blog posts, guides) compared to developer-focused competitors like Vercel.

10. **Vercel Blob as Niche Competitor**: Vercel Blob targets developers who are already on Vercel and want simple file uploads. S3 is general-purpose; Vercel Blob is specialized. Head-to-head comparison is segment-specific.
