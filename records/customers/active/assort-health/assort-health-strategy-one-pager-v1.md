# Assort Health Content Strategy: The Big Picture

## The starting point

Assort handles 100M+ patient interactions across 22 specialties. The product depth is real -- 1.6M edge cases, specialty-specific scheduling logic, the kind of complexity that wins deals once prospects see it.

But here's the problem: not enough prospects see it.

Over the last 90 days, Google showed Assort in search results 126,000 times for non-branded queries -- people searching for things like "voice AI for healthcare" and "orthopedic patient scheduling software." These are potential buyers actively looking for what Assort does. Almost none of them clicked through, because there's no content on the other side. The homepage is catching this traffic by accident. There's no blog post explaining how voice AI works in healthcare. No page for any specialty except orthopedics. No article comparing Assort to the alternatives buyers are evaluating.

The demand is already there. The content isn't.

## What we're solving

This isn't a brand awareness problem. It's a visibility gap between what Assort has built and what the market can see.

Today, Assort's strongest marketing assets -- case studies with real metrics like SENTA Partners' $1.3M in additional appointment revenue and OrthoIndy's 131% labor capacity increase -- sit behind a single page that gets 80 clicks in 90 days. The product's deepest advantages -- like handling an OB-GYN's double resource scheduling or routing an orthopedic shoulder pain call through surgical history, imaging, and mid-level assignment -- live in engineers' heads, not on the website.

The content strategy translates what Assort has already built into proof that buyers can find during research. Every piece of content we create is designed to answer a question your buyer is already asking Google, ChatGPT, or Perplexity -- and answer it with the kind of specificity that only Assort can provide.

## How we organize the work

We don't publish random articles and hope something sticks. We work in **clusters** -- groups of related content focused on a specific topic your buyers care about. Each cluster builds authority with search engines and AI systems, so the more you publish within a cluster, the stronger every piece in it becomes. Think of it as compounding -- each article makes the others rank better.

We've identified five clusters for Assort, prioritized by where the biggest gaps are, where competition is weakest, and where your product advantages are hardest to replicate.

## The five clusters

### 1. Voice AI for Healthcare (your home base)

**Why this matters:** "AI voice agent for healthcare" gets 1,600 monthly searches. Assort is already showing up on page 1 for some of these queries -- with nothing but the homepage. A company called Relatient currently holds the #1 spot for this term, but they bolt voice AI onto a legacy platform. It's a thin ranking. One comprehensive, well-structured article built on Assort's actual product depth could take that position.

**What we'd create:**

- **"What Is an AI Voice Agent for Healthcare?"** -- The definitive guide. Opens with a real scenario: a patient calls an orthopedic practice with shoulder pain. The AI looks up their visit history, checks for prior surgery, verifies imaging orders, identifies the right mid-level provider based on the day of the week, and books the appointment. This is 30 seconds of work that a human call center agent does in 8 minutes with a binder of protocols. This article becomes the page Google and AI systems point to when anyone asks about voice AI in healthcare. It builds trust because it shows the actual workflow, not a marketing claim.

- **"After-Hours Answering Services for Medical Offices: AI vs. Traditional"** -- 880 monthly searches, low competition. Your buyers are comparing options right now. This article walks through what a traditional after-hours service handles (take a message, forward to on-call) versus what AI can do (schedule appointments, check insurance, triage urgency, route by specialty rules). It earns credibility by being honest about what AI handles and what still needs a human -- which positions Assort as trustworthy, not salesy.

- **"Why Voice AI Demos Don't Tell the Full Story"** -- This turns your CEO's insight into a piece of content that changes how buyers evaluate every vendor, including Assort's competitors. The thesis: a polished demo shows one perfect call. Production reality means handling millions of combinations -- a patient who mentions chest pain and diabetes while calling about a knee, an insurance plan that requires prior authorization for certain visit types, a provider who only sees new patients on Tuesdays. Buyers who read this start asking harder questions in every vendor evaluation, which favors the company with the deepest dataset.

### 2. Specialty pages (your content moat)

**Why this matters:** Assort has one orthopedic specialty page. It's a relatively thin landing page. It generated 85,000 impressions in 90 days. That's more than any content page on the site other than the homepage. Now imagine what happens when 16 more specialties each have a dedicated page with real edge case stories, customer metrics, and FAQ content. The math is straightforward: if one page generates 85K impressions, a portfolio of specialty pages could realistically reach 500K-1M+ impressions.

No competitor can replicate this content. They don't have the data from 22 specialties and 100M+ interactions.

**What we'd create:**

- **"Voice AI for OB-GYN and Women's Health"** -- Walks through the complexity that OB-GYN practices deal with daily: a first pregnancy visit isn't one appointment, it's two linked appointments (ultrasound + OB history) across different resource types, with room constraints that prevent double-booking. The AI captures the patient's last menstrual period, calculates gestational age, and schedules both appointments in sequence. This page speaks directly to OB-GYN practice administrators who believe -- correctly -- that their scheduling is too complex for generic tools. It builds trust because it proves Assort actually understands their world.

- **"Voice AI for Ophthalmology"** -- Ophthalmology has a unique challenge: patients almost never know what type of appointment they need. The AI has to infer it from visit history. This page shows that Assort solves problems the buyer didn't even know a vendor could solve. And it includes NCRVA's results -- recovering one-third of previously missed calls, scheduling urgent retinal cases within 1-2 days. Real metrics from a real practice in their exact specialty.

- **"Voice AI for Behavioral Health"** -- Behavioral health has sensitivity constraints that general-purpose AI systems miss entirely: matching patients to providers based on clinical specialty, handling intake conversations that require more nuance, and managing scheduling logic that accounts for session types and provider availability. This page reaches a specialty that's underserved by every competitor in the market.

### 3. Competitor comparisons (the deal accelerator)

**Why this matters:** 10-15% of Assort's inbound comes from buyers frustrated with a prior vendor. They're searching for alternatives. Right now, there's no Assort content for them to find. A comparison page won't drive massive traffic -- but it's the highest-conversion content type in B2B. One buyer who reads a comparison page and requests a demo is worth more than 10,000 blog visitors.

**What we'd create:**

- **"Assort Health vs. Transform9"** -- Transform9 is the competitor Assort's sales team finds hardest to beat. They win on price ($0.88/call) and a 60-day free trial. But their system is rules-based, not AI-native -- when interactions don't follow the scripted flow, they break and transfer to staff. This page doesn't disparage Transform9. It lays out the honest differences: pricing, platform scope, scheduling complexity, proven outcomes. Then it includes edge case scenarios -- "try scheduling a first OB visit with linked ultrasound on your current system" -- that let the buyer test the difference themselves. This page is as much a sales tool as a marketing asset. Your reps can share the URL during competitive deals.

- **"Assort Health vs. Phreesia"** -- Phreesia is the most frequently encountered competitor in Assort's deals. They dominate digital check-in and forms-based intake. This comparison draws the line: Phreesia handles what happens on the tablet in the waiting room. Assort handles what happens on the phone before the patient ever walks in. These are different problems. This page helps buyers understand which one they're actually trying to solve -- and for practices with high call volume and complex scheduling, the answer is clear.

### 4. Agentic AI in healthcare (the category play)

**Why this matters:** "Agentic AI" is the buzzword of 2026. Every vendor is claiming it. No one has published the definitive explanation of what it actually means in healthcare. Artera recently started publishing agentic AI content -- they're trying to define the category around messaging. If Assort defines it first, around voice and patient access, that narrative sticks. Whoever publishes the best content on this topic becomes the reference point for every buyer, analyst, and AI system answering questions about it.

**What we'd create:**

- **"What Is Agentic AI in Healthcare?"** -- Defines the term with specificity no one else can match. Agentic AI isn't a chatbot that got smarter. It's an autonomous system that handles multi-step, multi-system workflows end-to-end: capturing a patient's symptoms, querying the EHR for visit history, checking insurance eligibility, applying payer-specific scheduling rules, and booking the right appointment with the right provider -- all in a single phone call. The article is structured for AI citation: clear definitions, real examples, FAQ schema. When a health system leader asks ChatGPT "what is agentic AI in healthcare," this article should be what comes back.

- **"The Future of the COO in Healthcare: From Operations Manager to AI Agent Builder"** -- A thought leadership piece built around the CEO's contrarian view: that the COO role is about to fundamentally change. Instead of managing call center staffing levels, COOs will be configuring and managing AI agents. This piece targets health system buyers -- 22% of Assort's pipeline -- who are "more intellectual and consultant-like" and want to understand the future before they buy a tool.

### 5. AI patient intake (the flanking play)

**Why this matters:** Phreesia owns "patient intake" in search with 67% of keywords in the cluster. But their version of intake is forms on a tablet. Assort's version is a live phone call where the AI collects symptoms, triages urgency, checks insurance, routes to the right provider, and books the appointment -- all before the patient walks in the door. These are different things that happen to share a name. Content that draws this distinction clearly opens a lane Phreesia can't follow Assort into.

**What we'd create:**

- **"AI Patient Intake: How Voice AI Automates Phone-Based Intake for Specialty Practices"** -- Draws the line between form-based intake (filling out fields on a tablet) and phone-based intake (a live conversation where the AI handles triage, routing, and scheduling in real time). This is the flanking move: instead of competing with Phreesia on their turf, we redefine what "intake" means for practices where most patients call instead of checking in digitally.

- **"What Is Telephone Triage? How AI Routes Patients to the Right Care in Real Time"** -- A patient calls and says "my knee hurts but I've also been having chest pains and I'm diabetic." That's not a form field -- it's a real-time triage decision. The AI needs to recognize the chest pain + diabetes combination as potentially urgent, route differently than the knee complaint alone, and make sure the patient gets to the right provider at the right speed. This article makes the invisible decision-making visible. It builds trust because it shows Assort handles the hard cases, not just the easy ones.
