## The Core Problem

**Discoverability, not product.** The product depth is real -- 1.6M edge cases, specialty-specific scheduling logic -- but none of it is visible in content.

- ~13 indexed pages. No blog, no specialty landing pages (except one), no guides, no comparison content.
- 45,000+ organic clicks over 90 days, but 96.4% are branded. Only 522 non-branded clicks.
- 126,000 non-branded impressions across 821 queries -- and almost zero clicks. The demand exists. The content doesn't.
- Invisible in ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews.
- Case studies are the strongest marketing asset but buried behind a single /customers page generating 80 clicks in 90 days.

## Content Angles That Win

Three themes to internalize -- these run through every piece of content:

1. **Lead with what breaks when voice AI isn't specialty-trained.** The edge cases are the moat. An orthopedic practice routes shoulder pain through a cascade of EHR queries, temporal logic, and mid-level team assignment. An OB-GYN practice schedules a "simple" first pregnancy visit as a linked pair of appointments across two resource types with room constraints. Competitors can't fake this without the underlying dataset.

2. **"Every specialty thinks it's special -- and they're right."** Validate the complexity, then prove Assort built for it. 1.6M edge cases across 22 specialties. Content should make the invisible visible: the binders of scheduling protocols, the payer-specific global periods, the provider preference matching in real time.

3. **Demos don't tell the full story.** The CEO's framing: *"A sexy demo is not representative of the experience patients will get. When a patient calls in, they can say anything -- millions of combinations of issues and stories. AI needs to handle every single one."* This is the wedge against every competitor selling a polished demo over production reality.
