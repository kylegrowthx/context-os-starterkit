Protect Jama's requirements management SEO equity through the blog and RM Guide as the homepage pivots to "AI-powered engineering management platform" -- while filling vertical content gaps in A&D, automotive, and semiconductor that fuel the 15-20% growth target.

## The Core Problem

**Repositioning is creating an SEO vacuum, and 1,200 URLs of existing content aren't pulling their weight.**

- The homepage is shedding "requirements management" and "traceability" from top-level messaging to speak to VP buyers. But buyers still search for those terms. The blog and RM Guide need to absorb that SEO weight.
- There was never a clear strategy delineating blog vs. RM Guide. Result: overlapping topics, competing keywords, confused search signals.
- **61% of blog posts rank for zero keywords.** Two-thirds generated fewer than 10 clicks over six months.
- The RM Guide punches way above its weight (12% of URLs → 64% of clicks), but even it is losing keyword rankings over the past year.
- Average position across the whole portfolio: **14.4** (page two). Weighted CTR: **0.38%.**

The problem isn't volume. It's that most of the content is invisible.

## Content Portfolio: What You're Working With

### The RM Guide is the engine

141 pages. 64% of all clicks. Pages rank for **6x more keywords** and generate **~8x more clicks** per ranked page vs. blog posts. The "Writing Requirements" subcategory alone (16 pages) drives 50% of all Guide traffic. The functional requirements page is the single most valuable URL on the site (7,799 clicks).

**Protect these pages.** Any refresh work on top Guide pages needs to be surgical -- improve without breaking what's working.

### The blog is mostly dead weight

1,072 posts. 35.8% of clicks. 657 posts rank for zero keywords. The top performer is the IBM DOORS comparison piece (970 clicks) -- notably a competitive/evaluation-stage article, not the informational content that dominates the blog.

The blog's center of gravity sits on page two (40% of posts in positions 11-20). Moving even a fraction of these to page one would dramatically shift performance.

### Traffic is extremely top-heavy

| Slice | Share of All Clicks |
|-------|---------------------|
| Top 10 URLs | 43.2% |
| Top 50 URLs | 70.2% |
| Everything else (1,163 URLs) | 29.8% |

This means the strategy needs two tracks: **defend the top performers** and **fix or prune the long tail.**

## Execution Guidance

### The blog vs. Guide question

This is the single biggest structural issue to resolve early. The lack of delineation caused the cannibalization problem. Here's a working framework:

- **RM Guide** = comprehensive, definitive reference content. Think "what is [concept]" pages, methodology deep-dives, standards explainers. These should be the cornerstone pages that rank for head terms. Update them regularly. They are the SEO workhorses.
- **Blog** = timely, opinionated, application-specific. Think use cases, customer stories, comparison pieces, trend commentary, "how Jama does X" content. Blog posts should link into Guide pages, not compete with them.

Enforce this distinction in the editorial calendar. When a new topic comes up, the first question is: does this belong in the Guide or the blog?

### Content mix per quarter

Don't just publish net new. Every quarter should include:

- **Net new content** for keyword gaps where competitors are visible and Jama is absent
- **Content refreshes** of high-impression, low-CTR pages (title tags, meta descriptions, intent alignment)
- **Optimization of top performers** to defend rankings and improve conversion
- **Pruning or consolidation** of zero-traffic pages that dilute the domain

A rough starting ratio: 40% new, 30% refresh, 20% optimize, 10% prune/redirect.

### Publishing cadence

Jama currently does 6-8 blog posts per quarter. The goal is weekly. Don't try to jump to weekly in month one -- ramp up:

- Months 1-2: 2 posts/month + 4-6 refreshes
- Months 3-4: 3 posts/month + 4-6 refreshes
- Months 5+: Weekly publishing + ongoing refresh cycle

### Internal knowledge gap

Jama doesn't have centralized personas, pain points, or messaging docs. Part of our early work should include helping them build (or building for them) a lightweight messaging foundation -- even if it's just a shared doc that maps personas to pain points to keywords. This will make content production dramatically smoother.

## Key Risks and Watchouts

1. **Don't break the Guide.** The RM Guide drives 64% of clicks from 12% of URLs. Any refresh work on top-performing Guide pages must be incremental. A/B test title changes. Don't restructure what's ranking.

2. **Repositioning creates a messaging trap.** The new "AI-powered engineering management" positioning hasn't earned search authority yet. We can't abandon "requirements management" vocabulary in content just because the homepage did. The blog and Guide should continue to lead with RM terminology and layer in the new category framing.

3. **Cannibalization needs an audit.** Before publishing new content in any cluster, audit which existing URLs already target those keywords. The worst outcome is publishing a new blog post that steals ranking from a Guide page. We may need to recommend consolidating or redirecting existing posts.

4. **Medical devices: high investment, low return right now.** 119 URLs, avg position 19.9. Before publishing more medtech content, figure out why the existing content isn't ranking. It may be a technical SEO issue (page speed, internal linking, schema) rather than a content quality issue.

5. **LLM visibility is a stated goal.** Fani built LLM attribution tracking in Salesforce. We need to include AEO considerations in every content brief -- structured data, entity-first writing, comprehensive coverage that makes content citable by AI models.
