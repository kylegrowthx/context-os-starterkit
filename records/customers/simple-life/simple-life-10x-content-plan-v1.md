## The Math to 10x

### Where We Are

Strip out the homepage, help center, TOS, and survey pages. What's left is content — blog posts, guides, articles. That's the number we're growing.

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Total organic traffic | 29,156/month |
| Homepage (branded) | 20,946 (72%) |
| Help/support/legal pages | ~2,400 (8%) |
| **Content page traffic** | **~5,800/month** |
| Content pages published | ~50 |
| Pages generating traffic | ~35 (70%) |
| Average traffic per content page | ~166 |

Simple's branded search moat is strong — the homepage alone drives $39K in traffic value. But the content engine is producing at a fraction of its potential. For context, Ruled.me (a single-author keto site with comparable domain authority) generates 52,700 monthly visits from content pages. Diet Doctor generates 130,800. Simple's blog is at 5,800.

### Where We're Going

**Target:** 58,000 monthly visits from content pages by Month 12. That's 10x.

This is a conservative benchmark relative to the competitive set. Ruled.me gets 52,700 from ~200 pages. Diet Doctor gets 130,800 from ~500. We're targeting 58,000 from 212 total content pages (50 existing + 162 new). That requires an average of ~274 visits per producing page — comparable to Ruled.me's 264/page average.

### The Three Levers

The gap is 52,200 monthly visits. Three sources close it:

| Lever | Mechanism | M12 Contribution |
|---|---|---|
| **1. Optimize existing pages** | Refresh 50 pages: improve depth, structure, internal linking, schema | +2,000/month |
| **2. Publish 162 new pages** | 20 clusters informed by competitor data, frontloaded production | +47,000/month |
| **3. Topical authority compounding** | Clusters with 15+ pages boost all pages 15-20%; post-peak growth on early cohorts | +3,200/month |
| **Total new traffic** | — | **+52,200/month** |
| **M12 content traffic** | Existing 5,800 + 52,200 new | **~58,000/month** |

The model isn't fantasy. It's anchored to what competitors actually achieve with similar or lower domain authority:

Simple already outranks Zero (169K), Fastic (100K), Autumn Elle (278K), and Lasta (136K) in domain authority. Simple's content just needs volume and the right topics. The per-page target of 274 sits between Ruled.me's average (264) and Zero's (388). This is achievable with research-informed content optimized from day one.

| Scenario | Per-Page Factor | Success Rate | M12 Content Traffic | Multiple |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Conservative** | 70% of moderate estimates | 75% | ~43,000 | **7.4x** |

## Core Assumptions

### Content Maturation Curve

New pages don't generate traffic immediately. From the GrowthX ROI model, the default ramp:

| Months After Publish | % of Peak Traffic | What's Happening |
|---|---|---|
| Month 1 | 0% | Indexing. Google discovers and crawls the page. |
| Month 2 | 25% | Initial rankings appear, mostly positions 10-30. |
| Month 3 | 50% | Rankings improve, CTR builds. |
| Month 4+ | 100% | Steady-state. Page has found its natural ranking. |

Post-peak growth: +2%/month for 4 months after hitting peak (interlinking effects, authority accumulation), then plateau.

### CTR by SERP Position

Industry averages used for traffic estimation:

| Position | Click-Through Rate | Source |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | 27.6% | Backlinko / Advanced Web Ranking |
| 2 | 15.8% | |

| 4-5 | 6-8% | |
| 6-10 | 2.5-5% | |
| 11-20 | 1-2% | |

### Simple's Ranking Power by Keyword Difficulty

Based on Simple's current performance (SEMRush rank 67,802) and observed positions:

| Keyword Difficulty | Expected Position (M4+) | Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| KD 0-15 | Positions 1-5 | Simple already ranks #1-3 for 12+ keywords in this range |

### Other Model Inputs

| Assumption | Value | Basis |
|---|---|---|
| Page success rate | 80% | ROI model default. 20% of pages won't rank meaningfully. |
| Topical authority multiplier | 1.15-1.20x | Clusters with 15+ pages see a 15-20% lift on all pages. Research shows topical clusters outperform isolated pages by 20-40%. Applied conservatively. |
| Existing page refresh lift | 30% average improvement | ROI model default. Refresh = update content, add schema, improve structure, cross-link to new content. |
| Monthly production rate | 13-14 pages/month | Frontloaded: 16-18/month in Q1, tapering to 11/month in Q4. |

## Risks & Dependencies

### What needs to go right

| Risk | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Calculator/tool pages underperform | High — Cluster 6 drives 20% of total plan | Build 2 calculators in M1, measure performance. If traffic < 1,000/page by M4, shift resources to blog clusters. |
| Google algorithm update penalizes new content | Medium — delays maturation curve | Diversify across 20 clusters. No single cluster exceeds 23% of plan. Quality-first approach (1,500+ word pages, original data, expert review). |
| Production falls below 13 pages/month | High — delays compounding | 10 pages/month minimum still achieves 7x by M12. Monitor weekly. |
| Simple's domain authority drops | Low probability | Brand search moat protects domain authority. Monitor SEMRush rank monthly. |
| Content quality doesn't match competitor benchmarks | Medium | Use competitor top pages as quality floor. Every page should match or exceed the ranking competitor's depth and specificity. |
| Programmatic pages (Cluster 18) get flagged as thin content | Low-medium | Each page must have 800+ words of unique analysis, not just nutrition tables. The "does it break a fast?" angle provides unique editorial value. |

### Dependencies

- **Technical:** Simple's engineering team needs to build and deploy 5 interactive calculator/tool pages. These require frontend development, not just content writing. If engineering bandwidth is limited, use no-code calculator tools (CalcSites, Outgrow, ConvertCalculator) embedded in blog posts as MVP.
- **Content operations:** 13-14 pages/month sustained for 12 months requires a dedicated content team or agency capacity. This is 3-4 articles per week.
- **Access to data:** Refresh program and results content benefit from real Simple user data (average weight loss by schedule, most popular fasting windows, etc.). Anonymous, aggregated app data makes content uniquely valuable and unbeatable by competitors who don't have it.

## Appendix B: Methodology

**Data sources:**
- SEMRush API via MCP, US database, February 2026
- GrowthX ROI Model (cohort maturation, success rates, compounding methodology)
- CTR by position benchmarks (Backlinko, Advanced Web Ranking, industry composites)

**Traffic estimation approach:**
Each cluster's per-page traffic estimate is anchored to actual competitor page performance. For each cluster, we identified the closest comparable competitor pages (same topic, similar content type), measured their actual SEMRush traffic, then adjusted for Simple's domain authority (SEMRush rank 67,802). Where Simple has comparable or higher authority than the benchmark competitor, we used 80-100% of their per-page traffic. Where the benchmark has meaningfully higher authority (Diet Doctor at rank 16,533, Ruled.me at 39,124), we discounted to 25-40% of their per-page numbers.

**Reports used for all 25 competitors:**
- `domain_organic_unique` — Top pages by organic traffic, with keyword count and traffic percentage
- `domain_rank` — Domain-level overview (rank, keywords, traffic, value) from prior competitive analysis
- `domain_organic` — Full keyword list for Simple.life deep audit
