Marcel Santilli (founder of GrowthX) introduced the company's AI-powered content creation services to Turing's marketing and product leadership, demonstrating a workflow engine that combines human experts with AI to create domain-specific editorial, programmatic, and custom content at scale. ## Context

Turing is a talent and AI services company operating multiple business lines under one domain: AI labs (building frontier AI models), applied AI (helping Fortune 500 enterprises build AI systems), and talent sourcing (providing engineers for scaling startups). The meeting was initiated through a mutual contact (Francois, who works with both companies) and brought together Marcel Santilli (GrowthX founder and former CMO at Scale AI) with Weston Hancock (Turing's head of web, SEO, and marketing operations) and Austin Yang (Turing's Chief of Staff leading corp dev and investor relations).

Weston emphasized that Turing's executive leadership — particularly the ELT (Executive Leadership Team) — has clear content priorities: AI labs is the highest-value focus (one deal in this space is worth more than crushing all other content areas), followed by applied AI content for enterprise customers, with talent sourcing content as a lower priority despite its SEO opportunities. This context matters because GrowthX's pitch around distribution and thought leadership for AI labs competes directly with search-driven, highly-verticalized content strategies for applied AI, which is where Weston sees the biggest untapped opportunity.
