## Key Topics

### GrowthX Overview and Jason's Role

### Jesus' Background and Experience

Jesus spent 3 years at HipTrain, a U.S.-based personal training app, progressing from Operations Associate to Operations Manager. He oversees the end-to-end customer lifecycle (sales, support, billing, trainer management) and is autonomous in multi-channel outreach—email, text, call, and LinkedIn. He uses RB2B for prospect discovery and Zendesk for communication. Jesus built and maintains manual lead trackers in Google Sheets, since the volume at HipTrain is low enough to manage manually while capturing conversion source attribution (something their automated tracker doesn't do). ### Outreach and Community Growth Strategy

Jason explained his need: identifying and messaging senior marketers to join GrowthX's AI-led growth community, positioning the relationship as almost "becoming friends" with them. Jesus shared practical examples of tools he's used: SendGrid for bulk email campaigns (used with 2,000+ email lists from previous business), Sales Navigator for LinkedIn targeting, Prospeo for email extraction, Dbounce for email validation, and Clay as a backup enrichment tool. They discussed the importance of offering genuine value as a hook for community participation—Jesus cited an example from an AI-led growth podcast where offering a valuable workflow encourages adoption.

### AI Background and Learning

Jason has worked with AI since 2018 at a company called Kite, which used AI for code completion (similar to what GitHub Copilot and Cursor do now). They experimented with natural language processing and GPT-2.5, but adoption was limited due to user concerns about code privacy and unclear pricing models. Jason still considers this foundational work valuable and emphasized Andrew Ng's machine learning course (from Coursera, titled "Intro to Machine Learning" or "Machine Learning") as essential reading for understanding how AI works under the hood. Jesus discovered AI in 2022 and is currently learning AI automation for data analytics in a bootcamp. He recently built a performance dashboard for HipTrain using Supabase (for SQL data), Bolt (frontend), and ChatGPT—impressed by how quickly he could build something production-quality.

### Communication and Working Style

Jason prefers proactive communication and independent problem-solving. He uses Slack for general updates, text for urgent matters, and keeps Zoom calls to a minimum. He emphasizes giving autonomy and encourages the VA to experiment, noting that mistakes are fine if the instruction was unclear. Jesus mirrors this approach—at HipTrain, he gives brief updates to the founders (CEO/COO) and the team executes. Both value judgment and resourcefulness over detailed direction.
