  - Bridget emphasized need for structured project management: written one-page plan, Slack channel ("Project FindICP"), recurring meetings with key stakeholders, transparency on progress
  - George to meet with mentor this afternoon for additional perspective; has already interviewed Jason and Daniel to understand their needs
  - Team agreement: Tyler needs interim criteria (even imperfect ones) to function this week; formal matrix will be more nuanced next week

### Quantitative vs. Qualitative ICP Criteria

  - **Quantitative:** Series B to Series E stage; domain authority ideally 40+; existing blog/content presence (measured in pages and traffic)
  - **Qualitative (equally important):** Client openness to GrowthX strategic direction and leadership; clarity of internal decision-making authority; realistic long-term vision for organic content; existing blog infrastructure they've invested in; minimal competitive/internal turf wars around content strategy
  - George's framework: Don't filter solely on domain authority or page count; instead assess: How much SEO/content infrastructure do they have? How open are they to pivoting strategy? Are stakeholders aligned? Can they commit to long-term organic growth?
  - Seed and Series A excluded (too unpredictable); large enterprises excluded (rigid expectations, hard to control scope)

### Client Case Studies

1.  **Field Guide (Series B, $30M):**
    - Blog exists but getting ~80 organic visits/month; essentially starting from scratch on content
    - Series B with unique positioning (low competitor count)
    - Team responsive and eager; open to GrowthX taking control of content strategy
    - Less traction quantitatively, but high-quality ICP fit qualitatively — exactly the client who'll trust GrowthX leadership
    - George: Keep slow, don't hard-close; add to end of October list

2.  **GetExcept (Series B):**
    - Domain authority near 40; organized blog doing well (driving majority of site traffic)
    - Super competitive space (digital sales rooms); asking detailed ROI questions upfront
    - Time zone challenges (Madrid, London); appears more difficult to work with
    - Client already knows content marketing; less likely to defer to GrowthX strategy
    - George: Qualitatively problematic despite strong quantitative metrics

3.  **Unnamed camera software company (founded 1995, modern SaaS pivot):**
    - Soft-shipped to GrowthX; understands the service clearly
    - Good access to CMO and team; ready to sign
    - 1995 foundation initially concerned Tyler, but George noted: founders who've pivoted to modern software often more open to new AI solutions and willing to let experts lead
    - Inbound signal is positive; decision: Schedule for November 4th or later
    - Good comparison to Ambient AI (gun detection software on cameras)

### Pricing and Work Stream Structure

  - **Maintain:** $30K base + $15K per work stream (currently $15K for editorial, $15K for programmatic)
  - **Standard approach:** Start all new clients with editorial only; measure success and results for 4+ months before upselling programmatic
  - **Rationale:** Galileo (ongoing ~1 year) is good upsell candidate because results are proven; starting clients on two work streams increases delivery risk and makes partnerships harder if one stream underperforms
  - **Tyler's messaging:** Frame as "We focus on editorial in phase 1. Once we see results, in phase 2 you can add programmatic if it makes sense."
  - **Concern:** Programmatic can finish in 2-3 months; unclear if it's sticky long-term for NRR; team may need to revisit pricing model later
