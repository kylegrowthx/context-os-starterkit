## Overview

- Agents in B2B marketing are still primarily used for research/evaluation rather than autonomous buying
- Companies are rethinking entire go-to-market strategies to incorporate AI/agents, from content creation to buyer journeys
- There's debate around the definition of "agents" - ranging from deterministic workflows to fully autonomous systems
- Governance, trust, and auditing of AI systems are critical concerns as adoption increases

---

## Key Topics

### Speaker Introductions & Company AI Use Cases

  - Omer (Salespeak): Focusing on improving B2B buying experience through intelligent conversations
  - Kevin (Tebra): Reorienting entire go-to-market team to embrace agents, rethinking buying journey
  - Marcel (Growthx.ai): Built coding agent for agentic workflows, powering content/growth for major brands
  - Tooba (Amoeba.ai): Using neurosymbolic AI to analyze data and provide actionable insights for businesses

### Agents in B2B Buying Process

  - Not yet seeing autonomous B2B buying agents, but expected in near future (3 months to 2 years)
  - Buyers using ChatGPT/Claude as personal research agents
  - LLM referral traffic converting 3-5x higher than brand traffic for some companies
  - Shift from SEO to "GEO" (Generative Engine Optimization) becoming crucial

### Agents as Distribution

  - Still mostly reactive rather than proactively distributing content/offers
  - Some companies building towards agent-to-agent distribution in roadmaps
  - Example: Simulating ad performance through agent interactions before running actual campaigns

### Agents as Interface

  - Companies rethinking website strategies to cater to both human and machine visitors
  - Focus on providing elevated, personalized experiences through AI
  - Shift from gatekeeping (e.g., SDRs, forms) to open, AI-powered interactions

### Agents as Operators

  - Amoeba.ai focuses on discovering useful data insights and recommending actions
  - Growthx.ai orchestrates strategy, content creation, and optimization
  - Challenge: achieving human-level quality or better before automating processes

### Governance and Trust

  - Need for establishing trust before full automation/orchestration
  - Importance of auditing mechanisms and periodic testing
  - Potential new role emerging: agent orchestrator/owner within companies

### Impact on Team Structure

  - Shift towards "experience engineers" in marketing roles
  - Growth/demand generation taking lead in AI orchestration
  - New skills required: AI/ML, data science, prompt engineering

### Future Predictions

  - Mixed opinions on B2B buying agents within 12-24 months
  - Shift from predictive to prescriptive insights expected
  - Potential for AI-native companies to leapfrog incumbents in certain areas
