## Key Topics

### Current Onboarding Progress

Liz is one of several new hires (also Hassan, Katia, Patricia starting recently), now on day two. She's reviewed extensive onboarding content including Marcel's workshops and the GEO workshop, and met with Eric (who started ~1 month ago and provides fresh perspective on navigation). She's pushing to complete onboarding meetings by Wednesday of the following week before going out the last two days.

### Account Assignments: Surge AI and Augment Code

Liz will likely join Jason's pod working on two flagship AI clients. Surge AI is GrowthX's largest client by far at $230,000/month—a unicorn that hit $1 billion revenue with zero outside funding and ~50 employees, scaling purely on product excellence with zero dedicated marketing until now. GrowthX's scope is broad: website redesign, CEO podcast appearances, conference presence, editorial content, and event planning. Augment Code is a more standard engagement at $50,000/month where GrowthX runs their full weekly newsletter (new offering with strong potential) plus programmatic pages and editorial. CMO Sylvan worked previously at Segment. The two accounts represent very different dynamics: Surge is "white glove" with open-ended scope; Augment has internal marketing stakeholders.

### Company Structure

Pre-sales operates lean: Tyler handles account executive/business development, Jason runs VP of Growth (marketing + strategy on custom accounts), and a new Events person is starting. Back office handles finance, HR, recruiting, legal, and tech infrastructure (IT/Yousef, EA/Alice). Engineering/Product team is separate. Client-facing "Deliver" org splits into two groups: the Strategy Sprint team (8-week onboarding, led by Eric with Ada on editorial), and regular pods (ongoing delivery). Structurally, there are four main pods: Kyle's, Mara's, Megan's, Jacob's, plus the sprint team.

### Client Engagement: The Sprint Model

New clients sign contracts for 8 weeks + 12 months. The first 8 weeks are intentionally designed as "sprint" or "pilot"—a calibration phase where GrowthX deep-dives the client's business, creates artifacts (brand guidelines, competitive analysis, personas, voice/tone), gains CMS/analytics access, and produces a few early outputs everyone loves. This removes the "why is nothing launched yet?" pressure that hits month-two in typical engagements. After eight weeks, clients either opt out (counted as pilot churn, not true churn) or roll into a regular pod for 12 months with dedicated strategist, managing editors, and content managers.

### Strategist Role: Beyond Reporting

Strategists own account strategy, commercial health, and overall account management. The evolution happening now is pushing strategists to think first-principles: don't just report "here's what we shipped and where it ranked"—ask "what should we drive toward?" and help clients articulate their goals. Role definitions are loose intentionally: CMs generate content via workflows, MEs edit and curate, but who owns keyword topics, agenda-setting, metrics reporting, and creative direction shifts based on pod setup. Panzer (VP of Quality/Content) is the expert on artifact governance and consistency, currently running three accounts as an experiment to help product improve.

### Roles Are Fluid and Evolving

GrowthX roles aren't static. Kyle started as one of the first strategists, ran marketing teams before GrowthX, did early ops work (Andy's role), briefly ran sales, and now leads a pod. Eric (newly hired) has consulting and AI consulting background, leads sprints. Panzer manages CMs across company but is also running accounts. Everyone on Liz's meeting list overlaps in some way. The organization is intentionally flat and skill-based; processes are being refined in real-time.
