## Summary

Marcel Santilli pitched GrowthX to growth equity investors Shelby Herrington and Cooper Harrison at Silversmith Capital Partners, detailing the company's rapid ascent from $3M to $11M ARR in 2025 with 50+ customers, and plans to raise $40-60M to accelerate go-to-market and engineering. GrowthX's differentiation lies in its expert-led content services model combining AI workflows with "forward-deployed experts" — experienced practitioners who guide strategy and maintain quality — rather than pure automation or freelancer marketplaces. Silversmith expressed strong interest given their portfolio ownership of Webflow (a GrowthX customer) and focus on workflow-oriented sales and marketing software; both sides agreed to continue discussions with reference calls to existing customers and detailed financial review.

## Context

Silversmith Capital Partners is a Boston-based growth equity firm that closed their fifth fund at $1.7B in August 2025. They operate across healthcare and technology with a specific focus on sales and marketing software through partners Shelby Herrington (7 years at firm) and Cooper Harrison. Silversmith invests $30-150M tickets, holding both minority and majority positions. Their portfolio includes Webflow, which Marcel mentioned is already a satisfied GrowthX customer providing a strong reference for the quality and market fit of GrowthX's services.

The meeting occurred as part of a rapid fundraising push by GrowthX, which was not originally planned but accelerated when potential investors reached out. Marcel mentioned conducting 40+ meetings in the preceding week. GrowthX's mandate as an expert-led AI content services company — combining proprietary workflows with human practitioners rather than self-serve tools or pure automation — represents a relatively nascent category in the 2025 market for AI-enabled B2B services, particularly in content and marketing.

## Key Topics

### GrowthX Overview and Traction

  - Founded by Marcel Santilli, former CMO with track record of building content engines at IBM, HP, HashiCorp, etc.
  - Launched Sept 2022, raised $12M seed in Feb 2023, now at $11M ARR run rate with 50+ customers
  - 72% gross margins as of August, burning only $3M total to reach current scale
  - Key clients include Webflow, Sentinel One, Abnormal Security, Ramp, Clerk
  - Seeing strong word-of-mouth growth and referrals, including from investors

### Product and Service Offering

  - AI-powered content creation platform with human experts in the loop
  - Combines strategy, AI workflows, and "forward deployed experts" for execution
  - 8-week strategy sprint + 12-month work stream model, $9K-$230K MRR per customer
  - Internal platform manages workflows, research, editing, fact-checking, etc.
  - Evolving toward customer-facing dashboard and expert marketplace

### Competitive Landscape

  - Not losing deals to agencies or other AI tools like Jasper, Copy.ai
  - Main competition is internal teams or DIY attempts with workflow tools
  - Differentiated by end-to-end service model vs. self-serve AI writing tools
  - Planning to launch CheckThat.ai as first standalone product for AI visibility

### Fundraising Plans

  - Considering $40-60M raise to accelerate growth and product development
  - Not demand constrained - could grow faster by increasing sales capacity
  - Want to invest more in go-to-market and engineering
  - Evaluating growth equity partners who can go deep on the business model

### Customer Success and Expansion

  - 120%+ net revenue retention, with multiple recent upsells
  - Long-term customers like Ramp (12+ months, 700+ content pieces)
  - Proactive upsells as customers expand use cases (e.g. Metronome, Superhuman)
  - Focused on ideal customer profile - firing some early misfit customers
