The company is founded by Paul Sinaï and Nicolas Lecomte, who are currently establishing their go-to-market strategy. Nicolas runs GTM operations and is actively hiring a technical writer to lead their engineering blog, while Paul drives the partnership and content strategy conversations. The core tension: Blaxel needs content urgently to establish authority in a competitive market, while GrowthX has moved toward higher-stage clients (Series B+) where they can work more efficiently.

## Key Topics

### Content Requirements and Audience

  - Target: Software engineers, engineering leaders, CTOs from tech companies/startups
  - Focus: Educational content on AI agents, infrastructure, and related topics
  - Depth: High-level technical, not deeply technical (e.g., no extensive code snippets)
  - Examples: "What is an agent?", "What is tool calling for agents?", "Why need a new type of cloud?"

### GrowthX Capabilities and Concerns

  - Can create technical content, but it's a "huge lift" and not scalable for current resources
  - Prefers clients with established blogs and domain authority (typically Series B/C+)
  - Concerned about Blaxel's early stage, lack of existing content, and technical requirements

### Blaxel's Content Strategy

  - Two-pronged approach: 1) Hiring technical writer for engineering blog, 2) Broader content creation
  - Urgency to start content creation for competitive positioning
  - Open to creating initial content during potential 8-week strategy phase with GrowthX

### Partnership Timing and Next Steps

  - GrowthX suggests Blaxel establish blog foundation (10-20 articles) before engagement
  - Blaxel emphasizes immediate need, unwilling to wait until next year
  - Tyler to discuss with GrowthX team, considering Blaxel's alignment with core services
