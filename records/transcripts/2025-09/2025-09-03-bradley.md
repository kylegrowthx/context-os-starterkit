## Key Topics

### Fundraising Process Dynamics

- Both fundraising participants experience the inefficiency and emotional toll of the process.
- Investors maintain ambiguity strategically to preserve optionality, which adds frustration.
- Early fundraising calls are anxiety-ridden and exhausting; after 3-4 weeks, pitches become autopilot and participants mentally check out.
- Humor and rhythm emerge with repetition — knowing when you can mentally disengage during a pitch deck section.

### Marketing Hiring Need

- Surge AI needs a dedicated senior communications/brand leader to partner with founder Edwin and execute marketing strategy.
- Target: Director, VP, or Senior Director level — senior enough to be in strategic conversations with Edwin, but junior enough to be comfortable with hands-on execution.
- Founder bottleneck is the core issue: Edwin owns the company narrative entirely, and team members (Bradley, Scott, Andrew, Nick) can't substitute for him, but also can't sustain marketing initiatives without delegation.
- New hire must first build shared context with Edwin (understanding his worldview, values, and where he wants to be aggressive), then transition to independent execution to unblock Edwin's time.

### Candidate Profiles

- **Profile 1 (Mentioned candidate):** Operating partner background, former managing editor at VC firm, built AI 30/Enterprise Tech 30 list (now a franchise program). Experience managing diverse stakeholders in high-trust (VC) environment. Best fit: someone who has done more than comms, has built programs, navigates stakeholder expectations.
- **Profile 2 (Alternative — Editor-in-Chief path):** Senior managing editor from top publications (e.g., The Information, TechCrunch) who's built programs (Battlefield at TechCrunch). Blocked from upward mobility at current org; ready for head-of-comms role. Requires: multi-industry experience, not single-publication career; has built something visible and successful.
- **Profile 3 (Emerging expertise path):** Someone from AI research institutions (e.g., HAI Institute at Stanford) or close to Fei-Fei Li/Andrew Ng ecosystem. Lower-profile comms execution but high credibility and trust with AI leaders.
- **Profiles to avoid:** Pure product marketers (wrong approach for Surge's narrative-focused, research-credibility strategy); highly senior CMOs from big teams (will find Surge's resource constraints unbearable and leave quickly).

### Marketing Strategy: Show Work, Not Just Tell

- Current challenge: Surge's competitors (Mercore, Handshake) win researcher mind-share partly because researchers don't know there's a better way; they're making sub-optimal decisions.
- Advantage: Researchers (unlike executives) have significant autonomy in vendor selection. Opportunities exist to reach them directly.
- Strategic approach: Move from founder-led narrative (always bottlenecked) to platform model. Rebrand data.notation.tech as an expert platform (like UFC promoting fighters or expert.com model), showcase Surge's work and expertise, and target researchers directly on why professional data annotation services beat DIY approaches.
- Execution: Increase "shots on goal" — podcasts, published processes, documentation, case studies, collaborations. Build reputation through repetition and demonstrated expertise, not messaging alone.
- Interim (60 days before new hire impact): Identify lanes Surge can own without Edwin bottleneck; lay groundwork on data.notation.tech positioning; start content/program building.

### Competitive & Market Insights

- Mercore and Handshake are winning in researcher markets partly through visibility (fundraising announcements, growth metrics) and partly because researchers don't understand the cost of DIY project management.
- Thousands of new AI researchers at Anthropic, Google, Meta, OpenAI are inherently poor at project execution (no budget/resources in PhD phase); they prefer platforms they control, unaware that professional services are faster/better.
- Surge has validated this at Google: Google trusted Surge like NVIDIA — not because of messaging, but because of track record and demonstrated legitimacy.
