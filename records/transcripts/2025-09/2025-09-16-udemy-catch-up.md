- **Original vision vs. current reality:** Jennifer pitched "SkillGraph" as an AI-focused microsite concept, but six weeks in, GrowthX discovered that Udemy's immediate need is blog content strategy (led by Krista) and SEO scaling (Justin spent 4 months on research). The opportunity is more adjacent to GrowthX's core strength than the original pitch.
- **New direction - SkillShift:** Rebranding to "SkillShift" — a publication-style theme that integrates with Udemy Business blog instead of a separate microsite. AI readiness remains a critical topic, but not the sole focus; the broader framework is around skills and organizational learning in the AI era.
- **Stakeholder alignment:** Jennifer likely doesn't know about Justin's work or fully understand the scope shift. The team agreed to keep Jennifer informed about the evolved strategy and emphasize that this phased approach sets up momentum to eventually deliver the original SkillGraph vision.

### Content Strategy and Execution

- **Starting point:** Begin with 1-2 pieces of content on "AI readiness" — an area where Justin's research overlaps with GrowthX's thinking. Calibrate these pieces with the client to demonstrate value and alignment.
- **Leveraging existing work:** Use Justin's topical research, transcript analysis, and course data to enrich content; extract audience personas, pain points, and course correlations from his 4-month research effort.
- **Approach:** Don't start from scratch — apply GrowthX's Strategy Sprint playbook to the existing Udemy blog: analyze structure, identify topic clusters, prioritize content, and produce using client research as input.
- **Calibration method:** Debate between sending drafts asynchronously vs. walking the client through the thinking live. Leaning toward live walk-throughs to build alignment and confidence, especially given the need for momentum in weeks 6-8.

- **Engagement length:** Week 6 of 8. Only 3 weeks remain (this week, next week, the week after).

- **Next steps:** Calibration call with client Thursday or deferred to next week depending on readiness of first piece. If piece is strong, walk client through thinking live to build momentum.

### Account Management and Transitions

- **New structure:** Matthew recently took over as manager for Udemy (from Kyle) while also taking on four other new accounts: Homebase, Seat, Innoel, and Tavern. This has stretched Matthew's attention; Udemy fell behind early in the week.
- **Leverage existing relationships:** Kyle built strong rapport with Carmel at Seat client by providing strategic support during pressure periods; this goodwill can be leveraged for expansion. Kyle offered to help Matthew with Seat account specifics.
