## Summary

LightSource, a Series A direct materials procurement software startup, kicked off an 8-week content marketing engagement with GrowthX designed to drive inbound pipeline generation and improve SEO/AEO visibility in procurement and manufacturing searches. Ben Hoehn (head of marketing) presented the company's positioning—an "explorer" brand akin to Anthony Bourdain—alongside detailed target personas (procurement managers, supply chain leaders, IT, engineering, quality teams at Global 2000 manufacturers) and current lead sources (referrals 50%, outbound 35%, events 28%, inbound 19%). GrowthX will develop content artifacts, tone of voice guidelines, and audience personas by end of week, followed by a product deep dive and weekly strategy syncs.

## Context

LightSource is a Series A SaaS company building software to streamline the direct materials procurement process for enterprise manufacturers. The company manages revision control, quote handling, and flexible data collection for complex manufacturing workflows—customers include Global 2000 and Fortune 500 companies in aerospace, defense, automotive, medical devices, and industrial sectors. Ben Hoehn, head of marketing for just under four months, is leading efforts to scale content production after revamping the website and messaging and launching a new RevOps infrastructure. The company's marketing has been highly fragmented—relying heavily on events, some digital outbound, and word-of-mouth—but inbound pipeline generation remains a significant gap. This engagement addresses that directly: working with George Haikal (GrowthX chief of staff) and Erik O'Brien (account strategist), LightSource aims to create differentiated, audience-first content that resonates with procurement teams, supply chain leaders, and IT buyers while improving both SEO and AI-powered search (AEO) visibility for procurement-related queries.
