## Key Topics

### Traditional Training Challenges & Exec's Positioning

- Training modalities traditionally fall into two categories: effective but hard-to-scale (human role-play, coaching) vs. easy-to-scale but ineffective (content without practice)
- Effective training occurs when practice environment closely resembles performance environment with fast feedback loops (examples: fighter pilots, Olympic athletes, firefighters)
- Exec's platform can work for any conversation-based training but focuses on highest-value business conversations:
    - Revenue-winning (sales)
    - Revenue-protecting (customer success/support)
    - Risk management (management/leadership)
    - Cost reduction (procurement)
- Companies with more human-to-human external conversations are ideal fits for the platform

### AI Role-Play Demo & Features

- Platform enables custom scenario creation in ~90 seconds versus previously time-intensive simulation creation
- Features include:
    - Context-specific scenarios with clearly defined objectives
    - Customizable evaluation rubrics with good/mid/bad performance criteria
    - AI-generated conversation partner that responds realistically to user inputs
    - Screen sharing capability for presenting materials during simulations
    - Post-simulation detailed feedback with specific improvement areas
    - AI coach that can discuss performance and provide targeted advice
    - Multiple scenario creation methods (templates, content upload, voice setup process)
    - Manager review capabilities for certification
- Implementation requires minimal customer effort as Exec's service team helps develop appropriate rubrics through call analysis, interviews, or reviewing training materials

### Competitive Differentiation

- Two types of competitors: embedded AI role-plays within existing platforms (generally lower quality) and standalone AI role-play products
- Exec wins against competitors through:
    - Superior UX/product design ("thoughtfully designed product by a competent company")
    - More sophisticated scenario generation from minimal inputs
    - Advanced features competitors haven't mastered (screen sharing, pitch deck sharing, tool integration)
    - Voice-based AI feedback coach
    - Higher quality scenarios that don't feel like "acting exercises"
- 100% win rate in head-to-head competitions against other AI role-play tools
- Upcoming call scoring product will further differentiate the offering

### Integration Capabilities

- Native integrations with call recording platforms currently being implemented
- LMS integration available but not native (can be implemented quickly)
- Customer-driven integration approach - tech team capable of building any integration customers request
- Most common integration requests are for LMS systems, followed by CRMs

### Coaching Platform vs. Role-Play Focus

- AI role-play is primary business focus as it drives fundraising and SaaS revenue
- Coaching product remains "phenomenal" and is used by major companies like ZoomInfo and LinkedIn
- Sales approach varies by audience - HR leaders typically receive pitches for both products
