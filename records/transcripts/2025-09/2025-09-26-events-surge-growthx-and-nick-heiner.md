## Key Topics

### Recent Event Feedback

Two SF happy hours had strong attendance but a 55% social media share rate (lower than expected). Key learnings: venue needs full privacy/exclusivity to control crowd flow and energy (no shared space with public), adequate shade and cocktail-style seating setup, and a clear guest list with verified check-in. Service quality varies — the venue coordinator from Kaio was not responsive; Surge prefers exploring other vendors. Branding needs improvement: while Surge prioritizes branding, the name tags saying "Surge" for everyone caused confusion (people crossed them out to write affiliations); multiple branded screens and 7-foot banners can address this in future venues.

### Attendee Management

Sullivan Whitely (Surge) is importing all SF event attendees into Airtable CRM (managed by Helena, their "Airtable architect") with a separate view for event invitees to avoid diluting the main CRM with 200+ happy-hour attendees. GrowthX will get access to see this data and potentially contribute improvement ideas. Real value comes from pre-event briefing: before each event, brief the Surge team on key targets (founders, researchers, high-profile engineers) with context (shared alumni, past employers, mutual connections found via LinkedIn). During check-in, flag important arrivals so team can engage immediately. Post-event, Surge team reviews photos (Will provided photos from the last event) to recall conversations and send personalized LinkedIn follow-ups.

### Future Events Planning

Seattle event planned for late October or early November; Sullivan to scout venues. GrowthX will assist with planning, provide branding materials (7-foot banners and swag), and help pull Seattle-specific attendee lists. Expansion cities include LA, Boston, London, and Paris. Key insight: invite lists should be location-specific — sending SF invites to PhD candidates in Ann Arbor (UMichigan) wastes effort. GrowthX to coordinate swag and banner vendors upfront so inventory can be stocked and reused across cities. Local Surge presence helps: Nick and Scott are in Seattle, supporting venue scouting.

### High-Impact Dinner Strategy

Nick wants to shift dinners from broad happy hours to exclusive, curated experiences using the "daisy chain" approach (inspired by conversations with Marcel about how Alex runs high-profile dinners). The challenge: VIPs only commit if other VIPs are already coming (chicken-and-egg problem). Solution: co-host dinners with established high-profile partners (e.g., Cursor, whose CEO/president attended the last event; Arc AGI). Co-host announcement and anchor names make it easier to fill the rest of the list. Venue approach: instead of Quince (restaurant), rent mansions or similar exclusive spaces and hire private chefs. GrowthX to develop a playbook and consult with Marcel on specific tactics and influencer mapping. Goal: create a "daisy chain" where VIPs bring other VIPs. Target: attract high-profile figures like Ilya Sutskever (OpenAI) by mapping mutual connections and warming introductions through co-hosts.

### Conference Planning

Nick is compiling keynote speaker ideas for CEO Edwin Chen to review. Currently has three ideas on a list; working to finalize and get Edwin's approval before locking in speakers. GrowthX following up to ensure momentum on the conference planning.
