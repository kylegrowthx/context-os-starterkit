## Summary

Growth Hackers Stockholm shared 18+ months of SEO work with Lovable, revealing that programmatic "how-to" content from Lovable's 10,000+ remixed projects is generating $60k-$100k monthly revenue despite brand-heavy search dominance and intermittent GA4 data. The key constraint is stakeholder churn and resource scarcity at Lovable—they've cycled through 5-6 stakeholders in two years—which shifted work from shipping to analyzing. GrowthHackers' contract was recently terminated but continues for a few more months; their recommendation is to double down on scaling "how-to" content (millions of untapped projects remain) while maintaining competitor comparison pages with an efficient, automated update process.

---

## Context

George Haikal (GrowthX Chief of Staff) conducted a deep-dive discussion with Berner Setterwall and Tom Ström from Growth Hackers Stockholm, a Swedish-based consultancy with 10 years of experience in growth marketing and technical SEO. GrowthHackers engaged with Lovable (a Y Combinator company in the AI code generation space) starting in Spring 2023 on a roughly half-time contract focused on programmatic SEO strategy and content creation. The meeting was called as part of GrowthX's accelerated onboarding process for Lovable, where the goal is to avoid duplicating efforts and learn from practitioners who have already invested significant effort in the account. George had already met with Lovable's paid team, marketing team, and data analysts, and sought to understand GrowthHackers' strategic thinking, what worked, what was challenging, and what opportunities they see for 10x value creation.

## Key Topics

### GrowthHackers' Background and Lovable Engagement

  - Berner & Tom: ex-startup founders, now running Growth Hackers Stockholm (10 years)
  - Engaged by Lovable in Spring 2023 for SEO analysis and content creation
  - Initial focus: "how-to" section and video content as proof of concept
  - Contract: \~half-time resource, recently terminated (continuing for a few more months)

### Lovable's SEO Landscape

  - Dominated by brand searches across multiple languages
  - Limited generic search terms; difficulty competing with established tech giants
  - Key areas: vibe coding, brand terms, competitor comparisons, partner integrations (e.g., Figma, Supabase)
  - Competitor pages (e.g., vs Bolt, Replit) valuable but require frequent updates

### Content Strategy and Challenges

  - Programmatic approach: leveraging top 10,000 remixed projects for "how-to" content
  - Quality concerns: some content flagged as controversial (e.g., cryptocurrency, social impersonation)
  - Stakeholder changes led to increased analysis vs. content production
  - Limited internal data on project performance (e.g., traffic rankings)

### Performance Metrics

  - GA4 data deemed "directionally correct" but intermittent due to free tier limitations
  - ROI positive on a month-to-month basis for SEO efforts
  - Example: Projects section generating est. $60k-$100k revenue in a few months

### Recommendations and Future Potential

  - Scale up "how-to" content: only 10,000 out of millions of projects covered so far
  - Maintain competitor comparison pages, but with efficient updating process
  - Leverage Lovable's category-defining position for link building
  - Explore content opportunities around integrations (e.g., Stripe, GitHub)
