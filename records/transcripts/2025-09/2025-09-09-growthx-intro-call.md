## Key Topics

### Project Discovery's Marketing Situation

Rachel is the founding marketing hire (started mid-July 2025) at Project Discovery, a Series A cybersecurity company founded by hackers. The company has a small go-to-market team (2 in business development, 1 in sales) and is raising Series B next year. Rachel's immediate priorities are building the marketing funnel, developing SEO strategy, and refining messaging and positioning. She wants to focus on inbound marketing experiments (like whitepapers and editorial content) before investing in demand generation in early 2026. The company's current blog is technically deep but needs more marketing-oriented editorial content. Rachel is also overseeing a website redesign (currently one large homepage, poor for SEO).

### Why Editorial Content Matters

Rachel explained that Project Discovery, as a cybersecurity company, has technically strong blog content that "surgically solves a technical problem" but lacks broader marketing-focused editorial content. The challenge is balancing technical credibility with appeal to a wider audience. Editorial content is her top priority before Series B — ahead of demand generation. A white paper is planned for end of September as an inbound marketing experiment.

### GrowthX's Value Proposition and Positioning

Tyler positioned GrowthX as an end-to-end content creation partner, not just a point solution or agency. The core insight: brands must become publishers. AI search visibility is shifting how buyers find solutions — Semrush data shows AI overview respondents are 4.4x more likely to convert and spend 3x longer on pages. GrowthX's edge is systematic content creation with human expertise in the loop: brief generation, deep research, fact-checking, expert review, and direct-to-CMS publishing. The outcome-focused pitch: "We drive traffic, not just deliverables."

### GrowthX's Two-Phase Engagement Model

**Phase 1: Strategy Sprint (8 weeks, $20K)**
- Develops key artifacts: audience mindshare (buyer personas), company context, and writing guidelines
- Produces 20+ published articles to prove the model works
- Includes SEO audit focused on topic mapping, keyword volume, and difficulty
- Tyler noted the first 4 weeks focus on artifact quality and writing voice; second 4 weeks focus on publishing to validate quality

**Phase 2: Growth Execution (ongoing, $12K/month for startups)**
- Daily publishing cadence (10+ articles/week)
- Continues content creation at scale using proven artifacts and workflows

### The AI Workflows Behind the Scenes

Tyler showed the platform's 12+ step content creation process. Key steps include: brief generation from keywords and editor direction, deep research against third-party sources, fact-checking (chunking the entire article, researching each passage, verifying accuracy), and article generation. Tyler noted that workflows don't replace editors—editors and clients review outputs, set priorities, and guide direction. The platform also uses hundreds of APIs and tokens across multiple tools (LLMs, SEO tools, research APIs). Teams get visibility via Slack, Airtable (content OS), and Notion (documentation).

### GrowthX's Team and Credentials

Marcel (CEO) built AI workflows at Deepgram that drove 24x traffic growth in 18 months, sparking the idea for GrowthX. He's led marketing at IBM, HP, Scale AI, and Deepgram. GrowthX's team includes managing editors and directors who ran SEO for Fanatics and TripAdvisor, editors who built the G2 website, and Chief Content Officer who was Editor-in-Chief of TechCrunch. The company crossed $10M ARR run rate in about a year and is raising a Series B round.

### Client Examples and Results

**Cybersecurity:** Abnormal Security and SentinelOne — GrowthX has built content that shows up in AI overviews and drives inbound.

**Tally (accounting/tax):** GrowthX built the entire blog. Example: technical guides on cost segregation for real estate investors. Accuracy is critical; cross-linking and formatting optimized for LLM scraping patterns.

**Augment Code (AI coding platform):** GrowthX created guides sections. Augment now appears in AI overviews for keywords where it previously lost to competitors like Cursor. Shows how GrowthX surfaces lesser-known tools in AI answers.

**Ramp (expense management):** 18 months of partnership, 1000+ pages created. Includes programmatic content (vendor catalog populated with internal spend data, per diem calculators by city, expense classifier tools).

### Discussion: Artifact Development and Client Input

Rachel asked whether clients provide messaging documents or ICP, or if GrowthX generates them. Tyler explained that GrowthX does a 1-hour kickoff and expert interviews, then ingests client assets (sales call recordings, sales decks, brand messaging, documentation). GrowthX layers in its own research and workflows to create the artifacts. It's a collaboration, not a handoff.

### Pricing and Timeline

**Strategy Sprint:** $20K fixed fee for 8 weeks. Includes publishing during sprint.

**Growth Execution:** $15K/month for established companies; $12K/month for startups.

Rachel noted October would be 4 weeks out from the call, fitting within GrowthX's 4-6 week booking window for strategy sprints. Tentative kickoff date possible if next steps are confirmed soon.
