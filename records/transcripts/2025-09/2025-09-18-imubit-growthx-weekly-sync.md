## Key Topics

### Content Performance and AI Search Signals

Sessions showed solid week-over-week growth at 20%, with engaged sessions up 34% from the previous week. Impressions tracked via Google Search Console show volatility, which Mariana explained is typical when new content launches—search engines throw content out widely, then stabilize performance over time. A new AI mentions report tracks referral traffic from LLMs (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude) and shows positive movement, though with week-to-week volatility. The team agreed to shift this report from weekly to monthly tracking for clearer signals. Crunch metrics for AI presence were reviewed but showed minimal change week-over-week.

### Content Funnel Strategy and Lead Capture

The team discussed using blank sidebar space in blog articles for lead capture or promotion. Rather than implementing a full content funnel immediately, Imubit and GrowthX agreed to start with an MVP that promotes Imubit's existing assessment tool in the sidebar. Dan Khan emphasized that they have infrastructure ready: a 13-email nurture sequence already in place, a new lead scoring system activating within 1-2 weeks, and Clay integration to enrich leads and assess ICP fit. The approach lets Imubit convert readers who are interested in learning more but not yet ready for a sales call.

### Middle-of-Funnel Content and SME Review

Tara Sybrant noted that while GrowthX is strong on top-of-funnel content, Imubit needs to build deeper consideration and decision-stage content. Rather than creating entirely new content, Tara proposed establishing an SME review process with Imubit teams (refining, mining, process industries) and external technical experts to validate and improve existing articles. The plan is to review approximately 5% of articles across different industries, then use those learnings to develop comprehensive writing guidelines that can be applied to the broader content library. This ensures internal SMEs and external prospects both validate the messaging.

### Blog Template and UX Improvements

Mariana proposed several template enhancements: adding estimated reading time at the top of articles, improving link visibility (currently too light blue), increasing font size, and improving line spacing for readability. Tim (Imubit) requested removing publication dates, though Tara pushed back—some readers rely on dates to assess relevance. The team compromised: remove specific dates from articles but ensure all content is kept current through a refresh cycle, and clearly mark dated pieces (e.g., "2026 Predictions") with the year so readers understand the timeframe.

### Visual Content and Platform Screenshots

Mariana requested shareable platform screenshots and images from Imubit to enhance blog content, noting that legacy content showed visual examples that helped readers understand concepts. Tara wasn't sure of the sensitivity/sharability of current platform images but committed to investigating. Mariana identified opportunities like "Advanced Process Control" (4,000+ monthly searches, currently ranking #10) and other top performers as candidates for visual enhancement.

### Upcoming Capabilities Deep-Dive

Tara and Mariana agreed to schedule a longer conversation outside the weekly sync so Tara could understand the full breadth and depth of GrowthX's capabilities and processes. This would allow Tara to map how GrowthX could support content across the entire funnel, from top through decision stage. Mara Leighton offered to schedule this for Tuesday afternoon at 1 PM Eastern.
