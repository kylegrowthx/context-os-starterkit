## Summary

GrowthX kicked off a content strategy sprint with HyperExponential, a UK-based insurance tech company expanding to the US market. The engagement will focus on building thought leadership content and organic visibility for their AI-native risk modeling platform and new submission triage tool, targeting actuaries, underwriters, and IT leaders. Deliverables include a content strategy framework, competitive positioning guides, and business-line-specific content, with a product deep dive scheduled for the following week.

---

## Context

HyperExponential is a specialized insurance technology company with dominant market share (50%) in the UK Lloyds of London market. Recently, the company has begun industrializing and expanding internationally, particularly into the US market, where it faces established competitors (Earnix, Federado, Sixfold, Indico) and significant in-house build competition. David Spitz, who joined as VP of Marketing two months prior, is driving this US expansion strategy and reached out to Marcel Santilli based on GrowthX's track record in B2B content marketing for technical audiences. This is the formal kick-off of a content collaboration between GrowthX's strategic team and HyperExponential's internal marketing team (David and Chris Camps, Product Marketing Manager).

## Key Topics

### HX Company & Product Overview

  - Niche success in UK insurance market with 50% market share
  - Expanding to US and international markets, facing new marketing challenges
  - Core product: AI-native platform for complex risk modeling and pricing
  - New product: AI-powered submission triage to accelerate underwriting process
  - Third offering: Portfolio-level risk analysis and repricing

### Target Audience & Personas

  - Actuaries: Current strength and primary champions
  - Underwriters: Growth target, own P\&L and full workflow
  - IT/Digital Transformation leaders: Emerging importance, especially in US market
  - Challenge: Balancing content for all personas across various insurance business lines

### Competitive Landscape

  - Main competitors: Earnix, Federado, Sixfold, Indico
  - HX has more revenue (except vs Earnix) but less mindshare in US market
  - Biggest "competitor" is often in-house build decisions
  - Positioning: HX as accelerator for in-house builds, not just "build vs buy"

### Content Strategy Needs

  - Increase organic presence and traffic in US market
  - Develop thought leadership around AI in underwriting
  - Create deep, solution-specific content (e.g. competitive guides, business line versions)
  - Potential media property launch (e.g. thecuo.com for Chief Underwriting Officers)
  - Balance between high-level thought leadership and detailed product/solution content

### Current Marketing Efforts

  - Primary lead sources: Word of mouth, outbound, industry events
  - Existing content: \~50 blog posts, annual "State of Pricing" report
  - New format: Roundtable discussions on AI in underwriting

### Challenges & Opportunities

  - Limited capacity for content creation, looking to AI-native workflows
  - Opportunity to leverage deep industry expertise for thought leadership
  - Need for programmatic approach to content across business lines
  - Potential to create sales enablement materials alongside public-facing content
