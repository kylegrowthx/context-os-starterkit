## Summary

Sylvain Giuliani, CEO of Augment Code, expressed frustration about unclear metrics and accountability from GrowthX despite investing $50K/month, demanding GrowthX take ownership of specific growth metrics—particularly driving 300K organic visitors monthly. The team agreed to shift from ad-hoc weekly planning to setting clear OKRs for organic traffic, LLM presence (targeting 28% to 60% in top 20 queries), and driving 5,000 LLM visits per month by end of November. Augment Code is also pivoting from self-serve (60%) to sales-led (40%) growth targeting professional developers at larger companies, with plans for an in-person strategy deep-dive on October 8th.

---

## Context

Augment Code is an AI coding assistant company founded by Sylvain Giuliani that focuses on serving professional developers. The company previously followed a paid-first strategy (paid ads, influencer marketing) in the first half of 2025 but is now entering the earned media phase with GrowthX, having invested in organic SEO, AI visibility optimization (AIO), and newsletter efforts. GrowthX, a B2B content marketing agency ($200K+/year engagements), began working with Augment Code in the second half of the year to drive organic and LLM-sourced traffic. However, Augment Code's leadership lacked clear metrics to measure GrowthX's contribution, creating friction around ROI—a common problem when a SaaS company scales but operating discipline hasn't caught up. Sylvain feels Augment Code is still running operations like a 20-person company despite rapid growth, making it hard to hold agencies accountable. The meeting was called to realign on what success looks like and how GrowthX should be positioned within that strategy.

## Relevance

**To GrowthX Delivery:**
- Augment Code needs a shift from flexible, ad-hoc weekly work to owning specific, measurable outcomes (organic traffic, LLM presence, sign-ups). This is a common demand signal from growing clients and should inform how GrowthX structures future engagements.
- Augment Code is considering deprioritizing newsletter work in favor of high-impact organic/LLM content, which could free capacity for SEO and AIO initiatives. Newsletter is equivalent effort to 3-8 articles per month but isn't moving core growth metrics.
- Website structure work (separating questions, guides, glossary sections) is underway and being tested. Citations are already being generated (Crunch showing 28% presence in top 20 AI coding queries), but the team needs to close the gap to ChatGPT and Google AI results.

**To CheckThat / AEO:**
- Augment Code's biggest growth lever right now is AIO/LLM traffic rather than traditional SEO. Target metrics: 5,000 visits/month from LLMs by end of November (at 4% conversion = 200-300 signups). This validates the strategic importance of AI visibility work.
- Current presence in ChatGPT, Google AI, and Perplexity for "best AI coding tools" queries is being tracked via Crunch but is nascent. Improving LLM citations is now the primary OKR and could be a reference case for CheckThat's value.
- Backlink strategy is working (listicles being cited), but ChatGPT results show evidence of bias toward recent citations, requiring ongoing optimization.

**To GrowthX Business Development:**
- Augment Code is growing fast and expanding headcount, creating internal friction around prioritization and delegation—classic Series A/B scaling problem. This signals they may be ready for deeper strategic engagement (quarterly strategy sessions vs. weekly task-based work).
- Renewal risk is moderate: Sylvain explicitly said "I'm giving you guys money and I don't know whether you fit" and considered canceling. Clear OKRs and weekly dashboard updates should reduce this risk.
- Expansion opportunity: Sylvain wants GrowthX to own more than content—potentially leading Product Hunt launches, CLI launch strategy, and mid-funnel initiatives. This is above current scope but signals desire for deeper partnership.

  - Current newsletter efforts may not align with primary growth objectives
