## Key Topics

### The Content Marketing Problem GrowthX Solves

Tyler explained that in the era of AI search (ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity), traditional SEO-focused content strategies no longer suffice. Companies must optimize for Generative Engine Optimization (GEO)—ensuring they show up in AI-generated answers and become the cited source. According to SEMrush research, AI search visitors are 4.4x more likely to convert and spend 3x longer on site. GrowthX helps clients "get in the answer" by creating well-researched, attributed, comprehensive, and up-to-date content that increases the odds of appearing in AI overviews.

### GrowthX's Two-Phase Service Model

**Phase 1 — Strategy Sprint ($20-40K):** GrowthX conducts deep research, SEO audits, competitive analysis, customer interviews, and ingests call recordings and brand documents to develop rich context artifacts (personas, company understanding) that feed into the AI workflows.

**Phase 2 — Content Creation ($15K/month, starting): GrowthX publishes 10 articles per week with a combination of new editorial pieces and content refreshes. The workflow includes keyword analysis, competitive research, deep research with third-party source validation, fact-checking with confidence intervals, and expert human review. Experts in the loop can make changes but content quality is ensured through multi-step validation. Additional custom work streams (e.g., programmatic content) cost $12K extra.

### Why Traditional Agencies Don't Work

Tyler contrasted GrowthX with traditional models: SEO agencies focus only on search but ignore content creation; creative agencies make content but ignore SEO/GEO; internal teams would take months to build workflows and struggle as AI models evolve. GrowthX is end-to-end: strategy to content creation to daily CMS publishing.

### Customer Success Examples

- **AugmentCode:** 5,000 → 50,000+ monthly traffic in 6 months; their content now shows up in AI overviews for high-value keywords like "enterprise AI coding tools"
- **Ramp:** GrowthX built vendor database pages, per diem calculator, and expense classifier—turning complex spend data into discoverable content
- **Biologica:** Required strict accuracy; GrowthX built a workflow step that simulates expert (CMO) review by learning from past examples and feedback
- **Town (accounting/tax):** GrowthX's deep research and fact-checking workflows were critical for accuracy-dependent content

### Onnivation's Business Model and Market Position

Saket explained that Onnivation identifies disruptive tech companies, becomes their extended sales team in India and the US, and manages C-suite relationships with top 100 strategic customers. They typically take 30-35% revenue share for SaaS partnerships. They already represent companies like Abnormal, Cloudflare, CoreLogix, Contentsware, Drata, and others—all venture-funded with scale and budget.

### The Partnership Opportunity

Tyler noted that GrowthX is currently people-dependent and capacity-constrained (booked 4-6 weeks for client kickoffs) because the platform that enables clients to self-serve hasn't launched yet (expected in 3-6 months). Once it does, partnership acceleration becomes viable. Saket countered that this is actually ideal timing: Onnivation can start bringing GrowthX clients now (as a services partner), and when GrowthX's platform launches, they can scale significantly.

### Revenue and Partnership Model Discussion

Saket asked how Onnivation and GrowthX would split revenue. Tyler explained that GrowthX hasn't done revenue sharing partnerships yet—they haven't had the need given strong inbound demand. However, Tyler acknowledged that services-for-services could work (GrowthX delivers for Onnivation, Onnivation brings clients for GrowthX), and that as GrowthX scales in 3-6 months, they'll need partnerships to grow.

### Test Case and Next Steps

Both agreed to start with one customer. Possibilities include:
- **Disney India:** Onnivation's client with CEO-level buy-in for AI solutions; leadership is visiting San Francisco Oct 9-10
- **Other Onnivation SaaS partners:** Companies like Cloudflare, CoreLogix, or Drata who need content elevation and are already in Saket's network

The plan: Saket identifies a first client and shares relevant context; Tyler coordinates with Marcel on the services-for-services terms; they pitch together and validate fit before committing to deeper partnership economics.
