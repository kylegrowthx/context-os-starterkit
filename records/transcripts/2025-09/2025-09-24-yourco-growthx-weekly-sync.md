## Key Topics

### Content Performance and Google Algorithm Changes

Recent impressions dropped significantly, likely due to Google algorithm changes. However, clicks have increased despite the impression decline—counterintuitive but positive. Keywords ranking on first three pages have shown strong improvement throughout September, with some nearly tripling from 90 to 300+ impressions. The drop appears driven by changes in how Google counts page impressions, not by a quality loss. Non-branded terms continue to drive performance; overall site traffic via GA4 shows consistent growth over recent weeks.

### Content Performance by Topic

Text-offline and open enrollment topics are performing well and on-trend with market demand. Open enrollment content saw 125% growth in sessions, partly due to new content additions and partly due to seasonal timing as people review benefits for 2026. SOPs and other content saw higher engagement. Jessica is satisfied with content performance—none of the results were surprising; they align with expectations based on market trends and timing.

### Content Buffer and Planning

Carrie is staging this week's content for Jessica's review immediately after the call. Next week's articles are already prepared; five are in review with Joe (the copywriter) finishing one more. Building a content buffer is important because Jessica will be away for a month in December (traveling to Asia with a 12-16 hour timezone difference), making weekly syncs impossible during that period. The buffer gives both teams flexibility to manage productivity around her absence.

### Looker Dashboard Improvements

The Looker dashboard needs several readability improvements: remove default numbers cluttering the view, rename columns to shorter titles so URLs aren't cut off, and bucket non-GrowthX articles into cluster/subcluster categories (currently showing as "non-growthx" articles). Jessica wants to see content organized by cluster (communication, use cases) and subcluster so she can analyze performance by content type. This requires pulling from Yourco's sitemap and manually mapping articles; Carrie notes prior attempts at automation broke the data, so manual mapping is more reliable.

### Content Structure Refinements

Jessica is pushing for tighter content structure: shorter intros (3-4 sentences max vs. current ~180 words), heavier use of bullet points for scanning, and direct answers to search intent upfront. The insight is that people come with a problem already in mind (like "how do I handle excessive absence?") and want the answer immediately, not lengthy problem-setting. Carrie is already experimenting with this—she removed intros from one recent article and is testing shorter, more direct structure. The team may update writing guidelines to specify intro length and structure.

### H3 Headers for SEO Performance

Carrie brought up that H3 headers in blog content could help SEO hierarchy. Currently, Yourco's blog templates don't support H3 headers (only H2), so subheadings show as bolded text instead of proper header tags. This limits structural clarity to Google's crawlbots. Jessica initially set up templates without H3 due to formatting concerns on the Yourco site, but Carrie notes H3s transfer over properly when content is copied from Google Docs and pasted into Prismic. The team is considering enabling H3 support and Jessica will research SEO benefits before committing resources.

### PDF Template Management

Yourco's design team recently created nicely branded PDF templates that are performing well. Jessica wants to consolidate all PDFs in one folder (they're currently scattered) and eventually host them on Yourco's site for better indexing and file management. Templates are a high-performing content cluster, so better management supports continued investment. Carrie will check where Joe stores PDFs and consolidate them; both will explore whether PDFs can be tracked even if not hosted on Yourco's site.

### Indexing Issues and Technical SEO

Two Yourco pages haven't indexed despite appearing valid: the "work systems" page (getting traffic but not indexed) and a "checklist for employee onboarding" page (not indexed). Carrie may try minor metadata changes or, following a successful prior strategy, delete-redirect the checklist to a similar indexed page. The team verified other "crawled but not indexed" URLs are actually indexed—those with trailing slashes show without them in Google Search Console.

### Upcoming "Frontline Intelligence" Feature

Yourco is launching "frontline intelligence" by end of 2025 or early 2026—a major feature that will encourage users to upload documentation (call-off data, attendance records, employee survey feedback) for AI analysis. The system will generate insights and monthly reports for management (e.g., "this location has lower attendance than others—try this"). Target audience is initially HR personas at enterprises with 5,000+ employees, with plans to expand to operations and internal comms once the product matures. Jessica has rough product docs from three months ago and will have more detailed feature specs in two weeks.

### Content Strategy for Frontline Intelligence Launch

Carrie and Jessica agreed to start building topical authority around frontline intelligence *before* launch, without mentioning the product feature yet. Content can begin in November, priming the audience for the December/early 2026 launch. This approach lets them run thought leadership, gather intelligence from user feedback, and build newsletter content around frontline worker topics. After launch, they can edit and enrich existing content to link to the feature. A new content cluster will be developed around frontline intelligence once Jessica shares detailed product specs.

### Yourco's Market Position and Competitive Advantage

Text-based communication is Yourco's core strength in reaching blue-collar and frontline industries. HR personas are easier to sell to than operations—they're more tech-forward and better-resourced. Operations teams are typically 5-10 years behind in tech adoption. HR Tech event feedback (from Brodie Meyer) was very positive: buyers are saying "text messaging works, mobile apps don't"—this is *not* coming from Yourco's sales pitch but from buyer feedback. Workers consistently refuse to download yet another app; text is ubiquitous and frictionless. Carrie sees this as a strong market position: older Gen Xers and younger Boomers running operations don't want "newfangled technology" but know how to text.
