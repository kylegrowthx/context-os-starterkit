## Context

Hex is a modern analytics platform serving multiple stakeholders with a complex, holistic approach to analytics that's difficult to communicate succinctly in the market. Peter Ryan (VP of Growth Marketing) and Bryanna Clancy (Marketing Strategy & Operations) reached out to GrowthX after seeing mentions of the agency through industry network connections—Peter recalled GrowthX through a mutual connection at Clay who works with Marcel Baldassano. The meeting was framed as an exploration of whether GrowthX could help Hex solve a specific problem: Hex hasn't built a scalable content engine or flywheel despite significant demand for visibility through both traditional search and emerging AI channels (GEO/AEO). While they know their ICP internally, they've been hesitant to stake a public position for fear of being "cornered" into a narrow product segment, which has slowed content creation efforts. They currently rely on gated content + paid social, an external SEO agency (with alignment challenges), and organic social—but struggle to coordinate these channels and scale content production. The timing of GrowthX's emphasis on AEO/GEO positioning aligned with strategic initiatives Hex was already considering.

## Key Topics

### GrowthX's Offering and Approach

  - End-to-end agency partner with internal AI platform (workflows + expert oversight)
  - Two-phase engagement: 8-week strategy sprint to develop personas/brand voice/editorial roadmap, followed by 12-month growth execution with continuous artifact refinement and publishing
  - Phase 1 artifacts (personas, brand voice, content topics) fed into AI workflows to ensure relevance and accuracy
  - AI workflows are agentic and iterative: research agent generates plan, fact-checking agent validates, quality evaluation loops until threshold met
  - Focus on editorial/blog content as primary distribution channel for GEO/AEO visibility, with potential expansion to custom workflows (Reddit, newsletters, short-form video) after editorial foundation is established
  - Emphasis on content that answers audience questions (framed as prompts they'd ask ChatGPT), designed to show up in both traditional search results and AI Answer boxes

### Hex's Content Marketing Challenges

  - Lack of established content engine or flywheel despite being mid-to-late stage company with market demand
  - Complex platform serving multiple stakeholders (technical and non-technical buyers) with holistic analytics approach—difficult to explain succinctly and fear of being cornered into single segment
  - Current distribution channels: gated content + expensive paid social campaigns, partnership with external SEO agency (misaligned on resourcing and messaging), organic social posting
  - Content bottleneck across full funnel and cross-team (sales and marketing)—producing mid/upper funnel content that has "less and less value" as AI Answers capture that information
  - Internal clarity on ICP exists (marketing ops and revenue ops teams have clear view), but organization is "tentative" and "scared to make a statement" publicly—wants to be "for everyone" and "all stakeholders," which dilutes messaging
  - Bryanna concerned about ability to scale brand thought leadership beyond founder's direct involvement (not just "Barry's mind to mouth to content")

### AI-Driven Content Creation Process

  - Uses company-specific artifacts (personas, brand voice, topic documentation) to inform AI workflows—critical because "that's what allows us to be scalable" and "deliver results more systematically"
  - Continuous updating of knowledge base and artifacts throughout engagement to reflect evolving product messaging and strategy
  - Multi-step agentic workflows that handle research, drafting, fact-checking, and quality evaluation—example from Augment Code shows 7-8 visible steps but multiple sub-steps in background (research plan generation, result synthesis, answer evaluation with re-running if gaps detected)
  - Knowledge base ingests external resources (sales decks, transcripts, Gong recordings, analysis) optimized for retrieval, connected to content topics to keep generation aligned with current messaging
  - Custom workflows beyond editorial (Reddit posting, newsletters, short-form video, guides) built after editorial foundation is strong—example: Augment Code guides section outperformed blog in certain metrics and attributed 300+ product signups
  - Demonstrated results: Augment Code saw 5-10x traffic increase in 6 months, product signups attributed to content, competitor mentions, and AI Answer citations

### Pricing and Value Proposition

  - Bryanna framed GrowthX pricing ($200k+/year implied) as "classic Bain consulting pricing"—concern that AI tooling should reduce costs, not increase them; contrasted with control of API credits and in-house experimentation
  - GrowthX justifies cost by positioning as replacement for: (1) external SEO agency, (2) internal content writers/editors or freelancers, (3) third-party LLM monitoring/analytics tools—bundling these services eliminates tool-stacking complexity and cost
  - Estimated cost: ~10 cents per word (lower than alternatives like hiring freelance writers or building internal teams)
  - 8-week strategy sprint (Phase 1) allows Hex to evaluate partnership, see results, and make low-risk decision before 12-month Phase 2 commitment; Tyler framed this as "low-risk way" for Hex to develop artifacts they could even use in internal workflows if they chose to go that route
  - Tyler acknowledged build-vs-buy question (AirOps, other no-code tools) but highlighted risks: model changes require workflow refactoring, API cost scaling, maintenance burden—GrowthX absorbs these risks

### Competitive Analysis and Next Steps

  - GrowthX to conduct competitive content analysis on Hex's top 3 competitors: Sigma, Omni, and Looker (Hex emphasizes Looker as highly established with ChatGPT integration capability)
  - Analysis will inform specific content strategy recommendations tailored to Hex's competitive positioning in follow-up meeting
  - Follow-up meeting scheduled for Tuesday, September 23rd at 4 PM Pacific (45 minutes reserved)
  - Ashley Stepien (Hex CMO) to be included in next call—currently absent but critical stakeholder for messaging and brand decisions
  - Hex to review GrowthX resources in interim; internal discussion between Peter, Bryanna, and Ashley on potential fit and value proposition
  - Tyler noted intention to be "more focused" in next call and avoid jumping around topics
