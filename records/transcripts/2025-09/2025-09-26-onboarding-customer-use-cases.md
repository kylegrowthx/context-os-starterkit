## Summary

Kyle Gaudreau walked Hassan Rashid and Liz Kushnereit through GrowthX's core service offerings and use cases, explaining how the company helps B2B clients shift from expensive paid search to cost-effective organic channels through editorial content and programmatic SEO. The team discussed why customers hire GrowthX (to broaden channel mix and drive organic growth), GrowthX's shift toward selectivity in taking on repeatable work, and the emerging opportunity to expand beyond top-of-funnel content into mid- and bottom-funnel assets like case studies. Key conversation threads included the complexity spectrum of programmatic SEO, the role of strategy sprints in scoping work, the potential to repurpose customer webinars and podcasts into articles, and how AEO (AI-Enabled Outcomes) visibility is changing content strategy.

## Overview

- GrowthX focuses primarily on editorial content creation and programmatic SEO to drive organic traffic growth for B2B clients
- The company is selective about taking on custom work, preferring to stick to repeatable processes for efficiency
- Strategy sprints (20-30k) are used to align on objectives and determine appropriate ongoing monthly engagement costs
- While currently top-of-funnel focused, there's interest in expanding more into mid/bottom-funnel content like case studies

---

## Key Topics

### Client Pain Points & Value Proposition

  - Many clients overly reliant on paid search with diminishing returns
  - GrowthX helps broaden channel mix, particularly organic, more cost-effectively
  - End-to-end creation, strategy, and execution allows faster growth and signal
  - Typical client profile: Looking to scale beyond paid, struggling with organic

### Core Service Offerings

  - Editorial content: Primary focus, 5-10 articles/week, $9-15k/month
  - Programmatic SEO: Templated pages at scale, similar pricing to editorial
  - Custom work: Occasionally taken on but often regretted due to resource drain
  - Strategy sprints: 20-30k to align on objectives and determine ongoing costs

### Content Types & Approach

  - Editorial: Longer-form blog posts tailored to client personas
  - Programmatic: Scalable templated pages (e.g. per diem calculators for RAMP)
  - Experimenting with repurposing client webinars/podcasts into articles
  - Primarily top-of-funnel focus currently, but interest in more mid/bottom-funnel

### AI/LLM Considerations

  - Rapid visibility possible in AI-generated results (e.g. AugmentCode example)
  - Exploring how to craft content knowing it may be directly quoted by AI

## Action Items

- Continue refining strategy for evolving client engagements at 6+ month mark
- Explore opportunities to expand into more mid/bottom-funnel content creation
- Consider how to measure and report on AI/LLM visibility and performance
- Evaluate potential to offer more social media content creation services in future
