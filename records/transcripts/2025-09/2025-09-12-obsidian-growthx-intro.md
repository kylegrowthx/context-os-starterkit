## Key Topics

### AI Answer Citation: A Shift in Search Behavior

Marcel outlined how search behavior has fundamentally shifted. Where users once clicked blue links for 30 seconds, they now spend 15-20 minutes reading AI-generated answers—and they psychologically treat those answers as gospel truth from experts. This creates a massive opportunity for companies like Obsidian: you must own long-tail topics with depth, nuance, and fresh data to appear in AI answers. Content freshness is now critical (at least every three months).

### GrowthX's Methodology: Strategy + Agentic Workflows + Human Loop

The engagement starts with a 2-week kickoff and discovery phase where GrowthX immerses in the client's world: interviews with subject matter experts, ingestion of sales calls and product documentation, competitive research, and persona mapping (e.g., "the strategically overextended CISO" for security clients). From there, GrowthX builds agentic workflows that do deep research, cite reputable sources (NIST, HHS, academic studies), and generate initial content drafts. A human editor then reviews, guides, tweaks, and refines—thinking of it as a co-pilot model, not a replacement. This keeps quality high while scaling volume.

### Examples of Client Success and Quality

Marcel shared specific proof points. Augment Code went from zero AI visibility to being mentioned in AI answers for "AI coding tools for enterprise"—6+ pieces of GrowthX content cited in a single AI response. The /guides section of Augment Code's site now drives more than double the blog traffic it did before. Abnormal Security's glossary pages rank well and stay current. Webflow integration pages were all powered by GrowthX workflows. Talent.com handles tax legislation accurately. Sunbit and DataGrid, both established domains, saw growth acceleration after GrowthX took over content strategy.

### Webflow Integration and Publishing Automation

Tina asked directly: "Can we click a button to publish?" Marcel confirmed GrowthX has tight Webflow integration (Webflow CPO is a GrowthX angel investor; ~33% of GrowthX customers use Webflow). Publishing workflows are custom-built and seamless, which matters because Obsidian is already on Webflow.

### Data Privacy, IP, and No Model Training

Tina raised a fair concern: if GrowthX works with competing security companies, will their internal data leak? Marcel was explicit: no. GrowthX maintains separate teams for competing clients so insights don't cross-pollinate. Private data (GA4, sales intel, internal docs) is never shared. Competitive research happens on publicly available info only. Critically, GrowthX does not train internal models on client data. Workflows (like "deep research") are generalizable across clients, but the sources, context, and product knowledge are entirely client-specific and stay confidential.

### Pricing and Commitment Structure

The model is designed to de-risk. The 8-week strategy phase is a one-time $20,000 investment—low commitment, high clarity. During those 8 weeks, GrowthX sets up the platform, builds context, designs workflows, calibrates quality with one pilot piece, and aligns on a 6-month topic strategy. Daily publishing starts by week 4-5. At the end, either side can walk. If it clicks (and GrowthX says 100% of clients have continued so far), the engagement moves to a 12-month growth execution phase starting at $15,000/month. This covers daily publishing, graphics, SEO optimization, AI visibility monitoring (CheckThat), dashboards, and reporting—a fully managed, outcomes-based service.

### Obsidian's 68,000 Opportunity

Marcel and Tina talked through Obsidian's competitive landscape: AppOmni (smaller), CrowdStrike (huge, partial overlap). But the real gap is long-tail coverage. Marcel identified 68,000+ potential content topics where Obsidian has zero content but competitors or customers are searching. Example: "What is a honeypot?" (a 2019 article—stale and ready to be beaten with fresh, comprehensive coverage). The strategy phase would systematically map these and plan execution.
