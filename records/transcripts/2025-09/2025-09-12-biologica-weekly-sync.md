## Key Topics

### Content Quality Issues

  - Inconsistent article quality (e.g. menopause brain fog article good, alcohol article poor)
  - Overuse of colons, complex sentences, and forced relatability examples
  - Some unrealistic suggestions in health-related content (e.g. logging drinks in real-time)

### New AI Pipeline Development

  - Current process: splits outline into sections, applies research, reassembles (can cause flow/repetition issues)
  - New pipeline (\~2 weeks): includes validators to check adherence to guidelines, scores performance, iterates until criteria met
  - Expected to significantly reduce editing time (from \~1 hour to \~5 minutes per article)

### Writing Guideline Refinement

  - Current guidelines too long (\~4000 words); aim to reduce to 1500-2000 words
  - Focus on declarative, shorter sentences with less forced relatability
  - Separate guiding principles from specific rules (e.g. "no colons ever")
  - Consider limiting relatability examples to article introductions only

### Potential Future Improvements

  - Experiment with writer-specific tones (e.g. "Liz's voice")
  - Possibility of fine-tuning AI model on \~25-30 pieces of existing content

### Medical Review Process

  - Nancy reviewing medical content; generally positive results with minimal material changes needed
