## Summary

Tyler Pavlas introduced GrowthX's end-to-end content partnership to Ghita Alami from ZeroEntropy, positioning it as an alternative to Profound.ai for scaling technical content at higher quality while reducing review burden. ZeroEntropy creates 5-10 programmatic SEO articles per week plus one flagship deep-dive every 3 weeks, and struggles with contractor review time and maintaining technical rigor. GrowthX pitched a two-phase engagement: $20K for an 8-week strategy sprint plus ongoing content production, followed by $12K-$15K/month for daily publishing through AI workflows staffed with human experts (including former TechCrunch editors). ## Context

ZeroEntropy is an AI infrastructure startup that builds custom ranking models (like their re-ranking model released in 2025). They operate primarily through technical content marketing to attract both free-tier signups and enterprise sales conversations, positioning themselves as a discoverable alternative to larger LLM providers like OpenAI. The company is in growth mode—Ghita Alami, their co-founder, recently raised seed funding and is aggressively pushing product-led growth and SEO visibility initiatives.

Tyler Pavlas from GrowthX (serving as account executive while founder Marcel Santilli focuses on Series B fundraising) was introduced by a mutual investor. The call was a first exploratory meeting to determine if GrowthX's human-in-the-loop, research-driven content process could outperform ZeroEntropy's current solution (Profound.ai with contractor review) in both quality and speed-of-review.
