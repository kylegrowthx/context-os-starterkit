## Summary

GrowthX reviewed its sales pipeline and strategy process, identifying critical gaps between sales and delivery on client expectations and CMS/content readiness. The team decided to maintain 2 kickoffs per week (rejecting a push to 3 per week) and committed to requiring upfront documentation of client capabilities—existing content, CMS status, and site infrastructure—before contract signature. Tyler will create a pre-contract qualification checklist, George and Tyler will revive the ICP matrix with recent customer insights to identify which early-stage founders are likely to succeed long-term, and Andi will compile a list of past client issues to flag red flags during sales conversations.

---

## Context

GrowthX is in the middle of closing a fundraise round, and investors are drilling into churn metrics. The team has experienced churn primarily from pre-seed and seed-stage companies—technical founders and engineering-led teams without go-to-market experience—though some seed-stage companies (town, Biologica, a16z portfolio companies) have been excellent customers because they come from founders with successful track records who understand how to build long-term content strategies. The core tension: GrowthX can technically do 3 strategy sprints per week if it had perfect processes, but every engagement outside pure editorial (adding PSEO, programmatic strategy, design, CMS setup, engineering resources) adds significant unbudgeted work. The sales team has been signing clients without communicating to delivery what was promised, and delivery discovers misaligned expectations only at kickoff—by which point it's too late to avoid either over-committing or having an angry client.

---

## Relevance

**To GrowthX Delivery:** Clients are arriving at kickoff with misaligned expectations about scope (editorial-only vs. programmatic) and infrastructure readiness (many lack CMSes or existing content strategies). George and Tyler must now coordinate on ICP to avoid accepting clients who require underfunded heavy lifting. The team will focus on process improvements (pre-contract checklist, clearer handoff documents in HubSpot) before scaling to 3 sprints per week. Lovable, Understory, and Udemy were sold things beyond editorial in phase one without delivery being aware—need to establish "work streams" field in HubSpot as source of truth.

**To CheckThat:** No direct mention, but clients like Augment Code are mentioned as examples of what's possible with programmatic/technical integration—potential use case for demonstrating AI visibility tools to prospects.

**To GrowthX Business Development:** Churn is concentrated in pre-seed founders without go-to-market experience; successful seed-stage customers tend to be either in Marcel's network or from operators with previous exits. The team should prioritize founder pedigree and go-to-market maturity in early-stage screening. Current pricing ($20K for phase-one editorial sprints) doesn't account for CMS setup or design work—phase-two pricing for additional lanes needs to be established upfront to manage scope creep.

## Key Topics

### Client Qualification and Expectation Setting

  - Current process shows gaps in communicating client expectations from sales to delivery team
  - Need for a document outlining key information required before client contract signing
  - Suggestion to incorporate more qualifying questions from kickoff into sales conversations
  - Importance of understanding client's existing content, CMS, and resources to better prepare for delivery

### ICP Matrix and Sales Process

  - Existing ICP matrix needs refinement and consistent application in sales process
  - George and Tyler to collaborate on updating and implementing ICP criteria
  - Discussion on whether to disqualify companies based on specific criteria (e.g., traffic, funding stage)
  - Caution against blanket disqualification of early-stage companies, as some have been successful clients

### Pricing and Scope for Additional Services

  - Debate on whether to establish pricing for additional lanes beyond editorial in phase two upfront
  - Suggestion to test including additional services in initial discussions and pricing
  - Current disconnect between sales commitments and delivery team awareness for some clients (e.g., Lovable, Understory)
  - Need to improve documentation and communication of agreed work streams in HubSpot

### Capacity and Growth Considerations

  - Team leans towards maintaining current capacity (2 kickoffs/week) rather than increasing to 3
  - Focus on improving qualification and handoff processes before expanding capacity
  - Concerns about potential churn if team overextends without proper processes in place

### Examples and Case Studies

  - Need for more diverse examples beyond Augment Code to showcase capabilities to potential clients
  - Suggestion to find and highlight wins from other clients, especially for editorial-focused engagements
