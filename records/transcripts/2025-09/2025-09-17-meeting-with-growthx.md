## Context

Obsidian Security is a cybersecurity company currently running a hybrid AI-assisted content strategy where they use LLMs to inform briefs, templates, and SEO formulas, but rely on their internal PMMs and writers to execute the final content. They've experimented with programmatic content (like AI-generated integration pages with human-written bullet points) but find their current approach resource-constrained—they want to scale content production without turning their team into a "content machine." This meeting was a sales/product conversation to help Obsidian evaluate whether GrowthX's Phase 1 strategy sprint is the right fit, following an earlier call between Marcel (GrowthX CEO) and Tina.

Tyler walked Obsidian through GrowthX's end-to-end content workflow—from SEO audits and artifact creation (company context, personas, brand voice guidelines) through daily publishing—using concrete examples from clients like Augment Code, Ramp, and Town (an accounting SaaS). The key selling points were GrowthX's ability to handle both high-volume editorial (e.g., 90 guides for Augment Code, 10X traffic growth in 6 months) and programmatic content at scale, combined with rigorous fact-checking, internal linking, and structured data implementation. Obsidian's main concern was avoiding Google penalties and content that feels "too AI-generated" or easily replicable by competitors—a concern Tyler addressed by emphasizing GrowthX's multi-step validation and SEO best-practices approach.
