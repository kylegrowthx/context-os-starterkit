## Summary

GrowthX and Deepgram's leadership team finalized a 4-cluster content strategy focused on voice AI builders (general and healthcare-specific), enterprise buyers in drive-thru and pharmacy, and developer resources. The strategy emphasizes a shift from API-centric messaging to solutions-oriented positioning for enterprise audiences, with specific plans to boost SEO rankings for high-intent keywords like "speech-to-text API" through a combination of long-form blogs and programmatic content creation. Deepgram committed to reviewing GrowthX's initial strategy artifacts (writing guidelines, audience personas, company context) and keyword research, with Hasan sharing access to the Notion product marketing version to avoid duplicate content production.

## Context

Deepgram is a voice AI platform that provides speech-to-text and related APIs for developers and enterprises. GrowthX was brought in week one to handle Deepgram's content strategy and marketing execution in an eight-week engagement. This is Deepgram's week-two sync, with new head of digital marketing Justin Epstein (formerly at Databricks/Tecton) joining the team just two days before the meeting. Aida Knežević from GrowthX had just shared three core strategy artifacts and was presenting the Atlas platform for content generation, along with proposed four-cluster content strategy to shift Deepgram's positioning from purely API/developer-focused messaging to capture multiple buyer personas across verticals (healthcare providers, enterprise QSR operators, and new developers). Deepgram has existing brand awareness and substantial traffic but is losing rankings on key commercial terms and needs to appear in AI search results to remain competitive.
