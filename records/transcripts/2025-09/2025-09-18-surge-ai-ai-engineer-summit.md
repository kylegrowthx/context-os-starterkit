## Key Topics

### Positioning Surge in the AI Ecosystem

George framed Surge as filling a critical gap in the summit's speaker roster by representing the "data people" of the AI ecosystem. While the summit features model makers (Anthropic, OpenAI) and builders (Cursor, Factory, Augment Code), Surge uniquely represents the data infrastructure and post-training expertise. This positioning allows the keynote to remain on-theme while introducing a differentiated perspective. Edwin's expertise in post-training sets Surge apart from competitors like Mercor, which emphasizes talent and labeling rather than the science of post-training itself.

### The AI Engineer Summit Format and Audience

The November summit targets AI engineers and builders, with confirmed speakers from major labs, AI coding platforms, and enterprise perspectives (McKinsey, Goldman Sachs). Most speakers are CEOs or senior executives. The organizers are deliberately positioning the event against typical conference formats by requiring "strongly stated opinions" and TED Talk-style presentations rather than fireside chats. Content is engineered for YouTube distribution and algorithmic reach (hundreds of thousands to millions of viewers online vs. 700 in-person), so keynotes are typically 20 minutes to optimize for attention span.

### Co-Presenter Strategy

George proposed bringing a current Surge customer—specifically mentioning one of the biggest AI coding tools (Swyx confirmed Cursor has a booth and is sending people)—on stage as a co-presenter. This grounds post-training theory in real-world application and makes the keynote more relevant to a coding-focused audience. The format would be interactive and opinionated, with both speakers sharing their strongest perspectives, not a passive fireside chat.

### Sponsorship Options

The morning workshop slot sold out since Ben and George's last conversation. The primary alternative is Platinum sponsorship, which includes workshop access, a booth with its own amplified stage, two scheduled booth sessions, demo stations, and presentation area. This is positioned as equivalent in value to a keynote slot. Lanyard sponsorship is also available but less prominent. George indicated that a keynote slot is preferable for establishing Edwin's voice and media presence, but agreed to evaluate all options with the Surge team.

### Broader Context on Surge

George highlighted that Surge has been "building in silence"—operating without external funding, achieving $1B ARR in five years, driven by a mission to steer AI responsibly rather than for profit. Now that Surge has clear customer evidence and is ready to communicate their story, the AI Engineer Summit keynote is part of a deliberate media tour launch (with prior appearances on No Priors and 20VC, and Moral Fabric Media upcoming) to establish Edwin and Surge as thought leaders in post-training.
