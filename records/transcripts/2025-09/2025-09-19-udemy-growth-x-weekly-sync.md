## Context

Udemy is a major B2B learning platform that competes directly with Coursera for instructional content and course-related resources. This is a $200k+ engagement for GrowthX to build a comprehensive content strategy pillar around AI transformation and professional skill development. Udemy's Krista Chism, Glenn Lehrman (who manages contracts), and Justin Luebke (who leads topic and keyword research) are driving the engagement on their side, with approval authority from higher levels. Krista flagged this directly because instructor participation is time-intensive on Udemy's end and must be planned upfront to avoid workflow bottlenecks—she doesn't want the team to deliver content that breaks from Udemy's established brand promise of instructor-driven differentiation.

## Key Topics

### Instructor Integration in Content Strategy

  - Krista emphasized instructors as the cornerstone of Udemy's content differentiation; they're involved in every piece Udemy currently produces
  - Current process includes instructor commentary programs (reviews for blogs), course transcript synthesis, and instructor-led video/social content
  - Bob Hunt recently took over instructor series work for video and social media, showing this is a structured program
  - GrowthX needs to define a repeatable process for instructor involvement in the new content strategy before production accelerates
  - Potential approaches: spotlight instructor stories, leverage course transcripts as research, repurpose existing course page data
  - Timeline for defining this process is critical—instructor work is resource-intensive on Udemy's end and delays could bottleneck the entire SoW

### Human-Generated Content Scoring

  - Udemy uses internal tool (Writer) to ensure 95% human-generated content score
  - Serves as protection against potential future AI content penalties
  - GrowthX's sample content scored 100% human-generated
  - Focus on customer-centric, value-driven content aligns with Udemy's goals

### Website Architecture Proposal

  - Recommended approach: business.udemy.com/skillshift
  - Balances SEO value, user experience, and technical constraints
  - Subnavigation structure to maintain hierarchy and distinct branding
  - Discussion on including "skillshift" in URL structure vs. using "blog"
  - Team leans towards branded blog concept (e.g., "Skillshift blog") with clear navigation and CTAs

### Content Differentiation Strategy

  - Udemy can't compete with Coursera's quantity (140,000+ articles)
  - Focus on quality and unique instructor-driven content as differentiator
  - Blend AI efficiency with human expertise and instructor insights
