## Summary

GrowthX and Understory aligned on a tiered GTM framework to manage audience fatigue across breakfast/dinner events, workshops, and community promotion campaigns. The team established a 2-month cooldown period for outreach, decided to test expanded workshop audience targeting (50+ employee filter), and committed to building an always-on trigger-based campaign for AI/SEO topics using social engagement signals.

---

## Context

GrowthX is scaling its events and community strategy after early wins with the KC workshop, which leveraged the co-host's pre-primed audience. However, high-volume event outreach risks audience fatigue and repeated contact with the same people. The meeting brought together the GrowthX team (Jason Gong, Gloria Willis, Jesus Yepez, Tyler Pavlas, Danni Roseman) and the Understory partnership team (Naufal Nugroho, Mark Lim, Alexander Fine, Natalia Moura Gaal) to design a framework that separates event tiers, implements audience controls, and explores community promotion tactics without over-contacting.

The core tension was balancing growth (reaching more people across events) with sustainability (preventing fatigue and maintaining data quality). The discussion revealed that current workshop targeting criteria—$5M+ funding filter—excluded many viable companies, and that junior-level participants showed strong engagement with workshops. The team also identified the need for a systematic approach to managing multiple outreach initiatives under a single cooldown and deduplication system.

## Key Topics

### The Audience Fatigue Problem

  - **Challenge:** High-volume event outreach risks fatiguing the audience.
  - **Solution:** Proactively share future event dates in positive replies to capture attention when engagement is highest.

### New GTM Framework

  - **Priority 1: Breakfasts & Dinners**
      - **Audience:** Exclusive (Director, VP+).
      - **Channels:** LinkedIn, personal messages.
      - **Rationale:** High-value events for the sales funnel; require a strict, high-quality audience.
  - **Priority 2: Workshops**
      - **Tier A (Broad):** Open to anyone in growth.
          - **Channels:** LinkedIn, email.
      - **Tier B (Co-Host Focused):** Targets the co-host's followers.
          - **Rationale:** Leverages a pre-primed audience, which drove the success of the KC workshop.
          - **Example:** Fletch workshop will target a list scraped from the co-host's 67k+ followers.
  - **Priority 3: "Always-On" Community Promotion**
      - **Tactic 1: Asset Repurposing**
          - Promote the KC workshop recording to a non-overlapping audience.
      - **Tactic 2: Trigger-Based Outreach**
          - **Concept:** Message users who engage with high-volume LinkedIn posts on AI/SEO.
          - **Offer:** A relevant workshop recording (e.g., ChatGPT & SEO with Webflow's evangelist).
          - **Rationale:** Captures users with demonstrated, real-time interest in a topic.

### Audience Expansion & Metrics

  - **Proposal:** Expand the workshop audience by removing the $5M+ funding filter and using a 50+ employee filter instead.
  - **Decision:** Test this change for workshops only.
  - **New Metric:** Track workshop registrants by title/seniority to measure the impact of the audience expansion.

  - **Cadence:** At least one breakfast and one workshop per month.
