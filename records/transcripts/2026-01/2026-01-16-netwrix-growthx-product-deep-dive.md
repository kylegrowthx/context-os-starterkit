## Overview

- **New Strategy:** Netwrix is unifying its portfolio under two pillars—Data Security and Identity Security—to solve the "attackers log in, not break in" problem, replacing the ineffective six-solution bundle approach.
- **Product Focus:** The SaaS platform, OneSecure, is the future and primary demo tool. Its on-prem predecessor, Auditor, remains key for compliance-heavy clients and the Varonis takeout play.
- **Content Opportunity:** OneSecure's ~80 reports are a direct source of customer pain points, creating a high-volume "how-to" content strategy to drive organic traffic.
- **Cross-Sell Challenge:** A low cross-sell rate (e.g., 9.5% Auditor → Endpoint Protector) is a major focus. GrowthX will analyze multi-product customer data to define a successful ICP and replicate their journey.

---

## Key Topics

### Challenge: Fragmented Portfolio & Low Cross-Sell

- Netwrix's portfolio of ~18 acquired products is fragmented, with low cross-sell rates.
- **Example:** Only 9.5% of Auditor customers also own Endpoint Protector.
- **Root Cause:** Most customers are on-prem, making product integration difficult.
- **Previous Attempt:** Six solution bundles failed because they were not integrated, and sellers struggled to position them.

### Solution: New Unified Strategy

- **Core Problem:** Attackers compromise identities to log in and steal data. Data security and identity security are often treated as separate disciplines.
- **Netwrix's Differentiator:** Unifies these two pillars to provide a holistic solution.
- **Ideal Customer Profile (ICP):**
  - **Microsoft Shop:** Uses Active Directory (AD) or Entra ID as the identity control plane.
  - **Heterogeneous Environment:** Has non-Microsoft data sources (e.g., NetApp, S3) where Microsoft's native tools are ineffective.
- **New Website Navigation:** Will reflect the two pillars, with a "Platform" nav for categories and a "Solutions" nav for 45+ specific use cases.

### Product Deep Dive: OneSecure (SaaS) vs. Auditor (On-Prem)

- **OneSecure (SaaS):** The future of the company.
  - **Focus:** Balanced mix of security and compliance.
  - **Key Features:**
    - **Risk Assessment:** Advanced version has >200 security checks with AI-based remediation recommendations.
    - **Reporting:** ~80 predefined reports across Activity, Compliance, and Risk categories.
    - **Sensitive Data Discovery:** Identifies and classifies sensitive data in SharePoint Online and Windows File Servers.
    - **Copilot Security:** Reports on Copilot interactions and sensitive data access to enable secure AI rollout.
- **Netwrix Auditor (On-Prem):** The legacy flagship product.
  - **Focus:** Primarily a compliance tool.
  - **Maturity:** ~20 years old and more feature-rich for compliance reporting.
  - **Strategic Role:** Supports the Varonis takeout play by offering a stable on-prem alternative to Varonis's forced cloud migration.

### Content Strategy Opportunities

- **"How-To" Content:** OneSecure's ~80 reports are a direct source of customer pain points, creating a high-volume content strategy.
  - **Examples:** "How to see permissions that were changed," "How to export a report of configurations."
- **Sensitive Data Focus:** Content can be built around specific data types (e.g., CAD files, source code) and their associated risks.
- **Varonis Takeout Play:** A key strategic focus. GrowthX will analyze Netwrix's internal competitive docs to create content targeting Varonis's on-prem customers.
- **Competitor Analysis:** Analyze content from competitors like ManageEngine to identify successful strategies and white space, without necessarily creating direct comparison pages.
