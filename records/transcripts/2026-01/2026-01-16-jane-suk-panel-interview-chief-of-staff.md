## Key Topics

### Marketplace Pilot Recommendation

Jane proposed a Timebox pilot to explore building a scalable marketplace model. The current challenge: GrowthX has strong demand but limited scaling ability due to headcount constraints. The marketplace approach draws inspiration from companies like Surge and Outlier—leveraging external human experts coupled with AI feedback loops to scale output.

Timing is strategic. GrowthX is financially stable (~$14M ARR), making now ideal to test this proactively rather than waiting until market pressures force rushed adoption.

### Success Criteria and Risks

Four conditions must be true for the model to work:

1. **Quality must hold** - External contributors need to meet the same bar as internal teams, measured by rubric scores, NPS, and rework rates
2. **AI must improve** - The system should show learning over time through faster turnaround and reduced human intervention
3. **Right people, right tasks** - Matching contributors to work by expertise, not just availability
4. **Margins must work** - Path to flat or better margins despite added marketplace costs

Key risks: quality drops damaging customer trust, internal team attrition if perceived as replacement, operational complexity slowing core business, and data/compliance barriers (California labor laws prevent platforms like Outlier from accepting contributors there).

### Phased Operating Plan

**Phase 1 - Discovery:** Small cross-functional team (6-8 people) from finance, delivery, ops validates assumptions and baselines internal KPIs. Jane suggested a quick MVP using freelancer platforms to benchmark output against internal teams.

**Phase 2 - Pilot Design:** Narrow scope—1-2 task types, 3-5 low-risk customers with opt-in. Marketplace handles only overflow work. Quality rubrics, routing logic, and financial models finalized before CEO approval to launch.

**Phase 3 - Pilot Execution:** Monthly checkpoints. Month 1 focuses on quality and customer feedback. Month 2 on operational performance and routing reliability. Month 3 (or more) for final decision: scale, iterate, or stop.

### Feedback on Timeline and Approach

Marcel pushed back hard on the timeline. Three months to decide if the pilot works is too long when this is core to the business. The question isn't whether to build a distributed output-based workforce—it's how to make it work and scale sustainably.

Key points from Marcel:
- No one scales AI + human-in-the-loop work with FTEs—it takes months to find and ramp them
- Scaling through employees creates cascading costs: management layers, more burn, less productivity
- This isn't a "pilot to decide if we should do this"—it's finding the right model quickly
- Speed is paramount; accelerate time to learning

Jane acknowledged the timeline could be compressed below the original 6-9 month range suggested in the case prompt.

## Open Questions

1. **How compressed can the timeline realistically get?** Jane will take feedback on accelerating phases below three months. Marcel's position: six months to find a model that works is "amazing"; if three months in no direction is clear, that's a failure condition.

2. **What are the go/no-go metrics?** Bridget noted lack of specific metrics. The key candidates are: output volume (vs. baseline), quality rubric scores, customer NPS/rework rates, AI improvement (faster turnaround, reduced human intervention), financial model (cost per unit, contribution margin), and internal team attrition. Thresholds need definition.

3. **Which task types go into the pilot?** Jane suggested 1-2 low-risk task types; unclear which editorial or QA work is least mission-critical.

4. **What's the contributor pool?** Freelancer platforms (tested by Jane), talent networks (Surge, Outlier), or hybrid? Geographic or role constraints (California labor law compliance)?

5. **How does governance work?** Who approves phase gates? When is CEO sign-off required? How are decisions escalated if pilot underperforms?

6. **Can pattern recognition be taught or assessed differently?** Marcel's concern about Jane's startup sensibility and pattern recognition is a hiring concern, not a project concern. Unclear whether this surfaces in the pilot or interview process.

7. **What's the relationship between this pilot and current resource constraints?** Is this additive work (new team) or does it require reallocating existing ops/finance/delivery capacity?
