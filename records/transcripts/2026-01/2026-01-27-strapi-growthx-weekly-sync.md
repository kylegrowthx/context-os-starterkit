## Overview

- **Performance:** Sessions are on pace to surpass December highs, and non-branded impressions are recovering to last year's April-May levels.
- **LLM Tracking Transition:** GrowthX is replacing Scrunch with CheckNet, a new tool that provides immediate historical data for library prompts and allows for more granular, custom tracking.
- **Strategic Challenge:** Strapi's current CheckNet category ("Website Builders") is too broad, forcing competition against giants like Webflow and WordPress. The immediate priority is defining a more specific category (e.g., "Headless CMS") to get accurate, actionable data.
- **Content Strategy:** The goal is to create content that *earns* citations for specific prompts, not just create prompts that *find* existing content. LLM-friendly content requires strong SEO fundamentals plus a "chunked" structure with self-contained sections.

      - **LLM Referral Traffic:** January is sluggish.
          - **Reason:** Users often get answers directly from LLMs (e.g., ChatGPT) without clicking through to the source URL.
          - **Implication:** Referral traffic alone is an incomplete measure of LLM visibility.

### CheckNet Demo & Strategy

  - **CheckNet Overview:**
      - **Purpose:** Track LLM citations and trends, replacing Scrunch.
      - **Key Features:**
          - **Immediate Historical Data:** Library prompts (shared across a category) provide up to 90 days of data instantly.
          - **Unlimited Library Prompts:** These don't count against a custom prompt limit, enabling more granular tracking.
          - **Custom Prompts:** Remain private to Strapi for competitive advantage.
  - **Strategic Challenge: Category Mismatch**
      - **Problem:** The default "Website Builders" category forces competition against giants like Webflow and WordPress, skewing performance data.
      - **Solution:** Define a more specific category (e.g., "Headless CMS") to track relevant developer-focused prompts and get accurate, actionable insights.
  - **Content Strategy for LLMs**
      - **Goal:** Create content that *earns* citations for specific prompts (e.g., for frontend developers), rather than just finding existing content.
      - **Core Principles:**
          - **Strong SEO Fundamentals:** 80–90% of LLM prioritization is traditional SEO.
          - **"Chunked" Structure:** Content must be human-readable but structured for AI consumption, with self-contained sections under clear headings.
          - **No Thin Content:** Avoid short articles on complex topics, as Google may flag them, hurting overall SEO and LLM visibility.
