## Key Topics

### Content Publishing Workflow

  - **Problem:** The standard publishing workflow is stalled.
      - **Blocker:** GrowthX lacks website access and cannot directly publish approved articles.
      - **Root Cause:** External collaborators (e.g., Suleman) cannot be added to the GitHub Projects board to manage labels and statuses. John has an open request with Sam to resolve this.
  - **Interim Solution:** Publish approved articles immediately to test the end-to-end process.
      - **Action:** Brock will tag Mike T. and Erik Shah on approved PRs to trigger publishing.
  - **Future Enhancement:** A blog landing page with articles organized by topic tags (e.g., "GitOps") is needed for better user experience and SEO.

### SEO Strategy: Descriptivist vs. Prescriptivist

  - **Debate:** Use industry-standard terms vs. correct inaccurate but popular search terms.
  - **Decision:** Adopt a "descriptivist" approach to capture traffic from user-facing search queries.
      - **Rationale:** To rank for a term, the article title must use that term. The article's body can then "correct the record" and educate the reader.
  - **Example: "Linux MDM"**
      - **Search Volume:** 110 monthly searches (SEMrush).
      - **Action:** Create the article. The title will use the search term to rank, while the content will explain why "Linux MDM" is a misnomer and introduce the correct concept of Linux endpoint management.

### Content Backlog Review

  - **"Mac Fleet Management"**
      - **Concern:** The generic use of "fleet" is confusing with the product name.
      - **Resolution:** The title is a strong keyword signal. The article will be written using the "descriptivist" approach to capture search traffic.
  - **"Corporate iPhone Management"**
      - **Action:** Update the year in the title for timeliness.
  - **"Best Linux MDM Solutions"**
      - **Action:** Approved, following the "descriptivist" strategy.
