## Context

Tavrn is undertaking a significant strategic repositioning to compete more effectively in the legal tech space. Previously operating with broad messaging ("make every case defensible"), the company is consolidating around the Personal Injury vertical, where it has 100+ customers versus only three in other practice areas. This focused strategy is reflected in website copy updates and content strategy. Simultaneously, Tavrn is developing confidential infrastructure (a "Plaid for healthcare" API gateway) to reduce friction in medical record retrieval, its primary product differentiator.

The introduction of CheckThat—a proprietary platform to track LLM visibility and citations—signals a strategic pivot for both companies toward AI-driven search and discoverability.

## Key Topics

### Tavrn's Strategic Repositioning

- **Primary Objective:** Consolidate around Personal Injury to "catch up" to competitors in content and positioning.
  - **Rationale:** Current broad messaging is ineffective. PI represents 100+ customers vs. ~3 in other practice areas.
  - **Action:** Website copy is being updated to reflect PI focus; agency work on updates scheduled for the following week.
- **Product Positioning:**
  - **Record Retrieval:** Key differentiator; dependent on external provider cooperation and friction reduction.
  - **Demand Letters:** Superior to generic AI outputs; brand-matched to law firm standards.
  - **Chronologies:** Strong product, but harder to differentiate and communicate.
- **Confidential Infrastructure:** A "Plaid for healthcare" API gateway is in development to solve record retrieval friction. Strategic moat play; discretion maintained until providers are onboarded.

### Marketing & Growth Performance

- **Organic Traffic:** Surged from 150 to 550 weekly sessions; sustained growth, high engagement.
- **Paid Ads (Meta):**
  - Challenge: Audience burnout limiting budget spend and lowering lead quality.
  - Action: Testing new channels (Google) to diversify away from Meta dependency.
  - Record Retrieval Price Test: A/B testing $20 vs. $40 pricing. Keeping $20 ad active as lead volume protection while testing $40 tier.
  - Sales Enablement: HubSpot integration will inform reps of the price a lead saw before the meeting.

### Future Content & Conversion Strategy

- **Demand Letter Templates:** Gated asset to capture qualified leads; keyword research shows significant volume and opportunity.
- **PI Glossary / Question Center:** Domain authority play; optimized for LLM queries (e.g., "What is a demand letter?") to signal authority and drive LLM citations.
- **Provider Directory:** Gated asset of provider contact info for record retrieval. High-value lead magnet but requires ongoing maintenance and accuracy verification.

### CheckThat Platform

- **Purpose:** GrowthX's in-house LLM visibility tracking platform.
- **Key Metrics:**
  - **Presence:** Tavrn recommended as a top tool in LLM responses.
  - **Citations:** LLMs generating responses from Tavrn's content.
- **Strategic Prompts:** Focus on high-intent prompts (e.g., "best medical record retrieval software," "AI-powered medical record retrieval") and niche/strategic prompts as Tavrn's product positioning evolves.
- **Rollout:** Official launch first week of February. Megan to provide input on priority prompts to track.
