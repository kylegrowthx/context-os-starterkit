## Context

Abnormal is mid-execution on a Web3.0 site redesign (launching March 12) while managing aggressive competitive pressure from Sublime Security, who has escalated brand bidding campaigns to capture over 50% of Abnormal's branded search impressions in January. Warren Duff, new VP/Director of Content Marketing at Abnormal (Week 2 in role), was introduced to the GrowthX partnership for the first time in this meeting. The team is operating under a new CEO directive (Evans) emphasizing speed and removing "sacred cows"—meaning faster, more aggressive content decisions. GrowthX has successfully executed similar "vs. competitor" content for other SaaS clients with legal review and strong LLM citation results. The education hub is a direct strategic response to both the Web3.0 launch window and competitive threat, designed to ship high-volume content without diluting the core blog's brand authority.

## Relevance

This meeting captures a major operational inflection point: Abnormal is moving from blog-centric content strategy to a hub-and-spoke model that separates brand/thought leadership content from high-volume, SEO/LLM-optimized educational content. The education hub enables GrowthX to operate independently (managing production, publishing, CMS scheduling) without requiring ongoing approval from Abnormal's internal product marketing team, which traditionally gatekeeps the core blog. The immediate action (Sublime comparison page) is designed for fast execution and LLM citation ROI, establishing a new operating cadence. Key insight: Abnormal leadership has moved from legal-conservative ("past directives") to speed-first ("publish and get our hands slapped"), removing a major blocker for competitive content. This shapes how future GrowthX deliverables will be positioned and reviewed.

## Overview

- **New "Education" Hub:** Create a separate `/education` hub for high-volume, SEO-focused content. This protects the main blog's brand while enabling rapid, strategic publishing.
- **Web3.0 Migration Plan:** The hub's launch is post-Web3.0 (March 12). During the Feb 6–March 12 content freeze, content will be front-loaded, tagged for migration, and published sparingly.
- **Competitive Content:** Counter Sublime Security's aggressive brand bidding (now \>50% of Abnormal's brand impressions) with a direct "Abnormal vs. Sublime" page designed for LLM citation.
- **Mid-Funnel CTAs:** Educational content will use mid-funnel CTAs (e.g., white papers) to better match user intent, moving away from high-friction demo requests.

## Key Topics

### New Content Strategy: "Education" Hub

  - **Problem:** High-volume, SEO-driven content risks diluting the main blog's brand and creating a bottleneck for the internal team.
  - **Solution:** Create a separate `/education` hub on the Abnormal site.
      - **Purpose:** Rapidly publish high-volume, educational content on broad cybersecurity topics.
      - **Audience:** Primarily search engines and LLMs for citation, not human readers.
      - **Goal:** Establish Abnormal as a citation source across many keywords.
  - **Execution:**
      - **Ownership:** GrowthX will manage content production and publishing.
      - **Structure:** Mimic the blog's functionality (topic grouping, date filters).
      - **Content:** Agnostic, non-promotional, and LLM-friendly (e.g., "Too Long to Read" summaries, FAQs).

### Web3.0 Migration & Content Freeze

  - **Constraint:** The new hub's launch is dependent on the Web3.0 site redesign.
  - **Timeline:**
      - **Content Freeze:** Feb 6 – March 12
      - **Web3.0 Go-Live:** March 12
      - **Post-Launch:** A 4–6 week stabilization period is expected.
  - **Migration Plan:**
      - **New Content:** Front-load publishing before the freeze. During the freeze, publish only essential content and use the new CMS for scheduling.
      - **Existing Content:** Tag relevant posts for migration to the new hub.
      - **SEO Preservation:** Prioritize preserving SEO value, especially for the top 20% of posts by traffic.
  - **Execution:**
      - **Tagging:** Emily will create a new blog tag/category for educational content.
      - **Guidelines:** Emily will create a template for the new content style (agnostic tone, LLM-friendly structure).

### Competitive Response: Sublime Security

  - **Problem:** Sublime Security's aggressive brand bidding has increased its share of Abnormal's brand impressions from 10% to \>50% in the last month.
  - **Solution:** Create an "Abnormal vs. Sublime" page to counter the narrative and capture search demand.
  - **Execution:**
      - **Format:** Model the page after Sublime's own LLM-optimized comparison page.
      - **Content:** Position Abnormal as the first choice, citing awards (Gartner, Voice Awards) and using neutral, paraphrased G2 data for competitors.
      - **Review:** This content requires internal review due to past legal directives. The new directive from leadership is to be more aggressive ("publish and get our hands slapped").
      - **Placement:** This page will reside in the new `/education` hub.
