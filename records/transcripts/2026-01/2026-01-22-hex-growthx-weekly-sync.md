## Key Topics

### Content Review Blockers

  - Recent content reviews revealed a misalignment with Hex's current messaging, blocking the publishing cadence.
  - **Root Cause:** GrowthX was not provided with two key artifacts created after the initial calibration:
    1.  **AI Competitive Guide:** A document from Hex's PMM (James) detailing competitor AI advantages and Hex's unique positioning.
    2.  **Product Note Sheet:** Updated notes on new features, including the upcoming Context Studio packaging.
  - **Resolution:** GrowthX will rework all articles written before Jan 9 to incorporate this new information. This saves Hex's team from line-editing outdated drafts.

### LLM Visibility & Comparison Content

  - GrowthX pitched comparison listicles as a powerful strategy to build domain authority and own the narrative in LLM responses.
  - **Evidence:** The "JupyterLab vs. Jupyter Notebook" article is already being cited by Plexity, demonstrating the strategy's immediate impact.
  - **Tool:** GrowthX's new internal tool, "Check That AI," tracks LLM visibility for Hex and its competitors, measuring how often each appears in key prompts.
  - **Internal Challenge (Peter Ryan):** The goal is to build a compelling case for Hex's marketing team, who are hesitant about creating competitor-focused content.
      - **Core Question:** Is the goal to be an objective authority or to control the narrative? The answer dictates the required effort and nuance.
      - **GrowthX's Rationale:** Playing in the space is necessary to influence LLM responses. High-quality content is required to be cited, and citations drive traffic and signal authority.
