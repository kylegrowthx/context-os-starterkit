## Context

Lovable is a GrowthX client that builds no-code website templates. The account recently upsold to $40k/month based on strong performance signals (3–4M impressions per month). The team is preparing for a major production ramp-up on January 26, targeting 10 templates/week (up from current production).

The primary challenge is contractor quality. The team sources templates from external contractors, but recent work has been low-quality and generic, failing to meet the internal standard of "human-made" templates. The team uses Airtable as the single source of truth for production tracking and relies on Nicolas's publishing bot and workflow system (Atlas) to automate template deployment.

Key team members: George (VP Sales/Client lead), Megan (Operations/Performance), Georgemaine (Design/Production Manager), Nicolas (Technical/Publishing), and Will (returning from vacation, handles portfolio and blog templates). A new technical SEO (Tom) is joining the client's team.

## Relevance

This meeting is critical to GrowthX's Q1 2026 growth strategy. The Lovable upsell demonstrates that high-volume, quality content can drive significant revenue (from ~$15–20k to $40k/month). However, the team's ability to execute the Jan 26 ramp-up—and maintain the quality standard that justified the upsell—depends entirely on solving the contractor quality problem.

The discussion also introduces an important strategic shift: moving from pure production volume to optimizing existing high-performers through refreshes and interlinking. This positions the team for diminishing returns management and sustained growth beyond the current production cycle. Relevant to: content ops, sales cycle management, and strategic planning for 2026.
