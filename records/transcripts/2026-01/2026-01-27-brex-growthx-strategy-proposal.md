## Summary

GrowthX and Brex aligned on a three-phase strategy to achieve Brex's 136% organic + LLM lead growth target by January 2027. Phase 1: Launch tools and templates section as a rapid-experimentation playground for high-intent content. Phase 2: Redesign the Pride Co domain (pry.co) into a premium content hub leveraging its TechCrunch backlinks. Phase 3: Systematically overhaul editorial quality and content processes to eliminate AI hallucinations, keyword cannibalization, and repetitive sourcing issues. The Capital One acquisition creates both uncertainty and urgency—GrowthX must establish defensible content assets and prove editorial excellence within 6-8 weeks.

## Context

Brex was acquired by Capital One for ~$5B in January 2026, creating both strategic uncertainty and urgency around defensible content assets. Brex's finance-focused SEO targets commercial B2B keywords (e.g., business credit cards, expense management) to drive sales-ready leads. Jon Kowieski leads a lean SEO team with limited engineering bandwidth, having advocated for tools/templates development for 6+ months with minimal traction from internal product org.

The Spend Trends section (personal finance guides) has achieved 3x traffic growth since October 2024, but Journal and Resources sections have declined 50%+ from peak. The deterioration stems from underperforming pages (e.g., Vision Statement Examples, Profit Margin) that lack basic web fundamentals (author, date, CTA, cross-linking) and inconsistent user experience across ~7,800 pages. GrowthX's initial engagement focused on "supercharging existing successes"; this meeting pivoted to an aggressive 2-3x traffic growth strategy addressing both high-intent commercial keywords AND fixing the foundational content experience.

## Overview

**Growth Target:** 136% increase in organic + LLM leads by January 2027, requiring 2-3x traffic growth. Current traffic performance: Spend Trends 3x growth since Oct 2024; Journal/Resources declining 50%+ from peak.

**Three-Phase Strategy:**

1. **Phase 1: Tools & Templates Launch** — New high-intent section with 20-30 pieces/week production, designed as experimentation playground for rapid learning and soft conversions (template downloads for remarketing)
2. **Phase 2: Pride Co (pry.co) Redesign** — Migrate 42 pages from legacy CMS to Sanity, leverage TechCrunch backlinks to build defensible authority hub; potential $50-100k value depending on Capital One direction
3. **Phase 3: Editorial Quality Overhaul** — Systematically address AI hallucinations, keyword cannibalization, repetitive sourcing, and excessive chunking through redesigned QA process and content validation framework

**Foundational Work:** Unify content experience across ~7,800 pages (Journal, Resources, Spend Trends). Two pages (Vision Statement Examples, Profit Margin) account for 21,000 lost visits/month from peak; both lack author, date, CTA, cross-linking, images.

**Technical Blockers:** Missing canonical tags on Stacker articles causing ranking drops; some pages completely deindexed. Leadership buy-in for web redesign no longer a blocker post-acquisition.

## Key Topics

### Traffic Analysis & Performance Gap

**Spend Trends (Performing):** 3x traffic growth since Oct 2024; consistent traffic-per-page; strong editorial quality with authors, dates, CTAs, consistent UX.

**Journal & Resources (Declining):** 50%+ traffic drop from peak. Two underperformers (Vision Statement Examples, Profit Margin) drive 21,000 lost visits/month combined. Root causes: missing author/date/CTA, inconsistent design, poor cross-linking, no table of contents.

**Segmentation:** Brex's traffic has significant brand component (not addressable via SEO alone); low-intent pages (college student guides) are depreciating assets; high-intent commercial keywords are conversion drivers.

### Web Experience Unification Strategy

Create consistent design patterns across ~7,800 pages: authors, dates, table of contents, CTAs, cross-linking, images. Jon attempted this 8-9 months ago but faced leadership resistance; Alex confirmed no longer a blocker post-acquisition. Existing Figma/Sanity designs available to accelerate from 2+ weeks → 1-2 weeks.

### Phase 1: Tools & Templates Launch

**Objective:** New high-intent section for rapid experimentation; 20-30 pieces/week in sandbox.

**Rationale:** "Playground" for testing content approaches with fewer review cycles; soft conversions (template downloads) enable remarketing; lower editing burden than current workflow.

**Risk/Mitigation:** Keyword cannibalization with existing Brex rankings (e.g., "business credit card"). Jon vetting every new keyword cluster against GSC to avoid overlap.

### Phase 2: Pride Co (pry.co) Content Hub

**Context:** 42-page legacy CMS domain; TechCrunch backlinks valuable for new-domain authority; currently at risk of sale post-acquisition.

**Technical Plan:** Migrate to Sanity, preserve URLs, launch premium content hub (e.g., "competitive benchmarks" content).

**Strategic Value:** Defensible asset post-Capital One integration; potential $50-100k value depending on how it fits Capital One's strategy.

**Timeline:** Urgent — 30 days to proof concept before strategic lock-in.

### Editorial Quality Crisis

**GrowthX Output Rating:** 6/10. Jon spending significant time editing articles before publication.

**Specific Issues:**
- AI hallucinations: Incorrect information, wrong sources
- Repetitive sourcing: Same source cited 8 times in one article
- Keyword cannibalization: Suggesting keywords Brex already ranks for (position 3)
- Chunking abuse: Short, choppy sentences despite explicit directive to stop (Google algorithm penalizes this pattern)
- Weak internal linking: Insufficient and poorly placed

**Impact:** Brex editorial team bottlenecked; slows content velocity; reduces confidence in GrowthX output quality.

**Solution:** Deep-dive session to systematize editorial process and build repeatable quality validation framework.

### Technical SEO Blockers

**Canonical Tags:** Stacker articles missing canonicals; causing duplicate content penalties and ranking drops.

**Deindexing:** Some pages completely wiped from rankings due to technical issues.

**Search Behavior:** Product pages (e.g., card comparisons) performing well; banking education pages eliminated for certain keywords post-algorithm update.

### Capital One Acquisition Context

**Timing:** Announced January 2026 (~$5B deal); creates strategic uncertainty across organization.

**Jon's Analysis:** Capital One and Discover SEO both underperforming (poor content strategy). Brex needs to establish how to integrate Capital One positively into future content.

**Unavailability:** Company-wide meetings Feb 10-13; requires rescheduling recurring GrowthX sync.
