## Key Topics

### Launch Strategy and Early Access

The team will soft-launch to community members before public announcement. Approach: pre-create accounts capped at 50 free prompts per account, then Marcel personally reaches out with messaging like "We want to give you early access and offer X free months + a 30-minute strategy session with me (normally $1,000)." This balances cost control (no unlimited access) with high-touch validation (30-min calls capture product resonance). Community breakfast Thursday will serve as first test of messaging—Jason will pre-create 1-2 sample workspaces.

### Product Positioning

Conversation with Quran surfaced "buyer intent graph for AI" as the resonant positioning framework. This captures CheckThat's core insight: tracking 5,800 brands across 172 categories using 1.6M AI responses/month. Current messaging ("index" sounds cold, "graph" may be too technical) misses this. Marcel must nail a crisp 2-sentence positioning before Wednesday video recording and use it across homepage, launch assets, and video script.

### Promotional Video

Recording Wednesday with experienced agency (worked on Gamma launch). Rough cut due Thursday EOD, final version Friday. Video narrative: open with user pain point (navigating 170+ AI tools), then frame CheckThat as the "buyer intent graph for AI." Agency handling curated outreach and newsletter blasts. Video timeline is tight; Marcel and Jason need to block time and finalize messaging.

### Organic Growth Challenges and Root Cause

Traffic plateaued at ~150 clicks/week with impressions dropping 1,000. Marcel's goal: 2,000 clicks January (already late), 5,000 February. At current pace, organic delivers only 4 paying customers/year—unacceptable. Jason believes root cause is content quality, not site newness. Evidence: when Jason rewrote 10 pricing pages, they immediately recovered traffic. Current issues: pages rank initially then drop off; many brand mentions aren't clickable; category pages lack descriptions; related brands don't link to each other. Debate: should alternatives pages be editorial listicles (better for SEO authority) or dynamic tables (better UX)? Marcel argues both can work if content quality is high.

### Indexing Deep Dive

Of 300+ pricing pages published, only 30 are indexed after weeks—beyond typical SEO timing. Possible causes: sitemap issues, backlink quality (Indexly flagged this), or content quality signals. Manual daily submissions to Indexly in early January yielded 300 pages indexed in one week; now slowed to 50/week. Team needs systematic approach: test sitemap structure, validate backlink quality, iterate content quality, monitor indexing velocity.

### Category and Prompt Architecture

Current 178 categories feel inadequate. Marcel has a curated list of 200+ new subcategories (e.g., breaking down "CRM" into verticals). Each new category would include 10-20 relevant prompts, expanding monthly prompt index from 11k to 14-15k (manageable cost increase for significantly better UX). This also addresses SEO: more categories = more landing pages = more keyword coverage and internal linking opportunity.

### Resource Constraints and Execution Bottlenecks

Marcel expressed frustration: all three growth levers (pipeline, community, CheckThat) feel stalled. Jason acknowledged the team is under-resourced: Carrie transitioning off, Talal firefighting other priorities, Nika departed, current CheckThat capacity is stretched thin. Team agreed to escalate by documenting: 1) CheckThat's concrete bottlenecks (e.g., "need 2 more engineers for category build"), 2) Weekly experiment plan with clear ownership, 3) Resource requests and impact (e.g., "one more eng unlocks 200 categories in 2 weeks vs. 6 weeks").
