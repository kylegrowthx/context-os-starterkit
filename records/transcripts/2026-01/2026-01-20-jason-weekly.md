## Context

GrowthX is at a critical inflection point heading into 2026. Over the past 3 months, monthly new opportunity creation has collapsed (Oct 35 → Nov 24 → Dec 16 → Jan MTD 16), threatening annual revenue targets. More broadly, Marcel is overwhelmed managing dozens of interconnected outcomes across delivery, renewals, sales pipeline, community, and Check That—everything requires his deep involvement. The company has deployed >$3M in budget and headcount but lacks structured outcomes-based management; teams are optimizing for activities (next webinar, next post) rather than end-to-end revenue lanes.

This meeting establishes a new management model: Marcel will set lane-level targets and budgets, then rely on Jason and team leads to synthesize outcomes weekly rather than requesting constant bottom-up activity inspection. The shift unlocks capacity for founder-led strategy while holding teams accountable to quarterly ARR/pipeline/membership goals, not task lists.

## Key Topics

### Pipeline performance and risk

  - New opps created: Oct = 35; Nov \~24; Dec = 16; Jan MTD = 16 → trending below plan
  - Thresholds: 35/mo sustains annual goals; 16/mo “fail miserably” → must lock ≥35/mo with buffer
  - Current management gap: no clear single owner driving monthly opp volume for GrowthX; “no-man’s land” between Jason/Tyler
  - Required shift: weekly, outcomes-led management with explicit targets, confidence levels, and consequences

### Operating model: outcomes \> activities

  - Problem: teams manage to “the next thing” (post/webinar/event) → resets to zero each cycle
  - Solution: define lanes with end-to-end economics and owners:
      - Example lane pattern → Source (e.g., newsletter/social) → Conversion system (email cadence/webinar) → Monetization (paid member/opps) → Reinvest (budget tied to measured unit economics)
      - Guardrails from Jason to team; inspect lanes, not micro-activities
  - Accountability: leaders synthesize for Marcel at outcomes level (ARR churn, sprint conversion, opps, retention), not tool updates

### Budget, priorities, and targets (2026)

  - Budget under Jason: \>$3M (programs + headcount)
  - Targets:
      - GrowthX: kickoffs 78 → 150; opp creation ≥35/mo locked
      - Community: MRR \~$4–5K → \~$90–100K; build scalable acquisition + retention engine
      - Check That: ARR $0 → $2–5M; “crank the dial” if unit economics work; \#1 priority to fund other bets
  - Investment rule: if CAC payback is proven and channel is scalable → scale spend aggressively

### Community: missing flywheel

  - Current state: activity-managed (webinars/videos), no end-to-end path; members consume and churn → retention weak
  - Gaps:
      - Acquisition: only workshops/social; no predictable subscriber growth engine
      - Conversion: newsletter sends convert “some,” but no funnel into durable products or paid tiers
      - Retention: no ongoing value to keep members post-consumption
  - Lane examples to validate:
      - Newsletter engine → X new subs/week → onboarding cadence → paid member conversion (e.g., 5/100) → revenue per cohort → reinvest to acquire more subs
      - Cohorts → standardize curriculum; re-run proven formats; predictable calendar and fill plan
      - Lightning sessions (1/mo) → high-impact spikes → list growth and conversion
  - Ownership: Jason fully owns community; must establish continuity so it runs without constant founder energy

### Events and webinars: under-leveraged

  - Expectation: by now events lane should “hum” under Head of Events and Gloria with minimal founder input
  - Reality: low volume, not scaling; webinars high effort from Marcel/Jason; December pipeline was abysmal; November slightly down
  - Needed: structured H1 calendar, attendance targets, opp yield models, repeatable plays (dinners, executive events, field), and amplification into community/newsletter

### Role scope and focus

  - One person cannot simultaneously own: GrowthX pipeline, Community, and Check That PMM/GTM at current stage
  - Jason is effective when dedicated to one lane (e.g., October pipeline surge) → focus, then hire/assign to sustain
  - Team management: if a lane requires constant de-risking and re-input, revisit fit for stage
