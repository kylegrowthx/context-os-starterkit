## Key Topics

### Time Tracking and Capacity Planning

Time Doctor is rolling out this week to track delivery times from Atlas to client readiness. The goal is integrating this data into dashboards for capacity planning. Matthew emphasized moving away from disjointed tools by rolling out pipelines for 70-80 deliverables to enable weekly updates directly in Atlas. A new capacity planning tool will factor in client difficulty, number of clients per person, and skill sets—enabling smarter client assignments.

### Content Strategy and EM Role Development

There's a pressing need to codify ADA's hybrid role and develop clear playbooks to train EMs on content strategy, meeting rhythms, and client communications. George suggested creating a detailed interview guide capturing ADA's content strategy approach, meeting prep, objections handled, and tactical steps across weeks one through eight. The team agreed that what's missing are week-by-week agendas and talk tracks with common client objections and responses.

### Process Improvements and Automation

Manual, error-prone tasks like publishing and keyword research are key friction points. Publishing should be automated across all headless CMSs to eliminate bottlenecks. Keyword research is inefficient and increasingly inaccurate with current tools. The team acknowledged some clients were working outside standardized processes (using Excel instead of Airtable)—a symptom of inconsistent Sprint workflows.

### Client Success and Goal Setting

Improving early goal setting, proactive "what's next" planning, and clear escalation paths are essential to reduce churn. A 90-minute working session was scheduled to address these issues. The team emphasized transitioning from reactive to proactive client guidance, delivering explicit playbooks that define contract scope versus additional value.

### Performance Escalation

Matthew offered to be the single point of contact for EMs and MEs to report delivery or capacity issues directly. The goal is avoiding communication breakdowns and enabling timely remediation with proper documentation.
