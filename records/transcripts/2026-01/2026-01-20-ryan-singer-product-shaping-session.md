## Summary

Ryan Singer (creator of Shape Up at Basecamp) walked through GrowthX's content performance tracking problem. The core challenge: teams publish content but fly blind on whether it works. They can't connect content work to outcomes (traffic, conversions, AI visibility) without digging through multiple disconnected tools.

The session landed on a key insight: stop thinking in tabs and sections. Instead, nail the "daily visit" screen first. Build something that makes you feel bad if you don't check it every day. Create Trello-level simplicity at the surface with Salesforce-level depth underneath.

---

## Context

GrowthX is building a content performance platform that replaces the current mess of Looker dashboards, Google Analytics, and Search Console. The analogy: become the Robinhood for websites. Today, managing a content portfolio feels like calling your broker and asking "how are my stocks doing?" The goal is real-time visibility into which content is rising, decaying, and why.

This session is the second in a series with Ryan Singer, following the writing calibration session. The next session (February 17th) will review progress on the concepts developed here.

---

## Relevance

**To the GrowthX service model:**
- Defines the data feedback loop that ties content work to outcomes
- Addresses the "flying blind" problem that causes engagement manager escalations
- Explains why Lovable succeeded (high domain authority + clear metrics) vs. engagements that stall

**To CheckThat product development:**
- Validates the shift from AI visibility tools (163 competitors) to actionable intelligence
- Reinforces the "open index" approach: public data drives trust and usage
- Shows how to think about leading indicators (impressions, click-through) vs. lagging (conversions)

**To content strategy:**
- The 1% rule: top 1% of pages drive 20%+ of results. Protect and perfect these.
- Content half-life is shrinking with AI. Pages need continuous improvement, not one-time publishing.
- LLM referral traffic converts like branded search (3-7x higher) because users arrive pre-sold.
