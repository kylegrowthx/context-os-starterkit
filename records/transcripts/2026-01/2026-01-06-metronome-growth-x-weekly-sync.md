## Key Topics

### Content Quality & Review Process

  - **Problem:** Metronome's review time is excessive because articles often deviate from the agreed-upon template and contain inaccurate data.
  - **Example:** The 11 Labs page incorrectly split a single product into two pages (consumer/API) despite its unified pricing model.
  - **Solution:** GrowthX will implement new checks to ensure:
      - Singular articles are not split into multiple pages.
      - Subheaders are included in Google Docs to eliminate manual additions.
      - JSON conversion is accurate to prevent data mismatches.

### New Workflow & Schedule

  - **Rationale:** To give Metronome more time for the critical review phase, the delivery schedule is being split.
  - **New Cadence:**
      - **Tuesdays:** New Google Docs for review.
      - **Thursdays:** JSON files for publication.
  - **This Week's Exception:** The 10 articles from the previous batch will be prioritized for publication.
      - **Wed, EOD:** 10 JSON files delivered.
      - **Thurs, EOD:** New batch of Google Docs delivered.

### Production Scaling

  - **Plan:** Increase production from 5 to 6 articles/week.
  - **Contingency:** The 6-article rate is dependent on quality improvements. If review time remains high, the rate will be capped at 5.
  - **Review Cadence:** Reviews are due Friday EOD or Monday AM.

### Tone & Categorization

  - **Tone:** Analysis sections must be objective ("what it is"), reserving opinion for the "Metronome's Take." This corrects the AI's tendency for overly promotional language.
  - **Categorization:** A new system is needed to provide consistency across articles.
      - **Challenge:** The system must account for product nuance (e.g., API vs. consumer) and varied pricing models.
      - **Status:** Will is developing a spreadsheet to define categories and will share it with GrowthX for implementation.
