## Overview

**Strategic Pivot: From Transactional to Value-Driven**
- Shift from selling features and month-to-month plans to selling value and longer-term commitments
- Rationale: AI receptionist customization requires iterative setup and refinement over weeks/months, not hours
- Unique positioning: Smith.ai "holds the string"—implementing, refining, and optimizing the AI solution rather than selling a DIY product
- Contrasts with competitors (Ruby, MyCase, Lex Reception) who position as software-only vendors

**Legal Industry as Strategic Focus**
- Priority #1: Reclaim #1 SEO ranking for "AI receptionist for lawyers" (previously held for "virtual receptionist")
- Current gap: Smith.ai is mentioned in AI overviews but not ranking in top 10 for "answering service for attorneys" and similar legal-specific terms
- Opportunity: Legal market moves slower, invests longer, and aligns with Smith.ai's new value-based narrative

- Serves as a contextual library that signals deep expertise to Google

- Internal linking strategy: Drive traffic from high-authority Knowledge Hub articles to "money pages" (industry pages, VR/AI product pages)

## Key Topics

### 2026 Business Priorities & Narrative Shift

**Problem with Current Homepage**
- Homepage tries to serve two audiences simultaneously: investors and customers
- Creates messaging misalignment; doesn't clearly articulate Smith.ai's unique value

**New Narrative: From DIY to Full-Service Partner**
- Old narrative: "Here's what we can do for you month-to-month—check the boxes and sign up"
- New narrative: "We hold the string. We implement, we refine, we make sure it works"
- Key difference: Virtual Receptionist works with human agents following instructions; AI Receptionist requires custom development ("lightweight development" = refinement, not engineering)
- Why longer plans matter: AI customization takes time. Customers expect control over many aspects (tone, chattiness, speed vs. service, decision logic). Sales needs 1-3 months to get it right; month-to-month plans don't allow this runway

**Value Proposition for Sales & Content**
- Smith.ai is a partner, not a DIY vendor
- This narrative supports longer-term deals and higher customer lifetime value
- Aligns with Knowledge Hub strategy: educate prospects, build authority, position as alternative to DIY

### Legal Industry Focus & SEO Strategy

**Why Legal Market?**
- Fastest growth segment
- Natural alignment with longer-term, value-based narrative
- Tech-forward (CTOs, tech leaders) but willing to outsource
- Knowledge Hub is perfect for this audience: they're researching solutions before buying

**SEO Gap to Close**
- Smith.ai mentioned in AI overviews but not ranking organically for high-intent legal terms
- Competitors outranking: Ruby (established), MyCase (larger company), Lex Reception, Reddit, Answering Legal
- Ranking terms to target: "answering service for attorneys," "AI receptionist for lawyers," "answering service for personal injury attorneys," etc.

**Three-Pronged SEO Approach**

1. **On-Page: Deep, Human-Focused Examples**
   - Instead of generic "personal injury," use specific scenarios: "car accident," "slip and fall," "workplace injury"
   - This signals deeper expertise to Google and matches real customer intent
   - Applies to industry pages and Knowledge Hub articles

2. **Off-Page: Direct Link-Building to Landing Pages**
   - Work with Kinga (link builder) on lawyer-specific terms
   - Target: industry pages, VR/AI product pages
   - Backlinks = 50-60% of ranking effort; on-page optimization alone won't cut it
   - Most link-building effort historically went to blog/Knowledge Hub; now direct some effort to landing pages

3. **Internal Linking: Distribute SEO Value from Hub to Money Pages**
   - Knowledge Hub drives authority and traffic
   - Link from Hub articles to industry pages and product pages
   - Goal: Make Google associate Smith.ai with legal terms and drive qualified traffic to conversion pages

**Avoiding Single-Industry Appearance**
- Content mix: 60-70% legal examples; 30-40% other professional services
- Prevents Google from thinking Smith.ai is only for lawyers (even though it's the priority)
- Maintains diversity in Knowledge Hub (family law, immigration, personal injury, etc.)

### Content & Persona Development

**Artifacts to Update**
- Audience personas (currently 3 generic personas; need legal-specific sub-personas)
- Customer testimonials (repurposed from videos, contain quotes and examples)
- Messaging and positioning documents

**Legal Persona Pipeline**
- Create dedicated pipeline for legal segment with 2-3 sub-personas:
  - Solo attorney
  - Firm partner / managing partner
  - Firm operations manager
- Separate from generic personas; enables more targeted content and personalization

**Content Development with Personas**
- Use personas to guide Knowledge Hub article examples
- Client testimonial artifact can seed both generic and legal-specific content
- Examples: Missing calls (pain point), call routing complexity, business growth scenarios
- Hypothetical scenarios are acceptable as long as labeled clearly (avoid promising specific features)

**Specificity in Examples**
- Instead of: "A personal injury lawyer missed important calls"
- Go deeper: "A solo personal injury attorney handling car accident cases missed intake calls during high season"
- Google picks up on specificity; it demonstrates expertise vs. generic positioning

### LLM Visibility & Tool Migration

**Current Problem**
- Scrunch data dropped dramatically in early January
- Jo suspects technical tracking issue, not actual performance decline
- Maddy confirms: no strategy changes, no brand name changes on Smith.ai's side
- Conclusion: Likely Scrunch tracking bug (technical issue on their end)

**Overhaul Plan**

1. **Audit & Redesign Prompts**
   - Review current Scrunch setup
   - Create 300-350 new prompts per persona and cluster
   - Align prompts with new 2026 strategy (legal focus, longer-term value narrative)
   - Organize by cluster/topic for consistency

2. **Migrate to Check That AI (February)**
   - GrowthX's new LLM visibility tool (included in engagement)
   - Better than Scrunch: offers industry benchmarking, dynamic competitor suggestions
   - Removes manual competitor list maintenance (users often miss half the competitors)
   - Removes manual topic selection (static lists don't capture emerging topics)

3. **Citation Analysis & Content Refresh**
   - Analyze where Smith.ai is cited in LLM responses
   - Identify high-intent pages frequently cited (prioritize refresh)
   - Find pages missing from LLM responses (opportunity for optimization)
   - This informs ongoing Knowledge Hub and product page updates

**Timeline**
- Week of Jan 27: Share updated artifacts, new prompt set, and Airtable link
- Early Feb: Finalize strategy, discuss asynchronously, commit to topics
- Feb+: Migrate to Check That AI; run citation analysis; refresh high-intent pages
