## Context

Rimkus's SEO and content strategy is entering a maturation phase. Earlier in January, GrowthX published content that drove organic growth from approximately 31 sessions per week to over 80 sessions per week—a significant 2.5x increase in traffic. This performance is driven by articles relevant to Rimkus's two core service lines: Built Environment Solutions (BES) and Forensics. The team acquired Sullivan years ago as a content acquisition, and now sees an opportunity to repurpose Sullivan's historically high-performing articles (which previously ranked well for terms like "Fathom") to accelerate keyword coverage and competitive visibility.

However, the team has hit a bottleneck with highly technical content refreshes. Articles like "Hydrogen Embrittlement," originally targeted for refresh to boost SEO performance, require author review and fact-checking. This process has proven time-consuming and is delaying the content calendar. Moving forward, Rimkus and GrowthX are redefining priorities: shift to net-new content for Forensics, retire technical refresh projects that have attributed authors, and focus on repurposing proven Sullivan content. Additionally, GrowthX is rolling out Check That, an internal LLM visibility tool, to give Rimkus deeper insight into their presence in AI-generated responses (Perplexity, ChatGPT, etc.) versus competitors.
