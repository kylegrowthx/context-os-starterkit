## Summary

GrowthX and You.com realigned content strategy to address a fundamental shift: You.com is now positioning itself as an "AI Search" company serving two distinct personas—AI Laggards (CEOs needing education) and AI Natives (developers needing technical depth). The strategy pivots from a one-size-fits-all approach to a bifurcated content model: GrowthX handles top-of-funnel (ToFu) content for CEOs, while You.com manages complex middle-of-funnel (MoFu) technical content. To unblock the slow SME review bottleneck and accelerate technical content production, both teams agreed to repurpose existing white papers into blog posts, bypassing manual approval cycles.

---

## Context

GrowthX has been working with You.com since October 2025, managing a 5-article-per-week editorial content program targeting search visibility. Marcus Taylor recently joined You.com as head of brand and product marketing; this was his first substantive alignment call with GrowthX. The partnership's foundation is built on content strategy tied to You.com's business goals—but those goals shifted in Q1 2026, requiring a strategic recalibration.

The core constraint: technical content for developers requires slow, manual SME reviews that block the production cadence. You.com's internal teams (DevRel, API PM) are still forming alignment on technical positioning, resulting in broad feedback that delays approvals. The emerging solution leverages You.com's existing white papers (already approved and published) as source material, allowing GrowthX to extract blog-post topics without re-running the SME gauntlet. Parallel to this, GrowthX is releasing a new prompt-monitoring tool (Feb 2-7) that will enable You.com to self-serve prompt edits and gain better visibility into which keywords and topics are driving results.

## Key Topics

### Challenge: Slow SME Reviews for Technical Content

  - **Problem:** Technical content requires slow, manual SME reviews, blocking the 5-article/week production cadence.
  - **Root Cause:** Internal alignment on technical positioning is still forming, making SME feedback broad and delaying approvals.
  - **Solution:** Use competitive articles (e.g., Perplexity, EXA) as a "forcing function" to drive internal alignment on technical capabilities.

### Solution: Dual-Persona Content Strategy

  - **New Direction:** You.com is now an "AI Search" company, building search for LLMs and agents.
  - **Audience Bifurcation:** The strategy targets two distinct personas:
      - **AI Laggards (CEOs):** Need education on AI's business value and operational use cases. Content must be simple and high-level.
      - **AI Natives (Developers):** Need technical depth to evaluate the platform. Content must be detailed to earn a spot on their shortlists.
  - **Go-to-Market:** A "bottom-up" (DevRel) and "top-down" (CTO/CPO) approach aims to meet in the middle.

### Solution: Repurposing White Papers for Technical Content

  - **Idea:** Accelerate technical content by repurposing existing white papers into blog posts.
  - **Rationale:** This bypasses the SME review bottleneck, as the source material is already approved and published.
  - **Process:** GrowthX will analyze existing white papers to propose a list of blog post topics, validated by keyword data and goals.

### Logistics & Reporting

  - **Scope:** The engagement is for 5 editorial articles/week.
  - **Reporting:** Bi-weekly performance snapshots and a monthly deep-dive report.
  - **LLM Visibility Metric:** A recent drop from 15 to 0 was caused by a Scrunch calculation change, not a performance issue.
  - **New Tool:** GrowthX's new prompt-monitoring tool launches Feb 2–7, enabling self-service prompt editing and more accurate tracking.
