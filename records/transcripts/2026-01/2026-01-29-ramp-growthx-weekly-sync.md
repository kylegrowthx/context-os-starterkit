  - **Key Finding:** Direct quotes from CFD articles are appearing verbatim in AI overviews, confirming the content's direct influence on LLM responses.

### Content Strategy for LLM Citation

  - **Goal:** Increase citation for broader, high-value queries (e.g., "finout concept") by breaking them into hyper-specific articles.
  - **Debate:**
      - **Victoria's Position:** Short, specific articles (e.g., 300 words) with aligned URLs/titles increase LLM retrieval probability. When URL and title match user query semantics, retrieval is higher.
      - **William's Position:** Past tests showed short articles lacked "SEO juice" and were not cited, especially if content was generic or mediocre. Longer pieces with substantive depth performed better.
  - **Resolution → Hybrid Model:**
      - **Short-Form:** For known-context queries where user already understands foundational concepts (e.g., "What is X?", "How do I switch from Y to Z?").
      - **Long-Form:** For conceptual topics requiring pre-education (e.g., balance sheet concepts, finout explanations).
      - **Implementation Insight:** Matt noted that topic nature determines format—hyper-specific answers work when LLM detects user has baseline context; longer explanations needed when user must be oriented to conceptual foundation first.
  - **Workflow:** Victoria will review content proposals in Airtable to identify sections that should be split into new, specific articles. Feedback delivered before articles are written, not after.
  - **Quality Control:** Short articles must avoid generic AI-generated content; they need unique insights and EAT signals, or LLMs won't cite them.

### Technical & Admin Updates

  - **Airtable Access:** Resolved during call; Victoria now has access.
  - **Content Review Workflow:** William will coordinate with Hassan to restructure Airtable statuses, making it easier for Victoria to find and review upcoming keywords before writing begins. Status filters to surface "next up" items clearly.
  - **Crawling Status:** Scrunch confirms pages are now being crawled, not just the `robots.txt` file.
      - **Insight:** Explicitly allowing crawlers in the sitemap was a key factor for this new site, a practice that may be less critical for sites with high domain authority. Domain authority and site magnetism have outsized impact on crawler behavior.
