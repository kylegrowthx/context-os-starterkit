## Key Topics

### New Website Launch

  - **Timeline:** Launching in \~2 weeks; QA starts next week.
  - **Rationale:** The current WordPress site is critically unstable, breaking pages just by opening them in the editor.
  - **Action:** GrowthX needs credentials for the new platform to maintain publishing continuity.
  - **Blog Template:** The new template is \~60% complete and will include a "Related Articles" block with industry and category tagging.
  - **Content Migration:** Tara will confirm with the dev team that all content published since the initial port is included. Matt will provide a list of relevant URLs.

### Q1 Content Strategy: Workforce Empowerment

  - **Focus:** Build out the "Workforce Empowerment" content cluster.
  - **Pillar Content:** "Digital Transformation in Manufacturing" will serve as the central hub article.
  - **Spoke Topics (Examples):**
      - Why AI adoption fails
      - Workforce transformation (defining it for manufacturing)
      - AI in workforce management
      - Knowledge loss / "silver tsunami"
      - Human-AI collaboration
      - Scaling AI across plants
  - **New Strategic Angle:** "Coordinated Decision-Making."
      - **Rationale:** To address "decision silos" where functional groups (e.g., maintenance, operations) make choices without transparency into cross-functional impacts.
      - **Goal:** Promote transparency to enable better organizational trade-offs, not to dictate decision authority.

### Q1 Initiative: Article Refresh

  - **Goal:** Refresh \~30 articles from early in the partnership to improve performance.
  - **Selection Criteria:**
      - **Declining Traffic:** Articles with a proven formula but falling clicks (e.g., clicks dropped from 81 to 51 on one piece).
      - **Underperformance:** Articles that failed to rank despite strong keywords (e.g., "AI for Industries" received only 1 click).
  - **Execution:**
      - **Small Lift:** Update with fresh data, better internal links.
      - **Large Lift:** Full rewrite, keyword realignment.

### New Content Format: TLDR Blocks

  - **Problem:** Standard articles can fail to answer immediate user questions, hurting SEO and engagement.
  - **Solution:** Integrate "TLDR" (Too Long; Didn't Read) summary blocks directly into articles.
  - **Mechanism:** A concise, accordion-style block at the top of an article that directly answers the user's search query.
  - **Benefits:**
      - **SEO:** Boosts visibility by providing direct answers for AI-powered search.
      - **Engagement:** Satisfies users with short attention spans while encouraging them to read the full article for detail.
  - **Precedent:** The Homebase website uses this format successfully.
