# GrowthX Intro with Factory

**Date:** January 8, 2026 | **Duration:** ~29 minutes | **Attendees:** Tyler Pavlas (GrowthX), Kristina Zweng, Caroline Ricksen (Factory)

## Summary

Tyler Pavlas introduces GrowthX's AI content platform to Factory's leadership, positioning it as a solution to build SEO/AEO authority from near-zero baseline. Factory is launching an AI agent harness targeting engineers and needs rapid content scale (5 articles/week), but balks at the 8-week, $18k Strategy Sprint. The conversation centers on calibration timelines, content quality assurance, and whether Factory can accelerate the process before committing.

## Context

Factory is a relatively young product-focused company (Caroline and Kristina mention being new to the company) building AI tooling for engineers. They have limited SEO work to date—mostly metadata and reports—and no backlinking strategy. Content creation has been internal (Caroline is Head of Strategy & Operations). Their competitive set includes Cognition, Cursor, Sourcegraph, and similar AI coding tools.

GrowthX's pitch is centered on the Augment Code case study: 3x organic traffic in 6 months (20k → 60k/month) using a `/guides` section strategy that preserves brand while enabling scale. Tyler frames this as a partnership rooted in SEO fundamentals but powered by AI content generation and human editorial oversight.

## Relevance

- **Sales Pipeline:** Prospect in early discussion phase, post-intro, pre-decision.
- **Product Positioning:** Testing positioning for AI/AEO services against brand-conscious CTOs/builders.
- **Client Concerns:** Urgency vs. quality, timeline pressure, need for proof points.
- **Lessons Learned:** Factory wants immediate results; GrowthX's sprint model is a friction point.

## Overview

This is a first intro call between GrowthX and Factory's leadership. Tyler leads with the Augment Code success story, presents GrowthX's end-to-end service model (Strategy Sprint → Growth Execution), and handles objections around timeline and content quality. The call surfaces Factory's desire to "start publishing ASAP" and their need for proof before committing to the strategy sprint. Path forward: Tyler sends materials for internal review by Francesca (VP of Ops), with a full demo to follow.

## Key Topics

- **Factory's SEO/AEO Gap:** No backlinking strategy, limited organic growth, content created in-house.
- **Competitive Set:** Cursor, Cognition, Sourcegraph, Cloud Code, Codex, Winsurf.
- **GrowthX's Offer:** 5 articles/week, 6-month roadmap, human-in-the-loop platform, SEO + AEO focus.
- **Proof Point:** Augment Code case study—3x traffic, published ~300 guides, cited in AI answers.
- **Model Detail:** Strategy Sprint (8 weeks, $18k, calibration) → Growth Execution (ongoing, 5/week).
- **Objection:** Timeline too long; Factory wants to publish immediately.
- **Resolution:** Materials sent for internal review; follow-up demo with decision-makers.
