## Summary

GrowthX and Netwrix aligned on a content partnership structure, establishing a workflow where GrowthX will handle most content production (strategy, copy, SEO, PMM review) while Netwrix controls final author approval and quality checks. The team discussed the 8-week project plan (kickoff → strategy → calibration articles → 5 articles/week) and identified a critical Sanity CMS bottleneck (3+ hours to stage articles with code) that must be streamlined to meet publication velocity targets.

## Context

This meeting launched the GrowthX-Netwrix content partnership, a critical engagement focused on building SEO-driven content to support Netwrix's "one secure" messaging and cross-sell initiatives. GrowthX, a content marketing services firm, and Netwrix, a zero-trust security software company, are establishing a structured workflow to produce content at scale (5 articles/week by week 6) while maintaining Netwrix's high editorial standards.

Netwrix has an established multi-stage content workflow (outline → copy → SEO review → content manager review → tone/voice review → PMM review → staging → final review → publish) but uses Sanity CMS, which has proven slow and cumbersome. This meeting focused on clarifying which process steps GrowthX would own, how approvals would work, and what technical requirements (translations, internal linking, CMS access) must be resolved to hit project velocity targets.

## Key Topics

### Content Strategy & Keyword Alignment

  - **Goal:** Avoid content overlap between GrowthX and Netwrix teams.
  - **Process:**
    1.  GrowthX delivers a content strategy with topics and keywords.
    2.  Netwrix reviews the strategy against its existing content bank.
    3.  Netwrix approves topics for production.
  - **Netwrix POC:** Ralf will be the approver while Mariel is on PTO.
  - **Content Refresh:** GrowthX will perform targeted updates on existing content, not full rewrites, to improve SEO without discarding prior research.

### Workflow & Approval Process

  - **Current Netwrix Workflow:** A multi-stage process ensures technical accuracy and brand consistency.
      - Outline → Copy → SEO Review → Content Manager Review → Tone/Voice Review → PMM Review → Staging → Final Review → Publish.
  - **GrowthX Integration:** GrowthX will manage most production steps (SEO, PMM, copy), leaving Netwrix responsible for:
      - **Author Approval:** Required for all articles to ensure ownership.
      - **Final Review:** A quick check before publishing.
  - **Target Review Time:** 24 hours from staging to publish, a critical goal for the project's velocity.

### Sanity CMS Bottleneck

  - **Problem:** The Sanity CMS is slow and difficult to use, creating a major bottleneck.
      - **Example:** One article with code snippets took 3 hours to stage.
  - **Impact:** This inefficiency risks the project's ability to publish enough content to test performance and gather data within the 8-week sprint.
  - **Solution:** GrowthX will investigate ways to streamline the Sanity workflow.

### Project Plan & Logistics

  - **8-Week Plan:**
      - **Wk 1:** Kickoff & Discovery.
      - **Wk 2:** Deliver strategy & keywords for approval.
      - **Wk 3:** Lock strategy; produce 1 calibration article for feedback.
      - **Wk 4:** Publish calibration article; produce 3 more for quality check.
      - **Wk 6–8:** Ramp up production to 5 articles/week.
  - **CMS Access:** GrowthX requires Sanity access to stage articles.
      - **Blocker:** James (on PTO until next Thursday) is the only one who can grant access.
      - **Timeline:** This is not a blocker for the initial strategy phase.
  - **Image Production:** GrowthX designers will create images using Netwrix's photography guide.
  - **Translations:** Netwrix requires content translation for 6 languages (Spanish, German, Portuguese, Korean, Japanese, Chinese).
      - **Requirement:** Internal links must be searched for within Sanity to ensure they resolve to the correct localized page URL.
