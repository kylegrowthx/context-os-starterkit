## Key Topics

### Project Context & Campaign Goal

GrowthX is launching a programmatic SEO campaign for HyperExponential (HX), an insurance software platform. The campaign targets insurance pricing factors across multiple lines of business (commercial fire, commercial allied lines, cyber, etc.). Each LOB will have a dedicated guide page (e.g., "Guide to Commercial Fire Insurance Pricing") containing detailed, AI-generated content about factors that affect premiums for that LOB. Bailey is responsible for finalizing the site map and page structure by end of week, using search volume analysis to confirm which topics warrant dedicated pages versus nested subtopics.

### The Template Ambiguity (Core Blocker)

The existing page template sketch includes multiple content sections (overview, factors, coverage, claims, FAQs, HX sales pitch) organized as H3 headings with bullet points. Kirkland expressed concern that this layout—while comprehensive—may appear as a "wall of text" and asked whether the final page should incorporate visual design elements (tables, icons) or remain text-based. Bailey confirmed that the client's expectation is likely for visual richness to break up content, but without an in-house design team, it's unclear who is responsible for defining that. This ambiguity is the primary blocker: Kirkland needs clarity on the final page structure before building an automated content pipeline.

### Resource & Ownership Constraints

The client has limited in-house design capacity and is working with an external design agency. Bailey noted this means "content is going to drive the template"—GrowthX will likely produce and hand off content before the client's design team is ready with a finalized layout. Kirkland raised a historical concern: the VAPI project (also pSEO, also for an AI company) required GrowthX to own the design, which became a "nightmare" due to complexity and timeline friction. The HX engagement is explicitly structured to avoid this: the client should own design, but resource constraints make execution unclear.

### Proposed Resolution: Manual POC + Leadership Alignment

Kirkland proposed generating a proof-of-concept (POC) page manually using Claude, applying the existing template to a new line of business (e.g., cyber insurance). This POC would then be shared with the client to elicit explicit feedback on layout preferences, information hierarchy, and content depth—effectively using the POC as a discovery tool. Bailey agreed this is sensible and committed to consulting GrowthX directors Andy and Kyle to determine:
1. Whether directors should themselves propose a design approach for the POC
2. How to establish a standard process for future "GrowthX content + client design" engagements
3. How to reconcile the need to deliver content quickly with the client's slower design pace
