## Context

GrowthX and Fleet have been collaborating on comparison articles for competitive positioning, particularly around MDM (Mobile Device Management) feature parity. Fleet is a newer product (two years on market) with recently added MDM capabilities, and faces market perception issues where AI search results incorrectly state "Fleet is not an MDM." The collaboration aims to create factual comparison pages that educate buyers about Fleet's parity with established competitors like Jamf and Intune.

This meeting represents a pivot point in the partnership. GrowthX's initial competitive article pipeline was designed to be neutral and factual, but this approach failed to support Fleet's strategic business goal of proving MDM parity and positioning Fleet as the superior choice. The Jamf article submitted by GrowthX was rejected for containing factual errors and even recommending a competitor, exposing a fundamental misalignment between content strategy and business objectives. Brock Walters (Fleet's Product Education Manager) has taken direct action, rewriting the Jamf article as a model and establishing new requirements for all future comparison content.

## Key Topics

### Problem: Content Fails Strategic Goal

  - The Jamf article was rejected for being factually incorrect and recommending a competitor.
  - **Strategic Goal:** Prove Fleet's MDM feature parity with competitors like Jamf.
      - **Rationale:** Counter market misinformation (e.g., AI search results stating "Fleet is not an MDM").
      - **Required Tone:** Honest comparisons that always position Fleet as the superior choice.
  - **Root Cause:** GrowthX's neutral content pipeline is misaligned with this strategic goal.

### Solution: New Process & Resources

  - **New Review Process:**
      - Brock will provide line-by-line feedback in GitHub comments.
      - **Rationale:** This enables GrowthX to make precise, tool-assisted edits, accelerating the review cycle.
  - **New Resources for GrowthX:**
      - Jamf battle card
      - Company-wide competitive matrix
      - **Rationale:** These internal documents provide the accurate, factual data needed to build credible comparisons.
  - **New Article Template:**
      - Comparison articles are now landing pages with a specific, mobile-responsive format.
      - **Rationale:** This requires a strict structure, including a single comparison table at the top and 4–10 FAQ questions.
      - Brock's edited Jamf article is the new model; he will provide a Figma wireframe screenshot.

### Example: "Linux MDM" Search Term

  - **Challenge:** A search term like "Linux MDM" is technically inaccurate but has high search volume.
  - **Solution:** Use the term in the headline to capture traffic, then educate the user in the article about the correct terminology and Fleet's capabilities.
  - **Process:** Brock will flag these terms in GitHub comments, indicating the need for an educational correction within the content.
