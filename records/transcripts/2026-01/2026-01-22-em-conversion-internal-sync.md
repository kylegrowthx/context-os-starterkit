## Summary

GrowthX EM team reviewed Marisol's newly built training platform designed to help EMs apply foundational principles to day-to-day work. The team identified three top priorities for playbook development: standardized reporting frameworks, proactive content refresh processes, and client analytics discovery guidance. Critical handoff gaps between Strategy Sprint and Phase 2 execution were highlighted as a pain point requiring clearer KPI definition and client readiness grading.

---

## Context

This meeting brought together the entire EM team and training leadership to address a fundamental challenge: GrowthX had strong foundational documentation and principles, but EMs struggled to translate this into real-time decision-making on client accounts. Ella Dillon, Head of EM Enablement, framed the problem: the company had brilliant concepts and thorough docs but hadn't drawn clear lines between first principles and day-to-day jobs-to-be-done.

Marisol Smith, acting as Training Architect, presented a new platform structure with foundational modules (company mission, talk tracks, objection handling) and master documents (week-by-week sprint guides, meeting checklists). The platform was built using Vygotsky's Zone of Proximal Development theory—scaffolding knowledge from foundational to applied. The team then workshopped pain points, gaps in the training, and how to operationalize learning so EMs could confidently apply new skills on live accounts.

## Key Topics

### EM Pain Points & Training Gaps

- **Reporting & Analytics:**
    - **Problem:** Reporting is inconsistent and time-consuming, requiring EMs to "reinvent the structure every time." This makes it hard to tell a clear story of impact to clients.
    - **Need:** A standardized framework for different reporting cadences (bi-weekly, monthly, quarterly) that connects signals (e.g., organic traffic, keyword rankings) to client business goals (e.g., conversions, pipeline).
    - **Need:** Guidance on setting up analytics (e.g., GA4 events, UTM tracking) and asking the right discovery questions to get necessary client-side data without creating friction.
    - **Insight:** Many EMs don't know how to help clients set up proper attribution, leading to incomplete reporting narratives.

- **Strategy Sprint Handoff:**
    - **Problem:** The handoff from Strategy Sprint to Phase 2 Execution is a major source of friction and account risk.
    - **Issues:**
        - **Unclear KPIs:** Goals are often "pie in the sky" (e.g., "3x traffic from zero baseline") and buried in Notion, making them impossible to track or celebrate progress against.
        - **Documentation Gaps:** Client "Operating Manuals" are often incomplete, lacking key Fathom call links, risk documentation, or specific product change triggers that could invalidate strategy.
        - **"Recalibration Period":** The high-touch, strategy-driven quality of Sprint is hard to maintain during Phase 2 Execution, leading to a period where new messaging or product launches can make previously approved content obsolete, forcing rework and client confusion.
    - **Proposed Solution:** A "readiness grading" system for clients exiting Sprint to flag maturity and risks (e.g., slow internal review cycles, competing agency, weak analytics setup).
    - **Client Perspective:** Clients want to see consistent momentum post-Sprint and need to feel like they're graduating to a proven execution engine, not being handed off to a less-capable team.

- **Proactive Content Refresh:**
    - **Problem:** The process for refreshing content is inconsistent and ad-hoc. EMs/MEs don't have a systematic way to identify refresh opportunities, and clients don't understand why refresh is valuable (vs. just "more content").
    - **Need:** A formal process for identifying refresh opportunities (e.g., based on performance signals like declining CTR, competitor moves, product pivots) and integrating them into the monthly/quarterly execution pipeline.
    - **Need:** Training for MEs/CMs on refresh tactics (repurposing, updating, republishing) and a clear client narrative explaining why refreshing high-performing content is a faster path to growth than building net-new from scratch.
    - **Insight:** Refresh should be positioned as strategic optimization, not as a "we ran out of ideas" fallback.

### New EM Training Platform

- **Philosophy:** Built on Vygotsky's Zone of Proximal Development—scaffolding knowledge from foundational first principles to applied, job-to-be-done tasks. Addresses the core problem: brilliant concepts don't translate to real-time decision-making if they're not tied to specific jobs.
- **Structure:**
    - **Foundational Modules (Everyone):** Core company principles and shared language
        - Company origin story, mission, vision
        - Rented attention vs. earned attention (content philosophy)
        - Growth Engine model (the core system)
        - Core beliefs (systems thinking, compounding, etc.)
        - Internal talk track (pitch, positioning, how we describe ourselves and clients)
        - Client talk track (external positioning)
        - Objection handling (difficult client scenarios with real call examples)
        - Non-negotiables (what we won't do)
        - EM vs. ME role clarity (to prevent overlap and confusion)
        - What is Sprint vs. what is Growth Execution (the cycle)
    - **Master Documents (Role-Specific):**
        - **Sprint EM Track:** Week-by-week guide mapping modules and modules to weekly tasks (kickoff through handoff)
        - **Growth Execution Track:** Ongoing execution, optimization, refresh, and client management
        - **Meeting-First Guide:** Checklists and scripts for key meetings (kickoff, strategy presentation, refresh conversations, etc.)
        - **Sprint Track Map:** Master index mapping all jobs-to-be-done (roles and tasks) to relevant modules for quick reference
- **Navigation & Consumption:**
    - Platform is designed for on-demand, needs-based learning, not linear front-to-back consumption
    - A "jobs-to-be-done" database is in development to improve searchability (e.g., "How do I handle a difficult client?" → pulls relevant modules + examples)
    - Recommendation: Each EM completes foundational modules on hire, then dips into master documents as needed for their current role/sprint
    - No formal test; instead, Ella proposed recurring peer-to-peer workshopping sessions where EMs apply training to real scenarios, giving Marisol feedback on gaps

### Client Success & Renewal Strategy

- **Positioning Shift:** As clients renew, GrowthX needs to be seen as a strategic growth partner, not just a content vendor. This requires EMs to proactively demonstrate value across the full customer journey, not just during Sprint.
- **Proactive Engagement Model:** Move from reactive "we'll do what you ask" to proactive strategy refresh conversations (with real data and recommendations) as a way to keep clients engaged, prevent churn, and position for higher-value renewals.
- **Real-World Examples:** Jo will share recordings of strategy refresh conversations with Marisol to serve as training examples for the broader team.
