## Key Topics

### Content Quality & SEO Gaps

  - **AI-Generated Feel:** The writing is unnatural, with repetitive citations (e.g., one source cited 7x) and generic meta titles ("Target Keyword in 2026 Guide").
  - **Factual Errors:** Articles contained "hallucinations" (9–10 in one), requiring significant fact-checking.
  - **Poor SEO Structure:**
      - **Header Hierarchy:** Incorrect (e.g., using bold text instead of H2/H3 tags), which harms search engine and AI model visibility.
      - **Internal Linking:** Non-existent, a key process step.
      - **Keyword Cannibalization:** Targeting keywords that compete with existing Brex content.
  - **SERP Analysis:** A recent Google update (Dec) shifted rankings to favor product pages over articles for some keywords.
      - **Action:** GrowthX must analyze the SERP for each target keyword to determine if an article can realistically rank in the top 10 or if the goal is purely AI visibility.

### Deep Dive: "Best Software for Automated Reconciliation" (Listicle)

  - **Competitor Framing:** The article's primary flaw is its overly positive framing of competitors, which undermines Brex's position.
      - **Example:** Highlighting a competitor's "number one rankings on multiple G2 grids" or listing their enterprise customers (Lululemon, DoorDash) provides social proof that could send leads to rivals.
      - **Required Standard:** Competitor descriptions must be neutral, focusing on features without recommending the product or listing impressive customer logos.
  - **Brex's "Cons" Section:** The listed cons are problematic.
      - **Example:** "Focuses primarily on spend management rather than standalone reconciliation" directly contradicts the article's purpose.
      - **Required Standard:** Brex's "cons" must align with its established messaging (e.g., "not for very small businesses").
  - **Header Structure:** The article's product descriptions use bold text instead of proper H2/H3 tags, which is an SEO error.
  - **Content Flow:** The article abruptly ends after the product list, lacking a proper conclusion.

### Deep Dive: "How to Conduct a Burn Rate Analysis" & "Days Cash on Hand"

  - **Burn Rate Analysis:** The article's main issue was its repetitive citation style, which made it read like a summary of another site.
  - **Days Cash on Hand:** This article was a good example of Jon's editing style.
      - **Process:** Jon's edits took \~30–40 minutes, focusing on improving flow and removing the "AI sound."
      - **Outcome:** The edits added authority, situational examples, and a more confident tone.
      - **Required Standard:** This editing style is the new benchmark for all content.
  - **External Linking Strategy:**
      - **Problem:** Articles link to external sites that compete directly with Brex's own content (e.g., an article on startup runway).
      - **Required Standard:** Avoid linking to direct competitors. For data citations, GrowthX should first check if Brex has its own authoritative content on the topic.

### Process & Tooling Updates

  - **Sanity CMS:** The current Sanity setup is inefficient for marketers. Aida will provide feedback based on a more user-friendly client implementation.
  - **Image Workflow:** Jon will now use the cover images attached in Airtable.
      - **Note:** Brex's brand team may provide image prompts for GrowthX to follow in the future.
