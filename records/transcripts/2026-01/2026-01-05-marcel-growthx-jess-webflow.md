## Overview & Key Topics

**Core Frameworks**
- **Outcomes → Atomic Unit → Output → Transformation Model**: Content as atomic unit of marketing; pages as primary output; outcomes are AI visibility, search visibility, traffic, conversion, revenue
- **Context Engineering**: Most enterprises lack foundational context (company narrative, personas, writing guidelines, competitive positioning, product catalogs); this must precede automation
- **AI Maturity Curve**: Enterprise audience skews toward low AI maturity with manual-first processes; goal is to move them across deterministic automation → agentic workflows
- **Outside-in vs. Inside-out**: Market trends (AEO, AI visibility, changing discovery patterns) vs. organizational operationalization (team productivity, cost, governance, autonomy)

**Key Discussion Topics**
- AI native vs. traditional marketing: operational patterns, team structure, decision-making speed
- "New problems" in AI-native worlds: autonomous agent drift, human oversight models, context management at scale
- Enterprise productivity failure modes: teams of 50 producing less than one; lack of documented context, written standards, clear outcomes
- Website/catalog as compounding asset: investment thesis (similar to Amazon's $1-2B annual catalog investment); AI acceleration of decay half-life
- Audience participation: Examples of campaigns, team transformations, channel/revenue challenges from attendees' own organizations

**Event Arc**
- Day 1: Marcel keynote → Webflow trends & insights → breakout discussions on operationalization
- Day 2 (TBD): Practitioner-focused sessions, hands-on workshops, peer learning
- Dinner (evening): Informal networking and relationship-building (Marcel's participation pending)
