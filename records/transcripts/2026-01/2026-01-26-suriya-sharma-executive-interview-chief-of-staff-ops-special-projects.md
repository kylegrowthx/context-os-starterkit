## Key Topics

### Company Context & Role Need

  - **Growth Trajectory:** The company is scaling rapidly, growing from \~$2M to $12M ARR last year with a goal of $36M ARR this year.
  - **Operational Model:** The business is operationally heavy, with a "build" side (AI workflows) and a "deliver" side (content creation).
      - **Delivery Team:** \~55 of 90+ employees, serving 60+ customers at an average of $200k/year.
      - **Margins:** Gross margin is \~70%.
  - **Role Rationale:** The company has outgrown its flat structure. The Chief of Staff will provide the operational systems, accountability, and structure needed for this next phase of scale.

### Candidate Background & Experience

  - **Career Path:** Consulting → Mixpanel (BizOps) → Airtable (Special Projects) → Everlance (BizOps → Head of Post-Sales).
  - **Motivation:** Driven by high-impact work and seeing the tangible results of process improvements.
  - **Current Situation:** Seeking a new role after Everlance's acquisition by a slow-moving, legacy company.

### Deep Dive: Everlance Reimbursement Ops Project

  - **Context:** Joined Everlance (35 employees) as an IC BizOps role reporting to the COO (Dan).
  - **Problem:** The B2B reimbursement process was a manual, spreadsheet-based system with critical failures.
      - **Error Rate:** 10% of payments were incorrect.
      - **Delay Rate:** 20% of customers were paid late.
  - **Solution:** Led a parallel product/process overhaul, collaborating closely with the product team.
      - **Approach:** Incremental product features were built in lockstep with process changes, ensuring the ops team could adapt without disruption.
      - **First Win:** A simple CSV upload feature for driver rates eliminated the error-prone `INDEX/MATCH` step.
  - **Outcome:** The project was handed off after achieving a 0% error rate and 0% delay rate. The process is now fully productized, with the ops team serving a QA function.

### Management & Collaboration Style

  - **Manager (Dan):** Described as a trusting leader who assumed success, which fostered growth.
  - **Self-Assessment:**
      - **Strengths:** Drives projects to completion, proactive, scrappy, and an advocate for the customer.
      - **Feedback:** Needs to invest more in small talk to build relationships, a skill Sharma is actively developing.
  - **Collaboration:** Prefers hybrid/in-person work for faster learning and relationship-building.
