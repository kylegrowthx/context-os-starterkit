## Summary

GrowthX conducted a strategic deep dive with Talon.One to align on content priorities and product positioning. Talon.One outlined their new Incentives Marketing framework spanning Loyalty Management, Offer Management, and Personalized Promotions, with emphasis on prioritizing three core verticals (Retail, QSR, Grocery) and leveraging T1 tech partnerships (Braze, Shopify, Adobe) to win both competitive conversions and enterprise digital transformation deals.

---

## Context

Talon.One is refreshing its product messaging after a major framework overhaul four months prior. The company serves two distinct acquisition paths—60% win from direct competitors (smaller agile brands like Gymshark seeking flexible solutions) and 40% from monolithic legacy platforms (large enterprises like Adidas in digital transformation). This split demands tailored content and partnership strategies. Talon.One's tech ecosystem integration (especially with Braze, Shopify, and Adobe) is critical to winning market share among brands modernizing their commerce and loyalty infrastructure. GrowthX is building a comprehensive content strategy to educate prospects on the value of the Incentives Marketing umbrella and address vertical-specific content gaps.

## Overview

- **Content Focus:** Prioritize Retail, QSR, and Grocery. Retail needs updates, QSR needs foundational content (blogs/e-books), and Grocery needs to leverage its strong logos to win new enterprise accounts.
- **Product Framework:** The new framework (Loyalty, Offer Mgmt, Personalized Promos) guides prospects from basic governance to advanced, profit-driving strategies, all under the "Incentives Marketing" umbrella.
- **Partnerships:** Content must feature T1 tech partners (Braze, Shopify, Adobe) to solidify Talon.One's position in modern tech stacks and attract prospects undergoing digital transformation.
- **Customer Acquisition:** The 60/40 customer mix (60% from competitors, 40% from monoliths) dictates two distinct content strategies: one for agile brands and another for large enterprises.

## Key Topics

### Product Framework & Content Pillars

  - **New Framework:** A new product framework (last 4 months) guides prospects from basic to advanced use cases.
  - **Content Pillars:**
      - **Loyalty Management:** Gamification is a key theme.
      - **Offer Management:** Basic governance for enterprise clients with thousands of SKUs.
          - **Goal:** Save time and centralize offer management.
          - **Use Case:** A "foot in the door" for clients with chaotic promotions.
      - **Personalized Promotions:** Advanced, event-triggered offers (e.g., churn-based discounts).
          - **Goal:** Drive profit margins.
          - **Requires:** Integration with a Customer Engagement Platform (CEP).
      - **Talon.One Predict:** AI for recommending optimal offers.
          - **Status:** In development; not a current content priority.
  - **Incentives Marketing:** The unifying concept. Content must educate prospects on combining loyalty and promotions, often by "firewalling" promotions behind the loyalty program.

### Industry Prioritization & Content Gaps

  - **Top 3 Priorities:** Retail & E-commerce, QSR & Dining, and Grocery.
      - **Rationale:** These industries represent the majority of clients and prospects.
      - **Retail:** Strongest presence, but content needs updating.
      - **QSR:** Strong case studies (e.g., Joe and the Juice), but needs foundational content (blogs, e-books).
      - **Grocery:** Strong logos (e.g., supermarkets), making it a key focus for winning enterprise accounts.
  - **Emerging Industries:** Financial Services and Travel & Hospitality.
      - **Challenge:** Use cases are highly varied, making messaging difficult.
      - **Strategy:** Focus on specific, proven use cases (e.g., Built Rewards, Trainline) rather than broad industry content.
  - **B2B:** A broad category with varied use cases (e.g., SiteOne, Carlsberg's B2B division).

### Partnership Ecosystem & Customer Acquisition

  - **T1 Tech Partners:** Braze, Shopify, and Adobe (integration live \~April).
      - **Goal:** Solidify Talon.One's position in modern tech stacks.
      - **Shopify Use Case:** Talon.One provides advanced loyalty features that native Shopify tools lack.
  - **System Integrators (SIs):** Consultancies (e.g., Deloitte, EPAM) that guide clients through digital transformations.
  - **Customer Acquisition Paths:**
      - **60% from Competitors:** Smaller, agile brands (e.g., Gymshark) replacing inflexible solutions.
      - **40% from Monoliths:** Large enterprises (e.g., Adidas) undergoing digital transformation.
      - **Significance:** This split requires two distinct content strategies.
