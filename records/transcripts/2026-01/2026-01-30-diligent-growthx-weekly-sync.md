## Context

This meeting marks a strategic inflection point for the GrowthX-Diligent partnership. GrowthX has developed CheckDat as a replacement for Scrunch, offering contextual accuracy, faster data retrieval, and evaluation-stage query focus. Rather than fully migrating, the team adopted a dual-tool approach that leverages each platform's strengths: CheckDat for discovery and competitive gap analysis, Scrunch for tracking high-priority prompts tied directly to Diligent's revenue expansion strategy. Simultaneously, Vivek Shankar completed the transition of all Diligent account responsibilities to Carrie Chowske, signaling a shift toward sustained, scaled delivery. The priority reordering reflects Diligent's business model—expansion revenue from existing customers (e.g., turning $50k Boards deals into $250k+ opportunities) requires deep competitive differentiation in specific product lines, not broad GRC refresh work.

## Key Topics

### New AI Visibility Tool: CheckDat

  - GrowthX demoed CheckDat, its new internal tool replacing Scrunch for AI visibility reporting.
  - **Key Differentiators from Scrunch:**
      - **Contextual Accuracy:** Uses deep brand context (via Exa) to filter out non-brand mentions, improving precision.
      - **Immediate Historical Data:** Library prompts provide up to 90 days of historical data instantly, enabling faster testing.
      - **Evaluation-Stage Focus:** Prompts target high-impact, evaluation-stage queries (e.g., "best tools for X") to capture users closer to conversion.
      - **Citation Tracking:** Links directly to the specific page cited by the AI model.
      - **Discovery Engine:** The "Sources" page acts as an AI SERP, identifying the most commonly cited pages for a given category.

### Strategic Rationale for Dual-Tool Strategy

  - Tyler Phelps outlined a dual-tool strategy, using both CheckDat and Scrunch to serve distinct purposes.
  - **Scrunch as Source of Truth:**
      - Essential for tracking performance against specific, high-priority prompts that drive revenue.
      - Diligent's growth strategy prioritizes expansion revenue over inbound for many solutions.
      - **Example:** The "Boards" business unit (BU) is a key entry point with a strong expansion motion, turning initial $50k deals into $250k+ opportunities.
      - Focus must be on widening the competitive gap in these specific, high-differentiation areas.
  - **CheckDat for Discovery & Gap Analysis:**
      - Ideal for finding new, high-potential prompts and identifying gaps in the current measurement set.
      - **Limitation:** The current competitor view is too broad and skews metrics. It fails to show the true competitive distance against direct rivals by including larger, less relevant players (e.g., Vanta).
      - Carrie will recommend a product update to allow for BU-specific competitor groupings.

### Project & Content Priorities

  - **New Priority Order:**
    1.  ACL & DMI
