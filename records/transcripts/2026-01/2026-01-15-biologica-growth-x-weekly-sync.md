## Summary

Biologica announced a non-renewal of its GrowthX engagement due to a 40+ article review backlog and competing internal priorities (CAC optimization, merchandising forecast reconciliation, and a clinical study launch). Despite the pause, initial SEO metrics are exceptionally strong: 34 articles live, 22 already generating organic sessions, "hockey stick" traffic growth in early December and late January, and Google rankings jumping from page 8 to 4-5 in one week. Biologica plans to re-engage in 2-3 months after clearing its backlog. GrowthX will deliver a final batch of 5 articles, complete one edit round, and hand over the Content OS and strategy documents by January 27-28. The team also demonstrated "Check That," GrowthX's new AI visibility tool launching end of January, which tracks brand mentions across LLM evaluation-stage prompts.

---

## Context

Biologica is a health and wellness brand (hellobdv.com) that launched December 8-9, 2025, focused on women's health products (primary, midlife, and post-menopause wellness). GrowthX began providing content marketing and SEO services approximately 6 months prior to this meeting, producing 34 published articles in a pipeline of 97 total (39 awaiting Biologica review, 12 in GrowthX link-checking phase).

Biologica has been in early-stage growth mode post-launch, operating on razor-thin margins and making rapid operational decisions. The company is currently dependent almost entirely on Meta ads for customer acquisition, a dependency Brian Magida explicitly described as untenable. To stabilize the business, Biologica must resolve three interconnected issues: (1) achieve a target Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) they feel confident deploying at scale, (2) understand their true product mix and customer demographics (currently skewed unexpectedly toward post-menopause due to a Meta ad targeting anomaly), and (3) finalize their next inventory buy without clarity on whether demographic patterns are structural or ad-driven. Additionally, a team member named Maggie is managing a clinical study launch, which has become the team's highest priority over content review work.

GrowthX team members present: Andi Bailey (likely Head of Content/Strategy) and Katya Luscomb (likely Head of Performance/Analytics). The relationship has been collaborative despite "bumps along the way," and both parties view the non-renewal as a pause, not an exit.

## Key Topics

### Biologica's Decision: Non-Renewal

  - **Reason:** A 40+ article review backlog is the primary blocker.
      - Continuing the 20 articles/month cadence would only worsen the backlog, making it an inefficient use of resources.
      - Internal review capacity is limited by team priorities (e.g., clinical studies).
  - **Core Business Focus:** Biologica is prioritizing immediate, high-ROI initiatives to stabilize the business post-launch.
      - **CAC:** Optimizing customer acquisition cost.
      - **Channel Diversification:** Reducing reliance on Meta ads.
      - **Merchandising:** Resolving a major forecast miss.
          - **Problem:** A Meta ad targeting a primary product is over-delivering to women over 50, driving high sales of post-menopause products.
          - **Impact:** This anomaly complicates the next inventory buy, as Biologica must determine if the sales trend is sustainable or a temporary ad-driven effect.
  - **Future Plan:** Re-engage in 2–3 months once the backlog is cleared and business metrics are more stable.

### SEO Performance & Roadmap

  - **Current Status:** 34 articles live; 97 total in the pipeline (39 with Biologica for review, 12 with GrowthX for link checking).
  - **Key Performance Signals:**
      - **Sessions:** "Hockey stick" growth observed in early December and the last week.
      - **Impressions:** Significant spike over the Christmas/New Year period.
      - **Ranking:** Average position jumped from page 8 to page 4–5 in one week.
      - **Indexing:** 22 articles are already generating organic sessions.
  - **Google Indexing Lag:** Google's slower indexing is normal for new sites with low site authority.

  - **GrowthX's "Check That" Demo:**

      - **Function:** Tracks how brands appear in LLM responses to evaluation-stage prompts.
      - **Metrics:** Share of voice, sentiment, and citations across major LLMs.
      - **Use Case:** Identifies content gaps and potential affiliate opportunities.
