## Key Topics

### New Article Format

- Articles now include "Key Takeaways" summary positioned directly after introduction.
  - **Rationale**: Answers search intent upfront (TOFU-level informational queries), improves SEO, and enhances readability.
  - **Label change**: Shifted from "TLDR" (perceived as too casual) to "Key Takeaways" (more professional for B2B forensics audience).
  - **CTA inclusion**: Optional; Jennifer will decide on a case-by-case basis.

### Content Guardrails & Litigation Risk Mitigation

- **Core principle**: Avoid making absolute claims that opposing counsel can use to undermine expert credibility in depositions or cross-examination.
  - **ACTAR credential example**: Cannot claim "all experts are ACTAR-certified" because ~10% of Rimkus team may be mechanical engineers or other specialists without ACTAR. Solution: "Our team includes ACTAR-accredited specialists."
  - **Testifying experience example**: Cannot claim "all experts have prior courtroom testimony" because new hires may not have testified. Solution: Focus on preparation process (mock cross-examinations, corporate counsel review) rather than guaranteeing prior experience.
- **Language approach**: Use "typically," "generally," and conditional phrasing ("may," "often") instead of absolute statements.
- **Action**: Jennifer will draft template promotional outro serving as reusable language for all future articles; GrowthX implements across suite.

### Expert Attribution & Content Refresh Strategy

- **Hydrogen Embrittlement refresh**: This article was originally authored by Alan Conway, a current Rimkus material science expert.
  - **Workflow**: Jennifer flags refresh to Conway; if he approves, he receives author credit (boosts EEAT and author credibility).
  - **SEO strategy**: High-volume keyword with informational intent. Option to refresh existing technical piece or create new top-funnel article while keeping original.
- **Weekly flagging process**: Matthew will share Monday topic list with Jennifer to flag expert-authored content, reducing manual review burden.

### Topic Scope Clarification: Global Energy Services

- **Current problem**: "Construction" scope was too narrow, missing adjacent high-value services.
- **Organizational structure at Rimkus**:
  - **Service group**: Global Energy Services
  - **Sub-services**: Construction, Energy (renewable energy, oil & gas)
- **Impact**: Current forensics content focus on construction alone leaves material coverage gaps.
- **Action**: Jennifer to provide updated website materials (being refreshed in next 1-2 weeks); GrowthX to expand topic categorization to include all Global Energy Services sub-services.
