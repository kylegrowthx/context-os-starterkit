## Summary

GrowthX kicks off an 8-week content marketing strategy sprint with Talaera, positioning their platform-first pivot around two core pillars: competitor comparisons to capture search visibility and pain-point-driven content for international communication. The engagement targets quantifiable inbound lead growth, from ~5 B2B leads/month to 20/month, with hundreds of B2C leads monthly.

---

## Context

Talaera is an international language and cross-cultural communication training company undergoing a strategic transformation from a services-only delivery model to a platform-first business. Their flagship product now includes "Talk to Tally," an AI coach integrated with a proprietary 900-point communication framework and analytics dashboard designed for L&D leaders at global enterprises.

The company faces a classic B2B SaaS visibility problem: strong product-market fit and high conversion rates on inbound leads (~5 leads/month), but insufficient top-of-funnel reach. They lose competitive positioning opportunities when target buyers research vendors during initial evaluation phases. The engagement addresses this by developing content that targets both direct buyers (HR/L&D professionals at global companies) and individual learners seeking professional English polish, with focus on competitor displacement and solving real-world cross-cultural communication challenges.

## Key Topics

### The Problem: Low Visibility & Inconsistent Lead Gen

  - **Core Issue:** A "top of funnel" problem. Talaera is not visible to buyers during their initial research, losing out on being one of the 2–3 vendors they evaluate.
  - **Current Lead Sources:** A mix of inbound (SEO, webinars, AI search), referrals, and cold outbound. Inbound leads convert at a high rate but are too few.
  - **Content Performance:**
      - **High-Converting:** Niche, pain-point-driven content (e.g., "How to work with Indian teams").
      - **Low-Converting:** Broad cultural guides.
      - **B2B Gap:** No strong content asset (e.g., whitepaper) specifically for business English training.
  - **Sales Process:**
      - **Triggers:** Vendor consolidation, dissatisfaction with competitors, or external events (e.g., M\&A, securing a major international client).
      - **Objections:** Historically, price (vs. Preply) and lack of multi-language support.
      - **New Objection Handling:** The new platform enables flexible tiers (e.g., 80% AI, 20% 1-on-1), solving the price objection by offering a scalable, integrated solution.

### The Solution: A Platform-First Content Strategy

  - **Strategic Pivot:** The company is rebranding and shifting its core offering from services to a platform-first experience.
      - **Key Differentiators:** Integrated analytics for L\&D leaders, a consistent 900-point communication framework, and the new AI coach, "Talk to Tally."
  - **Content Pillars:**
      - **1. Competitor Comparisons:** Create "vs." pages and listicles to capture search visibility from competitors (e.g., Preply, italki, Cambly).
      - **2. Pain-Point-Driven Content:** Focus on real-world challenges of international communication, not just language tutorials.
          - **Examples:** "How to run effective meetings in English," "Business English for sales teams," "Cross-cultural communication for teams in Saudi Arabia."
  - **Audience Targeting:**
      - **Primary B2B:** HR Leaders, L\&D, Global Learning, People Partners.
      - **Secondary B2B:** Support Leaders (team managers).
      - **B2C:** Professionals seeking to polish their English for credibility.
  - **Funnel Optimization:** GrowthX will help create targeted CTAs for blog posts to improve conversion rates.

### The Goal: Quantifiable Inbound Growth

  - **B2B Inbound Leads:** Increase from \~5/month to 20/month.
  - **B2C Inbound Leads:** Increase to hundreds/month.
  - **Long-Term Vision:** Shift marketing investment from cold outreach to organic inbound, with a goal of doubling revenue.
