## Context

Logic, an AI agent infrastructure platform, is preparing for fundraising and needs to prove GTM capability through content-driven user acquisition. GrowthX has been executing a content partnership to capture search intent and position Logic as the authority in agent-building and LLM infrastructure. The meeting reviewed three months of content work, discussed emerging quality improvements in the pipeline, and reset strategy around high-intent keywords and funnel-based metrics that resonate with investors. A new proprietary LLM visibility tracking platform launching Feb 2–7 will provide cleaner data on how Logic's content is cited by AI models, replacing reliance on Scrunch which conflates the company with the concept.

## Key Topics

### Content Performance & Quality

  - Recent articles show a "step change" in quality, adding helpful technical nuance.
  - Feedback is now "yellow" (editorial), not "red" (egregious errors), indicating significant progress.
  - A recurring issue is the reappearance of previously addressed feedback, such as including specific model version numbers (e.g., GPT-4).
      - **Cause:** A conflict in the source artifacts used by the content pipeline.
      - **Action:** Rachel will run a "conflict check" to resolve these issues.

### New Content Strategy & KPIs

  - **North Star:** Paying customers.
  - **Goal:** Prove GTM effectiveness for fundraising by demonstrating an ability to reliably acquire users.
  - **New Funnel-Based KPIs:**
      - **Impressions:** Shows presence for target keywords.
      - **Clicks:** Shows ability to drive traffic to the site.
      - **Sign-ups:** The most compelling metric, proving user acquisition and creating an optimization problem (monetization) for Logic to solve.
  - **Attribution:**
      - Logic uses Statsig to track traffic sources and user funnels.
      - No SEO/AEO conversions to paid customers yet.
      - Azzam will provide monthly Statsig data exports to inform the content strategy.

### Content Prioritization

  - **Tier 1: High-Intent Keywords**
      - **Focus:** "How to build an agent," "ship AI fast," "productionize AI agents."
      - **Rationale:** Capture users before they commit to competitors (e.g., LangChain, CrewAI), avoiding the high switching costs of targeting users already using another tool.
      - **Action:** Expand keyword research to find low-volume, high-intent queries reflecting engineer search behavior.
  - **Tier 1 Sub-Pillar: LLM Infra DevEx**
      - **Focus:** Capture search intent for infrastructure components Logic provides out-of-the-box.
      - **Examples:** "how to produce tests," "how to enforce output schema."
      - **Rationale:** Position Logic as the simple solution for complex infrastructure needs.
  - **Tier 2: Alternatives & Refreshes**
      - **Alternatives:** Continue targeting keywords like "LangChain alternatives" to capture bottom-of-funnel users researching options.
      - **Refreshes:** Prioritize refreshing existing AI use case content (e.g., content moderation) that is already cited by LLMs to capitalize on proven traction.
  - **Future Content:**
      - **"How to Build an Agent" Guide:** Azzam is creating a \~40-page, ungated guide for February to establish Logic's authority and promote its "spec-driven agents" philosophy.
      - **Mega-Content Series:** This guide will be the first in a series of monthly/bi-monthly, high-value, keyword-optimized pieces.
      - **Feature-Driven Content:** Validate search demand for new features (most shipping by April 2026) and schedule content refreshes for top-performing posts upon release.

### New LLM Visibility Platform

  - **Launch:** Rolling out to customers Feb 2–7.
  - **Function:** Tracks which blog posts are cited by LLMs, the prompts used, and Logic's placement in the answer.
  - **Advantage:** More accurate than Scrunch. The platform distinguishes between "Logic" (the company) and "logic" (the concept), providing cleaner, less skewed data.
