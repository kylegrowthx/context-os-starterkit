## Key Topics

### Product Value and Data Quality

Jason feels better about CheckThat after deeper product exploration. The validation-first mindset clicked—not trying to match Profound yet, just proving the model works. Demos are landing well: preloaded data, easy setup, and low cost resonate with prospects.

Data quality concerns remain. Panel data across the industry is essentially session tracking from privacy-compromising browser extensions. Marcel validated this with the Scrunch founder—everyone buys the same garbage data. CheckThat's approach (deep research + keyword data vs. clickstream) could be a differentiator worth surfacing.

### Community Growth Strategy

Marcel framed community with urgency: "Imagine it's your only income source as a solopreneur." Everything done for existing members should drive new memberships. The challenge is converting high-value workshop moments into shareable social content.

Current backend offers: AEO Masterclass (cohorted) and Creating Great AI Outputs (on-demand course). Both need validation through LinkedIn posts and ads before scaling. The test: can we get signups? What's the CAC? Only then double down.

### Marketing Budget and Spend Philosophy

Fiscal planning shows $3.4M marketing spend Q1-Q3, ~$5M all-in for the year (excluding sales). Marcel's stance: spend to learn, not to scale prematurely.

Key principles:
- $1,000 CAC is workable if subscription generates $200+/month
- Reinvest community revenue into acquisition programs
- Don't spend $100K to get 100 subscribers—validate at $10K first
- Competitors like Profound have $100M; GrowthX wins through creative, compounding, sustainable plays

### Carol Lu Role Definition

Carol declined other offers to join GrowthX in an interim capacity. Her consulting business exceeded $1M/year, and her network includes direct CMO relationships (Sentinel1 and Lovable both came through her intros).

Two potential scopes:
1. Forward-deployed CMO—client-facing relationship builder
2. Core marketing owner—positioning, packaging, pricing, website

Marcel's constraint: events and networking can't depend on his availability (personal situation requires 4:30pm hard stop). Carol could own event execution and external marketing autonomously.

### Services Pipeline Health

Critical numbers:
- 6/12 Q1 renewals at risk (Surge lost, plus Homebase, School AI, Superhuman, Abnormal, OCTA in danger)
- ~$4M ARR exposed
- Tyler maintaining 2 kickoffs/week is non-negotiable baseline
- Need 6 new qualified leads/week (33% win rate = 2 kickoffs)

Root causes: missed stakeholder relationships, scope creep without renewal conversations, leadership changes at clients. Without consistent kickoffs, revenue could halve by year-end.

### CheckThat Platform Development

Core product is solid; now it's about polish and finding the right hook. Marcel's testing pre-creating workspaces for prospect calls to validate onboarding. Backfill bugs are blocking the experience—workspaces aren't auto-populating with prompts and visibility data.

Proposed hook: "Check your website" audit showing AI visibility vs. competitors instantly. This creates an aha moment at signup and differentiates from competitors who lack the shared index.

### AI Visibility Reports

Marcel proposed weekly/monthly scheduled reports delivered via email:
- Snapshot of AI visibility and search visibility
- Narrative + charts + recommendations
- Shareable with executives (growth loop)
- Gated behind pro plan as upsell

No one acts on daily insights. Weekly cadence matches decision cycles. Start manual (pre-create for demos), validate usefulness, then programmatize. Jason compared to Strava's paywalled weekly reports—proven model.
