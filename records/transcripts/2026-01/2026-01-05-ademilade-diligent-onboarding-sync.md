## Key Topics

### Content Strategy & Product Positioning

  - **Audience Segmentation:** Articles can target multiple segments (SMB, Enterprise) via:
      - Contextual examples within the text.
      - Dedicated subheaders (H3s) for specific segments.
      - Use Claude to identify an article's primary audience.
  - **Product Positioning:**
      - **Rule:** Product mentions are confined to a single "How Diligent Helps" section before the conclusion.
      - **Exception:** Internal links to product pages are allowed within the body, but the Diligent brand name must not be mentioned.
      - **Rationale:** This strategy prioritizes thought leadership, building trust before introducing solutions.

### Workflow & Tools

  - **Airtable:** The source of truth for all content tasks.
      - **`Approved to Start` View:** Backlog of ~50 articles (10 weeks).
      - **`In Production` View:** Articles assigned for the current week.
      - **Process:** Move a completed article to `Review Internally` and notify Ademilade.
  - **Claude:** The primary tool for refreshes.
      - **Rationale:** Atlas is ineffective for refreshes; Claude's agentic capabilities are required.
      - **Pipeline:** Ademilade is building a new Claude pipeline to generate net-new articles, which will be manually integrated into refreshes.
  - **Infographics:** A new, automated pipeline is available.
      - **Types:** Quote, Definition, Checklist, Process View, Hub & Spoke.
      - **Status:** Awaiting client approval. Use with a note for Ademilade.

### Refresh Process: Step-by-Step

  - **1. Setup:**
      - Create a new Google Doc for the refresh.
      - Paste the original article's URL at the top for reference.
  - **2. Research & Analysis:**
      - **Claude (Audience Analysis):** In a new chat, paste the article and prompt Claude to identify the target audience, pain points, and concepts.
      - **Perplexity (SEO Analysis):** Use the refresh prompt to get an SEO-focused analysis and research suggestions.
  - **3. Generate Draft:**
      - **Claude (Main Draft):** In a new chat, use the refresh prompt, feeding it:
          - The original article.
          - The audience analysis from step 2.
          - The SEO analysis from step 2.
          - Links to 2-3 relevant Diligent internal reports (e.g., GC Risk Index).
      - **Note:** This process takes 5-10 minutes per article. Run prompts for multiple articles concurrently to maximize efficiency.
  - **4. Post-Processing & Editing:**
      - **Editorial Judgment:** The Claude output is a starting point. Edit heavily to match Diligent's tone, remove jargon, and ensure conciseness.
      - **Structure:**
          - **Intro:** Must include a "This guide covers..." bulleted list.
          - **"What is..." Section:** Add an H2/H3 definition section after the intro if missing.
          - **"How Diligent Helps":** Create this dedicated section before the conclusion.
          - **FAQs:** Add 4-5 questions. The heading must be "FAQs about [Topic]."
          - **Conclusion:** Ensure a transition paragraph and a clear CTA.
      - **CTAs:**
          - **Mid-Text:** Add 1-2 CTAs linking to product pages or Diligent Institute reports.
          - **End-of-Article:** Add a second CTA after the FAQs. Use different phrasing (e.g., "Schedule a demo" vs. "Book a call").
      - **Internal Linking:**
          - Add 6-7 internal links per ~2,000 words.
          - **Rules:**
              - Do not link to the primary keyword.
              - Use articles published in 2023 or later.
              - No competitor links.
              - No link spamming.
      - **Formatting:**
          - **Links:** Update any old `insights.diligence.com` links to the new `resources.diligence.com` blog.
          - **Images:** Add alt text to all images.
          - **Style:** No Oxford commas; follow specific M-dash rules.
