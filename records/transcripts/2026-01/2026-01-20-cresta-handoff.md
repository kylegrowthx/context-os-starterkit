## Summary

GrowthX leadership briefs Rachel Pasche and Bailey Seybolt on transitioning the Cresta account from sprint delivery to ongoing managed content production. Key focus: reducing product team review overhead through proactive alignment, resolving a critical content indexing issue, and establishing editorial workflows that maintain content quality while respecting Cresta's nuanced positioning as an AI overlay for contact centers.

## Context

Cresta recently converted from prospect to client, following a sprint-based discovery phase led by Aida Knežević. The company—an AI customer experience platform that runs on top of existing contact center solutions—has ambitious goals: drive middle-of-funnel leads and revenue within six months. However, the transition from sprint to ongoing delivery creates a critical handoff moment. Russell, Cresta's CMO, explicitly questioned this transition model, signaling concern about continuity. Simultaneously, a technical issue emerged: content published since January 1st hasn't been indexing in Google, despite recent fixes to a canonicalization error on the Cresta website. Rachel Pasche, GrowthX's first full-time editor, is stepping in as the primary content writer, with Ademilade handling editorial review and quality assurance. This meeting establishes the team structure, artifacts, workflows, and success criteria for the months ahead.
