## Key Topics

### Sales and Revenue Growth

The team must increase kickoff volume in January to offset churn risks and stabilize ARR.

- Lost Surge (still pays ~$700K for three months) and Homebase—$2.7M in ARR gone
- At risk: Abnormal School, AI, Octa, Exec, Superhuman—nearly $4M in potential churn
- Only January kickoffs contribute to Q1 ARR
- 25 kickoffs per quarter with stable conversion could still double ARR within a year
- Marcel will increase involvement on Wednesdays and hold more frequent syncs

Sales process needs balance: don't be overly protective in qualification. Sprints should feel like natural sales extensions.

### Content Strategy and Retention

Content must scale aggressively and focus on driving organic growth—not just publication quotas.

- Need to publish over 6,000 pages; ~2,800 currently indexed
- Five articles weekly leads to slow traffic growth; establish large surface area first
- Editors should enrich existing content with visuals and enhancements
- Example: Augment Code paying $50K/month but lacking basic screenshots
- Shift mindset from "content production" to "organic growth engine"

Editorial should lead before programmatic—programmatic took two months and five engineers to launch.

### Spendesk Client Prep

Spendesk is a European fintech (think Ramp/Brex for Europe) wanting to expand into the US.

**Key concerns from Spendesk:**
- Translation is critical—they're a European company with multilingual needs
- Resource allocation transparency—they want to understand time commitments
- Design asset quality—current examples looked repetitive or generic to them

**Approach for the call:**
- Lead with Ramp and Brex case studies
- Show Slack channel setup and detailed workflows
- Be honest about translation capabilities—start with Spanish, multi-language needs longer commitment
- Spendesk's Spanish blog gets ~22 clicks/month—whatever they're doing isn't working
- If translation scope creates friction early, be willing to walk away
