**Business Impact:** This directly addresses GrowthX's existential challenge—retention. The services business ($200k+/year ACV engagements) depends entirely on renewals. Five churned accounts in Q1 represents 25% churn on a 15-account cohort. At $24K-$90K per account, this translates to $120K-$450K in revenue loss—material to a sub-$5M company.

- **Critical logos at risk:** Engine (P0), Webflow ($24K investor logo, Q2, no owner), Udemy ($90K equivalent but likely 6-month terms, unclear ownership)—represent disproportionate revenue concentration.
- **Commercial skill gap:** Kyle and Carly (current renewal owners) lack negotiation and sales chops; George (only proven closer) unavailable because he's focused on new business. Risk: accounts slip away by default while operations are disorganized.

- **Visibility/reporting gap:** HubSpot has renewable-by-stage dashboards and conversion tracking, but not consistently used; churn discovered via Slack, not dashboards. No weekly heartbeat to flag accounts early.

**Broader Context:** Marcel outlined a tension between three competing priorities: (1) retaining existing revenue and maximizing churn-free growth (Marcel + Ella + Bridget focus), (2) building leverage and scale (Daniel's job; CheckThat launch), and (3) new business growth (Jason + Tyler).
