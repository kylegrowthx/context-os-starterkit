## Key Topics

### Google's AI Content Stance

  - **Problem:** Google's public stance against AI "chunking" (one paragraph per H3) has caused major ranking drops for Brex pages.
      - **Examples:** Corporate card policy page, business credit card rewards page (lost top-10 rankings).
  - **Hypothesis:** Google is now actively penalizing clear AI-generated patterns.
  - **Action:** Jon will personally refresh these high-priority, top-of-funnel articles.

### Internal Linking Strategy

  - **New Workflow:** GrowthX will implement a new process to ensure all internal links meet Brex's standards.
      - **Standard:** Links must be contextually relevant and target a page's primary keyword.
      - **Error:** Linking the same keyword to multiple pages (e.g., "corporate credit cards" to three different URLs) is a critical mistake.
      - **Rationale:** Brex's internal data shows a direct correlation between this specific linking strategy and ranking improvements.
  - **Resource:** Jon will provide the "mother doc" (content inventory) to help GrowthX understand the site's content and linking targets.

### Content Structure & "Information Gain"

  - **Goal:** Add "information gain" over SERP competitors to justify ranking.
  - **Tactics:**
      - **Unique Structures:** Use structures that are superior to top-ranking articles (e.g., NerdWallet's vs. article format).
      - **Proprietary Data:** Incorporate unique stats from Brex's data science team (ETA Feb).
      - **Case Studies:** Include mentions of Brex case studies where relevant.
      - **Unique Headers:** Create original, non-generic headers to stand out.
  - **Constraint:** Be cautious with competitor comparisons (e.g., Mercury) due to past legal threats.
