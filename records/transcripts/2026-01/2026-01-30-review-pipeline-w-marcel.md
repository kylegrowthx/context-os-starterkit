## Context

GrowthX is navigating Q1 2026 with pressure to meet forecasts and avoid "dark mark" quarters. The sales team operates with an inflated pipeline (~55 deals) that masks true deal velocity and distorts metrics like win rate (Q3's reported 70% win rate was a statistical anomaly from not closing lost deals). This directly affects forecast accuracy and pipeline coverage planning—a difference between 3x and 4x coverage represents ~$30M in pipeline requirements.

To address this, the team is implementing structural changes: daily pipeline hygiene with strict close-lost cadence, deploying CEO/Founder Marcel as an active sales closer to accelerate high-value deals (Vercel, Greenhouse, 11 Labs), and recruiting Andy (a Google Cloud veteran with 7 years in procurement/legal) to handle legal redlines and contract negotiations. The team is also testing flexible pricing ($15k entry point vs. $18k anchor) and launching Check That, a lower-effort offering for prospects not ready for full engagements. Success is measured by securing 2 kickoffs/week through March and establishing clean, reliable pipeline metrics by month-end.

## Key Topics

### Pipeline Health & Forecasting

  - **Problem:** The pipeline is inflated with \~55 deals, making it unmanageable and distorting key metrics.
  - **Impact:** Inaccurate data (win rate, deal cycle) makes forecasting and board reporting impossible.
      - **Example:** A 70% win rate reported in Q3 was a statistical anomaly caused by not closing lost deals.
      - **Consequence:** This prevents accurate pipeline coverage planning (e.g., 3x vs. 4x), which represents a $30M difference in required pipeline.
  - **Solution:** Implement a strict "close lost" cadence for inactive deals.
      - **Rationale:** This cleans the data and allows the sales team to focus on active opportunities.
      - **Safeguard:** Accounts will be protected for 6 months post-close-lost to prevent poaching.

### February Kickoff Strategy

  - **Goal:** Secure two kickoffs per week for February and March.
  - **Feb 9th Candidates:**
      - **Spendesk:** Legal redlines are minor; close is imminent.
      - **JAMA:** Stalled on legal terms (liability cap).
      - **Otter:** Unresponsive; Marcel to re-engage via a CMO community contact.
  - **Feb 16th Candidates:**
      - **Assort Health:** Ready to sign; verbal agreement secured.
      - **Vercel:** Top priority. A competitive win against AeroOps, driven by a strong relationship with the CMO.
          - **Context:** The CMO previously fired the same agency at Redis to hire us.
          - **Opportunity:** High-value programmatic work on a world-class site, with CEO attention.
      - **RunLayer:** Low priority due to a difficult contact and a poor renewal experience with a similar client (Interwell).

### Pricing & Negotiation Tactics

  - **Strategy:** Use the $15k price point to close deals quickly, especially competitive ones.
  - **Rationale:** Prioritize securing kickoffs to meet forecasts and build momentum. Upsells can be pursued later.
  - **Key Tactics:**
      - **Urgency:** Create a "fear of missing out" by stating only one slot is available.
      - **VC Funding:** Explain that VC funding allows us to offer premium talent at a lower price, which helps justify the value.
      - **Social Proof:** Name-drop high-profile prospects (e.g., "Vercel is in the process of signing") to build credibility.

### Leveraging Marcel & New Hire Andy

  - **Marcel's Role:**
      - **Cadence:** Book 4+ prospect calls on Marcel's calendar every Wednesday afternoon.
      - **Rationale:** Marcel's involvement accelerates deals, builds relationships, and validates the product roadmap by testing new messaging.
      - **Communication:** Use Slack channels for all prospects to enable faster, more direct communication.
  - **Andy's Role:**
      - **Background:** New hire with 7 years at Google Cloud negotiating major contracts.
      - **Function:** Assist Tyler with procurement and legal redlines to free up his time for sales activities.
      - **Systematization:** Help formalize our decision criteria for legal/procurement issues.

### Check That Product Strategy

  - **Goal:** Develop a Check That offering to capture leads not ready for a full sprint.
  - **Proposed Offering:** A $30k package for 18 months.
      - **Scope:** Includes the first two weeks of sprint work (strategy, context files, kickoff) but excludes the time-intensive editorial calibration.
      - **Rationale:** This creates a lower-effort, high-value entry point for prospects.
  - **Sales Ownership:** Marcel is the primary AE for Check That. Tyler will bring Marcel in for interested prospects.
