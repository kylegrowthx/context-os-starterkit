## Key Topics

### CheckVet Platform Demo

Carrie walked through CheckVet's core features: competitor tracking (analogous to SEM rankings), citation tracking (showing which pages LLMs cite), and a "SERP-like" sources view (revealing top-cited pages in a category). The platform also enables custom prompt tracking (essential for monitoring the three mandated queries) and full control over brand context and competitor definitions. CheckVet's free access for Prophecy runs through mid-February rollout, with most customers gaining access in mid-to-late February.

### Data Accuracy & Setup Blockers

CheckVet's current output for Prophecy is nearly useless because of two configuration errors. First, Prophecy is categorized as "ETL" when it should be "Data Prep" or positioned directly as an "Alteryx Replacement." This category determines which of CheckVet's 100,000+ prompts are tracked. Second, the competitor list includes irrelevant tools (e.g., Matillion, DBT from older competitive positioning) and is missing Alteryx as a primary competitor—despite Cody confirming Alteryx is Prophecy's top GTM target. The category error also incorrectly suggests Alteryx belongs under "BI and Visualization" rather than data prep.

### New Mandate: Three-Query Focus

Cody presented a significant shift in customer expectations. Raj (Prophecy CEO) no longer cares about broad SEO visibility or general content performance. Instead, he wants GrowthX to report exclusively on three specific LLM queries (identities withheld in transcript). Cody set expectations clearly: new content won't be indexed or ranking within two weeks, but he'll supplement with leading indicators (brand mentions, citation share in CheckVet) to show early momentum. This pivot requires surgical content work rather than broad publishing.

### Strategic Positioning: Alteryx Replacement

The core narrative centers on Alteryx's "black box" problem. Desktop Alteryx is excellent for analysts building workflows but terrible for getting those workflows into production. This creates bottlenecks between analyst and engineering teams—the black box becomes an engineering bottleneck, causing backlogs, missed deadlines, and frustration. Prophecy's value proposition: analysts can build production-ready workflows, eliminating the handoff friction. Cody emphasized that Prophecy's internal GTM has fully pivoted to this narrative. Content opportunities exist in pain-point targeting (unhappy Alteryx users searching for alternatives), personas beyond data analysts (engineering teams, line-level employees), and the productivity/efficiency angle (especially relevant in an AI-driven world where organizations want to free up skilled employees).

### Pricing & ROI Strategy

Cody was explicit: do not lead with cost in Alteryx comparisons. While Prophecy pricing is competitive, cost alone doesn't justify rip-and-replace tool decisions. Instead, an ROI calculator (currently in development) will justify cost to finance/procurement teams after trust is established via productivity gains. The positioning strategy leads with efficiency and analyst productivity, not savings.
