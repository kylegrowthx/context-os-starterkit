## Context

Spendesk is evaluating a partnership with GrowthX for content marketing (SEO/AEO focused). They're in Phase 1 (8-week discovery) and debating whether to commit to Phase 2. The partnership would cost $15k/month (discounted from $18k standard rate) with 5 articles/week guaranteed. Spendesk currently outsources strategic guidance to an external SEO/AEO consultant for 2 days/week ($15k/month spans roughly 20 articles at their ghostwriter rate) and worries that switching to GrowthX means losing that expert guidance unless GrowthX can explicitly quantify the human expertise included. They've also seen preliminary design work from GrowthX and found it generic—stock photos instead of topic-specific visuals. Critically, Spendesk operates in multiple languages and requires a fully integrated translation workflow from day one, which GrowthX cannot deliver during Phase 1 (they're still building the feature). This is the deal-breaker issue that led to the decision to loop in Marcel for final negotiation.

---

## Relevance

- **Buyer Psychology:** Exposes how enterprise buyers struggle with "deliverables-not-hours" SaaS-hybrid models when they've been burned by agencies—they want to see and quantify the human expertise they're paying for.
- **Product Positioning:** Surfaces a core tension in GrowthX's model: the team claims Account Managers drive weekly strategic reviews, but can't articulate how many hours or what "strategic guidance" looks like relative to what a dedicated AEO consultant provides.
- **Market Concern:** Translation is emerging as a non-negotiable feature for international companies. GrowthX views it as a future add-on; Spendesk sees it as essential day-one infrastructure.
- **Sales Lesson:** Ollie's repeated "I don't know what I'm buying without hours" signals a larger messaging problem. GrowthX needs to reframe value beyond just outputs—or accept that early-stage companies will always ask for resource commitments.
- **Design Gap:** Generic stock photos are table-stakes for $15k/month. Topic-specific visuals (graphs, data visualizations) aren't a luxury—they're expected from buyers who've been trained by premium agencies.

## Key Topics

### Spendesk's Core Concern: Resource Allocation & Expertise

  - Spendesk's primary hesitation is the lack of guaranteed human resource hours in the GrowthX contract.
  - **Rationale:**
      - **Past Experience:** Burned by agencies with misaligned incentives.
      - **Model Perception:** Views GrowthX as a "SaaS hybrid" with a strong tool component, raising questions about the depth of human expertise.
      - **Current Model:** Employs an external SEO/AEO consultant for 2 days/week of strategic guidance (briefing content gaps, performance analysis).
      - **Goal:** Avoid needing to hire another expert to fill a service gap if GrowthX's model doesn't provide sufficient strategic support.

### GrowthX's Model: Deliverables, Not Hours

  - GrowthX's model guarantees deliverables (5 articles/week) and quality, not specific hours.
  - **Rationale:**
      - **Quality Focus:** The team spends "as long as it takes" to achieve the required quality for each article.
      - **Resource Planning:** As an early-stage company, they rely on the 8-week Phase 1 to validate the partnership and cannot offer termination for convenience.
      - **Expertise:** Account Managers are AEO-experienced and lead weekly performance reviews.
      - **Proof Points:** Cited client results (e.g., Ramp's 3x traffic, Brex's annual upfront commitment) as evidence of their model's effectiveness.

### Contract & Scope Details

  - **Pricing:** Confirmed at $15k/month for Phase 2.
  - **Payment:** Quarterly in advance is acceptable.
  - **Add-ons:**
      - **Design:** Spendesk wants higher-quality, topic-specific images (graphs, data visualizations) instead of generic stock photos.
      - **Translations:** A non-negotiable requirement for a fully integrated workflow from day one.
      - **Process:** Additional scope (e.g., design hours) can be added via a contract amendment ("Annexa") after Phase 1 discovery.
