## Key Topics

### Sanity Workflow & Technical Issues

  - **Problem:** The current Sanity workflow is a major bottleneck, taking \~40 minutes to stage one article.
  - **Root Causes:**
      - **Slow Publishing:** A recent engineering update (last 45-60 days) caused pages to take longer to go live, preventing immediate post-publish review.
      - **Broken Previews:** Some page types cannot be previewed, leading to 404s when non-marketing teams publish content.
      - **UI Complexity:** The Sanity build is overly complex, with a persistent "drop cap bug" that frustrates daily use.
  - **Proposed Solution:** GrowthX's audit identified three key improvements to reduce staging time to \~5 minutes:
      - CTA library
      - Consolidated page types
      - Simplified main content area
  - **Action:** GrowthX will meet with Greg (Brex engineer focused on Core Web Vitals) to scope the fix. The fix will be applied first to "Spend Trends" pages, then to "Product" and "Platform" pages.

### RAMP Tool Development

  - **Project:** Build 5 new tools for RAMP, starting with the "Charge Finder."
  - **Timeline:** The first project requires a 4-week maximum build time due to initial setup. Subsequent tools will be faster.
  - **Dependencies:** The project is blocked pending access to:
      - Marketing site repo
      - Sanity (for new content types)
      - Merchant database (names, descriptions, location)
  - **Action:** Jon will connect GrowthX with Greg to grant access. A background check for GrowthX engineer Kirkland is required; the process should be quick as other team members have already completed it.

### Content Strategy & SEO

  - **Google Core Update Impact:**
      - The update hit older articles hard (e.g., "procurement policy"), with some dropping from page 1 to position 50.
      - **Key Finding:** Topical authority is now more critical.
      - **Action:** A keyword cannibalization analysis begins this week.
  - **Competitive Strategy:**
      - **Goal:** Aggressively target Ramp's top keywords, aiming to own "corporate card" by month-end.
      - **Tactic:** Create full articles from competitor "People Also Ask" (PAA) questions to outrank their FAQs.
          - **Rule:** If a topic can support \>1,200 words, it becomes an article. If not, it's an FAQ.
      - **Competitors:**
          - **Ramp:** Direct target.
          - **Mercury Business Banking:** Targeting their keywords.
          - **Expensify:** Noted for copying Brex content.
          - **Navan:** Emerging competitor in travel.
  - **Content Quality & "Information Gain":**
      - **Goal:** Create original, high-quality content that AI cannot easily replicate.
      - **Tactics:**
          - **Proprietary Data:** Incorporate 20-30 unique stats from Brex's data team.
          - **Unique Sources:** Use G2 reviews to find specific customer drawbacks.
          - **Customer Quotes:** Proactively gather quotes from top customers for articles.
      - **Internal Resource:** GrowthX created a searchable database of 52 Brex case studies to provide the team with quick access to stats, use cases, and quotes.
