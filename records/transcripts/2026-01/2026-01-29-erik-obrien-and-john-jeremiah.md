## Summary

Erik and John address the critical bottleneck in GrowthX's content review process: Brock is rewriting drafts instead of providing actionable feedback, preventing both the pipeline from learning and articles from publishing at velocity. They propose shifting to a Google Docs-based workflow with explicit feedback discipline, enforced by Brock's manager Ashish, and establishing a "revisions per article" metric to track improvement.

## Context

GrowthX is delivering B2B content marketing for FleetDM, but the engagement is stalled. FleetDM has recently hired a VP of Sales who is driving efficiency across pipeline, sales process, and marketing effectiveness. John Jeremiah, a founder with deep institutional knowledge, is pushing for operational rigor in how FleetDM's marketing team reviews and approves content. Currently, only 5 articles are live—far below target velocity. The problem: Brock, FleetDM's internal reviewer, rewrites articles instead of providing structured feedback, making it impossible for GrowthX to improve its content pipeline or for Gabby (the GrowthX editor) to understand what went wrong. This dynamic also creates perverse incentives: when Brock rewrites, Ashish (his manager) approval-by-rewrite becomes a shortcut that looks like progress but prevents learning. John is positioning himself as an ally to GrowthX, willing to enforce new discipline on FleetDM's side if the process can be fixed.

## Key Topics

### Problem: Ineffective Content Review Process

  - The current process is a bottleneck, with Brock rewriting drafts instead of providing specific feedback.
  - **Impact:**
      - **Stalled Publishing:** Only 5 articles are live, far below the target velocity.
      - **No Learning:** growthx.ai cannot improve its content pipeline without actionable feedback.
      - **Inefficient Editing:** Gabby (growthx.ai editor) wastes time interpreting Brock's changes instead of applying clear directions.
  - **Root Cause:** The GitHub PR workflow is ill-suited for the rapid, iterative feedback needed for content creation.

### Solution: New Workflow & Process Metrics

  - **New Workflow:**
      - **Drafting:** Initial drafts created in Google Docs.
      - **Review:** All feedback and revisions occur in the Google Doc.
      - **Publishing:** Approved drafts are converted to GitHub PRs by growthx.ai for final publication.
  - **Process Metric:** Track "revisions per article" to measure quality improvement.
      - **Rationale:** A decreasing average signals that growthx.ai's content pipeline is learning and producing higher-quality drafts from the start.
      - **Benchmark:** Erik noted that most clients achieve publication in 1–2 cycles with effective feedback.

### Context: FleetDM's Sales & Marketing Push

  - A new VP of Sales is driving a company-wide focus on pipeline and sales efficiency.
  - The previous "allow people to buy" model is shifting to a proactive, "help people succeed" approach.
  - **Tools:**
      - **Qualified:** Being implemented to gain website visitor visibility and enable agentic AI engagement.
      - **Muller Digital:** Hired for Google/LinkedIn ads and SEO; will be connected to growthx.ai.
