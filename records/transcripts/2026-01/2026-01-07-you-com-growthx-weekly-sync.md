## Key Topics

### Content Quality & Workflow Bottlenecks

  - Brooke's detailed edits on recent articles revealed workflow issues blocking publishing velocity.
  - **Primary Issues:**
      - Repetitive text between the TLDR and intro.
      - Technical inaccuracies about You.com's product capabilities.
      - Overly complex language for top-of-funnel content.
  - **Root Cause:** A disconnect between the Atlas pipeline's output and You.com's quality bar, compounded by a lack of clear guidelines for Brooke's review.

### Atlas Pipeline Enhancements

  - GrowthX will update its Atlas content pipeline to address the identified issues.
  - **Planned Updates:**
      - **Persona Simplification:** Reduce technical expectations for top-of-funnel personas → better match search intent.
      - **Repetition Check:** Add a pipeline stage to flag TLDR/intro repetition.
      - **Product Accuracy Check:** Add a stage to validate product claims against You.com's documentation.
  - **Clarification:** A human editor already reviews all content before delivery. The TLDR/intro repetition is a deliberate SEO tactic for LLM visibility, a trade-off for You.com to decide on.

### New Review Process & SME Integration

  - The new review process will differentiate between high-level content edits and final stylistic changes.
  - **New Workflow:**
    1.  Brooke focuses on high-level edits (accuracy, structure).
    2.  Articles are sent back to GrowthX for revision.
    3.  Brooke adds final stylistic touches before publishing.
  - **Rationale:** This ensures GrowthX's pipeline learns from feedback, preventing recurring errors.
  - **SME Integration:** For technical content, the goal is to codify SME knowledge into documentation *before* content creation. This enables GrowthX to build a custom pipeline for that content stream, automating accuracy checks and reducing the need for manual SME review.

### Q1 Strategy & Scaling

  - Justin Fink, You.com's new marketing lead, joined to align the partnership with 2026 goals and ensure content throughput is justified.
  - **Q1 Focus:**
      - **Prioritize Top-of-Funnel Content:** This stream requires less technical review → faster publishing velocity.
      - **Programmatic SEO:** Explore templated content (e.g., comparison pages) to scale output without scaling Brooke's workload.
  - **December Reporting:** Early signals are positive, with articles indexing naturally and generating clicks. Key metrics to track are LLM visibility (top-answer position) and citations.
