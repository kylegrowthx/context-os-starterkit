## Summary

Marcel Santilli and Tyler Pavlas from GrowthX met with Andrew Bennett (CMO, Elementum) to finalize a content partnership. Elementum is a mature agent orchestration platform generating $30M annually from a single enterprise customer, but faces a GTM gap with only one marketer on staff. GrowthX will act as a de facto product marketing team, beginning with context engineering (stakeholder interviews) and scaling to a full content engine designed to build brand authority and arm sales rather than generate inbound leads.

---

## Context

Elementum has achieved remarkable revenue scale ($30M/yr) through a single customer relationship that began as a $200k pilot and expanded to $17M/yr in just under two years, with a verbal commitment to $30M/yr. However, this success has masked a critical go-to-market immaturity: the company is replacing legacy enterprise systems (Coupa, ServiceNow) at the largest customers but lacks foundational marketing assets, messaging frameworks, battle cards, and a functional website. Andrew Bennett arrived as CMO only a month prior to this meeting, inheriting a single Snowflake partnership manager and three vacant leadership roles (Head of Product Marketing, Events, Campaigns). GrowthX's engagement is intended to clone Elementum's CEO's expertise into systematic, AI-native content that positions the company for a "lumpy" sales motion targeting 50 accounts at $20M+ each. This is not a performance marketing play; it is infrastructure for executive mindshare and sales enablement.

## Key Topics

### Elementum's GTM Imbalance

- **Product Maturity:** The agent orchestration platform is mature, with a 2-year-old product in-market.
- **Revenue Scale:** One client grew from a $200k pilot to a $17M/yr contract, now at a verbal $30M/yr. This client is replacing Coupa and possibly ServiceNow with Elementum.
- **Sales Strategy:** A high-end, "lumpy" motion targeting 50 accounts at $20M+ each.
  - **Entry Point:** Small use cases ($100k–$200k) with rapid deployment (6 weeks).
  - **Upsell:** Massive expansion driven by high business value (e.g., one use case saved a client €23M/yr).
- **GTM Immaturity:** The marketing team is one partner manager.
  - **Vacant Roles:** Head of Product Marketing, Events, and Campaigns.
  - **Missing Assets:** No messaging framework, battle cards, case studies, or effective website.

### Content Strategy & Differentiators

- **CIO Persona Focus:**
  - **Pain Point:** "Agents gone wild"—CIOs lack control over fragmented AI tools.
  - **Solution:** A platform for centralized agent control and predictable outcomes.
- **CDO Persona Focus:**
  - **Pain Point:** Low ROI on data investments (e.g., Snowflake) used only for dashboards.
  - **Solution:** Enable action against data, delivering a 10x ROI by automating processes (e.g., claims processing).
- **Core Differentiator:**
  - **Problem:** Probabilistic LLMs are unsuitable for auditable enterprise processes.
  - **Solution:** Elementum combines probabilistic agents with deterministic workflows, ensuring consistent, auditable results.

### GrowthX's Proposed Approach

- **Phase 1: Context Engineering:**
  - **Goal:** Build a deep understanding of Elementum's product, audience, and market.
  - **Method:** Conduct 1-hour interviews with key stakeholders (CEO, reps) to clone the CEO's expertise.
- **Phase 2: Content Engine Build:**
  - **Goal:** Systematically create content that builds authority and arms the sales team.
  - **Method:** Map audience questions to content opportunities, starting with foundational educational pieces.
  - **Execution:** Use an AI-native approach to scale content, potentially with interactive widgets.
