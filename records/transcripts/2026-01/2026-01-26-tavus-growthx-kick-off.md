## Key Topics

### The Problem: Outdated Content & Market Confusion

  - Tavus's long history (since 2020) has created a large volume of outdated content that misrepresents the current product.
  - **Impact:** LLMs cite these old descriptions (e.g., "static video gen"), causing confusion, damaging brand perception, and cannibalizing discoverability.
  - **Urgency:** This "bleeding" must be stopped immediately via content refreshes, which Tavus can execute if GrowthX provides a clear plan.

### The Solution: A Focused Enterprise Content Strategy

  - **Strategic Shift:** The sprint will focus exclusively on the B2B API (Conversational Video Interface, or CBI) business.
      - **Rationale:** The consumer PALs product is still in its infancy with an undefined ICP, making it a poor target for web acquisition.
  - **Core Goal:** Build a programmatic library of prescriptive use cases.
      - **Inspiration:** Webflow University's early model, which educated users on components and indexed well for SEO.
      - **Purpose:** Teach the market how to think about and apply Tavus's new technology.

### The Product: CBI API & Competitive Edge

  - **Product:** CBI is a real-time, low-latency API for building custom AI human experiences.
      - **Positioning:** Aims to be the "11 Labs for video agents."
  - **Competitive Differentiators:**
      - **Wins:** Superior video quality and a faster, proprietary knowledge base (RAD model) for more organic conversations.
      - **Losses:** Price, or competitors (e.g., Anom) offering custom replica creation as a standard feature.
  - **Key Insight:** Tavus's custom replica creation is a manual, ad-hoc process. Making it a standard feature is a high-priority engineering request to better compete.

### The Customer: Enterprise ICP & Acquisition

  - **Primary ICP:** Product, AI, and Innovation leads (CPO down to Head of Product).
  - **Acquisition Channels:**
      - Website visitors with high intent.
      - "Signal chasing" (e.g., LinkedIn followers, website visits) to feed a robust outbound motion.
      - LinkedIn remarketing with niche, prescriptive use cases.
  - **High-Potential Verticals:**
      - Learning & Development (coaching, role-playing)
      - Health Tech (patient intake, comms)
      - Insurance
      - Talent Acquisition
      - Customer Support
      - Sales Enablement

### The Process: GrowthX 8-Week Sprint

  - **Goal:** Build a scalable, high-quality content system using a human-in-the-loop AI pipeline.
  - **Key Phases:**
      - **Weeks 1–2 (Discovery):** Deep dives, artifact creation (ICP, tone), and CMS process review.
      - **Week 2 (Strategy):** Present topic clusters and a calibration article to align on tone and quality.
      - **Weeks 3–8 (Production):** Ramp to 5 articles/week (\~18–20 total), leveraging GrowthX's AI search analytics.
