## Key Topics

### Business Model & Customer Needs
- **All revenue is services-based:** GrowthX sells repeatable, AI-powered services, not DIY platform access
- **Customer preference is clear:** Clients want visibility, analytics, and a command center; none are asking for DIY self-service capability
- **Platform exists to serve delivery:** Atlas/OS is a tool for GrowthX teams to deliver the service faster and more consistently
- **Kickoff economics:** 8-week cycle; 70-75% conversion to 12-month contract; treated as extended sales process with payment upfront

### Growth Mechanics & Constraints
- **Kickoff capacity is the primary constraint:** Current 2/week = ~88/year at 75% conversion = $13M → $26M ARR (2x)
- **Escalating resource requirements:** Moving to 3 kickoffs/week requires new sprint team hiring (4 FTEs per kickoff), pod hiring (EM + editorial), and ramp time (2 months minimum)
- **Conversion rate is capped:** 75% → 80% by Q1 is achievable; even 100% conversion is incremental, not exponential
- **ACV is not a lever:** Already high; retention isn't strong enough to justify raising prices further
- **Sales pipeline must scale in parallel:** 33% win rate + 3:1 coverage ratio + 10-12 week cycle means demand creation must start 3+ months before capacity needs it

### Check That's Market Position
- **Nearly ready to launch:** Feature-complete; finalizing sign-up, onboarding, and Stripe integration; self-serve monetization goes live next week
- **Strong early traction:** ~30K daily impressions already; organic growth validating demand for AI visibility data
- **Product-market validation strategy:** Validate organic growth first, then self-serve monetization, then add sales-assist motion
- **Break-even bar is low:** Only needs 2 customers/month to pay for itself; even conservative estimates show payback within months
- **Replaces vendor dependency:** Reduces $11K/month Scrunch spend; gives GrowthX proprietary data asset
- **Cross-sell flywheel:** Can identify high-value brands and funnel them into service sales; low risk of brand confusion

### Brand & Portfolio Strategy
- **Separation principle:** Keep Check That distinct from GrowthX to prevent cannibalization risk (e.g., $200K customer seeing $200 alternative)
- **Multi-bet architecture:** Like Amazon (AWS vs. Marketplace vs. Prime Video), separate brands allow independent risk-taking and scaling
- **Alternative floated:** Carilu proposed "X family" branding (Check That X, OS X) for visual/conceptual unity
- **Marcel's decision:** Stick with separation for now; can cross-promote once both products are validated and independent
- **OS strategy:** Externalized Atlas; still building out editorial and assignments; goal is increased stickiness and delivery scalability

### Organizational & Talent Planning
- **AE expansion critical:** Scaling from 1 AE to 9 AEs this year to support Check That's sales-assist motion
- **Team visibility is siloed:** Finance (Marcel, Bridget, Daniel only); delivery (sprint + pod teams); product (separate); sales (separate). No one has full picture except Marcel
- **Carilu's role as CMO:** Must understand macro picture but also deeply understand sales and delivery mechanics before scaling demand
- **Carilu's insertion strategy:** Focus on pipeline, top-line impact, value selling, messaging, pricing; avoid isolated demand gen without delivery context

### Announcement & PR Calendar

- **HubSpot Ventures:** Early February announcement (HubSpot invested in GrowthX; also acquired X Funnel team to advise on launch)
