## Key Topics

**The Core Problem**
- Brex's page count (~1,200) is the primary growth bottleneck, not content quality
- Manual review process creates a severe ceiling on output volume
- Competitors operate at 10-200x scale: Wise (243k), Canva (43k), fintool.com (11k built rapidly)
- Growth math: to double traffic, Brex needs to at least triple page count to ~3,600, accounting for lower effectiveness of new pages

**The Solution: "Index of Indexes"**
- A programmatic content strategy that bypasses the manual review bottleneck
- Three-tier architecture:
  - **Main Index**: Grid of cards representing different index categories
  - **Sub-Index Page**: Detailed page with title, headline, explanation, and filterable table of entries
  - **Profile Page**: Rich profile for each entry with structured data (stage, location, team size) and cross-links to other indexes
- Hub-and-spoke tagging: every page tagged across multiple hubs (Role, Topic, Tool) to create interconnected knowledge graph
- Examples: Fortune 500 CFOs index, People Frustrated with CRM, etc.
- Inspiration: Kaggle's index structure, which generates massive organic traffic

**Execution Strategy**
- Start with 3-4 index clusters (learning the mechanics and content sourcing)
- Test and iterate over 2-3 weeks
- Scale the 1-2 highest-performing clusters
- Kill underperforming ones; de-risks the strategy
- Proof of concept: Check That generated significant traffic for a site with zero authority, demonstrating the model works at scale

**Prototype & Presentation Plan**
- George will build a clickable prototype this weekend + Monday morning before the Tuesday meeting
- Marcel will review and provide feedback
- Prototype should showcase: main index (card grid), one detailed index page, and one profile page
- Tuesday client meeting to present strategy, prototype, and competitive analysis
- Pitch framing: "Here's the math on your growth constraint. Here's the precedent we've proven. Here's how we de-risk execution."
