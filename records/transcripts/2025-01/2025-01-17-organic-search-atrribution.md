## Overview

- HubSpot CRM should be the primary source of truth for attribution, with GA4 used as a supplementary tool for trends and engagement metrics
- Implement event tracking based on form submission success rather than button clicks for more accurate conversion data
- Use a free-form field in forms asking "How did you hear about us?" to gather qualitative attribution insights
- Consider channel-specific attribution models (e.g., first-touch for blogs) rather than a one-size-fits-all approach

## Key Topics

### GA4 Limitations and Alternative Attribution Approaches

  - GA4 rollout has been problematic, with issues in data accuracy and channel attribution
  - Increasing prevalence of cookie blocking further complicates GA4 reliability
  - Recommendation: Use CRM (e.g., HubSpot) as the primary source of truth for attribution
  - Align segmentation and data collection with CRM to ensure consistency with sales team and executive reporting

### Conversion Tracking Best Practices

  - Current method: Button click events in GA4, showing discrepancies (e.g., 2 leads from SEO, but 0 shown in filtered view)
  - Recommended approach: Track form submission success events rather than button clicks
  - Options for implementation:
    1.  Use a thank you page (easier but may overcount if page is refreshed)
    2.  Fire event on form submission success (technically preferred but may require more dev support)
  - Utilize Google Tag Manager for easier implementation of tracking events

### Multi-Channel Attribution Strategies

  - No perfect attribution model exists; awareness of limitations is key
  - For Yourco's current scale: Use a simplified approach combining HubSpot and GA data in a master spreadsheet
  - As channels increase: Consider more sophisticated multi-touch attribution models (e.g., first-touch, last-touch, or split)
  - Channel-specific considerations:
      - Blogs: Consider first-touch or any-touch attribution to capture top-of-funnel impact
      - Evaluate content effectiveness by looking at its role in the overall customer journey

### Keyword and Content Performance Tracking

  - Challenge: Google's encryption of keyword data limits direct keyword tracking
  - Solution: Focus on page-level and topical analysis
    1.  Create a content inventory (e.g., in Airtable) with topical labeling
    2.  Cross-reference leads/conversions with touched pages
    3.  Use lookups and pivots to identify top-performing topics and content

### Qualitative Attribution Insights

  - Implement a free-form field in forms asking "How did you hear about us?"
  - Benefits:
    1.  Provides more detailed, qualitative insights into attribution
    2.  Avoids bias introduced by pick lists or randomized options
  - Challenge: Requires manual processing or potential AI-powered analysis to synthesize responses
