## Key Topics

### Content Organization and Strategy

  - Request to group blogs into relevant topic clusters (e.g., CRM best practices, business development)
  - Aim to identify content gaps and opportunities within the 7-8 main topic areas
  - Balance between construction-specific content and broader CRM topics to improve SEO performance
  - Preference to focus on buyer-centric content despite low search volume in the niche construction CRM market

### SEO and Keyword Analysis

  - Challenge: Competing with general CRM companies while specializing in construction
  - Low search volume for construction-specific CRM keywords limits impressions and clicks
  - Strategy: Focus on construction terms while selectively targeting broader CRM topics relevant to ProjectMark's offerings
  - Proposal: 60% construction-focused articles, 40% generic CRM articles to expand reach

### Content Performance and Optimization

  - Two blogs showing promise: "Construction Marketing" and "Benefits of Construction CRM for Architecture and Engineering"
  - Early-stage metrics: 2 clicks on these specific topics
  - Plan to optimize and expand on successful topics like construction marketing
  - Opportunity identified to target "construction marketing" keywords, potentially less competitive than "construction CRM"

### LinkedIn Content Strategy

  - Proposal to repurpose blog content for LinkedIn articles and thought leadership posts
  - Suggestion to create 30-60 second video summaries of blog content for LinkedIn engagement
  - Recommendation to use automation for LinkedIn connection requests (100/week) to build network in the industry

### Lead Generation and Tracking

  - Introduction to Clay tool for identifying blog visitors and qualifying leads
  - Capability to automate lead scoring and outreach based on ICP (Ideal Customer Profile) fit
  - Potential to set up automated email sequences for qualified leads visiting the blog
