## Key Topics

### Current Team Structure and Challenges

  - Mix of account leads and supporting roles, causing management complexity
  - Varying skill levels in client communication, strategy, and content creation
  - Need for more experienced client-facing managers to handle higher-tier accounts
  - Challenges in balancing workload and matching team members to appropriate accounts

### Proposed Team Structure Changes

  - Create tiered system for accounts based on complexity and revenue
  - Develop "pods" with 3-4 team members (account manager, support roles, writers)
  - Transition strong performers like Andre and Nika into more client-facing roles
  - Reassign accounts to better match team member strengths (e.g., technical knowledge)
  - Implement bi-weekly call cadence for lower-tier accounts to manage workload

### Individual Team Member Assessments

  - Andre: Improving but needs coaching on client communication and strategy
  - Nika: Strong potential for client-facing role, especially for technical accounts
  - Bissera: Excellent content skills, needs development in people management
  - Sammy: Performing well on some accounts, needs improvement in stakeholder management
  - Critique: Better suited for supporting role, working behind the scenes

### Account-Specific Discussions

  - Reddit: High-priority account requiring top talent and editorial quality
  - Project Mark: Challenges with narrow focus, need to push for broader content strategy
  - Metadata: Opportunity to develop refresh strategy for mature content accounts
  - WebStacks: Potential churn risk, requires strategic oversight

### Scaling and Future Growth

  - Goal to close 2 new accounts per week
  - Need to develop clear processes for onboarding new team members and accounts
  - Focus on creating uniform experiences for different account tiers
  - Importance of providing growth paths for team members to retain talent
