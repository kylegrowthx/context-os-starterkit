## Overview

- Agreement to move towards a platform fee model with usage costs at or near cost, reducing linear scaling of expenses
- Identified key areas for platform improvement: white labeling, cross-workspace asset sharing, parallelization, and permissioning
- Commitment to sign a short-term agreement (3 months) with plans to revisit for a longer-term commitment in February after progress on identified improvements

---

## Key Topics

### Pricing Model and Cost Optimization

  - Shifting from task-based pricing to a platform fee model with usage costs at or near cost
  - GXL to use their own API keys for LLMs to reduce costs
  - AirOps offering SEMrush API access at their cost, with potential for improved rates as they scale
  - Exploring alternatives to expensive API calls (e.g., SEMrush content gap analysis) to optimize costs

### Platform Enhancements

  - White labeling: Custom branding, removing AirOps branding, pre-configured workspaces for customers
  - Cross-workspace functionality: Sharing workflows, apps, and assets across workspaces
  - Improved permissioning: Granular access control for team members and clients
  - Parallelization: Addressing bottlenecks in workflow execution, particularly for iterative tasks

### Scalability Concerns

  - Current linear task execution causing slowdowns (e.g., 10-20 minute article generation times)
  - Need for improved concurrency and parallelization within workflows
  - Desire for more efficient handling of batch jobs and high-volume content production

### Partnership Alignment

  - AirOps emphasizing their shift towards helping GXL optimize and scale, rather than charging based on usage
  - GXL seeking transparency on cost structures to make informed build vs. buy decisions
  - Both parties interested in finding mutually beneficial scaling models

### Feature Requests and Roadmap

  - Prioritized list of improvements:
    1.  Parallelization/speed improvements
    2.  Cross-workspace asset sharing
    3.  Permissioning enhancements
    4.  White labeling options
    5.  Usage/billing transparency
  - Agreement to map these priorities to AirOps' planning and provide timelines
