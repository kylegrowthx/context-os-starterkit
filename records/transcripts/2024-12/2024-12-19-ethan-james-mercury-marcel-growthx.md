## Overview

- GrowthX is transitioning from an LLC to a C-Corp structure, seeking a more responsive banking partner for their rapid growth (4M ARR in 7 months)
- Mercury offers consolidated banking, treasury management (4.1-4.6% yield), and flexible credit limits based on deposits

## Key Topics

### Current Banking Situation and Pain Points

  - Using Chase (private client) and Ramp for business banking
  - Issues with Chase: unresponsive, slow credit limit increases, declined transactions
  - Ramp: limited credit increases despite strong financials
  - Need for better visibility, simplified operations, and technology-driven banking solutions

### Business Growth and Structure

  - Transitioning from LLC to C-Corp in early 2024
  - 4 million in ARR expected by January, bootstrapped and profitable
  - Planning to raise $10-15 million in January, potentially through a SAFE

### Mercury's Banking Solutions

  - Treasury product: No lock-up period, 4.1-4.6% yield through Vanguard or Morgan Stanley
  - Credit limits: \~25% of total deposits, including treasury funds
  - Account opening: Quick 10-15 minute application, same-day setup possible
  - "Day zero" credit card access for immediate operational needs
  - Sub-accounts with auto-transfer rules for organized fund management
  - SAFE agreement generation and execution tools
  - Integrations with cap table management tools like Carta

### Card Management and Expense Tracking

  - Virtual and physical cards with customizable limits and category locks
  - Subscription tracking and management
  - Mobile receipt capture integrated with QuickBooks

### Bill Pay and Vendor Management

  - OCR-enabled invoice inbox for streamlined bill payments
  - Lacks comprehensive bill pay with built-in W-9 collection and 1099 issuance
  - Integration with QuickBooks for transaction categorization and accounting
