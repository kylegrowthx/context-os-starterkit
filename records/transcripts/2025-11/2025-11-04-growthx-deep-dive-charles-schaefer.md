## Overview

- **Hex is redefining BI with an AI-native workflow.** It replaces the slow request-queue cycle with instant, trusted answers via natural language, enabling business users to self-serve and freeing data teams for advanced work.
- **The platform unifies the full data lifecycle:** natural language queries (`Threads`), advanced data science notebooks, and shareable low-code apps. This eliminates the need for multiple, disconnected tools.
- **Trust is built on transparency.** The AI's reasoning is fully auditable, showing all data sources, queries, and logic. This is enabled by customer-defined semantic models that provide essential business context.
- **The brand is modern and irreverent,** challenging the status quo of legacy BI tools. Content must be high-quality and align with a specific "internet-brained" tone set by the CEO.

## Key Topics

### The Problem: Legacy BI's Broken Workflow

  - Legacy BI tools (Tableau, Looker) create a slow, frustrating request-queue cycle for business users.
  - This forces data teams to spend time on one-off questions instead of high-impact projects.
  - The root cause is a fragmented toolchain (querying, science, reporting) that prevents a unified, AI-native experience.

### The Solution: Hex's Unified, AI-Native Platform

  - Hex solves this by unifying the data workflow in one platform with integrated AI.
  - **1. Threads (Self-Service):**
      - Natural language interface for business users to ask questions.
      - AI generates detailed reports with tables, charts, and explanations.
      - **Trust Mechanism:** The AI's reasoning is auditable, showing all sources (projects, semantic models, queries).
      - **Efficiency:** The AI first searches for existing answers to prevent duplicate work.
  - **2. Notebooks (Deep Data Work):**
      - `Threads` analysis can be instantly transferred to a notebook for deep data science.
      - The AI agent in notebooks can generate complex code (Python, SQL) for tasks like predictive modeling.
      - This accelerates expert workflows by providing a high-quality first draft.
  - **3. Apps (Shareable Insights):**
      - Notebooks can be published as low-code apps for easy distribution to non-technical users via a shareable link.

### The Technology: How Trust is Built

  - Hex uses Claude Sonnet (v4.5) but ensures accuracy by providing it with rich, customer-defined context.
  - **Semantic Models:** Define business logic for the AI:
      - Metric definitions (e.g., "Closed Won Conversion Rate").
      - Dimension descriptions (e.g., "UUX\_traffic\_ID7" → "Unique Website Visitors").
      - Table joins and relationships.
  - **Rules Files:** Enforce organizational standards (e.g., "for revenue questions, use this table").
  - **Endorsed Content:** Data teams can mark projects or tables as authoritative sources for the AI to prioritize.

### Market Positioning & Brand

  - **Competitive Landscape:**
      - **Legacy BI (Tableau, Looker):** Hex is now a direct competitor, offering a fundamentally different, AI-native approach.
      - **Modern BI (Omni, Sigma):** Hex's AI capabilities and advanced analytics (Python support) are key differentiators.
  - **Brand Identity:**
      - **Tone:** Modern, innovative, irreverent, and "internet-brained."
      - **Positioning:** Challenges the status quo of legacy BI, framing it as an outdated process.
  - **Future Vision:**
      - **Chat with your App:** Ask questions directly within a published app.
      - **Ambient Analytics:** Proactive, scheduled analysis (e.g., a weekly report on top reps).
      - **Agentic Analytics:** The AI agent will become even more powerful and autonomous.

### Content Strategy

  - **Quality Mandate:** All content must be high-quality and align with the CEO's specific tone.
      - **Video:** Commercial-grade production, not simple screen recordings.
      - **Written:** Irreverent, quirky, and "internet-brained" style.
  - **"Show, Don't Tell" Strategy:**
      - Create short vignettes showing the "old way" (request-queue) vs. the "new way" (instant AI answer).
      - Target specific user roles (e.g., growth marketer, sales leader).
  - **Content Placement:** Open to ideas for housing high-volume, top-of-funnel content (e.g., a separate section, SEO-only).
