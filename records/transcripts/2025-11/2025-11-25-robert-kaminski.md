## Summary

Jason and Robert explored a strategic partnership between GrowthX and FletchPMM, two agencies facing identical scaling challenges: both are at capacity, limited by founder time and non-scalable onboarding. They agreed to collaborate on a joint webinar where FletchPMM will critique GrowthX's client positioning documents to demonstrate the FletchPMM process, serving as a low-risk brand-building play and potential training validation for FletchPMM's plan to certify freelance "shadow product marketers." Jason also proposed a "positioning controller" service—monitoring how AI answers product-related questions—as a recurring revenue opportunity for FletchPMM, though Robert is skeptical about measurement feasibility but intrigued by the concept.

---

## Context

Robert Kaminski is a founder of FletchPMM, a high-margin, project-based positioning agency based in Austin with ~$2M annual revenue. Jason had initially contacted FletchPMM a year ago about a potential project, but the deal fell through. They're reconnecting now as Jason leads demand generation at GrowthX, where he recently spoke with Robert about exploring partnership opportunities.

FletchPMM's core business is a fixed-scope "positioning sprint" — a strategic service that helps B2B founders and marketing leaders define and sharpen their positioning. The business has strong demand (booked into February with zero outbound marketing) but is severely supply-constrained because positioning work requires founder credibility, and Robert and his co-founder Anthony have run hundreds of sprints themselves. They're now thinking about how to scale: either by hiring full-time product marketers or certifying freelancers as "shadow product marketers" who work under the FletchPMM model. Both Robert and Jason face nearly identical scaling bottlenecks despite very different business models — Jason runs a recurring-revenue, productized content agency while Robert runs a project-based positioning firm.

## Key Topics

### FletchPMM's Scaling Challenge

  - **Business Model:** Project-based agency focused on a single, high-margin positioning sprint.
  - **Revenue:** \~$2M annually.
  - **Growth Strategy:** Scale the core service to $5M before launching new initiatives (e.g., software).
  - **Primary Bottleneck:** Supply-side capacity, not demand.
      - The business is built on founder credibility, making it difficult to delegate.
      - Booked into February with zero outbound marketing.
  - **Proposed Solution:** A training course to certify freelancers in the FletchPMM process.
      - **Goal:** Build a "shadow product marketer" network (\~20 people) to increase capacity and run upsell projects.
      - **Dilemma:** A course would serve product marketers, not FletchPMM's core founder/DMO customer.

### GrowthX.ai's Productized Agency Model

  - **Business Model:** Recurring-revenue agency using a proprietary platform to deliver content at scale.
  - **Revenue:** \~$13M annually.
  - **Scaling Bottleneck:** Onboarding is a high-touch, non-scalable process (2–4 weeks/client).
  - **Community as a Funnel:** A separate community (newsletter, Circle) drives leads and serves as a pipeline for future software users.
      - **Members:** \~170 paying $30/mo.
      - **Lead Gen:** Webinars convert \~10% of attendees into qualified leads.

### Proposed Partnership & Recurring Revenue

  - **Shared Need:** Both agencies need to train talent to scale.
  - **Recurring Revenue Idea:** Jason proposed a "positioning controller" service for FletchPMM.
      - **Concept:** Continuously monitor how AI (e.g., ChatGPT) answers product-related questions.
      - **Goal:** Provide recurring insights on market perception and competitor framing.
      - **FletchPMM's Response:** Intrigued but skeptical of the scope creep and difficulty of quantitative measurement.
  - **Agreed Partnership:** A joint webinar.
      - **Format:** FletchPMM will "grill" GrowthX's client positioning documents, demonstrating their process.
      - **Rationale:** A low-risk way to validate FletchPMM's training concept and generate brand equity.
