## Key Topics

### Candidate Background & Experience

  - **Reel & Limited (Managing Editor):**
      - Built a content production pipeline from scratch to meet 24–48 hour delivery SLAs for white-label agencies and direct clients.
      - Managed the full content lifecycle: client strategy, writer assignments, and final QA.
      - Created detailed checklists to ensure high-quality output from a distributed team.
  - **Remote (Marketing Projects Producer):**
      - Managed end-to-end campaign production, including gated/ungated content and industry reports.
      - Facilitated the company's project management tool migration from Asana to Monday.
      - Developed a new workflow for campaigns, from idea submission (Notion) to production using enterprise GPT.

### Content Quality & AI Workflow

  - **Quality Judgment:**
      - Content must meet a measurable business goal (e.g., conversions, traffic).
      - Requires a balance of factual accuracy, audience engagement, and technical SEO optimization.
      - Uses a checklist covering SEO (SERP analysis, keywords), writing (flow, transitions), and brand alignment.
  - **AI Workflow Optimization:**
      - **Problem:** Initial AI outputs were factually inaccurate and lacked product-specific details.
      - **Solution:** Developed a prompt engineering system to train enterprise GPT for accuracy.
          - **1. Role Architecture:** Define the AI's persona.
          - **2. Context:** Provide specific product info, campaign data, and value propositions.
          - **3. Instruction:** Give clear, detailed tasks.
      - **Tools:** Uses enterprise GPT, Notion for prompt libraries, and Prompt Genie for refinement.

### GrowthX Role & Collaboration

  - **Collaboration with Engineering:**
      - **Visibility:** Open Slack channels and Linear provide transparency into EPD team work.
      - **Direct Interaction:** Editors tag engineers in shared channels or file tickets to request pipeline improvements.
  - **Role Expectations & Success Metrics:**
      - **Editorial Freedom:** Editors are key strategic partners, not just executors. They drive outcomes and can challenge content that won't meet client goals.
      - **Success Metrics:** Measured by increasing efficiency and leverage over time.
          - **Example:** Reducing the time to produce 5 articles for a high-scrutiny client from one week to one day.
      - **Leverage:** Frees up time for proactive work, like suggesting new content streams (e.g., a glossary) and driving account expansion.
      - **Efficiency Drivers:** Success depends on improving three areas:
          - **1. Organization:** Internal process management.
          - **2. Inputs:** Providing crisp, defined context and instructions to the pipeline.
          - **3. Tooling:** Identifying and fixing pipeline inefficiencies.
