## Summary

Hex and GrowthX reviewed calibration drafts for an AI Analytics article, deciding to blend conversational style with conciseness and reframe the competitor comparison table to highlight AI capabilities and limitations. The team confirmed a strategic pivot toward AI-forward content (context engineering, prompt engineering) while deprioritizing BI-focused articles until Hex's Context Studio launches in mid-January, providing clearer positioning and preventing costly rework on currently published content. ## Key Topics

### Content Calibration Draft Review

  - **Draft Versions:**
      - `Tone 1`: Strict adherence to Notion writing guidelines → felt "dry" and "start-and-stop."
      - `Tone 2`: More conversational, inspired by the Hex blog → felt "dense."
  - **Tone & Style:**
      - **Decision:** Combine the conversational style of `Tone 2` with the conciseness of `Tone 1`.
      - **Action:** Add more color and "show-not-tell" examples (e.g., quote a sample AI answer) to make technical concepts more engaging.
  - **AI Narrative:**
      - **Problem:** The draft’s "no SQL" framing is too binary and restrictive.
      - **Solution:** The narrative must be nuanced, acknowledging an iterative workflow where AI generates SQL and charts, and technical users can still read and interpret the underlying code.
      - **Rationale:** This approach supports Hex's broad user base, from non-technical self-analysts to accelerating data scientists, without alienating either group.
      - **Action:** Add this nuanced AI philosophy to the writing guidelines.
  - **Competitor Comparison Table:**
      - **Problem:** The table is ineffective because it doesn't clearly differentiate tools based on their AI capabilities, which is the article's core topic.
      - **Action:** Reframe the table to highlight each platform's specific AI approach, maturity, and limitations.

### Content Calendar Strategy

  - **AI & Context Engineering:**
      - **Priority:** Increase the number of topics focused on "context engineering" and other AI analytics keywords.
      - **Rationale:** This reinforces Hex's leadership in the AI analytics space.
  - **BI Content Strategy:**
      - **Problem:** Hex's BI positioning is still evolving (e.g., "redefine BI" vs. "replace BI").
      - **Decision:** De-prioritize BI-focused articles until mid-January.
      - **Rationale:** The "Context Studio" launch in January will provide a stronger, more defined narrative, preventing the need for costly rework on articles published now.
      - **Action:** Use a few BI-leaning drafts as internal "stress tests" to help define the positioning, clearly marking them as non-publishable drafts.
  - **Competitor Article Titles:**
      - **Problem:** AI-generated titles (e.g., "unified analytics") don't always reflect Hex's current positioning.
      - **Solution:** Titles are a starting point; GrowthX will revise them to align with Hex's current strategic focus on AI.

### Website Placement for SEO Content

  - **Decision:** SEO content will be indexed by Google but not featured on the main blog homepage.
  - **Rationale:** This keeps the homepage focused on editorial content while allowing SEO articles to live under a "Further Reading" tag.
  - **Action:** Confirm with Adam that this technical setup is feasible.
