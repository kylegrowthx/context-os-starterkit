## Key Discussion Points

### Community Leadership Hiring Strategy

The team agreed on prioritizing candidates who deeply understand and care about the community's core audience over those focused solely on tactics or typical community roles. Marcel Santilli emphasized hiring someone with a strong passion for education and resourcefulness, citing Natalie, a former hire with no tech background but exceptional grit and drive, as an ideal profile.

Natalie's experience running a forum on the same platform and managing monthly meetups demonstrated her ability to learn quickly and engage stakeholders. Marcel argued for either an education-focused leader or someone willing to outwork everyone while being curious about AI. This profile fits the business need for someone who can connect deeply with members, not just execute standard community tactics. The expected impact is higher engagement and authentic community growth by focusing on people rather than events.

Jason Gong highlighted the need to focus on understanding the community's essence and its members instead of generic event management, suggesting the community operates more like a product now. He noted that most people label community roles as event organizers, but the team needs someone who can think about member experience and long-term growth. Jason favored candidates who grasp the community's vision and can help grow membership beyond just content delivery.

### Community Vision and Role Definition

The group clarified that the community leader role must be specific to their unique AI education and engagement goals, not a generic community manager position. Jason Gong criticized a recent community plan for being too generic and not tailored to their specific audience, emphasizing the need for bespoke strategies. Marcel agreed, saying previous proposals lacked in-depth understanding of their community and goals. This specificity enables better alignment of leadership and tactics with the community's mission.

Marcel described the importance of a leader who can curate and connect people internally and externally, not just organize events. Natalie's story illustrated the value of internal relationship-building with OpenAI and other stakeholders to drive event success and community momentum. The leader should be able to curate a "tasty menu" of content and experiences that engage members meaningfully. This approach supports the vision of a community product that grows through rich, relevant member experiences.

### Content and Growth Coordination

The team recognized that content creation and community growth require distinct but complementary roles. Jason Gong explained that Danny will focus on creating course content, while the community leader should focus on growth and engagement strategies. This division ensures content quality while maintaining a strategic focus on expanding membership. Jason stressed the need for someone who can answer "how do we grow to 1,000 members?" rather than just producing courses. The community leader should provide directional input on the roadmap, with content creators executing the materials.

Marcel proposed possibly bringing in a hands-on marketing leader to complement the community lead and content roles. He mentioned Casey as a potential marketing leader who is well connected and passionate but might prefer a less intense role. This could help accelerate growth through marketing expertise while maintaining community authenticity. The team is weighing cost and fit considerations for this additional role.
