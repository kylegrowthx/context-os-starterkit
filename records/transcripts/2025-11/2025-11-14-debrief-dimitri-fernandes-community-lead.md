### Community Leadership Hiring Strategy

Marcel Santilli emphasized the critical need to hire someone with a strong passion for education and resourcefulness, citing Natalie Williamson as an ideal profile. Despite having no tech background, Natalie demonstrated exceptional grit and drive—she ran a forum on the same platform, managed monthly meetups, and learned quickly while engaging key stakeholders like OpenAI. Marcel argued the team should prioritize either an education-focused leader or someone willing to outwork everyone while remaining curious about AI.

Jason Gong highlighted that the community needs someone who understands the community's essence and its members rather than focusing on generic event management. He noted that most people label community roles as event organizers, but the team needs someone who can think strategically about member experience and long-term growth. Jason emphasized that the community now operates more like a product, requiring a leader who grasps the community's vision and can help grow membership beyond just content delivery.

Tucker Barker noted that finding such leaders is difficult through inbound applications, requiring extensive conversations and cultural fit assessment.

### Community Vision and Role Definition

The group clarified that the community leader role must be specific to their unique AI education and engagement goals, not a generic community manager position. Jason criticized a recent community plan for being too generic and not tailored to their specific audience, emphasizing the need for bespoke strategies. Marcel agreed, saying previous proposals lacked in-depth understanding of their community and goals.

Marcel described the importance of a leader who can curate and connect people internally and externally, not just organize events. Natalie's success illustrated the value of internal relationship-building with OpenAI and other stakeholders to drive event success and community momentum. The leader should be able to curate a "tasty menu" of content and experiences that engage members meaningfully, supporting the vision of a community product that grows through rich, relevant member experiences.

### Content and Growth Coordination

Jason Gong explained that Danny will focus on creating course content, while the community leader should focus on growth and engagement strategies. This division ensures content quality while maintaining a strategic focus on expanding membership toward 1,000 members. Jason stressed the need for someone who can answer "how do we grow to 1,000 members?" rather than just producing courses. The community leader should provide directional input on the roadmap, with content creators executing the materials.

Marcel proposed possibly bringing in a hands-on marketing leader to complement the community lead and content roles, mentioning Casey as a potential candidate who is well connected and passionate but might prefer a less intense role. This could help accelerate growth through marketing expertise while maintaining community authenticity. The team is weighing cost and fit considerations for this additional role.

### Candidate Evaluation and Next Steps

Luke is scheduled for a high-touch panel the following Wednesday, providing a near-term opportunity to assess his fit for the community lead role. Marcel and Jason agreed to continue slow-burn assessment of Luke while exploring other options.

Ryan remains a strong candidate with broad skills and positive interview feedback, but the team is unsure if he fits the focused community lead profile. Marcel planned to discuss Ryan's potential role and ensure no negative sentiment arises among key community members. Jason noted that past candidates with founder backgrounds did not align well with the specific needs of this community.

The team agreed to keep refining candidate profiles and roles based on ongoing conversations and feedback, planning to finalize hiring within two weeks while balancing experience, passion, and cost factors.
