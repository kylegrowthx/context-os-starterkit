## Key Topics

### Webflow: Scope Creep & Unclear Contract

  - **Problem:** Webflow expects a new "code components" workstream for free, citing an unclear early contract.
  - **Context:** The original SOW is outdated (e.g., 5,000 pages/year), forcing an exploration phase to define current value.
  - **Action Plan:**
      - Jason will deliver a small POC (2–3 examples) to fulfill a prior commitment.
      - **New Process:** All director-level strategy work (like this POC) will be tracked in Linear.
          - **Why:** To ensure visibility, enable accurate resource planning, and create a reusable playbook for future clients.

### Okta: Project Drift & Churn Risk

  - **Problem:** Okta's POC, Rachel, is overwhelmed by a new ABX role, causing project drift and inconsistent brief delivery.
  - **Impact:** The account is at risk of churn due to custom, unscalable work.
  - **Action Plan:**
      - Kyle will hold a 1-hour reset meeting with Rachel next week to define clear priorities and success metrics.
      - **Contingency:** If the reset fails, Kyle will prospect other Okta teams (e.g., the blog team) to secure the account. Tyler can assist.

### Interwell: Performance Risk from Client Errors

  - **Problem:** Interwell's dev team repeatedly breaks the site by failing to implement redirects after URL changes.
  - **Impact:** This causes traffic drops and undermines GrowthX's SEO work.
  - **Context:** The relationship has improved since a difficult feedback session, but execution remains a challenge.
  - **Action Plan:**
      - Jakub will hold a reset meeting to define 90-day goals and align on shared success metrics.
      - **Mitigation:** GrowthX now has Git access to implement redirects directly, reducing reliance on the client.

### Surge: Scaling Content Production

  - **Goal:** Justify the large MRR by scaling content production for the data annotation persona.
  - **Plan:** Increase output from 25 to 50 articles/week to meet the original ramp schedule.
  - **Editorial Process:** Surge has approved publishing non-DA-specific content without their review.
      - **Why:** This unblocks the pipeline and allows the team to focus on scaling production.
  - **Risk:** The forecast is aggressive, but the client understands the need for rapid iteration to find what works.

### New Team Structure (Effective Dec 1)

  - **Rationale:** To scale operations, create clear career paths, and build a pool of floating support.
  - **Key Changes:**
      - **Account Strategists (AS):** Focus on client relationships and high-level strategy (8–9 accounts).
      - **Managing Editors (ME):** Own end-to-end production (3 accounts).
      - **Content Managers (CM):** Own end-to-end production (2 accounts).
      - **Pooled Directors:** Provide strategic oversight.
  - **Rollout:** Announced at the Tuesday All Hands. Promotions will be communicated on Monday.
