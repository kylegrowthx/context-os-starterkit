## Key Topics

### GRC Content Strategy

  - **Problem:** The GRC topic is too broad, attracting low-intent "what is" searches.
  - **Solution:** Focus on high-intent sub-pillars (Compliance, Risk, Audit, Board Management) where users have specific problems to solve.
  - **Rationale:** This strategy mirrors Diligent's shift to solution- and role-based pages, aligning SEO with the business's go-to-market approach.
  - **Performance Data (Tyler):**
      - **Governance:** High share of voice/clicks, a direct result of aggressive H1/Q4 content investment.
      - **Compliance, Risk, Audit:** Significant opportunity to build a stronger baseline.
  - **Decision:** Pursue a multi-pillar GRC strategy to avoid limiting the brand to a single solution set.

### Content Cadence & Production

  - **November Progress:** 11 articles published, ahead of schedule.
  - **Buffer Article:** Publish the ready-to-go article now.
      - **Rationale:** Capitalize on higher November traffic before the holiday season dip.
  - **Nov/Dec Target:** 40 articles total.
  - **GRC Content Spreadsheet (Kezia):** Shared a spreadsheet of old GRC content, ranked by search volume, to guide refresh efforts.

### Comparison Article Strategy

  - **Goal:** Create comparison articles (e.g., "Compliance Automation Software") to capture high-intent search traffic.
  - **Legal Constraint:** Direct feature-vs-feature comparisons are prohibited due to past legal issues with competitors.
  - **Solution:** A neutral "buyer's guide" format that avoids direct competitor call-outs.
      - **Format:** List solutions with objective descriptions (use cases, market positioning), not feature tables.
      - **Positioning:** Use trust signals (awards, stats) to establish Diligent's leadership.
  - **Rationale:** This approach fulfills user search intent and provides clear signals for LLMs to surface Diligent as a category leader.
