## Key Topics

### Candidate Background & Motivation

  - **Career Path:** 10 years in digital, moving from Growth Marketing (SEO/CRO) to Product Management, and now to hands-on building (automation, prompt engineering).
  - **Motivation:** Driven by a deep curiosity ("how things work") and a desire for faster, more tangible results than larger teams often allow.
  - **Product Management Experience:** Worked at startups (12-person to Series C) on full-cycle product development, from security to SRE.

### Handling Vague Requirements & Unrealistic Expectations

  - **Process for Vague Requirements:**
    1.  **Define Success:** Establish clear success metrics or a vision.
    2.  **Interview Stakeholders:** Ask "why" to uncover core problems, timelines, and tech preferences.
    3.  **Propose a POC:** Build a lightweight prototype to validate the approach and clarify requirements.
  - **Process for Unrealistic Expectations:**
    1.  **Find Core Value:** Ask "what is the exact purpose?" to identify and remove non-essential requirements (often 30–40%).
    2.  **Negotiate Timeline vs. Feature:** Offer a clear trade-off: more features require more time or resources.
    3.  **Leverage Open Source:** Use existing tools to accelerate delivery for tight deadlines.

### Measuring Content Quality

  - **Challenge:** Content quality is subjective, requiring a multi-faceted approach.
  - **Proposed Metrics:**
      - **Objective SEO Analysis:** Analyze top-ranking articles for keyword density, H-tag structure, and meta descriptions to create a measurable benchmark.
      - **User Engagement:** Track time-on-page and heatmaps to measure user interest.
      - **Operational Feedback:** Monitor user regeneration counts and collect feedback on *why* a section was regenerated.
      - **Lagging Business KPIs:** Track long-term metrics like Google rankings, impressions, and clicks to measure business impact.

### GrowthX Team & Culture

  - **Stevie's Role:** Manages sprints, triages tickets, leads special projects (e.g., migrating to agentic workflows), and reviews technical content.
  - **Team Dynamics:** Engineers focus on building, often customizing existing workflows. The culture is scrappy, requiring a balance of speed and quality due to internal stakeholders' limited availability for feedback.
  - **Success Factor:** The most critical trait for success is a strong focus on quality and ownership, as this is a key challenge for the team.
