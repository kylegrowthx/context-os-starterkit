## Key Topics

### Account Transition & Cadence Change

  - Matthew Panzarino is transitioning out of the account manager role.
      - **New AM:** Katya will take over.
      - **Continuity:** Matthew will remain in Slack channels to ensure a smooth transition.
  - The sync cadence will change from weekly to bi-weekly.
      - **Rationale:** To reduce meeting overhead during periods of consistent performance.
      - **Flexibility:** Ad-hoc weekly syncs can be scheduled for new initiatives (e.g., product launches).
  - The November performance report is due December 3rd.
      - **Attendees:** Robert (Monograph) will join this meeting.

### New "Leads" Product Content Strategy

  - Monograph shared a spec sheet for the upcoming "Leads" product.
  - **Product Definition:** A pipeline management and forecasting tool, not a full CRM.
      - **Audience:** The primary user is the Business Development Representative (BDR).
      - **Context:** Pipeline functionality is considered "table stakes" in the market, not a "flashy" feature.
  - **Content Strategy:**
      - **Paid/Earned Media:** Pivot messaging to "all-in-one" software, de-emphasizing "CRM."
      - **SEO/Content:** Continue creating CRM-related content to build thought leadership.
          - **Positioning:** Frame Monograph's tool as the ideal, right-sized solution for architects.
  - **Persona Development:** Growth X will create a BDR persona to guide content creation.
      - **Rationale:** To ensure content speaks directly to the BDR's specific pain points and goals.
      - **Process:** Growth X will conduct the research, using its "context engineering" process.

### Civil Engineering Content Performance

  - A "Civil Engineer Salary Guide" post is a top traffic driver, but civil engineers are not Monograph's ICP.
      - **Context:** The original strategy was to avoid government/military projects, which are common for civil engineers.
  - **Performance Discrepancy:**
      - **SEMrush:** Reports "civil engineer" as the \#1 keyword (15% of traffic).
      - **Looker (GSC data):** Shows the "Structural Engineer Salary Guide" outperforming the civil post in sessions and growth.
  - **Decision:** Do not remove the post.
      - **Rationale:** The traffic is free, and there's an opportunity to capture ICP users who may be viewing the content.
  - **Refined Strategy:**
      - **Internal Linking:** Add links from the civil post to other engineering guides to encourage self-nurturing.
      - **Keyword Targeting:** Focus future civil engineering content on the private sector to attract qualified traffic.
      - **Lead Signal:** The "AI in Civil Engineering" post has generated two sales-qualified opportunities ("squoats"), one this month, suggesting some value in the topic.
