## Summary

GrowthX and Siit aligned on a critical content strategy shift: prioritizing ITSM and Service Desk keywords over lower-intent Help Desk terms to attract higher-quality leads and reduce churn among basic-plan customers. Matthew confirmed three quick wins for implementation: adding a CTA next to product images on tool pages (via Webflow), creating a "Siit Alternatives" page to drive direct conversions, and updating the editorial roadmap to focus on qualified ITSM prospects. Carmel shared marketing audit findings showing strong first-time visitor traffic but low returning visitors and pages-per-session, plus concerning blog performance (nearly all posts under 100 clicks in 6 months), creating urgency around content quality improvements. ## Context

Siit is a service desk and ITSM platform that GrowthX is providing content marketing services for. This weekly sync between Siit's head of marketing (Carmel Schetrit) and GrowthX's content lead (Matthew Alves-Hill) is a standing recurring meeting to align on content strategy, review marketing audit findings, and discuss upcoming content initiatives. GrowthX is helping Siit improve traffic quality, conversion rates, and reduce customer churn by repositioning their content strategy around high-intent ITSM/Service Desk keywords rather than lower-intent Help Desk terms. The relationship is active with regular deliverables: blog posts, tool review pages, alternative comparison pages, and technical integrations into Siit's website.

## Key Topics

### Problem: Low Engagement & High Churn

  - A marketing audit revealed two key issues:
      - **Low Engagement:** High first-time visitor traffic from tools/blog, but low returning visitors and pages-per-session.
      - **High Churn:** Customers on the basic Help Desk plan churn at a higher rate than those on ITSM/AI plans.

### Solution 1: Content Strategy Shift

  - **Decision:** Prioritize content targeting ITSM and Service Desk keywords.
  - **Rationale:** Attracts higher-quality leads who are more likely to convert and retain, even if it means less overall traffic.
  - **Action:** GrowthX will revise the editorial roadmap to align with this new focus.

### Solution 2: New CTA on Tool Pages

  - **Decision:** Add a CTA next to the tool's image on review pages (e.g., Linear).
  - **Rationale:** Captures user intent immediately, offering a natural next step for users who just read about an integration.
  - **Action:** GrowthX will implement the change via a Webflow developer.

### Solution 3: "Siit Alternatives" Page

  - **Decision:** Create a "Siit Alternatives" page to pitch Siit first, then list competitors.
  - **Rationale:** This page type was a top performer at Scribe, proving its effectiveness for direct conversion.
  - **Action:** GrowthX will draft the page for Carmel's review.

### Other Content Updates

  - **Listicles:**
      - Include Siit integrations at the top.
      - Include major legacy competitors (e.g., Freshservice) for comprehensive coverage.
      - Remove European competitors, as the market is not a focus.
  - **New CTA Copy:**
      - **Decision:** Use "Connect to Slack" over "Connect to Slack or Teams."
      - **Rationale:** 75% of customers use the Slack integration, making the copy more punchy and relevant to the majority.
