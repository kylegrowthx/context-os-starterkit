## Key Topics

### Execution & Prioritization

  - **Challenge:** Managing competing deadlines for a bank and a real estate client, both requiring high-volume (1500-word) blogs weekly.
  - **Solution:** Assessed content intent to determine business impact.
      - **Bank:** Transactional content → direct revenue (credit card applications) → **High Priority**
      - **Real Estate:** Brand-building content → lower direct revenue → **Lower Priority**
  - **Action:** Communicated the rationale to account managers and re-prioritized the bank's content.
  - **Outcome:** Both clients were satisfied; the bank's campaign launched on time and generated applications.
  - **Tool:** A custom, shared Google Sheet for real-time tracking and delegation.

### Content Quality & Process Improvement

  - **Challenge:** A new product launch was at risk from an Upwork freelancer's blog that lacked keywords, making it undiscoverable.
  - **Action:** Halted the launch, despite a manager's deadline, to rewrite the blog with proper keyword research.
  - **Outcome:** The rewritten blog became one of the company's highest-performing pieces.
  - **Process:** Implemented a mandatory, detailed checklist for all content, covering keywords, AI usage, and other quality standards.

### Leadership & Feedback Response

  - **Challenge:** Natalie's current CEO gave her feedback that she was taking on too much tactical work instead of delegating to focus on managerial/strategic tasks.
  - **Action:** She accepted the feedback constructively, explained she needed time to reset, and immediately shifted to a daily delegation-first routine.
  - **Outcome:** Changed her approach from independence (working alone for months while hiring was slow) to proactive delegation of any task possible.
  - **Growth trajectory:** Earlier in her career, Natalie took critical feedback from CEOs personally ("felt like a stab at the heart"), but learned to reframe it as a tool for improvement rather than defensiveness.

### GrowthX Context & Role Expectations

  - **Communication Channels:** Leadership prefers updates via Slack (with client-specific channels plus account strategy channel and all-team channel for flagging), weekly account health meetings, and Linear (for company-wide task tracking to reduce silos and repeat work).
  - **Managing Editor Role Restructure (Effective Dec 1):**
      - **Current model:** Managing Editors edit content from Content Managers who run workflows.
      - **New model:** Managing Editors own the end-to-end content production workflow — running Atlas workflows, managing Airtable content database, and communicating with Engagement Managers (who handle client-facing work).
      - **Why the shift:** Consolidate ownership, improve workflow quality, and reduce team members bypassing the system for external tools.
  - **First Challenge: Improving Atlas Platform Adoption & Output Quality**
      - **Core problem:** Team members sometimes bypass the internal Atlas platform to use ChatGPT directly, indicating the platform's workflows or artifacts aren't meeting their needs.
      - **Expectation:** Proactively troubleshoot — dive into workflows, review AI-generated artifacts, identify issues (e.g., weak prompts), and improve the core product rather than abandoning it.
      - **Success metric:** Making Atlas the go-to tool instead of external tools, and improving output quality enough that team members trust it.
