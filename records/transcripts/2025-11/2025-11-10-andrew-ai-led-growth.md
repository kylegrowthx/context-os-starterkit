## Summary

Jason conducted a user research call with Andrew Stephen, a 20+ year SEO specialist at WPP APAC, to understand how GrowthX content and the "systems, not software" framework inform his team's adoption of AI-led content workflows. Andrew shared that applying GrowthX's process to a client project — building a consumer guide to EV tyres — produced noticeably higher-quality output than traditional 800-word articles, and that he's building an internal business case at WPP to position Claude and GPT-5 workflows as an alternative to WPP's feature-disabled internal tool. ## Context

Andrew Stephen is an SEO specialist with 20+ years in the industry, currently working at WPP — a multinational advertising and marketing agency where he focuses on technical auditing, content development, and strategy. His team was recently merged with another group, and his manager gave him a green light to set up an experimental content working group to explore alternatives to WPP's internal tool (WPP Open), a "feature-disabled" system that Stephen believes will become obsolete within two years. He discovered GrowthX through a YouTube interview with the Scrunch founder while researching measurement solutions for clients navigating the SEO industry's disruption due to generative AI. The meeting was a user research call to gather Andrew's feedback on GrowthX content and product direction, and to understand how he's applying the frameworks in his work.
