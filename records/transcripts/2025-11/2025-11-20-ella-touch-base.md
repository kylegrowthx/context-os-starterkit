## Summary

Kyle Gaudreau (Director of Delivery) and Ella Dillon (operations consultant) discussed GrowthX's delivery challenges, including inconsistent quality across customer engagements, unclear success metrics, and the strain of high-touch custom accounts on senior resources. Kyle walked through the new Engagement Manager model (7–9 accounts per manager with biweekly cadence) and his new "shared strategy" team tasked with standardizing delivery, building repeatable playbooks, and systematizing knowledge sharing across accounts. The conversation surfaced the gap between defining best practices on paper and ensuring teams can execute them, including training needs around data storytelling and managing scope creep.

## Relevance

**To GrowthX Delivery:**
- New Engagement Manager model (7–9 accounts) is untested; needs real-world validation on workload, especially if moving to biweekly meeting cadence without losing momentum.
- Customer journey roadmap is fragmented. Need standardized exit criteria for strategy sprints, clear escalation paths, and consistent customer meeting agendas across all pods.
- Onboarding and "reboarding" processes are not documented or enforced consistently; training gaps exist around data storytelling, identifying success signals, and managing scope creep.
- High-touch custom accounts (Okta at $12k/mo, Surge, AugmentCode) drain senior resources (especially Jason) and block development of repeatable processes. Strategic decision needed on brand value vs. operational cost.

**To GrowthX Business Development:**
- Renewals at risk if customers don't understand the complexity of content/SEO strategy work. Need better customer education on why the work is hard and what GrowthX is solving.
- Weekly meeting cadence is treated as "motion" rather than "momentum" — some customers may not need weekly touchpoints. Opportunity to optimize accounts by moving to biweekly or async updates.
- Documentation of customer engagements lives in Fathom and Notion but is difficult to search and surface; could impact onboarding speed and institutional knowledge sharing.

**To GrowthX Strategy:**
- Shared strategy team should prioritize building 3–4 repeatable playbooks for common customer types (SaaS, B2B services, technical product, etc.) before expanding beyond core model.
- Metrics to track: ticket completion rate, customer health signals (via programmatic flags), playbook adoption rate, and engagement manager account density impact on renewal rates.

## Key Topics

### The Problem: Inconsistent Delivery & Unclear Success

  - Delivery is inconsistent, focusing on "business-as-usual" updates (e.g., content output) instead of strategic outcomes.
  - The root cause is a lack of a standardized customer journey and unclear success metrics, which leads to:
      - **Vague Strategy Sprint Exit Criteria:** No clear definition of what makes an account "ready" for ongoing delivery.
      - **Fragmented Onboarding:** No consistent, published engagement roadmap for new employees.
      - **Unclear Escalation Paths:** Team members are unsure when to escalate or to whom.
  - The current weekly meeting cadence often feels like "motion" without "momentum," as teams can struggle to find meaningful discussion points.

### The Solution: New Org Structure & Strategy Team

  - The new EM model (7–9 accounts) aims to create capacity by separating client-facing roles from editing duties.
  - The planned shift to biweekly meetings is intended to give EMs more time for strategic work, but this must be balanced against the risk of losing customer momentum.
  - Kyle's new "shared strategy" team will standardize delivery by:
      - **Building Playbooks:** Creating repeatable processes for common challenges.
      - **Capturing Insights:** Systematizing the sharing of learnings across accounts.
      - **Measuring Success:** Tracking metrics like ticket completion, client performance, and playbook creation.

### The Challenge: Unscalable Accounts

  - High-touch, custom accounts are a major drain on senior resources, especially Jason, and prevent the development of repeatable processes.
  - **Okta ($12k/mo):** A custom project building SDR playbooks. The work is not transferable, and the scope is creeping. Kyle is scheduling a level-setting conversation.
  - **Surge & AugmentCode:** High-paying but highly custom accounts that rely heavily on Jason.
  - **Strategic Decision:** GrowthX must decide if the brand value of these logos outweighs the operational cost and drag on scalability.
