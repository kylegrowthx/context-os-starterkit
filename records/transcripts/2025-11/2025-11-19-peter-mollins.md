## Overview

- **Partnership Model:** GrowthX's model starts with an 8-week "Strategy Sprint" to prove quality with \~10 articles before a 12-month commitment.
- **Value Proposition:** GrowthX is an agency providing full execution, contrasting with Nooks's current SEO tools that only provide recommendations.

## Key Topics

### Nooks's Priorities & Concerns

  - **Primary Need:** Rapid, high-volume content production.
  - **Desired Content Mix:**
      - High-volume editorial (blog posts).
      - More complex formats (e.g., ROI calculators, thought leadership).
  - **Core Concern:** Justifying the investment over using internal tools and AI (e.g., Claude) to get "90% of the way there."
  - **Pace Question:** Is a "blistering pace" of content production necessary, or would an initial burst suffice?

### GrowthX's Partnership Model

  - **Phase 1: 8-Week Strategy Sprint**
      - **Purpose:** Prove quality and build a 6-month roadmap.
      - **Opt-Out:** Nooks can cancel before the 12-month agreement.
      - **Deliverables:**
          - Editorial roadmap (Week 3).
          - \~10 "calibration" articles published by Week 7/8.
  - **Phase 2: 12-Month Agreement**
      - **Focus:** Scale editorial content for fast ROI.
      - **Future Work:** Introduce complex formats (calculators) based on opportunity.

### GrowthX's Value Proposition & Process

  - **Agency vs. Tool:** GrowthX is an agency providing full execution, not just recommendations.
  - **Execution Workflow:**
      - **Context Engineering:** Deeply understand Nooks's ICP and market differentiators.
      - **Sophisticated AI Workflows:** Use multiple models (Claude, OpenAI) for agentic research, drafting, and fact-checking.
      - **Expert Oversight:** Human experts are in the loop at each step.
  - **AEO (AI Engine Optimization):**
      - Tracks visibility against competitors across \~2,500 prompts.
      - Uses real-world conversations (Reddit, LinkedIn) to find high-value prompts.
      - **Example:** For Augment Code, tracking visibility for "AI enterprise coding" and its unique ability to handle large codebases.
