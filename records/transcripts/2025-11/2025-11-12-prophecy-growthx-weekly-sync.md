### Defining Content Success Metrics

  - **Objective:** Measure content performance across the funnel, from discovery to conversion.
  - **Metrics by Content Type:**
      - **Keyword-Focused (SEO):**
          - Organic site traffic (views, users, time on page).
          - LLM mentions (AEO/GEO).
      - **Thought Leadership:**
          - **Initial Hurdle:** Quality high enough for intentional distribution (social, nurture).
          - **Nurture Performance:** Higher open/click-through rates are a strong positive signal.
          - **Social Performance:** Outperforming other content is a positive signal, but the algorithm is unreliable.
      - **Conversions:**
          - **Enterprise:** Track demo requests.
          - **PLG:** Track signups for the "RealForce" free plan (launching next month).
  - **GrowthX Role:** Provide benchmarks and guidance on what constitutes "good" performance.
  - **Prophecy Action:** Cody will meet with Will to clarify conversion goals and tracking capabilities.

### Competitive & Partnership Strategy

  - **Primary Competitor: Alteryx**
      - **Rationale:** Prophecy is deliberately re-engaging Alteryx head-to-head, a strategy with a proven track record.
      - **Content Focus:** Highlight Alteryx's limitations (e.g., desktop vs. cloud) and position Prophecy as superior for analysts.
  - **Key Partnership: Databricks**
      - **Rationale:** Databricks prefers Prophecy over Alteryx because Prophecy drives higher platform usage.
      - **Mechanism:** Prophecy enables analysts to solve engineering backlogs, increasing Databricks compute consumption.
      - **GrowthX Opportunity:** Research topics that position Prophecy as a productivity layer for Databricks, capturing AEO intent from users facing common pipeline problems.
