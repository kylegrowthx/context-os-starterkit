## Summary

The shift is necessary because the current content strategy relies on a single interview, which lacks the depth to support multiple distinct articles per pillar and risks producing shallow, repetitive content (a lesson from the Hypergrowth project). ## Key Topics

### Content Strategy Pivot

  - **Problem:** The initial pillar-based plan is unworkable.
      - **Rationale:** It relies on a single interview, which lacks the depth for multiple, distinct articles per pillar.
      - **Risk:** This could lead to shallow, repetitive content, a known issue from the Hypergrowth project.
  - **Solution:** A 12-week publishing schedule.
      - **Format:** One \~700–800 word Substack article per week.
      - **Validation:** Each topic must have a clear thesis and supporting arguments to ensure sufficient depth.
      - **Example:** The Ambiance "gun detection" campaign is a model for this structured approach.
