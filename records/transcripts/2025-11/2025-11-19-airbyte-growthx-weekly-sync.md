## Context

Airbyte is a B2B data integration platform that traditionally targets data engineers and data teams building data pipelines. GrowthX is executing a content strategy for Airbyte to enter a new market: software and product engineers building AI agents who lack deep data engineering expertise. This shift requires repositioning Airbyte's messaging away from traditional data infrastructure language (ETL, connectors) toward simpler terms (tools, integrations) while maintaining trust by leveraging Airbyte's data engineering pedigree. The weekly sync is part of the content delivery workflow, tracking progress on the thought leadership and editorial content calendar, writing guidelines, and publishing workflow coordination.

## Key Topics

### Content Strategy & Cadence

  - **Initial Focus:** The first sprint prioritizes the thought leadership series to establish voice and tone.
  - **Cadence Goal:** Publish one article per week.
  - **Future Work:** After establishing the initial cadence, the team will explore more intensive PSEO (Programmatic SEO) initiatives.

### Content Types & Placement

  - **"Thought Leadership" Stream**
      - **Content:** High-level, strategic pieces (e.g., "9 layers of authentic infrastructure").
      - **Placement:** Published on the main Airbyte blog and Substack.
      - **Goal:** Amplify reach and build backlinks.
  - **"Agentic Data" Stream**
      - **Content:** Technical, educational pieces (e.g., "What is AI agent orchestration?").
      - **Placement:** A new, dedicated category on the Airbyte site.
      - **Goal:** Build topical authority in the agentic data space.

### Workflow & Collaboration

  - **Writing Guidelines:** Kushal will provide final feedback today, unblocking GrowthX to begin drafting.
  - **Content Review:** Kushal and Tanmay will review all drafts.
  - **Publishing:** The team will use the existing Webflow API workflow, managed by Tanmay.

### Audience & Positioning

  - **Challenge:** The new target audience—software and product engineers building AI agents—are not data experts.
  - **Solution:** Use simple, accessible language (e.g., "tools," "connectors") instead of data-specific jargon (e.g., "ETL").
  - **Rationale (Arcade Example):** Arcade's success comes from its clear, simple messaging. Airbyte will emulate this while leveraging its data engineering pedigree to build trust.
  - **Category Creation:** The strategy includes publishing on topics with low/no search volume to establish early authority in a new, emerging category.
