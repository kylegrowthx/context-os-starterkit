## Key Topics

### Content Strategy & Publishing Goal

  - **Goal:** Publish 20 articles in two weeks to increase "shots on goal" and accelerate performance testing.
  - **Priorities:**
    1.  **Competitor Blogs:** "vs." articles are performing well and align with the LLM's strengths.
    2.  **Country Guides:** Refine these articles to be more strategic and less regulatory.
    3.  **ISO/Payfac Content:** Develop topics for these key audiences.
  - **Deprioritized:** Foundational articles (e.g., "Gateway vs. Processor") will be published as is to clear the backlog and focus resources on new priorities.

### Competitor Blog Strategy

  - **Problem:** Titles like "Rapyd vs. Stripe for high-opportunity businesses" use internal jargon ("high-opportunity") that lacks SEO value and narrows the audience.
  - **Solution:**
      - **Title:** Use broad, SEO-friendly titles (e.g., "Rapyd vs. Stripe").
      - **Content:** Include a subsection on "complex" or "highly regulated industries" (e.g., iGaming, forex).
  - **Rationale:** This structure broadens appeal while still addressing a key segment. It also provides flexibility, as Rapyd's risk appetite for these industries can change abruptly.

### Country Guide Strategy (UK Article Review)

  - **Problem:** The UK guide was overly focused on regulatory minutiae, which is not Rapyd's expertise and burdens the review process.
  - **Solution:** Shift the persona from a compliance expert to a strategic business owner focused on market entry and payment acceptance.
  - **Key Directives:**
      - **Avoid Regulatory Advice:** Do not provide guidance on business administration (e.g., registering with Company House).
      - **Focus on Actionable Strategy:** Cover topics like:
          - Consumer payment preferences (online vs. in-store, mobile vs. contactless).
          - Localization best practices (checkout, currency, support).
          - Trust-building tactics for e-commerce.
      - **Never Discuss Fees:** Fees are bespoke and negotiated per client.
          - **Allowed:** General statements about fee drivers (e.g., "higher fees for non-local acquirers post-Brexit").
          - **Not Allowed:** Numerical figures, interchange rates, or comparative pricing.
      - **Clarify Local Acquiring:** Explain that local acquiring is a benefit for specific use cases (e.g., international corps with local entities, payment partners using Rapyd's license), not a universal requirement.

### ISO & Payfac Audience Definition

  - **ISO (Independent Sales Organization):** A partner that identifies merchants and resells Rapyd's services. Rapyd handles merchant onboarding and liability.
  - **Payfac (Payment Facilitator):** A partner that uses Rapyd's infrastructure but handles its own merchant onboarding and assumes liability. This requires the Payfac to be a regulated payment company with its own licensing and compliance team.
