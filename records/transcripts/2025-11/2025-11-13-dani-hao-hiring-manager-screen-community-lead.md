## Key Topics

### Mutiny: Building the "M2" Community

  - **Context:** Built the "M2" (Modern Marketers) community from scratch to drive pipeline during a period of marketing budget cuts.
  - **Strategy → "Second Lever" Theme:** Focused on improving conversion rates to generate pipeline without increasing top-of-funnel spend.
  - **Structure → Flywheel Model:**
      - **Acquisition:** Newsletter → Events → Slack Community (M2).
      - **Content Loop:** Sourced insights from M2 threads → Head of Content used for newsletter/blog posts.
      - **Engagement Loop:** Used newsletter to promote events → Drove M2 membership by offering a say in event planning.
  - **Key Programs & Tactics:**
      - **Virtual Conference ("Second Lever"):**
          - **Goal:** Maximize reach within the Ideal Customer Profile (ICP).
          - **Speaker Acquisition:** Used a CEO alias to send "personal favor" emails to CMOs from the VC network and ABM target accounts.
          - **Nurture:** Hosted private executive dinners post-event to convert high-value prospects.
      - **Customer Activation ("March Madness"):**
          - **Goal:** Increase customer activation, a key company metric.
          - **Mechanism:** A bracket-style contest where customers submitted conversion plays using the product.
          - **Outcomes:** Generated social buzz (custom "player cards"), drove event attendance, and awarded a grand prize ($1k cash or conference trip).
  - **Metrics:**
      - **Growth:** Net new community members.
      - **Engagement:** Weekly Active Members (WAM) / Total Members.
      - **Business Impact:** Net pipeline generated from community activities.

### Apollo: Scaling for Retention & Expansion

  - **Context:** Scaled the community for a Product-Led Growth (PLG) company with a large free user base.
  - **Business Metric:** Net Retention Rate (NRR) and expansion revenue.
  - **Proof of Value:** A quarterly cohort analysis showed community members were 2-3x more likely to retain and generated substantially more expansion revenue.
  - **Key Programs & Tactics:**
      - **"Apollo Days" Template Contest:**
          - **Goal:** Gather and share real-world AI prompt/workflow examples from power users.
          - **Mechanism:** Users submitted templates; the community voted on the most helpful.
          - **Outcome:** The contest led to a partnership with the Product/Eng team to build a 1-click "steal this playbook" feature directly into the product.
      - **Event Strategy:**
          - **Customer-Run Events:** Enabled customers to host their own local events with a $2k budget and support.
          - **Conference Happy Hours:** Used as a new member acquisition channel at major industry events (e.g., HubSpot Inbound).
  - **Cross-Functional Collaboration:**
      - **Marketing:** Partnered with email and social teams to repurpose community content (e.g., "inbound horror stories" campaign).
      - **Product:** Added "Join the Community" CTAs to knowledge base articles and product webinars.
      - **Sales/CS:** Provided enablement materials to invite prospects and new customers to the community.

### GrowthX: A New Paid Community

  - **Context:** The community is a core product, not a support channel. It launched this week and has already doubled its paying members from 70 to 140+.
  - **Early Learnings:**
      - **Accessibility:** Using common tools (ChatGPT, Perplexity) in workshops makes content tangible and accessible for members.
      - **Aspiration:** The community's value is currently driven by the caliber of its members, creating a strong aspirational draw.
  - **Key Challenges:**
      - **Scaling Content:** Needs a model to partner with niche influencers, offloading content creation while leveraging their audience for growth.
      - **"Anchor Moment":** Lacks a central, recurring event (like a summit or exclusive retreat) to rally the community.
      - **Vibe Management:** Must balance accessibility with maintaining a high-quality, "in-the-weeds" professional atmosphere.
