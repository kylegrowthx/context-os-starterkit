## Key Topics

### Design Partner Search

  - **Decision:** Superside is not a viable partner.
  - **Rationale:** Their model is misaligned with Smith.ai's needs.
      - **High Cost, Low Human Touch:** The AI-driven workflow does not translate to cost savings; Maddy suspects it's a high-margin play.
      - **Brand Inconsistency:** The model lacks sufficient human oversight to ensure brand adherence, a critical requirement.
  - **Action:** Megan will ask GrowthX's Head of Design for alternative agency referrals.

### Backlinking Strategy

  - **Decision:** GrowthX is discontinuing backlinking services.
  - **Rationale:** The service proved too labor-intensive.
  - **Status:** The current batch of backlinks will be the last. A final performance report is pending from Joe.

### AI Receptionist Knowledge Base

  - **Status:** Final designs are due this week.
  - **Structure:** The hub will use a hub-and-spoke model (hub page → pillar pages → supporting content).
  - **Goal:** A long-term play to build retargeting audiences, not for immediate lead capture.
      - **Mechanism:** Use content engagement data to create highly targeted ad segments.
      - **Benefit:** Reduces reliance on expensive third-party data providers.

### Competitive Landscape & Brand Strategy

  - **Primary Threat:** RingCentral's entry into AI receptionists.
      - **Why:** They are a "gorilla" competitor with massive resources, domain authority, and a vast channel network (MSPs, VARs) that dwarfs Smith.ai's.
  - **Smith.ai's Response:**
      - **Ad Budget Rebalance:** Shifted from a 90/10 lead gen/awareness split back to a 60-70/30-40 split after the aggressive lead-gen focus hurt performance.
      - **Future Focus:** Explore offline channels (e.g., stadium sponsorships) in 2026 to build brand awareness.
      - **Past Learnings:**
          - **Podcast Ads:** Too expensive to justify as a lead-gen channel; only viable for pure awareness.
          - **TV Ads (Hulu/Mountain):** Limited success due to a short run and poor creative.
  - **Opportunity:** Target demographics like WNBA fans, who are more likely to be small business owners than the tech-heavy NBA audience.
