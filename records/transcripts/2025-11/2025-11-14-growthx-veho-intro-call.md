## Context

Veho is a venture-backed B2B parcel delivery company that uses a gig-economy driver model to compete with FedEx and UPS for enterprise e-commerce shipping business. They partner with major brands like Macy's, Saks, Lululemon, and Adidas, and have a strong driver pool with a waiting list. Rachel Tyroler leads marketing and is currently hiring a content marketer because the small team has been unable to execute the strategic content needed. The call stemmed from a referral through Sharnaz, suggesting existing network connection to GrowthX. Veho's core challenge is that while they have excellent client testimonials, negative driver and customer sentiment on Reddit, YouTube, and review platforms is creating a credibility gap in competitive RFPs against well-established carriers. Enterprise buyers are increasingly using ChatGPT and LLMs to research vendors, but Veho has low share of voice there compared to smaller competitors.

## Overview

- **Veho's Goal:** Transition from a regional carrier to a national primary carrier, competing with FedEx/UPS for enterprise clients (Macy's, Lululemon).
- **Core Challenge:** A weak online reputation from driver/customer complaints on Reddit/YouTube is a major blocker for long sales cycles and large RFPs, despite strong client testimonials.
- **GrowthX's Pitch:** An AI-driven content engine (strategy, creation, publishing) to build authority and capture buyer intent in traditional search and LLMs (e.g., ChatGPT).

## Key Topics

### Veho's Business & Market Position

  - **Model:** B2B parcel delivery for major e-commerce brands (Macy's, Saks, Lululemon).
  - **Differentiation:** An asset-light, tech-first model using a gig economy driver network → lower costs and flexible capacity for clients.
  - **Strategic Goal:** Evolve from a regional carrier to a national primary carrier, competing directly with FedEx and UPS.

### The Reputation & Content Challenge

  - **ICP:** VPs of Transportation/Logistics, for whom shipping is a major P\&L line item.
  - **Sales Cycle:** Long and high-stakes (multi-year contracts, millions in value), making reputation critical.
  - **Key Blocker:** Negative content from driver/customer complaints on Reddit and YouTube is a major RFP risk.
  - **Buyer Journey Insight:** Veho's research shows enterprise buyers use ChatGPT to analyze vendors, but Veho has low share of voice in LLMs.
  - **Content Gap:** Current content is product-focused, missing the educational, SEO-driven articles needed to build authority and capture buyer intent.

### GrowthX's Proposed Solution

  - **Service:** An end-to-end content partner providing strategy, AI-driven creation, and publishing.
  - **Process:**
    1.  **Context Engineering:** Ingest Veho's brand/product docs to create a foundational knowledge base.
    2.  **Strategy:** Map keywords to topic clusters to build authority.
    3.  **Execution:** Use AI workflows with human oversight to draft articles.
    4.  **Publishing:** Handle CMS staging and publishing after Veho's review.
  - **Pricing:**
      - **Phase 1 (Strategy Sprint):** $40k one-time fee.
      - **Phase 2 (Ongoing):** $15k/month per workstream.
          - **Editorial:** 5 articles/week.
          - **Programmatic:** Large-scale content (e.g., vendor directories, state guides).
          - **Reddit:** Reputation management.
      - **Flexibility:** The Reddit workstream's cost could be lower than $15k/mo if a scalable workflow is defined.
