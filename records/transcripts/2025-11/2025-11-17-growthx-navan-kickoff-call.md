## Summary

GrowthX and Navan kicked off an 8-week content strategy engagement designed to shift Navan's organic growth from SMB/end-user focus to enterprise accounts (5,000+ employees), with the goal of improving paid search efficiency and establishing Navan as an AI visibility leader. The strategy centers on creating approximately 150 missing content pieces (pillar pages, review articles, competitor comparisons) through a two-phase approach: foundational artifact creation (weeks 1-3) followed by calibration and scaling to 5 articles per week by week 4-5. Key positioning challenges include overcoming lingering COVID-era perception issues around support quality and reestablishing Navan's superior 24/7 AI-first support model.

---

## Context

Navan is a travel and expense management platform recently celebrating an IPO, with sophisticated product capabilities (unified travel/expense, dynamic policies, rewards, AI-enabled 24/7 support) that appeal primarily to large enterprises dealing with M&A complexity. The relationship is led by Nima Asrar Haghighi, VP of Demand Generation and executive sponsor, who owns revenue responsibility for the engagement. Libby Zay, Director of Content Marketing (4 years at Navan), and Moran Brayer, SEO Lead (6 months in role), represent the content and SEO teams. The core challenge is tactical: Navan's current organic content strategy drives top-of-funnel traffic but fails to convert; they need bottom-of-funnel, persona-specific content targeting enterprise buyers (particularly CPOs, Finance Teams, Travel Managers) who are evaluating Navan against legacy competitors like Concur and FCM. Additionally, Navan must reframe perception around its AI capabilities and support quality, which have fully recovered from COVID-era resource constraints but carry lingering reputation damage in the market.

## Overview

- **Strategic Shift:** Move from an SMB/end-user focus to targeting enterprise clients (5,000+ employees) to capture higher-value accounts and improve paid search efficiency.
- **Content Gap:** Current content drives traffic but not conversions. The new strategy will focus on bottom-of-funnel content tailored to specific enterprise personas (e.g., CPO) and their "jobs to be done."
- **Positioning Challenge:** Navan must reframe its narrative to combat competitor FUD (Fear, Uncertainty, and Doubt) regarding its AI leadership and 24/7 support, which is superior but perceived as weak due to a lingering COVID-era reputation.

## Key Topics

### The Challenge: Inefficient Growth & Content Gaps

  - **Primary Driver:** Improve paid search efficiency by shifting focus to organic growth.
  - **Content Gap:** Current content drives top-of-funnel traffic but fails to convert.
      - **Goal:** Create bottom-of-funnel content that directly addresses enterprise persona needs.
      - **Need:** \~150 missing content pieces, including pillar pages, review articles, and competitor comparisons.
  - **Strategic Goal:** Expand from an SMB/end-user focus to targeting enterprise accounts (5,000+ employees).
      - **Rationale:** Enterprise buyers have distinct needs (e.g., M\&A impact, travel manager career paths) that require a different content approach than SMBs.

### The Solution: Targeted Content & Strategic Positioning

  - **Core Differentiators:**
      - **Single Platform:** Unifies travel and expense, solving data silos common in M\&A-heavy companies.
      - **Dynamic Policies & Rewards:** Adjusts spend caps based on real-time market rates and rewards users for booking under policy, driving adoption and savings.
      - **Superior Inventory:** Consolidates sources (direct, Expedia, NDC) for comprehensive, competitive options.
      - **24/7 Support:** AI-first with human backup for complex issues, resulting in faster resolution times.
  - **Positioning Strategy:**
      - **AI Leadership:** Reclaim the AI narrative by highlighting Navan's long-standing, embedded AI capabilities.
      - **Support Reputation:** Counter competitor FUD by proving the superiority of Navan's 24/7 support, which has fully recovered from COVID-era resource constraints.
      - **Legacy Competitors:** Overcome buyer inertia by demonstrating how Navan's tech solves specific pain points (e.g., Concur's data silos, FCM's 1,000-hour implementations).
      - **Travel Manager Objections:** Address fears of job displacement by framing Navan as a tool for career advancement and strategic leadership.

### The Plan: 8-Week Strategy Sprint

  - **Phase 1: Research & Artifact Creation (Weeks 1–3)**
      - **Goal:** Become experts on Navan's business, product, and audience.
      - **Deliverables:**
          - "What We Heard" document summarizing this call.
          - Foundational artifacts: Writing Guidelines, Audience Personas, Company Context.
          - Initial content strategy with topic clusters.
          - Keyword research → 40+ topic ideas.
  - **Phase 2: Calibration & Scaling (Weeks 3–8)**
      - **Goal:** Align on content voice, tone, and depth before scaling production.
      - **Process:**
          - Produce one calibration blog for review.
          - Produce three additional calibration blogs.
          - Scale production to **5 articles per week** by week 4–5.
  - **Content Operations:**
      - **Review Process:** Navan will manage internal proofreading and legal review. Legal review is required for product claims or "number one" statements.
      - **Publishing:** GrowthX will publish directly to Navan's Contentful CMS.
      - **Content Hygiene:** GrowthX will conduct an audit to prevent cannibalization and identify refresh opportunities.
