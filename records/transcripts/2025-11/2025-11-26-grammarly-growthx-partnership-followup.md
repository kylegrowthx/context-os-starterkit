## Key Topics

### Opportunity Sizing and Content Audit

The team identified a large content opportunity by analyzing competitor keyword gaps and potential new pages.

- **Marcel Santilli presented an initial audit revealing about 4,000 potential new pages from just four competitors, covering roughly 13 million monthly searches across 1,600 easy-to-target pages**
    - This audit filters out irrelevant content and clusters keywords by pages to create actionable content assignments.
    - Marcel explained the approach focuses on keyword gaps where Grammarly currently ranks zero to avoid duplication.
    - Editors will prioritize assignments weekly based on relevance, audience needs, and quality considerations.
    - This sizing exercise is an early Total Addressable Market estimate, with further refinement planned.

- **Mark Pauley raised concerns about deduplication and potential overlap in content targeting similar keywords across competitors**
    - Marcel clarified that the clustering and editorial process mitigate this by consolidating overlapping opportunities.
    - Multiple prioritization criteria will balance keyword gaps, audience questions, and product-based jobs to be done.
    - Weekly content prioritization will evolve with client input to maintain focus and avoid redundant efforts.

### Content Production Capacity and Timeline

The discussion clarified realistic content production rates and timelines based on client needs and past client experiences.

- **Marcel Santilli estimated that producing all 4,000 pages would depend on prioritization, quality standards, and content types, explaining throughput as a tradeoff between quality and speed**
    - He stressed that quality is layered, with iterative improvements driven by real user engagement signals.
    - Focusing on fewer content types initially allows workflow optimization and faster output scaling.
    - Past clients like Augment Code achieved roughly 200 pages in four months with strong organic results but experienced plateau phases requiring content enrichment.
    - The approach emphasizes collaboration to maximize output with available resources rather than blindly chasing volume.

- **Tyler Pavlas added the typical editorial target is five articles per week per work stream but proposed doubling this to 10 articles per week for initial scope**
    - Editorial work will focus on refreshing topical content and competitor comparisons.
    - Programmatic content scaling depends on developing templates and workflows, enabling hundreds or thousands of pages once ready.
    - Initial strategy sprints will clarify programmatic feasibility and execution scope.

- **Jess Mordo highlighted that different topical areas and multiple websites require tailored review levels and competitor sets, adding complexity to scaling**
    - Marcel provided an example with Engine.com, showing how audience personas and content types are grouped to manage complexity.
    - Each content group requires calibration before expanding to new groups to avoid spreading resources too thin.
    - Artifacts like writing guidelines can be forked for each persona or content type but increase complexity in maintenance.
    - Pipelines are configured per group and content type, balancing scalability with operational complexity.
