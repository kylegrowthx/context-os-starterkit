## Key Topics

### Client Context & Goals

  - **Superhuman:** Current contract is $12k/mo for 100k words/mo (\~10 articles/week), renewing Feb 18.
  - **Grammarly:** Wants to expand content efforts, providing specific use cases:
      - **Editorial:** "How to revise your writing," "What is AGI?"
      - **Programmatic:** "Guide to commonly confused words" (dictionary-style pages).
  - **Combined Goal:** Secure a single contract for both teams, with separate budgets.

### Proposed Solution: Editorial-First Approach

  - **Price:** $12k/mo, matching the Superhuman contract to anchor the deal.
  - **Scope:** Editorial-only, with a flexible mix of net-new articles and targeted refreshes.
      - **Rationale:** This structure is a win-win: it secures the renewal and addresses Joe's feedback that the current 10 articles/week commitment is a high volume.
  - **Programmatic Content:** Defer until later.
      - **Rationale:** Their tools appear to be custom dev builds, and we lack a clear path for API or data integration—a prerequisite for scalable programmatic work.
      - **Alternative:** Offer a $25k/mo bundle (editorial + programmatic) but actively discourage it, as it's premature without a clear integration strategy.

### Supporting Rationale: "Lovable FOMO"

  - **Tactic:** Leverage our success with Lovable AI as a proof point.
  - **Method:** Present a mini-strategy deck modeled on the Lovable proposal.
      - **Content:** A content gap analysis and competitor research (e.g., Copy.ai) to identify high-traffic clusters and justify our proposed scope.
