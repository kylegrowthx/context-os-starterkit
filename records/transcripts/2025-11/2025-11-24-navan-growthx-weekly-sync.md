## Key Topics

### Content Strategy Framework

  - GrowthX presented a draft strategy using a hub-and-spoke model to target decision-makers.
  - The framework has three main clusters:
    1.  **Persona-Based:** 7 subclusters targeting specific roles (CFOs, Procurement, etc.).
    2.  **Competitive Comparisons:** Blog posts and buyer guides.
    3.  **AI-Powered T\&E:** Thought leadership on Navan's AI agent.

### Strategic Feedback & Refinements

  - **Travel vs. Expense Balance:**
      - **Problem:** The initial persona examples were too expense-heavy.
      - **Rationale:** The strategy must reflect Navan's "land with travel, expand with expense" model. Finance leaders also experience travel pain points directly and own travel policy.
      - **Action:** Balance keywords to include more travel-related topics for all personas.
  - **"White Glove" Support:**
      - **Problem:** The draft strategy missed this key differentiator.
      - **Rationale:** Premium support is a core value for senior decision-makers.
      - **Action:** Add a content pillar on support. Research end-user pain points (e.g., flight changes) and audit competitor Reed & Mackay's keywords for directional guidance.
  - **Competitive Content Strategy:**
      - **Approach:** Use a mixed strategy for comparisons.
          - **Official Pages:** For strategic competitors.
          - **Blog Posts:** For long-tail keywords and reverse comparisons (e.g., "Spendesk vs. Navan") to capture more search territory and improve LLM readability.
      - **Conversion:** Blog posts must include strong CTAs (e.g., backlinks to comparison pages) to drive leads.
  - **Content Refresh & SEO:**
      - **Process:** Navan will identify existing content for refresh. GrowthX will then optimize those pieces by:
          - Closing content gaps vs. top-ranking pages.
          - Adding proprietary data for recency signals.
          - Adding LLM-friendly elements (FAQs, TLDR summaries).
          - Updating internal links and messaging.
