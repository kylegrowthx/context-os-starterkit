## Key Topics

### Nexla's Content Challenges

  - **Low Traffic & High Churn:** Past SEO content drove traffic but failed to convert visitors into repeat users.
  - **Uncertain Strategy:** The team is unsure which topics to prioritize for AI discovery, as current in-house blogs are written for a specific point of view, not search intent.
  - **Content Gaps:**
      - **Blogs:** Not structured for AI engines.
      - **Connectors:** Technical docs are too deep for top-of-funnel users searching for solutions (e.g., "SAP connector").
  - **Limited Bandwidth:** The small team (no product marketer) cannot scale content creation for 500+ connectors and daily releases.

### growthx.ai's Proposed Solution

  - **Phase 1: 8-Week Strategy Sprint ($30k)**
      - **Goal:** A proof-of-concept to align on quality and strategy before committing to a long-term contract.
      - **Process:**
          - **Weeks 1–2:** Create "artifacts" (knowledge base) from Nexla's resources (decks, docs) to enable technical writing.
          - **Weeks 3–4:** Publish 2–3 "calibration articles" to align on style and subject matter.
          - **Weeks 4–8:** Publish 5–10 articles to demonstrate value.
      - **Commitment:** \~2.5 hours/week from Nexla (kickoff, product deep dive, weekly sync).
  - **Phase 2: 12-Month Growth Execution ($15k/month)**
      - **Goal:** Scale content production to drive AI discoverability.
      - **Cadence:** \~3 articles/week, adjusted for technical review cycles.
      - **Scope Flexibility:** The monthly fee is fixed; the focus can be swapped between:
          - **Editorial:** Standard blog posts and guides.
          - **Programmatic:** High-volume, templated pages (e.g., for connectors).
      - **Reporting:** Custom Looker dashboard and a "Scrunch" dashboard to track AI citation performance against competitors.

### Programmatic Content for Connectors

  - **Opportunity:** Create high-level, SEO-friendly pages for each of Nexla's 500+ connectors.
  - **Rationale:** This captures top-of-funnel search intent (e.g., "SAP connector") that current technical docs miss, driving awareness and traffic.
  - **Example:** The Ramp "spender directory," where growthx.ai created a page for every vendor using internal data.
  - **Process:**
      - Leverages existing technical docs and product team input.
      - Requires an initial editorial phase to build context before scaling.
