## Summary

Tyler Pavlas (GrowthX) and Marylise Tauzia (Baseten) concluded their partnership after Baseten paused the engagement due to concerns about LLM-generated content quality and brand risk. Baseten's internal team rejected the initial content outline as insufficiently technical, and Marylise decided the partnership would only work if Baseten could hire deeply technical writers (engineers or ghost writers with deep product expertise) rather than relying on scalable LLM-based content. GrowthX remains open to future collaboration on lower-risk use cases like glossaries, landing pages, or programmatic content, and Tyler introduced a new process of requiring 2-5 content examples upfront to align expectations.

---

## Context

Baseten is an AI infrastructure company focused on engineering excellence and open-source contributions. GrowthX engaged with Marylise Tauzia (Head of Growth/Marketing at Baseten) to create scalable SEO content that would rank in LLM-based AI visibility indices and organic search. The initial engagement was treated as an experiment, but Baseten's engineering-focused culture and brand positioning made LLM-generated content a strategic misalignment. After the first outline disappointed the internal team, Marylise decided to pause the partnership and pursue a different approach focused on deeply technical writers who understand Baseten's products at an expert level.

- Demonstrates the market segment of highly technical companies willing to invest in quality over volume but unwilling to use pure LLM-generated content.

## Key Topics

### Partnership Paused Due to Brand Risk

  - Baseten paused the partnership after an initial LLM-generated outline failed to meet its high technical standards, causing internal team concern.
  - **Rationale:** The team felt the outline was "so off" it risked damaging the Baseten brand, which is built on engineering excellence and open-source contributions.
  - **Core Conflict:** Balancing the SEO/LLM traffic benefits of scalable content against the risk of publishing misinformation ("hallucinations").
  - **Outcome:** Baseten concluded the risk was too high and paused the engagement.

### Baseten's New Content Strategy

  - Baseten will now pursue a strategy of using deeply technical writers to ensure content quality.
  - **Writer Profile:** Must be deeply technical and understand Baseten's products.
  - **Source Options:**
      - **Internal:** Engineers or the Head of DevRel (a published author).
      - **External:** Ghostwriters with deep technical expertise.
  - **Goal:** Prioritize quality over volume, focusing on a few high-impact articles (e.g., GPU comparisons) that have previously performed well.

### Potential Future Collaboration

  - GrowthX remains open to future collaboration on specific, lower-risk use cases.
  - **Suggested Use Cases:**
      - Landing pages
      - Programmatic content
      - Glossaries
  - **GrowthX Process Improvement:** Now requires 2–5 content examples upfront to set realistic expectations and align on what can be scaled vs. what needs expert creation.

---

## Action Items

- **Baseten:** Pursue the new content strategy using deeply technical writers.
- **GrowthX:** Remain open to future collaboration on specific use cases.
