## Key Topics

### Content Strategy: Goals & Analysis

  - **Context:** Vizcom is starting its SEO content from scratch, with the current blog focused on product updates and company news.
  - **Primary Goals:**
      - Balance Vizcom's perception across verticals (beyond just automotive/footwear).
      - Drive measurable visibility via rankings and AI discoverability (measured with Looker & Scrunch).
      - Increase conversions from content → signups → paid users.
  - **Competitor Analysis:**
      - **Architecture/Manufacturing:** AutoCAD, Autodesk, SketchUp, Chaos.
      - **Fashion:** Newark, Newblack (blogs noted as low quality).
      - **High-Quality Example:** Chaos's blog, which blends foundational education with AI trends.

### Content Strategy: Proposed Clusters

  - **1. Product Rendering Techniques & Technology:**
      - **Focus:** Technical aspects and output quality of rendering.
      - **Goal:** Capture designers researching rendering methods and software.
      - **Tactic:** Use listicles (e.g., "best rendering software") to rank quickly in LLM responses.
  - **2. Sketch-to-3D Workflows & Design Iteration:**
      - **Focus:** Vizcom's unique multimodal input and rapid prototyping.
      - **Goal:** Position Vizcom as the solution for efficient design exploration.
      - **Tactic:** Mix high-volume educational topics with low-volume, high-intent keywords.
  - **3. Industry-Specific Design Solutions:**
      - **Focus:** Use cases across verticals (automotive, footwear, apparel, product design).
      - **Goal:** Demonstrate Vizcom's value in diverse industries.
      - **Tactic:** Expand the list of covered verticals as needed.
  - **4. Optimizing Design Workflows:**
      - **Focus:** Productivity, collaboration, and business value.
      - **Goal:** Address buyer concerns about efficiency and ROI.
  - **5. AI Tool Adoption & Prompt Engineering for Designers:**
      - **Focus:** Strategic use of AI in design, from tool selection to prompt writing.
      - **Goal:** Capture traffic for informational queries and commercial intent (e.g., "Midjourney alternatives").

### Content Generation Workflow: "Agentic Pipeline"

  - Aida demoed the "agentic pipeline" in the Atlas workspace, which uses AI agents to self-correct and ensure quality.
  - **Process:**
    1.  **Manual Outline:** GrowthX creates a detailed outline based on top-ranking content to ensure comprehensive, rank-worthy structure.
    2.  **Research:** An agent uses Tavoli & EXA (replacing Perplexity for higher quality) to find sources, then grades its own research and re-searches if needed.
    3.  **Drafting:** An agent writes the article, pulling from the research and Vizcom's artifacts (company brief, personas, writing guidelines).
    4.  **Self-Correction:** The agent grades the draft against writing guidelines and a proofreader checklist, then automatically revises to fix errors.
    5.  **Manual Review:** A human editor performs a final check for accuracy and adds internal/source links.
    6.  **Image Generation:** An in-house designer creates featured images using Vizcom's brand guidelines.
