## Summary

Tyler Pavlas introduced GrowthX's content-driven approach to AI search visibility (GEO) to Hugo Fredon, Prospeo's business development lead. Prospeo is pursuing an aggressive programmatic SEO strategy to build a 100M+ page directory targeting high-intent, long-tail search (e.g., "first name last name"), with the goal of capturing lead generation traffic like RocketReach does. However, Hugo made clear that Prospeo will not create editorial content because it would be outranked by the programmatic pages and damage the site's SEO profile. GrowthX's model—which relies on creating 5+ editorial pieces per week to feed AI search visibility—proved incompatible with Prospeo's content-free strategy, so both parties agreed there is no current partnership fit, though Tyler offered to reconnect when GrowthX launches its dedicated AI visibility product next year.

---

## Context

Prospeo is a B2B lead intelligence and contact enrichment platform that pivots toward lead generation with its upcoming v2 product. Hugo Fredon, a business development leader at Prospeo, was introduced to GrowthX by a mutual contact (Alex) to explore potential partnership around AI search visibility. Prospeo previously had a blog generating meaningful traffic but removed it due to trademark/LinkedIn legal issues and because programmatic SEO at scale is incompatible with editorial content strategy. The company is betting heavily on a RocketReach-style model: building and ranking a massive directory of people and company pages to capture searches for names and basic information, a strategy they believe can generate $100M+ in annual revenue.

## Overview

- **Prospeo's Strategy:** A hyper-focused programmatic SEO play (100M+ pages) to capture high-intent, long-tail search traffic (e.g., "first name last name").
- **GrowthX's Model:** An end-to-end content agency where AI search visibility (GEO) is a direct outcome of creating high-quality editorial content at scale.
- **Strategic Mismatch:** Prospeo's strategy explicitly avoids editorial content, as it would be outranked by the programmatic pages and negatively impact Google's perception of the site.
- **Outcome:** No partnership. GrowthX's model is incompatible with Prospeo's content-free strategy.

## Key Topics

### Prospeo's Strategy: Programmatic SEO

  - Prospeo is launching v2 of its product, shifting from contact enrichment to lead generation.
  - The core marketing strategy is programmatic SEO:
      - **Goal:** Build a directory of 100M+ people/companies.
      - **Rationale:** Capture high-intent, long-tail search traffic (e.g., "first name last name").
      - **Model:** Emulates companies like RocketReach, which generates \~$100M/year from this strategy.
  - **Content Rationale:** Prospeo removed its blog after legal issues with LinkedIn and will not create new editorial content.
      - **Why:** Editorial content would be outranked by the programmatic pages and negatively impact Google's perception of the site.
      - **Execution:** The programmatic SEO build is handled in-house by a specialized team.

### GrowthX's Model: Content-Driven GEO

  - GrowthX is an end-to-end content agency that integrates AI search visibility (GEO) with traditional SEO.
  - **Process:** An 8-week strategy sprint followed by ongoing content creation (\~5 pieces/week).
  - **Core Principle:** GEO is a direct outcome of creating high-quality editorial content at scale.
  - **Team:** Founded by Marcel (ex-HashiCorp, Scale.ai, Deepgram) and includes experts like a former TechCrunch editor-in-chief.
  - **Platform:** Uses a proprietary platform to manage workflows, with a client-facing version launching early next year.

### The Strategic Mismatch

  - Prospeo's need was for AI search visibility *without* creating editorial content.
  - GrowthX's model is built on content creation, making it an incompatible fit.
  - **Prospeo's View:** Creating content would be a waste of money, as it would be outranked and negatively impact the programmatic strategy.
  - **GrowthX's View:** Content is a necessary input for AI models to surface information about a brand.
