## Context

Jama Software is a requirements and compliance management platform serving systems engineers and technical product teams. Fani Yaneva, who leads content strategy at Jama, was introduced to GrowthX through XFunnel and initiated this call to explore content partnership options. Jama currently produces 6–8 blogs per quarter alongside other collateral (white papers, e-books, product documentation) but wants to significantly increase content output focused on SEO and AEO visibility. The core challenge is that Jama's highly technical audience (systems engineers) requires specialized expertise to create credible content, and retraining internal writers takes 6–12 months. ## Key Topics

### Jama's Content Needs

  - **Primary Goal:** Content creation for SEO and AEO (Answer Engine Optimization) to increase visibility in LLM overviews.
  - **Core Challenge:** Scaling quality technical content for a discerning audience (systems engineers) is difficult and time-consuming (6–12 month writer training).
  - **Current Output:** 6–8 blogs per quarter, plus other collateral.
  - **Desired Partner Profile:**
      - Specialized expertise for immediate impact.
      - Scalability to increase content volume.
      - Full execution capabilities (strategy → CMS publishing).

### GrowthX's Service Model

  - **End-to-End Partnership:** Manages the full content lifecycle: strategy, creation, execution, and analytics.
  - **Execution:** Publishes daily to solve for the long tail of AI search.
  - **Team as an Extension:** Frees up internal resources for pillar content (e.g., white papers).
  - **Technical Expertise:**
      - **Team:** Led by Matthew Panzarino (ex-TechCrunch EIC); client-facing staff have deep SEO/content backgrounds.
      - **Process:** Uses a hybrid AI/human workflow.
          - **AI:** Generates briefs and drafts using client artifacts (personas, brand guides).
          - **Human:** Editors provide commands and make final optimizations.
      - **Proof:** Work for technical clients like Abnormal Security (glossary), Augment Code (\~200 guides), and Ramp (900 pages).

### Engagement Structure & Logistics

  - **Phase 1: Strategy Sprint (8 weeks)**
      - **Goal:** Validate fit and quality before a long-term commitment.
      - **Deliverables:** Keyword analysis, content roadmap, and 5–10 published articles.
      - **Cadence:** Weekly meetings.
      - **Decision Point:** Jama can opt out at the end of this phase.
  - **Phase 2: Growth Execution (12 months)**
      - **Goal:** Execute the content roadmap at scale.
      - **Cadence:** Bi-weekly meetings to review performance and strategy.
      - **Reporting:** Looker dashboard for metrics; Scrunch (or GrowthX's upcoming platform) for LLM prompt tracking.
  - **Execution Details:**
      - **CMS Publishing:** GrowthX handles publishing to WordPress at no extra cost, provided a template exists.
      - **Pricing:** Flat fee for Phase 1, then a monthly fee for Phase 2. Limited negotiation room due to high demand.
      - **Client Focus:** GrowthX limits kickoffs to two per week to ensure dedicated attention and high churn retention.
