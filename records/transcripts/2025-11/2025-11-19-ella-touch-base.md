## Summary

Ella Dillon, an operations and customer success consultant, met with George Haikal to understand GrowthX's operational scaling challenges as the delivery team has grown to 55 people. The core issue is that Strategy Sprints work well for de-risking early engagements but lack execution rigor and integration with post-sprint delivery, leading to cases like LightSource churn and Bubble conversion failure. George outlined two systemic problems: siloed knowledge prevents delivery teams from using engineering tools (e.g., AI visibility workflows), and leadership meetings focus on activity reporting rather than goal-driven strategy. Ella will interview the broader team and help design a scalable operating model across the full customer journey (sales through retention) to support upcoming product launches and the goal of scaling beyond 100 clients.

## Context

GrowthX is a B2B content marketing services company that has recently scaled to 55 delivery staff and is preparing for rapid growth beyond 100 clients through upcoming product launches. George Haikal is the Chief of Staff and a key strategic lead (alongside Marcel Santilli, founder) who has been with GrowthX for 5 months. Ella Dillon is an experienced operations consultant with a background in building customer success and operational frameworks at companies like AT&T, DocuSign, and Outreach. She's been brought in by Marcel and Bridget to audit and redesign GrowthX's operational model.

The immediate backdrop is that GrowthX has built an 8-week Strategy Sprint process to de-risk new engagements by securing upfront alignment and access with clients, but the process breaks down in execution. Real examples include LightSource churning after content got stuck in legal review, and Bubble failing to convert because a promised technical SEO audit wasn't delivered. Meanwhile, George is split between fire-fighting operational gaps and strategic projects, with roughly 40% of his time on Chief of Staff duties, 50% on strategic initiatives like the account health system he built with Bridget, and 10% managing the custom Lovable account. Ella's role is to understand where the system leaks, where knowledge silos prevent teams from leveraging existing tools, and how to design processes that scale.

## Key Topics

### The Problem: Scaling Pains

  - **Context:** GrowthX's rapid growth to 55 delivery staff has outpaced its operational model, creating bottlenecks and inefficiencies.
  - **Ella's Role:** Build a scalable operating model for the full customer journey (sales → delivery → retention) to support the upcoming product launches and the goal of scaling beyond 100 clients.

### The Solution: Strategy Sprints

  - **Purpose:** A mandatory 8-week sprint to de-risk engagements and improve downstream execution by securing upfront alignment and access.
  - **Process:**
      - **ICP Matrix:** A scoreable matrix qualifies clients on factors like trust, long-term buy-in, and a streamlined approval process.
      - **Sales Handoff:** A detailed doc transfers context from sales to the sprint team.
      - **Kickoff Call:** A 1-hour deep dive with key stakeholders to define goals and identify knowledge gaps.
  - **Ownership:** Andy and Ada now manage the sprint process, which includes daily standups and Linear tracking.

### The Gaps: Where Sprints Fail

  - **Execution Failures:**
      - **LightSource (Churned):** Churned due to a content backlog stuck in legal review, blocking publication and preventing signals of success.
      - **Bubble (Failed Conversion):** Failed to convert after the sprint because a promised technical SEO audit was not delivered.
  - **Root Cause:** The sprint process lacks operational rigor to track specific SoW milestones and proactively flag risks.

### The New System: Account Health Tracking

  - **Purpose:** A new system to track account health and director activity, built by George and Bridget.
  - **Components:**
      - **Notion Dashboard:** Directors score accounts weekly (out of 25) and flag health status.
      - **Linear Tickets:** All director work is formalized into tickets tied to accounts and specific health metrics.
  - **Limitation:** The system is manual and not integrated with the Strategy Sprint process, limiting its ability to proactively flag issues early in the engagement.

### The Pain Points: Systemic Issues

  - **1. Context Sharing:** Siloed knowledge prevents delivery teams from using efficiency tools built by engineering.
      - **Example:** Delivery teams were manually creating AI visibility prompts, unaware of a pre-built workflow from Daniel (CTO) that could automate the task.
  - **2. Leadership Focus:** Leadership meetings are activity-based reports, not goal-driven strategy sessions.
      - **Impact:** This creates a top-down prioritization issue that George must manually correct.

### George's Ideal Role

  - **Preference:** Strategic, project-based work (e.g., fixing kickoffs, developing a scalable platform model) over repetitive process management.
  - **Current Time Allocation:**
      - **10%:** Managing the custom Lovable account.
      - **\~50%:** Strategic projects (e.g., building the account health system).
      - **\~40%:** CoS duties (e.g., managing EAs, prioritizing Marcel's requests).
