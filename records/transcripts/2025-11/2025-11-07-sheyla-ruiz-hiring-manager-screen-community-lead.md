## Overview

- **The Community as a Product:** The "AI-Led Growth" community is a standalone product, not a marketing channel. Its success is measured by member count (goal: 1k by EOY), with retention as the key value signal.
- **Problem: Passive Consumption:** The community is a passive content library. Members join for gated workshop recordings, not for interaction, resulting in low engagement and an undefined user journey.
- **Proposed Solution: Active Participation:** Shift from consumption to creation. Design a user journey that requires members to apply learnings (e.g., post content for feedback), creating a virtuous cycle of value and engagement.
- **Sheyla's Experience:** Sheyla's work at Monarch Money (financial literacy) and Couchbase (developer tools) shows a track record of building communities with distinct, audience-specific value propositions.

## Key Topics

### Problem: Passive Community & Unclear Value

  - The "AI-Led Growth" community (AIledgrowth.com) has 70 paying members ($150 upfront + $30/mo).
  - **Primary Use Case:** Members join to access gated workshop recordings.
  - **Result:** Low engagement. The community lacks a defined user journey or a clear path for members to apply learnings, making it a passive content library.
  - **Measurement Gap:** No formal engagement metrics exist. Monthly churn is the only current signal of value.

### Context: GrowthX Agency & Community Origin

  - **GrowthX Agency:** Builds AI-driven content systems for clients (e.g., Webflow, Ramp), publishing 30–40 articles/week.
  - **Community Origin:** Grew from marketing the agency's process. High demand for workshops (500–1k registrants) led to paid access, proving the value of the knowledge itself.
  - **Differentiator:** The community's value comes from sharing real-world methods from the agency's work, not from pitching a tool.

### Solution: Design for Active Participation

  - **Core Principle:** A community needs an independent value prop, not just product support.
      - **Example (Monarch Money):** The community's value is financial literacy and peer support, not just using the app.
  - **Proposed User Journey:**
      - **Step 1: Consume Content** → Watch a workshop or course.
      - **Step 2: Apply Learning** → Complete a task based on the content.
      - **Step 3: Share Output** → Post the result in the community for feedback.
  - **Goal:** Shift from passive consumption to active creation and peer interaction.
