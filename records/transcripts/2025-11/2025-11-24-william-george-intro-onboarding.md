## Key Topics

### Company Context & Reorg

  - **Problem:** The previous structure spread Director expertise too thin across multiple accounts, limiting strategic impact and causing team burnout.
  - **Solution:** A new model separates roles to maximize impact:
      - **Engagement Managers (EMs):** Own client relationships, project management, and weekly calls.
      - **Directors (Pooled Resource):** Provide high-level strategy on demand via a Linear ticketing system.
      - **Content Managers:** Generate content end-to-end in Atlas, including edits.

### William's Role & Strategic Focus

  - **Primary Focus:** Support the Strategy Sprint process, addressing a capacity bottleneck.
  - **Key Project:** Define clear exit criteria for the 8-week sprint.
      - **Goal:** Ensure clients are a good long-term fit and that GrowthX can deliver value before graduating them to a full contract.
      - **Rationale:** This formalizes the sprint's function as a mutual opt-in/opt-out period.

### The Strategy Sprint Process

  - **Function:** A paid, 8-week engagement (\~$20k avg) that acts as a mutual pilot to validate client fit and calibrate content quality.
  - **Phase 1: Kickoff & Discovery (Weeks 1–4)**
      - A 1-hour kickoff call uses 13 deep questions to assess client goals, ICP knowledge, and stakeholder buy-in.
      - **Deliverables:**
          - **Artifacts:** Personas, customer stories, and product deep-dives created in Atlas.
          - **Content OS:** An Airtable presenting a full content strategy (competitive analysis, keywords, clusters) for client approval.
          - **Calibration Article:** A test piece generated in Atlas to align on voice, tone, and style.
  - **Phase 2: Production Ramp-Up (Weeks 5–8)**
      - After calibration, the team ramps up to the contract's weekly publishing cadence (e.g., 5 articles/week).
      - **Outcome:** A successful sprint leads to a long-term contract; an unsuccessful one allows either party to walk away.

### Atlas Platform & SaaS Vision

  - **Atlas:** GrowthX's proprietary platform, built to fix pain points experienced with tools like AeroOps.
  - **Key Features:**
      - **Granular Context:** Stores detailed client artifacts (personas, product info) to ensure high-quality, on-brand content.
      - **Agentic Workflows:** Uses multi-step pipelines (e.g., Research, Brief Generation) where agents create and execute their own success criteria.
  - **SaaS Vision:** Transition to a hybrid model by Jan/Feb.
      - **Process:** GrowthX performs the initial high-touch setup (kickoff, artifacts, strategy) in Atlas.
      - **Outcome:** The client then takes over for self-serve content generation.
