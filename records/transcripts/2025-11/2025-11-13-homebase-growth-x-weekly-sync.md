## Key Topics

### Glossary Performance & Problem

  - Glossary traffic is flatlining, with some pages seeing significant drops.
  - **Primary Cause:** Google's AI Overviews are pushing informational content down the SERP.
  - **Secondary Hypotheses:**
      - **Internal Linking:** Inconsistent linking may be hindering Google's ability to crawl and re-index pages.
      - **Content Quality:** "Fine" content gets an initial boost from Homebase's high Domain Authority (DR) but then tanks when it fails to outperform competitors.
      - **Content Cannibalization:** Multiple pages targeting the same keyword may be causing them to compete against each other.

### New Optimization Strategy

  - The strategy will focus on two page groups, prioritized for their higher potential ROI:
    1.  **Pages with recent traffic drops:** These pages already ranked, so a refresh is more likely to restore performance.
    2.  **Underperforming pages:** Published before June 2025 with \<5 clicks/month.
  - **Tactics:**
      - **Refresh Content:** Add new information and resubmit to GSC for re-indexing.
      - **Add Publish Dates:** This leverages the "freshness" signal, which Google's LMs are now using as a key ranking proxy.
      - **Improve Internal Linking:** A Screaming Frog crawl will provide accurate link data, replacing manual analysis.
      - **Prune Low-Value Content:** Removing underperforming pages can improve the overall "working content" ratio, signaling higher quality to Google.

### Editorial Updates

  - **TLDRs:** The final 35 TLDRs will be completed this week.
  - **Question Center:** Two examples are ready for review.
      - **Goal:** Replace the TLDR cadence with Question Centers.
      - **Process:** Homebase team provides async feedback → GrowthX refines the pipeline → Production begins.
