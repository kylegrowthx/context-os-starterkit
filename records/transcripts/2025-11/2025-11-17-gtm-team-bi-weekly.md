## Key Topics

### GTM Meeting Restructure

  - **Problem:** Weekly GTM meetings have become inefficient status updates for a growing team.
  - **Solution:** Shift to a bi-weekly cadence focused on strategic discussion.
      - **New Cadence:** Weekly syncs for individual lanes (Events, Content, Community) will handle day-to-day updates.
      - **New Goal:** This bi-weekly meeting will focus on surfacing issues, making decisions, and aligning on cross-lane strategy.
      - **New Process:** Pre-read notes and flag discussion topics the day before to maximize meeting time.

### Community & Content Strategy

  - **Community Launch Success:**
      - **Members:** \~150 paying members.
      - **Revenue:** Comparable to a client, with high scalability.
      - **Engagement:** Active content consumption and discussions.
  - **Strategic Direction:**
      - **Goal:** Evolve the community into a content-heavy, premium destination (inspired by Reforged, Maven).
      - **Anchor Content:** A signature course is the top priority to provide core value.
          - **Delivery:** Test a live cohort format for the first offering, with a goal of eventually charging $500–$1,000.
      - **Website Feed:** The public feed was hidden due to low-value posts. The site will be redesigned to better showcase high-value content.
  - **Podcast Systemization:**
      - **Problem:** The current ad-hoc process requires heavy post-production and lacks a consistent cadence.
      - **Solution:** Formalize into two distinct formats to improve consistency and reduce prep time.
          - **Format 1 (High-Profile):** In-studio interviews with major figures (e.g., Anna, Gene Konto).
          - **Format 2 (Practical):** Virtual workshops with domain experts (e.g., Caleb on LinkedIn branding).
      - **Process:** Will and Jesus will manage pre-production (scheduling, prep materials) to minimize host involvement.

### Events & Holiday Gifting

  - **Upcoming Events:**
      - **CMO Dinner (This Wednesday):** 22 registrations, including execs from Kramerly, Deep Kram, Cursor, and Hex.
      - **CMO Breakfast (Dec 4):** Promotion launched today; outreach is segmented by Luma status (email vs. LinkedIn).
      - **Lovable Workshop:** A final promotional push is planned next week before the holidays.
  - **Event Roles & Responsibilities:**
      - **Casey Event:** Content team will define the program → Gloria manages logistics → Understory provides project management.
      - **Client Retention:** Delivery directors must provide lists of key clients for event invites.
  - **Holiday Gifting Program:**
      - **Problem:** The current manual process is inefficient and doesn't leverage delivery directors' client knowledge.
      - **Solution:** Test Gilded Box to automate logistics and empower directors.
          - **Process:** Directors select a gift box for a client contact → Gilded Box handles shipping.
          - **Budget:** Propose a $85–$120 range for most clients, with a special tier for key accounts (e.g., Surge, Augment).

### Demand Gen & Ops

  - **Audience Segmentation:**
      - **Status:** In progress. Kyle is building canonical segments (e.g., "Customers," "Bay Area") to enable turnkey event targeting.
      - **Blocker:** Requires complete HubSpot tagging, which is delayed by a delivery team restructuring.
  - **Tech Stack & Automation:**
      - **Zoom Webinar:** Upgrade to a team account to consolidate separate accounts (Jason's, Marcel's) and enable the EventHapply integration for automated data sync.
      - **Luma → HubSpot Sync:** Automate data transfer from Luma to HubSpot (currently a manual CSV upload) once the Zoom integration is complete.
