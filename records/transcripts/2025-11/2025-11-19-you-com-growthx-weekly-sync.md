## Key Topics

### Content Production Workflow

  - **Problem:** The 5 articles/week cadence was questioned, as the initial project scope anticipated a 3x production increase from an AI workflow.
  - **Solution:** The cadence is a quality-focused decision. The workflow is human-led to prevent the shallow, repetitive content that pure AI generation often produces.
  - **Workflow Breakdown:**
    1.  **Ideation & Outline (Human):** A human editor researches top-ranking content to create a detailed, SEO-optimized outline. This is a critical step, as a strong outline simplifies all subsequent editing.
    2.  **Assignment Brief (AI):** The workflow generates a brief using the SEMrush API for keyword analysis and content structure.
    3.  **Research (AI):** The AI gathers information to answer questions from the brief. This step is being upgraded to an agentic researcher that self-evaluates for information gaps.
    4.  **Drafting & Fact-Checking (AI):** The AI writes the draft, which is immediately run through a fact-checker. This tool verifies claims against the research document and rewrites unsupported statements (e.g., changing "50% of enterprises use X" to "Industry experts suggest...").
    5.  **Human Edit:** A human editor reads the entire draft to ensure flow, verify stats, correct links, and add internal links.
    6.  **Style Validation (AI):** A new final step evaluates the draft against writing guidelines and a proofreading checklist, then automatically fixes any issues.

### LLM Performance Tracking

  - **Problem:** Confusion exists around measuring GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) and how it relates to SEO.
  - **Solution:** The strategy uses a dual approach to track LLM visibility.
      - **Scrunch:** A proxy tool that estimates search presence for target queries.
      - **Looker Dashboard:** Tracks direct LLM referral traffic from GA4, showing which LLMs and pages drive visits.
  - **Key Insight:** SEO is the foundation for LLM visibility. Data shows that for clients with large knowledge bases, the vast majority of LLM referral traffic goes to blog posts.
  - **Third-Party Sites:** The team discussed the role of sites like Reddit and LinkedIn in LLM citations. Erik will provide high-level thoughts on engaging these platforms.

### Looker Dashboard Walkthrough

  - Aida reviewed the Looker dashboard, which pulls data from GA4 and Google Search Console.
  - **Key Reports:**
      - **Traffic:** Overall vs. Engaged Sessions (duration \>10s, scroll, or click).
      - **Non-Branded Page:** GSC data for organic traffic from non-branded queries.
      - **Conversion Events:** GA4 data on custom conversion events.
      - **Cohort Report:** Will track performance of newly published articles.
      - **LLM Referral Traffic:** Shows top LLMs and landing pages driving traffic.
  - **Context:** The homepage's 70% engagement rate is likely inflated by logged-in users landing directly on the chat interface, which is tracked under the same URL (`u.com`).

### New AI Image Pipeline

  - GrowthX demoed a new AI image generation pipeline for blog illustrations.
  - **Feedback:** Brooke liked the style and will share examples with the You.com design team.
  - **Action:** Brooke will provide brand hex codes. Aida noted the AI can follow a general color palette but struggles with exact hex codes.
