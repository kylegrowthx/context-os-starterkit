## Context

Airbyte is a well-established data integration platform used by thousands of companies to move data between systems. However, the market is shifting: AI teams now control budgets while traditional data platform budgets are contracting. Airbyte's brand, while strong in the data platform space, isn't top-of-mind for AI engineers building agents. The company is launching a new product to capture this opportunity—a platform that provides agents with access to data across hundreds of APIs and databases (what Airbyte is calling "AirBike Context" as a working name). This is fundamentally a repositioning from a "data platform" to an "agenting data platform." GrowthX is running a full strategy sprint to define go-to-market positioning, content strategy, and marketing approach for this new product line.

## Key Topics

### Strategic Pivot to AI

  - **Problem:** AI teams now control budgets, while data platform budgets are shrinking. Airbyte's brand is not top-of-mind for AI data solutions.
  - **Insight:** AI engineers face data engineering problems (discovery, transformation) but lack the expertise to solve them.
  - **Solution:** Become the trusted data layer for AI agents, leveraging Airbyte's credibility with data teams.
  - **Positioning:** Coexist with the data platform, then merge into a single "agenting data platform."

### Product Roadmap & Vision

  - **Vision:** A platform enabling agents to discover, normalize, and interact with data across systems (e.g., Salesforce, Zendesk) via an SDK.
  - **Phased Launch Plan:**
    1.  **MCP Tool (Dec/Jan):** A local, open-source library for basic data fetch, search, and write.
    2.  **Full Platform (Post-MCP):** Adds raw data hosting, entity mapping, and ontology for advanced agent capabilities.
    3.  **Merge:** Integrate the MCP tool and full platform into a single product.

### Target Audience & Competitors

  - **Primary ICP:** AI engineers and lead software engineers at startups or enterprise incubator teams.
      - **Profile:** Move fast, prefer self-serve tooling, and want to avoid building data infrastructure.
  - **Secondary ICP:** Enterprise product/engineering teams building internal agents with data sovereignty needs.
  - **Key Competitors:**
      - **Nango:** Offers easy auth but lacks data movement/transformation capabilities.
      - **Merge.dev:** An integration platform, not a data platform.
      - **Red Panda, Composio.dev:** Recently pivoted to this space.
      - **Superbase:** Just launched an ETL feature.

### Content Strategy & Goals

  - **6-Month Goals:**
      - **DevRel:** 10,000 GitHub stars.
      - **Marketing:** 3x increase in Talk-to-Sales leads (from \~30/mo to 90+/mo).
  - **Content Focus:**
      - **Technical Altitude:** 80% MoF/BoF (technical deep dives, diagrams) to build credibility; 20% ToF (high-level concepts) for awareness.
      - **Channels:** Prioritize developer-centric channels (GitHub, meetups) over traditional SEO.
      - **Format:** Move beyond blogs to interactive templates, directories, and GitHub-based content.
  - **Founder Voice:** Leverage Michelle's brand to build a new audience.
      - **Topics:** Data infra for AI, build vs. buy, scaling AI agents, and the AI ecosystem.
      - **Method:** Use Airbyte's AI agent, trained on Michelle's content and performance data, for social posts.
