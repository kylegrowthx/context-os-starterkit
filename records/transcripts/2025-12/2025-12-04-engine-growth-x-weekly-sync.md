## Key Topics

### Content Performance & Strategy Validation

  - **New MoFu Content Outperforms ToFu:** Analysis of the "expense/financial management" and "industry-specific pain points" content bets confirms the strategy is working.
      - **Conversions:** 34x higher rate of product page visits vs. other business travel guides.
      - **Engagement:** 10x higher rate of product page visits; 74% engagement vs. 57% for other content.
  - **LLM Referrals Opportunity:** LLM traffic dipped in late Nov (likely holiday-related), but citations and position remain strong.
      - **Key Insight:** Scrutiny data shows 78% of Hotel Engine mentions go to third-party sources, not linking back to the site.
      - **Opportunity:** The FAQ glossary can capture this value by providing structured Q\&A content that LLMs can easily cite.

### FAQ Glossary Strategy & Execution

  - **Launch Plan:**
      - **Schema Markup:** Launch without it.
          - **Rationale:** Prioritize speed. Past tests were inconclusive, and on-page structure is considered more critical for SEO.
      - **Content Structure:** Use a non-collapsible, glossary-like format with Q\&A pairs.
          - **Rationale:** This structure is designed for findability by LLMs and avoids JavaScript dependencies that can hinder crawling.
      - **Batching:** Publish in batches (2 glossary pages ≈ 1 article) to build critical mass quickly.
  - **Content Creation:**
      - **First Draft:** A \~500-word glossary page is in the review queue to align on format and tone.
      - **Prioritization:** Use sales call transcripts to identify high-frequency questions and validate topics sourced from Reddit.

### Q1 Strategic Planning

  - **Roadmap:** The current content bets have a 4+ month runway. A Q1 strategy session will reassess priorities and scope new projects.
  - **Potential Q1 Projects:**
      - **Business Credit Card Content:** Overhaul existing content and create new pieces for the Hotel Engine card.
      - **Programmatic Travel Pages:** Revisit a past initiative that failed.
          - **Challenge:** The market is highly competitive.
          - **Opportunity:** Build a unique workflow to succeed where past attempts failed.
