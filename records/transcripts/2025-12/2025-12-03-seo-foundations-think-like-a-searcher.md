## Key Topics

### SEO's Evolution: From Keywords to Intent

  - SEO has evolved from simple keyword matching to understanding complex user intent.
  - **Key Historical Shifts:**
      - **Early 2000s:** Keyword-focused; black-hat tactics like keyword stuffing were common.
      - **2007–2014 (Content Marketing):** Focus shifted to content quality and context.
      - **2015–2022 (Hub & Spoke):** Topic clusters and answering "People Also Ask" questions became standard.
      - **Today (Quality & Intent):** Google prioritizes deep expertise and original data, driven by its E-E-A-T framework.

### The New Searcher & AI-Enhanced Optimization (AEO)

  - Users now interact with AI agents (e.g., Alexa, Siri) via voice, not just text.
  - **Impact on SEO:** AI agents still process traditional SEO signals (proximity, delivery, hours) to generate answers.
  - **AEO Principles:**
      - **Format for AI Parsing:** Use clean structures, definitions, comparisons, and FAQs.
      - **Prioritize Clarity:** Direct first sentences and simple language are key.
      - **Reverse-Engineer:** Use AI to analyze what it cites.
        1.  Ask an AI agent a question.
        2.  Analyze the cited sources for structure, content, and format.
        3.  Replicate successful patterns in your own content.

### The 4 Cs Content Framework

  - A framework for creating content that aligns with modern SEO and AEO.
  - **1. Craving:** Understand user intent (Informational, Navigational, Commercial, Transactional).
  - **2. Competition:** Analyze SERP results to identify content gaps and differentiation opportunities.
  - **3. Composition:** Generate content in Atlas, applying AEO principles (e.g., FAQs, tables).
  - **4. Conversion:** Ensure content is actionable with clear CTAs, lead magnets, and internal/external links.

### Practical Workflow: SEMrush → Airtable → Atlas

  - A live demo showed the standard process for keyword research and content generation.
  - **1. Keyword Research (SEMrush):**
      - Use the Keyword Magic Tool to find keywords with high volume and low difficulty.
      - Use the Keyword Gap tool to find keywords competitors rank for that you don't.
  - **2. Import to Airtable:**
      - Export selected keywords from SEMrush as a CSV.
      - Import the CSV into the client's Content OS in Airtable, mapping all data fields correctly.
  - **3. Generate Assignments (Atlas):**
      - In Atlas, import the keyword list to the assignments pipeline.
      - Set `validate relevance` to `true` to have Atlas analyze the SERP and generate a brief based on the dominant content type (e.g., listicle, guide).
  - **Critical Rule:** Create only one article per keyword to prevent "cannibalization," where multiple pages compete for the same search term.
