## Summary

Ella Dillon conducted a listening tour interview with Usman Adepoju (Content Manager, 7 months in) to identify operational friction points for GrowthX's scaling from $14M to $50M+. Key friction emerged around three areas: the rushed Atlas pipeline migration forcing CMs to test and optimize while delivering articles (high post-processing overhead); fractured training where critical knowledge is gained informally rather than systematically; and unsustainable workload expansion (CMs now doing strategy + research + writing with unchanged quotas). Ella committed to exploring a formal GTM launch plan for the February product release with structured training and change management, plus investigating weekly product update recaps that explain impact on CM workflows.

## Relevance

**To GrowthX Delivery:**
- Atlas pipeline migration was rushed and staggered (not all-at-once), forcing CMs to optimize workflows while delivering articles — resulting in high post-processing overhead (fact-checking, linking, accuracy checks) that consumes entire CM workday
- Proposed solution: Pair each CM with the engineer building/updating their Atlas pipeline for 1-2 days during creation to optimize before handoff, reducing testing/optimization cycle
- CM role has expanded to include strategy and research on top of writing, but article quotas remain unchanged — scaling is impossible without tooling that produces near-final drafts from Atlas
- Training is fractured: "agentic meetings" show possibilities but lack actionable steps; critical knowledge gained informally (Usman's optimization tips came from peer mentoring with Panza), creating inconsistent skill levels across team
- Handoff process needs live sync between outgoing and incoming CM (currently only Notion docs exist); new CMs lack time to absorb client nuances (specific phrases to avoid, preferences) before deadline pressure hits
- Product updates are lost in Slack noise; propose weekly/monthly recap consolidating changes and explaining direct impact on CM workflows

**To GrowthX Business Development:**
- Listening tour reveals scaling constraints now, before February product launch; recommend GTM-style go-to-market plan with structured training, change management, and documentation to level-set all teams
- Client communication friction: Clients questioning authenticity over single word choices suggests need for client-facing lexicon to standardize terminology

## Overview

- **Atlas Workflow Friction:** The Atlas pipeline migration was rushed, forcing Content Managers (CMs) to optimize workflows while delivering articles, leading to inefficient post-processing (e.g., fact-checking, linking).
- **Ineffective Training:** Current training is fractured. "Agentic meetings" show potential but lack actionable steps for CMs, while critical knowledge is gained informally from peers or experts like Panza.
- **Unsustainable Workload:** CMs now handle strategy and research on top of writing, without a corresponding reduction in article quotas. This makes scaling impossible without a tool that produces near-final drafts.
- **Communication Overload:** Critical product updates are lost in Slack noise. A weekly/monthly recap is needed to consolidate changes and explain their impact on CM workflows.

---

## Key Topics

### Atlas Workflow Friction

  - The rushed Atlas pipeline migration (from AirOps) created a major bottleneck.
  - **Problem:** Engineers build pipelines in isolation, then hand them off to CMs for testing and optimization.
  - **Impact:** CMs must optimize workflows while simultaneously delivering articles, creating a time conflict.
  - **Result:** High post-processing overhead (fact-checking, linking) is required to get articles from Atlas to a usable state.
  - **Proposed Solution:** A 1–2 day, dedicated collaboration session between the CM and engineer during pipeline creation.

### Ineffective Training & Knowledge Sharing

  - Current training methods fail to create a baseline of knowledge across the team.
  - **"Agentic Meetings":** Show what's possible but don't provide actionable steps for CMs to implement in their own workflows.
  - **Informal Knowledge Gaps:** Critical knowledge is gained informally (e.g., Usman learned key workflow optimizations from Panza), creating inconsistent skill levels.
  - **Proposed Training:**
      - Peer-led training from top-performing CMs on best practices.
      - Expert-led (Panza, Nana) review of individual CM pipelines to identify optimization opportunities.

### Unsustainable Workload & Handoffs

  - The CM role has expanded to include strategy and research, but article quotas remain unchanged.
  - **Impact:** This expanded scope makes scaling impossible without a tool that produces near-final drafts.
  - **Handoffs:**
      - Notion documentation is effective.
      - **Critical Need:** A live meeting between the outgoing and incoming CM to transfer client nuances (e.g., specific phrases to avoid).
      - **Constraint:** Insufficient time for new CMs to absorb context before delivery deadlines.

### Communication Overload

  - Critical product updates are lost in the high volume of daily Slack messages.
  - **Proposed Solution:** A weekly or monthly recap consolidating updates and explaining their direct impact on CM workflows.
  - **Client Communication:** A client-facing lexicon is needed to prevent misunderstandings (e.g., a client questioned Usman's native English after a single phrase).
