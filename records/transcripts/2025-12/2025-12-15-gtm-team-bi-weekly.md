## Summary

GrowthX's GTM team reviewed community program performance and finalized the KC workshop execution strategy, focusing on a scalable co-host model to drive member acquisition. Key outcomes: the team adopted promo-code-based attribution to replace unreliable UTMs, designed a gated lead magnet funnel (KC's "3 Test Patterns" guide) integrated with HubSpot and Beehive for GDPR compliance, and initiated a strategic review of community pricing and growth benchmarks against Maven. Next steps are immediate—lead magnet copy due EOD, and full workshop launch before year-end.

## Context

This is GrowthX's internal GTM team bi-weekly sync, led by Jason Gong (VP GTM). The team manages Beehive, the company's community platform with ~9,000 members, alongside a newsletter (45% open rate) and a growing event portfolio. The Lovable workshop in November generated ~17 new members but exposed critical gaps in attribution and operational execution. The team is now implementing a more scalable, co-host-driven model: rather than managing every aspect of community events, GrowthX will partner with established external partners (like KC, who has ~25k followers) to leverage their audiences and reduce GrowthX's operational burden. This meeting focused on finalizing the KC workshop as a proof-of-concept for this model and implementing infrastructure improvements (promo codes, gated lead magnets, reporting dashboards) to support future growth toward a 10,000-member target.

## Key Topics

### Performance & Attribution

  - **Lovable Workshop:**
      - **Result:** \~17 new members joined via the promo code.
      - **Insight:** Co-host promotion (e.g., Lovable's Discord) drove a registration surge from 350 to 1,200.
      - **Problem:** Attribution is unreliable; UTMs fail, and the source of 500-600 new Luma registrants is unknown.
  - **Newsletter:**
      - **KPI:** 45% open rate.
      - **Learnings:**
          - **Successful:** Numbered subjects, direct takeaways.
          - **Unsuccessful:** Abstract concepts, too many CTAs (\>10 links).
      - **Goal:** Evolve into a brand with external contributions to reduce the team's workload.

### Community Strategy & Programs

  - **Program Simplification:** Consolidating community activities into core programs:
      - **Workshops:** Continue as a key member acquisition channel.
      - **Build-A-Breakfasts:** Member-only events with a structured content flywheel.
      - **Newsletter:** Iterating based on performance learnings.
      - **Masterclass:** A new, live cohorted workshop launching in late January.
      - **YouTube:** Experimenting with short videos (5-15 min) driving to lead magnets.
      - **Lead Magnet Library:** A central hub for gated content.
  - **Event Strategy:**
      - **CXO Dinners (re-named "Council Dinners"):**
          - **Audience:** Top-tier "Council" members, held quarterly.
          - **Purpose:** Curated dialogue, not content distribution.
          - **Potential Flywheel:** Sell access directly to attract high-value prospects.
      - **Conference Happy Hours:**
          - **Audience:** Open to members and prospects.
          - **Purpose:** Networking and lead capture.
          - **Goal:** Co-host with partners to reduce costs.
  - **Strategic Review:**
      - Gloria will lead a review of community programs and pricing tiers.
      - **Growth Target:** The 10,000-member goal is aggressive; Maven is a more relevant benchmark than Pavilion, which took 6 years and acquired communities.

### KC Workshop Execution

  - **Goal:** Test the scalable co-host model by leveraging KC's \~25k followers.
  - **Lead Magnet:** A free guide ("3 Test Patterns") will be offered to prospects who reply to outreach.
      - **Content:** Danni to create from KC's notes.
      - **Format:** A simple webpage built in Sanity (not a PDF) to enable quick iteration.
  - **Process Improvements:**
      - **Timeline:** Start slide creation earlier to avoid last-minute chaos.
      - **Co-host Leverage:** Push co-hosts for more promotional support, as they benefit from our audience.

### Technical Infrastructure

  - **Lead Magnet Funnel (HubSpot/Sanity):**
      - **Form:** Ed to build on the website.
      - **Workflow:** Kyle to build in HubSpot.
      - **Data Flow:** Ed will pass the content link via a hidden form field → Kyle's workflow will use it to send the correct asset.
      - **Compliance:** A required, unchecked marketing opt-in checkbox is mandatory for GDPR compliance.
  - **Data Syncs:**
      - **Luma → HubSpot → Beehive:** Live via Zapier.
      - **Lead Magnet → HubSpot → Beehive:** Ed to build.
      - **Circle → HubSpot:** Low priority.
  - **Community Reporting:**
      - **Problem:** Circle's native reporting is insufficient.
      - **Solution:** Kyle to help define key KPIs before building a custom HubSpot dashboard.
