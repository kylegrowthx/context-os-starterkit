## Key Topics

### Performance Review

  - **Organic Traffic:** Rebounded from a post-Thanksgiving dip, confirming it was a seasonal fluctuation.
  - **Engagement:** Engagement rate is up, signaling content is effectively meeting search intent.
  - **Top-Performing Content:**
      - Articles on "AI recruiting assistant" and "how does AI reduce bias" are gaining traction.
      - Comparison articles ("X vs. Y," "alternatives to Z") remain the strongest performers.
  - **Scrunch Data:**
      - **Visibility:** Increasing from a low baseline.
      - **Citations:** Alex and competitors both show low citation volume.
          - **Hypothesis:** This suggests a potential flaw in the AI content generation prompts, as the AI may not be trained to cite these specific brands.
          - **Alternative:** The AI may be citing larger, established players like Greenhouse and BrightHire, which are not direct competitors for Alex's specific AI-focused niche.

### Paid Search Strategy

  - **Core Principle:** Match keyword intent to the offer.
      - **High-Intent Keywords:** Target users ready to convert (e.g., "best AI recruiter").
          - **Offer:** Direct call-to-action (e.g., "Request a Demo").
      - **Lower-Intent Keywords:** Target users in the research phase (e.g., "recruiting software").
          - **Offer:** Educational content (e.g., webinars, guides).
  - **Automation Goal:** Achieve \~1 conversion/day to enable Google's Target CPA bidding, which Kyle noted is difficult to outperform manually.
  - **Retargeting:**
      - **Google:** Retarget blog traffic that hasn't converted.
      - **LinkedIn:** Retarget website visitors, layering on firmographic data to filter for the ICP.
  - **Budget:** Kyle suggested a minimum monthly budget of $10k is typically needed for meaningful experimentation.
