## Key Topics

### Client Context & Priorities

  - **Primary Concern:** Accuracy and up-to-date data are paramount for compliance topics.
      - **Example:** A recent draft cited outdated NIST data (Dec vs. Mar), which required correction.
      - **Process Note:** Atlas can sometimes get confused by minor updates to compliance documents, requiring manual verification.
  - **Sourcing Strategy:** Citing authoritative sources (e.g., NIST, financial guidelines) is a strength, not a weakness.
      - **Rationale:** It demonstrates a rigorous, research-based approach that Fieldguide's internal content team values.
  - **Feedback Loop:** Fieldguide reviews are slow due to internal sharing, but their overall feedback has been positive.

### Content Production Pipeline

Ife Adekoya demonstrated the 3-step pipeline designed to produce high-quality, focused content efficiently.

  - **Step 1: Initial Research (Perplexity)**
      - **Goal:** Generate a broad, SEO-focused outline.
      - **Process:** Use a prompt with the keyword, search volume, and audience from ContentOS.
  - **Step 2: Manual Outline & Direction**
      - **Goal:** Create a direction-driven outline that guides Atlas effectively.
      - **Process:**
          - Refine the Perplexity outline using competitor analysis and Google's AI Overview to align with user intent.
          - Structure the outline with clear "Section Objectives" and "Research Directions" instead of prescriptive content.
          - Add a 100–150 word count per section to prevent Atlas from writing entire articles within single sections.
          - For the conclusion, instruct Atlas to provide a value-driven CTA, not a summary.
  - **Step 3: Draft Generation & Editing (Atlas)**
      - **Goal:** Generate a high-quality draft and perform initial edits.
      - **Process:**
          - Input the direction-driven outline into Atlas.
          - Adjust settings: change "Target Read Time" to "Target Keywords," set article length to 2,000 words, and limit hyperlinks to 2–3 words.
          - Use Atlas's "Ask AI" tool for edits, highlighting specific sections for best results (avoids errors like deleting the entire article).
