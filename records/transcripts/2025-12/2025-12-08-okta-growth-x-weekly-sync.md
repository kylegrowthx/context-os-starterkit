## Key Topics

### GrowthX Team Structure Update

  - **New Roles & Rationale:** GrowthX implemented a new structure on Dec 1 to improve client clarity and capacity planning.
      - **Erik O'Brien (Engagement Manager):** Primary point of contact for daily delivery, coordinating execution and managing communications.
      - **Kyle Gaudreau (Strategy Lead):** Leads a shared strategy team, engaged by Erik for major strategic pivots or revamps.
      - **Sydney Arin Go:** Will transition from day-to-day delivery to a strategic support role.
  - **Erik O'Brien's Background:** Joined GrowthX in August; previously in AI and general consulting.

### Strategic Pivot: From Verticals to Signals

  - **Problem:** Okta's current vertical-based targeting (e.g., retail) may be inefficient.
  - **Hypothesis:** A universal signal-based approach will be more effective for identifying high-value accounts.
  - **Core Mission:** Use identity signals to position Okta as a growth driver by addressing four key pain points:
    1.  **Identity Setup:** How it creates revenue roadblocks or customer friction.
    2.  **Tool Adoption:** How it slows innovation (e.g., AI, cloud migration).
    3.  **Security Posture:** How identity complexity weakens security.
    4.  **Growth Potential:** How fixing identity can unlock 2-3x growth.
  - **Goal:** Define a Target Account List (TEL) by prioritizing accounts based on these identity signals, not just their industry.

### New Tech Stack & Scaling Challenge

  - **Common Room Evaluation:** Okta is evaluating Common Room to create a unified UI for sales, XDR, and marketing, driving accountability with accurate, real-time data.
  - **Data Strategy:**
      - **Intel Briefs:** GrowthX briefs will be pushed into Common Room to provide stronger signals than its native data.
      - **Clay:** Okta will hire a Clay contractor to scrape objective account data (e.g., number of logins, competitor use) to build the initial TEL.
  - **Scaling Intel Briefs:**
      - **Constraint:** The current manual process is limited to \~22 accounts/week.
      - **Need:** Scale to cover a target list of \~50 high-value accounts for 1-to-1 and 1-to-few ABX.
      - **Rationale:** The briefs are critical for these high-touch accounts, while a broader message can serve the larger 1-to-many segment.
