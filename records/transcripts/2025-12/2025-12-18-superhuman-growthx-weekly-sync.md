## Summary

Jo Kaminska presented GrowthX's new content strategy for Superhuman Mail, which consolidates the existing 12 topic clusters into 5 focused clusters to improve performance tracking and increase organic visibility. The strategy prioritizes Superhuman Mail as the primary product with intentional LLM visibility targeting for zero-presence categories (Enterprise, Cost & ROI), backed by 40 articles published in December ahead of the New Year. GrowthX and Superhuman aligned on implementing the new cluster structure starting in January, with Morgan Maurer to share the January campaign brief and Jo to prepare a Q1 proposal.

---

## Context

Superhuman is a rebanded bundle of three products: Superhuman Mail, Grammarly, and Coda. The primary focus of this engagement with GrowthX is driving organic growth and brand awareness for Superhuman Mail, the core email productivity product that was recently launched as a standalone offering. Alex Rodrigues (Head of Growth/Product at Superhuman) and Morgan Maurer (Marketing/Communications) are working with Jo Kaminska (GrowthX) on a content marketing strategy to address the company's biggest challenge: low awareness of the Superhuman Mail product despite rebrand efforts. Prior to this meeting, AI-driven referral traffic had dropped dramatically post-rebrand (from ~200 visits/day to near zero), and the previous 12-cluster content taxonomy had become unwieldy and diluted performance visibility. This meeting aligns the team on a simplified strategy and cluster structure for Q1.

## Key Topics

### Content Performance & Review

  - **December Production:** 40 articles will be published by EOY to build SEO traction for the new year.
      - **Source:** Content audit-identified refreshes and new competitor pieces.
  - **Competitor Content Review:** Superhuman will retroactively review competitor comparison posts (e.g., Fixer, Big Surverses) to provide product feedback.
      - **Rationale:** Ensure accuracy and effective positioning against key threats.
      - **Process:** Jo will send Google Docs for in-doc collaboration.

### New Content Strategy: Superhuman Mail Focus

  - **Primary Goal:** Drive organic clicks and build topical authority for Superhuman Mail.
  - **Rationale:** Address the primary challenge of low brand awareness for the new product.
  - **Product Focus:** Exclusively Superhuman Mail. Grammarly and Coda will only be mentioned contextually, not as primary topics.
  - **Bundle Strategy:** Deprioritized for now. The bundle's disjointed brands require internal alignment (Q1) before a content strategy can be developed.

### Strategy Pillar 1: LLM Visibility

  - **Opportunity:** Superhuman's brand presence in AI answers is 26–31% across \~15k tracked answers.
  - **Tactic:** Create net-new content for "zero visibility" prompt categories to directly influence AI summaries.
      - **Initial Targets:** Enterprise team adoption, cost & ROI justification.
  - **Context:** Superhuman's own data shows AI referral traffic (from ChatGPT, Perplexity) has dropped from \~200 visits/day to near zero post-rebrand.

### Strategy Pillar 2: New Topic Cluster Structure

  - **Rationale:** The previous 12 clusters were overlapping and diluted performance tracking. The new 5-cluster structure provides a clearer view of wins and losses.
  - **The 5 Clusters:**
    1.  **Email Productivity & AI Management:** Core product-focused content.
    2.  **AI Workflows & Automation:** Targets knowledge workers; covers AI adoption and productivity stacks.
    3.  **Listicles & Commercial Keywords:** Software comparisons and reviews.
          - **Action:** Add Fixer to the list of competitors to track.
    4.  **Team Collaboration & Sales & Revenue Productivity:**
          - **Sales Content:** Deprioritized (low-to-mid priority) due to low PMF and poor conversion of top-of-funnel leads to deals.
          - **Action:** GrowthX will research query volume for other high-adoption teams (e.g., leadership, operations) to find a replacement focus.
