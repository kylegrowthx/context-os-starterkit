## Summary

Refine the strategy sprint process to improve client conversion and retention.

---

## Context

GrowthX's strategy sprint process—the 8-week engagement that bridges sales (Phase 1) to ongoing content delivery (Phase 2)—has been suffering from churn and conversion failures. Recent client losses (Bubble, Light Source, Base 10, Fleet, and likely Spector Office) revealed systemic failures: decision-maker drift when new stakeholders (e.g., CMOs) joined mid-sprint, misaligned expectations on deliverable quality and scale, fragmented ownership across sales (Tyler) → engagement manager → editorial, and insufficient pre-kickoff preparation. Andy led this meeting to align the internal team on a new handoff and ownership model that keeps the engagement manager as the single point of contact from pre-kickoff through sprint graduation, supported by Tyler's strategic touchpoints at critical milestones. The conversation included detailed debate about pre-kickoff research depth, staffing needs for engagement managers, and when/how to flag high-risk deals before they become churn statistics.

---

## Relevance

**To GrowthX Delivery:**
- New engagement manager ownership model: EMs own Phase 1 sprints end-to-end (pre-kickoff through graduation), replacing fragmented Tyler → EM → Ada handoffs. Directly addresses conversion failure root cause.
- Pre-kickoff research depth required: EMs must conduct 1-2 day deep research (like Eric's Airbyte analysis) to deliver personalized, trust-building kickoffs. Resource constraint: need more EMs or streamlined research workflows.
- Sales-led milestone touchpoints: Tyler joins pre-kickoff handoff, client kickoff, Week 4 check-in, and graduation call to maintain decision-maker relationships and drive Phase 2 conversion.
- Client education artifact: Post-sale "What to Expect" timeline (PDF or Figma) clarifying process, roles, KPIs, and client accountability to prevent mid-sprint decision-maker surprises.

**To GrowthX Business Development:**
- Churn root-cause analysis: Fleet, Bubble, Light Source, Base 10 all churn or non-convert due to decision-maker drift (new CMO mid-sprint), scope/quality misalignment, or unrealistic review processes. Updated deal review process needed.
- High-technical-bar client risk: Clients needing wordsmithing over scale (e.g., custom language, complex approval chains) should be DQ'd or requalified to lower SoW commitments (e.g., 2-3 articles/week vs. 5). Content engine positioning clarification in sales process.
- Weekly deal review already exists; opportunity to deepen pre-close discovery on approval processes, competitor content, and KPI clarity to flag churn signals earlier.
- Expansion into Phase 2: Successful kickoffs (like Relay, Airbyte examples) build trust and increase conversion when decision-maker continuity is maintained; new handoff model directly impacts Phase 2 revenue.

## Overview

- **New Handoff Model:** Sales (Tyler) will conduct a live, internal handoff with the Engagement Manager (EM) to transfer deep client context, enabling the EM to own the relationship end-to-end.
- **Sales-Led Milestones:** Tyler will join key client calls (kickoff, Week 4 check-in) to maintain executive-level rapport, prevent decision-maker drift, and drive Phase 2 conversion.
- **Pre-Kickoff Research:** EMs will perform deep pre-kickoff research (like Eric's 2-day analysis for Airbyte) to build immediate trust and deliver a highly tailored kickoff.
- **Client Education:** A shared "What to Expect" timeline will be provided post-sale to set clear expectations on process, deliverables, and client accountability.

---

## Key Topics

### Problem: Client Churn & Conversion Failures

  - Recent churn (Bubble, Light Source, Base 10, Fleet) and a likely non-conversion (Spector Office) revealed systemic issues.
  - **Root Causes:**
      - **Decision-Maker Drift:** New stakeholders (e.g., CMO at Fleet) join mid-sprint, derailing progress.
      - **Misaligned Expectations:** Clients with complex, technical content needs struggle to meet the 5 articles/week SoW, leading to friction.
      - **Lack of Ownership:** The client experience felt fragmented (Tyler → EM → Ada), preventing a single owner from building deep rapport.
      - **Insufficient Pre-Kickoff Prep:** EMs often have minimal prep time (e.g., 1.5 hours for Brex), forcing basic discovery during the kickoff call instead of delivering immediate value.

### Solution: A New Handoff & Ownership Model

  - **Live Sales-to-EM Handoff:**
      - A mandatory, live meeting replaces reliance on documentation.
      - **Why:** Transfers deep client context, including red flags and nuances, directly from the person who built the initial relationship.
  - **EM as End-to-End Owner:**
      - The EM who joins the handoff owns the client relationship from pre-kickoff through sprint completion.
      - **Why:** Builds deep rapport and trust by ensuring continuity and a single point of contact.
  - **Sales-Led Milestones:**
      - Tyler will join key client calls to maintain executive-level relationships and drive conversion.
      - **Why:** Prevents decision-maker drift and ensures the original buyer's context is maintained.
      - **Key Calls:**
          - Pre-kickoff internal handoff
          - Client kickoff
          - Week 4 check-in
          - Sprint completion/graduation call

### Solution: Pre-Kickoff Research & Client Education

  - **Deep Pre-Kickoff Research:**
      - EMs will conduct in-depth research before the kickoff call.
      - **Why:** Builds immediate trust and allows the team to deliver a highly tailored, value-driven kickoff.
  - **Client "What to Expect" Timeline:**
      - A shared document (e.g., PDF, Figma) will be provided post-sale.
      - **Why:** Sets clear expectations on process, deliverables, and client accountability, preventing surprises.
      - **Content:**
          - Detailed 8-week process diagram
          - Roles and responsibilities (EM, strategist, editorial)
          - Key milestones, including Tyler's check-ins
          - Clarification on the team transition from sprint to Phase 2
