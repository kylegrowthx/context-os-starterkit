## Context

GrowthX is a B2B content marketing agency ($200k+/year engagements) with an eight-week strategy sprint as its core engagement model. The company has grown to ~60 people in delivery but is facing a critical scaling problem: as headcount increased, capacity to serve clients did not grow linearly—it actually declined. The root cause is that the current sprint process relies on deep, customized research and client relationship management that doesn't scale without careful process design. This meeting involved key delivery leadership (Marcel, George, Erik, Ella, Tyler) discussing how to restructure the sprint to be more predictable, scalable, and trust-building—especially for new directors (Hassan, William) being onboarded. The platform product is a critical dependency, targeted for client-ready launch by end of January 2025.

## Overview

- **Problem:** The current sprint process is unpredictable, with a declining conversion rate (\~70%) that is unsustainable for the business. This is caused by insufficient pre-kickoff research, leading to a lack of client trust and poor strategic alignment.
- **Solution:** Implement a "Day Zero" process to shift deep strategic work (10–20+ hours) before the kickoff. This builds immediate client trust and enables a more prescriptive, high-value engagement from the start.
- **New Offer:** A "Mini-Sprint" ($20k–$30k) will be sold to non-ICP clients. It delivers a full strategy and platform setup, offloading the editorial burden to the client and freeing up internal resources for high-value accounts.

## Key Topics

### The Sprint Problem: Unpredictability & Declining Conversion

  - The 8-week strategy sprint is inconsistent, with a declining conversion rate from 100% to \~70%.
  - **Root Cause:** Insufficient pre-kickoff research and a poor sales-to-delivery handoff.
      - Strategists often review sales docs only hours before a kickoff, preventing deep internalization of client context.
      - This leads to generic questions and a failure to build immediate trust.
  - **Consequences:**
      - **Client Churn:** The Clerks account failed after a key stakeholder (Director of Product) was introduced late and found the work misaligned with expectations.
      - **Unsustainable Model:** The current conversion rate requires an unrealistic 4 kickoffs per week to hit growth targets.

### The Solution: A "Day Zero" Process for Deep Research

  - The "Day Zero" concept shifts deep, non-customer-facing research to before the kickoff.
  - **Goal:** Enable the strategist to arrive at the kickoff with expert-level knowledge of the client's business, building immediate trust.
  - **Example (Airbyte):** Eric's 2-day deep dive (reviewing LinkedIn, YouTube, etc.) for a 1-hour CEO interview yielded a 12-week thought leadership series.
      - **Impact:** The CEO was "blown away," solidifying the $40k/mo upsell and validating the value of the engagement to the CFO.
  - **Required Effort:** This deep research is a 10–20+ hour investment per client, not a quick checklist.

### New Offer: The "Mini-Sprint"

  - A new offer for non-ICP clients (e.g., Seed/Series A, those focused on "word babysitting").
  - **Scope:** A 2-week engagement delivering a full strategy and platform setup.
      - **Deliverables:** Company context, a catalog of opportunities, and a system with analytics and assignment templates.
      - **Cost:** $20k–$30k.
  - **Rationale:** Offloads the high-touch editorial burden from internal resources, freeing them for high-value accounts like Lovable.

### Scaling & Ownership

  - **Resource Constraint:** The team lacks the bandwidth for deep research, as top performers (George, Eric) are already on high-value accounts.
  - **Process Ownership:** George is the DRI for defining and scaling the new sprint process.
  - **Sales Handoff:** A recurring meeting between Sales and George is needed to ensure the strategist receives all necessary context.
  - **Platform Dependency:** The "Mini-Sprint" is contingent on the platform being ready for client login, which is targeted for the end of January.
