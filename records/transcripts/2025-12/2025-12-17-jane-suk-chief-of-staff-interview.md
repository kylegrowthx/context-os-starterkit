**Motivation for Joining GrowthX**

**Jane Suk:** I'm motivated by a few things. First, there's a compelling company mission focused on AI, and I want to work with a leader who's deeply connected to the AI space. And third, I love GrowthX's phased approach: start with services delivery, then transition to a scalable platform. That's smart.

I'm genuinely excited by the potential to help scale from what feels like organizational chaos right now into structured, efficient systems.

**GrowthX Vision and Strategy**

**Marcel Santilli:** We have a multi-product ambition. Output AI is our core platform—it supports all our operations and client services. Then we have two main products: CheckThat AI, which is our AI visibility tool, and content marketing services. Our strategic goal is to transition clients from fully managed services to platform ownership within 2-3 months.

We're focused on organic growth—targeting B2B companies with $5-50M revenue who are committed to content-driven website optimization. Organic growth is our sustainable moat versus paid acquisition.

**Current Operational Context**

**Marcel Santilli:** Here's the tension: our sales are strong. We closed nearly $5M in deals recently with an 85% conversion rate. But our operations can only handle two client kickoffs per week. That's the bottleneck that prevents scaling.

We're operating with 90 people across delivery, build, and engagement management, but we have minimal management structure. And financially, we have 70% gross margins, we're well-capitalized with $12M in the bank, we just took investment from HubSpot at a $250M valuation, and our burn rate is approximately $2.3M per month.

**Jane's Leadership Philosophy**

**Jane Suk:** My approach centers on what I call the three Cs: consistency, clarity, and care. I practice empathy and direct feedback with leaders. I build clarity from organizational chaos through frameworks and genuine people engagement. I stay adaptable, communicate proactively, and maintain strong executive presence. And everything I do is backed by data and customer insight.

I've had mentors like Nate Vogel—he was VP Go-to-Market Enablement at Gong—who shaped my belief that you can maintain executive presence while staying connected to team dynamics. That balance matters.

**Desired Leadership Impact**

**Jane Suk:** I want to manage and empower teams. I want to develop balance between generalist approaches and deep functional expertise. I optimize for three key outcomes: ARR growth, customer satisfaction, and employee belief and engagement.

If I were hypothetically stepping into a CEO role, I'd first establish clear operating principles and a 2-year North Star vision. Then I'd break down silos with clear accountability and outcome orientation.

**Why Leaving Rasa**

**Jane Suk:** I'm leaving Rasa because there's mission misalignment. There are cultural differences between the US and European teams that affect speed. I've seen turnover disrupt personal connections. And honestly, the pace isn't fast enough—critical in a competitive AI space. I prefer direct communication and feedback with leadership, and I'm not getting that consistency there.

**Cultural and Strategic Fit**

**Jane Suk:** I deeply value speed as a core operating principle. I want direct communication over layers of feedback. I need all projects aligned with company OKRs. **Marcel Santilli:** That resonates completely. GrowthX leadership values high energy and intellectual curiosity. We need people ready to think big and follow through on details. The Chief of Staff role is a pivot point for owning the hardest problems, ensuring leadership vision gets operationalized with discipline and accountability, balancing speed with direction, and distinguishing reversible decisions so we can iterate fast.
