## Key Topics

### The Problem: Strategy Sprint Opt-Outs

  - **Crisis:** Strategy Sprint opt-outs jumped from a healthy \~10% to 50% in November, with more forecast for December.
  - **Root Causes:**
      - **Non-Regrettable Attrition:** Clients who are a poor fit (e.g., toxic POCs like CodeRabbit).
      - **Regrettable Attrition:**
          - Clients who are not a close ICP match.
          - Process failures (e.g., inadequate strategy setting).
  - **Goal:** Use the Strategy Sprint to vet mutual fit and set a strong foundation, preventing future delivery issues.

### The Solution: Strategy Sprint Process

  - **Origin:** Created to secure 12-month contracts by de-risking the commitment with a mutual opt-out after an 8-week pilot.
  - **Process:** A structured 8-week program to build a strong foundation.
    1.  **Deep Dives:** Engage product/technical teams to understand the business beyond a simple kickoff.
    2.  **Artifact Creation:** Develop foundational documents (e.g., company briefs, writing guidelines).
    3.  **Strategy Alignment:** Use artifacts to build and align on a robust strategy.
    4.  **Execution & Calibration:** Begin execution to test the strategy and establish a feedback loop before the full 12-month term.
  - **Outcome:** A handoff package for the Delivery team with a clear plan, context, and key contacts, enabling them to "move fast on the right things."

### Kathy's Onboarding & Resources

  - **First Week:** Self-directed learning to get up to speed.
  - **Key Resources:**
      - **Fathom:** Meeting recordings (kickoffs, demos) to see how the team engages clients.
      - **Notion:** Client workspaces with internal/external channels and artifacts.
      - **Linear:** Onboarding task list.
  - **Team Structure:**
      - **Strategy Sprint Team:** Kathy's initial focus.
      - **Shared Strategy Team:** A floating team for deep audits and resets, supporting Engagement Managers.
