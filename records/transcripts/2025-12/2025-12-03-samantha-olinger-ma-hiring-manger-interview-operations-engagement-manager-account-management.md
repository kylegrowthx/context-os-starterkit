## Key Topics

### GrowthX: Agency & Product Strategy

  - **Business Model:** A hybrid agency (1+ year old) building an internal AI platform for content generation.
  - **Strategic Rationale:** The services business provides financial runway, de-risking the product launch and enabling engineering to innovate without immediate revenue pressure.
  - **Future Vision:** The internal AI platform may become an external SaaS product, creating a dual services/product business.

### Engagement Manager Role & Structure

  - **Team Structure:** A traditional "pod" model:
      - **Engagement Managers (EMs):** Own client relationships, strategy, and renewals.
      - **Managing Editors (MEs):** Own end-to-end content production using the AI platform.
      - **Director-Level Specialists:** Provide quarterly strategic support (e.g., QBRs).
  - **EM Responsibilities:**
      - Relationship management and client communication (Slack).
      - Performance tracking against goals.
      - Project management (no hands-on content editing).
  - **Success Metrics:** Renewals, contract expansion, and defining new workstreams.

### Candidate Experience: Samantha Olinger

  - **Background:** Senior Customer Success Manager at BrightEdge, with prior experience at a small agency.
  - **AI Adoption Strategy:** Focuses on discovery to tailor workflows to a client's comfort level, using a "train, demo, feedback" loop to build muscle memory.
  - **Cross-Functional Coordination:** Acts as the single point of contact for clients, managing internal teams (Eng, Product) to provide a seamless experience.
  - **Project Management:** Prioritizes accounts weekly based on health signals (churn risk, renewal status) to proactively manage the team's workload.
  - **Tooling:** Proficient with Asana, Monday, Salesforce, and Gainsight.
      - **Gainsight Feedback:** Valuable for tracking user adoption and identifying struggles, but noted for integration issues.

### Example: Recovering an Off-Track Project

  - **Scenario:** A client derailed a 2025 SEO strategy (pillar pages → segment pages) to pursue a new AI content project.
  - **Recovery:** Olinger refocused the client by tying the new AI project back to the original strategy.
  - **Rationale:** This maintained workflow consistency, prevented progress loss, and ensured measurable results for end-of-year reporting.

### Example: Managing Executive Stakeholders

  - **Scenario:** A CEO signed a deal but is not involved in day-to-day work.
  - **Strategy:** Keep the CEO engaged via email on all follow-ups, recordings, and strategic updates.
  - **Rationale:** This maintains executive buy-in and reinforces the day-to-day contact's authority by referencing the CEO's approval.
