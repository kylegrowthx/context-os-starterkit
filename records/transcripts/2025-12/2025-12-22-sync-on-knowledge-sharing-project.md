## Summary

Marcel, Marisol, and Will synced on GrowthX's year-end P0: building a centralized knowledge hub to replace fragmented, archived content that's hindering new hire ramp-up. The team agreed to inventory ~30 hours of video and documentation across Loom, distill it to a core 20-hour curriculum, and organize it into a structured system (Handbook, Delivery section, SOP library, and Notion client template). Marisol will source and flag Strategy Sprint examples while Will edits content into digestible chapters, with both reviewing each iteration against company messaging.

---

## Context

GrowthX's knowledge base is fractured. Over the past year, Marcel created high-quality training content, SOPs, and strategic frameworks to guide the delivery team through the 8-week Sprint methodology (covering client onboarding, Strategy Sprint execution, and core principles like "own their goals" and "infinite mindset"). However, much of this work has been archived, lost, or replaced by overly granular, task-focused documentation that lacks strategic context. Meanwhile, newer hires and team members are forced to dig through disconnected sources—old Looms, fragmented guides, Fathom recordings—to understand how the company operates.

This meeting brings together Marcel (CEO/main content creator), Marisol (Strategy Sprint operator with day-to-day context), and Will (content editor responsible for organizing and packaging materials) to tackle this as Marcel's Priority Zero for year-end. The goal is to surface the best versions of existing content, consolidate everything in one place, and prepare the knowledge system before new hires arrive in 2026.

---

- **To Project Timeline:** This is Marcel's stated P0 for year-end, to be ready when new hires arrive in January 2026. Team availability is constrained by holiday schedules, but early progress is already underway with Will downloading and organizing content.

## Key Topics

### The Knowledge Fragmentation Problem

  - Critical knowledge from the last year is archived or lost, replaced by overly granular, task-focused SOPs that lack strategic context.
  - **Example:** A 12-week delivery guide with core principles ("own their goals," "infinite mindset") was replaced by a fragmented process that forces users to dig for information.
  - This forces new hires to "connect the dots" themselves, hindering their ramp-up.
  - **Example:** A superior video is unused while an old, less effective one is linked from job applications.

### The Solution: A Centralized Knowledge Hub

  - **Objective:** Inventory all content to create a single source of truth, not to "boil the ocean."
  - **Process:**
    1.  **Consolidate:** Gather all content in one place.
    2.  **Transcribe:** Process all video content.
    3.  **Organize:** Add clear titles and chapters.
    4.  **Curate:** Distill \~30 hours of footage into a core 20-hour curriculum.
  - **Platform:** Loom will be the central hub, leveraging its AI features and existing team familiarity.

### Roles & Responsibilities

  - **Marisol:**
      - Source Strategy Sprint examples (e.g., Argument client kickoff, deep dives).
      - Flag key content (e.g., daily stand-up recordings) that Will wouldn't know existed.
      - Review Will's edits to ensure they are "on message."
  - **Will:**
      - Download and organize all video content.
      - Edit videos into concise, single-angle clips.
      - Decompose long videos (e.g., the 1.5-hour whiteboarding session) into digestible chapters.
      - Use AI prompts to process content.

### Future State: A Structured System

  - This project will feed into a structured knowledge system:
      - **Handbook:** How we work.
      - **Delivery Section:** Summary of the 8-week sprint.
      - **SOP Library:** Task-specific videos.
      - **Notion Client Template:** Uses sync blocks for up-to-date instructions.
