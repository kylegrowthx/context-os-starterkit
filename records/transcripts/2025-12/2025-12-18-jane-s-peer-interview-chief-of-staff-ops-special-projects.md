## Key Topics

### The Problem: Lack of Strategic Direction

  - Rasa lacked a formal strategy, relying on a few high-level goals instead of a defined plan for achieving them.
  - Feedback from leaders and engagement surveys revealed key pain points:
      - Exclusion from the strategy-setting process.
      - Unrealistic 2024 OKRs.
      - No shared understanding of how goals were set or would be met.

### The Solution: A New Strategic Framework

  - Jane Suk led the development of a new strategy, using the "Good Strategy, Bad Strategy" framework to structure the process.
  - **Diagnosis:** Identified two core challenges:
      - **Steep Learning Curve:** A highly technical product led to a long time-to-value.
      - **Low Brand Awareness:** The company was unknown in the market.
  - **Guiding Principles:** A series of workshops led to the "Prove Value Faster" thesis, with two pillars:
    1.  **Product:** Make the product simpler and easier to use.
    2.  **Marketing:** Build brand awareness and champions.
  - **Action Plan:** Translated principles into a single, measurable success metric per pillar.
      - **Product Metric:** Weekly Active Users (WAU) who successfully train a model.
          - **Rationale:** Training a model is the key "aha moment" that proves value, not just a download.
      - **Execution:**
          - **Product:** Launched a web-based "playground" sandbox for a low-friction trial, funneling users to the full product.
          - **Marketing:** Ran targeted campaigns and created content (white papers, blogs, meetups) for technical and business users.

### Learnings & Process Improvements

  - **Resource Focus:**
      - **Mistake:** Spreading resources across minor initiatives (e.g., a free trial for the interface product).
      - **Correction:** Pivoted resources to fix fundamental bugs and address feedback on the core interface product.
      - **Lesson:** Master core products first; spin-offs are a distraction when fundamentals are weak.
  - **Strategic Reinforcement:**
      - **Mistake:** A one-time strategy rollout in Q1 led to details being forgotten by Q2.
      - **Correction:** Implemented constant reinforcement in all-hands meetings and empowered managers to tie daily work to the strategy.
  - **Accountability & Cadence:**
      - **ELT:** Restructured meetings to include quarterly retros and used dashboards (Ops/Finance) to track OKRs and leading indicators.
      - **Managers:** Revitalized monthly meetings for alignment and accountability, requiring managers to present progress to the ELT.
      - **Documentation:** Used Lattice for OKR tracking and involved managers in board updates.

### Self-Assessment & Personal Drivers

  - **Self-Rating:** 9/10.
      - **Strengths:** Added structure to chaos, delivered a clear strategy and vision, and synthesized complex info for the CEO.
      - **Growth Area:** Adapting to a leader's specific communication style (e.g., a CEO who prefers extensive pre-reads).
  - **Personal Drivers:**
      - **Motivation:** Driven by a constant desire for 1% daily improvement, balanced with learning to pause and celebrate progress.
      - **Motto:** "Stay hungry, stay curious."
      - **Interests:** Snowboarding and running.
