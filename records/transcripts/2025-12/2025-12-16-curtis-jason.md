## Key Topics

### Webflow University's Content Scaling Challenge

  - **Problem:** The team cannot keep pace with Webflow's rapid feature releases.
      - Engineering finishes features the night before launch day, providing no buffer for content creation.
      - Current capacity: Maybe 2–3 videos/week with existing approach.
  - **Bottlenecks:**
      - **Scripting:** The most significant hurdle. A custom GPT helps, but requires heavy manual editing to remove poor AI-generated humor (Curtis estimates ~90% of AI humor needs to be cut).
      - **Post-Production:** High cost and effort for motion graphics to explain abstract concepts not visible in the UI. Tools like Jitter.video help but are still early-stage for high-quality output.
  - **Brand Constraint:** The "human" brand is central to Webflow University identity. Requires polished, studio-quality video, which rules out fully automated AI video tools. Studio setup is dialed in, so recording itself isn't the bottleneck — getting to a quality script and handling post-production are.

### GrowthX's Solution: The "Content Machine"

  - **Core Philosophy:** Treat content creation as an engineering problem (systems, process, tools) rather than a pure writing task. Use AI as leverage for expert humans, not as a replacement.
  - **How It Works:** Break down complex information (API docs, user needs) into structured guides. Use AI to draft and assemble at scale. Keep human experts in the loop for quality control and expertise.
  - **Webflow Integration Documentation Case Study:**
      - **Before:** Small DevRel team could produce maybe 2–3 docs/week.
      - **After:** Scaled to 30–40 integration docs/week.
      - **Volume:** Hundreds of live docs on Webflow's integrations page.
      - **Impact:** 30k–50k weekly readers; content appears in sales calls and cited as useful.
      - **Expansion:** Now scaling to deeper content (e.g., "How to build a subscription box site with Stripe + Webflow").
  - **GrowthX's Additional Offering:** Platform and training allowing clients to run the system themselves (white-glove service is one option; self-service with training is another).

### Proposed Collaboration: AEO Course

  - **Opportunity:** Webflow University is building a new AEO course with multiple segments. Guy has done the high-level intro. Ethan Smith (long-form interview) covers AEO fundamentals. GrowthX can fill the gap on implementation/scaling.
  - **Format:** Interview-style segment with Jason Gong, similar structure to Ethan Smith interview (longer form, deep dive, interview conducted by CRO Adrian or Webflow contact Guy).
  - **Why GrowthX:** Curtis sees scaling high-impact content as the critical missing piece in AEO education. Most companies struggle with velocity while maintaining quality. GrowthX lives this problem and has proven systems.
  - **Interviewer Options:** Adrian (Webflow CRO, interviewed Ethan) or Guy (has done podcast and workshop with Jason and Marcel already).
  - **Key Points for Interview:** Systematic approach to content creation, not just "use AI." Fundamental things to teach (customer/pain-point artifacts). How to measure and prioritize content opportunities. How to maintain quality at scale.
  - **Investor Context:** Webflow is an investor in GrowthX, aligning interests.
