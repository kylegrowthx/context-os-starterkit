## Key Topics

### Comparison Article Feedback

  - **Problem:** Articles are misaligned with the target audience and contain factual errors.
  - **Audience Mismatch:** Content is too actuarial, failing to resonate with the primary underwriting searcher.
      - **Example (Federato):** Article positions HX as a pricing/actuarial tool vs. Federato's underwriting platform, missing HX's own underwriting capabilities.
  - **Factual Inaccuracies:** Articles contain errors like incorrect product names (`HX underwriting platform`) and misrepresent competitor features (e.g., Conwer's workbench).
  - **Format & Positioning Issues:**
      - **Length:** Articles are too long, hindering readability and increasing the chance of errors.
      - **Structure:** The current "HX vs. Competitor" format is ineffective.
      - **Positioning:** Articles sometimes disqualify HX from serving customer segments (e.g., MGAs) that it actually serves.

### PSEO Proposal: Line of Business + Pricing Factors

  - **Goal:** Create a scalable, programmatic content engine for "Line of Business + Pricing Factors" guides.
  - **Rationale:**
      - **Efficiency:** Faster content production with reduced editorial oversight.
      - **Business Impact:** Targets down-funnel keywords to drive qualified leads.
  - **Taxonomy:** Use both HX's internal taxonomy and the "messy" customer-facing list to capture real-world search intent.
  - **Content Structure:**
      - **Headers:** Use precise actuarial terms (`Exposure measures`, `Risk attributes`, `Pricing methods`, `Rating factors`).
      - **Content:** Include a `Data sources` section to create a robust, interlinked content hub.
  - **Source Material:**
      - **Primary:** The AI pipeline will generate content from its own research.
      - **Secondary:** Supplement with HX demo transcripts and academic papers (e.g., Parodi's book on modeling).
