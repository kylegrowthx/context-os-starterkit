## Summary

Andi, Aida, and Ella discussed restructuring GrowthX's Engagement Manager (EM) model to address scalability constraints and context loss. The current two-person model—where relational EMs handle kickoffs and editorial EMs execute strategy—creates handoffs that miss critical client context (demonstrated by Brex's example). The solution: shift to single end-to-end EM ownership from kickoff through Week 8, with Aida moving from executing deliverables to coaching and standardizing. The team will pilot this with Will on a January account, with a goal of scaling from 2 to 3 client starts per week by March while maintaining 85% conversion rates.

## Context

This is an internal GrowthX leadership meeting with Ella Dillon (CEO), Andi Bailey (Head of Delivery/Operations), and Aida Knezevic (Editorial EM). The team is reviewing and redesigning the customer journey process for GrowthX's sprint strategy offering, which involves an 8-week engagement where clients receive a content strategy and supporting assets. The meeting stems from visible scaling constraints: conversion rates dropped to 50% in December (well below the 85% target), and Aida is managing 16 accounts while bottlenecked by editorial work. The goal is to document the current state, identify gaps, and design a scaled model that allows the company to grow from 2 to 3 customer starts per week.

## Overview

- **Problem:** The current two-person EM model (relational vs. editorial) causes context loss and prevents scaling. Aida's editorial bottleneck (16 accounts) is the primary constraint.
- **Solution:** Shift to a single, end-to-end EM model where one person owns the client relationship and deliverables from kickoff through Week 8.
- **Path Forward:** Pilot the new model with Will in January. Aida will coach him on the strategy process but will not engage with the client, enabling her to document and standardize the workflow.

---

## Key Topics

### Problem: Current EM Model & Bottlenecks

  - The current two-person EM model creates a disconnect between client discovery and strategy execution.
      - **Relational EM (e.g., Eric):** Manages the client relationship and discovery (kickoff, CMS walkthroughs).
      - **Editorial EM (Aida):** Joins at Week 2 to lead calls and execute deliverables (strategy, content calendar).
  - **Result:** Aida lacks direct kickoff context, which can lead to misaligned strategies.
      - **Example (Brex):** A key client goal (leads, not traffic) was missed in the kickoff and only discovered in a separate call, nearly derailing the Week 2 strategy presentation.
  - Aida's editorial workload is the primary bottleneck.
      - **Scope:** Edits nearly all content for 16 accounts, consuming a full day each week.
      - **Rationale:** Ensures quality and prevents client-facing errors, but is unsustainable.
      - **Trust:** Aida trusts experienced Content Managers (Sydney, Natalie, Kavishka) to deliver client-ready content without her review.

### Solution: End-to-End EM Ownership

  - The team agreed on a new model: a single EM owns the client relationship and deliverables from kickoff through Week 8.
  - **Rationale:** This provides a consistent client experience, prevents context loss, and makes the process scalable.
  - **Aida's Role:**
      - Shift from editorial execution to coaching and standardization.
      - Reduce account load to 5–7 to enable deeper client engagement.
  - **Editorial Process:**
      - Aida will delegate editing to trusted Content Managers.
      - The team will message the "calibration" phase as a temporary, tactical deep-dive to manage client expectations and prevent scope creep.

### Goal: Increase Sprint Capacity

  - The new model is a prerequisite for scaling the business.
  - **Target:** Increase sprint capacity from 2 to 3 clients per week by March.
  - **Success Metrics:**
      - Maintain or exceed an 85% client conversion rate.
      - Ensure a high-quality client and employee experience.
