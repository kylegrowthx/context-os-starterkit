## Summary

Aembit and GrowthX discussed persistent content quality concerns around technical accuracy, narrative framing, and terminology consistency. GrowthX is implementing new agentic workflows to address these issues and better leverage their knowledge base, while Apurva Dave (Aembit) expressed concern that content quality hasn't improved significantly despite recent process changes. The team decided to pause publishing temporarily and test outline reviews before producing full articles to reduce editing cycles and catch issues earlier.

## Context

Aembit is a security company focused on workload identity and access management. GrowthX is a B2B content marketing agency producing strategic content for Aembit. This is a weekly sync focused on content production, workflow, and quality assurance. The relationship is over a year old, with recent friction around content quality—specifically articles feeling disjointed, lacking proper framing for comparison pieces, missing narrative hooks, and inconsistent use of Aembit's preferred terminology (workload identities, machine identities, AI agents, MCP protocol). Apurva Dave declined the meeting but provided extensive written feedback; Darrell Etherington and Ehtisham Hussain are representing GrowthX's delivery and ops teams.
