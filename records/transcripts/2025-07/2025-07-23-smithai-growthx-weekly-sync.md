## Summary

Maddy Martin (Smith.ai) and Megan Dickey (GrowthX) collaborated on a major content strategy pivot for Smith.ai's website: shifting from an equal 50/50 focus on AI and virtual receptionists to 85% AI receptionist emphasis, while preserving the virtual receptionist offering via a dedicated callout. ## Context

Smith.ai is an AI receptionist and answering service company. GrowthX is supporting them through a major market and positioning shift: as AI receptionist demand has grown from 50% to 85% of customer preference in just a few months, Smith.ai is rebalancing their website content strategy to lead with AI while preserving their virtual receptionist offering. Megan Dickey (GrowthX Content Ops) has been working with Maddy Martin (Smith.ai Head of Marketing) to audit and revise high-traffic feature pages like the 24/7 phone answering page. This weekly sync was a deep-dive working session where Maddy reviewed detailed copywriting feedback to ensure the revised content accurately represents Smith.ai's positioning, clarifies the role of human agents in the system (predefined escalation rules + caller-requested human transfer options), and distinguishes Smith.ai's customization and white-glove support from competitors who only offer a basic subscription model.

## Key Topics

### Content Strategy Shift

  - Moving from 50/50 AI/virtual receptionist to 85% AI focus
  - Preserve virtual receptionist offering with a call-out section
  - Maintain SEO value for "virtual receptionist" keyword
  - Emphasize AI receptionist with human support as primary offering

### Copywriting Refinements

  - Use "callers with complex situations" instead of just "complex situations"
  - Adjust call duration claims (e.g., "completed in a few minutes" vs. specific times)
  - Vary language beyond "backup" for human support (e.g., extension, escalation)
  - Ensure clarity on AI capabilities vs. human intervention points

### Differentiation Strategy

  - Highlight customization: "custom AI prompts, flexible instructions tailored to business needs"
  - Emphasize white-glove setup and support as a key differentiator
  - Stress adaptability of Smith.ai solution compared to competitors

### Pricing and Features

  - Clarify AI receptionist pricing (starts at $292.50, not $95)
  - Add details on overage pricing, contract terms, and flexibility
  - Balance "AI receptionist" and "answering service" terminology
  - Provide examples of industries/use cases without implying limitations

### Website Update Process

  - Create a hidden copy of current page in Webflow for review
  - Apply learnings to other high-traffic pages (e.g., after-hours)
  - Seek leadership approval before full rollout due to significant strategy shift
