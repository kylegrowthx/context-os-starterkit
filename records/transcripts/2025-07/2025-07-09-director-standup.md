## Context

This is an internal GrowthX director standup where the leadership team (Andi Bailey, Darrell Etherington, Jakub Rudnik, Jason Gong, Mara Leighton, Matthew Panzarino) collectively reviewed recent client dynamics and emerging patterns in the market. The meeting opened with personal check-ins about schedule chaos and heat/AC challenges—a cultural note that shows how external stressors impact team energy and focus. The core discussion centered on the Ambit account, a key client navigating GrowthX's transition from old processes to the Atlas platform. Ambit's decision-maker (Aperva) had been frustrated by operational misalignment and unclear strategy, but a recent call where Matthew Panzarino presented the platform's long-term vision and clustering strategy shifted sentiment dramatically. The second half pivoted to an emerging market challenge: clients are increasingly confused about whether to optimize for SEO (human search) or LLM outputs (like ChatGPT), and some clients like Smith are explicitly deprioritizing traditional SEO in favor of LLM visibility. GrowthX lacks a coherent, client-facing position on this question, creating an opportunity to differentiate but also a risk of losing credibility if the team's advice appears reactive or inconsistent.
