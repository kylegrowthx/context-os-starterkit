## Summary

Marcel interviewed Andrew Reese for a Founding Account Executive role, exploring his sales philosophy, deal-closing strategies, and fit with GrowthX's rapid deal cycles ($720K+ deals closing in weeks rather than months). Andrew shared two signature deals—the Edward Jones engagement at Canva ($250K, 12-month relationship-building cycle involving 20+ customer conversations) and the Nubank deal at Miro ($1.4M, where he recovered a relationship after aggressive internal sales leadership nearly killed it—showing his ability to rebuild trust and adapt negotiation strategy. The key challenge surfaced: GrowthX's extremely short sales cycles (<1 month) and outcome-focused model (no expansion through product usage) require a fundamentally different approach than the multi-month relationship-building and proof-of-concept strategies Andrew perfected at Canva and Miro.

## Context

This is an executive interview as part of GrowthX's hiring process for a Founding Account Executive position. Andrew Reese is a sales leader with deep experience at three high-growth SaaS companies: Canva (design platform), Miro (collaboration whiteboarding), and SOLDA (unspecified SaaS). He's interviewing with Marcel Santilli, founder of GrowthX. The background context: GrowthX is a B2B content marketing agency with ~$7M in annual revenue, recently built a rapid-deployment model where deals close in days or weeks at $15K-$30K/month price points, with an opt-out clause for the first 60 days. Andrew's previous experience was built on the opposite model—long-cycle sales (12 months), relationship-based proof-of-concepts, and products with built-in expansion paths where customers "grow into" higher usage. This interview explores whether his consultative, relationship-first, multi-stakeholder sales approach can adapt to GrowthX's high-velocity, outcome-focused model.

---

## Relevance

- **To GrowthX Business Development:** Andrew demonstrates mastery of complex, relationship-driven sales cycles and recovery from near-lost deals—valuable for QBR management and account expansion. However, his core playbook (12-month cycles, multi-stakeholder consensus-building, proof-of-concepts with pilot cohorts) is inversely optimized for GrowthX's business model. His strength is building case studies and expanding within accounts through demonstrable ROI; GrowthX's model is faster, outcome-assumed, with expansion based on process discovery rather than usage growth. This is a hire only if Andrew can fundamentally shift his motion to high-velocity, high-confidence deal closing and then transition into post-sale account scaling.

- **To GrowthX Delivery:** Andrew's experience with multi-stakeholder engagement, understanding customer workflows end-to-end, and building internal business cases (as shown in the Nubank recovery) could be valuable for scaling QBRs and listening tours. His approach to gathering direct customer insights (surveys, interviews, building comprehensive briefs) aligns with GrowthX's emphasis on understanding inputs before designing strategy, though GrowthX must do this work on a much faster timeline.

- **To Sales Process Design:** The Nubank example surfaces a critical lesson: internal misalignment and leadership involvement in mature deals can destroy relationships, especially with relationship-focused cultures (e.g., Brazilian stakeholders). This has implications for GrowthX's hiring—aggressive American sales tactics may not work in all customer cultures. Andrew's recovery playbook (falling on his own sword, rebuilding internal alignment, tailoring commercial terms to customer procurement requirements) is instructive for any high-touch sales team.

## Key Topics

### Andrew's Professional Strengths

  - Customer-centric approach: Focuses on building relationships and problem-solving
  - Holistic perspective: Looks beyond sales to understand go-to-market foundations (ICPs, personas, messaging)
  - Process-oriented: Takes a first-principles approach to sales
  - Consultative selling: Positions himself as a trusted advisor, becoming an expert on the product and market

### Successful Deal Example: Edward Jones at Canva

  - Largest deal of Q2 globally, 5th biggest deal of all time at Canva
  - Year-long process, resulting in a $250K deal with plans to grow to $1M ARR
  - Approach:
      - Talked to 20+ branch office administrators (out of 20,000)
      - Developed strong point of view based on collected insights
      - Conducted surveys and gathered direct snippets from users
      - Formalized a 3-month pilot ($15K) with 300 test users
      - Focused on building relationships and aligning on success metrics
      - Resulted in overwhelming support and strong case for full implementation

### Challenging Deal Example: Nubank at Miro

  - 1.4M ARR deal ($700K/year for 2 years)
  - Initially started with a 50-license deal, grew to 500-750 users
  - Challenges:
      - New sales leadership involvement led to misalignment with customer expectations
      - Cultural misunderstanding in sales approach (American vs. Brazilian style)
  - Resolution:
      - Andrew took ownership, rebuilt internal alignment
      - Created a tailored proposal meeting customer's specific requirements
      - Successfully closed the deal after addressing relationship concerns

### Adapting to Current Company's Sales Model

  - Much shorter sales cycles (often \<1 month) for high-value deals
  - No usage-based growth model, different from previous experiences
  - Strategies for adaptation:
      - Focus on getting customers into the 60-day opt-out period quickly
      - Leverage existing proof points and clearly explain the process
      - Maintain relationship-building through scaled methods (e.g., Slack channels, AI-assisted insights gathering)
      - Balance high-momentum approach with strategic relationship development
