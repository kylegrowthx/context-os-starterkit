## Key Topics

### Content Marketing Performance

  - 30% increase in organic traffic over the last 3 months compared to prior period
  - Grammarly acquisition news drove approximately 1,000 visits over a 3-day span and spiked career page traffic by 50%
  - 4% monthly increase in top 10 keyword rankings between June and July (1,000 additional keywords)
  - AI-related content performing exceptionally well, including stat-based articles like "10 Cool Facts on AI," "AI Email Agents," and "AI Power Task Management"
  - LLM referrals growing significantly; the burnout statistics article generated more LLM referrals than organic clicks, indicating AI visibility is becoming a major traffic driver
  - Homepage, pricing page, and blog posts are top traffic sources alongside acquisition news

### Acquisition and Rebranding

  - Superhuman was acquired by Grammarly; Coda is also part of the new company structure under the same parent company
  - Major rebranding and product bundle launch planned for October 6th—a complex initiative affecting sales, marketing, and product
  - New overarching platform brand, visual identity, and positioning will include Superhuman (email), Grammarly (writing/grammar), and Coda (docs/collaboration)
  - The new platform will feature bundled integrations between Grammarly and Coda (not directly with Superhuman yet) and an overarching narrative around AI-powered productivity
  - Superhuman team is just now joining the rebranding workstreams; significant changes ahead for website structure, signup flow, and SEO architecture
  - Acknowledged risks: SEO migration risk, potential seat growth cannibalization from Grammarly side, and Superhuman will be converting to Notion as an org tool (away from Coda)

### Content Strategy Shift

  - Shifting from individual user focus to AI adoption for teams and businesses—targeting 200+ employee companies with AI budgets
  - New ICP developed by Alex's team using AI persona framework; needs alignment with Grammarly/Coda teams on prioritization and target personas
  - Content positioning: "productivity stack" approach featuring end-to-end workflows with Superhuman, Grammarly, and Coda
  - Five key content pillars identified: docs (Coda), communication/grammar/content writing (Grammarly), calendaring/email (Superhuman), chat, and transcription/collaboration tools
  - Superhuman spending 50-70% of user time in email; Grammarly data shows 50% of user actions in email, suggesting email-centric productivity stack is core positioning
  - Elvis suggested 50+ potential topic ideas around productivity stacks and tools (e.g., "How to build foundational AI stack for teams," "Ultimate tech stack for your productivity")
  - Harmony cautions against narrowing ICP to just "marketing"—broader narrative needed to capture enterprise AI budgets across functions

### Analytics and Tracking

  - Superhuman evaluating Profound ($500/month) vs. Scrunch for AI/LLM analytics; both track LLM mentions, visibility, and content performance in AI chatbots (ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, Claude, etc.)
  - Elvis offered to set up Profound or Scrunch with GrowthX's help; can organize topic clusters (20-50 queries per topic) to track visibility across keyword groups
  - Alex proposed building a conversion funnel dashboard (using existing BI tool) to track: blog traffic → signups (email capture) → leads (sales conversations) → purchases (seats)
  - Current challenge: Superhuman migrating to new BI tool; GA4 and Search Console data in place but need request process to add blog-to-funnel tracking
  - Elvis committed to developing tracking approach using current tools and presenting back to Superhuman team; he'll present seat impact calculation methodology
  - Goal: measure content ROI by attributing seat growth to blog traffic at ~1% conversion rate (thousands of clicks could generate ~10 seats/week)
