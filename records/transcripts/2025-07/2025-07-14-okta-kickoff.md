## Summary

GrowthX kicked off a content strategy engagement with Okta/Auth0, focusing on pipeline generation and developer-focused marketing for Auth0 (the external-facing CIAM product under Okta Inc.). Rachael Tiow outlined Auth0's current challenges: declining inbound traffic, slow internal processes, and the need for faster execution on marketing experiments. GrowthX will conduct a one-week strategy sprint (following their proven 16+ sprint engagement model) to diagnose core issues and identify high-impact opportunities before proposing a detailed execution roadmap. Key opportunities identified include leveraging Auth0's strong domain authority (22.5M backlinks), developer engagement, competitive differentiation (AI authentication, fine-grained authorization), and reviving use-case-specific content strategies.

---

## Context

Okta Inc. owns and operates two distinct identity products: Okta (employee SSO for internal identity) and Auth0 (customer identity and access management for external-facing applications). Rachael Tiow recently returned to Auth0 in a senior role and is tasked with reviving the product's inbound pipeline and marketing effectiveness. She leads a team that was historically strong on developer marketing but has experienced drift in content strategy and execution speed over the past few years. The engagement with GrowthX stems from Rachael's prior successful work with Marcel Santilli at another company (Transmit), giving her confidence in GrowthX's capabilities to audit Auth0's current state, uncover what's no longer working, and propose bold experiments aligned with 2025+ market conditions rather than replaying dated strategies.

## Key Topics

### Auth0 vs Okta Business Context

- Okta Inc. owns both Okta (employee SSO) and Auth0 (external-facing CIAM)
- Auth0 focuses on customer identity and access management (CIAM)
- Auth0 brand performs better than Okta branding for Auth0 products
- Revenue and pipeline generation are key priorities for Auth0

### Target Audience and ICP

- Primary focus: Engineering and product orgs, digital transformation leads, IAM teams
- Key verticals: Retail, hospitality, B2B SaaS, FinServe, healthcare, media, entertainment
- Emerging trends: AI agents, non-human identities, fine-grained authorization
- Security roles are a potential new target audience

### Pipeline Generation Challenges

- Inbound leads declining (previously a major source)
- Current split: ~50% inbound, ~50% outbound (ads, events, SDR efforts)
- Marketing contributes to ~80% of pipeline and revenue
- Slow internal processes hinder quick execution of marketing initiatives

### Experimental Approach

- Focus on use case-specific experiments aligned with business narratives
- Explore competitive takedowns and emerging trends (e.g., AI authentication)
- Test experiences for nuanced industries and revenue models
- No strict "no-go" areas identified, but careful coordination with developer marketing team required

### Auth0's Unique Strengths

- Strong domain authority (22.5 million backlinks)
- Developer-friendly documentation and UI/UX
- Modular and extensible authentication pipeline

### Internal Challenges

- Slow processes for account targeting and list management (e.g., 6 weeks to tag Akamai customer list)
- Disconnect between developer marketing and other marketing efforts
- Decline in developer-focused content and events
