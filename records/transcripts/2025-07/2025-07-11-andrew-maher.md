## Key Topics

### Runway Implementation and Value

  - Andrew implemented Runway at both Superhuman and Moonvalley
  - Initial setup took \~2 weeks of part-time focus
  - Key benefits:
      - Flexible modeling (retains spreadsheet-like capabilities)
      - Powerful scenario planning with easy updates across scenarios
      - User-specific dashboards for controlled sharing
  - Current pricing: $15-17k/year for Andrew (early adopter); Bridget quoted $24k

### Burn Forecasting Techniques

  - Focus on material expenses and cash timing adjustments
  - Use net operating income/loss as a starting point
  - Adjust for significant non-P\&L items (e.g., laptop purchases)
  - For early-stage companies, simplify by overestimating slightly for a built-in buffer
  - Increase granularity as runway shortens (e.g., \<10 months left)

### Spend Management Best Practices

  - Use dedicated vendor cards (e.g., through Brex) for better tracking
  - Implement a procurement process to control spending
  - Create budget hierarchies that align with P\&L structure
  - Link credit card spend and bill payments to specific budgets for real-time tracking

### Transitioning from Spreadsheets

  - Spreadsheets still useful for quick, collaborative modeling
  - Runway becomes the "production environment" for official forecasts and budgets
  - Export capabilities exist if needed to revert to spreadsheets
