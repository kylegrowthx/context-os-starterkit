## Key Topics

### Logic's Current Focus and Hiring

  - Outreach campaign launching in August, targeting 1,500 organizations with contractors assisting on targeting and data compilation
  - Hiring for head of growth role (JD open 80 days, 5-6 serious candidates, conducting working sessions with finalists to evaluate fit)
  - Seeking a non-technical generalist comfortable flexing across marketing disciplines (product marketing, brand, content, growth)
  - Ideal candidate profile: zero-to-one experience required or demonstrated hunger to do zero-to-one work; specific interest in hiring from Auth0, Okta, Iterable, or StatSig (technical product companies with dual buyer personas)

### GrowthX's Content Creation Services and Pricing

  - Offers both editorial and programmatic content creation with a tiered approach
  - Editorial content: 5-15 pieces per week ($9k at low end for 5/week; $12k-$15k for 10/week; mid-20s+ when combined with programmatic and custom workflows)
  - All engagements start with a "sprint phase" ($20k minimum, sometimes higher for regulated industries like healthcare with doctor review requirements) to define strategy, scope, inputs, and KPIs
  - Programmatic content: template-based pages targeting variants and use cases, providing unique user experiences that scale through AI-generated customization
  - Kyle emphasized that sprint phase prevents over/underselling and builds the right foundation for successful AI-powered content delivery

### Programmatic Content Examples

  - **Vappy.ai**: Identified 3,500+ potential agent use cases; started with 100, measured indexing speed and engagement, then scaled based on positive signals. GrowthX created the template, design, system prompts, and identified use cases; each page lets users interact with a custom cancellation-management agent, combining product showcase with value delivery
  - **Ramp**: Identified per-diem rate searches (city/state variants) where government websites dominated with poor UX; created navigational pages with better UX and scraped actual rates, turning search volume into organic traffic
  - Philosophy: Successful programmatic avoids the trap of conventional SaaS structure (solution → feature → use-case content). Instead, find unique user experiences that provide genuine value, fit the customer's real intent, and scale through variants

### Content Distribution and Audience Building

  - Primary focus on organic growth; Kyle has deep paid background and can advise strategically but doesn't position paid as core service offering (most brands come to GrowthX to reduce paid reliance)
  - Multi-channel repurposing: one piece of content (e.g., video podcast) feeds into articles, email nurture, social clips, YouTube for organic reach, and even paid campaigns if designed correctly
  - Bundling strategy: Create "content kits" grouping related assets (video + blog + eBook) and package with different themes, gated/non-gated mix, for promotion across LinkedIn, long-form pages, and organic channels
  - Experimentation mindset: With low-cost AI-powered content, Kyle advocates for rapid hypothesis testing on topics, styles, audience messaging—get signal back quickly and iterate

### Potential Collaboration Areas and Next Steps

  - Steve explicitly interested in GrowthX's programmatic approach, especially custom agents/workflows (Logic is building an internal workflow library from customer use cases, which parallels the VAPI and Ramp examples)
  - Key insight: If GrowthX helps Logic generate programmatic content for workflow variations, Logic can free up their engineer currently assigned to the workflow library project and redeploy to product development
  - Immediate decision point: Steve is considering whether to move timeline up; will discuss internally with co-founder and prepare specific engagement questions for next meeting
  - Next call will drill into specifics: exact engagement model, what "done" looks like, division of labor, timeline, and cost
