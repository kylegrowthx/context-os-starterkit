## Key Topics

### Tyler's Background and Experience

Tyler has spent 15+ years in enterprise software sales and marketing roles spanning multiple verticals:
- **Rainforest (crowdsource QA):** Early-stage experience in startup culture and team building
- **Segment (data infrastructure):** Worked alongside G (later grew Segment to IPO/acquisition), experience selling to data-driven enterprises
- **AIR (creative operations platform):** Sold directly to marketers and creative teams; faced challenge of product-led sales at early stage (every customer wanted custom features)
- **Seros (currently):** Enterprise AE role
- **Houston-based:** Recently relocated back to Texas (hometown) with fiancée

He uses AI natively for sales productivity: note consolidation, opportunity prep, and reality-checking his thinking. He demonstrated understanding of GrowthX's unique positioning and immediately grasped why outcome-based, done-for-you services eliminate the main objections that plague software-centric competitors.

### GrowthX's Current State and Scaling Challenges

Kyle outlined GrowthX's transition phase:
- Recently launched proprietary platform (was previously using Airtable + workflow abstraction layer)
- Shifting from 90-day pilots to Strategy Sprints (shorter, more focused presales and exploration engagements)
- Current cadence: 2-3 kickoffs per week, target: 5-10 within the year
- Scaling through three levers: people, process, and technology (not just hiring)
- Core positioning: outcome-based content strategy ($200k+ ACV deals, often closed in 2-3 calls with Marcel's network)

Kyle is still learning the true conversion rate and deal size patterns with Strategy Sprints and needs to build more scalable sales infrastructure (repeatable discovery, better case studies, story-based assets).

### Sales Process and Motion

GrowthX's sales motion is hybrid and somewhat non-traditional:
- Intro call: light qualification + discovery + early solutioning and inspiration
- Follow-up call: deeper discovery if needed, more solutioning, sharing relevant case examples
- Close: often after just 2-3 total meetings (helped by Marcel's credibility and network)
- Challenge: moving beyond founder-led sales as network exhausts requires more structured discovery, clearer messaging, and repeatable processes

Kyle acknowledged this is harder than transactional software sales and requires salespeople who understand consultative selling. Tyler reinforced this by noting the key is getting prospects to articulate their own challenges in their own words—then story-based selling becomes much easier.

### Case Studies and Demonstrating Impact

Both Kyle and Tyler identified this as a major growth lever:
- GrowthX has closed customers and driven real impact, but hasn't systematized capturing and telling those stories
- Kyle struggles to walk prospects through end-to-end narratives (problem → strategy → execution → results)
- Tyler suggested using simple story formats (situation, task, action, result) tied to specific customer wins, avoiding generic templates
- This gap makes the sales process slower than necessary; Tyler sees this as solvable and urgent

### AI and Sales Efficiency

Tyler shared his approach: using AI for notes, opportunity prep, reality-checking. Kyle and Tyler discussed how GrowthX can become "AI native" in its own sales operations (not just selling solutions to buyers). This aligns with GrowthX's expertise angle and internal credibility.

### Culture and Growth Mindset

Kyle emphasized leaning into discomfort as a core value:
- GrowthX values consultative, strategic selling over transactional plays
- Rewards people who embrace the messiness and uncertainty of early scaling
- Expects team members to take on varied hats (Kyle does sales and internal strategy; Jason does marketing and delivery refinement)

Tyler explicitly cited this as his draw—after years at comfortable, post-product-market-fit companies, he wants to build something meaningful at a moment when he can make strategic impact. Both saw this as cultural alignment.

### Hiring Process and Next Steps

- Kyle moved Tyler forward immediately and enthusiastically
- Next round: presentation/working session with Marcel, Bridget, and Kyle
- Final step: short call with Marcel
- Kyle wants to move fast but keep quality in mind
- Bridget (Kyle's hiring manager peer/partner) had already spoken with Tyler separately and also responded positively
