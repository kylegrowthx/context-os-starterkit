## Key Topics

### Content Strategy and Audience Focus

  - Blog content targets two main audiences:
      - Customers and prospects (from hands-on practitioners to CISOs/CIOs)
      - Broader security community (peers, partners, influencers, analysts)
  - Shifted away from using blog as a "company junk drawer" for unrelated content (e.g., talent brand, venture arm updates)
  - Audience is highly technical, skeptical, and averse to promotional content
  - Credibility and providing actionable information (e.g., IOCs) are prioritized over direct product promotion

### Content Contributors and Types

  - Sentinel Labs team: Novel threat research, "From the Front Lines" analysis
  - Product Marketing: Product launches, features, market-related thought leadership
  - Technical Marketing and Evangelism: In-depth technical content for practitioners
  - Ad hoc contributions from other teams (e.g., detections engineering)

### Quality Control Process

  - Abstract review system:
      - Contributors submit \~100-word abstract before writing full post
      - Responses categorized as Yes, No, or Yes-but with guidance
  - Content Council:
      - Cross-functional editorial board for idea sharing and coordination
      - Current setup is overly democratized; needs streamlining
  - Craig VerColen reviews all blog content, focusing on:
      - Messaging consistency
      - Alignment with executive priorities
      - Avoiding implications of product weaknesses

### Areas for Improvement

  - Develop comprehensive messaging guides for products and features
  - Streamline Content Council process and clarify stakeholder roles
  - Improve coordination with Product Marketing for consistent messaging
  - Establish clearer guidelines for Cyber 101 content to maintain quality standards

### Key Stakeholders

  - Craig VerColen: Communications and corporate oversight
  - Drew Wright: SEO and digital marketing
  - Tim Mackey and Brian Gale: Product Marketing leadership
  - Adam Hall: Technical Marketing and Evangelism lead
  - Joshua (new hire): Brand and style guidelines
  - Allie: Demand generation with PR and PMM experience
