## Context

Engine is a blue-collar travel management platform that recently rebranded from Hotel Engine after adding flights and other offerings. GrowthX has been working on a significant engagement to produce bottom-funnel content focused on construction, oil & gas, manufacturing, and sports teams — their core blue-collar audiences. This meeting was a formal handoff: Jo Kaminska, who had been leading the account, transferred ownership to Carrie Chowske and Darrell Etherington as GrowthX scales the engagement. The context is critical because Engine's CMO James is detail-oriented about product representation, prefers batch reviews via Slack, and is hands-off with Airtable tracking — a different workflow than many GrowthX clients.

## Relevance

- **To GrowthX Delivery:** Engine content requires vertical-specific personas (construction vs. manufacturing vs. oil & gas) with tailored messaging. Tone of voice is a critical delivery parameter — the article refresher pipeline currently pulls Engine's dry, down-to-earth voice but should reflect GrowthX's punchy, audience-suited approach (with James noting current content is 10% too edgy). Batch review workflows and Slack-based feedback loops differ from standard Airtable-centric processes.

- **To GrowthX Business Development:** Engine is 8+ weeks into engagement with clear renewal signals: James is engaged, approving published content with minimal changes, and requesting consistent batch delivery (20-30 topics at a time). Account expansion opportunity: Dave is building Looker dashboards for performance visibility, which could strengthen James's trust and reveal new content gaps.

- **To CheckThat / AEO:** Engine has extensive Gong call data with valuable insights about their blue-collar customer segment but is hesitant to share due to IP concerns. Future opportunity: if anonymized/sanitized, this data could power differentiated content. Competitors (Navan, TravelPerk, Etsy Light) are strong organic search players — Engine's content strategy includes targeting these competitors' keywords.
