## Key Topics

### Website Updates

GrowthX engineering team unlocked guides functionality on augmentcode.com. The new guides section and MCP directory (a programmatic, tailored alternative to Code Library) are both ready to publish end of week. GrowthX content team drafted the MCP directory content and sent it to Chris for review. Megan, Augment Code's designer, will review and tweak the design with GrowthX. GrowthX is treating the website as a fast-moving, iterative project — they're willing to ship incomplete work in order to get pages indexed and seeded quickly rather than waiting for perfection.

### Editorial Content Strategy

Aida generated an initial blog post using Augment Code's company artifacts from Notion (writing guidelines, company overview, audience personas). The goal is to validate voice and tone before ramping production. If organic traffic is a priority alongside the high-intent keywords, Aida can expand the content clusters to include middle and top-of-funnel content. GrowthX will provide 30-day and 60-day ranking forecasts. Content production can scale rapidly once the initial post is approved.

### Newsletter Development

GrowthX signed a year-long contract with Beehive and completed the backend setup. Two sample newsletter editions were created and are ready for content and design review. The newsletter is named "AI Coding" to optimize for SEO (11,000 monthly searches) over generic terms like "AI Dev" (500 searches). Sylvain briefly explored alternative names like "AI.dev" to own "software agent" positioning long-term, but ultimately agreed AI Coding is better for short-term searchability. The design can be renamed later once subscriber base is built. Beehive's customization is limited, so designers are working with Beehive's resources to refine templates.

### Cross-Content Strategy

GrowthX's newsletter curation work will enrich editorial content by surfacing interesting engineering blogs and challenges. Over time, they'll reach out to featured engineers to link back to Augment Code's blog — similar to the Surge.ai strategy that led to First Million podcast appearance, ink stories, and 20BC podcast outreach. This creates a virtuous cycle: newsletter → earned media → brand awareness → link equity.

### SEO and Competitive Landscape

AI coding keywords are low-difficulty and high-intent because the category is brand new. Search volume in SEMrush appears low due to historical data limitations, but actual search volume is likely much higher. Competitors like Kodo are ranking with generic content, suggesting first-mover advantage is real. GrowthX will be aggressive but thoughtful to avoid the mistake Play made (lost 70% traffic after publishing 8,000 pages in 18 months with an agency partner). The strategy is to build content clusters showing depth rather than spray-and-pray programmatic content.

### Video and Podcast Placements

Videographer is booked for a half-day recording session. Sylvain suggested featuring Guy, a co-founder and ex-Google researcher, for self-serve/PLG audience content (vs. Scott, the CEO, who is better for enterprise/CTO sells). Guy is willing to do podcast appearances. George emphasized that "getting your founder on podcasts" is one of the highest-ROI activities — even uncomfortable first appearances compound into easier, bigger placements. They're treating this as medium-term focus after website and newsletter ramp up.

### Metrics and Success Definition

Sylvain emphasized the gap: multiple content initiatives are launching simultaneously, but the team hasn't defined quantifiable goals or success metrics. He wants to track weekly progress — specifically rankings, traffic, newsletter subscriber growth, interaction rates, and how many new users each channel brings. George committed to providing quantified metrics for editorial by end of week, and metrics for all content lanes early next week. Goals and tracking in SEMrush are critical before scaling further.
