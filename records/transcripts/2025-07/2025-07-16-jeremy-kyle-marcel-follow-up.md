- Chief Content Officer previously ran TechCrunch (10 years)
- Dave ran TripAdvisor and Helpline.com (100M+ visitors), plus Fanatics
- Leadership mix of CMO/growth, SEO, editorial, and technical expertise
- VC-backed team enables bench strength not typical of agencies

### Engagement Structure

- 8-week strategy sprint ($20k) to define content roadmap, test platform, and establish working relationship
- Execution phase typically 12 months at $15-25k/month based on output volume
- Strategy sprint deliverables: strategic artifacts, 10+ published articles, roadmap validation
- Full execution: 15+ editorial pieces/week, 100+ programmatic pages, ongoing optimization

### Jeremy's Evaluation Framework

- Attracted to GrowthX's unpublished expertise and human strategy layer beyond just platform automation
- Concerned about long-term costs and dependencies; prefers retained advisory relationships (models himself on Kevin Indig approach: advisor/mentor + execution partnership, not pure vendor)
- Key decision factor: Can GrowthX provide continued strategic guidance post-engagement?
- Evaluating opportunity cost: in-house build + hire vs. outsourced execution + advisory

### Pricing and Contract Flexibility

- Strategy sprint: $20k fixed for 8 weeks
- Execution: $15-25k/month based on output volume and complexity
- Kyle indicated openness to shorter initial term (6 months vs. 12) if it reduces commitment
- No current offering for platform-only licensing post-engagement (Jeremy flagged interest; this is open question)

### Vendor Comparison Context

- AirOps: Tool-first approach with templated workflows; Marcel noted repeat issues where clients build workflows but then lack strategic direction
- Finder's in-house option: Talent exists but opportunity cost of diverting internal resources; Marcel positioned GrowthX as bringing execution bandwidth + proven process
- Kevin Indig: 5-year advisory relationship with Finder; Jeremy values this model and is exploring whether GrowthX could provide similar strategic depth
