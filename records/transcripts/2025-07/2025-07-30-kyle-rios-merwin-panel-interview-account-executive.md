## Key Topics

### Role Play Setup

- Kyle pitched GrowthX services to a fictional Apollo.io team (Kyle Gaudreau as VP Growth, Jason Gong as Content Lead, Bridget McGillivray as CFO)
- Scenario: Apollo recently hit $150M ARR and is transitioning from startup to enterprise company, looking to expand content capabilities and operationalize AI across the GTM team
- Kyle created detailed context and pitch deck based solely on GrowthX website information, demonstrating independent research capability

### Discovery Process

- Kyle conducted thorough discovery, asking about current challenges, urgency, existing solutions, and internal vs. external approaches (DIY tools vs. agencies)
- Identified key pain points: scaling content production without adding headcount, maintaining quality at scale, increasing velocity, and operationalizing AI adoption
- Feedback noted the discovery phase was strong but slightly long—could have accelerated based on prospect reactions rather than covering all planned questions
- Kyle's approach of asking what the prospect is spending before introducing pricing was highlighted as excellent leverage-building technique

### GrowthX Value Proposition

- Presented GrowthX as expert-led, AI-powered growth—combining services and software to scale volume without adding headcount
- Highlighted key differentiators: expert-led content that converts (vs. low-quality volume), fast implementation and iteration (weeks vs. months/years), fully managed execution, and always-current technology (vs. tool risk)
- Contrasted GrowthX's approach against two status quo options: DIY tools (low precision, high learning curve, outdated quickly) and agencies/contractors (expensive to scale, quality inconsistency, slow delivery, high oversight burden)
- Positioned GrowthX as solving the "golden quadrant" goal of high quantity plus high quality without adding internal complexity

### Pricing and Next Steps

- Transparent pricing model: $20k one-time strategy sprint, $12k-$60k monthly for AI growth engine (depending on scope)
- Emphasized quick ROI timeline (8 weeks to measurable results) and fully managed, non-black-box solution
- Created urgency with limited onboarding slots and potential September 1st start date, using FOMO as closing technique
- Clear next steps: prepare comprehensive proposal with multiple pricing tiers and introduce a growth expert for deeper execution planning

### Feedback and Areas for Improvement

- **Discovery pacing:** Bridget noted discovery was great but could have been shorter—read prospect reactions to determine whether to accelerate through planned questions
- **More pointed questions:** Instead of open-ended "Do you have questions on this slide?", prepare specific pre-planned questions that guide prospects toward admitting pain points or validating key assumptions
- **Reduce slide reliance:** Jason noted that for executive-level conversations (CMO/VP Growth level), going through entire deck is unnecessary—use discovery insights to focus presentation on what matters to that specific prospect
- **Deflection technique:** Develop better ways to maintain interest and momentum when questions arise that Kyle can't answer directly, using language like "We have people on the team who specialize in that" rather than pure avoidance
- **Challenge assumptions:** Kyle Gaudreau highlighted that Marcel (presumably the top AE) does this well—look for opportunities to challenge prospect assumptions rather than just validating their stated needs
- **Balance consultative vs. transactional:** The panel emphasized that GrowthX's strength is the casual, consultative, expert-driven approach—Kyle should lean into that rather than shifting toward conventional, slide-heavy sales tactics. Slides can be a tool but shouldn't drive the conversation.

### Feedback Highlights

- Bridget: "I really liked how before you showed pricing, you asked how much you're spending. That gives you major leverage." The FOMO close ("Get on the train or get out") was clear and effective.
- Jason: "The way you presented makes me think the website needs a shitload of work." Noted that for decision-maker level people (CMO), the full deck is unnecessary if discovery is done well—just focus on what matters to that buyer.
- Kyle Gaudreau: Praised Kyle's thoughtful anticipation of friction points and proactive addressing of them. Emphasized the need to challenge assumptions (like Marcel does) and maintain the consultative, casual tone that differentiates GrowthX. Noted that sometimes the team jumps too quickly to showing platform/execution without doubling-clicking on key prospect insights.

### Next Steps and Follow-ups

- Kyle to review GrowthX's actual discovery deck and sales process to understand the team's current approach and templates
- GrowthX team to evaluate website improvements around clearer positioning of the expert-led + AI model, broader service scope visibility, and stronger case study narratives
- Consider additional sales training on pointed questioning, assumption-testing, and executive-level conversation pacing
- Experiment with different discovery-to-pitch ratios and slide usage depending on buyer level and prior context
