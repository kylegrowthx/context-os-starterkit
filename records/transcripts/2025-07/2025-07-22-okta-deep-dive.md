## Key Topics

### Auth0 Strategic Positioning and Market Segmentation

Auth0 wins in seven core verticals: retail, healthcare, health tech, FinServ/FinTech, insurance, hospitality, and media/entertainment. The team's thesis is that industry is a useful proxy for understanding buyer behavior, but the real differentiator is revenue model — companies with external application logins (requiring identity management) are the prime targets. Starting with retail because Auth0 already has marquee customers (Gymshark, Porsche, potential partnership with Shopify via identity services). Next quarter will launch formal programs for retail, FinServ, healthcare, and B2B SaaS, with geographic focus on UKI and USA for retail to start (avoiding Australia, narrowing scope for fast wins).

### Artifact-Based Go-to-Market Foundation

Instead of automate-first, build artifact-first. Three tiers of artifacts: foundational (product-level: what Auth0 solves, use cases, differentiators; industry-level: vertical-specific buying signals, personas, pain points, regulatory context; use-case-level: solution-specific positioning); company-specific (deep research beyond basic enrichment — scrape website, identify founding story, audience, personas, current initiatives); persona-level (buyer journey, priorities, relevant signals). These artifacts live in a shared library that any AE, marketer, or SDR can pull into a ChatGPT project and use for research. The goal is not mass automation (6,000 emails) but enabling high-touch work with context.

### Center of Excellence and Signal Engine

Rachael is building this out in her team. It's not just executing campaigns; it's surfacing market signals (what signals matter, what are the top 5 per industry, how do they change) and integrating with Sixth Sense data. Includes a "growth lab" concept — test messaging internally, validate it works, then scale it out to regional and field marketing teams. The idea is to make marketing an engine that feeds into demand gen, SDR, and sales teams.

### Website and Content Strategy

Critical missed opportunity. Auth0's website has content from 2019, which is a massive LLM-readiness problem. Fresh, updated customer references, and product positioning are essential. Recommendation from George: make website refresh priority #1 (before outbound, before everything else). Parallel work: interview domain experts (people who keynote at RSA, Black Hat, etc.) rather than relying on PMM-filtered positioning.

### Partnership and Expansion Opportunities

Shopify partnership is significant. Shopify doesn't want to manage identity; if Auth0 becomes the recommended solution via Shopify, it opens the door to thousands of retail customers. This is a major lever for retail segment expansion.

### Teaching Marketers to Think in AI-Native Workflows

The team is not just doing work for marketers; it's teaching them how to be AI-native. This includes workshops (like the Officeverse 2-hour workshop George runs) on how to generate artifacts, use context effectively, and think about personalization differently. Long-term: potential "GrowthX Lab School" concept to train Auth0 cohorts.
