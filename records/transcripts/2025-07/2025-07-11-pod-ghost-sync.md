## Summary

Jason Gong and Rafid Kabir dug into GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) as a content strategy lever — specifically clarifying that GEO is a new distribution channel for existing content, not a replacement for SEO. Traffic from LLMs has grown from near-zero to 5-30% for many sites, and while informational content is being disrupted by AI responses, product-related searches remain valuable. The core challenge: there's no way to measure GEO effectiveness today because AI platforms don't expose analytics on what people prompt for or which content gets cited. Rafid is preparing content on this topic for LinkedIn; Jason recommended focusing on the hard problems of measurement, since understanding the barriers is itself valuable context for audiences trying to navigate GEO strategy.

---

## Key Topics

### GEO vs SEO Relationship

  - GEO is not a replacement for SEO but an additional channel to optimize
  - Traffic from LLMs (Large Language Models) has grown from 0% to 5-30% for many sites
  - Some content categories (e.g., informational queries) are being disrupted by AI responses
  - Product-related searches still drive significant traffic and remain relevant for optimization

### Challenges in Measuring GEO Effectiveness

  - Lack of analytics from AI models and tools
  - Difficulty in tracking actual usage and prompts
  - Personalization in AI responses makes tracking complex
  - Attribution breaks as there's often no direct link from AI responses to websites
  - Resource-intensive to track diverse user contexts and needs

### Content Creation and Distribution

  - Content remains crucial for telling product stories and spreading information
  - The consumption method is shifting, with more content being accessed through AI interfaces
  - Good content still attracts people, but distribution channels are evolving

### Future of GEO and AI in Marketing

  - Potential for AI platforms to introduce monetization (e.g., ads) which could lead to more analytics
  - Information might shift to more closed, personal environments (e.g., private communities)
  - Rapid changes in AI capabilities could quickly alter the landscape

### Industries Benefiting from GEO

  - Difficult to pinpoint specific industries due to rapid changes in AI capabilities
  - Recommendation to invest in GEO across all product categories as AI is becoming ubiquitous
