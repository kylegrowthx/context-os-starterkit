## Key Topics

### Engineer vs Architect Project Management

  - Engineers work on subsets of larger projects, with shorter timelines and higher concurrency
  - Focus on automating bulk actions and speeding up processes (e.g., invoicing, contract management)
  - Less emphasis on long-term planning tools compared to architects

### Engineering Firm Structure and Decision Making

  - Most Monograph clients are pure engineering firms, collaborating with separate architectural firms
  - Key decision-makers: firm principals/owners, project managers, and finance leads (e.g., CFOs, bookkeepers)
  - Financial workflows (invoicing, payments) are often the most complex, requiring buy-in from finance personnel

### Invoicing and Billing Challenges

  - Service-based billing (hourly rates, time tracking) adds complexity
  - Multiple project phases and the need for clear client communication complicate invoicing
  - Generic tools like QuickBooks aren't optimized for these workflows, leading to manual processes

### Monograph Adoption and Success Factors

  - Smaller, less established firms tend to be more adaptable to Monograph's methods
  - Full benefits realized when firms are willing to change multiple workflows (PM, invoicing, staffing)
  - High-volume, quick-action features are particularly valued by engineering firms

### Top Operational Challenges for Engineering Firms

1.  Cash flow management (often due to being "two steps away" from the payer)
2.  Staffing and resource allocation across numerous concurrent projects
3.  Project management logistics (status tracking, on-site work coordination)

### Engineer-Architect Collaboration Issues

  - Communication challenges around timeline changes and scope adjustments
  - Limited direct contact with end clients/developers can complicate project management

### Monograph vs Traditional PM Software

  - Legacy tools often lack usability and modern workflow optimization
  - Monograph's opinionated approach enables better automation and data accuracy
  - Reduced need for dedicated tool management personnel

### Content Strategy for Engineering Firms

1.  Cash flow optimization strategies (invoicing frequency, electronic payments, pricing)
2.  Authentic exploration of automation and AI applications in engineering workflows
3.  Best practices and success stories from peer firms using Monograph
