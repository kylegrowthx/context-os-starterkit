## Key Topics

### Company Backgrounds and Current Initiatives

GrowthX AI is a B2B content marketing agency serving major SaaS companies in the US, with expertise in GEO (Generative AI Optimization) monitoring and actionable insights. Peec AI, based in Berlin, recently raised $7M seed funding and is at $1.5M ARR, growing approximately $200k/week. They launched a self-serve product 3-4 weeks before this meeting and plan a Series B by end of 2026. Both companies are investing heavily in content creation and thought leadership in the GEO space; Peec AI's CMO (Malte Landwehr) has deep SEO/GEO expertise and is building out a content team.

### Content Partnership Opportunities

GrowthX is seeking a partner to create high-quality, data-driven GEO content without a sales-first angle. Peec AI has proprietary data on GEO metrics and tactics, plus a CMO experienced in thought leadership and content production. The two companies discussed joint workshops (GrowthX's Friday session expected to draw 400-600 attendees, mostly from ideal customer profile) followed by collaborative content distribution. The partnership would combine Peec AI's data insights and technical expertise with GrowthX's distribution channels, brand credibility, and workshop audience access.

### GEO Content and Education Gap

Most existing GEO content is sales-driven (e.g., from Scrunch, Profound) rather than educational. Jason noted that neither Scrunch nor Profound prioritizes marketing well enough to create truly independent GEO guidance. The market lacks comprehensive, conclusive answers to foundational GEO questions: which prompts should companies track? How do you measure GEO effectiveness? What does content optimization for LLMs actually look like? Jason and Noah agreed this gap represents an opportunity for credible, non-sales-driven content.

### GEO Tactics and Implementation

GrowthX uses GEO monitoring data to inform content creation strategies for clients. They help clients make content "LLM-friendly" through structure and formatting—adding takeaways at the beginning, FAQs at the end, and better chunking. GrowthX also advises on syndication and community engagement (Reddit, industry forums) to improve citation chances. Jason highlighted that earned channels (syndication, community contributions, PR coverage) shape LLM training data more than owned content, making cross-functional effort (PR, community, product marketing) essential—yet most companies assign GEO to SEO teams only, creating organizational friction.

### Organizational Structure and GEO Maturity

GEO optimization is traditionally falling to SEO teams, but the tactics required (PR, community engagement, product marketing) are outside traditional SEO scope. This creates clunky, siloed execution at most companies. However, larger enterprises are already building dedicated GEO teams: Expedia, for example, is "super deep" into GEO with a dedicated GEO manager and clear strategy. This signals that as GEO traffic grows, companies will need structural changes and dedicated resource allocation.

### Industry Trends and Competitive Landscape

The GEO tooling space is heating up with well-funded competitors: Profound just raised $40M Series A (led by Sequoia), Scrunch raised Series A, and multiple startups are racing to build features. If a major player like Google introduces an AI-first search mode, the entire industry could shift overnight. Enterprises dependent on search traffic (e.g., Expedia) are preparing now. For SaaS companies just exploring GEO, there's urgency around understanding the landscape before Google moves.
