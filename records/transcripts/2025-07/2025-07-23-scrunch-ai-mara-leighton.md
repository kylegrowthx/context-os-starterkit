## Overview

- LLM searches are significantly longer (avg. 23 words) than Google searches (avg. 4 words), focusing on solutions and intent
- AI search optimization still relies heavily on SEO fundamentals; content must rank in top 10-15 results to be considered
- Prompts should be long-tail, conversational, and focused on specific dimensions or use cases rather than broad keywords
- Lists, reviews, and authoritative citations are crucial for establishing credibility in AI search results

---

## Key Topics

### AI Search Query Processing

  - ChatGPT condenses long-tail prompts into core keywords for Bing search
  - Results are then evaluated for credibility using E-E-A-T model
  - Most authoritative sources are synthesized for LLM response
  - Recency bias exists; refreshing content is crucial for maintaining relevance

### Prompt Strategy Optimization

  - Focus on intent and specific dimensions (e.g., product features, customer service)
  - Consider both company perspective and potential customer questions
  - Avoid duplicate prompts with similar core keywords
  - Aim for solution-focused, longer queries (avg. 23 words for LLMs)
  - Iterative process: test prompts and refine based on valuable insights

### Best Practices for AI Optimization

  - Prioritize getting featured in influential lists and review platforms (e.g., G2 for SaaS)
  - Implement schema markup for better AI interpretation of website content
  - Include unique facts, citations, quotes, and statistics to demonstrate authority
  - Consider creating AI-optimized versions of web pages (separate from human-optimized versions)
  - Maintain strong SEO practices as they remain crucial for AI search visibility

### Scrunch AI Tool Usage

  - Use the Insights tab to identify gaps in competitive coverage
  - Analyze condensed search queries to understand how prompts are processed
  - Leverage competitor comparisons but focus on specific attributes or use cases
