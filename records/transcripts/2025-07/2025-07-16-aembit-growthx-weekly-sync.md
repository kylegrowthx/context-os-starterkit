## Summary

GrowthX and Aembit aligned on a streamlined content workflow and management process designed to improve speed and quality. They established a clear weekly cadence: keywords sent via Slack for approval on Wednesday, outlines drafted by Thursday and reviewed Friday with the full Aembit team, and content created the following week. GrowthX committed to transitioning the content tracking system to Airtable with new statuses specific to Aembit's approval workflow, while Aembit will focus review effort on newly created articles using revised artifacts and defer older content to secondary priority. The team also flagged repetitive content in older articles as a legacy artifact of SEO-focused workflows and agreed to monthly product launch check-ins to keep artifacts current.

---

## Context

Aembit is a security infrastructure company and GrowthX is executing a content marketing engagement with them. The relationship is about 2-3 months in at this point (based on references to "before we moved to Atlas" and iterating on artifacts). GrowthX has been delivering blog content around topics like workload identity, API security, and Kubernetes secrets management, tailored to Aembit's technical audience. The challenge Aembit flagged was slow approval cycles and repetitive content from older workflows that prioritized SEO tactics over reader experience. This meeting happened after a week of feedback cycles and is meant to establish a faster, more predictable process where Aembit has clear visibility into what they're reviewing and when.
