## Summary

GrowthX and Datagrid held a strategic conversation about AI adoption trends and Datagrid's product positioning. Thiago da Costa shared that AI adoption is moving beyond search and summarization to executing complex, draining tasks—particularly for industries like construction and engineering that rely on multi-hundred-page documents. A key insight is that AI will first replace tasks people hate doing (like manually sifting through 1,700-page PDFs) rather than entire roles. The team discussed how workforce dynamics are shifting: recent graduates entering the workforce are "AI native" while experienced workers who mastered AI through self-directed learning have a significant advantage, similar to early internet adoption.

---

## Key Topics

### AI Adoption Trends

  - Initial AI use cases focused on search and summarization
  - Current shift towards automating complex, tedious tasks people dislike
  - Example: Analyzing 1,700-page PDFs to extract specific information (e.g., electrical installation tasks in construction schedules)
  - Gap between finding information and executing tasks is closing
  - Prediction: AI will first replace tasks people hate doing, not entire roles

### AI Proficiency and Workforce Dynamics

  - Recent graduates and those learning AI now have an advantage
  - Experienced workers struggle to infuse their expertise with AI capabilities
  - Mastering AI could become a valuable career skill
  - Comparison to early internet adoption: those who mastered it gained significant advantages
  - Current AI users either self-taught (like Marcel learning until midnight) or new workforce entrants

### Datagrid's AI Approach

  - Focus on anticipating user needs and completing multi-step processes
  - Example: Filing specific document sections into SharePoint without manual steps
  - AI should understand the final objective and work towards it proactively
  - Document intelligence core to their platform—extracting meaning from unstructured engineering specs and construction schedules
  - Enhances user output, e.g., improving LinkedIn bios or creating detailed HubSpot deals

### AI as a Collaborative Tool

  - Shift from using AI as autocomplete to treating it as a colleague
  - AI can provide comprehensive insights and prepare users for meetings
  - Example: Analyzing calendar events and providing detailed background on attendees
  - Datagrid pioneering workflows that go beyond simple Q&A interactions
  - Physical-world industries (construction, manufacturing) produce vast documentation for inter-team communication where AI can add value
