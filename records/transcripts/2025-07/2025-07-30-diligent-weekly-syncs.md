## Key Topics

### Content Audit and Unified View

GrowthX created a unified content view in ContentOS combining GSC URLs, SEMrush keyword rankings, publish dates, and three-month performance comparisons for all content from 2021-2025. The audit identified 150 blogs losing significant traffic in the last three months. Recommended actions for each piece: merge (consolidate similar content), prune (remove outdated posts), refresh (update high-potential underperformers), or keep (maintain strong performers, especially governance and nonprofit content serving non-board customers). The team also surfaced 404 errors from Diligent's prior migration and provided redirect mapping to Sanity CMS.

### Performance Goals and Product Page Focus

Tyler emphasized that while the North Star is 50% qualified organic traffic, the team should focus on two controllable metrics: (1) ranking strength for core product pages (GRC, ERM, board management) and (2) qualified MQL generation. Product page ranking is currently a weakness; branded search is strong. Tyler wants to avoid attributing MQL shortfalls to SEO when the actual problem is internal conversion funnel or seasonality. He proposed using paid search budget to test messaging and validate what drives conversions before scaling organic efforts, creating a faster feedback loop for content strategy.

### Content Refresh Workflow

GrowthX will select one blog identified as losing traffic, run it through the article refresher (using updated artifacts from Eleni's product conversation), and submit for review Friday. Feedback from John and Tyler will establish a template for scaling to remaining refreshes without requiring extensive comment cycles. The team has created Notion docs for artifacts (company brief, audience personas, writing guidelines) which will evolve as Diligent's product messaging changes.

### LLM Visibility (Crunch Tool)

Crunch monitors Diligent's presence in ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, and Gemini responses by periodically running curated prompts and analyzing answers. Current competitive presence: 42%, leading vs. OnBoard, BoardVantage, and Zencast. The tool tracks sentiment (positive/negative/mixed) and citation counts. Tyler can improve prompt accuracy by feeding questions from Gong calls or sales conversations into the system, ensuring Crunch tracks language prospects actually use.

### Tech Stack and Access

GrowthX is using Notion, Airtable, Looker, SEMrush, GA4, Google Search Console, and Diligent's Sanity CMS. Tyler requested visibility into the full tech stack structure and whether accounts are client-owned or agency-owned, so he can build similar monitoring for Diligent's internal teams. Jo noted that ContentOS (the Airtable table) serves as the source of truth for decisions, with SEMrush data exported there. Jo to investigate giving Tyler SEMrush viewing access.

### Future Opportunities

Tyler is interested in leveraging agentic AI for internal linking optimization (using Claude or similar to scan XML, contextualize content, and recommend internal links). This would reduce manual effort on tactical SEO tasks and free up budget for strategic experiments and content development. GrowthX can also expand scope to include net new content creation and comprehensive editorial strategy once refresh workflows stabilize.
