- Publication plan: Substack is obvious choice over building new website; gives variety beyond current playbook-heavy content

### "Post-Browser World" Concept

- Guillaume's emerging thesis: AI agents (ChatGPT, Claude, etc.) will mediate purchases through chat, eliminating browser-based e-commerce
- Scenario: Customer describes jewelry purchase criteria in chat → AI presents carousel of options → customer picks option B and says "order it" → AI completes purchase through MCP (Model Context Protocol) with stored payment details
- Outcome: Shopify dead, search ads dead, retargeting dead, email newsletters dead, traditional marketing funnels extinct (no human touches e-commerce site anymore, only AI agent)
- Incentive layer: LLMs take transaction fees (30 cents to 1 euro per order) to reduce friction
- Resistance: Some consumers enjoy "window shopping" experience, but efficiency-focused users (like Guillaume) will adopt frictionless purchase model
- B2B application: ZoomInfo data queries through chat (pull 50,000 CTOs, export CSV) already possible via MCP; missing piece is payment layer so LLM can autonomously execute data purchases
- Timeline: Technology (payment integrations) is the blocker, not consumer adoption

### B2C vs. B2B Dynamics

- B2C: Post-browser world likely to start here; Marcel sees himself as "Subscribe and forget" user (Amazon Subscribe model)
- B2B: Less obvious path (unlikely to auto-sign contracts), but transaction-based models (ZoomInfo, transactional tools) could shift to LLM-mediated purchasing
- Guillaume's broader claim: This mindset will "disseminate" through both B2C and B2B by year-end

### AI Coding & Vibe Coding

- Two flavors of AI coding tools: Cursor (for engineers, actually helpful) vs. Replit (for non-engineers, less effective)
- Observation: Vibe coding not yet mature enough to replace full product engineering teams (cited Jason and others publicly struggling)
- Implication: IC automation is progressing; management automation is not

### LinkedIn Engagement Strategy

- Marcel's recent post hit 700+ comments
- Strategy: Post quality, thought-provoking content → commenters engage → Marcel DMs each commenter with follow-up link/ask for feedback → creates multi-message conversation thread
- LinkedIn algorithm heavily weights back-and-forth DM conversations; next post shows up first in feeds of people you've messaged
- Marcel's philosophy: Quality over frequency (not posting daily; when he posts, it drives real engagement and signal)
- Aida's hot take: Doesn't include emojis in LinkedIn posts (counter to typical LinkedIn style)
