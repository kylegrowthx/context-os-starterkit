## Summary

GrowthX and Smith.ai aligned on three key priorities: (1) repositioning Smith.ai as a technical authority in AI calling by building a call flow design content hub with a 5,000-word pillar article and supporting technical content, complete with visual diagrams and glossary definitions; (2) launching an urgent website content refresh across ~60 pages (30 features + 30 industry pages) to position Smith.ai as an AI receptionist service with human backup rather than a live virtual receptionist service, driven by board visibility; and (3) continuing work with the Raze team on estimate template improvements, including logo upload functionality and cleaner terminology. The AI receptionist content refresh is now priority #1 due to SEO and functional impact, with the call flow design hub and estimate template work to follow once the website updates are complete.

## Context

Smith.ai is an AI receptionist service provider that uses AI to answer calls and qualify leads, with live human agents available for escalation and backup. GrowthX is running their content marketing engagement, focusing on positioning Smith.ai as a technical thought leader in AI calling and updating their website to emphasize AI-first positioning while maintaining their human-backup value proposition. The relationship is strategic and ongoing: GrowthX has already completed work on estimate templates and is now expanding into a major website positioning refresh and technical content hub development. Maddy Martin leads product and strategy at Smith.ai; the GrowthX team (Megan Dickey, Elvis Goren, Andi Bailey) handles content, design, and delivery. This meeting reflects a shifting priority at Smith.ai's leadership level — the board is pushing for stronger public positioning of their AI capabilities, which requires rapid, systematic updates to high-traffic pages that currently emphasize live human receptionists over AI.
