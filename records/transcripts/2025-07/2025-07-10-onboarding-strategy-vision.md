## Relevance

**To GrowthX Delivery:**
- Design workflows should follow an 80/20 split: systematize the 80% of routine work (templates, components, building blocks) to free up 20% for high-value creative expertise and innovation.
- Reusable visual components and guardrails can accelerate delivery without compromising quality — referenced Canva's three-to-four-element approach as successful model.
- Mental models and frameworks should have visual language (e.g., Splatch PMM style) that can be templated and adapted for social assets, article enrichment, and client deliverables.
- AI-assisted tagging and mood board databases can streamline mood boarding and archetype creation, reducing research time from "100 hours" to automated efficiency.

- The "messy middle" content approach (sharing process, thought process, and iteration) should extend to visual design — showing how design decisions are made can become marketing content itself.
- Mood boarding and visual archetype work for client brands mirrors the writing archetype process already in use — same systems thinking applies across modalities (graphics, motion, video, text).

**To GrowthX Business Development:**
- Katia brings agency experience and AI-integrated creative workflows — talent acquisition signal of GrowthX's shift toward AI-augmented services rather than traditional creative labor.
- Visual system templates and building blocks are sellable client deliverables (article enrichment, social post templates, brand frameworks) — opportunity to package design systems as premium service offerings.
- Design focus on clarity and simplicity (three-to-four elements, guardrails, templates) aligns with CheckThat's mission of AI visibility and clarity in complex domains.

## Key Topics

### Company Structure and Vision

  - Three-layer model: service, AI/technology (workflow), and expert layers
  - Aiming to build a portfolio of incubated ventures, similar to Tiny (owns Dribbble, etc.)
  - Focus on building a common core and community, not tied to a single brand
  - Emphasis on authenticity, vulnerability, and human connection in marketing and community building

### Design Philosophy and Approach

  - Balance between systemization and creativity
  - Create reusable components and guardrails while allowing for creative flexibility
  - Streamline workflows (80%) to focus more on expertise and creative input (20%)
  - Utilize mood boarding and archetype creation for brand and content development
  - Explore AI-assisted tagging and database creation for efficient mood board generation

### Content Creation and Visual Systems

  - Develop visual language for mental models and frameworks
  - Create easily adaptable templates for social posts, article enrichment, and client deliverables
  - Ensure consistency across various content types (social, articles, videos)
  - Example: Splatch PMM's simple yet effective visual style for communicating concepts

### Katia's Motivation and Background

  - Transitioning from traditional agency work to AI-integrated creative processes
  - Seeking opportunities to learn and incorporate new tools into workflow
  - Attracted to GrowthX's unique position between agency and tech startup
