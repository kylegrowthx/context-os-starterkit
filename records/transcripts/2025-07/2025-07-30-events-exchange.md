## Key Topics

### Event Types and Strategies

  - Two main event types: third-party conferences with adjacent activations and customer dinners
  - Initially focused on larger dinners (50-70 people), now pivoting to smaller, more intimate gatherings (18-25 people)
  - Emphasis on choosing exclusive venues that attendees can't easily access otherwise
  - For larger networking events, aim for 200+ people

### Venue Recommendations

  - High-end options: Quince, Benu, Lazy Bear, Angler SF
  - Mid-range options: The Progress, Flour + Water, Sorrel, Cotogna
  - Unique experiences: Mr. Jiu's (with Moon Gate Lounge), Californios (full buyout only)
  - New hotspot: The Shota (Japanese cuisine near Moscone Center)

### Logistics and Planning

  - Use Airtable for event management and task assignment
  - Create a timeline with at least 2-3 months lead time for larger events
  - Develop a strategy brief to share with the team
  - Use a "shot clock" system in Airtable to track task progress
  - For dinners, coordinate outreach with sales team to prioritize invitees
  - Send pre-event emails 2 days before to confirm attendance and share details

### Attendee Management

  - Create tiered invite lists: priority, short list, and long list
  - Send invitations in waves to manage limited space effectively
  - Keep a record of declined invitations for future events
  - Work with executives to plan seating arrangements

### In-House Events

  - For office happy hours, consider custom cocktails and appetizers
  - Explore unique variations like wine tastings or themed experiences
  - Use resources like SF Travel Association and The Knot for vendor recommendations
