## Summary

GrowthX completed June with strong cash management — net cash out of $374k (down $300k from May) and $10.3M cash on hand, plus $52k investment income. The team identified critical improvements needed in expense tracking and communication, particularly around pass-through costs and RAMP card governance, and outlined a potential restructuring of the delivery team from $2.8M to $1.6M annual costs while maintaining quality. Key focus areas include defining a narrower ICP to reduce the 14% churn rate, standardizing the software expense audit, and improving alignment between delivery, finance, and operations on costs and billing.

## Context

This was an internal GrowthX financial review involving the CEO (Marcel Santilli), CFO (Ericksson Abad), COO/Operations (Bridget McGillivray), and a team member (Daniel Lopes). The meeting covered June 2025 results and explored cost optimization and restructuring opportunities. The context is a maturing B2B SaaS-adjacent company (content marketing services at $200k+/year engagements) with ~$10M in cash reserves, actively managing delivery team costs and struggling with expense tracking and pass-through billing accuracy. ## Relevance

**To GrowthX Delivery:** Proposed restructuring could cut delivery team costs from $2.8M to $1.6M annually (43% reduction) by right-sizing the Content Manager layer and focusing on hiring 8-16 account managers per high performer. This directly impacts project staffing, QA, and delivery timelines. Current challenge: tracking pass-through expenses (video production, freelancer costs) that clients should pay for — Marcel estimated $30-40k in untracked Surge/Metronome video shoot costs in June alone. Better documentation system needed.

**To GrowthX Business Development:** Churn stands at 14% — goal is single digits within three months. Strong client feedback from Monograph, Engine.com, and Fathom on service quality. Team consensus: narrower ICP (technical companies and startups with limited in-house marketing) and more rigorous prospect qualification will improve retention. Revenue recognition opportunity: video production costs can be billed to clients to offset delivery expenses.

**To GrowthX Operations:** Expense tracking is a critical weakness. 500+ transactions in June created reconciliation burden on Ericksson. RAMP card governance needs enforcement: users must upload receipts, add memos, categorize spend (COGS vs Marketing vs R&D), or cards will be suspended. Identified quick wins: $50k+ in software subscriptions can be trimmed (AirOps going to $0, Airtable being rationalized); software audit should break down by department (APD, Delivery, Go-to-Market). Implementation by Ericksson (CFO) and Jason (operations) — Bridget to set up cross-functional communication cadence.

## Key Topics

### Financial Performance

  - June net cash out: $374k ($300k less than May)
  - Cash on hand: $10.3M across all accounts
  - P\&L net loss: $522k (higher due to accrued expenses)
  - Investment income: $52k from May and June

### Expense Management

  - Credit card payment timing issue ($200k) to be resolved in July
  - 500+ transactions processed in June
  - Need for improved expense tracking and communication, especially for marketing expenses
  - Proposal to use expiring cards for one-off expenses to prevent forgotten subscriptions

### Delivery Team Restructuring

  - Potential to reduce delivery team costs from $2.8M to $1.6M annually
  - Proposed structure: 5 senior-level (Ada/George equivalent), 5 mid-level (Sydney equivalent), and 14 junior-level staff
  - Focus on hiring high-performers who can manage 8-16 accounts each
  - Aim to eliminate most of the CM (Content Manager) layer, retaining only top performers

### Client Retention and Growth

  - Positive feedback from clients like Monograph, Engine.com, and Fathom
  - Need to define and target ideal customer profile (ICP) more precisely
  - Goal to reduce churn from 14% to single digits within three months
  - Focus on technical companies and startups with limited marketing teams

### Marketing and Events Strategy

  - Plan to offset video production costs by having clients pay for content
  - Upcoming events in September (Inbound conference, Place conference)
  - Aim to keep marketing costs below 10% of revenue
  - Need for clearer guidelines on event planning and budgeting for marketing team

### Revenue Recognition and Accounting

  - Discussion on how to account for pass-through expenses and potential new service offerings
  - Consideration of recognizing certain expenses as revenue when appropriate
