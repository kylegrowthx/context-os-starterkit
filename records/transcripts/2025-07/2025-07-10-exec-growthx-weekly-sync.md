## Key Topics

### GrowthX Marketing Progress and LLM Visibility Strategy

- Nearly 2K monthly visitors (28-day period), substantial growth from January baseline
- Strong LLM referrals across ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Claude; Gemini and Google significantly behind
- SEMrush showing increased keyword rankings with first-page SERPs climbing
- Hypothesis on Google lag: traditional search has slower recency bias than ChatGPT/Perplexity; LLMs may be reading older content or using different ranking criteria
- LLM discovery insight: LLMs don't read full article length, so brands must appear in top 25-30% of content to be cited or recommended

### Competitive Analysis and Content Strategy

- Competitors consistently showing up in Gemini/Google but not in ChatGPT/Perplexity: Pitch Monster, Awareathon (despite inactive blog since 2023)
- Pitch Monster requires deeper competitive research: may have marketing pages (not blog) ranking well
- Strategy: Create "top 10" comparison articles targeting various keyword variations (e.g., "AI training scenario," "AI feedback," "AI for coaching") to capture diverse search intent
- "Discover the Five Best AI Roleplay Platforms" article currently performs well in references but list appears 60% down the page, limiting LLM visibility and brand mentions
- Decision: Move product list to top of article, remove Utili competitor from recommendations, add table of contents for better structure

### Profound Content Cluster Expansion

- Existing AI role plays cluster performing well but needs expansion and refinement
- Remove stale AI feedback cluster entirely
- Expand AI role plays cluster to cover: sales training, onboarding, coaching, manager training/development, change management, customer support
- Create new L&D + AI combination cluster; massive untapped opportunity with no competing content online
- Strategy: Build internal database of Exec features and use to fuel detailed blog content on L&D applications

### Exec Product Demo: Roleplay Capabilities and Differentiation

- Demonstrated ease of creating detailed scenarios with deep character backstories and situational triggers
- Key differentiator: granular access controls (personal/workspace/test account specific)
- Scenario tagging and organization: lets large teams organize and find specific scenario types (QA, sales, etc.)
- Auto-generation of scenarios: users tell Exec what they want, system generates custom scenarios they can tweak or keep as-is
- Industry-specific auto-customization: team now working towards landing pages targeting healthcare, auto dealerships, insurance; self-serve signup will auto-customize global scenarios for each industry
- AI-to-AI roleplay testing: can run two AIs against each other to evaluate bot performance with custom character constraints and Gen Z persona options
- Platform depth widely perceived as superior to competitors but messaging and positioning still emerging

### Upcoming Product Features and Roadmap

- New Head of Growth (Garrett) joining July 28 to ensure follow-through on committed deliverables
- Self-serve signup with industry-specific onboarding and auto-customized scenarios (major milestone)
- Industry-specific use case pages (healthcare, auto, insurance) with relevant value propositions
- Interactive demo widgets: working towards embeds for website and blog; users get ~1 minute to try scenario before sign-up gate
- Alternative demo format: recording clips of most engaging/compelling parts of scenarios for passive viewing
- Website scenario previews coming before blog rollout

### AI Technology Stack and Preferences

- Exec uses GPT-4.0 for conversational roleplay (not newer reasoning models like Grok); prefers personality and instruction-following over pure reasoning
- Reasoning models (Grok, etc.) excel at math/engineering but have "wrong vibes" and weaker personalities for conversational use
- Eleven Labs voice synthesis selected for quality; team waiting for new high-fidelity model to optimize for real-time performance (currently too slow)
