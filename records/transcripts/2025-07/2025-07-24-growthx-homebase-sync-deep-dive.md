## Summary

GrowthX and Homebase conducted a strategic deep-dive to review progress on Homebase's goal to double starter trials by year-end, with emphasis on organic growth and SEO. Andi Bailey presented a high-level audit of the content work completed (260+ articles published, state payroll pages in review), new SEO workflows, and Atlas tooling for scaled content production, while also flagging that recurring alignment discussions suggest the need to reset on brand voice, ICP, and content strategy artifacts. The team discussed growth targets (moving from 10% to 50% growth company), explored AI-driven content strategies including TLDR sections and LLM citation tracking, and committed to follow-up actions on timeline clarity, NPS feedback collection, and next meeting scheduling.

## Context

Homebase is a scheduling, payroll, and workforce management platform for small businesses. GrowthX has been working with Homebase for several months on a multi-faceted content strategy aimed at driving organic sign-ups and conversions. Kerry McCreadie leads content strategy at Homebase, recently joined by Sonia Urlando (who is rethinking brand voice and positioning) and Mark Velarga. On the GrowthX side, Andi Bailey oversees the account with delivery support from Jakub Rudnik (SEO), Jess Scott (operations), and Bisera Stankovska. Homebase's financial performance is tracking at 105% of plan for H2, but the company is shifting its growth strategy to reduce reliance on paid acquisition and double down on organic channels where they see superior conversion rates (SEO drives 33% of paid customer MRR despite only 20% of sign-ups). Prior alignment calls revealed recurring discussions about content direction and strategy, prompting this deeper sync to reset on shared understanding of brand voice, ideal customer profile, and how to structure deliverables.

## Key Topics

### Current Performance and Goals

  - Homebase is trending at 105% of financial plan goal for H2
  - Primary KPIs: organic sign-ups and "starter" conversions (new businesses)
  - SEO converts better: 20% of sign-ups but 33% of paid customers and total MRR
  - Aim to reduce reliance on paid acquisition and strengthen organic channels

### Product Strengths and Customer Feedback

  - Ease of use in scheduling, especially recreating past schedules
  - Seamless integration of time tracking and scheduling with payroll
  - Strong mobile experience for employees
  - Comprehensive ecosystem for new businesses (job posting, recruitment, onboarding, scheduling, time tracking, payroll)

### Content Strategy and SEO Improvements

  - Content strategy has evolved from "slapping up blogs" to more strategic, regular, and quality-focused approach
  - Need to strengthen SEO and GEO as subchannels within organic
  - GrowthX presented workflows for article refreshes and PSEO to scale content production
  - Plans to implement AI-driven content strategies, including TLDR sections and potential ChatGPT integration

### Competitive Landscape and Integration

  - Integration with existing systems (QuickBooks, Gusto, POS) is a key consideration for prospects
  - Homebase's built-in payroll is a unique selling point among scheduling competitors
  - Challenge: prospects often already have payroll systems in place, making it harder to switch

### AI and LLM Strategies

  - Implementing TLDR sections for better Google indexing and LLM citations
  - Exploring tools like Scrunch for LLM citation tracking and insights
  - Considering experiments with AI-summarized content and ChatGPT integration

### Growth Challenges and Opportunities

  - Need to mature organic channels to reduce reliance on paid acquisition
  - Goal: transition from 10% growth to 50% growth company
  - Opportunity to leverage SEO's higher conversion rates to drive more sign-ups and paid customers
