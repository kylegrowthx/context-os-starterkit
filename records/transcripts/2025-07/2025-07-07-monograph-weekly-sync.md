## Summary

GrowthX and Monograph aligned on a content strategy to drive SQLs from 30 to 60 per month within six months by balancing authority-building topics with high-intent keywords targeting both architects and engineers. The strategy focuses on three content clusters: practice management (expanding to engineers), financial performance, and software comparisons, with particular emphasis on winning against Blueprints/BQE in engineering keywords where Monograph currently lacks visibility. GrowthX outlined its methodology around input-focused metrics, voice and tone alignment, and persona differentiation, with immediate next steps being scheduling product deep dives with Monograph leads (Robert Yuen and Chris Morgan) to gather product nuance, use case details, and AI feature information before accelerating content creation.

## Context

Monograph is a practice management platform for architecture and engineering firms. This is an engagement review sync following Monograph's kickoff call with GrowthX, where Monograph is seeking to double inbound SQLs (from 30 to 60/month) through a six-month content strategy. Monograph has substantial existing content for its primary architect audience (8-15 person residential architecture firms) but limited engineering firm content, and BQE/Blueprints currently dominates SEO visibility in engineering-focused keywords. Chris Morgan (Monograph's content lead) had just returned from leave and was joining the engagement; this meeting brought the full Monograph team up to speed on GrowthX's content strategy approach, competitor positioning, and immediate next steps. GrowthX positioned this engagement as fast-tracked compared to typical onboarding because of Monograph's well-defined audience, existing content foundation, and clear category positioning.

## Key Topics

### Content Strategy Overview

  - Balancing authority-building topics with high SEO potential
  - Targeting both architects and engineers, acknowledging differences in content needs
  - Focusing on practice management, financial performance, and software comparisons
  - Refreshing existing content and creating new pages based on keyword opportunities
  - Emphasizing practical, direct language in content creation

### Persona and Audience Insights

  - Primary target: residential architecture firms (8-15 people, expanding to 5-50)
  - Engineering firms now a key focus, with less existing content
  - Architects: more artistic, less focused on business aspects
  - Engineers: more systematic, quantitative thinkers
  - Both use spreadsheets extensively, presenting an opportunity for Monograph

### Content Refresh and SEO Strategy

  - Analyzing internal URLs for keyword ranking and click potential
  - Prioritizing content updates based on search volume and keyword difficulty
  - Emphasizing recency and well-cited content to improve LLM citations
  - Exploring engineering-specific keywords where Monograph currently lacks visibility

### Voice and Tone Guidelines

  - Practical, direct communication style
  - Transparent expertise: confident but not arrogant
  - Reader-focused approach, emphasizing empathy and real-world examples
  - Highlighting the contrast between project management skills and business management

### AI Content Opportunities

  - Exploring long-tail keywords combining AI with specific use cases
  - Leveraging Monograph's system of record positioning for a strong AI story
  - Considering content around time tracking for specific engineering disciplines
