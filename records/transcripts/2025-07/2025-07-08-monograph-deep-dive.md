## Overview

- Monograph is prioritizing three key areas for Q3: new feature releases (including AI integration), engineering-focused content, and developing the "Monograph Method"
- Core differentiators are ease-of-use and industry-specific features, summarized as "designed for architects by architects"
- Key pain points for customers include project financial management, invoicing, and handling paused projects
- Content strategy should balance addressing specific use cases with targeting high-volume search terms

---

## Key Topics

### Q3 Product Strategy

  - Three main focus areas:
    1.  New feature releases (including AI integration)
    2.  Engineering-focused content
    3.  Developing the "Monograph Method"
  - Staggered approach: July for new features, August for engineering content, September for Monograph Method
  - Aim to position Monograph as an AI-enabled project management solution for architects

### Core Product Features

  - Project Overview: Visual Gantt-style chart showing all projects in one view
  - MoneyGantt: Unique feature visualizing project finances across stages (budgeted, time spent, invoiced, paid)
  - Invoicing: Addresses major pain point of billing for long-term projects (avg. 18 months, $100k)

### Customer Segments and Use Cases

  - Primary users: Architectural firms, varying from disengaged principals to hands-on managers
  - Key use cases:
    1.  Project creation and proposal building (AI-enabled)
    2.  Staff allocation and availability tracking
    3.  Financial management across multiple long-term projects
    4.  Handling paused projects (25-30% of projects typically on pause)

### Content Strategy

  - Balance between addressing specific use cases and targeting high-volume search terms
  - Opportunity to create dedicated pages for unique features (e.g., MoneyGantt)
  - Leverage existing case studies, particularly those with metrics (e.g., Brooks and Scarpa, Workbench, Woodhull, Garrison)
  - Develop content around the "Monograph Method" - best practices tied to product features

### SEO and CRO Considerations

  - Current top-performing content: "Best project management software for architects" blog post
  - Opportunity to optimize landing pages for search, not just blog content
  - Consider AEO (AI Engine Optimization) tactics, such as incorporating customer testimonials and feature highlights on landing pages
