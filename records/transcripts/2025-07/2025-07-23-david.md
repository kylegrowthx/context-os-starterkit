## Summary

David Walker, Director of Engineering at Aembit, discussed the company's relentless focus on quality and enterprise-grade stability (targeting 4-5 nines availability), positioning Aembit to compete in high-stakes environments like FinTech where outages cost millions per minute. He emphasized that hands-on leadership—with engineering leaders coding alongside their teams—is non-negotiable, even at scale. The key challenge: reducing implementation complexity and cognitive load for customers over the next 3-6 months, particularly as Aembit scales to support thousands of workloads. Forward-looking, David sees the convergence of workload IAM and user IAM as a major growth vector, especially around developer workstations in an agentic-AI future.

## Context

Aembit is a three-year-old identity and access management (IAM) company focused on workload IAM—managing credentials and access for automated systems, APIs, and microservices. David Walker has deep roots with the founders (20+ years), having known them from TristDigital, and joined three and a half years ago. GrowthX is working with Aembit on content strategy and deeper positioning, partly to address the perception that young companies have lower quality standards.
