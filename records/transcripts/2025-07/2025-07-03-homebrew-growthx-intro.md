## Context

Homebrew is an early-stage venture capital fund founded in 2012 by Hunter Walk and Satya Patel after they left prominent roles at Google/YouTube and Twitter. The fund operates an unusual model: rather than raising money from limited partners and being beholden to return multiples, Homebrew is now self-funded (as of Fund 4 in 2022), giving founders more flexibility in deal structures and terms. They make 10-15 investments per year, focused on seed and pre-seed rounds ($100K-$500K), with a philosophy of being "in the industry, but not of the industry" — offering founders a diverse toolkit beyond just capital.

GrowthX is a content-as-a-service company founded by Marcel Santilli that has evolved from a workshop-based offering into a full-service AI-powered platform. Marcel built his reputation over years in large enterprise marketing (IBM, HP, HashiCorp) before spending time at DeepGram developing AI workflows for content at scale. GrowthX now offers monthly retainer engagements ($9K-$60K/month) with initial 8-week strategy sprints, using a proprietary AI workflow engine paired with human editorial experts. This was an introductory conversation to explore potential alignment and investment opportunity for GrowthX's Series A.
