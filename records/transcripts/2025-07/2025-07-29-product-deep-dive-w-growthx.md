## Summary

Eleni Vokas from Diligent walked GrowthX through Diligent Boards' AI-powered feature suite—including Smart Board Book Builder, Smart Risk Scanner, SmartPrep, and AI Smart Minutes—and positioned the product for SMB companies preparing for major transactions (IPO, M&A, Series A-D). The conversation focused on how AI differentiates Diligent from competitors, SMB market triggers (adding external investors, formalizing governance), the product suite (board portal, virtual data room, NetSuite integration), and how personas differ between SMBs and enterprises—specifically that smaller companies rely more on CFOs and executive leadership for board prep while enterprises have dedicated corporate secretaries and legal teams.

## Key Topics

### AI Capabilities in Diligent Boards

- Smart Board Book Builder: Synthesizes raw information into first draft, saving time
- Smart Risk Scanner: Reviews documents for risky language before publishing
- AI Smart Book Summary: Summarizes long board books for quick review
- SmartPrep: Generates questions and discussion topics for meeting preparation
- AI Smart Minutes: Converts meeting transcripts into minutes or drafts based on board book
- AI Smart News and Competitive Insights: Delivers industry news to dashboard

### SMB Market Strategy

- Narrative focuses on preparing companies for transactions (IPO, M&A, Series A-D)
- Suite includes board management software, virtual data room (Diligent Data Room), and board reporting (NetSuite integration)
- Target audience: Companies with $10M-$500M revenue, emphasis on tech startups
- Key trigger: Adding external institutional investors, formalizing board structure

### Global Product Considerations

- Multi-language support for meetings and documents
- Potential security adaptations for different regions (specifics to be provided later)
- No significant product differences noted between US and international markets

### Corporate Secretary/General Counsel Persona

- SMBs: Board meeting prep often involves CFO and executive leadership due to flatter structures
- Enterprises: More robust legal departments, dedicated corporate secretaries handle board meeting preparation
