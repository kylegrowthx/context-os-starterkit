## Key Topics

### Town's Client Base and Tax Manager Role

  - Jess interacts directly with clients, prepares tax packages, and conducts reviews
  - Primary clients: tech startups (C-Corps), with a mix of S-Corps and partnerships
  - Small business clients range from personal training to consulting and rental real estate

### Startup vs. SMB Tax Planning Distinctions

  - Startups: Focus on R\&D expenses, startup costs, and organizational expenses
  - Startups often have 3-4 years of product development before profitability
  - SMBs: Generally ready to generate revenue immediately, fewer startup-specific expenses

### Common SMB Tax Misconceptions and Mistakes

  - Partnerships: Partners incorrectly paying themselves as employees
      - Consequences: Potential IRS issues, difficulty reclaiming paid taxes
      - Solution: Correct ASAP, use guaranteed payments instead of payroll
  - S-Corps: Owners unaware of limitations on deductions (e.g., home office, mileage)
      - Solution: Implement an accountable plan through payroll for similar benefits
  - Distributions in excess of basis: Often occurs due to lack of regular tax planning
      - Can result in unexpected taxation on distributions

### Tax Planning for Rapid Revenue Growth

  - Crucial to adjust estimated tax payments to avoid year-end surprises
  - Opportunity to leverage losses or explore tax credits (e.g., vehicle purchases)
  - Importance of quarterly check-ins for proactive planning

### Business Entity Structures for Different Industries

  - Rental real estate: Partnership model preferred over C-Corp or S-Corp
      - Benefits: Step-up in basis, ability to use debt as basis
  - Medical professionals: Typically S-Corps for individual practices, partnerships for larger groups
  - Entity choice depends on specific business goals and activities

### Rental Property Tax Considerations

  - Common mistakes in depreciation calculations:
      - Incorrect basis establishment (e.g., excluding closing costs)
      - Misclassification of improvements vs. repairs
  - Cost segregation studies: More applicable to manufacturing than residential/commercial rentals

### Tax Optimization for Medical Professionals

  - Often overlooked deductions: Travel expenses for continuing education, speaking engagements
  - Equipment depreciation: Utilize bonus depreciation and Section 179 when appropriate

### Retirement Planning for Small Business Owners

  - Major overlooked opportunity for tax optimization across all business types
  - Small business owners often lack knowledge about available retirement plan options beyond 401(k)s and IRAs
  - Complexity of choosing appropriate plans (e.g., SIMPLE IRA vs. SEP IRA) can be overwhelming
