## Key Topics

### Julius AI Overview

- AI-powered data analysis tool for students, professors, and knowledge workers
- 2 million users, primarily from academic community
- Shifting focus towards business users in marketing, finance, ops, logistics, etc.
- Main acquisition channel: Google search; secondary: word-of-mouth
- Currently developing features like data connectors for business users

### GrowthX's Approach and Capabilities

- Hybrid of AI-powered platform and expert services (editorial team, strategy)
- 70+ employees with editorial expertise from TechCrunch, ClickUp, TripAdvisor
- Focus on creating high-quality, persona-driven content that drives SEO and conversion
- AI workflows include research (Perplexity Pro), drafting, fact-checking, internal linking, rewriting
- Every workflow step includes human editorial review; no step is fully automated

### GrowthX's Strategy Sprint Model

- 6-8 week engagement structure
- Develops Ideal Customer Profiles (ICPs), voice/style guides, content roadmap
- Includes customer interviews and discussions with Julius's technical teams
- Generates artifacts (personas, guidelines) used as inputs for content workflows
- Calibrates on content quality; establishes "definition of done" before scaling
- No pressure to continue beyond strategy phase; clients can use sprint artifacts independently

### AI and Human Collaboration in Content Creation

- Every content piece is reviewed and edited by managing editors at multiple stages
- Workflows are modular and tool-agnostic; GrowthX uses Perplexity, ChatGPT, Claude, SEMrush, etc., interchangeably
- Fact-checking workflow: extracts passages, generates validation questions, scores confidence, flags inaccuracies for human review or rewriting
- Handles high-stakes content (medical, legal) through expert review or restricted-source verification

### Expanding Beyond Academic Users: Strategic Considerations

- Julius's core revenue still comes from academic users; expansion must not cannibalize that
- GrowthX approach: segment personas carefully, tailor UX and positioning for each, create overlapping content where possible
- Multi-touch journey required; no single-channel solution
- Personas for Julius: academic users (students, professors) vs. business users (individual contributors, managers, data analysts in non-tech companies)
