## Summary

Marcel Santilli (GrowthX) connected with Beeri Amiel and Neri Bluman (XFunnel) to explore complementary positioning in AI-driven marketing services. XFunnel is leveraging AI search visibility as a wedge into the $850 billion marketing agency industry, while GrowthX delivers high-touch, scaled services with AI agents. ## Key Topics

### Company Backgrounds and Approaches

- XFunnel started with AI search visibility as a wedge into broader marketing services, building on Beeri's experience in complex operations (Avro, Convoy) and Neri's startup ecosystem exposure
- GrowthX focuses on content creation and workflow automation with a strong services component, leveraging AI agents for delivery at scale
- Both companies emphasize the importance of human expertise alongside AI tools—hybrid models, not pure SaaS
- GrowthX has grown to 70 employees since January 2025 with nearly $10 million in annual revenue

### Market Opportunity and Challenges

- Traditional digital marketing agency industry estimated at $850 billion annually
- Many existing agencies are slow, unprofessional, and not tech-savvy—ripe for disruption
- Challenge: managing customer expectations and educating clients on what "good" looks like in modern marketing
- Opportunity to build a new layer between customers and traditional agencies, particularly around AI-driven strategy and content
- Both teams are deliberately avoiding the VC race-to-consolidate-a-category dynamic, prioritizing sustainable margins and founder control

### AI Search and Content Strategy

- Focus on behavior change: longer, more specific search queries as users adapt to AI search interfaces
- Content creation needs to address the long tail of specific questions, not just broad head terms
- Importance of off-site content (85-95% of content AI search engines consider), shifting investment from on-site optimization
- Need for a holistic approach: content creation, distribution, and continuous optimization
- XFunnel beginning to expand beyond AI search visibility into execution and partner enablement

### Business Models and Growth

- Both companies currently avoid self-serve models, focusing on high-touch service
- GrowthX building a common core (workflow engine, AI coding agent, runtime layer) with potential for future platformization
- Challenges in scaling service businesses while maintaining quality and profitability—requires agentic workflows with human oversight
- Key differentiator: successful deployment requires continuous human intervention and evaluation loops, not hands-off automation

### Hiring, Scaling, and Team Composition

- Both teams emphasize hiring for growth mindset and ability to operate in ambiguity, not just technical skills
- XFunnel leveraging people with diverse startup experience (operations, growth, execution) to rapidly expand service offerings
- GrowthX's team composition includes both marketing experts and software engineers to bridge services and product

### Funding and Investor Relations

- Importance of finding strategic investors who understand the hybrid software/services model, not pushing toward pure SaaS scaling
- GrowthX raised from trusted investors, focusing on long-term alignment rather than maximizing valuation
- Both teams willing to pass on capital that comes with pressure to compete in crowded SaaS categories

### Competitive Positioning

- Neither XFunnel nor GrowthX view themselves as competing with Profound, SEMrush, or other VC-backed point tools
- Operating philosophy: there's a massive blue ocean opportunity in marketing services; no need to fight over the SaaS tool niche
- Organic inbound demand from enterprise/mid-market customers validates the positioning
- Both teams have deliberately fired or declined low-fit customers, prioritizing quality over growth
