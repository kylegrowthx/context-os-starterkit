## Summary

Kyle Gaudreau and Marcel Santilli from GrowthX followed up with the Braze content and growth team on a potential partnership to improve their content-to-pipeline conversion. Braze has achieved strong keyword rankings (60% of target keywords in positions 1-3) but is struggling to translate that SEO traction into meaningful pipeline increases—a challenge Marcel attributed partly to evolving search landscape factors like brand visibility in LLM responses and AI overviews. GrowthX proposed an 8-week strategy sprint ($20K) to refine Braze's content approach, followed by ongoing execution at $12-15K/month for ~10 articles per week, with a focus on programmatic content opportunities and better attribution frameworks to measure impact across channels.

## Context

Braze is a leading customer engagement platform in the B2B SaaS space that has been investing heavily in organic content marketing over the past year. Starting in October 2024, they began producing SEO-optimized content and scaled production significantly from 2-3 pieces per month (pre-March 2025) to 10+ pieces per month as of the call date. Douglas da Silva, Hedy Payghambari, and Madison Gardner lead content and marketing strategy at Braze, with specific focus on converting keyword rankings into pipeline impact.

This follow-up call was the second touchpoint between Braze and GrowthX after an initial discovery conversation. Kyle Gaudreau (GrowthX VP) and Marcel Santilli (GrowthX CEO) came prepared with competitive research, a preliminary SEO opportunity audit in Airtable, and refined pricing options. The Braze team wanted deeper understanding of GrowthX's day-to-day content production workflows, how they handle legal/SME approvals in regulated industries, and proof points from similar SaaS customers. The meeting also included a substantive discussion of how GrowthX approaches the increasingly complex attribution challenge in modern search, factoring in LLM visibility, brand citations, and multi-touch conversion paths.

  - Creating programmatic content variants (e.g., industry-specific pages)
