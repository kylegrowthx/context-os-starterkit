## Summary

Jennifer flagged technical inaccuracies in GrowthX-generated articles that Rimkus published, creating legal and brand risk for an industry known for litigation. The team agreed to implement a two-pronged solution: Jennifer will propose a technical review workflow routing articles through Rimkus subject-matter experts, while Matt commits to investigating improvements to GrowthX's internal quality control to catch inaccuracies upstream.

---

## Context

GrowthX is producing technical content for Rimkus's blog to drive organic traffic and SEO visibility—a strategic priority that's showing measurable success in visibility and engagement. However, when Jennifer and Nora reviewed articles post-publication, they discovered minor but critical technical inaccuracies (e.g., overstating code requirements, unsupported claims) that marketing teams cannot catch without domain expertise. This is a significant problem for Rimkus because the engineering and construction industries are highly litigious, and inaccurate technical claims can appear in discovery during depositions or damage the company's brand as a scientific authority. Additionally, Rimkus's standard internal process for all peer-published content includes technical review and a Central Processing Center sign-off—a step being skipped for GrowthX articles, creating a compliance gap.

Jennifer plans to approach Vitaly (the point person on Rimkus's side) with a proposal to add a technical review routing step, working with designated technical leaders in practices like Energy & Construction to assign articles to consultants for review. The challenge is that these consultants are busy, so Jennifer is exploring incentives like author credit. Matt and Jennifer agreed that improving GrowthX's upstream quality control (stronger researcher, additional QC phases) may also help reduce the burden on Rimkus's review process.

## Key Topics

### The Problem: Technical Inaccuracy & Risk

  - **Trigger:** An author flagged "inaccuracies" in a refreshed "hydrogen embrittlement" article, prompting a review of other content.
  - **Findings:** Nora's AI fact-check revealed minor but critical issues (e.g., misstating code requirements), which Rimkus's marketing team cannot catch.
  - **Business Risk:** The industry's litigious nature means even minor errors can be used against Rimkus in depositions, threatening its brand and scientific credibility.
  - **Process Gap:** The current workflow omits the standard internal peer review required for all other Rimkus-published content, creating a misalignment with company policy.

### Proposed Solution: Internal Technical Review

  - **Proposal:** Jennifer will ask Vitaly to add a technical review step to the workflow.
  - **Process:** Articles would be routed through a designated technical leader (e.g., in Energy & Construction) for assignment to a consultant.
  - **Incentive:** To secure non-billable time from busy consultants, Jennifer proposed offering them author credit for articles they significantly improve. Matt will confirm this is feasible on GrowthX's side.

### GrowthX's Response: Process Improvement & Support

  - **Commitment:** Matt acknowledged the risk and committed to improving GrowthX's internal quality control.
  - **Context:** Matt noted the "hydrogen embrittlement" article was an outlier, as it was a refresh of an expert's work. The focus should remain on top-of-funnel, informative content.
  - **Support:** Matt offered to speak with Vitaly to help make the case for the new process and demonstrate GrowthX's commitment to accuracy.
