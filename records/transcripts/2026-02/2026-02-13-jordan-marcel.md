## Key Topics

### Background: Marcel and GrowthX

Marcel walked Jordan through his background: graphic design → marketing leadership (IBM, HP, Hashicorp, Scale AI) → GrowthX. At IBM, he built SecureIntelligence.com which generated $17M in year one. At Hashicorp, he led growth from $6M to $100M through IPO. GrowthX started 18 months ago from workshops, raised a Series A in February last year, and is now at 70 customers, $13M ARR, 90 employees.

### Jordan's Concern: Audience Relevance Over Traffic Volume

Jordan was direct: he'd rather have 10,000 highly relevant visitors than 1 million irrelevant ones. Vizcom's audience is industrial designers — people who follow Design Burger, use Autodesk tools, draw, and 3D print their ideas. They're not Canva users. Generic traffic won't convert. The current GrowthX content felt like it was targeting too broadly and didn't match the sensibility of this community.

### Lovable Case Study as a Model

Marcel shared the Lovable work as an example of product-led growth: GrowthX built a programmatic engine that analyzes user prompts, categorizes use cases, and generates ready-to-use templates via Lovable's API. The result: design engineer time per template dropped from 6 hours to 1–2 hours; templates are now integrated into Lovable's product discovery and drive ~1,000 daily high-intent visitors. Jordan immediately saw the parallel — "This is the direction we need," he said. Not vanity traffic. Not AI discourse. Just templates that solve real problems for the community.

### Vizcom's Brand Constraint: No AI Discourse

Vizcom doesn't mention AI anywhere on its website — no model names, no buzzwords. "The process is the product and the technology is almost secondary." Content that leads with AI framing is off-brand. The community cares about craft, physical products, and design culture — not the underlying technology stack. This is a hard constraint GrowthX needs to internalize.

### Re-Kickoff Plan

Marcel proposed an immediate re-kickoff to do a proper intake with Jordan — something the original kickoff lacked. Jordan agreed. They scheduled it for 12:30 PM the same day. Jordan offered to share inspiration examples (starting with Runway ML) and community insights about what Vizcom's audience is curious about. Marcel committed to researching the audience further and preparing the Lovable case study and programmatic workflow examples.

The proposed direction: study guides, reference libraries, mental model content, and community-driven inspiration — positioned as curation, not authority. Authentic, information-dense, respectful of the craft.
