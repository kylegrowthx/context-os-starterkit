### GrowthX Agency + Platform Model

Marcel explained GrowthX's positioning as an agency-plus-platform: SEO, AEO, content, and engineering delivered as a service. He walked through case studies to show the model works across company stages:

- **Lovable**: Fired two SEO agencies creating garbage content. GrowthX fixed the site end-to-end and continues SEO/AEO support.

- **Ramp**: Built vendor catalog, per diem pages, and expense category pages; also created content.
- **Webflow**: Built integration pages and developer documentation pages (Webflow is now an investor in GrowthX).
- **Data Grid**: Started with a two-page website, built domain authority with GrowthX, acquired by Procore (public company).

The implication: Eon (with 60 random pages and zero authority) is an ideal fit for this model.

### CheckThat Demo & AI Perception Framework

Marcel showed Eon's workspace in CheckThat, which was already set up with 120 pre-built prompts collecting data for ~90 days, accumulating 32,000 AI-generated responses. Key insight: Eon appears in only one prompt ("what is the most reliable AWS backup solution") and nowhere else. This historical data is irreplaceable—models are updated daily, so you cannot backdate responses.

Marcel then introduced a two-axis framework for competitive positioning:

- **Reputation**: Community peer reviews + analyst presence (Gartner, G2, TrustRadius, etc.)
- **AI Perception**: How AI engines qualitatively rank and describe a brand within buyer-relevant attributes

He classified competitors into tiers: Leaders, Strong Performers, Emerging, Hidden Gems, Off the Map. Eon landed in "Hidden Gem" tier—high potential, no established presence. Marcel advised against Product Hunt (prosumer audience, not enterprise) and flagged G2 and TrustRadius as quick wins for building reputation.

- **Category expansion roadmap**: Eon's core is backup/DR; future expansion into data lake, data warehouse, data governance will require additional buying categories in CheckThat

Jenn is still learning the space (came from Webflow) but confirmed the category direction and the need for category expansion as Eon's positioning evolves.
