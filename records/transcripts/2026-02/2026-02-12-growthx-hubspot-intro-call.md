## Key Topics

### HubSpot's Current AI Content Stack

Rory outlined how HubSpot uses AI across properties. The website team builds use case and industry pages using proprietary data and case studies, targeting AI citation rates. They've created an FAQ glossary from internal marketing/sales terms for AI visibility. The blog team has been using Air Ops for five months on brief creation and is signing off a 3-month pilot for programmatic post refreshes. They're deliberately avoiding fully AI-generated original content due to algorithmic penalty risk.

### Three Pilot Opportunities

**1. Templates Library Enrichment** — Existing templates on resources subfolder are lead gen machines (download → nurture). Opportunity to enrich them with product feature connections to drive free signups and trials. Amanda noted these templates have been resilient through AI headwinds.

**2. Project Perfect Match (Industry/Use Case Pages)** — Built internally with Amanda's team and dev. Currently evaluating whether to expand to more pages or enrich existing ones for quality. Primarily an AEO play — adding statistics, modern elements, and human-created feel to programmatically generated pages.

**3. Workflows Feature Library (Net New)** — Rory's most excited about this one. HubSpot's workflows feature (pro tier) has no public-facing library of workflow use cases. Sized via TAM analysis benchmarked against N8N, Zapier. Long-term potential to let users import discovered workflows directly into their HubSpot portal. Dani (absent) has a brief ready to share.

### GrowthX's Proposed Approach

Tyler proposed running the templates and industry page enrichment in parallel as phase 1, while scoping the workflows pipeline for phase 2. Referenced Lovable (programmatically created apps via API with forward-deployed design engineers) and Webflow (ingested docs to build integration pages and enrich definitions pages) as proof points. The strategy sprint (8 weeks) would start with kickoff, product deep dives, and roadmap — content creation as early as week 4, targeting 10-20 refreshed pages.

### Pricing and Terms Discussion

Rory flagged that the ~$18k/month price point is high. HubSpot wants short-term pilot with break clauses to demonstrate value before committing to larger engagement. They've already acquired X Funnel and have some overlapping features. Flexibility on terms will determine how much of phase 1 focuses on templates vs. broader scoping.
