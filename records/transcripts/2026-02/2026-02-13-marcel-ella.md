## Key Topics

### Platform Bundling Strategy

CheckThat and OS must be bundled to provide a coherent customer experience. Marcel emphasized that CheckThat is core data infrastructure for OS — brand research, AI visibility insights, and buying category mapping. OS buyers get basic CheckThat data ingestion; broader CheckThat capabilities require a separate rollout. The bundle will use two SKUs with combined pricing to simplify contracts and prevent conflicting messaging at renewal.

### Service Tier Definition

Four main service categories were defined:
1. **Custom AI engineering** — separate workstream, bespoke deployments
2. **Strategy and support overlay** — mandatory for nearly all enterprise customers
3. **OS + CheckThat platform** — core product
4. **Editorial/production services** — expected to decline as customers take on more publishing responsibility

The offerings table will be updated to remove confusing blanket "services" categories.

### Enterprise Expansion and Workspace Architecture

Customers like Microsoft or Salesforce can manage multiple brands through separate workspaces in CheckThat — each billed separately. This enables viral adoption within large enterprises while maintaining clear pricing. Current customer base is mostly single-brand, but multi-brand complexity is recognized for future planning.

### Renewal and Pricing

Renewal pricing for OS + CheckThat should reflect new platform capabilities and service layers. Medium sites: ~$10K/month. Smaller sites less likely to buy due to limited scale. Ella committed to stress-testing pricing with Andy and aligning messaging across sales teams before OS launches fully.

### Team Readiness for Launch

Ella shared a draft "bill of goods" document outlining skills, roles, and operational principles needed for launch. Marcel offered to refine it. Both agreed that skill gaps need to be addressed before launch, and hiring efforts will support readiness.

### Compensation and Equity

Ella requested a base salary increase to $325K (total target $400K). Marcel explained the equity structure: directors receive ~10 basis points; Ella's offer is ~70 basis points — significantly higher than typical. Exercise costs are kept low via capped valuation. One-year exercise window extends with tenure. Marcel committed to transparency around any fundraising events. Ella will review the offer details and follow up; Marcel will have Bridget draft formal written language.
