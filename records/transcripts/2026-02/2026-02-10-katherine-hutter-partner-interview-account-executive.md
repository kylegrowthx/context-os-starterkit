## Key Topics

### Candidate Background & Experience

  - **Career Path:** SDR → Channel Sales → Hotjar (New Business, Expansion, Agency Partners) → ContentSquare (post-acquisition).
  - **Hotjar Sales Motion:** Built a sales-assisted PLG process to upsell users from free plans.
      - **Problem:** Users self-served to the lowest-tier plan (500 sessions/day), which sampled traffic and provided incomplete data.
      - **Solution:** Used product analytics to identify high-potential accounts (e.g., 10k+ sessions/day) and pitched the value of "100% capture" for accurate decision-making.
  - **Acquisition Experience:** Navigated the integration of Hotjar and Heap Analytics into ContentSquare, managing the transition from startup to enterprise.

### Product Positioning & Differentiation

  - **Value Prop:** Traditional analytics (e.g., GA4) show *what* happened; ContentSquare/Hotjar shows *why* by visualizing user behavior (heatmaps, session recordings).
  - **Key Differentiators:**
      - **Ease of Use:** Simple, one-tag installation for immediate insights.
      - **Transparency:** Public pricing and a free trial contrast with competitors' gated models.
      - **Security:** Positioned as a European company with robust data protection.
  - **Competitors:** Amplitude, PostHog, Adobe Analytics, Full Story.

### Major Deal Example: Blackhawk Network

  - **Context:** Consolidated disparate, credit-card-paid accounts into a single enterprise contract.
  - **Pain Point:** Teams were getting negative survey feedback but lacked full data capture to analyze the user sessions and diagnose the root cause.
  - **Strategy:**
      - **Business Case:** Built an unbranded document using the prospect's own language to help them champion the deal internally.
      - **Relationship Building:** Built trust with the economic buyer (Yulia) by being highly responsive and reliable, acting as a partner.
  - **Self-Assessment:** Rated the deal a "7/10."
      - **Strengths:** Persistence, scoping disparate teams into one contract.
      - **Areas for Improvement:**
          - **Internal Support:** Could have built more support with regional leads to increase deal scope.
          - **Cross-selling:** Missed opportunities to position other ContentSquare solutions.

### GrowthX: Business Model & Sales Process

  - **Business Model:** Evolving from a services firm to a product company.
      - **Rationale:** Build software based on proven operational expertise to ensure product-market fit.
  - **Services Sales Process:**
      - **Phases:** Discovery → Demo → Partnership Doc → Review Content Plan.
      - **Pricing:** Phase 2 starts at $180k/year.
      - **Structure:** A two-phase model allows clients to opt out after Phase 1, enabling a faster initial sale.
  - **Product Sales:**
      - **"Check That":** AI visibility and monitoring platform ($18k/year).
      - **Future Vision:** Open internal content creation tools to clients in 3–4 months.
