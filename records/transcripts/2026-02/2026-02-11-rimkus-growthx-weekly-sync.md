## Summary

Align on a new content strategy to resolve technical accuracy concerns.

---

## Context

Rimkus is a forensic investigation firm that provides expert services across Building Envelope Services (BES), forensics, and life sciences. GrowthX is helping Rimkus with a content marketing engagement, writing articles designed to drive visibility in AI model responses (AEO) and funnel potential leads to their consulting services. The partnership focuses on publishing research-backed content that demonstrates technical expertise and positions Rimkus as a thought leader. In this meeting, Vitali Edrenkine (VP of Strategy) joined Matthew Alves-Hill (GrowthX) and the Rimkus marketing team (Jennifer Bulmash, Nohora Davila) to address a publishing bottleneck caused by technical accuracy concerns with AI-generated content and to realign on strategy.

---

## Relevance

- **To GrowthX Delivery:** Established a concrete accuracy target (~90% technical accuracy) and pivoting content strategy from mixed top/bottom-of-funnel to pure top-of-funnel content for a one-month experiment. This changes research process requirements and publishing velocity expectations. Increased volume commitment from 5 to 7 articles/week requires process optimization.

- **To CheckThat / AEO:** The explicit focus on top-of-funnel content improves positioning in AI model responses (generic, high-demand topics like "common causes of fire accidents"). This validates the AEO approach and creates opportunity to measure traffic and AI citation patterns over the one-month experiment window.

- **To GrowthX Business Development:** Rimkus account is healthy and values the partnership (positive engagement signals), but technical accuracy bottleneck was blocking scaling. The pivot to TOFU content and proposed internal expert review (with byline/bio incentives) indicates Rimkus is willing to invest in the relationship. Potential reference customer for AEO success stories.

---

## Overview

- **New Strategy:** Pivot to Top-of-Funnel (TOFU) content for one month. This will increase publishing velocity by reducing the need for intensive technical review.
- **Accuracy Target:** Rimkus requires \~90% accuracy for content to be publishable without expert review. This is the new baseline GrowthX will work toward.
- **BES Content:** The current BES content strategy is effective. GrowthX will refine its research process to fix recent factual errors, which should resolve Nohora's concerns.
- **Volume Increase:** The weekly article count will increase from 5 to 7, enabled by the lower-effort TOFU approach. Rimkus will provide the new topic distribution.

---

## Key Topics

### The Accuracy Problem

  - **Root Cause:** Rimkus cannot publish content with factual errors, as it undermines their credibility.
  - **Impact:** The current review process is a bottleneck, creating a publishing backlog.
  - **BES vs. Forensics:**
      - **BES:** The strategy is sound, but recent factual errors require a process fix.
      - **Forensics:** The content is too technical, requiring expert review. A refreshed article by expert Alan Conway was deemed inaccurate, highlighting the issue.

### The Solution: Pivot to TOFU Content

  - **Rationale:** TOFU content (e.g., "common causes of fire accidents") is less technical and easier to get right, enabling faster publishing.
  - **Trade-off:** This approach prioritizes reach over direct lead generation. It may not tell the "Rimkus story" as effectively as detailed, bottom-of-funnel content.
  - **Experiment:** The team will test this TOFU-only approach for one month to measure its impact on traffic and conversions.
  - **Future State:** If successful, the strategy may evolve into a hybrid model, with experts reviewing a smaller volume of high-value, bottom-of-funnel content.
