## Key topics

### Giga

Giga is an early-stage Voice AI agents company with near-zero web presence and founder-led sales. They focus on support automation for industries that rely on large customer support teams. The market is crowded with adjacent competitors like Parloa and Cresta — GrowthX has worked with both. Their former CMO at Dialpad signed the contract without any negotiation or redlines, which Tyler reads as urgency and full confidence in GrowthX's expertise.

One concern: the CMO (Morgan) may be the sole content reviewer, which typically creates bottlenecks. Tyler's plan is to establish fast-publish expectations at the kickoff — frame it as: if we're going to move fast and get pages indexed quickly, we need a streamlined review. The team should avoid routing basic content through a technical reviewer like the CTO unless absolutely necessary.

An upsell ask came up during the sales process: the CMO hinted that the "standard package" might not get them where they need to go, and mentioned LinkedIn posts for their CRO. The team quickly aligned: no LinkedIn ghostwriting — it's resource-intensive and difficult to execute at quality. Focus stays on the website as the core asset, with LinkedIn as a potential secondary workstream only after foundational content is publishing.

Account assignment: Kathy will lead Giga (Aida's timezone makes the 11am PST Monday kickoff impractical at 8pm her time). Tyler will attempt to reschedule to 8–9am PST.

### Knox Systems

Knox helps software companies get FedRAMP certified so they can sell to the US federal government. The certification process is expensive and slow — Knox makes it faster and cheaper. Their CEO is extremely bought in, and the referral came from Francois at Augment Code (a close contact). The VP of Marketing (Heidi) will be the primary point of contact, though she's relatively new to the role.

Content opportunity is significant. Legacy competitors barely create content and still outrank Knox on critical searches like "how to get FedRAMP certified." There's a wide variety of content GrowthX can produce — federal sales process, certification steps, compliance frameworks — that doesn't need to be deeply technical to drive traffic. Aida flagged that FedRAMP compliance content is less constrained than, say, medical research (like Biologica), because the requirements are publicly documented.

Programmatic upsell: Knox is interested in a database tool tied to government contracts (sam.gov or usaspending.gov) — similar to the Ramp vendor directory GrowthX built. Teams currently pay $30k/year to research this manually. Tyler positioned it as a phase-two expansion after editorial gets established.

Account assignment: Aida will lead Knox, given her comfort with compliance content. This was the initial assignment before the time zone discussion — the swap conversation resolved with Kathy going to Giga and Aida staying on Knox.

### Team capacity and resourcing

George confirmed he's at hard capacity: Ramp, Brex (= 3 clients), Daylight, and incoming Vercel. He will bring Megan in on Brex when it stabilizes, but isn't ready to hand it off yet. Neither Giga nor Knox can go to him.

Two EM candidates are at final interview stage and interviewing this week. George offered to review current onboarding materials to make sure new hires are set up for success when they start. William will coordinate scheduling flexibility around the Giga kickoff timing.

Tyler offered to share his Claude AI project chats — the same ones he uses to build handoff docs — so EMs can ask follow-up questions directly without needing to loop Tyler back in for every detail.
