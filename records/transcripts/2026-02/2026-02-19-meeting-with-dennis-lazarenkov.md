## Key topics

### Content strategy and volume goals

Tyler opened with context from his conversation with Marcel since the previous call (Feb 17). Marcel's analysis of Simple Life's site shows 575 indexed pages total, 216 blog pages, but only 5 have a modification date in the last year — a significant refresh opportunity. Tyler's argument: starting with 5–10 high-quality articles/week and scaling based on positive signals (time on page, conversions) is safer than trying to create hundreds of pages at once.

Dennis pushed back directly. He shared updated data: 45.5k monthly organic traffic, 20k downloads, 757 purchases tracked via GA4. His team has analyzed 4,853 potential articles clustered into categories: habits/motivation (51 articles), walking and movement (252), mindfulness (25), recipes (82), fitness basics, calculators, top-best lists, and general wellness. The spreadsheet quantifies brand lift, trust effect, delayed conversions, and LTV growth to project what these articles could generate.

Dennis's KPI for 2026 is to 10x organic purchases from 757 to approximately 7,500 by year-end. He doesn't believe 20 articles/month (or 5/week) is enough to get there.

### The backlink problem

Dennis did his own analysis of GrowthX client case studies. He found that AI-generated pages on Augment Code's site (tools, learn, guides sections) show zero organic traffic on Ahrefs. Lovable templates pages show some traffic — but they have backlinks. His conclusion: AI content without backlinks drives zero traffic; content with backlinks performs.

He noted Google has also started penalizing self-promoting listicles (beginning with Shopify before expanding to other brands) — which affects some of the article categories his team identified (top-best lists).

Tyler asked which specific Augment Code pages he's referencing to clarify the comparison. Dennis shared his screen to show the Ahrefs data. Tyler pointed to three comparable companies to check on Ahrefs/Semrush instead, committing to share those before the Marcel call.

### Medical content bottleneck

The core constraint for Simple Life is that medical content requires doctor approval — creating a review bottleneck that caps velocity at ~20 articles/month. Dennis's solution is to focus exclusively on non-medical informational content (habits, mindfulness, general wellness, fitness basics) that doesn't require clinical sign-off.

He's skeptical that calculators work — his CMO reports calculator conversion rates of 0.01% currently. Dennis saw that a client (Deepgram) had AI-driven traffic spike to 1.5 million then drop to 300k after 6 months — Google crossed out a large chunk of indexed pages. He worries the same pattern will repeat for Simple Life.

### Attribution and conversion tracking

Dennis's team spent two months before year-end fixing attribution. They now use GA4 + Mixpanel to track registrations, downloads, clicks, and purchases from organic traffic. Numbers he trusts. Some conversions may be missed due to user behavior, but the core data is solid.

The key metric: 757 organic purchases/year (full-site organic), 130 specifically from blog traffic. Goal is to 10x the 757 number by year-end.

### Budget and ROI expectations

Simple Life is spending ~$1M/month on paid ads. Rising CPAs due to Meta's AI algorithm changes (Andromeda) are driving the push to find an organic channel. Dennis said the budget for a content project could be $30–50k/month — but only if GrowthX can show clear predictions: CPA, conversion rates, ROI timeline.

Dennis made clear he can't sell "5 articles/week" internally. He needs something that justifies a significant spend with measurable outcomes. He's asking for case studies — not at Lovable scale (which is huge), but smaller-scale examples that show AI content with backlinks driving sustained, durable traffic growth.

### Next steps

Tyler agreed the right next step is to connect Dennis directly with Marcel. The two of them set up a call for the following day (Feb 20) at 4:30pm London time. Tyler committed to sharing the spreadsheet Dennis showed with Marcel in advance, along with additional case studies and Semrush data for comparable companies.

Dennis committed to sending his full analysis spreadsheet to Tyler. He's open to the engagement starting in April if the data checks out — but wants to understand the realistic path to 10x before committing.
