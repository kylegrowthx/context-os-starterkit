## Key Topics

## **Product Vision and Market Positioning**

The company is focused on solving a massive and evolving challenge around AI-driven website visibility and content management, aiming to become essential for businesses navigating AI-powered discovery and engagement .

- **Strategic focus on AI visibility for websites as a growth driver**  
  - Marcel Santilli highlighted the rapid shift in buyer behavior toward AI engagement with content.  
  - The product targets the complexity of managing thousands of web pages, optimizing them for AI agents to fetch and recommend.  
  - The significance lies in the fact that if businesses' websites aren't surfaced by AI, they effectively lose market presence.  
  - This creates a huge opportunity to monetize AI visibility and content optimization, especially in mid-market to enterprise segments.

- **Market opportunity grounded in existing spending patterns**  
  - Santilli compared competitors like Semrush with **25,000 customers paying $40,000-$60,000 annually** for basic SEO tools.  
  - He suggested capturing a fraction of this market at a higher average contract value (ACV) could generate billions in annual recurring revenue.  
  - The company currently has minimal marketing and just **two salespeople**, signaling potential to scale through technology and product improvements.

- **Positioning between CMS and AI data layers**  
  - The product integrates with existing CMS platforms like Webflow and Vercel, which are customers and investors.  
  - Marcel emphasized not building redundant CMS features but focusing on AI-driven content orchestration and publishing agents that connect to CMS APIs.  
  - The fast-evolving CMS market and AI modder ecosystem create a dynamic competitive environment beneficial for innovation.

- **Long-term vision to build a generational, founder-led company**  
  - Marcel framed this as his "life's work," blending marketing expertise with AI and product architecture to solve real industry problems.  
  - The goal is sustainable growth balancing ambition and discipline, focusing on solving customer pain points rather than chasing rapid exits.  
  - He stressed the importance of practical, focused problem solving and leveraging AI to accelerate learning and decision-making.

## **Check That AI Product Insights and Challenges**

Check That AI serves as a public-facing index and benchmarking tool for AI visibility, but requires refinement to drive customer activation and actionable insights .

- **Initial impressions highlight strong benchmarking but limited actionable guidance**  
  - Barnett Klane noted the product's value in quantifying market position relative to peers using a scoring mechanism.  
  - However, he observed a lack of clarity on what users should do next to improve their scores or AI presence.  
  - The current presentation shows many zeros or missing data for smaller companies, limiting immediate usefulness.

- **Data sourcing and cost considerations impact product design**  
  - Marcel explained the team built the data collection in-house due to **API costs being three times higher externally** and the need for consistent, deep research.  
  - This enables an "open index" model where much data is free, with paid options for advanced tracking, positioning it uniquely in the market.  
  - Barnett expressed interest in how costs scale and how the company manages ongoing data procurement.

- **Need for higher-level aggregation beyond granular prompt responses**  
  - Marcel described the challenge of presenting **26,000 prompt responses over 60 days** in a digestible way.  
  - The product currently tracks many individual AI prompts but lacks a summarized view that marketers can act on.  
  - Barnett agreed that users want insights by buying category or business area, not by individual prompts.

- **Emphasis on buying categories and user-centric journey mapping**  
  - Both agreed on the importance of categorizing prompts into meaningful buying criteria within a buying category (e.g., payments pricing, compliance).  
  - This categorization should reflect buyer priorities and evolving search trends to guide marketing investments.  
  - Barnett suggested implementing a feedback loop or dropdown to help users and the product team refine these categories based on real usage and needs.

## **Product Strategy and Roadmap Priorities**

The product team plans to evolve Check That AI's methodology, focusing on actionable composite scores and clearer user journeys to drive customer engagement and conversion .

- **Development of composite scores measuring presence, reputation, perception, and influence**  
  - Marcel explained presence is most developed, measuring consistent AI visibility across multiple services.  
  - Reputation and perception scores are less mature and need refinement to provide meaningful insights.  
  - These scores aim to form an "AI Brand Health" metric that marketers can track over time.

- **Transition from raw data to practical guidance**  
  - The team wants to move users from seeing raw prompt data to understanding strategic gaps and next steps.  
  - Marcel emphasized the need for a "boiled down" view that points marketers to priority actions rather than overwhelming them with data.  
  - Barnett advised linking insights directly to competitor comparisons and market changes to make the value clear.

- **Integration of market context and search trend analysis**  
  - Barnett recommended combining prompt data with external search trends to prioritize categories that matter most to buyers.  
  - Marcel confirmed the team uses workflows combining community data, competitors, and keywords to generate prompts but acknowledged room to refine this.  
  - This approach supports a dynamic product roadmap that adapts to evolving buyer intent signals.

- **Focus on scalable user engagement and activation**  
  - The product currently generates **5 to 10 signups daily** but struggles with activation and clear onboarding.  
  - Marcel noted the need to build technology to **10x capacity** to handle more customers efficiently.  
  - The roadmap includes improving user journeys from awareness (landing pages) to deeper engagement and conversion.
