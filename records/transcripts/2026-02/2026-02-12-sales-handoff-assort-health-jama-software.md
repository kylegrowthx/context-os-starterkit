## Key Topics

### Process Improvement: Pre-Kickoff Prep

  - The team identified a recurring gap: the 2-week pre-kickoff preparation window is consistently missed.
  - **Root Cause:** Late client documentation and short-notice handoffs.
  - **Solution:** Focus on stabilizing the current system before adding new steps.
      - **Goal:** Ensure handoffs occur at least 2 weeks before kickoff.
      - **Enabler:** The upcoming hiring of more Engagement Managers (EMs).

### Client Handoff: JAMA Software

  - **Source:** Referral from XFunnel (acquired by HubSpot), which can no longer serve clients.
  - **Primary Contact:** Fonny (Amsterdam-based), a results-focused "speed demon" with internal influence.
  - **Key Stakeholders:**
      - **CRO (Tom):** Involved to connect content to sales pipeline.
      - **Creative Manager (Mario):** U.S.-based, manages writers.
  - **Challenge: Technical Content Quality**
      - **Client Requirement:** "Technical content quality that won't embarrass JAMA."
      - **Risk:** Our standard workflow may not meet this SME-level depth, potentially leading to client dissatisfaction.
  - **Mitigating Factors:**
      - **Current Content:** JAMA's top-performing articles are basic (e.g., "What is the AEC industry?"), suggesting low-hanging fruit for improvement.
      - **Focus:** 80% Top-of-Funnel (ToFu) content, which is less technical.
      - **Commitment:** Fonny views it as a minimum 1-year engagement, providing a long runway for results.

### Solution: Managing Technical Content Expectations

  - The team defined a two-part strategy for clients with high technical demands.
  - **External Communication (Sales & Kickoff):**
      - Set clear expectations: "We are not subject matter experts (SMEs) in your field."
      - Frame our role as driving visibility and traffic, not creating thought leadership.
      - Position the client's internal experts as the source for SME depth (e.g., voice notes).
  - **Internal Execution (Pipeline Optimization):**
      - Involve a pipeline optimizer (e.g., Tammy) early (Week 2–3) to customize the workflow.
      - Train the pipeline on "good examples" provided by the client to align with their quality standards.
      - Create a dedicated "artifacts" folder with these examples for the pipeline to reference.

### Client Handoff: Assort Health

  - **Lead:** Kathy requested this client due to industry interest.
  - **Profile:** SF-based AI voice agent company for healthcare.
  - **Status:** Early-stage with high growth potential.
  - **Key Details:**
      - Strong funding.
      - Highly engaged CEO.
      - Low current traffic → 2x growth is a reasonable goal.
      - Collaborative client.
