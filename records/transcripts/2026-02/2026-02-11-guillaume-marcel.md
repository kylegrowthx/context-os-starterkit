## Key Topics

### GrowthX Scaling Phase

Marcel described a phase where internal compounding is accelerating — narrowed focus on core primitives, leadership team upgrades, faster product releases. Still demand-positive with organic word-of-mouth. But competition is getting aggressive. One AE is closing ~$2M/month — time to hire more. Raised via SAFE (HubSpot Ventures, Webflow, one other) rather than Series B. Cash flow positive 3 of 5 months, $10M credit line, no fundraising urgency.

### Output AI Open-Source Launch

GrowthX is open-sourcing the internal agentic framework that has powered their workflows for 18 months — called Output AI, releasing as an NPM package. It includes planning, scenario evaluation, LLM-as-judge loops, runtime layer for retry/fail handling, tracing, and observability. Designed for AI engineers and developers building custom automation workflows. Primary audience: developers and forward-deployed engineers. Marcel demo'd the transcript-to-LinkedIn-post workflow as an example — multi-step pipeline with quality scoring and iterative improvement loops.

Guillaume's reaction: he left GrowthX as a client because he needed more hands-on access to AI workflows, not just the managed output. Output AI directly addresses that — the shift from black-box to programmable tools. This opens a new segment: technically sophisticated users who want to build their own workflows on GrowthX's infrastructure.

### AI Market Shifts (Guillaume's Observations)

Guillaume observed that over the past 6-8 weeks, client expectations on AI output quality have risen sharply — driven by practitioners (like Nico Drewell) building clean, self-improving Claude Code setups with client-specific skills and context. These systems make recommendations, run audits, and improve over time. CMOs are now comparing what's possible, not just what was hard.

Second observation: AI is reducing the cost of consumer actions — including complaints, returns, disputes — that previously weren't worth pursuing. This will change business-consumer power dynamics meaningfully. Third: software churn costs are coming down, which could erode CRM moats. Guillaume cited Ramp as nearly replacing HubSpot and Salesforce with an internal AI-native tool — only friction is human resistance.

### Collaboration and Referral Program

Guillaume has two new large accounts (Otter, Smith Ox in healthcare). He acknowledged that referral tracking hasn't been clean — needs to be formalized. Marcel confirmed GrowthX can now track referrals in their CRM, making it easy for Guillaume's team to hand off without chasing updates. Agreement to structure this properly going forward.

Joint events: Guillaume is in SF April 15-16 for Chargebee's annual conference. Returns to SF in July as usual. Both agreed to organize events targeting mid-stage B2B founders who need someone to "shake up the growth team." Marcel will give his team a heads up to plan around the April dates.
