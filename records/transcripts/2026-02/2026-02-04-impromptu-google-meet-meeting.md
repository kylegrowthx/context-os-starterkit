## Key Topics

### Client Hesitation on Competitor Listicles

  - **Problem:** A client (Flosum) is blocking approved competitor listicles due to fear of "backlash," despite the content being critical for capturing bottom-funnel executive traffic.
  - **Opportunity:** The upcoming QBR with the client's CEO is the ideal moment to pitch this strategy, as the CEO's focus is on AI visibility and lead generation.

### The "Own the Narrative" Strategy

  - **Core Message:** If you don't write about competitors, they will write about you. This content allows the client to control the narrative.
  - **Dual Benefit:**
    1.  **Frame the Competition:** Define how competitors are perceived.
    2.  **Control Your Story:** Ensure your product's strengths are highlighted in comparison.

### Evidence: LLM Citation Patterns (CheckThat.ai)

  - **Proof Point:** CheckThat.ai data shows that competitor listicles are the primary sources cited by LLMs for evaluation-stage prompts (e.g., "best software for X").
  - **Action:** Use this data to show the client that this content type is the industry standard for AI visibility.

### Evidence: Tavern Case Study

  - **Proof Point:** The Tavern case study demonstrates the strategy's success.
  - **Result:** A Tavern listicle ranks \#1 for the high-intent query "best medical record retrieval companies," driving traffic and earning a feature in Google's AI Overview.
  - **Significance:** This shows the client a tangible, repeatable win. The article's fair analysis (pros/cons) also signals trustworthiness, addressing the backlash concern.

### Content Refresh Strategy for Conversion

  - **Context:** For clients with large blogs but stalled conversions, a content refresh strategy can improve funnel performance.
  - **Process:**
    1.  Audit the site to identify the top 50 ICP-aligned pages.
    2.  Pause net-new content creation.
    3.  Optimize the top pages for conversion (e.g., better CTAs, updated content).
  - **Rationale:** This is a more controllable and impactful lever for influencing conversion than creating new content.
