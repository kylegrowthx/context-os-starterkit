## Key Topics

### Competitor Analysis & Gaps

  - **Initial Analysis:** Reviewed HeyGen, Synthesia, Soul Machines, and Anam.
      - **HeyGen & Synthesia:** Large keyword footprints, but much is irrelevant (e.g., deepfakes). The opportunity is to own real-time conversational training, a gap they don't address.
      - **Soul Machines:** Small footprint, but its relevance is now questionable due to a "notice of receivership" on its site.
      - **Anam:** A direct head-to-head competitor with a small footprint, presenting an immediate opportunity to own the space.
  - **Required Revisions:**
      - **Add D-ID.com:** A critical keyword competitor with high search visibility, despite Tavus's view of its product quality.
      - **Investigate HeyGen's LiveAvatar.com:** Analyze this separate domain's strategy to inform a plan for poaching its upcoming renewals.
      - **Refine content focus:** Avoid static video topics. Instead, create real-time equivalents of enterprise use cases (e.g., e-learning) that competitors own.

### Proposed Content Clusters & Feedback

  - **Cluster 1: Conversational AI Infrastructure**
      - **Goal:** Position Tavus as infrastructure, not a point solution.
      - **Feedback:** Good direction. Layer in key differentiators:
          - **Emotional Intelligence (EQ):** A core model strength that distinguishes Tavus from text-only AI.
          - **Audio-Only Mode:** Use this existing feature as a low-friction entry point for users searching for voice-only AI.
  - **Cluster 2: Real-time Video Technology**
      - **Feedback:** Problematic. The term is too generic and conflicts with partners like Daily.co, diluting Tavus's focus.
  - **Cluster 3: Category Creation (e.g., "Digital Humans")**
      - **Goal:** Define and own a new term to establish long-term market leadership.
      - **Feedback:** This is a top priority. The specific term is still undefined internally, but the cluster must be created.
      - **Action:** GrowthX will research potential terms (e.g., "AI humans") with leadership input to find a balance between future vision and current search volume.
  - **Cluster 4 & 5: Verticals (L\&D, Sales Enablement, Recruitment, CX/CS)**
      - **Feedback:** The clusters are \~70% correct but need restructuring.
      - **Action:** Split into distinct, programmatic verticals:
          - **L\&D:** Focus on corporate training, language learning, and simulation.
          - **Martech:** Target RevOps, AI SDRs/BDRs, and sales agents.
          - **CX/CS:** Focus on customer service training and employee onboarding.
  - **General Feedback:**
      - **"Proof" Content:** Prioritize case studies and programmatic use-case examples to educate the market on what's possible with real-time video.
      - **Messaging Clarity:** Be explicit to avoid confusion with past "text-to-video" offerings, which carry SEO baggage.
