## Key Topics

### The Production Performance Gap

  - **Performance Snapshot:**
      - **Good (30%):** Meets all targets (quality, performance, TTD \<30 min).
      - **Within Striking Distance (\~33%):** Meets some targets but needs improvement.
      - **Far Away (\~33%):** Significantly misses targets.
  - **The Cascading Problem:** High TTD is a leading indicator of pipeline issues.
      - **Pipeline Failure →** MEs spend time on structural fixes (rewrites) instead of quality improvements (SEO, narrative).
      - **Result →** Lower-quality content and client dissatisfaction.
  - **Key Metric:**
      - **Average TTD:** 1 hour, 4 minutes.
      - **Red Flag:** TTD \>1.5–2 hours, indicating a need for immediate action.
  - **High-Effort Clients:** Relay, Diligent, Field Guide, Rimkus, Cresta, Navon.
      - **Rimkus Example:** High TTD is due to complex legal review and pipeline issues. Leadership is now involved (e.g., stakeholder calls in March) to resolve this.

### New Support Structure: Team Leads

  - **Purpose:** Bridge the gap between the EM and ME layers to provide focused, hands-on support.
  - **Role:** Diagnose and fix pipeline issues, not edit every deliverable.
      - **Focus:** High-effort clients (TTD \>200–300% of average).
      - **Method:** Audit pipelines, identify root causes (process, pipeline, or editor), and build scalable solutions.
  - **Sprint Team's Role:**
      - **Sydney:** Ensure pipelines are performant *before* they leave Sprint.
      - **Goal:** Deliver pipelines that meet client expectations and TTD targets from day one.
      - **Benefit:** A well-calibrated Sprint pipeline directly improves the Production team's ability to maintain quality.

### Root Cause Analysis: Pipeline Inputs

  - **Primary Cause of High TTD:** Poor pipeline inputs, not editor skill.
  - **Two Main Issues:**
    1.  **Artifact Conflicts:** Conflicting instructions across pipeline artifacts.
          - **Solution:** Use the **Convergence Report** to identify conflicts.
    2.  **Lack of Context:** Instructions requiring information the AI doesn't have.
          - **Example:** "Write something funnier than last time." The AI has no memory of past runs.
          - [**Solution:** Provide explicit context (e.g., "Write a CTA like this example: \[paste text\]").](https://fathom.video/share/Svv7pVAZGQxCx5oKNg4FwRVdiAY7vn97?tab=summary&timestamp=1579.0)
  - **Client Perception:** Clients are skeptical of AI-generated content and will scrutinize every deliverable for errors.
      - **Action:** Editors must be vigilant to build and maintain client trust.

### Administrative Items

  - **Sprint Team Pipeline Work:** The Sprint team is fixing org-wide pipelines (e.g., the "refresh" pipeline), which will benefit all MEs.
  - **Artifact Management:** New content types (e.g., comparison articles) require new, context-specific artifacts. Do not reuse artifacts from standard editorial pipelines.
  - **Skills Assessment:** Complete the form to help Matthew assign clients that align with your experience.
