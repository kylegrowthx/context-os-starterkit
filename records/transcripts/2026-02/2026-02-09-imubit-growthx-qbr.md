## Summary

GrowthX and Imubit reviewed Q4 2025 performance on their organic content engagement, which delivered 30% of site clicks and 80% of impressions for Imubit, with 224 pieces published (7/week), 250 new organic keywords gained, and 50% Domain Authority growth. The team aligned on strategic evolution for 2026: shifting positioning from a "closed-loop AI" focus to a broader "workforce enablement" narrative, launching GrowthX's CheckThat tool to track Imubit's LLM citations against competitors in evaluation-stage prompts, and coordinating with a new paid agency (New Breed) to ensure organic content informs paid campaigns. ## Context

Imubit is an industrial AI software company (founded/backed timeline unclear from discussion) that provides closed-loop AI optimization solutions for manufacturing and industrial operations. GrowthX began working with Imubit in April 2025 after Imubit identified three core constraints: lack of clear category definition in the market (buyers couldn't quickly understand what Imubit does or how it compared to alternatives), heavy reliance on sales and events for early-stage market education, and limited internal marketing capacity to scale organic content production without creating review bottlenecks. GrowthX's mandate was to define the "closed-loop AI optimization" category, build an ungated educational content library to shift early-stage education to organic channels, and establish a scalable editorial production engine using AI (Atlas platform) with human oversight. After 10 months of partnership, Matthew Alves-Hill (GrowthX's Engagement Manager, joined the account in October 2025) delivered results in this QBR and is now briefing Tim Yandel, who recently took over marketing at Imubit. Tara Sybrant is Imubit's strategy counterpart who has been collaborating closely on content evolution and messaging.
