## Context

Rimkus is a structural engineering and building science firm. GrowthX is managing a content marketing engagement focused on AEO (AI Engine Optimization) and organic visibility. The partnership began in December 2025, with the goal of publishing technical content around mechanical, electrical, life safety (BES) systems and forensics topics to rank for LLM prompts and traditional search. A three-week publishing pause started in late February due to internal concerns at Rimkus over legal liability from the technical depth of published content. Despite this pause, the existing content body is performing exceptionally well, which creates urgency to resume publishing to maintain LLM visibility momentum in Profound (an AI visibility tool).

- Content strategy is validated: 82% engagement on BES cluster, 96% on "Florida building codes" article. The content resonates with target audience despite legal concerns.

**To GrowthX Business Development:**

- Expansion opportunity: Rimkus has ~300 target prompts in their main vertical plus ~300 in forensics. Full strategy execution (once unblocked) could drive significant growth and lock in a strategic reference client.
- Renewal/expansion risk: If publishing stays paused, LLM visibility will stall. Rapid resolution of legal concerns is essential to demonstrate ROI and secure renewal.

  - **Proposal:** Ask Rimkus's legal team if the standard disclaimer (e.g., "for advice only") is sufficient legal protection.

## Decisions & Commitments

- **Legal Disclaimer Path:** Rather than overhauling the content strategy or maintaining an unsustainable internal review process, Nohora will consult Rimkus's legal team on whether the standard disclaimer (currently used on Rimkus blog) provides sufficient protection. Matt cited precedent from other GrowthX clients where legal teams approved similar disclaimers.
- **Continued Content Staging:** GrowthX will continue producing content under the revised (less technical) approach while awaiting legal feedback. Once approved, the staged content will be published immediately to make up for lost publishing time.
- **Technical Review:** Rimkus's internal technical expert will audit the revised content approach one time to validate the pivot before full production resumes. This is explicitly a one-time process, not an ongoing requirement.

---

## Open Questions

- **Timeline on Legal Review:** Nohora did not commit to a specific timeline for checking with Rimkus's legal team. Matt flagged that the three-week pause is already causing LLM visibility declines, so speed is important.
- **Scope of Technical Pivot:** It's unclear how much the content approach is actually changing. Matt referenced a "new, less technical style" but didn't detail what that entails. The high performance of technical content (96% engagement on "Florida building codes") suggests the revision may be minimal.
- **Disclaimer Sufficiency:** The outcome depends entirely on Rimkus legal's view. If they determine the disclaimer is insufficient, the path forward becomes unclear and may require deeper content strategy changes.
