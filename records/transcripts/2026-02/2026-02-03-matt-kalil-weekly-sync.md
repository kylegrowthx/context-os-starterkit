## Overview

The audit exposed fundamental misalignment between Immubit's content strategy and user search intent. A "Trojan horse" approach—targeting broad, top-of-funnel keywords then immediately pivoting to product messaging—underperforms significantly: 220 articles generate only 150 clicks/week compared to competitor Tavern's 1,000 clicks/week from 60 articles. Matthew and Kalil defined a four-category action framework (Rewrite, Refresh, Merge, Kill) to rebuild the content library as a lean, ICP-focused resource. The goal is to provide the client with a clear narrative justifying the shift from high-volume to high-quality content.

---

## Key Topics

### The Problem: Underperforming Content Library

  - **Performance:** 220 articles yield only 150 clicks/week; competitor Tavern achieves 1,000 clicks/week with just 60 articles.
  - **Root Cause:** The "Trojan horse" strategy failed systematically.
      - **Execution:** Articles target broad, top-of-funnel (ToFU) keywords but immediately pivot to Immubit AI messaging, missing user's initial intent.
      - **Example:** "Hydrocrackers" article lacks a basic definition ("What is a hydrocracker?") and immediately jumps to "How AI closes the control gap."
      - **Scope:** Approximately 96 articles (44% of library) have significant keyword/intent mismatches; about half the library needs major rewrites.
  - **Cannibalization:** Multiple articles target overlapping keywords, fragmenting authority.
      - **Example:** Five separate articles target "digital transformation"; two dedicated articles on "manufacturing optimization/visibility" cover the same topic identically.
      - **Impact:** Articles compete internally, preventing any single piece from accumulating enough authority to rank strongly.
  - **Content Imbalance:** Articles missing basic information users seek—readers looking for process definitions don't find them; instead, encounter immediate AI optimization pitch.

### The Solution: A Lean, Focused Content Library

  - **Goal:** Create a lean, hyper-focused blog that drives ICP conversion and aligns with user search intent.
  - **Four-Action Audit Framework:** Classify all articles into one category for clear prioritization:
      - **Rewrite:** Major intent mismatches (estimated 50% of library).
          - **Approach:** Fully satisfy the ToFU keyword first—answer "What is a Hydrocracker?"—then dedicate a small concluding section (e.g., ~10% of article) to Immubit's AI solution and explicit tie-ins.
          - **Distinction:** This is substantively different from a refresh; it requires significant rewriting.
      - **Refresh:** High-performing articles aligned with search intent (estimated 50% of library).
          - **Light-touch actions:** Add TLDRs, FAQs, internal linking, and thicken content on process/industry specifics to improve ranking without losing product messaging alignment.
          - **Rationale:** These pieces are already capturing demand; enhance rather than rewrite.
      - **Merge:** Consolidate cannibalizing content into single comprehensive pieces.
          - **Pattern:** Top-ranking content often covers topics holistically in 4,000–5,000 word pieces; consolidate five "digital transformation" articles into one authoritative piece that outranks all predecessors.
          - **Benefit:** Signals to Google that Immubit is producing focused, authoritative content, not chasing volume.
      - **Kill:** Remove irrelevant, low-quality, or ICP-misaligned articles.
          - **Criteria:** Articles that don't serve Immubit's ICP, don't support domain authority, or were written only because competitors ranked for the keyword.
          - **Strategic:** Pruning signals quality and focus, essential for renewal pitch.
  - **Client Narrative:** This framework transforms the historical 50,000 words/month strategy into a defensible quality-first approach, enabling Matthew to explain why GrowthX now recommends cutting 100+ articles rather than continuing to publish broadly.

## Decisions & Commitments

- **Strategy Shift:** Move from high-volume (50,000 words/month) to lean, hyper-focused content library prioritizing ICP conversion and search intent alignment.
- **Audit Framework Approved:** Use four-action classification (Rewrite, Refresh, Merge, Kill) as the basis for all future content optimization.
- **Execution Approach:** Process articles one-by-one in production; avoid pre-generating detailed task lists for each piece. Focus on high-level classifications and let tactical optimization emerge during execution.
- **Client Positioning:** Matthew will use the audit findings and proposed framework to pitch renewal strategy to Immubit, with focus on quality-over-quantity narrative.
