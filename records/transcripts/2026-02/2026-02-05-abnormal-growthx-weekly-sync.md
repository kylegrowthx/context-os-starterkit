## Summary

## Context

GrowthX operates as Abnormal's content marketing partner, responsible for developing and executing the content strategy across the blog and planned Education Hub. A content freeze was originally planned but has been canceled to maintain continuous publishing momentum and avoid a projected traffic dip. Abnormal is undergoing a significant internal shift with "Web 3.0," which affects the Education Hub timeline. The Education Hub was initially planned as a co-designed project between GrowthX and Abnormal's internal team, but is now being built entirely in-house using a rigid component library and design standards. GrowthX's role has evolved from wireframe design to providing best practices guidance to Abnormal's development team.

The broader context includes Abnormal's product roadmap priorities: the Education Hub is the primary focus, followed by AI Phishing Coach (a new product requiring mid-funnel content), competitive defense (bottom-of-funnel listicles), public sector growth expansion, and geographic expansion to India. Abnormal is also evaluating LLM visibility as a key channel for reaching high-intent users, with GrowthX's in-house CheckDot tool being set up as the measurement infrastructure. There are also 5-8 previously unpublished articles (deactivated for being off-brand) that represent quick win opportunities for traffic recovery without product mentions.

## Decisions & Commitments

**Decision: Content Freeze is Canceled**
- Publishing continues uninterrupted to prevent projected traffic dip
- No change to GrowthX scope or budget—same monthly commitment and team resources (Jo, editors, infrastructure, Andy/VP Ops as needed)

**Decision: Education Hub Becomes Internal Abnormal Build**
- Abnormal will design and develop internally using their component library and design standards
- GrowthX will provide best practices (not wireframes or design files) for Abnormal's team to implement
- Timeline: TBD pending Web 3.0 clarity (originally estimated April/May, now uncertain)

**Decision: Interim Content Publishing Strategy**
- New content tagged with "edu" tag immediately, even before the Hub is ready
- Tagged content is published but not navigable on the website until the Hub launches
- Automatic migration of all "edu"-tagged content to the Hub once it's live
- This allows GrowthX to maintain publishing velocity without waiting for Hub readiness

**Decision: Dual-Lane Content Strategy**
- Education Lane: 15 articles/week targeting broad cybersecurity topics (6M+ monthly search volume), brand-agnostic, light review process
- Blog Lane: 5 articles/week for campaign-aligned content (Education Hub support, AI Phishing Coach, competitive defense, public sector growth, India geo-expansion)
- Purpose: Education Lane drives volume and builds topical authority for UFDL ad targeting; Blog Lane drives product-specific conversions

**Commitment: Republish Deactivated Articles**
- 5-8 high-traffic articles previously unpublished for being off-brand will be reactivated
- Abnormal will remove all product mentions
- Content will be republished under original URLs (no 404s) to preserve SEO value
- Tagged with "edu" for future Hub migration

**Commitment: LLM Visibility as Material Channel**
- CheckDot (GrowthX's in-house tool) will be set up to measure LLM visibility
- Differentiator vs. Scrunch: industry benchmark + ICP-tailored prompts vs. generic approaches
- LLM content (listicles, comparison pieces) will be prioritized in Blog Lane
- Early experiments show 3-4 day lag between publication and LLM citation (LLMs favor fresh content)

**Commitment: Quarterly Content Refresh Program**
- Every 3 months: audit top winners and losers, identify refresh opportunities, prevent content decay
- Part of Education Lane output (15/week includes new + webinar repurposing + quarterly refreshes)
