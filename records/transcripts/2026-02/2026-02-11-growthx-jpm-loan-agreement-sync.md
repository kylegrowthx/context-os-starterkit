GrowthX is in final negotiations on a working capital facility with JPMorgan—a strategic multi-year banking relationship expected to close by end of February 2026. The company has evolved from a pure content marketing agency (founded as a services business) to a SaaS platform with recurring 12-month contracts, which JPMorgan understands and has built into ARR-based covenant definitions. Bridget McGillivray is leading the close on GrowthX's side, supported by two co-founders (including Ericksson in engineering), outside counsel Richard Garvan, and advisor Dane Patterson. The stakes are significant: the loan will fund working capital, and the company recently disclosed a lost $2.7M ARR client (replacement expected within 90 days)—a detail that became material to covenants around "material contracts."

## Key Topics

### Negative Covenants and M&A

JPMorgan's standard template prohibits use of loan funds for acquisitions. Team is seeking carve-outs for small acqui-hires with lender sign-off. General corporate use and working capital are the primary purposes. Convertible notes and SAFEs will be allowed with subordination clauses — JPMorgan holds seniority, no prior approval needed.

### Board Composition

Current board: two co-founders and one lead investor, equal voting rights. JPMorgan's concern is wholesale control changes, not normal director turnover. Team will push back on overly restrictive board change clauses to allow fundraising-driven adjustments.

### Material Contracts

JPMorgan's "material contracts" notification requirement was flagged as vague. Richard recommended narrowing the definition to contracts fundamental to business health. A single lost contract ($2.7M ARR) is not material if overall revenue is replaced within 90 days — which Bridget confirmed. Team will disclose proactively and provide updated client contracts and financial forecasts.

### Board Materials and KPI Reporting

VCs don't want confidential board materials shared with the bank. Team agreed to provide board packs (not full minutes) with carve-outs for confidential/privileged content. Will push back on unlimited KPI requests — propose reasonable lead times and scope limits.

### Loan Terms

- Maturity date discrepancy: term sheet says Feb 28, 2030; agreement says Feb 1, 2030. Richard recommending the later date.
- Incremental borrowing: currently limited to 2 draws minimum $500K each. Team requesting up to 5 draws.
- Prepayment and insurance proceeds: 6-month reinvestment window agreed.
- Audit: waived if board doesn't require — internal financial statements within 60 days instead.

### Asset Management Account

JPMorgan wants funds moved out of their Asset Management account (currently yielding ~$30K/month) into bank accounts they control. Richard described this as unusual. Bridget will push back directly with JPMorgan contact. Investors will be looped in if escalation is needed.

### Subsidiary Formation and Secondary Sales

Subsidiaries permitted if they sign guarantees and security agreements within 1 month. Secondary sales are likely restricted — team will clarify and negotiate terms for normal Series B fundraising activities.

### Timeline

Close target: end of February. JPMorgan targeting mid-March. Richard committed to pushing for February. Communication cadence: Bridget will update board on M&A and subsidiary sign-off language, then coordinate with Richard and Dane on follow-up.
