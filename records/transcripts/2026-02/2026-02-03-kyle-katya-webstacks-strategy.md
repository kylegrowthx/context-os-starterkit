## Key Topics

### Critical Performance Drop & Investigation

- **Symptom:** Non-brand traffic collapsed 50% (7.4k → 3.7k sessions/week) starting mid-January, coinciding with WebStacks' website redesign and structural changes to core service pages.
- **Root Cause Hypotheses:** (1) Broken redirects or 404s from redesign (some GrowthX content had to be republished due to page deletions); (2) Ranking loss from internal structural changes; (3) Data tracking anomaly (Looker shows nearly 1:1 session-to-engagement ratio, atypical and suspicious).
- **Investigation Plan:** Kyle will dig into Looker and Google Search Console today, compare two-week periods, check for 404s and rank drops, validate data integrity, and report to Katya by EOD.
- **Client Communication:** Katya will flag the issue at Thursday meeting and position GrowthX as proactive diagnostician offering recommendations.
- **Urgency:** Traffic drop undermines annual contract narrative; urgent to diagnose and offer remediation path.

### Annual Contract Strategy & CFO Resistance

- **Negotiation Status:** Jesse (WebStacks) initially agreed to annual amendment; CFO Sean then demanded 20% discount, blocking deal.
- **GrowthX Counter-Argument:** Current rate is a "screaming deal" for content volume and quality; no additional discount warranted. Position annual contract as validation of strategic partnership value, not commodity renegotiation.
- **Strategic Narrative:** Use 6-month roadmap to demonstrate long-term thinking and prove GrowthX isn't vendor but partner invested in client's business outcomes.
- **Blocker:** Katya trying to get Sean to the table for the Thursday call; email response pending.

### Strategic Roadmap Framework (6-Month, Flexible)

- **Philosophy:** Structured but flexible—identify themes and bets rather than month-by-month lockdown; allows pivot if client OKRs shift or test results change.
- **Step 1 - Identify Themes:** Based on client's business goals, past test results, and traffic signals showing promise (not just volume).
- **Step 2 - Audit Content:** For each theme, assess whether gaps require net-new content, refreshes, or content consolidation.
- **Step 3 - Enhance Engagement:** Layer in elements from past tests that worked (e.g., internal linking, format variations, CTAs).
- **Key Opportunity:** Topical authority play—improve internal linking between blog articles and underperforming product/solution pages (e.g., CMS partner pages). This creates topical web, boosts rankings on solution pages, and could unlock upsell (building new supporting content).
- **Audit History:** Kyle conducted content audit 12+ months ago; needs refresh on what client has implemented vs. ignored; content refresh/consolidation recommendations still relevant.

### Q1 Initiative: CMS Partner Hub Build-Out

- **Executive Summary:** Create dynamic CMS partner hubs (starting with Sanity, expand to other platforms if successful) as both SEO bet and campaign asset engine.
- **Current Design:** Replace static "SEO bet" footer (dumping content with no user value) with dynamic banner cycling through 5 key blog topics per CMS partner.
- **Selection Logic:** Partner hub topics chosen based on partnerships WebStacks is actively strengthening/renewing (e.g., Sanity), not just keyword volume.
- **Q1 Focus:** Sanity CMS partner hub; parallel build-out of shareable campaign assets for client's big partner campaigns (both Q1 focus area and lead generation tactic).
- **Future Expansion:** If Sanity hub performs well (traffic + conversion signals), replicate for tools/platforms or other high-priority topics in Q2+.
- **Dual Benefit:** SEO traffic driver + campaign asset library for WebStacks' own partnerships; demonstrates strategic thinking beyond content volume.

- **Why It Matters:** Migration clarity needed to firm up technical scope and show client GrowthX is operationally organized for annual engagement.
