## Summary

Align on content strategy to boost Text-to-Speech (TTS) growth.

---

## Context

This is a biweekly strategic sync between GrowthX (a B2B content marketing agency) and Deepgram (an AI speech recognition platform). GrowthX is delivering content marketing and SEO strategy to drive organic traffic and conversions to Deepgram's website. The partnership focuses on content production, keyword research, and technical SEO optimization. Erik O'Brien leads GrowthX's delivery, while Troy Blanchard and Hasan Jilani lead Deepgram's content and product strategy sides. This meeting was scheduled mid-February to review January performance and realign on content priorities, particularly addressing an executive concern about TTS (text-to-speech) traffic underperformance relative to STT (speech-to-text).

---

## Relevance

**To GrowthX Delivery:**
- Clear strategic pivot toward developer-focused TTS content (evaluation, latency, concurrency, integrations) requires new keyword research and content planning methodology.
- GrowthX team must coordinate with Deepgram product teams and execute 301 redirects for the site migration issue; may involve external vendor (Weeplash) if GrowthX lacks capacity.
- Publishing cadence is strong (70 ideas in backlog, ~15 draft articles), with two major content clusters (competitive comparisons and Voice AI) driving 74% of organic sessions.
- LLM traffic is emerging as a middle-funnel channel (151 sessions in January, ~2% of total traffic) with high engagement; monitoring Gemini growth (20% month-over-month) and ChatGPT decline.

**To Deepgram Account:**
- January organic growth (2,500 sessions, beating December) shows content strategy is working; competitive content ("11 Labs Alternative") is highest ROI driver.
- TTS growth gap (200 clicks/week vs. 1,000 clicks/week for STT) is an executive priority; new developer-focused positioning could unlock significant expansion.
- Site migration from "AI apps" to "Voice AI apps" created revenue leak (LensGo page dropped from ~400 clicks/day to 410 error); 301 redirect implementation is urgent.
- Deepgram product roadmap items (pricing page, TTS evaluation docs, latency benchmarks) align with proposed content pillars; opportunity for content-product collaboration.

---

## Decisions & Commitments

**Shift TTS Content Strategy to Developer Audience.** The team agreed to pivot from voice quality and creator-focused messaging (competing with 11 Labs on TikTok and AI movie tools) to technical, developer-centric positioning. Content will focus on TTS evaluation in production, latency & concurrency benchmarks, and integration patterns — areas where Deepgram's technical advantages matter most to application builders.

**Implement 301 Redirects for Site Migration.** Troy agreed to implement 301 redirects from the old "AI apps" URLs to "Voice AI apps" pages. The Sanity team's initial concern that 301s would create "soft 404s" was overruled in favor of preserving link equity and user experience over returning hard 410 errors.

**Use Hasan's TTS Mind Map to Accelerate Content Production.** Hasan will create a mind map of TTS topics and opportunities to help Erik's keyword research move faster and align content pillars with product capabilities and market demand.

**Continue "11 Labs Alternative" Content Strategy.** Despite executives' concern about being defined by 11 Labs positioning, the team confirmed that "11 Labs Alternative" content is the highest traffic driver and should continue as a tactic alongside the new developer-focused angle.

## Key Topics

### TTS Growth Strategy

  - **Problem:** TTS organic traffic (\~200 clicks/wk) is significantly lower than STT (\~1,000 clicks/wk), raising executive concern.
  - **Solution:** Shift content strategy from voice quality to developer-centric topics.
      - **New Content Pillars:**
          - TTS evaluation in production
          - TTS latency & concurrency
          - Integrations & API usage
  - **Competitive Angle:** Target the "builder" (developer) market, where technical specs (latency, concurrency) are critical, rather than competing directly with 11 Labs for the "creator" market.
  - **Tactics:**
      - Continue creating "11 Labs Alternative" content, a proven traffic driver.
      - Use Hasan's mind map to accelerate keyword research and content production.

### January Performance Review

  - **Overall Growth:** Sessions increased to 2,500, beating the prior month.
  - **Top Content Drivers:**
      - Competitive content (e.g., "11 Labs Alternative"): \~40% of traffic.
      - "Voice AI" topic cluster: \~34% of traffic.
  - **LLM Referrals:**
      - Grew to 151 sessions, with ChatGPT at 64%.
      - "11 Labs Alternative" article drove 44 sessions with 80% engagement.
      - **Insight:** LLM traffic is middle-funnel (active evaluation) and has higher engagement but low volume (\~2% of total traffic).
      - **Opportunity:** Monitor Gemini, which grew 20% last month and indexes content faster than ChatGPT.

### Site Migration Error

  - **Issue:** A recent migration from `AI apps` to `Voice AI apps` created 410 errors for old URLs instead of using 301 redirects.
  - **Impact:** Significant traffic loss from high-value pages, including LensGo (\~400 clicks/day).
  - **Rationale:** The Sanity team argued 301s to a less relevant page would be a "soft 404" to Google.
  - **Decision:** The team agreed to implement 301 redirects, as a relevant redirect is superior to a dead page for both UX and SEO.
