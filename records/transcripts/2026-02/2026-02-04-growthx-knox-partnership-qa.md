## Key Topics

### Knox's Position and Opportunity

Knox runs the largest FedRamp managed cloud, helping tech companies like Atlassian, Adobe, and OutSystems achieve FedRamp certification "in 90 days for 90% off." Beyond compliance, Knox introduces customers directly to government buyers—high-touch relationship selling.

Irina's vision extends beyond FedRamp certification to becoming the "shepherd" for tech vendors navigating government sales. The opportunity: aggregate publicly-available federal procurement data (SAM.gov, USASpending.gov) into a usable, queryable platform that alerts vendors to relevant RFPs.

### GrowthX's Technical Approach

Marcel demonstrated several technical capabilities:
- **Agentic workflow platform (Output AI)**: Workflows-as-code framework built by former HashiCorp, AirByte, and Meltwater engineers
- **Research supervisor workflow**: Multi-loop agentic research with quality evals at every step, sourcing from verified sources only
- **Programmatic content**: Examples from Airbyte (connector library) and Augment (MCP library) showing automated data-enriched content

Key insight: For early-stage domains, you need more "quality seeds" because domain authority is low. Volume + strategic link-building is critical.

### Domain Authority Strategy

Marcel's recommendations:
1. Never waste a press release without getting a link back
2. Create a FedRamp vendor directory (similar to Ramp vendors library) that vendors will link to publicly
3. Pursue government links—they're 100x more valuable
4. Example: Procore just got FedRamp certified—a procore.com backlink would be massive

The approach: Editorial content first to build foundation and credibility, then layer programmatic content as authority grows. This is a compounding engine that requires patience but yields exponential organic growth.
