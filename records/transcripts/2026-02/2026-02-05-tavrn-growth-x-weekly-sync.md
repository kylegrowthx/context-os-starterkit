## Key Topics

### Content Performance & Strategy Pivot

  - **Performance:** The high-volume strategy is working, with 92 articles published and consistent week-over-week growth in sessions.
  - **Organic Traffic Growth (Sept→Jan):**
      - Daily Impressions: 1,000 → 30,000
      - Engaged Sessions: Growing steadily
  - **Rationale for Pivot:** The current "shooting from the hip" approach was effective for learning, but a refresh is now needed to maximize ROI from existing assets.
  - **Refresh Goals:**
      - **Improve Top Performers:** Optimize high-traffic articles to "squeeze more juice" out of them.
      - **Salvage Underperformers:** Rework or prune articles on page 2 of Google that aren't landing.
      - **Enhance Site Health:** Add forward-looking internal links to older content, creating new user pathways and signaling site structure to Google.

### Execution Plan & Communication

  - **Plan:** GrowthX will audit all 92 articles to scope the refresh effort.
  - **Cadence:** New content will continue next week to maintain momentum. The audit's findings will then determine the long-term mix of new vs. refreshed articles.
  - **Communication:** Megan agreed to the plan. Matthew will prepare a performance recap for Pedro to proactively address any concerns about pausing new content.

### Future Content & Conversion Tactics

  - **Case Studies:** Tavrn has two case studies ready and a third pending.
      - **Status:** Approved for public use on the website.
      - **Opportunity:** "Gold dust" for organic growth. Can be used as blog content, embedded CTAs, or a soft conversion event before a demo.
  - **Glossary Project:** Proposed as a separate, programmatic SEO workstream.
      - **Purpose:** Drive traffic and build domain authority without cluttering the main blog.
      - **Scope:** \~100–150 terms identified.
      - **Format:** Un-gated, definitional content structured for AI visibility.
  - **Templated Pages:** Proposed as a custom project.
      - **Purpose:** A powerful conversion tool (e.g., a free demand letter template) that lowers the barrier to entry compared to a "request a demo" CTA.
