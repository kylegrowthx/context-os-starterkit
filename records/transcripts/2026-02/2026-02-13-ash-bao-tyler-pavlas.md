## Key topics

### Ashley's current content challenges
Ashley's biggest pain point is getting timely thought leader input. She manually calls, emails, and texts SMEs to extract their POV for content. The best-case scenario is an SME who writes their own piece; more often she runs a sourcing call on Monday and doesn't get approved content out until the following week. She wants a faster approval loop with minimal SME editing required, and content that reflects both the SME's perspective and current market signals.

Content collaboration is also fragmented. Marketing and content live in Notion (Notion licenses aren't shared with the rest of the org). SME collaboration happens in Word Docs (they're a Microsoft shop). Social media manager needs content as plain text in Notion to post quickly. Result: knowledge lives in three places — Word Docs, SharePoint, and Notion — none of them clean.

There's also a distinction between content types: Triyan (internal technical writer) ships SEO-focused content fast. SME byline content is slower and more thought leadership oriented. GrowthX would likely work on the SEO/AEO volume layer first.

### GrowthX workflow architecture (demonstrated with client case studies)

**Strategy sprint phase (8 weeks, $40k):**
- Keyword universe analysis + topic cluster planning (approved with client)
- Build client-specific artifacts: personas (one per target vertical), writing guidelines (example: one client at 25 versions), competitive mapping, case studies/statistics libraries
- Establish source requirements artifact if needed (e.g., "only fitness industry publications for gym business content")
- Result: all downstream workflows have rich context for personalized output

**Artifact system (living in Airtable):**
- **Personas:** By target vertical/industry. Example: Engine's business travel personas (finance manager, operations manager, project manager) with key value props and decision drivers
- **Writing guidelines:** Good/bad examples, structure rules, terminology rules, competitor reference guidelines. Evolve as feedback comes in
- **Reference libraries:** Case studies, statistics, source exclusions/requirements

**Content production pipeline (5 articles/week execution phase):**
Each article flows through:
1. Research planning (using managing editor assignments + artifacts as context)
2. Agentic research synthesis (Claude Opus latest version + EXA/Tavily for sources)
3. Quality evaluation (auto-reject if below quality threshold; rerun research)
4. Drafting (Claude Opus with writing guidelines + persona context)
5. Validation against guidelines (rule-checking step)
6. Fact-checking (article split into passages; each passage validated against sources)
7. Metadata optimization
8. Image generation
→ Send draft to client for review → Incorporate feedback into artifacts

Human expert from GrowthX adjusts words, removes sentences, re-embeds context at each step.

**Pipeline customization examples:**
- **Biologica (medical):** Added custom compliance review step. Ingredients integration, medical claims annotation, regulatory accuracy editing
- **Webflow:** Ingested full docs library; built custom integration pages pipeline
- **Engine:** Source requirement artifact—"exclude generic small business stats; only fitness industry publications"

Each client gets a unique pipeline; any improvement applies to all clients quickly.

### Fact-checking and source validation
Ashley raised the question of how sources are handled for regulated industries. Tyler explained: the workflow auto-generates source requirements based on the assignment topic (e.g., "only use fitness industry publications for a gym business article"). Clients can also specify approved sources as an artifact — this gets embedded into the workflow. For non-regulated industries, GrowthX can start without pre-approved sources and refine through feedback. For clients like Biologica (MedTech), specific compliance source requirements are built in from the start.
