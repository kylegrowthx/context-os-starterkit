## Key topics

### Ashley's current content challenges
Ashley's biggest pain point is getting timely thought leader input. She manually calls, emails, and texts SMEs to extract their POV for content. The best-case scenario is an SME who writes their own piece; more often she runs a sourcing call on Monday and doesn't get approved content out until the following week. She wants a faster approval loop with minimal SME editing required, and content that reflects both the SME's perspective and current market signals.

Content collaboration is also fragmented. Marketing and content live in Notion (Notion licenses aren't shared with the rest of the org). SME collaboration happens in Word Docs (they're a Microsoft shop). Social media manager needs content as plain text in Notion to post quickly. Result: knowledge lives in three places — Word Docs, SharePoint, and Notion — none of them clean.

There's also a distinction between content types: Triyan (internal technical writer) ships SEO-focused content fast. SME byline content is slower and more thought leadership oriented. GrowthX would likely work on the SEO/AEO volume layer first.

### GrowthX workflow demo
Tyler walked through the full platform demo:

**Strategy sprint:** 8 weeks, $40k. Starts with keyword universe analysis, topic cluster planning (approved with client), and building out client-specific artifacts — personas, writing guidelines (updated 25+ times for one client), competitive mapping. This gives all subsequent content production the context it needs to produce relevant, personalized output.

**Artifact system:** Personas are built for every target vertical/industry. Writing guidelines include good/bad examples, structure rules, terminology to use or avoid, how to reference competitors. Case studies and statistics are brought in as artifacts too. These live in Airtable and evolve as feedback comes in.

**Content production pipeline (5 articles/week):** Each article goes through: research planning → agentic research synthesis → quality evaluation → drafting (Claude Opus + writing guidelines) → validation against guidelines → fact-checking (article split into passages, each validated against sources) → metadata optimization → image generation. Human editors from GrowthX are in the loop at each step, making adjustments before the pipeline proceeds.

**Pipeline customization example:** Biologica (medical client) has a custom compliance review step where ingredients are checked, medical claims are annotated, and content is edited for regulatory accuracy. Webflow's pipeline ingested their full docs library to build integration pages. Each pipeline is client-specific.

### Fact-checking and source validation
Ashley raised the question of how sources are handled for regulated industries. Tyler explained: the workflow auto-generates source requirements based on the assignment topic (e.g., "only use fitness industry publications for a gym business article"). Clients can also specify approved sources as an artifact — this gets embedded into the workflow. For non-regulated industries, GrowthX can start without pre-approved sources and refine through feedback. For clients like Biologica (MedTech), specific compliance source requirements are built in from the start.

### Pricing and results timeline
- Strategy sprint: $40k for 8 weeks
- Growth execution: $18k/month
- Negotiable based on fit and scope
- Results timeline: first significant organic traffic uptick at 3–6 months; some clients have achieved 20x traffic growth in 6 months

### New beta product

Tyler confirmed there are now multiple entry points: services, CheckThat for visibility, and the beta platform. Tyler mentioned Nigel (recently hired) as the main contact for the beta product track.
