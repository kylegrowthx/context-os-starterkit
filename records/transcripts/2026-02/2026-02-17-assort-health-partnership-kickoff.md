## Key topics

### 8-week onboarding and content strategy

George explained that 70 clients have now gone through the 8-week onboarding. The model starts with broad discovery to understand ICP, audience, and goals, then builds content strategy artifacts (topic clusters, competitor analysis, keyword mapping) before writing a single article. The first four weeks are collaboration-heavy — calibration articles and strategy feedback sessions set up the voice and tone for the entire engagement. Publishing ramps in weeks 5–6.

Kathy walked through the timeline: content strategy draft next week, partial lock in week 3, calibration article in week 4, 3 articles in week 5, then 5/week from week 6 onward. Week 7 is the phase-2 decision point.

Brooke asked to include Assort's CEO in the content strategy session and calibration article review. Kathy agreed and folded this into the planned "CEO deep dive" session.

### Assort Health's differentiators and product positioning

Brooke described Assort's core differentiation as handling real-world healthcare complexity and edge cases that other AI systems can't manage — for example, a patient calling about knee pain who also has chest symptoms, diabetes, and hypertension, where the AI must triage urgency correctly. Or OB/GYN double-booking requirements based on patient stage of pregnancy. Or applying different payer global period rules per insurance carrier.

This complexity mastery is the dual narrative: handling edge cases means fewer human transfers, which means labor savings and higher appointment volumes. Key customer proof points: 57% decrease in abandoned call rate, 53% of total scheduling volume handled by AI.

Brooke shared an investor deck and a Becker's Healthcare article (authored by Assort's CEO) that illustrates this through a specific dermatology patient case. She flagged that Assort has historically been too generic in describing these capabilities — the push now is to get very specific.

### Buyer personas and sales process

Target buyers: COO, Director of Operations, Director of Patient Access, CEO/President of the practice. For larger organizations (50+ providers), clinical executives sometimes get involved. Minimum deal size is 8 providers.

Mid-market (8–49 providers): $100k–$200k ACV. Enterprise (50–250 providers): $200k–$300k ACV. Health systems (ambulatory): $500k+. Assort's bread-and-butter is the 50–250 provider enterprise group.

Most customers were previously using human call agents or outsourced call centers — not AI. About 10–15% are switching from other AI vendors frustrated by slow implementation or inaccurate outputs. Sales objections center on price and contract terms (Assort does a 3-month paid pilot before a 12-month+ contract).

### Competitive landscape

Legacy competitors: Relation, Artera, Friesia — established patient engagement vendors bolting on AI. The AI-native competitors are newer startups. Transform 9 is the competitor Assort's sales team feels least confident against — similar AI-native positioning, similar market claims.

Assort's angle against legacy players: faster, more accurate, purpose-built AI. Against AI-native startups like Transform 9: deeper healthcare specificity and edge case coverage.

### Lead sources and pipeline

Events drive ~30% of pipeline. Outbound SDR cold outreach is active. Inbound from organic and AI search is minimal today — roughly 12 qualified opportunities/month. Goal is 24/month within 6 months.

The best leads come from events and warm introductions. Case studies and the Becker's Healthcare article are the content that resonates most with current prospects.

### Technical and content operations

Site has only 13 indexed pages — Kathy proposed adding a technical audit and sitemap as quick wins. George confirmed the team can deliver this.

Content approval requires sign-off from Brooke and the CEO (John). For technical or product-heavy content, the Head of Engineering or Product may also review. Brooke noted this is a clean, minimal approval chain — which will help GrowthX hit publishing pace in weeks 5–6. Webflow CMS access confirmed.

### Growth goals and metrics

- Current: 142,474 annual sessions (direct + organic + referral)
- Target: 192,000 annual sessions — a 17% quarterly increase
- Top-3 rankings for 6 priority keywords by year-end
- AI visibility: baseline 5%, target 15%
- Inbound opportunities: 12/month → 24/month in 6 months

Brooke committed to sharing Assort's OKRs so GrowthX can align content to business priorities.
