## Key Topics

### Healthcare Market Opportunity

Howard is an orthopedic surgeon dealing with a structural problem: large hospital systems with hundreds of doctors dominate referrals, while smaller specialized practices — often better positioned for complex cases — struggle to reach patients. The spine surgery market is particularly high-value: individual cases generate $200K–$400K, meaning even a few additional surgeries per week translates to millions in annual revenue.

The practice currently has ~4,000 monthly website visitors. Howard's aspiration is to build a patient acquisition engine that works without relying on hospital referrals or physician churn risk.

### GrowthX's Content Approach

Marcel walked Howard through GrowthX's process: audience research → topic mapping → daily AI-generated, medically reviewed content → site optimization → biweekly performance check-ins. The content system does deep competitor research, produces evidence-backed articles from reputable medical journals, and includes automated compliance checks and doctor annotations before publishing. Educational disclaimers are embedded throughout.

Howard's 30-year practice reputation is a constraint — content accuracy is non-negotiable. Marcel confirmed GrowthX's compliance workflow addresses this, and Howard mentioned having healthcare lawyers available for final legal review.

### Pricing, Commitment, and Exclusivity

The service is priced at $18K/month with a 12-month minimum commitment. This covers end-to-end content creation, site management, and continuous optimization. Additional specialty "lanes" can be added later.

Howard raised a budget alignment issue: his spine surgeon partner Jay is paid from collections, so budget decisions require joint buy-in. Marcel agreed to send a simple, clear engagement summary — not a complex proposal — for Howard and Jay to review together.

Geographic exclusivity was discussed and agreed: GrowthX will not work with competing local practices. Internal data confidentiality policies ensure no information is shared across clients.
