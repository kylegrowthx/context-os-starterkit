## Key Topics

### Sales Pipeline and Lead Management

February was a poor month for new deal creation. Several warm leads — including Whisper Flow — went weeks without follow-up. Tyler's bandwidth is the bottleneck; many deals stall after initial contact. The team plans to have Dig and Tyler create HubSpot opportunities retroactively for any inbound interest found in email. Marcel's EA has inbox access and can surface missed leads. Jeff (Rev Ops) will define the right deal stages for early inbound.

Marcel proposed a self-booking widget on the GrowthX website — previously drove a 50–60% win rate from single meetings but was disabled. Five inbound bookings per week still arrive via other channels.

### CheckThat Product and Conversion Strategy

CheckThat launched roughly three weeks ago with ~300 signups and ~10 daily new signups. No conversions from self-service yet. Nigel's strategy sessions are the primary conversion path: demo call → two-week trial → decision. Marcel also books some meetings separately.

Anna raised that CheckThat is difficult to find on the GrowthX website. Marcel's rationale: don't distract services-focused visitors. Anna's counterpoint: it should be a top-of-funnel lead generator with a clear path back to services. Marcel is prototyping an embedded presentation layer — enter a domain, get AEO audit insights without leaving GrowthX's environment. Strategy sessions currently validate the experience manually before any technical build-out.

### Content Quality and Editorial Challenges

A recent deep quality review surfaced disconnects between content output and client audience expectations. Sophisticated B2B audiences are receiving content that feels generic or off-target. William is fast-tracking EM hiring and onboarding. Bi-weekly calibration sessions between William, Sydney, Jen, and Panzer will be the editorial governance mechanism. Two "leads editor" roles are introduced to ensure cross-account consistency.

Marcel's framing: effectiveness (quality) before efficiency, even at a cost to margins. Publishing delays are mostly quality-related — not operational — and that accountability now sits with Panzer.

### Renewals and Client Health

Renewals are 6% below target at 74% for the quarter. Several contracts are out for signature (Airbyte, Abnormal, In a Rows). Q2 looks riskier: roughly 50% of accounts are red or at risk. Prophecy and Ambit are likely churns (CEO disengaged; technical mismatch). Udemy's restructuring is disrupting contacts. Logic faces a pipeline/go-to-market mismatch with small contracts.

Marcel's category saturation play: own all the accounts in a segment. Procore's recent acquisition of a B2B SaaS company opens adjacent budget — Hilti and Slashwire are named targets.

Healthy ARR currently at 72.25%. Leah is building combined Sheets + HubSpot tracking. Weekly ARR health tracking is recommended as the north star metric.

### Operational Visibility and Process Improvements

Jeff (Rev Ops) started February 5. He's building HubSpot syncs and a Linear board to manage incoming requests. First pipeline activity reports are just emerging. Jason is cleaning up the marketing calendar and funnel strategy for visibility. The team will document MQL volumes and lead sources to help Anna and others understand funnel health.
