## Key Topics

### DataGrid Account Reset & New Strategy

  - **Problem:** High spend yielded low ROI, with organic traffic growth lagging expectations.
      - **Context:** A November website migration caused a major traffic dip (404s, broken links, missing meta tags) that required significant recovery work.
      - **Root Cause:** A "move fast and break things" approach created a blog with high content volume but poor structure and significant cannibalization.
  - **Solution:** A strategic reset to focus on quality and measurable outcomes.
      - **New Mandate:** Treat DataGrid as a priority account, acting as a strategic partner to build a scalable organic growth machine.
      - **New Workflow:** The "custom account" process provides priority access to internal engineering, eliminating the web dev bottlenecks that previously forced a blog-only content strategy.

### Content & SEO Strategy

  - **Content Focus:** Shift from volume to quality.
      - **Output Mix:** 80% net new content, 20% refreshing high-performing legacy posts.
      - **Rationale:** Prevent cannibalization and leverage existing authority.
  - [**Taxonomy:** The final URL structure is `/agents/[agent-name]`, `/agents/for/[use-case]`, and `/guides/[guide-name]`.](https://fathom.video/share/injsR3ehZ5LHAbCXUrDeLzC7H_bmXCm5?tab=summary&timestamp=1755.0)
      - **Rationale:** This structure is simpler and avoids the complexity of a separate `/category/` structure.
  - **Author Authority:** Use real authors with LinkedIn profiles to build E-E-A-T (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness).
      - **Rationale:** Signals credibility to Google and improves content ranking.
  - **AI Visibility:** The goal is to be the recommended tool in AI-generated SERP features.
      - **Challenge:** This requires creating content that LLMs value (e.g., statistical listicles) while also driving direct conversions—a problem no one has fully solved.
