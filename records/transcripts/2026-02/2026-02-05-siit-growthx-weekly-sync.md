## Context

This is a weekly sync between Siit (an AI-powered ITSM platform) and GrowthX, the B2B content marketing agency driving their demand generation. Matthew Alves-Hill, a GrowthX strategist, and Carmel Schetrit, Siit's content/marketing lead, had been working together for nearly a year on a high-volume content strategy that produced 290 blog posts, 190 tool pages, and 129 glossary entries. January 2026 delivered exceptional results—exceeding targets with 58 marketing-qualified leads and $5k+ in pipeline creation—but it also surfaced a critical insight: the sales team reported that prospects still question Siit's legitimacy and credibility, despite strong traffic. Rather than continuing to chase volume, the team decided to pivot toward conversion optimization, focusing on the content that already works and adding the proof points (customer testimonials, logos, competitive positioning) that prospects are actually looking for.

## Key Topics

### January Performance & Traffic Growth

  - **Results:**
      - **MQLs:** 58 (trials & demos)
      - **Pipeline:** \>$5k created (vs. $1.4k goal), with one outlier deal \>$1k
  - **Traffic:** Website traffic grew 60% to 20k visitors.
      - Organic traffic to GrowthX URLs mirrors this growth, scaling from \~4k to \~12k sessions.
  - **Attribution Gap:** Inbound channel attribution remains unclear, a known issue to be fixed.

### Content Audit & Optimization Strategy

  - **Analysis:** The top 100 pages (driving \>30 sessions) were analyzed to identify optimization opportunities.
      - **Listicles (21 pages):** High traffic, but often broad (e.g., "Best Procurement Software"). Optimization goal → move ICP traffic down the funnel.
      - **ITSM Guides (\~50% of top pages):** Directly target ICPs with topics like "Microsoft Teams IT Ticketing System Guide." These are the primary focus for conversion optimization.
  - **LLM Referrals:** Minimal (\~85 total site-wide), but traffic is likely highly qualified. Attribution is difficult, as users often engage in multi-shot conversations before clicking.

### New Conversion Tactics

  - **1. Social Proof in Articles**
      - **Problem:** Sales team reports prospects question Siit's legitimacy due to a lack of visible customer proof.
      - **Solution:** Embed customer quotes and logos directly into high-performing articles.
      - **Process:** Carmel will source quotes for key industries (e.g., FinTech, Healthcare).
  - **2. Competitor Comparison Pages**
      - **Problem:** High-intent prospects are visiting competitor review pages (e.g., Serval) on the Siit site.
      - **Solution:** Create dedicated comparison pages (e.g., Siit vs. Serval), modeled on Hex's "Why companies choose Hex over Omni" page.
      - **Rationale:** This scalable format uses generic elements (FAQ, video) with specific competitive points, requiring less unique content per page.
      - **Initial Focus:** JSM, Azure Service Management, and Serval.

### Okta OAG Campaign

  - **Context:** Pausing net-new content, but will produce articles for the Okta OAG campaign.
  - **Messaging:** Position Siit as the one-stop ITSM solution, replacing the need for Okta OAG.
      - **OAG Stack:** Okta + OAG + ITSM (e.g., JSM)
      - **Siit Stack:** Okta + Siit
      - **Value Prop:** Siit eliminates tool burden by consolidating identity, app access, and ITSM.

### Messaging & Personnel Updates

  - **AI Messaging:** Adopt "AI ITSM" over "AI-powered" to signal native, integrated AI capabilities.
  - **Personnel:** Carmel is on maternity leave from May 1 to end of November. Manu will join next week as the replacement.
