## Key Topics

### Vizcom's Product and Market

Vizcom helps industrial designers transform rough sketches into photo-realistic product visualizations. Industrial design is the discipline behind physical products — desks, chairs, shoes, car interiors. Vizcom's revenue breaks down as: automotive ~45%, footwear/apparel ~35% (apparel is fastest growing). The product started as a fast rendering tool; it's evolved to emphasize physical realism as the differentiating signal in a market flooded with generic AI visuals.

### AI as Force Multiplier (and Noise Generator)

Marcel and Jordan aligned on AI's dual nature: it amplifies both productivity and noise. Vizcom targets users with clear design intent — people who know what they want to make, not just people who want to make something. This is a meaningful differentiation from consumer-facing AI art tools. The "slop" problem (low-quality AI visuals) is actually a tailwind for Vizcom — physical realism and craft become more valuable as generic AI output proliferates.

### Content Strategy Principles

Building credibility requires storytelling and inspiration, not promotion. Jordan shared examples: internally created posts showcasing iconic product designs generated strong engagement without overt selling. Marcel proposed a content approach that:
- Accelerates users' time to inspiration through curated expert and community content pools
- Blends visual appeal with substance
- Uses nostalgia and legacy design figures as emotional anchors
- Avoids "cosplaying" the target persona

Marcel also proposed a dedicated "guide" section on the Vizcom website to capture search and AI-driven traffic — unlisted from main nav to avoid disrupting brand channels.

### AI Search and Discovery

Marcel explained the complexity of AI-driven search: these engines use vast, layered signals to surface content, requiring strategic content clusters to influence outcomes. Social excels at awareness and engagement; it fails during deeper purchase/evaluation phases where authoritative content matters more. Jordan was skeptical about AI search as a direct discovery channel given its opacity — Marcel framed it as requiring experimental, adaptive strategies rather than predictable playbooks.

### Workflow and Research Approach

Marcel described GrowthX's AI-augmented research workflow: using agents to pull from forums, Reddit, and trusted sources to curate inspiration and credible data. For Vizcom specifically, the approach will blend low-intervention content curation with higher-complexity domain-specific content requiring Vizcom's internal design expertise for QA.
