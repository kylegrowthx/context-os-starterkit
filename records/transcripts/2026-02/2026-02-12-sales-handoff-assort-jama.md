### JAMA Software

JAMA came through Xfunnel — a company that does similar SEO/AEO work and was acquired by HubSpot. Post-acquisition, Xfunnel can no longer take on clients, so they made warm introductions to GrowthX for about 10 accounts. JAMA was the clearest fit for SEO and AEO services.

Main contact is Fonny (Amsterdam), who is results-focused and fast-moving. She introduced their CRO Tom during the sales process, which was useful because it helped connect content work to pipeline value for the sales side. Mario (US-based) manages their writers and creative team and will be a day-to-day contact. Fonny is the escalation path if the review process slows.

JAMA currently produces 6–8 blog posts per quarter — low, partly because they don't use AI for content. Their audience is technical (systems engineers, AEC industry), but their actual top-performing content is basic — "What is the AEC industry?" — which is low depth and drives traffic. Tyler's view: that's the opportunity. Lean into content that's working at a basic level, refresh and expand it, and then grow from there. Their competitors are huge vendors with gaps GrowthX can target.

The key tension on JAMA: they explicitly listed "technical content quality that won't embarrass JAMA" as a must-have in their partnership criteria. The team was direct — GrowthX cannot hire a systems engineering expert to write their content. But the team can get close by: (1) being transparent with the client about what's realistic, (2) bringing Tammy in early to optimize the pipeline using examples of good content, and (3) leveraging client input (voice notes, expert review of drafts) to close the gap. Sydney shared that this exact approach worked with Hex, Panther, and Redis.

The JAMA kickoff is February 17 at 7am PT. The onboarding package hadn't arrived as of this call (expected later in the week), so Tyler advised against heavily personalizing the kickoff questions — use broad discovery questions and let the client fill in the detail.

Tyler also flagged that JAMA is not a high-revenue client compared to others. The team should calibrate effort accordingly — strong service, but not disproportionate resource allocation.
