## Key Topics

### Enterprise Growth and Brand Awareness
Nerdio is transitioning from the MSP channel to targeting mid-market enterprises with 750+ employees. The main challenge is brand recognition — enterprise buyers don't know Nerdio the way MSP partners do. Positioning depends heavily on Microsoft integrations, making it hard to build independent brand visibility.

### Content Production Bottlenecks
The product marketing team currently reviews all content for technical accuracy, which limits output to about five pages per month. Carisa's team is small and relatively new. Bryan wants to shift PMMs from content execution to strategic oversight — approving source materials and content plans rather than every draft.

### Flat Budget Against 50% Growth Target
Marketing headcount and program budget will remain flat this year despite ambitious revenue goals. The team must find ways to produce significantly more content without adding resources, making an external content partner attractive.

### SEO and AI-Driven Content Performance
Marcel showed that Nerdio's organic visibility is stable but not growing. He demonstrated a direct correlation between organic search traffic and AI referrals, reinforcing the value of content investment for both traditional SEO and emerging AI discovery channels. The 800-page catalog needs regular refresh to prevent decay.

### Calibration Process and Engagement Path
Marcel proposed a structured onboarding: a one-hour kickoff interview with Carisa, identification of SMEs, tiered content review (PMMs review direction and key pieces, not every output), and iterative calibration over four weeks. Bryan suggested starting with lower-stakes topics to build confidence before scaling to core product content.
