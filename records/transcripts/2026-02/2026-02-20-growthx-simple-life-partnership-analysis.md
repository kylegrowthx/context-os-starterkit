## Context

Simple Life (fasting/weight loss app) operates in a competitive consumer wellness space with strong product-market fit: 45.5k monthly organic visits converting to 20k downloads and 757 purchases (3.8% conversion rate). The company just raised $35M and spends ~$1M/month on paid ads (primarily Meta), but faces rising CPAs and pressure from Meta's Andromeda AI testing. CEO Dennis Lazarenkov, who has previously worked in growth, marketing, research, and has fractional head of growth experience, is actively seeking organic acquisition to offset paid ad cost escalation.

GrowthX sees this as a strategic "lighthouse" case study in consumer health—a category rarely addressed in its B2B SaaS-focused portfolio. Marcel proposed an 8-week pilot ($25k-30k estimated, below the eventual $45k/month retainer) to validate content velocity, technical SEO improvements, and early conversion signals. Success in the pilot would justify a full-scale engagement scaling to 5,000+ pages over 12+ months.

## Key Topics

### Content Strategy and Scaling
Simple Life wants to expand from 120 blog pages to 5,000+ pages to achieve 10x organic sales. The current medical content is bottlenecked at 3 articles/month due to doctor review requirements. The strategy pivots to non-medical content (lifestyle, nutrition science, competitor comparisons, calculators, listicles) with ~4,500 identified opportunities. Marcel proposed a phased approach: start with 5 high-quality articles/week, measure indexing velocity and engagement signals, then ramp if metrics support. Content will be AI-assisted but human-reviewed, with citations and reputable sources to build search engine trust. Dennis acknowledged the risk of scaling too aggressively (having seen clients publish 2,000-3,000 pages with zero results in 6 months), making him receptive to a measured, data-driven ramp.

### Traffic, Conversion, and ROI Analysis
Current baseline: 45.5k monthly organic visits → 50% download conversion (20k downloads) → 3.8% purchase conversion (757 purchases/month). Average blog page gets 40-50 monthly visits with potential to double or triple. LTV is ~$70/customer/year. ROI projections are intentionally conservative: first year unlikely to show strong returns, but organic traffic compounds and becomes profitable by year two. Dennis needs granular CTR and conversion data by content category (health info, comparisons, lifestyle, etc.) to build internal business cases for the board. Committed to providing this data by March to support procurement approval for April start.

### LLM and AI Traffic Opportunity
Simple Life's site currently blocks AI bot indexing (robots.txt restrictions), resulting in zero LLM traffic. Marcel estimates this is a material missed opportunity: LLM traffic could reach 0.5x to 2x organic traffic once unblocked and content quality improves. During the call, they tested ChatGPT's response to "best fasting apps"—Simple Life wasn't mentioned despite being a top-3 app in app stores. Competitors with mediocre content dominate LLM-powered recommendations. This unlocks a new distribution channel at scale: ChatGPT + Bing's LLM search reach billions of users monthly. Marcel stressed this is as important as organic Google search and represents untapped inventory.

### Technical SEO and Site Issues
Immediate audit needed: crawlability (site structure, internal linking), page speed, mobile optimization, indexing velocity. AI bot blocking (robots.txt) must be resolved. Site has subdomains (help.simplelife.com) that complicate SEO tracking. Marcel committed to assigning a dedicated engineer and designer during the pilot to fix infrastructure issues. These fixes are prerequisites for both organic and LLM traffic scaling.

### Pilot Partnership Structure
An 8-week no-risk pilot starting in April includes: comprehensive site audit, launch of new non-medical content section, aggressive content publishing (5+ articles/week), and strategic refreshes of existing winning pages. Cost will be $25k-30k (discounted vs. $45k/month eventual retainer), reflecting GrowthX's confidence and investment in the relationship. Pilot results, engagement metrics (CTR, time-on-page), and Marcel's ROI model will be used to secure Simple Life's board buy-in and navigate procurement processes. Success triggers transition to full-scale engagement.

### Procurement and Internal Alignment
Dennis outlined the approval workflow: results from the pilot will be presented to Simple Life's CMO, who will require evidence of velocity, traction, and predictive models for the rest of the year. If early metrics support 10x projections by year-end, board approval for a full retainer becomes achievable. Marcel's spreadsheet model (showing traffic and revenue impact based on content velocity, conversion rates, and LTV) is critical to this conversation—it must be precise, conservative, and defensible. Dennis committed to gathering missing inputs (CTR by category, traffic segmentation, exact LTV calculations) by early March.
