### Website Structure and Templates

Elvis presented three URL structure options and recommended the simplest: business templates > specific template (e.g., HVAC invoice template). This avoids the URL complexity of offering both "choose by template" and "choose by industry" paths. The team agreed that templates are industry-agnostic — HVAC and legal templates are fundamentally different products, not variations. Maddy emphasized keeping pages one level deep from the homepage to maximize SEO backlink value. Vol (developer) will reuse existing site design for efficiency; if new design templates aren't available in Webflow, he'll resize the old templates rather than building new ones. Mara shared case study page recommendations with Julian (designer).

### AI Receptionist Product Positioning and Messaging

Maddy raised a critical issue: ChatGPT's responses to "best AI receptionist" queries misrepresent SmithAI as more expensive and not fully self-serve, which is inaccurate. The team explored whether "AI receptionist" is even the right keyword to target, given that questions are phrased differently (e.g., "best receptionist service" vs. "best AI receptionist service"). They identified gaps: SmithAI has onboarding services, but doesn't market itself as a DIY/self-serve option effectively. The team agreed to focus content on clarifying self-serve capabilities and to audit old blog posts for outdated pricing, features, or terminology that ChatGPT might cite.

### AI Visibility and Content Strategy

Jason Gong introduced GrowthX's AEO approach: track specific prompts via Scrunch, analyze which sources ChatGPT cites, and publish content to fill citation gaps. The team debated whether to track broad keyword variations (60+ competitor prompts on "AI receptionist alternatives") or focus narrowly on high-intent bottom-funnel queries (feature, pricing, competitor comparisons). Elvis shared a case study: targeting "AI roleplay training," they expanded to 150+ keyword clusters across industries and achieved 50% LLM visibility in 4 months. Jason argued that prompts mentioning product recommendations are more valuable than pure informational prompts. The team agreed that understanding how ChatGPT phrases questions and what it cites is key to strategy. Maddy will share top-converting keywords and a Salesforce export of lead-generating pages to refine the prompt list.

### Scrunch Account Setup and Next Steps

SmithAI already signed up for Scrunch (recommended by Marcel) and has an account ready. Jason proposed that GrowthX track prompts and manage the account jointly. The team agreed to grant GrowthX access to SmithAI's Scrunch login. Jason will join the next SmithAI sync (3–4:30 PM ET next week) to continue the conversation and align on content priorities. The team also discussed future potential for PR, UGC, and backlink strategies, noting that citations in news, case studies, and reviews carry weight in LLM responses.
