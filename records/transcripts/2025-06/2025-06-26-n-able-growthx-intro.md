## Context

N-Able is a $500M public company spun off from SolarWinds approximately four years ago. The company was traditionally focused on IT management and remote monitoring tools (RMM) but is undergoing a strategic pivot to cybersecurity. This shift was catalyzed by the acquisition of Adlumen, a security vendor, in November 2024. N-Able now operates three business units—endpoint management, security operations, and data protection—and is the rare player in the mid-market that owns all three pieces of the solution. The company operates with a channel-led go-to-market motion, selling primarily through managed service providers (MSPs) to mid-market organizations (250–2,500 employees). Vikram Ramesh, the CMO, met Marcel Santilli before the Adlumen acquisition and has been seeking to partner with GrowthX to support this transformation. Andrew Beyer leads demand generation; Kyle Gaudreau, a GrowthX customer from his prior role at Homebase, now serves as VP of Customer Growth at N-Able. This is the first substantive conversation between the full N-Able leadership team and the GrowthX core team.
