## Key Topics

### Content Performance and Strategy

- 30 articles published in June with 12 additional articles planned for next week; all refreshes are now live
- Clicks and impressions improving month-over-month (25 million impressions in June); trend shows consistent month-to-month growth
- HubSpot data shows 34 total leads, 3 U.S. leads (down significantly from 12 in prior months); new content batch focused on beauty segment and executive-focused content intended to improve lead generation
- Topics to be shared in Slack for approval before publishing; Ritesh and Vincent (CEO) will review and sign off on next week's content topics

### Lead Generation Concerns

  - Significant decrease in U.S. leads for June (3) compared to previous months (12)
  - Team to investigate reasons behind the drop in U.S. leads
  - Discussion on hierarchy of conversions: demos \> free trials \> other (e.g., playbook downloads, webinars)

### CTA Optimization

- Current strategy heavily favors "Book a Demo" CTAs across site and blog; free trial CTA exists only at bottom of page, below the fold
- Jenn and Megan proposed testing additional free trial CTAs higher on blog pages to capture readers not ready for demo commitment but willing to trial the product
- Ritesh confirmed previous A/B test on homepage showed 2x free trial signups when option was presented; however, business model prioritizes enterprise demos over volume free trials (large gap in revenue between basic and enterprise plans)
- Cameron to develop new CMS setting to enable A/B testing blog sidebar CTAs; will pair similar-traffic blogs with demo-only vs. free-trial CTA variants to measure conversion impact without needing expensive paid testing software

### Content Improvements

  - Suggestion to add more images and videos to blog posts to improve readability and engagement
  - Consider adding GIFs and visual elements to break up text-heavy content

### Translation Strategy

- Megan confirmed translation capability was discussed with Marcel (CEO); Spanish selected as MVP language with option to expand later (French, Portuguese, etc.)
- Translation would require additional cost beyond original SOW; pilot approach minimizes investment before full rollout
- Cameron will manually translate top 20 performing blogs using Webflow's built-in translation tools; Ritesh noted Fireflow recently upgraded to Jetflow with AI localization features already used for homepage, product pages, and demo pages
- GrowthX native Spanish speakers will QA translations internally; team will monitor traffic impact over 1-2 weeks before deciding to scale translation effort
- Success metric: Spanish blog traffic and lead generation must match English equivalent to justify expansion

### Technical SEO Improvements

- Sitemap architecture problem: WordPress site still serves as the default root site, with Webflow URLs nested underneath for specific pages; sitemap points to WordPress, causing new Webflow blog pages to be indexed slower and with less priority
- Webflow has native auto-sitemap feature (toggleable per page) that would resolve indexing; immediate solution is to move sitemap control to Webflow and complete WordPress retirement
- Jenn confirmed GSC shows many /blog pages still not indexed; agreed to scan for pending indexing issues and check whether redirect/duplicate issues exist
- Cameron will focus on completing WordPress-Webflow migration in parallel with marketing team's homepage refresh requests; migration is higher-priority from SEO perspective and will unlock benefits across all new content
- Technical vs. content SEO gap: GrowthX team focuses heavily on content-driven SEO while Webflow setup enables technical SEO; both needed for optimal performance
