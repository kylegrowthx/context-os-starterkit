## Summary

Two major team transitions happened this week: Aida moved into a senior onboarding role while Jessalynn joined as her replacement, and Devon announced his last week at Webstacks before preparing a seamless handoff roadmap. The team discussed strong blog performance — 1,068 clicks (9% week-over-week growth) as an all-time high — but acknowledged that lead generation has dropped since December due to a strategic shift from lead-gen download panels to middle-funnel conversion pages. ## Key Topics

### Team Transitions

- Aida Knezevic transitioning from account manager to senior ME focused on client onboarding; moving from day-to-day Webstacks support to scaling GrowthX's onboarding process
- Jessalynn Jones joining as new ME, replacing Aida on Webstacks account; brings editorial background and tech marketing experience from Slack, Ramp, AppCues, and agency/internal team experience
- Aida will remain in weekly syncs for next 2-3 weeks to ensure smooth knowledge transfer to Jessalynn
- Devon Wood announcing departure next week from Webstacks; committed to delivering a roadmap by week's end to maintain continuity through transition
- Content team remains stable: Joseph continues as content manager, Jakub stays as strategy lead

### Content Performance & Lead Quality

- Best week for blog traffic: 1,068 clicks, 9% week-over-week increase
- GA4 Explorer reports show increasing completion rates for active users landing on blog
- Lead generation volume decreased since December (not due to traffic drop, but due to conversion panel strategy shift)
- Original lead-gen strategy: download guides captured many low-quality leads who never engaged with sales
- High-quality lead came from CMS comparison blog this week, validating shift toward evaluative content despite lower overall traffic
- Next steps: deep dive into conversion panel performance across high-traffic blogs to determine ROI

### Content Strategy: Messaging Framework

- Webstacks developed positioning framework based on Gong call analysis of client and partner conversations
- Framework emphasizes three core elements: website migration challenges, CMS selection pain points, and Webstacks' market differentiation
- Goal: ensure all new content reflects this positioning consistently — "should all feel like one coherent narrative"
- Implementation: integrate framework into content polishing instructions rather than rewriting all historical content
- Testing approach: CMS comparison blogs already succeeding; will test framework on thought leadership pieces and "build in public" content
- Thought leadership generation: Using Gong keyword tracking and AI features to extract topic ideas at scale from hundreds of weekly client calls

### Upcoming Content

- Web3 content: 5 new pieces publishing tomorrow to Webstacks blog
- BDB (Business Database) content batch: Devon preparing to share with GrowthX for integration with messaging framework
- Thought leadership topics: To be generated by Devon using new messaging framework and Gong insights
- Content creation workflow: iterate on how to apply framework across blog types while maintaining SEO rigor

### Conversion Panel Optimization

- Strategic shift from lead-generation panels (download guides) to middle-funnel panels (service pages, case studies, book-a-call CTAs)
- Challenge: many high-traffic blogs now have no conversion panel or mismatched panels (e.g., 150-view blog with no applicable panel)
- Analysis needed: identify which conversion panels work best, which blog types benefit most, and where gaps exist
- Higher quality bar: prioritizing medium-fit and high-fit leads over volume
- Pattern matching work: finding which content types (CMS comparisons vs. educational vs. trend pieces) convert best and why
