## Context

Dimmo is a B2B SaaS marketplace that connects buyers with product demos across 550+ companies. Troy Munson (co-founder, with Lucas handling operations) signed up with GrowthX about 5 months ago to drive organic traffic to their product review pages—a unique position because, unlike typical SaaS, Dimmo's revenue depends on people visiting individual product pages to watch demos and signal buying intent.

The engagement started with a blog-heavy strategy (first 3 months), then pivoted about a month ago to enriching product pages with unique content assets: AI-generated feature breakdowns, transcriptions, and demo embeds. This reflects Dimmo's core business: they control the demo data, so pages can offer proprietary insights Google doesn't see elsewhere.

GrowthX has maintained high publishing velocity: 45 product reviews published since Friday, June 13. 8 more reviews are on track for completion this week. The team is managing the transition smoothly despite Prateep's departure and content deadlines remain on schedule.

### The Traffic Problem: Impressions vs. Clicks

Dimmo ranks for keywords and gets impressions, but isn't converting them to clicks. Performance metrics: ~50 clicks/week to product pages (down from a spike to 70, which then settled back to baseline). Troy is tracking monthly visitors: expected 100k in 6 months per earlier GrowthX benchmarks, actually at ~200/month. Root cause unclear—could be titles, structure, headers, internal linking, or keyword selection.

### Strategy Evolution and Technical Foundation

Initial engagement (first 3 months): blog-heavy approach. Current approach (last 1-2 months): enriching product pages with proprietary demo data, transcriptions, and AI-extracted features. Technical SEO: 98% of recommended improvements implemented; performing well on technical fundamentals. Challenge: technical excellence isn't translating to traffic growth, so next focus is strategic (keyword research, page structure, competitive analysis).

### Competitive and Structural Analysis

Jenn and Megan noted that Dimmo pages are overly long compared to top-ranking competitors. Header repetition is excessive. Titles should emphasize "pricing + features + demo" since that's what buyers search for (brand name + pricing, or demo access). Competitor comparison: G2 is the closest model (review authority platform), but G2 has a 10-year head start and established authority. Jenn proposed a refresh strategy: focus on top 200 highest-search-volume keywords, then refresh the next 80 cohort—a strategy Prateep had started.

### Renewal Risk and Next Steps

Troy flagged that by the 6-month mark (late June), if clicks haven't substantially increased beyond 50/week, the engagement becomes hard to justify financially. He may shift to paid/outbound campaigns instead. Megan committed to delivering a complete strategy overhaul (including keyword research, competitive analysis, and page optimization priorities) by Wednesday, June 20. Team will skip the normal Tuesday call to allow prep time.
