## Context

This is an internal GrowthX pod meeting led by Mara Leighton, the delivery lead for the pod, with her direct reports and collaborators. The team recently transitioned from AirOps to Atlas, a new content generation and management platform, as part of GrowthX's broader operational shift. The meeting took place at the end of the first week of this transition (June 20, 2025) and focused on gauging team comfort with the new tool, addressing concerns about workload and capacity, and ensuring alignment on weekly deliverables for key client accounts including Abnormal Security, DataGrid, RAMP, Strapi, Prophecy, and Galileo. The pod also works with external team members at GrowthX Labs (Nika, Mariana, Ismail, Vivek), adding complexity to coordination and knowledge-sharing around the new platform.

## Relevance

**To GrowthX Delivery:**
- Atlas is proving significantly faster than AirOps for content generation (49 seconds for research tasks vs. longer AirOps timelines), directly improving delivery velocity on core client work.
- Uneven adoption and time constraints are creating risk: Timmy had minimal Atlas exposure and is considering weekend work to catch up; Ismail was absent but has already generated 2 RAMP articles and updated artifacts for three clients (Prophecy, Galileo, RAMP).
- New methodology for documentation and knowledge capture needed: handoff meetings are happening verbally without captured context, creating inefficiency when onboarding team members or scaling help.
- Workload distribution problem emerging on article review: Mariana flagged concern about volume of articles to review weekly, suggesting the new platform's speed may be outpacing review capacity.

**To CheckThat:**
- Atlas AI assistant is being tested and showing promise for content editing tasks (highlighting sections to adjust tone/content), though early errors suggest refinement needed. This is relevant to CheckThat's prompt engineering and AI visibility work.

**To GrowthX Business Development:**
- Team sentiment is positive on Atlas despite growing pains: faster performance and AI editing features are valued, suggesting the tool supports better client delivery quality and timeline expectations going forward.
