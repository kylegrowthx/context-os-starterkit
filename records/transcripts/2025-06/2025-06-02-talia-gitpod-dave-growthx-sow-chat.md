## Key Topics

### Current Challenges with Content Creation

- Quality calibration issues on SEO content—internal team flags work as not matching Gitpod's tone and voice
- SME gap: Jen (GrowthX's managing editor) lacks technical background to catch industry-level errors that an expert would spot immediately
- High internal bar: every piece must be shareable to Gitpod's audience; employees don't want bylines on "pure SEO" pieces
- Talia is bottleneck: reviews take ~1 hour per article due to multiple topics and fact-checking against industry standards (not just product)

### Gitpod's Upcoming Changes

- Tomorrow (June 3): Soft launch of ONA (Gitpod's new software engineering agent)
- September: Full rebrand from Gitpod → ONA; new domain, new brand identity
- Platform expansion: Securing enterprise AI tools (e.g., running Cursor with enterprise security). Targets regulated industries (financial services, etc.)
- Infrastructure: Migrating website from all-in-Gitpod to Next.js + CMS; currently using 100-page contractor for migration but full content strategy still uncertain
- Risk: New domain = zero domain authority; will need to invest in backlinks and domain building regardless of content strategy

### GrowthX's Proposed Solutions

- 4-week accelerated strategy sprint using Jobs-to-be-Done framework (trained by Techstars/JTBD creator); team: Marcel, George (ex-Techstars), Ada (best ME), Marcel (best content manager, first-principles thinker), Kirkland & Marcus (AI engineers)
- Dev-in-the-loop model: developers ($30–50/hour) embedded in content review to catch technical errors; trade-off is volume vs. quality
- Technical content lanes: DGraph model (foundational conceptual content that ranks well, not just code docs). Also building glossaries, tutorials, API enrichment.
- Fact-checking workflow: decompose articles into chunks, extract facts, validate against Gitpod's docs and public sources
- Website migration support: 100+ pages, taxonomy improvement, kill non-performing content, handle URL migrations, image CDN, alt text—based on experience with Abnormal, HyperMode, Augment Code

### Contract and Pricing Discussion

- 3-month commitment at $9K/month (vs. GrowthX standard $15K+ starting rate). Marcel willing to discount due to relationship and confidence they can carve out a strong lane.
- Rationale: 3 months = time to prove ROI and run strategy sprint; ties to Gitpod's September launch timeline
- Talia needs to get CFO approval; wants internal memo and materials from Marcel to justify to finance
- If deal doesn't work: "No hard feelings," Marcel says. But positioning this as GrowthX betting on the partnership (burden mostly on GrowthX to deliver).
