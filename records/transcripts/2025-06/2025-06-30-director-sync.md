## Summary

Following the on-site retreat, the GrowthX leadership team aligned on three operational imperatives: proactively addressing bottlenecks (publishing, PSEO workflows) and treating every client request as an opportunity to build reusable artifacts; implementing a holistic client health dashboard in Notion to track stakeholder engagement, output delivery, quality perception, and performance; and ensuring weekly visibility into account status to catch churn risks early — with 50% customer turnover this year and losses like Credit Karma ($18k/month) caught too late, directors now own real-time reporting accountability to prevent future surprises.

## Context

GrowthX is a B2B content marketing services firm in the middle of a critical inflection. The team has grown delivery capacity (29+ active customers, expanding engineer pool with new resources alongside Kirkland) and recently completed an on-site retreat to reset operations. This director sync is a post-retreat alignment meeting where leadership is working to operationalize the insights and commitments from that retreat. The core tension: Marcel has zero visibility into which accounts are healthy, growing, or at risk — he's getting surprised by churn at the last minute (Flossum, Credit Karma) despite running a lean organization with more headcount per client than ever before. The meeting reflects a shift toward process-driven account health monitoring and proactive bottleneck resolution rather than reactive firefighting.
