## Key Topics

### Atlas Platform Architecture

Atlas is GrowthX's newly-launched content generation platform that orchestrates a multi-step workflow with human intervention at each stage. The system starts with canonical AI documents (audience personas, company context, writing guidelines) that inject client-specific direction into every generation step. The workflow is: input (topic/keyword/angle) → assignment brief (SEO metadata, keyword analysis, top-ranking competitors, proposed structure) → research (information gathering) → outline (extensive editing for structure, word count, coverage) → article draft → fact-checking → flagging for medical review.

### Fact-Checking and Quality Controls

The fact-checking pipeline extracts passages with specific claims and verifies them against external sources, providing links where found. If a claim cannot be verified, the system inserts an auto-generated disclaimer explaining that the claim lacks research support (e.g., "deep breathing for anger lacks scientific backing"). Editors can flag any section requiring human medical review using VR tags in the article, allowing reviewers to jump directly to flagged sections rather than reading the full piece. This combination of automated fact-checking and human-in-the-loop review significantly reduces AI quality issues (artifacts and prompt artifacts).

### Content Strategy: Dossier-First Sourcing

Brian emphasized that facts should be pulled from Biologica's approved dossiers (substantiation documents) rather than third-party tools like Perplexity. This ensures content is grounded in Biologica's own research. The team is still in dossier-building phase (currently one dossier available, two more coming soon), and the development team is exploring whether to build a knowledge base or use APIs to integrate research LLMs like Perplexity with the dossiers.

### Content Clusters and SEO Approach

Biologica's content OS is organized into five clusters: three generation-based (women in 20s, perimenopause, men 50+), ingredients/supplement education, and HRT/medication/supplement synergy. The team uses two keyword research approaches: (1) identify audience pain points, then use SEMrush to find matching search queries, and (2) analyze direct competitors' content (e.g., HelloLine) to extract keywords they rank for. The strategy balances high-difficulty foundational content (needed for domain authority) with easier-win keyword opportunities. Current focus is on perimenopause, with expectation of broader coverage by end of September.

### Content Review and Calibration Process

The calibration article on "perimenopausal anger" is a test to validate that the artifacts are working as intended. Brian will review the article using Google Docs suggestion mode for line edits, plus top-of-doc summary feedback covering tone, accuracy, and Biologica mentions. The content should strike a balance between approachable/relatable and straightforward/credible (especially for medical/science-backed claims). Aida is out of office Friday (available Thursday morning), so comments are due EOD Friday to maintain momentum.

### Next Test: Ingredient-Based Content

After calibrating on "perimenopausal anger," the next article will focus on an ingredient topic. This will pressure-test the system's ability to correctly reference and extract information from Biologica's dossiers, rather than pulling from general sources.
