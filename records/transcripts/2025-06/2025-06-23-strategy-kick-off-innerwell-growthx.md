## Summary

GrowthX and Innerwell held a strategy reset meeting to rebuild confidence in their partnership after execution challenges. Anthony Mangia and Andrew Berman (Innerwell leadership) emphasized that speed and quality execution are the priority, while George Haikal (GrowthX) proposed a two-pronged SEO strategy: city-level pages leveraging Innerwell's clinic/provider data across 27+ states, and programmatic long-tail content built from ICD codes and symptom-based conditions. The key blocker is scaling content creation within Innerwell's medical review requirements, but GrowthX outlined a template-based approach with pre-defined content frameworks to balance compliance and scale, committing to present city-level and editorial databases by end of week.

---

## Context

Innerwell is a modern behavioral healthcare practice focused on ketamine-assisted therapy, with revenue currently split 85% from ketamine and 15% from traditional therapy, psychiatry, and EMDR. They operate across 27 states (targeting 35 by year-end) and differentiate on insurance coverage for ketamine (reducing patient costs from $1,000 to ~$600), clinical rigor (matching patients with psychiatrists and licensed therapists rather than guides), and cutting-edge treatment options. They compete primarily with Mindbloom in ketamine (where they're #2) and with broader platforms like Headway and BetterHelp for general therapy. The engagement with GrowthX started with a goal to reduce paid media spend and drive organic patient acquisition, but execution issues have eroded confidence. This reset meeting represents an inflection point: Innerwell leadership wants to see speed, quality delivery, and clear strategic thinking before continuing the partnership.

## Relevance

**To GrowthX Delivery:**
- Medical review bottleneck is the critical path item — Innerwell's process currently requires human review of all content, blocking scale. Proposed solution: template-based programmatic content with pre-defined frameworks reduces review scope to flagging medical claims only.
- Prismic CMS is a known bottleneck; GrowthX signaled willingness to explore alternatives and allocate design/dev resources to solve publishing workflow.
- Provider data quality is the input layer for city-level pages — need to enrich Innerwell's clinic/provider database with locations, specialties, and appointment availability before launching pages.

**To GrowthX Business Development:**
- Innerwell is actively expanding (6 new states launched YTD, targeting 35 by EOY) with strong insurance partnerships driving conversion (insurance-accepted therapy now competitive vs. Mindbloom on price).
- High execution-risk account: leadership explicitly flagged that execution failures (not strategy misses) are the issue. Failure to deliver on databases by end of week will further damage trust.
- Opportunity for expansion: as demand scales, Innerwell will need help recruiting providers — potential upsell if supply-side pressure materializes.
- CheckThat relevance: Innerwell's competitive advantage depends partly on search visibility and content authority. Long-tail condition/symptom content could serve dual purpose of driving SEO and supporting AI-powered symptom matching.

## Key Topics

### Current Business Model and Offerings

  - 85% of revenue from ketamine-assisted therapy, aiming for 60/40 split with traditional services in the future
  - Expanded to 27 states, targeting 35 by year-end
  - Significant focus on insurance coverage to increase accessibility
  - Offers ketamine therapy, EMDR, psychiatry, and traditional therapy
  - Positioned as a modern behavioral healthcare practice with access to cutting-edge treatments

### Competitive Landscape

  - Main ketamine competitor: Mindbloom (Innerwell is \#2 in the market)
  - Broader competition: Headway, BetterHelp, Talkspace
  - Differentiation: Insurance coverage for ketamine, comprehensive care options, clinical rigor

### Provider Recruitment and Value Proposition

  - Strong inbound interest from providers due to:
    1.  Novel treatment modalities (especially ketamine)
    2.  Insurance coverage
    3.  Clinical rigor and training opportunities
  - Currently maintaining 3-5 day appointment availability in most markets

### Proposed Growth Strategy

1.  City-level SEO:
      - Creating extensive city/county pages
      - Leveraging existing data for provider profiles and listings
2.  Programmatic content creation:
      - Building a database of conditions, symptoms, and topics
      - Creating long-tail, personalized content at scale
      - Balancing medical review requirements with scalability

### Execution Challenges

  - Need for efficient medical review process for scaled content creation
  - Potential bottlenecks in CMS (Prismic) and content publishing workflow
