## Key Topics

### Content Strategy Process Overview

  - Start with internal audit using SEMrush to analyze existing blog content
  - Create keyword database in Airtable, importing CSVs from SEMrush exports
  - Find external opportunities by identifying relevant competitor sites
  - Use filters to narrow down keyword sets (e.g., keyword difficulty, search volume)
  - Enrich data with rollups, AI-generated descriptions, and difficulty levels
  - Create topic clusters and assignments based on keyword data
  - Final output: assignments categorized by status, difficulty, and topic cluster

### Tools and Workflow

  - Airtable for large datasets (50,000+ keywords)
  - Notion for client-facing presentations and smaller projects
  - SEMrush for keyword research and competitor analysis
  - AI-powered assignment creation workflows (coming soon to internal system)

### Client-Specific Approaches

  - Town.com: Focus on 100-200 low-hanging fruit opportunities, avoid overwhelming
  - Engine: Demonstrate extensive opportunities to encourage scaling up
  - Tailor strategy depth to client maturity (e.g., Biologica as a new site needs higher quality focus)

### Competitive Research

  - Create database of competitors (direct and indirect)
  - Analyze traffic, page count, and relationship to client
  - Categorize competitors (e.g., behemoths, content-forward SaaS, niche players)
  - Identify 3 exemplary sites as potential inspiration for client's approach

### Time Estimation

  - Thorough content strategy development: 3-4 hours per client (with experience)
  - Initial attempts may take longer; efficiency improves with practice
