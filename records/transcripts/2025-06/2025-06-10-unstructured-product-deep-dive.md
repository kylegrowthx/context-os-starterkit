## Summary

GrowthX and Unstructured aligned on a content strategy partnership to produce 50 articles per month targeting top-of-funnel awareness in the AI and data engineering space, with light mentions of Unstructured woven throughout. Key tension emerged around balancing volume with accuracy: while GrowthX emphasizes SEO rankings and LLM influence, Unstructured's DevRel team worried about inaccurate product claims or unsupported feature mentions. ## Context

GrowthX (B2B content marketing services) is exploring a partnership with Unstructured (AI/ML data processing platform) to create high-volume awareness content. Unstructured's product bridges multiple personas—from data engineers to marketers—and can process unstructured data (documents, PDFs, images) for use in AI applications and LLM contexts. The meeting brought together Jason Gong (GrowthX content strategist) and Saskia Wartnaby (GrowthX) with Unstructured's product and DevRel team (Maria Khalusova, Paul Cornell, Nicholas Van Nest, Stefanie Segar) to resolve a strategic misalignment: GrowthX wants to write broad, top-of-funnel content that ranks for generic keywords and influences LLM responses, while Unstructured's team is concerned about protecting product positioning and avoiding inaccurate claims at scale.

## Key Topics

### Content Strategy Overview

  - Goal is to create top-of-funnel awareness content, not deep product marketing
  - Articles should rank for relevant keywords and influence LLM responses
  - Light mentions of Unstructured acceptable, full accuracy required when product is discussed
  - Volume (50 articles/month) is key for SEO impact

### DevRel Team Concerns

  - Worried about inaccuracies or implying product capabilities that don't exist
  - Time/effort required to thoroughly review high volume of content
  - Unsure of value-add for broad, non-product-specific articles
  - Suggested product managers may be better suited for some reviews

### Balancing Accuracy and Volume

  - GrowthX to improve grounding mentions of Unstructured in official docs/website content
  - Consider separating articles into "light touch" (minimal review) and "high priority" (full review)
  - Iterate on initial set of articles to calibrate process and expectations
  - May not need to explicitly call out every unsupported feature if product isn't central focus

### LLM Influence Strategy

  - More product-specific content needed to shape LLM responses about Unstructured
  - Balance between broad keyword ranking and targeted LLM-optimized content
  - Consider separating strategy for Google search vs LLM influence

### Next Steps and Evaluation

  - GrowthX to revise initial articles based on feedback
  - DevRel to review revised articles, assess improvement
  - Decide on sustainable review process within 1-2 weeks
  - Consider building automated fact-checking system using Unstructured's public docs
