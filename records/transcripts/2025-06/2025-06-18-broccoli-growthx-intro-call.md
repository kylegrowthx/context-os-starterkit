## Summary

Broccoli and GrowthX aligned on exploring a potential partnership to accelerate Broccoli's content and marketing strategy. Broccoli, a fast-growing SaaS platform that achieved $2M ARR in 12 months following a product pivot, is looking to transition growth from word-of-mouth to scalable channels. GrowthX presented its two-phase engagement model — a $20K 6-8 week strategy sprint followed by ongoing content execution at ~$12K/month — with the goal of building differentiated content experiences (e.g., interactive use case pages, AI-powered interactive demos) rather than generic blog posts. Both teams aligned on next steps: GrowthX will prepare Broccoli-specific opportunities ahead of a follow-up call on June 26th.

## Context

Broccoli is a B2B SaaS platform founded 4 years ago that pivoted to a new product offering 12 months ago and has since grown to $2M annual revenue with a team of 9 people. The company has been built primarily through inbound word-of-mouth — customers discovering them via Facebook groups and referrals from marketing agencies. Broccoli recently raised capital and is now looking to invest in building a more systematic marketing and content foundation to accelerate beyond their current organic growth channels. They're exploring working with external partners like GrowthX to help establish this infrastructure.

GrowthX is a B2B content marketing services firm that helps early-stage and growth-stage software companies scale organic traffic and demand through editorial content creation powered by AI workflows. The firm was founded by veterans including a former TechCrunch editor-in-chief and offers a structured engagement model: a strategy sprint to deeply understand the customer's market and build a repeatable content playbook, followed by ongoing execution.
