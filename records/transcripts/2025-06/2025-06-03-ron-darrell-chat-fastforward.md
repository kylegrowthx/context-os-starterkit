## Summary

Ron Miller, the founder of FastForward at Boldstart Ventures, discusses launching a high-quality executive interview publication featuring CIOs, CISOs, and CTOs from major enterprises. The conversation covers the rise of journalists transitioning to content and brand roles at tech companies and VCs, driven by declining traditional journalism opportunities and companies' desire to build quality owned audiences. Darrell and Ron explore how AI is reshaping content creation—tools like Claude help with editing and proofreading but risk creating derivative training data—and the opportunity for high-quality original content to rank well in AI-powered answer engines like Perplexity.

---

## Context

Ron Miller is an experienced journalist who left TechCrunch to launch FastForward with Boldstart Ventures in late 2022. FastForward is a high-quality editorial publication featuring in-depth interviews with enterprise C-suite executives (CIOs, CISOs, CTOs) from major companies like Goldman Sachs, Salesforce, Snowflake, and Databricks. The goal is to build a valuable network of executives that benefits Boldstart's portfolio companies while establishing FastForward as a trusted source in the enterprise technology space. This conversation with Darrell Etherington explores broader industry trends around quality content strategy and the evolving role of journalism in tech marketing.

## Key Topics

### Fast Forward Origin and Purpose

  - Ed and Elliot from Boldstart approached Ron 3-4 years ago about a media project
  - Ron initially declined, but reconsidered in 2022 due to changes at TechCrunch
  - Launched in December 2022 after months of planning and initial interviews
  - Focus on featuring CIOs, CISOs, CTOs from significant companies, not startups
  - Goal: Build a network of executives that could eventually benefit portfolio companies through events and connections

### Content Strategy and Growth

  - Initial challenge of attracting high-profile guests without an established publication
  - Targeted 4-5 high-quality company names to start (e.g. Salesforce CIO, Blackstone CISO)
  - Experienced "FOMO effect" with PR teams reaching out after initial interviews published
  - Now receiving inbound requests from major tech companies (Goldman Sachs, Snowflake, Databricks)
  - Newsletter drives traffic to blog; includes weekly commentary, blog highlights, news briefs

### Journalism Trends and Brand Content

  - Increasing trend of experienced journalists moving to content roles at tech companies/VCs
  - Reasons include fewer traditional journalism jobs and better compensation
  - Companies recognize value of quality content for building and owning audiences
  - Shift from mass audiences to engaged niche audiences who deeply care about specific topics
  - Challenge: How to train new journalists when entry-level positions are disappearing

### AI and Content Creation

  - Ron experiments with AI tools like Claude for proofreading and editing assistance
  - Mixed results: Helpful for catching repeated phrases, but also makes errors (e.g. suggesting non-existent changes)
  - Concern about AI models becoming derivative if not trained on fresh, original content
  - Human expertise still crucial for producing truly valuable, insightful content
  - Discussion of Google I/O announcements and OpenAI's marketing approach
