## Summary

Andi Bailey conducted a feedback call with Andy Drukarev (FOSSA) to understand why FOSSA decided to bring content generation in-house after a short engagement with GrowthX. FOSSA's technical product, GitHub-based publishing workflow, and reliance on proprietary internal data made it inefficient to work with an external agency—Andy is now using AI for roughly 40% of content creation in-house. Andy emphasized that GrowthX's future opportunity lies in offering true subject matter experts for QA in technical industries, which would differentiate GrowthX from the current 95% AI + surface-level human QA model.

---

## Context

FOSSA is a developer-focused cybersecurity company with a technical product and sophisticated audience. They reached out to GrowthX (likely through a CEO-to-CEO connection) with the goal of modernizing and scaling their content program using AI tools. Andy Drukarev leads content strategy and recognized that FOSSA needed to move faster with quality content—both long-form editorial and PSEO assets. However, during the engagement, it became clear that FOSSA's constraints (no CMS, all content lives in a GitHub repository and requires manual Markdown/code publishing; need for subject matter expertise; reliance on proprietary internal data for PSEO content) made working with an external agency inefficient. The partnership ended after a short period, but Andy reported strong onboarding from GrowthX and appreciated the extra layer of QA on code snippets. The call was part of GrowthX's post-engagement feedback process to understand where they succeeded and where they could improve for technical clients.

### Challenges and Reasons for Bringing Content In-House

  - Subject matter expertise: High bar for quality in technical product/audience
  - PSEO content quality reliant on internal data, difficult to communicate externally
  - Non-traditional content publishing process:
      - No CMS, content assets in GitHub repo
      - Manual publishing in Markdown and code
  - Inefficient to work with external agency given these factors

### Current Content Generation Approach

  - Andy using AI for \~40% of content creation

  - Not yet comfortable with 90%+ AI-generated content for longer-form editorial
  - Open to more AI involvement in PSEO content

### Feedback on GrowthX Partnership

  - Strong onboarding, despite initial competing priorities
  - Appreciated extra layer of QA for code snippets
  - Valuable learning experience, though ultimately decided in-house approach was necessary

### Future Content Strategy Considerations

  - Potential shift towards 40-50% AI involvement in content creation
  - Anticipation of increased focus on craftsmanship and quality
  - Suggestion for GrowthX: Offer true subject matter experts for technical industries

### GrowthX's Evolving Platform and Workflows

  - Transition to internal platform with custom code base
  - More specific prompting and better content control
  - Developing company-specific artifacts (personas, writing guidelines, technical guardrails)
  - Integration of deep research and evolving brand context
  - Potential for more accurate and tonally relevant content generation
