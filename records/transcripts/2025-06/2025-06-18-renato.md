## Key Topics

### AI-Powered Research and Writer Style Analysis

- Company research prompt: ingests company data, runs deep research, generates customer quotes and reviews.
- Writer style analysis: identifies 30+ authors/writers/personalities with unique voices that appeal to the target audience; includes 5 quotes, style descriptions, and archetype names for each.
- Manual curation layer: Marcel scans results or uses ChatGPT to filter top 3-5 writers; may refine with more prescriptive prompts (e.g., "find ones resonating with blue-collar audiences") if initial results miss the mark.

### Tone and Voice Calibration

- Marcel's iterative approach: manually validates tone calibration using adjective mapping and sentence mood boards to understand where clients want to land on the directness spectrum.
- Example from Engine: "Blue-collar directness" (direct, no fluff, no hedge, respect for time) vs. nuanced corporate tone (organizations may benefit from examining potential inefficiencies...).
- Current process: custom CloudFlare project with examples → AI generates tone/voice guide → adjective matrix for client reaction → refinement.
- Goal: more automated, client-interactive calibration so client can self-select preferences upfront rather than iterating post-delivery.

### Content Inventory and Assignment Generation

- SEMrush-based: ingest client URLs, filter keywords for evergreen content by removing brand mentions, homepage, and irrelevant site sections.
- Keyword data includes volume, difficulty, internal/external backlinks, quality, description, title.
- Metadata tagging: topic clusters (how strategy is communicated), plus client-specific tags (group types, personas, industries for Engine; product pillars, job roles, content pillars for other clients).
- Manual editor review: editors scan and approve opportunities; Marcel estimates 500 opportunities can be reviewed in ~1 hour to select ~50 high-priority items.
- Airtable tracking: approved assignments imported with statuses (backlog, considering, approved to start, in production, reviewing, ready to publish, published, archived).

### Client Feedback Loop and Business Continuity

- Rapid case study: client business changes rapidly → CTAs, company context, writing guidelines go stale quickly → requires regular refreshes.
- Current challenge: editors don't have clear systems to know when and how to refresh artifacts; need process to flag and guide updates.
- Opportunity: build systems where calibration/preference capture happens upfront so updates can be applied systematically without re-discovering client preferences.

### Cross-Tool Integration and Future State

- Current workflow: Notion (strategy docs/artifacts) → Airtable (assignment tracking) → Atlas (client presentation).
- Tension: manually created Notion docs are high-touch but not directly consumed by internal systems; artists present these in Notion but work happens in Airtable/Atlas separately.
- Vision: Notion artifacts (tone guides, research, personas) should feed directly into GrowthX systems; clients should consume in GrowthX interface with comments and feedback, rather than separate Notion review.
