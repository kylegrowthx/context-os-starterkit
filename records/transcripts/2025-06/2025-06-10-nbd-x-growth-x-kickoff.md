## Summary

GrowthX and Noboringdesign (NBD) kicked off a comprehensive rebranding project designed to refine GrowthX's brand identity and balance human expertise with AI-first capabilities. The team aligned on five core brand pillars (bold, experimental, simple yet refined, bursting with energy, and premium), drawing design inspiration from the 70s-90s transition to digital design with emphasis on grid systems, systemic typography, and data visualization. The ~1.5-month project timeline spans moodboarding, concepting, brand identity development, and guidelines, with NBD responsible for creating Figma files, PDFs, and brand assets.

---

## Context

GrowthX engaged Noboringdesign (NBD), a design agency with team members based in Chile and internationally, to lead a rebrand project. This is a service exchange—not a traditional client engagement. Renato Valdes Olmos (GrowthX) has been working with NBD on blog positioning and has high confidence in the team's creative direction. The rebrand became necessary because while GrowthX's current brand has visual weight and gravitas, it lacks refinement and consistency—particularly in typography and the balance between human-made and AI-enabled aesthetics. As GrowthX scales both its AI-first growth marketing services and its proprietary software platform, the brand needs to mature alongside the company's ambitions.

## Key Topics

### GrowthX's Business Model and Brand Vision

  - Delivers AI-first growth marketing services at scale
  - Building proprietary AI-enabled workflow software for marketing professionals
  - Seeks brand identity reflecting both human expertise and AI capabilities
  - Core values: bold, experimental, simple, refined, bursting with energy, premium

### Current Brand Challenges

  - Logo lacks refinement despite having appropriate weight
  - Typography inconsistent and contrasting
  - Desire for more uniformity in overall brand presentation
  - Current black/white photography contrasts with tech elements, but feels superficial

### Design Direction and Inspiration

  - Draw from 70s-90s transition to digital design era
  - Incorporate grid systems, systemic design, strong typography
  - Balance human touch with confident AI expertise
  - Reduce playfulness in primary elements; consider motion for energy
  - Simplify typography, potentially moving to one primary font family
  - Data visualization and infographics important for showcasing metrics

### Project Timeline and Phases

1.  Kickoff (current meeting)
2.  Moodboarding (\~1 week)
3.  Concepting (\~2 weeks)
4.  Brand Identity Development
5.  Brand Guidelines (\~2 weeks)
6.  Final Deliverables: Figma file, PDF, necessary assets
