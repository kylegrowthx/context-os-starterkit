## Key Topics

### R\&D Credit Pitfalls and Opportunities

  - Two tax offset types: income (profit-based) and payroll (wage-based)
  - 90%+ startups use R\&D credit for payroll tax offset due to lack of profitability
  - Critical mistake: late tax filing nullifies R\&D credit's payroll tax offset benefit
  - Potential $25k+ penalties for missed foreign disclosures (e.g., foreign subsidiaries/parent companies)

### State-Level Startup Incentives

  - 30+ states offer R\&D credit versions to incentivize local R\&D work
  - Some states provide partial sales tax exemptions on R\&D equipment purchases

### Startup Tax Evolution

  - Pre-seed/seed funding triggers more complex tax planning
  - Common progression: LLC formation → Delaware C-Corp conversion (investor mandate)
  - Early-stage options: DIY (budget constraints) or existing personal CPA relationship
  - Post-funding: Often upgrade to startup-specialized tax advisors (investor/attorney recommendation)

### Multi-State Operations Challenges

  - Registration complexities: Each state has unique processes and ID number requirements
  - Minimum taxes: Many states impose fees regardless of profitability (e.g., CA: $800/year, MA: $456/year)
  - Compliance headache: Navigating varied state-specific rules, deadlines, and filing requirements

### Common Misconceptions and Surprises

  - Belief that no revenue means no filing requirement (false; entity formation typically necessitates annual filing)
  - Shock at needing to file with IRS pre-revenue

### Recent and Potential Tax Law Changes

  - R\&D expense treatment shift: From immediate write-off to capitalization (caused unexpected startup taxes)
  - Potential reversal in new tax bill: Back to immediate expensing, possibly retroactive
  - IRS adopting AI for audit targeting and execution

### Town.com's Unique Value Proposition

  - AI-enabled platform reduces grunt work in tax preparation
  - Increased advisor availability for strategic discussions and responsive communication
  - Data ingestion from various systems (e.g., QuickBooks, payroll) for streamlined preparation
  - AI-drafted returns with human review and finalization
