## Summary

## Context

GrowthX is a B2B content marketing services firm ($200k+/year engagements) with a strategic software product, CheckThat. This kick-off meeting launched a growth engagement with Augment Code, an AI coding assistant competing directly with Cursor. The engagement aims to capture market demand for Augment's superior product through strategic content, positioning, and user acquisition tactics. Augment Code recently launched a self-serve model and has demonstrated strong product differentiation, particularly for large codebases. The team — led by co-founder Molisha Shah and head of marketing Sylvain Giuliani — is seeking GrowthX's expertise to accelerate awareness and user growth.

Marcel Santilli (GrowthX CEO) and George Haikal (GrowthX team member) are leading the engagement. The relationship is new, and both teams are establishing a rapid, collaborative process for research, strategy, and content development across discovery deep dives, product positioning, and customer acquisition channels.

## Key Topics

### Current Market Position

  - Recently launched self-serve, improved product quality
  - Consistently winning against competitors (e.g., Cursor) in sales conversations
  - Superior performance on large codebases (e.g., 350,000 files) due to advanced context engine

### Customer Acquisition Strategy

  - Primarily organic growth and word-of-mouth
  - $250k/month on paid ads, mostly search (capturing intent)
  - YouTube ads and influencer partnerships showing strong results
  - Exploring top-of-funnel strategies (e.g., micro-sites, tools, newsletters)

### Product Differentiation

  - Excels at understanding large, complex codebases
  - Default 200k context window (larger than competitors)
  - Proprietary code retrieval and real-time indexing
  - Strong customer support and solutions engineering

### Growth Metrics and Goals

  - Currently at 6,000 sign-ups per day (organic)
  - Aiming for 3x growth in the near term
  - LTV estimate: $150 (conservative)
  - Willing to spend up to $200 to acquire a user

### Brand Positioning

  - Targeting software engineers who want to write code with AI
  - Avoiding association with "script kitties" or casual coders
  - Exploring lifestyle brand marketing tactics for developer audience
