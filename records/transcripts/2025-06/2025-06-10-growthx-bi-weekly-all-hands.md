## Summary

GrowthX held its bi-weekly all-hands meeting on June 10 to introduce four new hires (Ehtisham Hussain as Managing Editor, Georgemaine Lourens as Designer, Joanna Kaminska as Managing Editor, and Bianca Nieves as Go-to-Market/Growth lead), announce Dave's transition to a supporting role across pods and strategy sprints, and introduce "Audit Week" — a planned pause from publishing for healthy accounts to train teams on the new Atlas platform, optimize workflows, and conduct client listening tours. The team also celebrated five major new customer wins (Augment Code at $30k strategy sprint, Town.com, Airbyte expansion, Engine.com from a competitor, and Biologica) and reviewed a strong pipeline of late-stage deals including potential logos like Okta, Sennawan, and Outreach.

## Context

GrowthX's bi-weekly all-hands is the company's primary internal alignment meeting, held every two weeks across the entire organization. This particular meeting reflects a company in growth — four new hires in a single sprint suggests the delivery and GTM teams are expanding to handle the escalation in customer demand and strategic opportunities. The Audit Week announcement signals a deliberate infrastructure investment: transitioning off AirOps (a borrowed/external workflow system) onto the company's new internal platform, Atlas, built by the in-house build team. Concurrent with technical infrastructure changes, the company is executing a major pivot in its sales approach — from selling ongoing services to positioning strategy sprints as 8-week mutual evaluation engagements, where both GrowthX and the client decide whether to continue after the sprint. This positioning shift is reflected in customer wins like Augment Code and Engine.com, where rapid deal closure and accelerated delivery are driving momentum.
