## Summary

GrowthX and Biologica kicked off their partnership to launch a content strategy for Biologica's women's health supplement brand, designed for three hormonal life stages (reproductive years, perimenopause, postmenopause). The team agreed to produce 75-100 high-quality educational content pieces by September 24th launch, focusing on credibility and education over pure SEO, with a September 24th website launch deadline. Key deliverables include setting up a medical review process with Biologica's OB/GYN advisory board, developing a comprehensive hormonal health hub, and leveraging consumer research interviews to inform content angles on how supplements address the confusion women face in understanding hormonal health and supplementation.

---

## Context

Biologica is a newly founded women's health supplement company founded by Joey Rodgers (formerly CEO of Allbirds) and Brian Magida (fourth employee at Warby Parker). The company developed its core product through a year of consumer research and nine months of product development, resulting in a hormone-stage-specific supplement drink mix available in sachets. The team identified a major gap in the market: women face fragmented, confusing information about supplements tailored to their hormonal stages, and no major supplement brand has built around this biological segmentation.

GrowthX is engaging as the content strategy and production partner. Marcel Santilli (CEO/co-founder of GrowthX), Aida Knezevic (Senior Managing Editor), and Kyle Gaudreau (VP of Customer Growth) are leading the engagement. The partnership aims to build credibility and educational authority ahead of the September 24th website and product launch, with the team expecting to begin uploading content to the CMS in late August.

## Key Topics

### Product Overview

  - Supplement drink mix in sachets, 30 per tin
  - Formulated for 3 hormonal stages: reproductive years (18-40s), perimenopause, postmenopause
  - Contains 2-5 branded, clinically-researched ingredients per formula
  - Focuses on key issues like sleep, anxiety, nutrient needs for each stage
  - Positioned as a "co-pilot" for hormonal health, not fixing something "wrong"

### Brand Positioning

  - Science-forward, focusing on clinically-proven ingredients
  - Simplifying women's health information and supplementation
  - Transparency on ingredient sourcing and quality
  - Balancing multivitamin approach with targeted health outcomes
  - Not trying to be the "shiny new ingredient" but reliable, researched support

### Content Strategy

  - Build a comprehensive knowledge hub on women's hormonal health
  - Focus on educating about hormonal stages and related health needs
  - Highlight clinical research behind ingredients and formulations
  - Address misconceptions about supplement regulation and efficacy
  - Create content for 3 main personas aligned with hormonal stages

### Medical Credibility

  - 3 OB/GYNs on medical advisory board for content creation/review
  - VP of Innovation is a naturopathic doctor, available for content
  - Need streamlined process for medical review of content

### Competitor Landscape

  - Key competitors: Bonafide (indication-focused), O Positive (PMS support)
  - Parallel (fertility-focused) doing okay in the space
  - Most women getting information from podcasts and Instagram influencers

### Consumer Research

  - Conducted video interviews with target consumers
  - AI transcripts and summaries available, full videos can be shared
  - Key insight: Women find conflicting, confusing supplement information
