## Summary

Marcel led the GrowthX delivery leadership team through a major pod rebalancing initiative to address gaps in account management, technical expertise, and delivery quality that emerged after a period of rapid hiring and account onboarding. The team discussed a 4-week transition plan (prep week, quiet week, in-office client transition meetings, full implementation) and outlined a new reporting structure where managing directors report to Marcel for strategic guidance. The team also agreed to develop a systematic approach to tracking account health across relationship, results, execution quality, and project management dimensions.

---

## Context

GrowthX is in the middle of a major operational restructuring of its delivery organization. Over the past six months, the company hired four managing directors (Jakub Rudnik, Darrell Etherington, Megan Dickey, and others) to take on client accounts previously managed by Kyle and Marcel. Simultaneously, four new managing experts (Ada, Vivek, Mariana, and Jess) were hired to support delivery. However, the onboarding and account distribution didn't go smoothly—new directors were given full account responsibility with new MEs and CMs all learning simultaneously, leading to inconsistent execution quality, poor account health visibility, and varying delivery standards across the book of business. This meeting was called to implement a rebalancing: moving accounts to optimize for director-ME-CM strength alignment, addressing known issues like content quality and relationship management, and establishing better systems for account health tracking going forward.

## Key Topics

### Pod Rebalancing Plan

- 4-week transition plan outlined:
  - Week 1 (current): Prep and document account statuses (POCs, deliverables, backlog, contract items not started)
  - Week 2: Quiet week positioned as "audit week" internally and to clients; team notified of transitions
  - Week 3: In-office transition meetings with clients (all directors in office June 23)
  - Week 4: New pod structure fully operational
- Goal for directors: manage $1.5-2M book of business ($125-167K MRR) at steady state
- Adjustments made to maintain some continuity where possible (e.g., Jakub keeping certain MEs)
- Consideration given to technical expertise when assigning accounts (e.g., Darrell's comfort with developer-focused clients)
- Special handling for accounts transitioning under Dave Capone (moving to strategy sprint sooner) and Ada's accounts (needing ramp time)

### Rationale and Context for Rebalancing

- Recent rapid growth (signed many new customers Dec-Apr) led to 2 kickoffs per week, new CM layer only initially, then Kyle + Jimmy as directors
- Hired 4 MEs (Ada, Vivek, Mariana, Jess) in December; hired 4 directors (Jakub, Darrell, Megan, and others) starting in 2025
- Challenges: inconsistent account strategy (spectrum from "complete garbage" to "okay"), unstandardized onboarding, new directors with new MEs and new CMs all learning simultaneously
- Visibility gaps: unclear which accounts were in trouble, inconsistent reporting, poor use of systems (Airtable, workflows, grids)
- Content quality audit revealed 80% of output unacceptable; production quality and CM relationship need restructuring

### Organizational Changes

- Strategy sprint remains centralized (Marcel with George and Ada initially; adding more over time) due to complexity; not easy to delegate immediately
- Production/content quality layer (led by Matthew Panzarino) will have more centralized oversight and training/enablement while CMs remain embedded in pods for relationship continuity
- Delivery/client management: Marcel stepping in to be more hands-on initially to set direction and build relationships, then stepping back over time as directors mature
- MDs now report to Marcel (reporting change) to enable strategic oversight of pod structure and implementation

### Example: Vappy Account Turnaround

- One of the most complex accounts, previously unclear, now highly aligned
- Issue: unclear what client wanted; multiple work streams not clearly scoped; updates mixed
- Solution: within 6 hours of Marcel's time (3 meetings), reset expectations, tweaked strategy toward more aggressive volume with less review (aligned to CEO's preference to move fast and learn aggressively)
- Result: clear weekly plan, strong signals, customer very happy, relationship on text-message basis with CEO

### Quiet Week Planning

- Position as "audit week" to clients
- Activities: strategy audits, site audits, competitive analysis for accounts that go quiet
- Flag accounts that need continued publishing support and come up with tight strategy for how to handle them
- Use time for new pod preparation, transition planning, testing systems, potentially building content backlog

### Account Health Tracking System

- Proposed 4 dimensions to track on 1-5 or 1-10 scale:
  1. Relationship strength
  2. Account growth and results
  3. Quality of deliverables (execution)
  4. Project/account management
- Focus on tying activities to client's desired outcomes, not just meeting contractual obligations
- Will help surface early warning signals and inform account prioritization

### Communication and Stakeholder Management

- Some stakeholders (Etisham and Joe) already found out about reporting changes through Notion; guidance to announce changes only when fully decided
- When changes are announced: directors will set up one-on-ones with new reports
- Jakub flagged concern: he's losing all 15 account relationships through this rebalance; Marcel acknowledged relationship continuity is critical and will consider stable accounts for matching moves
- In-office meetings on June 23 will formalize transitions and reinforce trust/delivery commitments to clients
