## Summary

GrowthX's strategy sprint standup for six active client projects (GetPod, Engine, Augman, Biologica, Hypergirl, and Town) focused on delivery clarity and client momentum. Marcel outlined a new artifact-based onboarding process using calibration guides and persona research, which significantly improved team alignment on deliverables — especially for Engine. The core issue across all projects: maintaining responsiveness and scheduled touchpoints after kickoff. The team agreed to a standard model of one hour post-kickoff sync plus weekly 30-minute checkpoints and async updates to keep clients engaged through the six-week sales cycle.

## Context

GrowthX is in the middle of multiple strategy sprint engagements where the team (Marcel, Aida, Joanna, Bridget, and Marisol) helps clients define content strategy, build artifact libraries (persona guides, content clusters, calibration examples), and execute initial content. This standup reviewed progress across six simultaneous clients at varying stages: GetPod and Engine nearing strategy alignment, Augman and Biologica in setup phases, and Hypergirl and Town waiting for client inputs. The team's main challenge is maintaining momentum between meetings — previously Marcel was recording Looms to fill gaps, but the new structured template process aims to provide more visible progress. Marcel demonstrated the workflow (using Perplexity, Claude, and Atlas to create personas, writing guidelines, and calibration examples in 20 minutes or less), which helped Aida and Joanna understand what polished deliverables should look like.

## Relevance

**To GrowthX Delivery:**
- Standardized post-kickoff timeline now critical: 1-hour sync within a week of kickoff, then weekly 30-min checkpoints plus async updates. Prevents perception of lost momentum and keeps clients engaged through six-week sales cycle.
- New artifact creation workflow (persona research → writing guidelines → calibration guide with before/after examples) is working: takes ~20 minutes for baseline, scales better than Loom approach, provides tangible progress evidence.
- Calibration process needs visual comparison (original article vs. refreshed version with explanation) rather than lengthy documents — clients can spot-check quality without reading 5,000 words.
- Engine project shows clear quality bar: client's recent "hotels vs. motels" refresh is shallow (poor cross-linking, few references), creating opportunity to demonstrate value through deep, well-researched rewrites.

**To CheckThat:**
- Biologica project includes AI/ML component: needs knowledge base built for retrieval, likely using embeddings and CheckThat-adjacent AI visibility concepts. Medical oversight process suggests prompt engineering for fact-checking and claim extraction.
- Hypergirl project is newsletter/publication angle that could showcase thought leadership content repurposing (interview transcripts → newsletter → social).

**To GrowthX Business Development:**
- GetPod: complex multi-product transition (new brand, new company, new website, new product launch) — client expectations likely misaligned (thinks 3 months, realistic timeline 1.5+ years based on DataGrid precedent). Renewal risk if strategy looks unachievable.
- Engine: high-trust opportunity — flagging broken URL and suggesting quality improvements shows proactive partnership beyond content creation. Could be reference account.
- Biologica: founder-led, well-resourced, willing to co-invest in technical infrastructure (knowledge base, medical oversight). High-profile name and health-tech angle make it strong reference candidate post-launch. Requires executive research work (Sadira assigned for competitor deep-dives).
- Town: kicked off Friday (off-cycle), catching up this week. John (client lead) is time-constrained — async-first approach with one full hour/week is right model.
