## Key Topics

### Harmony Partners Investment Strategy

  - VC fund based in New York, investing since 2010 (currently on Fund 6); Clemens joined at the end of Fund 2 (~10 years as partner)
  - Focus: Early expansion rounds (Series B) in B2B software and tech-enabled businesses
  - Check sizes: $10–20M; lead rounds of $15–40M
  - Philosophy: Flexible round construction — check size doesn't dictate raise amount; founders optimize for budget + buffer
  - Governance: No mandatory board seats. Ownership % determines engagement (15%+ = board seat; ~5% = information rights; in-between = observer)
  - Portfolio: Drift, Iterable, Klaviyo, Offsite (AI-enabled marketing, acquired, first AI investment), Contently (content marketing platform, failed — learned software wasn't the model)

### GrowthX Platform Overview

  - **Core:** AI-powered workflow engine built on code-based infrastructure (not low-code/no-code) with agentic orchestration and human-in-the-loop task management
  - **Why code matters:** Low-code tools create abstraction on top of code; GrowthX went directly to code after nearly 2M ARR on custom workflows broke. Co-founder experience building IFTTT (distributed infrastructure, connectors) informed the architecture.
  - **Key capabilities:**
      - Automated content generation with fact-checking (verifies every passage for accuracy)
      - Deep research workflows (scraping, API calls, filtering, vector DB retrieval)
      - Research brief generation and content optimization
      - Image generation (article cover ideation, selection, search)
      - Starter kit templates (one-shot app/workflow generation)
      - Data analysis and user behavior categorization (e.g., Bolt session analysis)
  - **Execution model:** Four-file system (helpers, prompts, types, workflows) + README; AI coding agent translates plans into executable code; workflow runtime handles hundreds of thousands of runs/week
  - **Monetization path:** Workflows → API endpoints → fine-tuning/distillation opportunities → task-specific models. CheckThat.ai (fact-checking) is first public API.
  - **Use cases:** SEO content, brand building, product launches, e-commerce catalog enrichment, user research categorization

### GrowthX Business Model and Traction

  - **Revenue model:** Primarily content services (strategy sprints + ongoing execution); secondary: CheckThat.ai API; tertiary: e-commerce catalog enrichment
  - **Client wins (recent):** Hadtown.com (SMB tax content pre-season), Allbirds founder's new health venture (stealth), Engine.com (1000+ legacy pages, new product lanes for wedding travel/off-site)
  - **Execution:** 4–8 week strategy sprints with elite team to define strategy, set up internal artifacts/instance, align on brand voice/tone/style, establish KPIs
  - **Capital efficiency:** Maintains 1:1 headcount costs to revenue ratio; breakeven this month (June 2025), burned some in May
  - **Traction:** $2M+ ARR, recently breakeven; 7 product lanes with principal engineers assigned
  - **Fundraising readiness:** No institutional capital until Feb 2025; planning Aug/Sep fundraise for scaling product/ops

### Future Plans and Fundraising

  - **Use of capital:** Hire principal engineers; double down on 7 high-confidence product lanes (not exploratory, trade-offs are currently painful)
  - **Expansion:** E-commerce catalog enrichment (e.g., RealReal partnership for massive enrichment project); leverage partnerships like RealReal for new revenue streams
