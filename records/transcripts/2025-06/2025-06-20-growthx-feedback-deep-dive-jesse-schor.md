## Key Topics

### WebStacks Business Evolution

  - Focused on pitching upmarket clients since August
  - Launched new website at start of year
  - Expanded brand positioning for enterprise and higher mid-market accounts
  - Introduced WebSnacks for small mid-market companies
  - Integrated web strategy as a service offering
  - Emphasized thought leadership in the composable web space

### Content Strategy Transformation

  - Initial 3 months: Surface-level, AI-driven content based on high-volume keywords
  - Shift in strategy: Incorporating WebStacks' unique perspective and expertise
  - Key improvements:
      - Adding complementary resources (Ada, Kyle, Jacob)
      - Utilizing Gong call recordings and team insights
      - Focusing on both high-volume and high-intent keywords
  - Results: Improved content quality and relevance, leading to better performance

### Performance Measurement Evolution

  - Initial focus: Impressions and eyeballs on content
  - Progression:
    1.  Overall impressions
    2.  Engagement (time on page, click-through rates)
    3.  Conversions (click-throughs to solution pages, case studies, capabilities pages)
    4.  Pipeline generation
  - Grouped blogs into cohorts based on goals
  - 4-week evaluation period for content performance

### AI Impact on WebStacks

  - Significant increase in traffic and conversions from AI sources
  - AI transparency in execution and client work differentiates WebStacks
  - Leveraging AI in internal workflows and client projects
  - Exploring programmatic content generation for long-tail topics

### Future Collaboration Opportunities

  - Potential for creating a website library/directory similar to Landbook
  - Exploring AI-driven content generation and classification systems
  - Possibility of building affiliate sites and tools
