## Key Topics

### Daniël's Exit from Ghost

Daniël worked at Ghost from late 2023 to mid-2025 as a design engineer—hands-on design with code. He built significant features and worked on brand identity, enjoying the calm, small-team environment and open-source ethos. However, the design ambition level was substantially lower than he wanted. The CEO had opinions on product design but lacked understanding of brand design strategy. When Daniël and a recently-hired head of design proposed expanding Ghost's marketing reach beyond the "indie hackers" community—targeting news organizations, journalists, and publications—the company lacked appetite for that vision. The design team was small (3 good designers) but lacked the quality bar and ambition Daniël expected. He decided to leave a month before this meeting.

### Daniël's Career Criteria

Daniël is exploring, not locked into a specific path. He's conducted 6-7 prospect conversations after posting to his network. Key preferences: (1) balance between hands-on craft and strategic thinking—not a pure manager role, but more than pure IC; (2) team that deeply understands and values design, reducing friction to explain why work matters; (3) work-life balance to maintain side projects (magazine, editorial work, writing); (4) remote-first with Europe-based team preferred (concerns about US time zone overlap on deep work); (5) €130k+ compensation; (6) timeline: ideally find something this month to start after August vacation. Currently interviewing American studios and New York Times simultaneously—editorial design and journalism appeal to him as much as product/brand design.
