## Summary

Marcel Santilli shared his experience building Transform/TransformX conferences at Scale AI, advising Nancy Tran on Vapicon strategy and positioning Vapi for enterprise adoption. Key decisions: shift marketing focus to enterprise customers (treating self-serve community as an innovation engine), implement a multi-pronged outreach approach combining relationship-building dinners, FOMO-driven campaigns showcasing enterprise logos, and selective high-ROI conference presence (~$100k budget for 3-4 annual conferences like Contact Center Week). ## Context

Vapi is a voice AI platform that had previously pursued a dual-market strategy targeting both self-serve developers and enterprise customers. Nancy Tran, a marketing lead at Vapi, reached out to Marcel Santilli for strategic guidance on how to position Vapi for enterprise growth. Vapi's go-to-market team had observed that self-serve messaging (developer-friendly, community-driven) was actually deterring enterprise prospects, so the company committed to clarifying enterprise as their primary revenue driver. Marcel, who led high-profile virtual conference initiatives at Scale AI (Transform/TransformX with 10,000-35,000 attendees) and managed enterprise marketing at DeepGram, brought direct experience in building enterprise brand awareness and implementing effective conference strategies. The meeting was positioned as a deep-dive strategy session to align Vapi's entire marketing and operations approach around enterprise acquisition, not just Vapicon conference planning in isolation.

## Overview

- Shift go-to-market messaging and positioning entirely to enterprise (treat self-serve community as innovation engine, not separate revenue pillar)
- Implement three-pronged enterprise engagement strategy: (1) relationship-building via VIP dinners, (2) FOMO-generation through enterprise logos and use cases, (3) problem-clarity via case studies and conference proof-points
- Retarget existing programs (happy hours, email campaigns) specifically at enterprise employees (engineers/PMs at Airbnb, Bank of America, Google, Hertz, etc.) instead of startup developers
- Fix website UX for enterprise buyers: add clear "Schedule Demo" CTA, prominently showcase enterprise customer logos, create dedicated enterprise-focused pages and personas
- Implement signal-based warm outbound using tools like Common Room or Koala to trigger personalized outreach when high-intent companies visit website
- Attend 3-4 major enterprise-focused conferences annually (Contact Center Week recommended) with significant investment (~$100k per conference) for booth presence and VIP dinners
- Run Vapicon (October 2, SF, in-person) as a "podcast season launch" — weekly promotions, speaker recruitment, email database leverage to reach 500 attendees and create PR buzz

## Key Topics

### Enterprise Marketing Strategy

  - Prioritize enterprise-focused messaging and content
  - Create relationship-building opportunities (e.g., dinners, events)
  - Generate FOMO through showcasing enterprise logos and use cases
  - Demonstrate problem-solving capabilities with case studies and customer stories
  - Leverage self-serve community innovations to appeal to enterprises

### Website and Content Optimization

  - Make enterprise focus more prominent on the homepage
  - Add clear path for enterprise visitors (e.g., "Schedule a Demo" button)
  - Showcase enterprise customer logos and use cases
  - Create dedicated enterprise-focused pages and materials

### Lead Generation and Outreach

  - Implement tools like Common Room or Koala to track high-intent website visitors and usage patterns
  - Use trigger-based, warm outbound outreach based on website activity (similar to scale at HashiCorp: "three people from Bank of America downloaded Terraform")
  - Monitor product usage data and free tier sign-ups from enterprise domains (e.g., employees from Google, Airbnb, Twilio signing up)
  - Target engineers and decision-makers specifically from enterprise companies for happy hours and exclusive dinners
  - Create separate enterprise-focused email list within Customer.io for targeted promotions (distinct from general developer newsletter)

### Conference Strategy

  - Attend 3-4 major enterprise-focused conferences annually (e.g., Contact Center Week)
  - Plan for significant investment (\~$100k) for effective presence at top conferences
  - Host AI-focused happy hours to attract enterprise attendees and create buzz
  - Organize exclusive dinners for VP-level and above prospects

### Vapicon Conference Planning

  - Set for October 2nd with a goal of 500 attendees (currently at 150)
  - Treat conference promotion like launching a podcast season
  - Leverage existing email database for promotion
  - Consider creating a separate enterprise-focused email list for targeted communications
