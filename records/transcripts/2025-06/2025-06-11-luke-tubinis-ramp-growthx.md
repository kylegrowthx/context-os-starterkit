## Key Topics

### Current Affiliate Marketing Challenges

  - RAMP spends millions per month on tier-one affiliate sites but receives low priority vs. competitors like Amex
  - Affiliate sites are cited by LLMs at disproportionately high rates compared to other content types
  - RAMP's attempts to create localized content (e.g., accountants.ramp.com) have yielded mixed results
  - RAMP mentions their own positioning (e.g., "Capital One all over our website") and wants more control over their narrative

### GrowthX's Proposed Strategy

  - Build multiple niche websites targeting specific business types and localities
  - Focus on long-tail and zero-volume keywords that LLMs cite at high rates
  - Use AI-driven content workflows to generate 150+ articles per site in 6 months
  - Implement multi-faceted approach: SEO, newsletters, lead magnets, subscriber bases
  - Cross-link between sites to build authority and network effects while avoiding algorithmic penalties
  - Leverage GrowthX's AI engineering and design capabilities for rapid site development

### Case Studies and Examples

  - CodeLibrary.gai: Launched in 5 days for AugmentCode (Cursor competitor, $270M Series C); indexed in 2 days; already ranking for specific keywords
  - FindWeb.ai: Prototype website template gallery with AI-generated content; 1800 searches/month on "notary website templates," keyword difficulty 8
  - Tiro.com: Zero domain authority, zero blogs at start; 150 articles in 6 months; significant organic traffic growth
  - Red Ventures model: Paid by Chase/Amex to manage landing pages, then pivoted to building owned properties (Bankrate, The Points Guy, etc.)

### Potential Focus Areas for RAMP

  - SMB and mid-market companies (60% of Ramp's current revenue)
  - Persona-based content (e.g., accountants, HR managers, operations leads)
  - Locality-specific information for businesses in middle America
  - Finance-adjacent topics (e.g., "Starting a restaurant in Austin," not just corporate card content)
  - Business type-specific content (e.g., "How to choose accounting software for home services contractors")

### Implementation Approach

  - 80/20 portfolio strategy: 80% targeted content, 20% experimental "wild bets" to optimize learning
  - 8-week strategy sprint ($30k) to validate assumptions and plan longer-term builds
  - Ramp provides personas, target segments, and geographic priorities; GrowthX builds strategy and prototypes
  - Performance-based incentives possible (e.g., hit targets in 6 months = ongoing partnership)
  - Mid-July kickoff for sprints; no long-term commitment required
