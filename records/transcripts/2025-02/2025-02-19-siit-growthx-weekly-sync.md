## Key Topics

### Content Quality Validation

Carmel reviewed the latest content batch and confirmed significant improvements. The tone is now "more casual" and "less AI-generated," and—most critically—technical accuracy has improved dramatically, cutting editing time significantly. Carmel explicitly praised today's batch as "the best ones yet." This validation came from Becerra's focused work improving content development processes.

### Strategic Pivot to FinTech/Finance

Jess proposed pivoting from the previously approved healthcare vertical to FinTech and Finance, where keyword research showed stronger opportunity. Carmel approved the shift. Jess has updated the Airtable with new FinTech/Finance topics and offered to provide batches of 10-15 for review, but Carmel committed to reviewing the full updated table. The team also leaned into manufacturing topics (based on complementary keyword data) and employee experience, particularly onboarding. Carmel emphasized keeping onboarding/offboarding focused on automation and streamlining, not how-to guides.

### Pillar + Supporting Article Structure

Jess introduced a new content structure: pillar articles (longer, broad, foundational) with multiple supporting articles underneath, all heavily cross-linked. This structure will be replicated across verticals (e.g., FinTech pillar with supporting articles, healthcare pillar with supporting articles). For employee experience, "onboarding" became the pillar, with "offboarding" and automated workflow articles as supporting content.

### Blog Design Simplification

Kyle and Jess identified that producing hero images was the bottleneck slowing content publication. Kyle proposed simplifying to logo + title for the blog post hero image. This would automatically become the OG image (via HTML metadata reference), eliminating the need for separate OG image design. Carmel's designer will create the new blog template with this simplified format. The team discussed whether to add vertical-specific icons (e.g., one icon per vertical), but agreed that's a future enhancement—logo and title should be sufficient initially.

### CTA Testing

Jess and Kyle outlined a test plan: of the next 10 articles, 5 will have a mid-article CTA (requesting demo/free trial) and 5 will keep the bottom CTA. Goal is to test whether earlier CTA placement improves click-through rates and conversions.

### Production Timeline

Six articles are currently in production with placeholder images. These will launch "ASAP," with designers pulling final imagery by end of day or end of the following day. Previously refreshed articles are already live. Once articles ship, Jess will send Carmel the final images for approval before going live (as a safeguard). On Friday, the team will select 10-15 next-batch topics from the approved Airtable for the following sprint.

### Performance Expectations and Tracking

Kyle explained the typical organic growth curve: early impressions in Search Console, clicks starting to compound in weeks 2-3, with the first major traffic spike usually arriving around month 3. These patterns vary by account and keyword difficulty. The team will track this data in Google Search Console and will include weekly performance updates in the meeting agenda and written reports so Carmel doesn't have to hunt for it.

### Customer Story Backlinks

Carmel has written three customer stories for FinTech companies and is waiting for the customer stories page to launch. Jess confirmed that once live, backlinks from the new FinTech content to these stories will be valuable—tying real-world examples to educational content. Jess will maintain an ongoing list of story links and ensure new content references them appropriately.

### Content Refresh: "Build and Run in IT Operations"

Carmel requested that a refresh of "What is build and run in revenue operations" be adapted to "What is build and run in IT operations" for the next refresh batch. She noted that "build and run" is an established concept in IT/infrastructure language and wants to test it in that domain. The original article was one of Siit's better-performing pieces, suggesting strong potential in IT operations.
