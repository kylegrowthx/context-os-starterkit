## Key Topics

### fwd.digital's AI-Powered Content Platform

Marcel explained that fwd.digital built their platform in response to feedback from programmatic SEO workshops: clients wanted outcomes, not just tools. The platform combines AI workflows with expert-in-loop editorial processes. Currently publishing 20 million words/month across 35 clients using a custom framework built on a Temporal runtime layer (not LangChain). The system breaks content generation into discrete components: research workflow (generates research questions, parallel processes searches across multiple sources, filters by relevance), content drafting, optimization, fact-checking, and cross-linking. Everything is built as APIs, allowing editors and clients to interact through workflows rather than complicated UIs. The researcher step alone costs about $2 in compute per article, not counting editorial review, but fwd.digital runs this aggressively to ensure quality.

### Statsig's Current Content Generation Efforts

Skye built their in-house AI system during holidays as a side project. V0 was basic (memory store, Google searches, AI-generated articles) but worked—producing 5x organic traffic growth. More importantly, 40-50% of live demo sign-ups now trace back to these AI-generated blog posts as first touch. Recently rebuilt the entire workflow for better output quality (previously had issues like repetitive phrases, poor links). Now the system generates multiple blog posts, with a content person handling thoughtful title strategy and keyword planning. Volume is high: producing a couple hundred blog articles per week. Main bottleneck now is improving content strategy and expanding beyond blog-only approaches.

### Potential Areas for Improvement at Statsig

Skye identified three gaps: (1) Content strategy—currently self-rated as C-level, too focused on competitive keywords and output keywords, not user-journey focused. (2) Programmatic SEO—they've tried things but haven't implemented a full programmatic play despite clear opportunity in the testing/experimentation space. (3) Content diversification and images—current system only generates blog posts for SEO; haven't integrated images or expanded to other content types. Skye also mentioned killing a ton of low-value pages recently and has room for both aggressive tutorial content and programmatic expansion given their existing velocity.

### fwd.digital's Pricing and Engagement Model

Pricing is transparent and volume-based, not discriminatory. Most clients start with two streams (24,000 words/month and 1,200 pages/year) but can scale to 100,000 words/month if they have content already. Pages are priced per year (not per month) to allow flexibility: companies can choose to create net-new pages or enrich existing ones. Additional services include link building (though Statsig likely doesn't need this given their domain authority). The human editorial review step is the main cost/capacity constraint, not compute—fwd.digital is willing to be flexible on caps if the client is comfortable with volume, but can't go unlimited without more editors. Marcel emphasized they're flexible on pricing because they want to work with Statsig and because their investors back the relationship.

### Addressing Skye's Pricing Concerns

Skye's main concern: pricing tiers shouldn't create surprise bills (e.g., a month where volume spiked costs from $0 to $40k-$200k). Marcel acknowledged this and offered to make the cap "way more flexible." He explained that most cost fear comes from people thinking compute is the bottleneck, but it's actually human review. Skye noted their internal compute costs are about $1 per blog article; fwd.digital's researcher step alone is ~$2 in compute, but they don't worry about going over-budget on compute because the real value is in editorial quality. Marcel indicated they've worked with clients at high volume from day one because of low domain authority and have flexibility models available.

### Next Steps and Timeline

Marcel will send Skye a sample Statement of Work (SOW) and Slack invite for easy interaction. He also flagged that fwd.digital is booking dates with March 10 available. The timeline constraint is both Skye's Series B fundraise (in the middle of it now) and scheduling—fwd.digital has 54 deals in their pipeline. Skye will present the proposal to Statsig's CEO and commit to a decision by end of week.
