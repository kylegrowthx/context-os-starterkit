## Key Topics

### SEO Performance Update

Andrej shared the performance dashboard showing 34 articles published with 33 indexed. The campaign is showing early positive momentum: 70 total clicks with a significant increase in impressions compared to the previous week. Andrej is manually submitting new articles to Google Search Console to accelerate indexing rather than waiting for Googlebot to discover them naturally. Jessica requested an updated performance dashboard with a new "best-in keywords" column to track keyword rankings by article and total keyword coverage (team is tracking ~300-400 keywords ranking currently).

### Content Production Pipeline

11 new articles are publishing today (6-7 already uploaded, last 5 being finalized), with 8 more articles in the Airtable queue ready for review. The January employee texting cluster generated 10 complete articles pending Andrej's final review before moving to the published folder. The February priority is the employee engagement strategies and metrics cluster, which aligns with the broader strategy to target medium-funnel keywords that will strengthen topical authority and help the entire content hub rank higher.

### Publishing Process Improvements and Quality Control

Andrej and Sadira (Sadira is referred to as "Deera" in the call) are taking more hands-on control of the publishing process. They will be the gatekeepers to ensure all elements (meta descriptions, titles, CTAs, imagery) are correct before articles go live. This tightens quality control to prevent post-publication errors being flagged on Slack. Kyle mentioned something about "editing a button" which turned out to be about the gatekeeper approach.

### Conversion Rate Optimization (CRO) Strategy

The team discussed moving beyond the existing text-only CTA at the bottom of articles to implement visual CTA banners placed strategically throughout the content. Andrej recommended 2-3 CTAs per article, visually distinct from the body content so they "pop." The strategy is to use reusable CTA templates rather than customizing each one per article. Jessica will ask the design team to create CTA banner images that can be inserted via the existing image blocks without requiring code changes. Once the design team has bandwidth, these can be hard-coded for more flexibility. Jessica expressed concern about white paper CTAs not converting (low email collection) and asked whether demo/sales CTAs might perform better, though Andrej noted that similar clients successfully use both email-gated content and direct conversion CTAs depending on the article topic.

### Content Refresh Best Practices

Jessica highlighted that previously-published, best-performing articles should be refreshed and republished. Best practice includes: updating the article publication date to signal freshness to both users and search engines, adding CTA banners, and potentially including custom infographics. Andrej recommended custom infographics that summarize key points, as they help with both Google Image search visibility and user experience. The team agreed to prioritize refreshing the best-performing articles first, but acknowledged they can work on new articles in parallel. Andrej will review the refreshed content and suggest image/infographic opportunities.
