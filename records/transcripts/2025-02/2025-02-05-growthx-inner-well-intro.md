## Summary

Marcel Santilli and Jimmy Pal from GrowthX presented the company's AI-powered content operations platform to Andrew Berman and Anthony Mangia from Innerwell, a multi-state psychiatry and telehealth company doing $8M+ in revenue. GrowthX outlined a comprehensive approach combining custom-built AI workflows, human editors, and global workforce augmentation to scale content creation from 50,000-100,000 words/month (editorial) to programmatic content at higher volumes, with pricing starting at $9,000/month and $12,000/month respectively. The teams agreed to a 90-day pilot period followed by a 12-month commitment, with potential start date of March 3rd pending contract negotiation and volume targets from Innerwell.

---

## Context

Innerwell is a multi-state virtual psychiatry and mental health services company generating $8M+ in annual revenue with strong capital efficiency and a mission to grow significantly in 2025. The company currently relies on 90% paid acquisition (largely Google Ads) and operates 1,200+ state-level service pages built in-house using Webflow. Andrew Berman, co-founder and Chief Product Officer, is a multi-time founder with deep expertise in SEO from his time at Zapier (where he was Director of AI) and prior companies. Anthony Mangia is co-founder and the team is actively looking to diversify customer acquisition and expand organic reach without building the full content operation in-house.

GrowthX is Marcel Santilli's latest venture, distilled from 15+ years of content operations and SEO strategy work across IBM, HP, DeveloperIntelligence.com, TechBeacon (1,000+ contributors, 1M+ monthly visitors generating $800k/month in attributed traffic value), Hashi Corp, Service Titan, and DeepGram. The company has built proprietary code-based AI workflows that generate editorial research, outlines, drafts, and quality assurance in parallel, significantly reducing turnaround time while maintaining brand voice and quality standards. Jimmy Pal, a GrowthX team member, presented case studies including major engagements with Homebase (real estate/ADU content), Ramp (expense categorization), and Him (healthcare drug comparisons at scale with domain expert review).
