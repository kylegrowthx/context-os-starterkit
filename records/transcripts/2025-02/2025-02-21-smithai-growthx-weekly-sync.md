### Midjourney Image Generation Strategy

- SmithAI exploring Midjourney as interim image solution while waiting for Onbox to establish style system
- Brand consistency is a constraint: SmithAI's design-focused co-founder requires images that feel on-brand (not generic AI-generated)
- Approach: use custom icons and brand elements as "wrapping" around Midjourney-generated images to increase brand fit
- Minimal test first: Kyle to generate a few Midjourney examples for Maddy's review to see if team is "in the ballpark"
- Square (1:1) image format is mandatory for social sharing versatility (especially Facebook)
- Preference for image files over HTML callouts/boxes for social shareability

### Content Submission and Workflow Standards

- All article drafts must include at top: primary keyword, search volume, top 3 adjacent keywords, and brief summary of SmithAI's angle
- This contextual header helps Maddy provide efficient feedback and reduces her need to reverse-engineer each article's focus
- Acronyms must be defined on first use (e.g., "small business (SMB)") to ensure clarity for readers
- Use "gen AI" alongside "generative AI" for natural language variation
- Subheadings (H3s) should state the solution/benefit, not just be directional headings (e.g., "Customer Service Enhancements" is better than "Customer Service")
- Articles need graphic/header breaks between H2 sections to break up visual density
- Include embedded real-world examples (screenshots, links to other companies' implementations) in applications sections, not just theory

### Flagship Content Requirements (e.g., AI Articles)

- Flagship articles (CEO/co-founder shareable on LinkedIn) need significantly more depth, examples, and visual richness than standard SEO content
- Include comparison tables, diagrams, and actionable examples (screenshots of tools like Scribe AI, Ordinal, IBM Watson usage)
- Stronger actionable content: show "how-to" examples and concrete business outcomes
- Every application section should reference specific companies and tools, not just generic benefits
- Articles should end with clear CTAs: link to contact form, booking page, or AI Receptionist-specific booking page

### Product Naming Update

- "Voice Assistant" is officially changing to "AI Receptionist"
- Update all article workflows to use "AI receptionist" terminology going forward
- There is a specific product booking page for AI Receptionist that should be linked in relevant content
