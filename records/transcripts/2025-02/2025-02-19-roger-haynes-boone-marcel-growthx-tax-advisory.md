## Summary

Marcel and Roger discussed a critical tax structuring problem that emerged during GrowthX's Series A close: the co-founder received options instead of founder stock when the company transitioned from LLC to C-corp, exposing him to significant ordinary income taxes (37% vs 0% QSBS) on option exercise. Roger proposed replacing the options with shares purchased via a partially-recourse promissory note with annual forgiveness matched to vesting, allowing the co-founder to lock in current value and potentially qualify for QSBS treatment. GrowthX is closing a $12M Series A at $80M post from Andreessen Horowitz, with pressure to close fast complicating the restructuring effort.

---

## Context

Marcel founded GrowthX as an AI workflow services company in late 2023. The company grew from zero to $5.3M ARR in approximately 15 months by building AI-powered services for B2B clients. In September 2024, Marcel brought on a CTO as co-founder, initially structured as a contractor to the underlying LLC. As growth accelerated and Marcel prepared to scale with external capital, he worked with counsel to form a C-corp and transfer LLC assets — a standard move for startups preparing for Series A fundraising.

However, the structuring was suboptimal. Instead of having the co-founder form the C-corp first and take founder stock at par value (which would have qualified both founders' equity for Qualified Small Business Stock tax treatment), the lawyer structured it so Marcel contributed all LLC assets in exchange for shares, while the co-founder received only options. This creates a significant tax asymmetry: Marcel's shares will receive QSBS treatment if held five years (0% tax on gains above basis), but the co-founder's option exercise will trigger ordinary income taxation at ~37% California combined rate. The Company is raising Series A capital from Andreessen Horowitz with a closing targeted for late February 2025, adding urgency to resolve founder equity issues.

## Relevance

**To GrowthX Business / Founder Alignment:**
- Critical tax asymmetry created an unintended 37-percentage-point disadvantage for the co-founder, creating tension and risk to founder alignment during rapid growth phase
- Proposed solution (partially-recourse promissory note with annual forgiveness) allows co-founder to lock in current valuation, reduce QSBS disparity, and make informed risk/benefit choice about exercising founder equity
- Compromise vesting structure (auto-vest 50% upfront, 50% over 2 years with 51% recourse on the note) reduces the co-founder's loan liability exposure from $8M to ~$2M, balancing tax benefit with personal financial risk tolerance
- A16z committed to accepting partially-recourse structure, removing a potential financing blocker around founder equity restructuring

**To GrowthX Legal & Governance:**
- Critical reminder that early-stage structuring decisions (LLC-to-C-corp transition timing, founder equity instruments, QSBS qualification) have outsized tax consequences for founders — early involvement of specialized corporate tax counsel is essential
- Loan forgiveness terms should be documented in writing, matched to vesting schedule, to create clarity and avoid future disputes
- Services-business status raises QSBS qualification flags (net 5% test on qualified small business property) but is usually surmountable with proper documentation
