## Key Topics

### fwd.digital's Series A Momentum and Model

- Recently raised $12M Series A (first institutional capital), closed with strong multiples
- Crossed $1M ARR last week; 61 deals in pipeline
- Business model: outcomes-based services for post-PMF companies, not tools or expensive agency hours
- Targets companies that need rapid execution, don't want more tools, and want hands-on expert guidance with AI leverage
- Started with content production because it's easily valuated, repeatable, and methodology transfers across knowledge work domains

### Knowledge Work as Code: The Technical Innovation

- Developed system to encode knowledge work as executable, auto-documented code (inspired by infrastructure-as-code patterns)
- Workflows combine AI agents, LLMs, API calls, and expert collaboration at multiple decision points
- Example: Research workflow—validates input, develops questions, searches sources (with competitor blacklist), fetches/scrapes results, cleans content, creates briefs
- Runtime layer enables massive parallelization: 10-20 hours of manual work per output, executed at scale in minutes
- Vision: Extend to recruiting, HR, finance, and other domains; experts stay in the loop during AI scaling
- As workflows improve via data and reinforcement learning, humans become less necessary; currently humans remain as key bottlenecks

### Candy Lee's Positioning: CMO Advising and Network

- Exited Home Base (Series B, construction tech) after 2.5 years as CMO due to burnout; equity story undershooting original narrative
- Took 4-5 months fully off to recover; deleted LinkedIn app to prevent recruiter pressure
- Now consulting/advising early-stage companies (seed to Series A), finding it fulfilling despite initial skepticism ("walk before I run")
- Co-chair of the Marketing Academy: nonprofit CMO fellowship and alumni program sponsored by Salesforce and ServiceTitan
- Alumni network: ~1,000 CMOs controlling $35B+ in marketing budgets; 60-70% non-tech (manufacturing, construction, B2B services); 30-40% big tech (Salesforce, Pinterest, Google)
- Observes that tech CMOs are exploring AI strategies; non-tech CMOs don't yet understand what an LLM is—large gap in awareness

### Equity and Burnout as Shared Context

- Both have been fired/displaced from startup equity situations; both view equity as "icing on cake" going forward
- Both experienced toxicity: board/CEO dynamics, underfunded despite contributions, last-minute layoffs that claw back equity
- Marcel's story: laid off 1 month before 2-year cliff, company deemed it "for cause," clawed back $15M in equity that later became profitable (Fieldwire)
- Both are now insulated by personal brands and diversified income; both prefer optionality over equity-dependent outcomes

### Collaboration Vectors: Three Pathways

1. **Advisory + Referrals**: Candy will deep-dive with Kyle on fwd.digital product and use cases; Marcel and Candy to regroup in 2-3 weeks; identify warm referral flow for post-PMF companies
2. **Network Monetization**: Explore ways to reach Marketing Academy CMOs (especially non-tech) who need AI strategy and execution; "something interesting there" if fwd.digital can serve this segment
3. **Co-Development of Verticals**: Consider building non-tech-specific practices together (e.g., construction, home services, manufacturing) where Candy has network relationships and fwd.digital can deliver turnkey AI workflows
- Note: fwd.digital exploring "accelerator path" for smaller companies; Candy noted her current clients are mostly pre-PMF, so they're not a fit yet
- Both see opportunity beyond tech; lots of potential in legacy industries with large budgets and outdated marketing ops
