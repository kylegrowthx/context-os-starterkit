## Summary

GrowthX and SchoolAI aligned on a content strategy that prioritizes student outcomes over AI messaging, with editorial content leading the way and programmatic content to follow. Colton emphasized that 90% of SchoolAI's messaging should focus on tangible student results and platform capabilities rather than AI features, positioning the company as a holistic solution provider against competitors who are "just GPT wrappers." The team will start with calibration articles to establish tone and brand voice, then scale across ~1,000 planned content topics in Airtable, including regional and state-specific opportunities.

## Context

SchoolAI is an AI-enabled education platform that helps schools and districts manage and optimize learning outcomes. Colton Taylor leads product and strategy; the company recently hired former educators—teachers, tech directors, instructional coaches, and a principal—to inform product development and go-to-market strategy. GrowthX is providing outsourced content marketing and SEO expertise through a $200k+ engagement. The team is one week into execution after the initial kickoff, with Colton returning from a family vacation (babymoon in Cancun ahead of a May birth). The meeting was called to review GrowthX's initial content strategy framework and get alignment on approach, scope, and next steps before full production begins.

## Key Topics

### Content Strategy Overview

  - Airtable structure presented with content clusters for programmatic and editorial topics
  - \~50 programmatic topics per category, multiple editorial categories
  - Titles are placeholders; final titles will be SEO-optimized
  - Content refreshes de-prioritized; focus on net new content

### AI Messaging Approach

  - Minimize explicit AI focus (90% on student outcomes)
  - Position SchoolAI as delivering holistic solutions across entire school/district
  - Differentiate with depth of thought leadership vs. competitors' "GPT wrapper" approach

### Regional Content Opportunities

  - Exploring state-specific and regional school district content
  - Potential to fill gap where competitors aren't addressing regional topics

### Content Approval Process

  - Colton to create shared Slack channel for internal team review
  - FWD team to propose batches of articles for approval

### Publishing and Images

  - FWD team typically handles publishing; auto-publishing from Airtable in development
  - Image strategy needed: stock photos vs. AI-generated vs. custom brand-aligned approach
  - Authorship strategy to be determined by SchoolAI

### Programmatic vs. Editorial Balance

  - Initial focus on editorial content for faster implementation
  - Programmatic content may require new page templates
  - Consider testing programmatic concepts as blog posts before full implementation
