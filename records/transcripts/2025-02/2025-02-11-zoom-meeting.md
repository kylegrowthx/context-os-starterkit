## Key Topics

### Lead Qualification & Screening Process

Marcel and Bridget discussed the gap between strong inbound lead flow and wasted time on unqualified meetings. fwd.digital gets ~25% conversion from qualified meetings to deal closure — actually quite strong — but the screening upstream is weak. Low-quality meetings (no budget, weak companies, website doesn't exist) block Marcel's calendar and break momentum. They agree Alice (operations) and Marcel's brother need to be more proactive about weeding out obvious rejects a week before the meeting. Implementing a form-based qualification system with specific questions about budget and urgency would make it easier to route leads or reschedule, though Marcel prefers not to add friction (having experienced form fatigue as a buyer). Decision: Keep current calendar-based flow but improve pre-screening.

### LinkedIn Outreach & Recruitment Efficiency

Current limitations: Marcel's LinkedIn recruiter account can only send 25 messages at a time, requiring manual 1-by-1 outreach to candidates. Growth Machine tool was identified as superior solution — Bridget has used it before and it enables message sequences and bulk outreach through Marcel's LinkedIn. The tool also maintains conversation continuity (Marcel's concern about handoff to email/Molly is resolved if outreach stays LinkedIn-to-email pipeline). Growth Machine was used successfully to find early Airbnb employees. Security note: Marcel's LinkedIn has been blocked before when an EA didn't use a VPN during outreach, so proper access controls matter.

### Recruiting Calendar Blocks & Marcel's Time Management

The core problem: Marcel jumps between recruiting, client work, internal strategy, and fundraising. Context-switching is expensive. Solution: Block 2 afternoons per week + Friday mornings for recruiting calls exclusively. Alice can schedule candidate meetings into these blocks without need for Marcel to coordinate. This creates predictable capacity for ~15-minute pitches with candidates, then routing to hiring manager screens. Bridget offered to do intro calls for referred candidates (to avoid first interaction being Molly, their current recruiter, who Marcel describes as "not super on top of it"). Bridget will need to watch Marcel's pitch first (he did a strong one with Jacob).

### Molly & Recruiter Constraints

Molly is the current recruiter but has availability gaps and isn't proactive. Previous recruiting agency charged 100/hour and cost $8K for 3 months with minimal results. Marcel decided not to continue that arrangement but values Molly's contacts. Strategy: Use Molly for specific introductions and context, but reserve first interactions for Marcel or Bridget to set proper tone.

### Quantum Talent Engagement for Principal Engineers

Marcel is engaging Chris from Quantum Talent to hire principal engineers. Chris is efficient, well-networked, and can move fast (unlike "artisanal" recruiting sweatshops like Artisanal that hit candidates multiple times without coordination). Chris has successfully placed entire leadership teams. Strategy: Chris's commission (~30% of first-year salary, which is standard) is worth it because he does the selling (pitch building, world-building) and leverages his network. For principal engineers specifically, Chris's network is exactly the fit. Decision: Only using Chris for principal engineers right now, not general hiring. Quantum Talent will also help with H1 hiring planning and compensation benchmarking.

### Sales/Operations Operations Role

Marcel and Bridget identified need for dedicated person focused on sales operations and lead management. This person would be first touch on inbound (qualifying, scheduling), handling customer management, and freeing Alice from sales ops overhead so she can focus on financial operations and Marcel's calendar. Candidates discussed: Jeff (high risk/high reward, expensive, previously worked with Marcel, was first sales rep at G2 and first account manager at Airbnb), Danny (same background as Jeff, nearly hired but Series A prep and equity negotiation timing prevented it). Ideal profile: Someone who can do "traffic control" and make inbound process efficient without requiring Marcel's involvement in scheduling details.

### Candidate Re-engagement & H1 Hiring Pipeline

Marcel and Bridget agreed to re-engage strong candidates who have made it far in the hiring process. Bridget identified Gabriela (agency experience, strong operations background, doing marketing + operations at her current role) as worth reaching out to about operational roles available post-Series A. 72 people have already submitted video assessments for previous roles. Other candidates mentioned: Sarah (in video interview stage, unclear if she progressed), candidates in Mexico offering cost advantage and timezone compatibility. Strategy: Reach out to Gabriela, Margarita (previously sent), Dupritte (Vancouver, Zapier background, technical/recruiting ops background). Marcel will handle most outreach while Bridget focuses on the three above plus potential intro calls.

### Mexico Hiring Strategy

Bridget expressed enthusiasm for Mexico hiring — timezone advantage for US-based company, cost-effectiveness, good English proficiency, easy travel (SF or wherever). Marcel has a candidate there who previously worked at Skill (very demanding company, worked 100-hour weeks) and is asking for $6K/month with stock options. Marcel would negotiate to ~$6.5K or even lower. Person fits business operations/resource profile. Philosophy: "Hiring in Mexico is the best."

### H1 Hiring Roadmap & Bottleneck Identification

Marcel and Bridget want to create structured H1 roadmap that: (1) Lists all roles they want to hire for, (2) Sequences hiring before vs. after they have hiring help in place, (3) Identifies bottlenecks preventing growth (training is major one — new hires coming in faster than onboarding capacity). Need to understand company bottlenecks to be intentional about which roles solve them. Focus should be on execution-oriented, resourceful earlier-career people — not more strategic contributors (they have enough of those: Marcel, Daniel, Bridget). The team doesn't need more people contributing ideas to strategy; they need execution.

### Employee Referral Bonus Program

Implementing structured referral program: $10K for engineers, $3K for non-engineers, paid at 6-month anniversary (not upfront). This creates good economics — if each new hire brings 1-2 more through referrals, the bonus is worth it. Announced upfront to team as incentive but with clarity that payout happens after 6 months. Bridget expects this to be particularly valuable if Daniel (presumably a strong engineer or technical leader) can bring 1-2 more engineers through network.

### Financial Operations & QuickBooks Automation

Invoice, receivables, and payables management is a critical pain point — Alice is spending significant time on manual entry. Marcel is nervous about anything related to money being handled manually. Solution: Automate deal closures and QuickBooks integration so that when a deal closes in the system, it automatically creates invoice/payables entries in QuickBooks. This removes manual data entry and reduces fraud/error risk. May also outsource this function or find better solution than current Alice-manual approach.

### Carda Access Issues

Marcel added Bridget to a Carda call (appears to be a vendor/partner interaction). Bridget reported she can't access Carda — the system requires a backup email, and Carda flagged that Marcel's personal email already has a Carda account, blocking her entry. Marcel offered to resolve this access issue.
