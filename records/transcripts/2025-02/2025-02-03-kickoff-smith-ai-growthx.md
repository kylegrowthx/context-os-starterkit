## Overview

- Smith.ai offers two main products: Virtual Receptionists (VR, human-led) and Voice Assistants (VA, AI-led) with some overlap in features
- Primary focus is on acquiring high-quality traffic for AI answering service while maintaining VR positioning
- Target audience: Small/medium service-based businesses with high client value (e.g. lawyers, roofers) and their marketing agencies
- Key pain point: Busy business owners know they need better systems but struggle to prioritize implementation

## Key Topics

### Product Overview: VR vs VA

  - VR: Human-led, higher cost, handles complex interactions
  - VA: AI-led, lower cost, handles simpler tasks
  - Overlap in basic functions like message taking, intake
  - Challenge: Balancing SEO/content strategy for both products as they evolve

### Target Audience & Pain Points

  - Small/medium service businesses (lawyers, roofers, etc.) with high client value
  - 2+ phone calls per day minimum
  - Pain points: Too busy, lack business skills, know they need better systems
  - Trigger for change often involves losing a big client or staff quitting

### Content Strategy & SEO Focus

  - Prioritize acquiring high-quality traffic for "AI answering service"
  - Maintain positioning for established "virtual receptionist" keywords
  - Improve landing pages and homepage for better conversion
  - Consider interactive elements like calculators to boost engagement

### Integration & Technical Considerations

  - Integrations are a key value prop (e.g. CRMs, practice management software)
  - In-house web dev team uses Webflow for implementation

### Current Performance & Goals

  - 15-20% of new monthly clients come from organic search
  - Aim to improve landing page conversion rates
  - Balance content strategy between VA and VR products
