## Key Topics

### Headcount Planning

  - Current: \~25 employees, 6 "pods" (small teams)
  - Target: 45 by June, 65 by December
  - Key ratios:
      - 8 accounts per pod initially, increasing to 10 later
      - 3 pods per director
      - 3 account managers per pod
  - Adding VP layer when reaching \~10 directors (likely 2026)
  - Aggressive hiring needed for foundation, but concerns about maintaining quality/culture

### Compensation Strategy

  - Prioritizing cash compensation over equity for L3/L4 roles
  - L5+ roles (directors, VPs) to receive more substantial equity (10-40 basis points)
  - Considering delayed equity grants for L3s (e.g., qualify after 3 months)
  - Aiming for 50th percentile compensation benchmarks, with flexibility
  - Exploring aggressive bonus structures (e.g., 100% bonus if hitting $20M ARR)

### Equity Considerations

  - Current ESOP pool: \~8% (diluting to 6.5%)
  - Founders diluting to \~57% ownership
  - Balancing equity grants to avoid depleting ESOP too quickly
  - Planning for potential ESOP increase after next funding round

### Financial Modeling

  - Direct people costs projected at $420k/month by June, $616k/month by December
  - Additional budget allocated for G\&A, sales & marketing (\~20% of revenue)
  - Aiming to stay within raised capital without needing to increase ESOP for 6+ months

### Recruiting Strategy

  - Leveraging current market conditions (layoffs, return-to-office mandates)
  - Targeting high-quality candidates who may prioritize remote work, interesting projects
  - Considering global talent pool for cost-effective hiring (e.g., $200k/year in Brazil or Mexico for top talent)
