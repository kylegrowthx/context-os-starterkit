### Brand Direction and Image Generation

Pixis' brand is shifting from dark to light — new color palette moves toward lighter backgrounds. Current color system: purple-green gradient (primary elements), tan-gray (background and secondary), darker versions for accents or dark-mode pages. Jason provided color codes and direction to use lighter imagery. AI-generated images from Midjourney underperform; Jason prefers stock photography where possible. For the overspending on Google Ads article, suggested approach is a graph showing spend/performance divergence (not dollar bills with "weird ghosts on the faces"). For the LLMs in marketing article, Evan suggested showing popular LLM names (ChatGPT, Claude, Anthropic) with social-feed-style imagery suggesting information overload. Pixis' tool offers a capability to keep static brand elements (logos, backgrounds, color treatments) static while AI generates variations around them — this supports scaling and consistency. Aida will redo images with new brand direction and stage/publish by end of week.

### Content Production and Strategy

GrowthX is delivering 10 new blog articles by end of week, plus 25 glossary items (longer than current website entries). Aida implemented "research guidance" in AirOps — a field where you specify AI expertise context ("you are a senior digital marketer, user of AI tools, etc.") and top-ranking articles, and AI pulls specific research based on that guidance. Daniel investigating how research guidance affects outline structure. Internal linking automation now live in the workflow; new content batches automatically pull internal links. Kyle noted distinction between outbound internal links (new articles linking outward) vs. inbound internal links (existing content linking to new articles) — inbound still manual, but exploring Surfer SEO integration for automated discovery. Future possibility of external link building once internal structure is solid.

### Content Quality and AI Expertise

Jason maintains high expectations for content quality — doesn't want it to read as AI-generated. Evan shared technique of using persona prompting ("write this like David Ogilvy") and domain expertise classification to improve outputs. Kyle's team experimenting with prompting styles and personas. Aida implementing research guidance to lock in expertise context. Team agreed to iterate quickly and not delay over perfect imagery — plan to try new images, see what works, refine over time.

### Ongoing Projects and Strategic Planning

Beam Content (Brooklin) working on paid ad benchmark report with data from Google and Meta, plus interview-based insights for each benchmark. Launching in conversation with Jason tomorrow. Also developing "project brief" (team brief) to establish content themes, specific topics within each theme, and SME interviews — foundation for thought leadership and SME content calendar. Pending two SME interviews later this week. Once brief is finalized, will help planning for programmatic content production and ensure new content fits cohesive narrative. Brooklin noted that benchmark report could be used to spin up new content pages; prior example: "LinkedIn CTA benchmarks" metadata report spawned first-page rankings and dozens of follow-up articles.
