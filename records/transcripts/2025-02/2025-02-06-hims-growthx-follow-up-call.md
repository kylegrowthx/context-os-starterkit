## Summary

Marcel and Mike worked to bridge a significant price gap on a potential GrowthX-Hims content partnership: Hims budgeted $9-11k/month while GrowthX's proposal came in at $45k/month, driven by medical research integrations, multi-layer review processes, and onboarded medical experts. Marcel offered to remove the medical expert onboarding work stream, reduce volume from 3,000+ pages to ~1,200 pages annually, and discount to approximately $18k/month—a significant reduction he framed as building a case study. Both parties agreed to explore a 90-day pilot period to test the partnership and calibrate processes, with Marcel committing to a revised proposal that explicitly details cost drivers and the GrowthX workflow engine's capabilities.

## Context

Hims is a direct-to-consumer telehealth company with a large, quality-sensitive content footprint. This call was a follow-up negotiation after GrowthX's initial proposal came in significantly higher than Hims' budget expectations. Marcel had delayed responses due to fwd.digital's Series A funding process, which had consumed substantial time and legal bandwidth. The discussion centered on aligning Hims' budget constraints with GrowthX's cost structure for programmatic SEO—specifically, the labor and compute costs associated with custom medical research database integrations, multi-layer content reviews, and medical expert onboarding. Hims' internal stakeholders had become eager to move forward after a lengthy evaluation period, and they had also received competing proposals from other vendors at lower price points, creating urgency to find a workable structure.

## Key Topics

### Recent Company Updates

- Marcel's fwd.digital signed a Series A term sheet with desired investor and terms
- Funding enables increased investment in engineering talent and provides credibility with top-tier VC backing
- Series A process consumed extensive time on legal and administrative work, causing delayed responses

### Budget and Pricing Concerns

  - Hims initially budgeted for $9-11k per month, significantly lower than GrowthX's proposal
  - GrowthX's standard rate: $12k/month for 1200 pages/year of programmatic SEO
  - Higher costs driven by custom integrations, medical research databases, and multi-layer review processes
  - Marcel offered to reduce scope and price to \~$18k/month (from $45k) by removing certain work streams

### Content Quality and Review Process

- GrowthX's standard medical content process includes multiple layers of review: editors, source vetting, claim flagging, and optionally medical expert bylines
- Every step of GrowthX's workflow includes human editors; the cost difference is whether GrowthX onboards and manages medical experts (higher cost) or Hims' internal experts review after delivery (lower cost)
- Marcel emphasized that medical expert bylines require the expert to read the full content, represent a $150+/hour cost floor even if streamlined, and cannot be outsourced to international profiles with equivalent credibility
- Hims wants adequate vetting without duplicating work; they prefer putting the burden on their team rather than GrowthX hiring specialists

### GrowthX's Technical Capabilities

- Marcel demoed GrowthX's workflow engine: an AI-driven system that conducts parallel research, searches sources, validates relevance, and produces structured research briefs
- The workflows are "code-as-workflows"—fully customizable via a runtime layer; quality gates can be added (e.g., accuracy checks, authority checks, domain-specific filtering)
- Calibration is central: the team provides feedback after seeing the first output, processes are tweaked, and the lift required from Hims' internal review decreases over time
- Transparency into the system (what was added and why) is critical for calibration; "black box" results make iterative improvement impossible

### Potential Partnership Structure

- 90-day pilot period: allows Hims to stress-test the internal review process and GrowthX to calibrate workflows based on feedback
- GrowthX accepts upfront losses and risk; Marcel positioned this as justified by the case study opportunity in healthcare (Hims is a prestige customer in a regulated, high-value vertical)
- Open variables: content output volume, contract length beyond the pilot, and the mechanics of internal review (how much Hims' team needs to invest)
- Marcel emphasized that competitors offering significantly below $12k/month are either not delivering legitimate quality or using international resources without equivalent credibility
