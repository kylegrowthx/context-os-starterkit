## Key Topics

### Kyle's Experience with GrowthX at Homebase

  - At Homebase, shifted SEO strategy from generic keyword-driven content to user experience and intent-driven approach, achieving 140-170% YoY non-brand traffic growth by Q2 2024.
  - Faced budget constraints: $400k+ per quarter SEO spend across contractors, agencies, and internal team (head of content + editors + web team). Content pieces ranged from $600-1000+ each.
  - Discovered GrowthX through Marcel's LinkedIn workshop in April/June 2024. GrowthX's workflows reduced per-piece cost to ~$130 while maintaining quality.
  - Key strategic insight: shifted payroll marketing focus from direct payroll keywords (too much friction) to "starter" signups (businesses in year 1 without payroll solution), which had higher adoption rates than rip-and-replace scenarios.

### GrowthX's Approach and Capabilities

  - Acts as an extension of the client's team, plugging into existing workflows and processes. Not a content factory, not a replacement for in-house teams.
  - Flexible across the funnel: primarily works on top-of-funnel, but can tackle bottom-funnel demand capture (localized variants, state labor law pages, etc.) when opportunities align with ICP and volume potential.
  - Podcast workflow example: converts podcast transcripts into key takeaways, extracts quotes, generates blog post variations, and creates multi-channel derivative content from a single asset.
  - Uses expert-in-the-loop architecture: AI workflows do the generation and optimization, humans validate voice, tone, strategy, and ensure true user value. Not button-pressing content automation.
  - Enables 10x capacity increase for client teams without requiring 10x hiring. Frees up time for creative strategy and experimentation vs. pure execution.

### 7shifts' Current Content and SEO Situation

  - Organic traffic declined, direct traffic flat in 2024. Playing defense on paid acquisition, which is expensive and volatile (especially after Google updates).
  - Four-person content team: 1 copywriter supporting product team (short-form copy), 2 content specialists (1 started Nov 2024, focuses long-form; 1 handles blog, video), 1 senior content writer focused on podcast.
  - External SEO strategist helps bridge technical and content, with traffic and conversion targets. But team is hitting a ceiling on content production with current resources.
  - Pivoting toward channel diversification: podcast content (not currently being repurposed), YouTube, TikTok, webinars. Goal is to increase output and reach beyond paid.
  - Payroll product is early (2-3 years in): single-location only (multi-location on 2025 roadmap), US-only, not a payroll-first brand. Core identity is scheduling. Challenge: how to capture demand in payroll space without confusing brand positioning.

### Potential Partnership Structure

  - Model would be flexible: additive opportunities where GrowthX owns production (e.g., podcast repurposing, webinar derivative content) with 7shifts team providing feedback on voice and tone.
  - Engagement could expand existing initiatives: if 7shifts launches YouTube or webinar series, GrowthX builds workflow to maximize reach and derivative content from that one asset.
  - Pricing: $144k/year was referenced as a potential investment level. Libby needs to build business case and reallocate from existing SEO contractor or other budget.
  - Critical positioning: content must be ICP-focused and provide true user value. The market has shifted away from broad topical plays (HubSpot, GreenLight, Zapier-style "how many inches in a mile" content). AI content without strategic intent is now getting penalized by Google.
  - Proof-of-concept structure: 90-day trial discussed as validation period before full commitment.
