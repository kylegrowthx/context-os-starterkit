## Summary

GrowthX and Aftershoot kicked off their 90-day SEO pilot with Kyle Gaudreau, David Galic, Rasheed Ahamed, Monalisa Roy, and Frank Visciano. The team aligned on Aftershoot's core objective: scale non-branded organic traffic to drive new trials and customers, with a secondary focus on establishing a repeatable playbook for future product launches (AI Retouching launching next month, Raw Editing in H2). The target audience is professional photographers who generate income from their work—wedding, portrait, sports, and headshot photographers—avoiding hobbyists in landscape/wildlife/street photography. Key activation metric: 5 culling/editing sessions during trial period; key conversion driver: the speed and efficiency Aftershoot provides compared to manual workflows. GrowthX committed to providing content strategy, competitive analysis, and initial article drafts within 2-3 weeks.

## Context

Aftershoot is an AI-powered photo editing platform helping professional photographers streamline their culling and editing workflows. The company's flagship product batch-edits images at scale—uploading raw photos and delivering polished results in minutes rather than hours—with two major product launches underway: AI Retouching (February 2025) and Raw Editing (H2 2025). Aftershoot currently generates organic traffic primarily in branded search, with the majority of customer acquisition from direct/organic traffic, YouTube, and social media—heavily leveraging creator partnerships with professional photographers to drive awareness and credibility.

Rasheed Ahamed leads marketing, having connected with Marcel previously through GrowthX's growth methodology. Frank Visciano serves as a marketing advisor and former Udemy CEO providing strategic oversight on prioritization and product positioning. Monalisa Roy owns content marketing and will be the main point of contact for GrowthX. Kyle Gaudreau leads the account from GrowthX's side with David Galic as the managing editor (first week at GrowthX) heading the content pod. The engagement is structured around a 90-day pilot to establish SEO baseline and playbook before scaling to additional products and international markets (localization is a company priority this year).

## Overview

- Primary goal: Scale non-branded organic traffic to drive trials and new customers, especially to support upcoming product launches (AI Retouching Feb 2025, Raw Editing H2 2025)
- Company north star: Double active subscribers this year; secondary priorities include international expansion (localization) and establishing repeatable SEO playbook

## Key Topics

### Aftershoot's Business Goals

  - Double active subscribers this year
  - Drive new trials through marketing efforts
  - Expand internationally through localization (website in multiple languages)
  - Successfully launch new products: AI Retouching (next month) and Raw Editing (H2)

### Target Audience Refinement

  - Focus on professional photographers who earn income from photography
  - Key genres: Weddings, portraits, events, sports, headshots
  - Avoid: Wildlife, street, landscape photographers (typically hobbyists)
  - Look for "client-driven" photography categories

### Content Strategy Considerations

  - Leverage existing creator/influencer content for credibility
  - Explore repurposing opportunities (e.g., podcast transcripts to blog posts)
  - Balance AI-generated content with photographer-created content
  - Aim for speed and efficiency as key value propositions in content

### Measurement and KPIs

  - Primary metric: Active subscribers
  - Content attribution: First-touch (14-day window)
  - Key activation metric: 5 culling/editing sessions during trial period
  - Tools: Post-hog for user behavior, Google Analytics for traffic

### SEO Engagement Process

  - Week 1-2: Research, competitive analysis, initial content strategy
  - Week 3-4: Refine inputs, establish content creation process
  - Month 2-3: Expect initial traffic uptick (150-200 clicks/week)
  - Month 4-6: Analyze traffic quality, conversion rates, adjust strategy
