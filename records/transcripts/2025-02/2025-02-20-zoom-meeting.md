## Key Topics

### Sales Process Bottleneck

Marcel is running 14+ back-to-back meetings daily with qualified prospects but has no systematic post-call follow-up. The result: high-value deals like Arcade (Series A, $21k/month potential) are at immediate risk of lost follow-up. Current friction: recording access (Zoom doesn't auto-share), unclear owner assignment, and team spread too thin. Bruno articulated the exact workflow he can handle: get link → create SLW → set up Slack channel → send follow-up email/audio. Marcel's ask: prioritize his meetings above Jimmy's (his are more qualified and higher-value).

### Meeting Recording Access Issue

Zoom is configured for privacy (some meetings private, some shared) with no automatic broadcast. Solution proposed: create a team@ email account, invite it to all sales calls, ensure automatic recording access. Immediate: Bruno will ping for links post-meeting.

### Pipeline Quality vs. Process Capacity

Bruno flagged that current SLW strategy inflates pipeline with unlikely prospects (50% of pipeline is "in flux" pending follow-ups that won't convert). His recommendation: send SLWs only to verbally committed prospects, not to every meeting. This is a separate strategic issue from the hot-prospect follow-up problem, but both need to be addressed.

### Compensation and Burnout

Marcel recently closed $12M and is hiring 75 people. Bruno is the operational hub but financially constrained—recently withdrew $11k from personal savings and earning what he called "Brazil money." He's hesitant about 12-14 hour days because past burnout. Marcel capped Bruno at $200k/year (can't justify higher for his current experience level; comparable account managers earn $5k/month). Marcel offered equity as upside but emphasized the opportunity: if Bruno performs, he'll make more than ever before. The tension: different financial pressures and different risk tolerances—Marcel is gambling on rapid scaling, Bruno is protecting against burnout and questioning whether the current deal justifies the hours.
