## Key Topics

### Current Trademark Situation

  - GrowthX Labs discovered another company with a similar "GrowthX" trademark
  - Other company's registered services are broad (e.g., "go-to-market services", "marketing webinars")
  - Reality: Other company appears to be a venture capital fund, not a SaaS product like GrowthX Labs

### Trademark Strategy Options

  - "Fly under the radar" approach:
      - Continue using mark without filing for registration
      - Minimize likelihood of confusion through distinct usage
      - Pros: Low-key, potentially years of coexistence strengthening argument
      - Cons: Harder to enforce rights without registration
  - File for registration approach:
      - Attempt to register own trademark
      - Pros: Stronger protection, presumption of validity
      - Cons: Increases likelihood of confrontation with prior user

### Legal Considerations

  - Courts consider actual marketplace reality, not just registered services
  - Coexistence possible (e.g., United Airlines vs. United Van Lines)
  - Options if trademark office cites prior registration:
      - Argue no confusion
      - Potentially file for partial cancellation of other mark

### International Trademark Process

  - U.S. companies typically file U.S. application first
  - Paris Convention allows 6-month priority in member countries
  - U.S. requires use before registration; most foreign countries don't
  - Foreign registrations often open to non-use cancellation after 3 years

### Womble Dickinson Services

  - Large firm with \~250 IP attorneys
  - Offers full spectrum of IP services: trademarks, patents, etc.
  - Engagement process: Send engagement letter with representation details
