## Key Topics

### Content Production Updates

Two calibration articles passed review and target upload by EOD Feb 21. The team approved 10 new article proposals spanning diverse topics and use cases, with production ramping the following week. Jenn demonstrated the AirOps workflow that powers their content pipeline: keyword research and company context feed into tone-of-voice configuration, then a new briefing stage lets the team specify exact content strategy, guidelines, and keyword targets. From the brief, the system generates an outline and keyword list, which Jenn can refine, and then moves through title generation, draft, meta/description, internal linking, and final human review before publishing. Adrian suggested incorporating code samples and images into future articles where appropriate—for these two calibration pieces, Jenn will add AI-generated images to break up text. PNG output works fine; Zuplo's infrastructure automatically compresses and serves in modern formats like WebP.

### SEO and Backlink Strategy

Backlink strategy starts with Zuplo's internal partner network—the path of least resistance in the early ramp. In parallel, Jimmy Pal is working on outbound backlink acquisition, which has become significantly more complex than in the past due to email warming requirements, domain warming, and changing deliverability constraints. Kyle wants Jimmy to join the next sync to discuss progress and specific details. Kyle also flagged a contractual obligation around monthly backlink volume and quality; the team expects to ramp more aggressively in subsequent months but wants to track both volume and quality. Adrian raised a key AI visibility insight from Scrunch: LLMs like ChatGPT prioritize direct brand mentions over backlinks when generating recommendations, so the team should bias outreach toward opportunities for brand mentions within Zuplo's partner content network where possible.

### Tag Structure and Site Architecture

Adrian explained Zuplo's tag system: articles are tagged hierarchically (e.g., API Management as a parent, with children like API Security, and grandchildren like Authentication), and tag pages are generated dynamically at the bottom of relevant articles with FAQ structured data. These pages don't necessarily rank independently but improve discoverability and site authority by making it easier for Google crawlers to navigate the content graph. Adrian will send the full tag list and hierarchy so GrowthX can incorporate tag generation into the AirOps workflow. Kyle mentioned the team can probably build this into the production process with help from Daniel. Adrian is also open to new tags as long as they follow the tree structure for crawlability.

### GitHub Integration and Expert Opinion Elements

Jenn received GitHub access and will use the video guide Adrian provided to upload articles step by step, with support from internal GrowthX GitHub experts to minimize burden on Adrian. Adrian will send a quick message with instructions on adding "expert opinion" elements to articles—it's a simple one-line addition that Adrian can then generate the component for. Jenn expects to learn the process by doing it with the first batch.

### Future Collaboration

Adrian has Scrunch AI collaboration on his backlog and will reach out later in the week to explore potential partnership around content generation or AI visibility analysis. Onboarding and integration will likely take a few days.
