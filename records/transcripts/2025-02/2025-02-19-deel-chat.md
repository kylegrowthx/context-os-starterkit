## Key Topics

### Deel Transition Timeline and Mechanics

Set up all employees and contractors in Deel with a March 1st start date. GrowthX will announce the transition by Friday (Feb 21st) to give everyone until Feb 28th to complete onboarding and Deel paperwork. Terminate all contractors in Remote on Feb 28th (cannot set explicit end dates for contractors, so Bridget will need to manually process). Yousef is researching the Remote API to automate mass termination and avoid accidental double-payments. The key coordination point: approve all final payments in Remote before termination so contractors get paid cleanly through Feb 28th.

### Health Insurance Plan Selection

Three tiers of free basic coverage (Bronze, Regional HMO, and HSA) available to all employees—GrowthX messaging this as "free health care." Silver Plan (70% coverage) and Gold Plan (75% coverage) offered as premium upgrades. Coverage split: for individual plans, company covers the specified percentage; for family plans, company covers that percentage of the family plan cost. Nadia raised concern about Kaiser availability for employees currently on Kaiser plans—not offered in the new plans. Regional plans are Anthem HMO (California-only). HSA plans are the cheapest but attract tax-advantaged savers.

### Executive Benefits Tier (Deferred)

Team discussed whether to offer a separate executive benefit group (e.g., 100% coverage on Gold or Silver), but Bridget deferred the decision to focus on launching with standard tiers. Could revisit in 2027. Challenges: if executives want higher coverage, it's either an explicit salary bump (tax implications) or difficult to expense the gap. Nadia flagged she may need multiple surgeries this year, so deferring until 2027 is risky for benefits planning.

### Vision, Dental, Life, and Disability

Vision and dental: considering a basic plan with a buy-up option. Life and AD&D: typically voluntary (employee-paid) at GrowthX's stage. Short-term and long-term disability (STD/LTD): Bridget asked Connor for clarification on whether the offered policy is supplemental (covers the delta above state-mandated coverage) or mandatory, and what "contributory" and "gross-up" mean in practice. Nadia noted that California mandates 1% employee contribution for STD, so the additional insurance is likely a gap-filler.

### Domain Asset Transfer

Content2marketing.com is listed on the asset transfer document but ownership is unclear—it's registered under Shami (a historical vendor who managed freelance content). Marcel may have already taken over the domain for personal use, or it may be unused. Yousef asked Nadia to confirm with Marcel whether he intends to use the domain; if not, it should be pulled from the transfer list. Bridget needs a final asset list today to hand off to the legal team.
