## Key Topics

### Legal Representation & Albert's Track Record

Albert is their current corporate/IP lawyer, but multiple recommendations are raising flags. Marcel analyzed round documents using ChatGPT and identified gaps Albert missed. VCs have expressed concern when Albert explains his recommendations (e.g., creating a new corporation to distribute assets—flagged as "unusual" by Madrona Capital and others). Previous IP law advice proved "completely not true" on second opinion. Marcel is hesitant to fire Albert immediately because Albert holds critical documents they'd need to transfer, but Karun (likely an advisor or investor) recommended switching post-round. Strategy: find a second-opinion lawyer before signing anything; consider switching to a T1 firm like Goodwin after round closure (potentially with a post-closure discount in exchange for switching).

### Founder Equity Problem: Options vs. Shares

Daniel, Marcel's co-founder, is exploring better equity arrangements. Current structure: stock options with prohibitive tax costs. If Daniel exercises monthly, he'd pay $500-600k/year in taxes—cost-prohibitive unless the company sells secondary shares or goes crazy in a secondary round. If Daniel can't exercise and needs to leave, he has zero protection and walks away with nothing (historical example: co-founder who spent 7 years at a company but couldn't exercise options; company stalled and is now worth nothing). Ideal structure: both founders own founder shares (70/30 split) from day one with vesting as protection against leaving—no exercise, no AMT taxes. Meeting scheduled with Jesse Berg tomorrow to explore alternative equity options.

### Revenue Impact of Legal Friction

Marcel quantifies opportunity cost: he had two calls today that are "turning into deals" but skipped five sales meetings due to legal coordination. He estimates missing 3-4 of these high-value calls per week costs $300-400k/year in ARR. Observation: buyer momentum is fragile—"when you linger, then it's like, Oh, you know, and then it goes away." Moving legal management off Marcel's critical path is strategic, not just administrative.

### Workflow Change: Daily Standups & Delegation

Marcel proposes daily 5-minute standups with Bridget instead of email threads. Current problem: too many parallel email threads (Goodwin asking one set of questions, Albert asking another). Marcel and Daniel have to confirm the same information twice. Proposal: Bridget owns legal/admin coordination; Marcel only reviews decisions in standup + attends meetings that require founder input. Bridget gets a daily checklist: decisions needed today, urgent responses to draft, meeting prep.

### Contractor Risk & Freelancer Access

Concern: 20+ freelancers without background checks have access to client data (including public company sites where they're publishing content). This is flagged as "crazy operational risk." Freelancers are already more expensive than full-time hires. Adding vetting, IP assignment, and access controls is urgent.

### Future Legal Complexity

By June, hiring in-house general counsel. Why: potential M&As, multiple brands launching, AI model training (which brings IP/training data liability). Earlier example: a SaaS deep brand without in-house counsel got slammed with $150k/month legal bills for some months. Once general counsel salary threshold is crossed, it pays for itself on employee matters alone.
