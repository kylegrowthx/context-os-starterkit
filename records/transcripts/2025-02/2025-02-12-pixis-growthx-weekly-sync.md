## Key Topics

### Content Production and Strategy

Pixis and GrowthX have shipped 2 articles so far and will publish 10 more this week, with 25 glossary pages (500 words each) already completed. The team is working to scale article length to 3000+ words by using a more sophisticated research approach: identifying competing articles, extracting all their headings, then focusing the AI on content gaps to generate more substantive sections. The core infrastructure uses AirOps to manage the editorial-to-outline-to-article pipeline, with Aida using ChatGPT for manual research infill when automated research is insufficient. The team has identified 100-218 glossary terms to cover, which at 25 pages/week represents about one month of programmatic content work. After glossary completion, a strategic pivot is under discussion: shift the contract from programmatic content to external link building for 3-6 months to build authority ahead of the rebrand.

### Website Structure and SEO Implementation

The Pixis website has a critical SEO issue: the AI glossary is currently behind a modal overlay, which means the glossary entries are not separately indexable by search engines. Evan (Pixis) will investigate WordPress permissions and contact internal WordPress developers (likely Anurag) to create a proper glossary page type so that each term has its own URL and can be indexed. This fix must be in place before publishing the 25+ new glossary pages, or they won't provide SEO value. The team also discussed internal linking strategy: Aida feeds the sitemap into AirOps to auto-suggest internal links, but the AI suggestions are frequently wrong, so she manually reviews and fixes them.

### Authority Building and the Rebrand Timeline

Pixis is preparing a major rebrand that will likely cause a 30% loss of domain authority. Evan has been researching how to minimize and recover from that loss. He's been studying how other fast-growing B2B and developer platforms handle authority building: Netflix has a developer blog, Convoy has a data strategy blog, and Snowflake/Looker lead thought leadership in data. Evan is also investigating try.profound.com (an LLM-visibility service that uses old-school PR link building to tier-1 publications like Forbes and HuffPost). The core insight: external link building becomes more valuable than programmatic content in the near term, because the rebrand will reset authority and the team needs a recovery mechanism. There's also untapped distribution: Pixis has 130K+ LinkedIn followers (mostly Indian developers who auto-followed when they applied for jobs via LinkedIn Easy Apply). This audience could be leveraged for traffic and authority building, but it's not a monetizable audience for the product (it's developer enthusiasm, not buyers).

### Image Production Workflow

Aida initially used Midjourney to generate blog post header images, but found the process time-consuming (requiring 10+ iterations for acceptable results). She pivoted to template-based Canva designs using the new brand colors, which are faster to produce. Jason approved the concept and offered to have the Pixis design team create a more polished, branded template that Aida can populate with article titles, eliminating the need for her to change individual image designs. Aida will send Jason a brief with image sizes and specifications; the design team will ship a template in a couple of days.

### Content Type Split and Contract Clarity

The contract has two buckets: editorial (strategic, longer articles) and programmatic (scalable glossary pages and similar content). The 12 articles shipping this week are editorial. The 25 glossary pages are programmatic. This split matters because it affects how to measure against contract obligations and plan service transitions. Jason raised the question of whether Pixis is set up for programmatic content (tone, scale, subject matter), and whether the focus should shift post-glossary.
