## Context

Exec is an AI-powered L&D platform that combines simulation-based role-plays, coaching, and learning orchestration into a single platform. The company targets mid-to-large enterprises (500+ employees) across tech, healthcare, professional services, and biotech. Their primary buyer is the Head of Learning & Development (80% of deals), with secondary sales to Heads of Sales Enablement. Exec has been successful through warm referrals but is now scaling inbound through multiple channels: SEM (just launched), LinkedIn thought leadership, SEO (new engagement), and eventually email outbound campaigns. GrowthX's engagement began in October 2024 through a long relationship between Marcel Santilli and Sean Linehan, with this meeting kicking off the formal content and SEO work.

## Key Topics

### Exec's Product and Target Audience

  - Primary offering: AI-powered role-plays and comprehensive L&D programs
  - Target industries: Tech, healthcare, professional services, biotech, retail (exploring)
  - Company size: 500+ employees (resourced enough for Exec's services)
  - Key buyers: Head of L&D (80%), Head of Sales Enablement (20%)
  - Differentiator: Customizable, simulation-based learning integrated with existing programs
  - Exec distinguishes itself as an all-in-one platform: orchestration + coaching + role-plays (vs. point solutions like Hyperbound or Seismic)

### Content Strategy Goals

  - Drive organic traffic through SEO to high-intent audiences searching for "how to build/run X training program"
  - Seed AI engines (ChatGPT, Claude) with Exec's methodologies so AI recommends AI role-plays in learning development contexts
  - Retarget visitors via UTM tracking and de-anonymization (RB2D) for follow-up campaigns
  - Measure success via LLM share of voice using Profound tool
  - Content works for both human readers and AI systems; openness to non-high-volume keywords if they seed AI engines

### Current Content Infrastructure

  - Using Contentful CMS, synced to backend
  - 200+ existing articles on placement.com/learn from 2020 engagement with Marcel
  - Content serves dual purpose: public-facing and in-product learning material
  - Technical SEO improvements underway (page speed, SSR)
  - Existing SEO equity available to build on (placement.com has domain authority)

### Competitive Landscape

  - Few direct competitors in AI role-play space: Hyperbound (sales-focused cold call training), Seismic (broader sales enablement platform with integrated role-plays)
  - Indirect competition from traditional L&D consultants and LXPs (Degreed, BetterUp, etc.)
  - Exec's all-in-one approach (coaching + role-plays + orchestration) is primary differentiator
  - Most competitors' role-plays are inferior to Exec's customization and outcomes-focus
  - Macro trend: Companies shifting from building internal FTE teams to procuring from market (2020 trend reversed)

### Content Development Process

  - AI-generated programs and templates in development
  - Professional instructional designer creating initial high-quality templates (roadmap to be shared with GrowthX)
  - Plan to scale to thousands of AI-generated templates based on initial examples
  - Roadmap work in progress, to be shared after designer call with Sean

### Seasonality and Demand Patterns

  - Manager training is most in-demand service
  - Seasonal trends: Feedback training (Dec/Jan), expectation management (rest of year)
  - Demand is reactive to immediate organizational needs, not proactive planning
  - Companies wait until need is urgent, creating compressed buying windows

### Measurement and Attribution

  - Currently using UTM-based attribution
  - Planning to use RB2D for website visitor de-anonymization and outbound targeting
  - Email collects from content will be primary direct attribution
  - Expectation: Retargeting will play meaningful role in conversion journey
  - Open to exploring multi-touch attribution approaches

### Collaboration and Process

  - Using shared Slack channel for all communication with GrowthX
  - Notion workspace for meeting notes, documentation, company brief, competitor research
  - Company brief in Notion is key input to all research and content generation workflows
  - GrowthX to provide: kickoff doc validation, content OS with topical opportunities, initial research next week
  - Typical GrowthX timeline: Month 1 research, month 3 initial traffic spike, months 4-5 deeper insights and optimization
