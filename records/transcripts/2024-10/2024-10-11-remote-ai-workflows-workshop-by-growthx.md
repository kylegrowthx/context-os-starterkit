## Overview

- AI workflows can significantly enhance productivity by automating complex tasks like research, content creation, and follow-ups
- Effective prompting techniques (e.g. chain of thought, role definition) are crucial for getting high-quality outputs from AI models
- AirOps platform enables non-technical users to build powerful AI workflows through a visual interface
- Integrating company data and following data usage policies is important when implementing AI solutions at Ramp

---

## Key Topics

### Introduction to AI Workflows

  - Marcel introduced the concept of AI workflows and their potential to automate complex tasks
  - Emphasized the importance of decomposing work into "jobs to be done" and thinking from first principles
  - Highlighted the value of delegation skills in the AI era

### AirOps Platform Overview

  - Demonstrated key features: Grids, Workflows, Knowledge Bases, and Variables
  - Explained different AI models available (GPT-4, Claude, Perplexity) and their strengths
  - Showed how to build workflows using a visual interface with drag-and-drop components

### Prompt Engineering Techniques

  - Covered best practices for writing effective prompts (role definition, context, task description)
  - Demonstrated advanced techniques like chain of thought prompting
  - Created a custom GPT "Prompting Teacher" to help participants improve their prompting skills

### Live Workflow Building

  - Built several example workflows including:
      - Page to FAQ generator
      - Support question responder
      - Call transcript to feature request analyzer
  - Walked through the process of iteratively improving prompts and outputs

### Ramp-Specific Use Case: AE Follow-up Emails

  - Collaborated with Dean to build a workflow for generating personalized follow-up emails after sales calls
  - Integrated multiple data sources: call transcript, LinkedIn profiles, company info
  - Demonstrated how to refine the output using Ramp's style guide and tone preferences
