## Key Topics

### Galileo's Business Goals and Target Audience

  - Targeting 3x revenue growth to $10M ARR by end of next year
  - Two key audience segments:
    1.  Thought leaders and Silicon Valley influencers in AI space
    2.  Developer community (shifting from ML engineers to broader software developers/PMs)
  - ICP: Global 2000 enterprises pursuing AI initiatives and digital natives with $20M+ funding and active AI projects
  - 250 named target accounts, refreshed quarterly with 15-80 new accounts

### Galileo's Product and Positioning

  - Positioned as an "evaluation and intelligence platform" for generative AI
  - Helps teams evaluate and monitor AI model quality, costs, and issues across development and production
  - Key modules: Evaluate (testing/iteration), Observe (production monitoring), Protect (proactive risk mitigation)
  - Differentiator: Out-of-the-box metrics without requiring ground truth data

### Competitive Landscape

  - Key competitors: Arize, Braintrust, LangChain (LangSmith), Patronus AI
  - "Good enough" alternatives: Databricks, MongoDB
  - Scale AI pushing aggressively into the space

### Current Content Strategy and Challenges

  - Focus on big thematic trends (e.g. RAD, agents) with multi-part blog series and gated e-books
  - Lack of systematic content funnel and limited organic search presence
  - Resource constraints: outsourced design, website updates, technical expertise time
  - Basic SEO issues discovered (e.g. improper sitemap setup)

### GrowthX Approach and Next Steps

  - Develop "mind map" of key themes and subtopics to build topical authority
  - Start with calibration articles in weeks 2-3 to nail quality before ramping up production
  - Explore improvements to site structure, cross-linking, and topic pages over time
  - Set up deeper dive sessions on publishing process, content strategy brainstorming
