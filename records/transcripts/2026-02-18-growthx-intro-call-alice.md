## Key topics

### Alice's brand evolution and content challenges
Alice moved from "Active Fence" (user-generated content, trust and safety) to "Alice" with a focus on Gen AI assurance for enterprises. The rebrand is recent, and brand awareness in the new category is still low. Their content must now speak to a deeply technical audience across industries — healthcare, financial services — with different pain points and lenses. Buying committees are large and slow. Oliver noted they're in A/B testing mode to figure out what content actually works.

Philip leads content creation across marketing pods, using AI tools to replace the first draft but with heavy human editing after. He wants content to sound like a person — not AI — especially because their product is literally a layer on top of AI systems. He was also concerned about centralizing workflows across pods and avoiding content that creates idle time on the agency side.

### GrowthX's engagement model
Tyler walked through the two-phase structure:
- **Phase 1 (strategy sprint, 8 weeks):** Keyword universe analysis, topic cluster planning, competitive content mapping, artifact development (personas, writing guidelines, company context). ~10 content pieces published by the end.
- **Phase 2 (growth execution):** Ongoing production at scale, ~1 article per day, mix of editorial, programmatic, integration pages, and SEO/AEO content.

Clients get an Airtable workspace for content scheduling (current week, next week, two weeks out). One main point of contact reviews drafts; Tyler prefers no more than 2–3 reviewers.

### AI workflow demo
Tyler showed the content pipeline live: agentic researcher → brief generation → drafting → validation against writing guidelines → fact-checking at the passage level → metadata optimization → image generation. Each step builds on the previous. Artifacts are customizable per client and include personas, writing guidelines (updated 25+ times with Engine as an example), and company/product context.

Tyler showed Augment Code (130 technical guides, 200+ tools/comparisons, 35 thought leadership pieces) and Lovable as reference clients. He noted GrowthX works with technical software companies and can create content with code snippets, technical depth, and cross-linking.

### LLM visibility and competitive tracking
Alice's technical audience uses Gemini, Claude, and ChatGPT heavily. Oliver asked how GrowthX's LLM tracking (Check That) differs from Profound and Scrunch. Tyler acknowledged he's still learning the Check That specifics and committed to a deeper technical comparison in the follow-up. Check That connects to Google Analytics, Search Console, and tracks LLM referrals and prompt visibility.

### Pricing and next steps
- Phase 1: $40k fixed fee. Phase 2: $18k/month (negotiable based on volume/speed).
- No free pilots. Phase 1 can be treated as the pilot — opt-out available at the end.
- Tyler committed to sending: media kit, explainer videos, example reports, competitive analysis, and the call recording (including Arman on the email thread).
