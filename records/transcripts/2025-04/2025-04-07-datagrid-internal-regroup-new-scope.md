## Summary

GrowthX internal team met to align on DataGrid's needs and develop a product-led growth (PLG) strategy as DataGrid shifts from outbound to self-serve motion, aiming to accelerate user acquisition and product-market fit. DataGrid is paying $40k/month and Tiago (founder) wants to leverage a new AI agent marketplace domain alongside existing efforts, with SOW items including webinars, case studies, and content still underexecuted. The team agreed to conduct internal strategy work, then schedule an external brainstorming session with Tiago to present a structured approach balancing DataGrid's ambitious needs with scalable GrowthX offerings, treating them as a strategic partnership similar to AirOps.

---

## Context

DataGrid is a B2B SaaS product in the agentic AI space, built on top of Tiago's previous company Torc (which focused on construction use cases). Tiago has a longstanding relationship with Marcel and GrowthX—Marcel was interim head of marketing at Torc and helped facilitate the pivot to DataGrid. DataGrid started with GrowthX in an Outbound engagement but has evolved into a broader retainer for $40k/month covering content, SEO, use case development, webinars, events, and now PLG strategy. The team faces a critical challenge: Tiago sees GrowthX as his extended marketing team (he has no internal marketing capacity) and is shooting many ideas at them, but the work is nascent—they're still figuring out product-market fit and target personas. Current work streams (outbound, programmatic content, webinar planning) haven't yet correlated with visible user growth, and Tiago is becoming more demanding about PLG motion, website redesign, and CRO. The relationship is also strategic beyond the immediate contract—Tiago was considering investing in GrowthX and could be a significant reference customer if DataGrid succeeds.

## Key Topics

### DataGrid's Current State and Needs

  - Early-stage SaaS startup seeking product-market fit; revenue unclear but paying GrowthX $40k/month
  - Founder (Tiago) has prior exits (Torc); considering investing in GrowthX
  - Primary metric: user growth; frustrated that outbound and content work haven't directly driven signups
  - Wants to pivot from outbound to product-led growth (self-serve onboarding, case studies, webinars)
  - Purchased AI agent marketplace domain as potential top-of-funnel play; concept still nebulous

### GrowthX's Current Engagement

  - Started as outbound-only; now mixed between outbound (paused), content, SEO, and use case development
  - Programmatic content blocked: freelancer building use case templates hit complexity limits; no reusable playbooks
  - SOW signed December 2024 includes 3 webinars, case studies, thought leadership posts on founder's LinkedIn ($15k/month allocated to Marcel's visibility work), website redesign, and CRO—none fully executed yet
  - Team uncertain if DataGrid has 1-2 people actually building, testing use cases, or just outsourcing everything to GrowthX
  - No clear feedback loop between marketing efforts and product learning

### Strategic Considerations

  - Kyle explicitly compared DataGrid to AirOps—wants to treat them as a strategic partnership (branded media, case studies, positioning) rather than a standard retainer
  - Tension between "do what they ask" (retain at all costs) vs. "push back on unrealistic scope" (maintain scalability); team leaning toward high-touch retention given $40k/month spend and Tiago's investor potential
  - Tiago expects GrowthX to hand-hold him through zero-to-one product positioning, but that's expensive and hard to scale to other clients
  - Concern: if GrowthX can't show visible user growth in 6-8 weeks, risk of churn or downsize to cheaper services

### Proposed Solutions and Next Steps

  - **Internal brainstorm this week:** Review SOW, map unexecuted vs. in-scope items, identify what GrowthX can realistically deliver vs. what Tiago owns; develop a 1-month strategy roadmap with specific use cases and messaging
  - **External brainstorm next week:** Present 3-5 concrete ideas to Tiago (e.g., user story format, product-specific LinkedIn content, webinar topics), set boundaries on scope, clarify resource allocation
  - **Potential near-term tactics** (contingent on strategy clarity):
      - User stories/case studies in tactical, product-focused format (not thought leadership yet)
      - LinkedIn content: product education + pain point posts (from both Tiago and Marcel), not broad founder advice
      - Webinars: use-case-focused, not generic product demos
      - Website: focus on core domain (datagrid.com) rather than side projects; improve use case section, add social proof
      - CRO: secondary priority until positioning is clear

### Resource Allocation

  - Jason (new hire) has freest calendar; best positioned to do upfront strategy work and own DataGrid relationship
  - Kyle willing to jump in as needed; wants all-hands-on-deck if committing to DataGrid as strategic partner
  - Team consensus: need to hire or reallocate if DataGrid scales to full-time resource (Jimmy mentioned this becoming a full-time job if they do lifecycle nurturing + full GTM)
  - No consensus on whether to staff dedicated person now (too early) or trial with Jason + others first
  - Concern: margins are high YoY; better to spend on retaining $40k/month revenue than leave money on table
