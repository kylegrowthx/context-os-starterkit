## Key Topics

### Competitive Analysis Results

Syndigo (half-billion-dollar company with 14,000 global brands) shows minimal SEO strength: 22,000 global monthly visitors with only 5 pages generating traffic out of 8,000 total pages. Most traffic is brand-specific customer landing pages, not educational content. Bazaarvoice demonstrates stronger SEO with 12,000 monthly blog visitors but focuses primarily on B2C content (Instagram size dimensions, influencer marketing) rather than B2B. Neither competitor offers direct topic inspiration for Firework's video-commerce positioning. GrowthX strategy: use their coverage gaps as validation that Firework can dominate narrower, ICP-targeted topics rather than compete on generic, broad keywords.

### Account-Based Content Strategy

Firework is shifting from organic, broad-keyword strategy to targeted, account-based content marketing. Target accounts include major footwear brands (Nike, Puma, Crocs, A6) and expanding into fashion, D2C, DTC, and CPG categories in the US. Content must engage multiple ICPs at each company: marketing teams, e-commerce teams, merchandising teams, digital transformation leads. Prateek tasked with research on: what these brands are searching for, how they're using video across channels (website, email, social), and how video-commerce fits into their omnichannel strategies. Expected to include white-glove collaboration with Firework's product team (Sam) to ensure quality.

### Traffic and Lead Generation Update

March closed at 7,000 visits. April on pace for 7,500-8,000 (4,000 achieved in first 15 days). 16 leads generated to date (8 new this week). Lead quality and conversion remain concerns: playbook downloads are high-volume, but conversion to demos and trials is low. Ritesh assigned to get engagement metrics from Celine on playbook follow-up emails to understand where people drop off. Team running weekly email campaigns to keep qualified prospects in pipeline for when their needs shift.

### Content Planning and Topics

10 new topics in development: 6 IGC-related (user-generated content), 4 on content creation and scaling. Strategy emphasizes narrow, ICP-targeted topics (even 2-3 searches with right ICP > 5,000 generic searches). Suggestions to incorporate "video" into article titles and explore content repurposing strategies (how to distribute Instagram/social content across email, SMS, website). Topics include scaling content without headcount, outsourced content creation, content sourcing, micro-influencer strategies, and how to leverage UGC/IGC across channels.

### Blog Categorization and Multi-Reference Tags

Current single-category system creates content organization problems: new categories end up empty with <3 related articles in "related posts" suggestions. Prateek has already categorized many articles and discovered that some topics require 2-3 categories (e.g., "Video Commerce for Fashion Brands" is both industry and product category). Cameron tasked with researching feasibility of multi-reference category field and impact on 700+ existing articles. Implementation expected to improve discoverability and content clustering. Monday meeting scheduled to review Prateek's 50-100 top article categorizations and finalize approach.
