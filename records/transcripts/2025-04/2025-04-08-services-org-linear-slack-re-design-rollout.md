## Overview

- Transitioning to Linear for project management, integrated with Slack for improved visibility and collaboration
- Restructuring Notion for clearer separation between client-facing and internal documentation
- Implementing a 12-week onboarding process for new clients with standardized milestones and tasks
- Expanding content manager (CM) layer to aim for 1:1 CM-to-client ratio, reducing reliance on freelancers
- Matthew Panzarino introduced as new Chief Content Officer, overseeing content quality and client satisfaction

---

## Key Topics

### Notion Restructuring

  - Client database renamed from clientOS, new column added for Linear project links
  - General handbook separated into delivery and build spaces for clearer organization
  - New "How we use Notion" and "How we use Slack" guides added to handbook
  - Important documents highlighted: "First 12 Weeks" and "Best Hire Mindset"
  - Personal spaces concept introduced for individual work and one-on-ones

### Slack Channel Organization

  - Channels prefixed with 'D' for delivery team, 'B' for build team
  - External channels without prefix for partnerships, 'D' prefix for active clients
  - Emphasis on using channels over DMs for improved transparency and knowledge sharing
  - New suffixes: '-project' for temporary projects, '-alert' for automated notifications

### Linear Implementation

  - Replacing previous task management in Notion
  - Strong integration with Slack for notifications and updates
  - Projects created for each client, following 12-week template structure
  - High-level task tracking encouraged, avoiding micro-management
  - Flexibility in view options (board vs. list) and notification settings

### 12-Week Client Onboarding Process

  - Standardized timeline with specific goals and tasks for each week
  - Focus on research and understanding client in first weeks
  - Calibration and polish instructions development mid-way
  - Performance tracking and regular client updates in later weeks
  - Template available in Linear for consistent application across clients

### Organizational Changes

  - Matthew Panzarino introduced as Chief Content Officer, overseeing content quality and client satisfaction
  - Kyle transitioning to sales and go-to-market strategy roles
  - Directors managing multiple pods, each typically overseeing two pods
  - Aggressive hiring planned for content managers and managing editors
  - Goal to reach approximately 1:1 ratio of customers to team members

### Hiring and Team Expansion

  - Focus on hiring content managers (CMs) and managing editors (MEs)
  - Directors involved in hiring process, leveraging their networks
  - Flexible hiring budget based on book of business and client demands
  - Permanent open positions for CMs and MEs due to rapid growth
