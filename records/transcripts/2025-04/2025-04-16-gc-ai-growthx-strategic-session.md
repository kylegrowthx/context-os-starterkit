## Context

GC-AI is an AI-powered legal platform built specifically for in-house counsel. The company is engaged with GrowthX on a content marketing engagement to expand organic visibility and establish thought leadership in the legal AI space. This strategic session represents an inflection point in GC-AI's content strategy: they've realized their initial approach (targeting broad legal AI keywords like "legal AI," "AI for lawyers") pulls in law firm traffic, which doesn't align with their ICP (ideal customer profile). GC-AI's founder is a former general counsel, and the team has strong conviction that in-house counsel have fundamentally different workflows, pain points, and needs than law firms — they care about approvals workflows, vendor negotiations, contract types, compliance specialties, and daily operational tasks rather than litigation or billable hours. GrowthX was brought in to help refocus the keyword strategy and content plan to dominate this niche audience rather than compete in the broader, more crowded legal AI space.

## Key Topics

### SEO Audit Results

- Core web vitals passing: Site loads under 2 seconds with excellent performance metrics
- On-page SEO issues identified: 55 pages with duplicate meta descriptions, some duplicate titles, few broken internal links (all easily fixable once CMS access granted)
- Organic keyword rankings: GC-AI ranking for ~200 keywords, majority on page 5 and below
- Traffic composition: 75%+ from branded search (GC.AI and variations), indicating heavy reliance on direct brand awareness rather than non-branded organic discovery

### Keyword Research and Content Planning

- **812 potential topics**: Identified through competitor keyword gap analysis of 8 competitors (Harvey AI, AI Lawyer, and others). Topics span AI, automation, contract management, and legal workflows.
- **Primary cluster**: AI and automation recommended as initial focus — hot topic, lower competition than legacy categories like contract management.
- **Workflow**: Airtable system with columns for topic title, search volume, keyword, assignment status, review status, and document links for iterative calibration.

### Target Audience Refinement

- **Strategic pivot**: Shift from broad legal AI keywords (e.g., "legal AI," "AI for lawyers") to in-house counsel-specific content. Alessandra emphasized that law firm traffic doesn't convert — GC-AI is built for in-house teams.
- **Focus areas**: Daily tasks, specialties (compliance, contract types, vendor negotiations, approvals workflows), and job titles (legal ops managers, product counsel, commercial counsel).
- **Topical relevance over volume**: Willing to accept lower search volume in exchange for audience alignment. Initial 6-month strategy focuses on high-conviction, niche keywords.

### Content Strategy Adjustment

- **JTBD-first approach**: Start with key personas (legal ops managers, product counsel, commercial counsel), map their daily jobs-to-be-done, then identify relevant keywords. This flips traditional SEO workflow.
- **Research sources**: Analyze In-House Connect and L Suite websites for trending topics and content gaps. Mine Cecilia's webinars and prompting workshops for real problems in-house counsel are solving.
- **High-opportunity content area**: Contract-related topics represent a rich vein — contract types, how-tos (e.g., "how to redline an NDA"), vendor negotiations, approval workflows.
- **Whale articles (10%)**: Layer in high-traffic keywords (e.g., "how to add a signature in Gmail" equivalent) for domain authority while maintaining 90% focus on niche, in-house counsel topics.
