## Summary

Daniel walked Renato through the two core product systems at GrowthX Labs: the AI workflow engine (a Temporal-based runtime with IDE for creating and executing 41+ workflows) and the content marketing platform (currently built on Airtable and Airops, with plans to unify five areas: production, strategy, context storage, client interaction, and performance tracking). The session focused on how the high learning curve for users is driving near-term UX simplification work while longer-term plans include opening workflows to non-technical users via an IDE and potentially exposing them as API endpoints or micro-apps. Daniel also discussed the small design team—currently just Renato—and ongoing interviews with Georgie and John Piedo.

## Context

Renato Valdes Olmos is a new designer joining GrowthX Labs, starting on the day of this meeting. This was his first substantive onboarding conversation with Daniel Lopes, the founder/leader of GrowthX Labs (growthxlabs.com). Renato was brought in as the company's first dedicated designer to help reduce the complexity of their products and improve the user experience. Daniel had already shared reading materials and connected Renato with other team members (Brad, George) for earlier onboarding sessions. The company operates two distinct but interconnected products: an AI workflow engine built on Temporal (used internally and by clients for custom workflows), and a content marketing platform (used to manage content strategy, production, and distribution). Both systems have become too complex for end users—especially content creators who lack engineering backgrounds—and GrowthX Labs is at an inflection point where design simplification is blocking further growth.
