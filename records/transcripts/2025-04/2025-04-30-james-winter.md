## Key Topics

### Marcel's Startup Journey & Growth Timeline

- Started August 2024 as side project while at DeepGram, initially charging $9K/month with 10-client model (30 min/week meetings, team does bulk work)
- Hit $1.5M ARR after Ramps founder meeting resulted in $27K/month contract
- Closed over $1M ARR in August alone without sales effort (organic LinkedIn inbound)
- Paused sales to scale operations, brought on co-founder Daniel (ex-IFTTT product engineering lead)
- Closed Series A in 30 days with $12M raised; announcing the week after call
- Now 60 employees, approaching $8M ARR

### Technical Architecture

- Co-founder Daniel experienced 1B+ monthly workflow runs at IFTTT, saw scale issues with low-code tools (Zapier ate their lunch because they delayed monetization)
- Built AI coding agent that generates workflows in code rather than low-code/no-code
- Code-based approach enables speed (minute vs. hour runtimes), observability, and prompt management at scale
- Infrastructure stack: AI agent → code generation → runtime layer → observability → learning layer that improves from every run and edit
- Recent metrics: 31,000 workflow runs and 445,000 activities in one month alone; each activity represents ~1 hour of expert work

### Current Service Model

- Outcome-based services (clients pay for results, not retainers)
- Work streams bundled by problem area (content, distribution, conversion; could expand to hiring, sales, operations)
- Full-service delivery: managing director, managing editor, hands-on team doing "grunt work" (publishing, graphics, staging)
- Team composition: insanely senior people (replicating expert-level thinking vs. intern-level work)
- Targeting marketing teams (have budget) rather than sales/RevOps; typically $9K-$27K+ per month engagement

### Platform & Roadmap

- Currently exploring custom workflows for existing customers (account research, prospecting, transcript processing, CRM enrichment)
- Building IDE (Cursor-like experience) so non-engineers can build/modify workflows
- Developing evaluation/learning layer to capture "messy middle" (iteration, research, decision-making) — more valuable than final output
- Exposed APIs as micro-apps: deep researcher, fact checker, style adapter, call analyzer (already running for 45+ clients daily)
- Plan to open self-serve platform (cursor-like) in couple months; Vista Equity Partners and other PEs inquiring
- Exploring royalties/revenue sharing with experts who help build workflows
- Launching AI-ledgrowth.com community for growth/marketing practitioners (less about tools, more about first-principles thinking and AI-native delegation)

### GrowthX vs. Low-Code Competitors

- Gumloop, AirOps, Make: require 100+ hours to build something decent, break after a month, become slow and outdated
- No operator overhead: low-code tools force every organization to maintain 2-3 AI engineers (unrealistic)
- GrowthX advantage: code-based, observable, learnable, fast execution (31k runs in a month = leverage at scale)

### Engine.com & Potential Collaboration

- James Winter: interim CMO, $100M+ revenue business travel platform, 12-person marketing team
- Market: B2B travel for construction, manufacturing, facilities, consulting — 80% cold-call driven (not search-driven)
- Recent expansion: consumer product opening wedding use cases
- SEO challenge: mid-market/blue-collar industries don't search for business travel; massive opportunity in content authority rather than product keywords
- Parallel: Surfside went from "nobody searches for HVAC software" to 60%+ organic revenue via authority content (contractor salaries, load calculators, route planning guides)
- James' portfolio: multiple portfolio companies beyond Engine with distinct pain points
- Next step: 30-min deep dive with Kyle (VP Growth, GrowthX first customer) before GrowthX Series A announcement
