## Overview

- Pricing model based on words/month for editorial content, pages/year for programmatic content
- Programmatic content strategy focuses on scalable, repeatable templates targeting high-variance keywords
- Content repurposing (e.g., from podcasts, webinars) is a valuable service offering and differentiator
- Early alignment on success metrics and attribution is crucial for demonstrating value to clients

---

## Key Topics

### Pricing and Production Models

  - Editorial content priced by words/month (e.g., 1500 words/article, 7-15 pieces/week)
  - Programmatic content priced by pages/year (e.g., 1200 base level at $12K/month)
  - Need for improved tracking of production vs. commitments
  - Opportunity to automate and streamline processes without over-complicating

### Content Management and Planning

  - Airtable used for organizing content by clusters, topics, and status
  - Potential need for calendar view to improve planning
  - Importance of taking a longer-term view on content strategy to align with client growth goals

### Programmatic Content Strategy

  - Targets high-variance head terms (e.g., per diem allowances by location)
  - Uses repeatable templates to scale content production efficiently
  - Can generate significant traffic but requires creative thinking to match business needs
  - Examples: Tray.io's integration pages, glossary pages for definitions

### Link Building and AI Outbound Campaigns

  - Recent focus on internal link building among client base
  - Exploring partnerships for quality backlinks
  - AI outbound campaigns currently paused due to logistical challenges

### Content Repurposing

  - Opportunity to expand services by repurposing existing client content (e.g., webinars, podcasts, PDFs)
  - Example: Creating articles from Galileo's podcast transcripts
  - Helps showcase value and differentiation to clients

### Measuring Success and Attribution

  - Importance of understanding client attribution systems early in engagement
  - Need to educate clients on holistic impact of content (e.g., overall domain authority)
  - Developing playbooks for navigating different client situations and metrics
