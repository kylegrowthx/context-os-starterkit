## Summary

GrowthX and Unstructured.io aligned on a comprehensive SEO and content strategy to address a critical gap: while Unstructured has strong brand awareness and 20+ million downloads, the company ranks poorly for organic search terms related to Gen AI data tools and techniques. GrowthX will create 30–70 optimized articles per month (up to 50,000 words monthly) targeting both technical champions and business buyers through calibration, topic clusters, and a production-ready style guide. Unstructured's new website launches in June–July on Sanity CMS, which will enable full content implementation and SEO optimization once live.

---

## Context

Unstructured.io is an open-source data platform for processing unstructured data (PDFs, images, tables, etc.) for AI/ML applications. The company has achieved strong product-market fit with 20+ million downloads and 20,000+ companies using the platform, primarily driven by direct traffic and brand awareness. However, there's a significant opportunity to capture organic search traffic: prospects searching for solutions to common data challenges ("How to extract tables from PDFs," "RAG for structured data," "Gen AI data pipelines") are currently landing on competitors' websites instead. GrowthX's role is to own the SEO conversation around Gen AI and data infrastructure by creating authoritative, conversion-focused content that targets both technical decision-makers (like Tarun, Unstructured's technical lead) and business buyers evaluating tooling. The engagement includes monthly content production, a dedicated content manager reporting to Jürgen Linde (GrowthX managing editor), and integration with Unstructured's sales and marketing teams for feedback loops.

## Key Topics

### Current Website Status

  - Built on Framer in 5 weeks, 2 years ago; was never meant to last this long
  - New site being built on Sanity CMS on phased rollout, expected launch June/July (Stefanie to confirm exact timeline in 2 weeks)
  - Current site cannot be modified for SEO optimizations (Framer access blocked due to billing dispute); GrowthX identified some broken links and can address in new CMS
  - Site redesign will address existing architecture issues and enable internal linking optimization

### SEO and Content Strategy

  - GrowthX performed keyword gap analysis vs. competitors (Reducto, Llama Index) sent by Unstructured; cross-referenced with Unstructured's internal keyword spreadsheet
  - Created topic clusters in Airtable grouped by buyer's journey stage (informational, commercial, buying intent), with search volume and competitor reference articles
  - Content targets two ICPs: technical champions (like Tarun—deep product knowledge, evaluating implementation) and business buyers (CTOs, heads of data—strategic fit, ROI)
  - Single articles resonate across both personas by leading with business outcomes and backing with technical details; buyers get high-level insights, champions understand implementation depth
  - Messaging framework: problems the company solves → how Unstructured fits the ecosystem → specific features/capabilities
  - Planned scale: up to 50,000 words per month (~30–70 articles depending on depth), with articles ranging 700–1,500 words based on topic complexity
  - Contract contingency: GrowthX has NDAs and non-competes in place; separate teams assigned to competing clients (e.g., if Reducto or Llama Index become GrowthX clients)

### Content Creation Process

  - Calibration phase: first article will be thorough and detailed to establish tone, voice, and quality bar; Unstructured will provide extensive feedback
  - Style guide created from Unstructured's reference articles: down-to-earth, accessible to both technical and business audiences, conversational rather than academic, includes specific examples
  - Airtable workflow: each topic links competitor reference article, keyword volume, search intent, and target ICPs; can mark topics as "Yes," "No," or "Ready to Start" for batch processing
  - Workflow built on Unstructured's ICP document, voice/tone guides, and feedback loops to prevent AI hallucinations (unlike AirOps, which Unstructured abandoned)
  - Tarun will own the final sign-off on calibration article and is now invited to all strategic meetings; he is the primary approval authority on content quality
  - Post-calibration: GrowthX will scale content production after incorporating feedback and updating style guide

### Integration with Sales and Marketing

  - New weekly marketing meeting starting after Unstructured's all-hands offsite; attendees: Stefanie Segar, Tarun, Ajay, Maria (Unstructured content/marketing team)
  - Sales team will share emerging themes, prospect questions, and problems they're hearing in the field
  - Tarun (and team) will review GrowthX-produced content against sales feedback: Is this resonating with what prospects ask? Does it address real buyer pain points?
  - Content flagging: Unstructured can recommend GrowthX articles for sales team to include in outreach emails and prospect communications
  - Feedback loop enables GrowthX to refine future topics and messaging based on ground truth from sales conversations

### GrowthX Operations

  - Dedicated content manager assigned to Unstructured account, reports to Jürgen Linde (GrowthX managing editor)
  - Separate teams/pods assigned to competing clients to avoid conflicts of interest; enforced via contract with NDAs and non-competes
  - Jürgen provides managing editor oversight: reviews content, ensures alignment, signs off before publication
  - Communication: Jürgen and team will monitor and respond to Slack questions and feedback daily; frequent communication encouraged to catch issues early
  - Next meeting: cancelled during Unstructured offsite week; regroup the following week for progress check
