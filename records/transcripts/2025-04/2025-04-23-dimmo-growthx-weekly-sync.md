## Key Topics

### Product Page Template and Content Structure

Prateek walked through the approved template structure: short summary on page load, with a "Read More" option that expands to show full details. Content flows: short summary → pricing section (if clicked) → features section → integrations section (notes 400+ apps available) → user reviews section. Each section keeps content concise, with expandable details to avoid overwhelming visitors with information. Troy approved the structure and content. Lucas committed to implementing the "Read More" functionality by the weekend. Prateek requested screenshots (or video) showing the CMS publishing workflow for different sections and lead-mode publishing process — Lucas agreed to send screenshots rather than video.

### Title Tag Optimization for Product Pages

Prateek presented 11 title tag template variations designed to handle both long and short product names (e.g., "Zoom Info" vs. "Salesforce CRM"). Templates emphasize Dimmo's key differentiator: instant demo videos available. Initially proposed A/B testing across all 500 product pages, but Lucas noted the site doesn't have enough traffic to run a valid A/B test. Troy and the team decided to select the best-performing template and apply it site-wide. After discussion, the team converged on "Watch Instant Demo + All You Need to Decide" as the primary template because it communicates both the demo value and bottom-of-funnel decision-readiness. Prateek to use ChatGPT to validate the title choice and confirm it's optimized for click-through in SEO context.

### Internal Linking Strategy

Prateek mapped a two-tier linking structure for product pages. First tier: links to all related articles about that specific product (e.g., "Maximizer features," "Maximizer alternatives," "Maximizer pricing"). Second tier: links to category-wide comparison and alternative articles (e.g., from a Zoom Info page, link to all CRM alternatives, all sales tools, etc.). This structure improves SEO by creating dense internal link networks within product silos and across categories. Example: visitor on Zoom Info page sees product-related links first, then related software alternatives. Prateek targeting 45 high-performing product reviews for cross-linking implementation. The strategy aims to boost internal link equity and improve crawlability.

### SEO Performance Update

Out of 7 recently published product pages, 25 keywords are already ranking in the top 10 positions on Google. Standout rankings include "Maximizer features" at #2, "Channel sales management software" at #3, and "Lucid features" at #4. Prateek noted that while click volume is still modest (some pages getting ~25 clicks/week), the ranking performance is strong early signal — the pages are discoverable and satisfying search intent. He emphasized this validates the content structure and template approach.

### Google Search Analytics Insight

Prateek clarified an important distinction: Google's ranking factors are informed by Google Search Console analytics (user behavior on the search results page itself), not site-level analytics like Google Analytics. When a user clicks link #1 on the SERP and then returns to click link #2, Google interprets this as: link #1 didn't satisfy the query. This signal influences future ranking decisions. Google has complete visibility into search results behavior across millions of queries, allowing them to understand which pages truly answer user intent. Dimmo can't directly leverage this, but understanding this mechanic explains why high click-through rate and low bounce-back rate matter for SEO.
