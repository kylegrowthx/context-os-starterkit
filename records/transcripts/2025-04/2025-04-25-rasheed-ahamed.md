## Summary

Rasheed Ahamed from AfterShoot and Kyle Gaudreau from GrowthX discussed scope reduction for their content partnership and explored contract flexibility. AfterShoot is undergoing significant internal changes—hiring a Head of Content, onboarding new team members, and potentially launching a free hobbyist product—and requested a 6-month post-pilot contract instead of 12 months to give them time to stabilize. ---

## Context

AfterShoot is a photography software company built for professional event photographers. The product solves a critical pain point for photographers—rapid image culling and editing—and has generated strong word-of-mouth and referral demand. GrowthX began working with AfterShoot in February 2025 on a 12-month engagement (with a 3-month pilot period) focused on content strategy and non-branded search visibility. This meeting was a mid-engagement check-in to revisit scope and contract terms. Rasheed heads content and marketing at AfterShoot and is managing a period of significant organizational change, including team restructuring, new hires, and strategic product decisions. Kyle Gaudreau from GrowthX is exploring how the partnership could evolve beyond traditional SEO work into integrated content that spans video and programmatic opportunities.

---

## Relevance

**To GrowthX Delivery:** AfterShoot's marketing mix is heavily skewed toward social, brand campaigns, referrals, and partnerships—not search. Non-branded search (organic and paid) represents <10% of their marketing spend and has minimal impact on bottom-line customer acquisition. Kyle sees an opportunity to expand GrowthX's value proposition beyond pure SEO by incorporating AfterShoot's existing video content and programmatic content opportunities to drive distribution across multiple channels. Rasheed is open to programmatic content strategy but cautious about commitment during internal reorganization.

**To GrowthX Business Development:** Contract flexibility request signals opportunity. AfterShoot is not dissatisfied with GrowthX work; they're requesting 6 months post-pilot (vs. full 12-month term) specifically to buy time for internal stabilization without abandoning the partnership. Rasheed emphasized intent to continue working with GrowthX and indicated willingness to extend if/when product launches succeed. If AfterShoot launches their free hobbyist product and it gains traction, Rasheed signaled potential to convert to a long-term "billion-dollar contract." Account health is solid; this is a structuring issue, not a satisfaction issue.

  - Social media (organic + paid): Major focus, very active on Instagram and Facebook
  - Brand campaigns: Events, sponsorships (e.g., $1M fund for event organizers)
  - Referrals/word-of-mouth: Strong due to product's impact
  - Partnerships: Influencers and ambassadors creating promotional content
  - Search (organic + paid): Minimal impact, 90% branded searches

  - Struggle to scale long-form video content production
  - Tried in-house production (team too busy) and agency production (lack of subject expertise)

  - Continued investment in video, but workflow still a work in progress

  - Explore ways to incorporate existing video content into SEO strategy
  - Consider programmatic content opportunities for educational purposes
