## Summary

Initial pitch meeting where Mike Bosserman (founder and GTM lead at Discern, a registered agent and state compliance automation platform) spoke with Marcel and Bruno (GrowthX) about content marketing services to compete with established players like CSC and CT Corporation. GrowthX outlined their approach: AI-powered editorial and programmatic content strategies, with expert editors (including a former TechCrunch editor) to build differentiated, high-quality content addressing the 200k untapped keywords in the registered agent space. Mike emphasized the need for accuracy in legal/compliance content and discussed his existing ~40 pages of manually researched, source-verified content. The team agreed to move fast — GrowthX proposed a detailed proposal for editorial ($9k/month) and/or programmatic ($12k/month) workstreams, with potential April onboarding if Discern commits quickly.

## Context

Discern is an early-stage (launched ~1 year ago, growing 20%/month) registered agent and state compliance automation platform targeting funds, PE firms, and growing tech companies managing multi-state operations. Mike positions Discern as differentiated from retail SMB-focused competitors (LegalZoom, ZenBusiness) by software-first automation capabilities, and sees its main competition as legacy players CSC and CT Corporation — both 125+ years old with $1B+ annual revenue and deep SEO authority. Mike came to GrowthX because competitor content (especially Harbor Compliance) has weak quality and there's an obvious opportunity to win market share through superior, accurately-sourced compliance content. This is a warm lead — Marco had been introduced to Mike by Fabrizio Barbini (a long-time GrowthX partner and advisor), and Mike's background in VC, finance, and go-to-market roles gives him credibility as a founder who understands the value of content marketing at scale.
