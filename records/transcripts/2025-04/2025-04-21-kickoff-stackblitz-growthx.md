## Context

StackBlitz is an AI-powered, in-browser code editor and deployment platform that lets users build full applications through natural language prompts. The company released its consumer product (Bolt) in October 2024, capitalizing on Claude 3.5 Sonnet's capabilities to create text-to-app functionality. This engagement marks the start of GrowthX's programmatic content marketing partnership with StackBlitz, exactly one year after the initial workshop that introduced the relationship. Mitchell Wright leads growth marketing and data for StackBlitz and is the primary contact for this engagement. The relationship involves a GrowthX team of content strategy, editorial, and SEO expertise (Dave Capone as director, Mariana Montezzana as managing editor, Fatima Tahir as content manager, Matthew Panzarino as chief content officer, and Kyle Gaudreau supporting sales and strategy) focused on helping StackBlitz acquire and convert non-technical users who see the tool as an alternative to traditional no-code platforms.

## Key Topics

### Project Goals & KPIs

  - Main goal: Drive paying subscribers through content
  - KPIs still being defined internally at StackBlitz; Kyle suggested measuring LTV and conversion rates at 30/60/90 days to understand which content types convert users
  - Target audience: Non-technical to semi-technical users (e.g., Wix/Squarespace users moving to text-to-app tools); "vibe coders" identified as emerging segment
  - Success metric: Conversion of users within 2-3 months of engaging with content

### Competitive Landscape

  - StackBlitz was among first "text-to-app" tools after Claude 3.5 Sonnet release; Bolt launched in October 2024
  - Key competitors: Lovable, Replit, V0 (which was previously front-end only), and emerging tools from Firebase AI and other large incumbents
  - Product differentiation challenging as capabilities converge (all using similar underlying models); StackBlitz's web container technology is technical differentiator but less marketable to non-technical audience
  - Actual differentiation for target audience centers on ease-of-use, outcome focus (what users want to build, not technical features), and prompt quality improvements
  - Content opportunity: Mariana suggested positioning StackBlitz as helping people move from traditional no-code tools (Wix, Squarespace) to AI-powered alternatives; positioning around launch/monetization opportunities for builders

### Content Strategy & Workflow

  - GrowthX to create programmatic content (~1,200/year); StackBlitz to handle editorial (new hire Mallory starting today)
  - Calibration phase: GrowthX will create 2-3 sample articles for Mitchell's review; Mariana already extracted style guide from StackBlitz office hours webinar capturing conversational, enthusiastic tone
  - Primary content: How-to articles (e.g., "build a CRM for lawyers") targeting specific industries and use cases
  - Secondary content: Prompt templates directory for finance, CRMs, and other verticals
  - Competitor treatment: Cautious approach; content will focus on StackBlitz's strengths rather than direct comparisons, though specific head-to-head comparisons (e.g., "StackBlitz vs. Lovable") are acceptable if approached thoughtfully
  - Workflow: GrowthX creates content in documents → delivered for client review → iterative feedback → scaled publishing once calibrated (content can go to CMS or be delivered as CSV/XML feed)

### Technical Setup

  - StackBlitz needs to implement a headless CMS; Strapi or Sanity recommended (WebFlow not preferred); Kyle and Kyle discussed pros/cons—Sanity and Strapi roughly equivalent, both better than Contentful which over-engineers experiences
  - GrowthX to advise on CMS architecture, SEO best practices, and site structure; currently StackBlitz manages content through GitHub which slows workflow
  - Options for content delivery: (1) GrowthX provides CSV/XML feed that StackBlitz ingests, (2) GrowthX publishes directly to CMS, or (3) StackBlitz publishes after review
  - GrowthX to provide wireframes/mockups of programmatic page templates showing multi-page architecture (not just single article pages) to guide CMS and SEO strategy
  - StackBlitz domain architecture: Bolt.new is new subdomain for all content; StackBlitz.com domain will continue but essentially retire

### Additional Opportunities

  - **Hackathon support:** StackBlitz has a hackathon end of May; Matthew offered to help build programmatic project showcase pages with descriptions, screenshots, and videos for each submitted project; potential content and SEO opportunity
  - **Early access program (EAP):** Matthew flagged EAP as significant revenue driver (can drive millions in conversions) if implemented with reciprocal value prop (early access to features in exchange for prompt contributions or project submissions featured in directory)
  - **Influencer and paid ad amplification:** Kyle mentioned StackBlitz running influencer and paid campaigns; GrowthX content can support and amplify these initiatives
  - **Open source positioning:** Bolt.dy is open source version of Bolt (independently developed, separate codebase); potential content opportunity around open source community and positioning
  - **Scope flexibility:** Kyle emphasized GrowthX is open to exploring ideas beyond initial SOW if they drive value (example: podcast transcription workflow creating structured articles for another client)
