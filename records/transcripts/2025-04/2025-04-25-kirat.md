## Key Topics

### Webflow's Competitive Landscape

  - Concern about bottom-up disruption from AI-generated website builders like Lovable
  - Webflow started in a similar space as Squarespace, targeting beginners
  - Questions about where users "graduate" to after outgrowing AI-generated solutions
  - Stackable and Bolt (growthx.ai customers) experiencing rapid growth but uncertain long-term strategy

### Webflow Team Structure and Properties

  - University team is separate, manages its own content and production
  - Marketplace team (under Kirat) handles supply, templates, pricing, and behavior
  - Marketing team owns SEO for marketplace
  - Developers.webflow.com is owned by Kirat's team (Anushree)
  - Integration pages on university site are outdated and can be moved

### SEO Strategy and Collaboration

  - growthx.ai to focus on developers.webflow.com for quick wins
  - Potential to expand into headless CMS and MCP server-related content
  - Need to balance efforts with existing marketing team and agency relationships
  - Aim to show tangible impact within 2-3 months (e.g., increased pages, improved rankings)

### MCP Server and AI Initiatives

  - Webflow is developing MCP server capabilities
  - Opportunity to create content around use cases and be early in the space
  - Instrumented to track professional usage for gathering case studies

### Documentation Site Structure

  - Need to plan thoughtful structure for new content to avoid "spaghetti" organization
  - Balance between technical documentation and SEO-driven content
