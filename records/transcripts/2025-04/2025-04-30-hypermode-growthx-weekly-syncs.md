  - Three-step optimization: (1) update meta descriptions, (2) add internal links to related pieces, (3) adjust content when necessary
  - Exploring related high-volume keywords (e.g., "model agnostic") to cross-link and surface low-volume content

### PR Pitching Strategy

- Mara shared best practices from her experience as a tech reporter; recommendations based on what reporters actually care about:
  - **Headline (most critical):** Must be newsworthy and reportable — something the reporter could have written themselves. Consider using AI to brainstorm multiple angles and subject lines.
  - **Newsworthy angle:** Answer "why now?" and "why will our readers care?" Most pitches miss this bar. Must be new in some way.
  - **Narrative framing:** Tie to industry trends reporters can cover as their analysis. Examples: AI agents moving from backend to individual contributors (power shift), or positioning as democratizing force. Themes like labor and power dynamics resonate.
  - **Supporting materials:** Day-in-life demo, specific use cases, early data, compelling founder quote. Keep it tight — reporters get hundreds of emails daily and won't deep-dive unless hooked by headline.
  - **Tightness matters:** Reporters need to quickly understand what the story is, why it matters, and why their audience will care.
- Timing constraint: Hypermode's "drip launch" process from engineering creates friction with newsworthy timing windows.

### Thought Leadership Content Strategy

- Conceptually different from (but complementary to) SEO content — dovetails nicely but can diverge
- Publish originally researched, technically deep, well-structured content addressing real industry challenges
- Source unique insights and "hot takes" from internal experts and network; focus on topical themes where company has deep expertise and playbook
- Leverage notable figures in network for expert guides (borrowed authority) and unique insights
- Keyword optimization is table stakes; pair with active promotion across LinkedIn and email drips to build authority and organic inbound
- Can work with lower domain authority by layering direct audience touchpoints alongside organic visibility
- Reference peer and large tech company examples for structure and formatting, but start with company's own zone of interest and expertise
