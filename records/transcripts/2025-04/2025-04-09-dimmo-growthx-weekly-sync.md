## Summary

GrowthX and Dimmo refined the structure for Dimmo's product review pages, moving from a long-form linear design to an accordion-based layout with summarized headers and expandable "Read More" sections for detailed content. The team agreed on specific H2 headers, the importance of unique elements like screenshots and user experiences to improve EAT signals for Google rankings, and a plan to migrate 108-110 existing articles into category-specific directories with internal linking at the bottom of each page.

---

## Context

Dimmo is a B2B SaaS company in the product review space, building a platform (or content site) that publishes detailed product reviews and comparisons. The team includes Troy Munson and Lucas Swartsenburg on the Dimmo side, who work with Prateek Gupta and the GrowthX team. This is a weekly sync between the client and GrowthX's content and technical team to discuss the implementation of product review pages — specifically, how to structure content to balance comprehensive coverage for SEO with user experience and load time. The meeting was called because the current long-form page structure was creating usability issues, and the team needed alignment on a new approach before Dimmo's writers create content at scale.

## Overview

- Proposed a new accordion-based structure for product review pages with expandable sections (H2 headers with "Read More" buttons) to balance comprehensive content and user-friendly design
- Agreed to create summarized content for each section with "Read More" buttons that expand to full detailed information, preventing JavaScript overload that harms SEO
- Emphasized the importance of adding unique elements like product screenshots, user experiences, and video timestamps to improve EAT (Expertise, Authority, Trustworthiness) signals and avoid AI-generated content penalties from Google
- Planned to implement internal linking sections at the bottom of each page (product-specific links and category-specific links) to improve site structure and SEO across 108-110 articles

## Key Topics

### Product Review Page Structure

  - Prateek presented a comprehensive structure for product review pages, including company overview, pricing, features, integrations, and competitive analysis
  - Troy suggested restructuring the content to make it more digestible, proposing expandable sections for each topic
  - Agreed on creating a template with H2 headers and short summaries, with detailed content accessible via "Read More" buttons
  - Discussed adding table of contents-style jump links to improve navigation

### SEO Considerations

  - Prateek emphasized the importance of comprehensive content for ranking, while balancing user experience
  - Discussed the need to avoid appearing as AI-generated content to prevent potential Google penalties
  - Agreed on the importance of adding unique elements like screenshots and user experiences to improve EAT (Expertise, Authority, Trustworthiness)

### Content Generation and Management

  - Discussed the challenge of sourcing authentic screenshots and user experiences for products
  - Proposed using AI to identify key features from video content and automatically capture relevant screenshots
  - Planned to migrate existing articles into specific directories and implement internal linking strategies

### Author Profiles and Dynamic Content

  - Lucas clarified that author profiles are currently set statically to Troy
  - Discussed the relative importance of dynamic author profiles for SEO, with Prateek noting that Google can detect artificial author creation

### Internal Linking Strategy

  - Prateek suggested adding two sections of internal links at the bottom of each article:
    1.  Links to articles about the same product
    2.  Links to articles in the same category (e.g., features, alternatives)
