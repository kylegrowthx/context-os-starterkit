## Key Topics

### Company Structure and Roles

  - Directors (like Stewart and Elvis) typically lead two pods, each with a managing editor
  - Model evolving toward content managers one-to-one on accounts for deeper engagement and specialization
  - Pods ideally handle 4-5 accounts, but account complexity matters—e.g., Adventures group is technically one client but covers three Mexico travel websites in both English and Spanish, reducing the account count per director
  - Managing editors and content managers benefit from hybrid profiles: enough SEO experience to guide strategy, but strong content creation skills to refine output; Kyle notes this beats pure SEO-first hires who lack content depth
  - Jürgen (just hired) brings strong client-facing skills and some SEO knowledge; Elvis brings a hybrid background with prior independent clients and AI exploration experience

### Current Accounts and Challenges

  - Elvis manages three accounts: Smith AI, exec, and Superhuman
  - **Smith AI** (urgent priority): Programmatic SEO progress stalled; editorial is strong (40+ articles in backlog), but Matthew (marketing lead, former Devstudio background) is frustrated with lack of programmatic momentum and questioning ROI. Kyle and Elvis identified a gap—feature pages and industry pages lack connective content (e.g., appointment booking for lawyers across variants). GrowthX offering free link-building as goodwill. Image implementation is slowing publication; Kyle advocating for MVP approach (80% is better than waiting for perfection).
  - **Superhuman** (early-stage, 3-4 weeks in): CEO is brilliant, builds custom tools, excited about partnerships. Focus is showing momentum and demonstrating value early.
  - **exec** (positive and steady): Strong relationship, no major concerns; CEO proactive and collaborative

### Content Creation Process

  - Airtable is the operational foundation: two levels for each client workspace (internal—draft space, not client-ready; and shared—client-facing, polished)
  - Workflow: Intro call → research document → company brief → kickoff doc preparation
  - Article generation uses "grids"—chained workflows where initial outputs feed into subsequent workflows (e.g., keyword research → brief generation → article output)
  - Iterative methodology is core: gather client feedback on initial content, identify patterns (e.g., messaging too surface-level, needs deeper persona research), adjust inputs/parameters in the workflow, rerun, test hypothesis
  - Goal: Build repeatable recipes—codified processes that solve for client preferences programmatically so manual intervention decreases over time
  - MVP mindset: "80% out the door beats 100% never shipped"; better to publish and refine than wait for perfection

### Onboarding and Training

  - Training materials exist (Fathom videos, Circle platform resources) covering client workspace setup—Kyle to ensure all links are provided
  - Hands-on experience critical: Stewart to set up GCAI's workspace before the Monday kickoff to understand the process firsthand
  - Key skills to master: assignment creation workflow and article generation grids; Kyle notes this is more about understanding the "levers and dials" (template structure, parameter options) than deep technical prowess
  - Future tooling (mentioned in all-hands): context-aware chat interface for Airtable, which will further reduce friction
  - Learning approach: Mix of new (GCAI) and existing accounts to see different engagement stages and what's working/not working
