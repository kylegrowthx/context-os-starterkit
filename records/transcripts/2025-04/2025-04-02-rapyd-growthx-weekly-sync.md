## Key Topics

### AI-Assisted Content Creation Process

  - AirOps grid-based workflow: keyword input → company name + AI context description (300-400 words max) → polishing instructions with example paragraph
  - AI generates assignment brief with target audience and keyword analysis from SEMrush API (like pulling a SEMrush content brief)
  - Outline generation via ChatGPT (better control than AirOps; Aerofs outlines often too broad for bottom-of-funnel keywords)
  - Research stage uses Perplexity to research brief points and generate research document with references and links
  - Title generation and outline editing (always edited before proceeding)
  - Article draft generation using Claude (better at long-form content than ChatGPT; produces longer introductions that get shortened)
  - Post-processing applies polishing instructions to first draft
  - Human feedback stage: editor reviews article, adds feedback (e.g., "shorten intro to 150 words, remove expert quotes"), feedback column generates final article with revisions
  - Internal linking via Aerofs using Rapyd's sitemap database; generates workflow that finds link opportunities but links often tacked on awkwardly and require manual anchor text repositioning
  - Final review by Aida before sending to client

### Content Performance Metrics

  - Mark Stiltner's primary HubSpot dashboard tracks:
      - **Contacts created**: most important metric — people coming to website, filling contact forms, or downloading gated content (not signups, which don't convert)
      - **Organic search traffic**: split by contact form submissions and gated content downloads (GDPR sometimes captures organic in direct search)
      - **First-page blog post conversions**: blog posts that are landing page in user journey and later lead to form submission; shows which blogs drive conversions and how many
      - Currently long-tail strategy with majority of conversions from single views of granular blog posts
  - **Gated content reporting**: tracks which gated reports are downloaded; system automatically inserts relevant gated content CTAs based on blog post topic categories
  - **Future funnel extension**: Marc Winitz wants to involve marketing operations team (Mikhail) to track impact through sales accepted leads and sales engaged leads, and get granularity on which blog posts drive which gated report submissions
  - **Channel partner considerations**: Much of Rapyd's pipeline goes through channel partners; need to account for this at direct merchant level
  - **Conversions vs. first-touch attribution**: Not everything is first-touch; looking at broader marketing influence

### KPI Development and Reporting

  - **Phased KPI approach (Jakub Rudnik)**:
      - **Month 1**: Operational metrics — focus on publishing cadence, are we shipping?
      - **Month 2-3**: Keyword growth and impression growth (easy to track with existing website setup)
      - **Month 6+**: Traffic growth and conversions
  - **Methodology**: Break down total keyword volume by publishing volume/week and build in ramp timing; benchmark against similar clients with comparable volume and keyword difficulty; project traffic using waterfall model where month 1 content stacks each month, creating compounding traffic growth (3-, 6-, 12-month projections)
  - **Conversion modeling**: Apply historical conversion rates from best-performing existing blog posts to projected traffic, adjusting for outliers; test conversions on new content
  - **Tracking by category**: Tag content by product/audience to track which category/audience performs best and understand what works for Rapyd
  - **North Star metric discussion** (Marc Winitz): Single metric to check Monday morning for weekly directional view — not a deep dashboard, but one number that says "how are we doing?" Jakub clarified that North Star evolves: early stage is publishing, month 2 is keyword growth, month 6 is conversions
  - **Looker Studio dashboard**: GrowthX to set up week-over-week performance tracking, accessible to Rapyd team anytime
  - **Future: Full funnel reporting**: Involve marketing operations team (Mikhail) to track impact through sales accepted leads and closed won deals — start with basic traffic → conversion model, then extend to show marketing influence further down pipeline
