## Overview

- GrowthX is building a scalable content creation and distribution platform (Content OS) while delivering high-quality content services to clients
- The company aims to elevate the "floor" of content creation, allowing good performers to execute at expert levels
- GrowthX is developing workflow engines and AI models to capture expert knowledge and automate processes
- Future plans include scaling the core business, opening up the platform, and validating the model in new domains (e.g., recruiting)

---

## Key Topics

### GrowthX's Business Model and Vision

  - Two-pronged approach: content services delivery and platform building
  - Inspired by companies like Tiny, aiming for a portfolio of businesses
  - Core focus: helping brands think like publishers and leaders like influencers
  - Building an AI-native company that transforms expertise into executable code

### Content Creation Challenges and GrowthX's Solution

  - Content creation is expensive, requires specialists, and is time-consuming
  - Agencies often struggle with profitability and quality
  - GrowthX offers comprehensive content strategy and execution for $9,000-$18,000/month
  - Leverages AI and expert workflows to deliver high-quality content efficiently

### Platform Development and Data Collection

  - Building workflows to capture expertise, processes, and patterns
  - Creating a "Content OS" that learns and improves over time
  - Instrumenting the stack to track inputs, outputs, and feedback loops
  - Developing task-specific, domain-specific, and company-specific AI models

### Current and Future Work Streams

  - Editorial and content refreshes
  - Programmatic SEO
  - Social distribution and ghostwriting
  - Interview-based content and case studies
  - Graphics and creative production
  - Lead magnets and newsletter creation
  - Sales enablement and conversion support

### Expansion and Future Plans

1.  Scale the Content OS to $15-20 million in revenue
2.  Open up the platform (e.g., fact-checker, deep researcher tools)
3.  Validate the core model in new domains (e.g., recruiting)

### Brand and Product Strategy

  - Consider separate branding for different products/services
  - Potential for a holding company structure
  - Building in public and maintaining transparency
