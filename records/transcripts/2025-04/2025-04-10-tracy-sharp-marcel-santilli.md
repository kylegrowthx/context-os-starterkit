## Key Topics

### Kaptio's Business and Challenge

Kaptio is a B2B SaaS platform specializing in multi-day travel bookings and management. Clients include major luxury brands like Intrepid, Rocky Mountaineer, Disney Vacations, and Uniworld Cruises. Deal sizes are substantial: £400k ARR per contract with implementation costs of £600k-£1M. However, the sales cycle is exceptionally long (6-9 months on average), which Tracy Sharp (VP Commercial) views as a critical bottleneck. Key buyers are CTOs, CIOs, and technology directors—senior technical leaders who must evaluate Kaptio's complex feature set (handling 30-40+ elements per multi-day itinerary, legacy system integration, documentation requirements). Kaptio currently has mid-to-large customers but aspires to land enterprise-level reference accounts to unlock larger deals.

### Tracy Sharp's Strategic Vision

Tracy has been at Kaptio for six months and was brought in specifically to accelerate new customer acquisition and improve sales velocity. She came from a background in high-velocity B2B SaaS (sell-to-market model, 2-3k GBP/month deals, 3-4 day sales cycles) and recognizes that Kaptio's 6-9 month cycle is both a liability and an opportunity. She sees content and brand authority as potential catalysts: if Kaptio becomes the recognized authority in travel tech, CTOs and their influencers will find them during research phases rather than late in buying journeys. This would shift the dynamic and potentially compress sales cycles. Tracy has budget allocated and decision authority, though internal alignment with her executive team will be required.

### GrowthX's Approach and Track Record

Bruno Santilli outlined GrowthX's approach: treating clients like media companies (not product companies), identifying jobs-to-be-done and pain points in the target audience, mapping competitive mindshare, and publishing high-quality editorial content. His team experience is deep: Dave ran SEO and content for TripAdvisor and built Fanatics to $600M; Stewart ran SEO for NerdWallet (hundreds of millions of visitors) and Walmart/Nordstrom; Matthew ran TechCrunch for 10 years. Customers include Reddit, Webflow, and Ramp (fintech, valued at $13B). At DeepGram, Bruno drove 24x traffic increase, resulting in 4x increase in branded search volume and revenue inflection. He then developed AI-powered workflows that automate persona development, jobs-to-be-done mapping, and content planning, allowing GrowthX to deliver at scale.

### The Editorial Program Proposal

GrowthX recommended their Editorial Program: 50,000 words/month of targeted, original content; weekly website audits; content staging and image creation; and full A-to-Z content management. Pricing: less than 10 cents per word (cheaper than hiring writers individually at $1-2 per word). The program includes a dedicated managing director and editor assigned to Kaptio. The 12-week kickoff process involves understanding audience, defining strategy, auditing the current site, mapping content gaps, and then publishing at high velocity. Emphasis on both SEO optimization and AI visibility optimization (30-40% of referral value now comes from AI search results for their customers).

### AI Visibility as Strategic Advantage

Bruno highlighted that content strategy must optimize for both traditional search rankings and AI engine visibility. When CTOs search for solutions to complex travel tech problems, they're increasingly getting AI-generated summaries and recommendations. Clients like Ramp, Sentinel-1, and others with massive enterprise motions are seeing 30-40% of their referrals from AI sources. Kaptio's competitors may be neglecting this channel entirely, creating a window for first-mover advantage in AI-powered visibility for travel tech.
