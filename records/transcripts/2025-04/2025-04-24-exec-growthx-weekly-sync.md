Elvis conducted competitive analysis on Exec's core space and identified multiple high-opportunity keywords:

- **Alternative-based articles** — systematic approach: "best apps/software for learning & development," "best apps for sales enablement," "best apps for sales training"; Elvis generated 100+ options in ChatGPT to prioritize
- **Strategy constraint:** Avoid Trainual (it's a paying client and partner); can link to but don't want direct competitive positioning

### Sales Compensation Calculator Asset

Sean's sales comp calculator is a sophisticated interactive tool that far exceeds simpler competitor offerings. It allows users to model salary, commissions, rates, quota ramps, and guarantees—designed to help both salespeople calculate earnings and leaders model total cost. The calculator ranks #8 for a related keyword despite competitors having cruder tools. Opportunity: create supporting blog content around the calculator (similar to how competitors embed simple percentage calculators with surrounding content) to drive more traffic and conversions.

### Video Strategy

Sean has 2 existing videos ready (product walkthrough and marketing video) that can go live immediately. Long-term vision: create article-specific demos for each high-performing piece (e.g., custom video for the sales roleplay article, custom video for types of selling). Videos will be hosted on Vimeo with auto-update capability, so future product improvements can be swapped in without changing article links.

### Content Production and Reporting

- Bi-weekly reporting cadence preferred over weekly; Elvis building Looker Studio dashboard but noted it's atrocious for non-SQL users (Sean uses Metabase internally for his own SQL queries)

### AI and Design Demonstration

Sean demoed Claude's ability to generate React applications with real-time rendering. Started with a basic sales calculator ("sales amount: 500, commission percent: 5, calculate"), then iteratively improved it with prompts like "make the design pop." Claude reinterpreted that as visually appealing design and added vibrant colors, better visual hierarchy, and subtle animations. The demo showed how simple prompts to AI can dramatically improve UI without requiring a designer or engineer—even "make it pop" works as a design direction.
