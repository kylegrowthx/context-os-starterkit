## Key Topics

### Editorial-First Content Strategy

Marcel outlined GrowthX's philosophy: help brands become publishers by building trust with audiences, not just selling products. The approach starts with deep research into audience jobs-to-be-done and pain points, then develops a unique "publication" voice and style guide. Marcel emphasized a 12-week process (kickoff, research, style guide, content calibration) that results in a "Content OS" — a repeatable system for generating and managing content at scale. The goal is to ensure the client is "the best answer" to every question their audience asks, across both search and AI engines.

### AI Workflows and Scaling

GrowthX uses AI to augment expert editors and writers in collaboration, not replace them. Marcel showed an example workflow for superhuman (employee productivity topic) where a "deep researcher" generates research questions, finds sources, and combines results — all guided by expert context and managed editors. The team has assembled managing directors (e.g., Jacob from G2, Dave from TripAdvisor/Fanatics, former TechCrunch Chief Content Officer) to ensure quality. For Abnormal Security, they generated 1,000+ potential content assignments across clusters like email security, complete with search volume and keyword difficulty metrics.

### Content Volume and Execution

Marcel recommended starting with 100,000 words/month (~50 articles), a mix of new content and refreshes. The team takes on all "grunt work" — staging, publishing, graphics creation — to free up the client's internal team. For Ramp, this meant saving their SEO/content team 2 hours/day by handling CMS staging alone. GrowthX also provides weekly results updates, strategy calibration meetings, and continuous optimization of published content.

### Technical SEO and Site Health

Marcel outlined a pragmatic approach: conduct an initial audit to understand the site's state, then identify quick wins vs. big projects (e.g., domain migrations, CMS migrations, outdated sections). Rather than hand off a long list to the client's web dev team, GrowthX prioritizes actionable items for their team to own directly if they have CMS access. They use specific workflows for gap analysis (comparing current rankings to potential if ranking #1-3 on all relevant keywords), cross-linking, and content refreshes (complete rewrites vs. targeted updates for product changes, CTAs, stats, formatting).

### Outreach-Specific Integration

Outreach uses Craft CMS. In week 1-2, GrowthX conducts a CMS walkthrough and publishing process training via screen share, recording the session. They then calibrate on one piece of content until Outreach is happy with the voice, style, and quality, with GrowthX triple-checking or drafting before publication if needed. Once ramped, GrowthX publishes directly; some security-conscious clients prefer a staging → production workflow. Marcel mentioned GrowthX's familiarity with Outreach's platform (Kyle and others have managed Outreach implementations and RevOps teams), reducing onboarding risk.

### Content Repurposing and Expansion

Marcel emphasized starting with editorial because it becomes the foundation for everything else. Once the editorial base is strong, they can repurpose content into long-form pieces, lead magnets, newsletters, podcasts, or vice versa (enriching articles with podcast interviews). Deanna asked about this specifically, and Marcel confirmed: start editorial, get the foundation right, then layer repurposing on top. If you skip editorial and start with repurposing, you lose quality and effectiveness.

### Addressing Outreach's Bottlenecks

Hadassah (SEO) asked about the biggest bottleneck to organic growth at Outreach — bandwidth. She noted they have strategy and ideas but execution takes time, and her team is stretched across other initiatives. She's also a "pragmatic SEO" advocate, aware that the landscape is shifting with AI. Marcel drew a parallel to Ramp's situation: they needed to respond fast to market changes (new products, Super Bowl ads) and refresh hundreds of pages. GrowthX's model lets Outreach's team focus on "connecting the dots" while GrowthX handles execution.
