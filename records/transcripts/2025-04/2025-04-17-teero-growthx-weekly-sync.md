## Key Topics

### Programmatic SEO Strategy for Job/Salary Data

Jakub and Nate discussed a mid-to-long-term play: creating location-based pages for dental job and salary information across the top 85 US metro areas plus suburbs. The strategy uses three data sources: Teero's internal marketplace data (which shows actual temp premiums and full-time rates), Indeed scraping for baseline salary and job count data, and public job sites. The team agreed on a 20% baseline temp premium — the difference between temporary and full-time hourly rates — which can be adjusted per location. To avoid oversupply markets, they'll filter for locations with population thresholds around 50k-90k for suburbs, with a possible USPS database as a clean source for city/location lists. Nate emphasized that programmatic pages don't need custom content if the underlying data is original and useful; Google has indexed 3,000+ pages per day for similar sites without ranking penalties as long as content answers user queries better than competitors.

### Content Performance Update

Week 14 (ending April 17) was Teero's biggest traffic week yet, with a 17% increase over the previous week. The March Google core update rolled out throughout the month and had a mostly positive impact: 150 keywords improved rankings while only 35 declined — a strong signal. Aida noted that AI-generated content myths are overblown; the sites losing traffic are those with low-quality content, not helpful AI-assisted content. The team published 8 new pieces this week and has 4 more scheduled for the following week.

### Content Strategy and Topics

The team is building out four content clusters: insurance-related (10-15 articles published so far), W-2 and 1099 topics (taxes, worker's comp, narrower sub-topics), patient-focused content (likely around patient education), and dental management topics. Nate mentioned insurance is a second product Teero is actively working on, so insurance articles help build product reputation and are easier to write quickly. W-2 articles require deeper domain expertise from Nate and go through more editorial back-and-forth, but Rachel is now writing follow-up W-2 articles with better context, which should reduce iteration cycles. Aida will add more granular W-2/1099 angles (e.g., W-2 vs. 1099 tax comparison, worker's comp under each classification) to build out a pillar cluster.

### Conversion Optimization and Tracking

Aida discovered conversion panels on one of Teero's blog pages that Frank had implemented unintentionally. The current setup places CTAs only at the bottom of articles. Nate wants to move beyond raw traffic metrics to understand conversion by source — specifically whether clicks drive dentist signups, office signups, or neither. Aida plans to request the ability to place conversion panels strategically throughout articles, not just at the bottom, because most readers don't read to the end. For high-intent readers who do reach the end, Nate suggested offering something premium (e.g., "Book a call" or "Get a personal quote") rather than a generic CTA. Nate will send examples of good vs. bad conversion block placements so Frank knows what to build.
