## Key Topics

### Vercel Engagement Overview

Cody (ex-Redis head of growth, now at Vercel) brought the deal. Vercel is building a new content section of the site — Cody has full editorial control over it, separate from the main site that goes through the CEO. The engagement is structured as:
- **Months 1–6:** $20k/month, editorial + programmatic content
- **Months 7–12:** $30k/month as Vercel transitions budget away from their current agency
- **Volume:** 25–100 programmatic pages/month
- **No opt-out clause** — 12-month term with staged payments

### Content Priorities: Gateway + Index

Marcel narrowed the initial scope to two things:

1. **AI Gateway enrichment** — Vercel's gateway index page lists all supported AI models but each model page has only one sentence. Goal: enrich every model page with latency, token usage, input/output specs, cost, and answers to common user questions. Analogy: the MCP catalog GrowthX built for other clients, or the vulnerabilities database. Target: rank #1 for model-specific search queries (e.g., "what is the latency of [model]").

2. **Index / editorial section** — Modeled after Anthropic's content index. Cody created a `/i` section as a backdoor around the CEO's editorial control over the main blog. Still needs to be high quality — Marcel was emphatic that "no slop" is the bar. Study the CEO's posts and every published piece from Vercel to calibrate voice and standards.

### Quality and Speed Expectations

Marcel was direct: this cannot be mediocre. He called out a recent example of a Sprint team client where the blog content was poorly written and convoluted — "I don't know what I'm reading within five seconds — that's not good." With Vercel, quality failure is not recoverable given the CEO's standards and the visibility this account brings. At the same time, speed is everything — Cody's explicit ask. Plan: set up the workspace, ingest all public Vercel docs, study the CEO's Twitter, build the best possible artifacts before the kickoff so the first deliverable looks like GrowthX moved "crazy fast."

### Stakeholder Relationships

- **Dan Fine** — Senior Technical PMM; has the CEO's respect; key person to make happy. Marcel connected with him on LinkedIn.
- **Eric Dodds** — Technical Marketing Content Engineer; 5 months at Vercel; former head of product at RudderStack. Marcel connecting on LinkedIn.
- **Cody** — Internal champion; connected to CC (CMO, former Redis CMO). Strong bridge.
- Marcel wants to be included in the kickoff and the first few weeks of delivery.

### Brex Phase Two

Brex verbally committed and passed their opt-out window. Invoice date is March 11 for a full upfront annual payment (~$400k). Open question: whether the Capital One acquisition changes anything on their payment side — they may ask to switch to quarterly. George is leading delivery with Kirkland on interactive tools/templates and George Main on the Resources Hub redesign. Final data access from Brex still pending to complete the proprietary card data pipelines.

### Ops Issues: Andrea + Follow-Ups

Marcel flagged several operational drops:
- Single email follow-ups sent and then never followed up (e.g., a missed intro to 11 Labs)
- Calendar meetings sent without context about who the person is or what the meeting is for
- Calendar blocking and meeting acceptance not consistently managed
- Salary data posted in a public Slack channel (flagged as a serious mistake)

George is meeting with Andrea to address these. He proposed building an automated system that checks calendar entries against existing rules, flags violations, and surfaces them in a Slack message for Andrea and Mia to clear. Marcel endorsed the approach.
