## Summary

GrowthX (Bridget and Jason) connected with Madison Schott, a data engineering technical writer formerly at Airbyte, to explore how to build a sustainable model for reviewing AI-generated technical content at scale. Madison shared her process for reviewing technical articles (2-5 hours per piece, charged at $0.75-$1/word alongside her writing rate) and emphasized that technical expertise built over years is what truly drives value, making automated or junior-writer review insufficient. GrowthX is considering building a network of domain experts across different technical verticals (data, DevOps, frontend) to offer deeper technical review services to clients, though cost and expertise availability remain significant constraints.

## Context

Madison Schott is a data engineering and analytics writer with deep hands-on experience in tools like Python, R, SQL, and data pipeline platforms. She previously worked as a technical writer for Airbyte, a data integration platform, where she produced approximately one article every two weeks (10-20 hours per article) and worked closely with Airbyte's DevRel lead Ari (who is also on GrowthX's call list). Bridget McGillivray recently joined GrowthX from Airbyte where she worked on the overall team and is familiar with how Airbyte structures technical content. GrowthX is an AI-augmented growth agency that partners with technical companies (Airbyte, Gitpod, Webflow, Strapi) to own segments of their content and SEO strategies. The core challenge: GrowthX uses AI plus writers to create content at scale and lower cost, but their writers typically lack the technical depth to catch errors in code snippets or identify outdated framework concepts. This meeting was specifically about exploring whether Madison might serve as a technical reviewer for high-volume content projects, or whether partnerships with specialists like her could fill the gap.

  - Technical expertise (e.g., 10 years of experience) is a significant factor in content quality

  - Madison's review process: 2-5 hours per article, depending on setup requirements
  - Involves thorough documentation review and hands-on code testing
  - Charges same rate for reviewing as writing ($0.75-$1 per word)
  - Value comes from unique skill set, experience, and perspective, not just time spent

### GrowthX's Content Strategy

  - Aiming to produce 20-30 technical articles per week for clients
  - Considering hiring domain experts for each technical area to review content
  - Challenges in scaling this approach due to high costs and limited availability of experts
  - Exploring balance between SEO-driven content and providing genuine value to readers

### Madison's Writing Approach

  - Focuses on making technical concepts approachable and easy to understand
  - Identifies gaps in existing documentation and addresses real user needs
  - Combines company documentation with external resources and personal insights
  - Balances client-requested topics with self-generated ideas based on market value

### Collaboration Opportunities

  - Madison open to potential collaboration, typically works with direct clients
