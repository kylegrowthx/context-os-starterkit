## Context

Passbolt is a B2B open-source password manager that serves IT/DevOps teams, particularly in government and military sectors. The company recently closed Series A funding while remaining profitable pre-investment. Their key differentiator is encryption at the individual password level (rather than vault-level like 1Password), combined with open-source code, self-hosting options, and cloud SaaS alternatives. They're currently targeting SMB IT/DevOps teams (3-10 person teams) but are seeing increasing traction with government and military buyers who value on-premises deployment and security. Passbolt hired Sandro Groganz as CMO in February 2025 after 19 years in marketing consulting focused on commercial open-source vendors. Sandro joined specifically to solve their top-of-funnel problem: despite excellent conversion rates once leads are in-funnel, Passbolt lacks sufficient lead volume. Previous broad-targeting paid ad campaigns ("best password manager") failed because prospects didn't realize the product was teams-only. The company is now shifting from product-centric to customer value-centric messaging, conducting customer surveys to understand what problems Passbolt solves, and planning to hire a product marketing manager and invest heavily in influencer partnerships (which have delivered 300% ROI). GrowthX is a B2B content marketing agency that recently closed Series A and is now building its sales and delivery teams. Kyle Gaudreau, VP of Customer Growth, was GrowthX's first customer when leading growth at Homebase; he joined GrowthX as head of client services and recently shifted to sales. Bruno Santilli, also on the sales team, joined in January from 16 years in B2B sales in oil and gas.

## Key Topics

### Passbolt's Current Situation and Challenges

  - Recently closed Series A funding, profitable before investment
  - Main challenge: not enough leads at top of funnel, despite good conversion rates later in funnel
  - Previous paid ad attempts were ineffective due to broad targeting (e.g. "best password manager")

### Passbolt's Product and Market Position

  - B2B password manager for teams, with unique encryption at individual password level
  - Open-source with self-hosting option, appealing to government/military clients
  - Primary use cases: centralizing passwords and secure password sharing within teams
  - Current customer base mostly SMB IT/DevOps teams (3-10 admins)

### Passbolt's Marketing Strategy and Goals

  - Aiming for 30% increase in captured leads over next 1.5-2 years
  - Focus on organic growth, content marketing, and influencer partnerships
  - Hiring a product marketing manager and conducting customer surveys
  - Shifting from product-centric to customer value-focused messaging
  - Exploring content expansion on website and YouTube influencer collaborations

### GrowthX's Offering and Approach

  - AI-powered content creation workflows combined with human expertise
  - Focus on editorial and programmatic content, tailored to customer journey
  - Platform allows flexible integration with various AI tools (e.g., Perplexity Pro)
  - Team includes experienced SEO and content marketing professionals
  - Emphasis on iterative process, continual calibration with client feedback

### Potential Collaboration Considerations

  - Passbolt's European market focus requires less "awesome" and more grounded messaging
  - GDPR compliance is crucial for any new tools/partnerships
  - Budget starting at $9k/month, pending CEO approval and demonstration of value
