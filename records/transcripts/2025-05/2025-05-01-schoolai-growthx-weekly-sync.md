## Key Topics

### Content Performance Recovery

Site dropped last week during Easter and spring break (kids out of school), a pattern across all GrowthX accounts. Strong recovery this week with 5,700 more clicks and 24K more impressions overall. GrowthX content specifically is down in clicks but up 1,700+ impressions, showing week-to-week volatility is normal. "AI homework helper tools" is the standout performer with 300+ more clicks than any other page.

### AI Homework Helper Tools as Strategic Topic

The surge in searches for "AI homework helper tools" signals a major opportunity. Rather than targeting students directly (SchoolAI doesn't have a direct student funnel yet), the content should target educators looking for ways to empower their students with AI-assisted homework help. This positions SchoolAI's platform as a tool teachers can use to provide better support, not a direct student app.

### New Product Experience and Space Pages

Complete product rebuild with foundational tech improvements and new UX. All spaces will now have rich pages with learning outcomes, standards, agendas, and visible steps. New interface features an AI assistant called "Dot" and a power-up stage where teachers and students can access different tools (translation, podcast creator, physics simulator, etc.). Each space gets a unique, SEO-friendly URL.

### Indexing Strategy for Spaces

Product team will build a manifest system to control which app.schoolai.com spaces index. Starting point: Nikki provides CSV of high-performing spaces to preserve. Dave provides list of app spaces with meaningful traffic. Non-performing spaces will be no-indexed rather than 301'd (preserves user access for small audiences still using them). Remixed spaces will use canonical tags pointing to the original space to avoid duplicate content penalties. Future: automated system based on launch frequency and quality scores.

### Website Platform Transition

SchoolAI has hit Framer's limits (can't build tables, table of contents, or custom layouts easily). Plan is to hire a top developer and let them choose the CMS (Webflow, Strapi, or others). Dave mentioned GrowthX has worked with Strapi and can make an introduction. Colton has a coffee meeting with one potential developer next week. Migration can't happen until after September (buying season ends when school year starts). GrowthX ready to support the migration and preserve all SEO work.
