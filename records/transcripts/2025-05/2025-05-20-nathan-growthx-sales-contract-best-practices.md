## Overview

- GrowthX is transitioning from a rigid 12-month contract model to a more flexible, agency-like approach with an initial paid calibration phase
- The company faces challenges with over-promising and resource allocation due to rapid sales growth and platform development lag
- Nathan advises embracing the pilot/sprint model if it fits the market, standardizing offerings, and framing proposals as longer-term engagements with de-risking elements

---

## Key Topics

### GrowthX's Contract Evolution

  - Shifted from a 12-month term with defined deliverables to a more flexible, agency-like model
  - Previous 90-day opt-out pilot period caused issues with customer commitment and value demonstration
  - New approach: 8-week paid calibration phase, followed by an ongoing engagement
  - Challenges include over-promising and allocating engineers to individual engagements, impacting velocity

### Market Positioning and Product Offering

  - Current offering resembles an AI content/growth agency rather than a traditional SaaS product
  - 15 engineers working on platform development, but customer-facing product not yet available
  - Content creation primarily focused on SEO, which requires 6-12 months to show significant impact
  - Debate on necessity of custom work for each engagement vs. standardized offerings

### Contract Structure and Sales Strategy

  - Nathan suggests embracing the pilot/sprint model if it aligns with market demands
  - Recommends standardizing 3-4 month sprints to cover 80% of use cases, enabling scalability
  - Advises framing proposals as 12-15 month contracts with de-risking elements (e.g., initial paid calibration phase)
  - Emphasizes importance of clear success criteria, adoption cadences, and regular check-ins during pilot periods

### Lessons from Outreach's Growth

  - Nathan joined Outreach at 10 employees, left at 275+ employees
  - Witnessed evolution from flexible, "any-which-way" contracting to more structured approaches
  - Highlights importance of standardization and scalability in contract models

## Action Items

- Establish clear success criteria and check-in schedules for pilot periods
- Consider how to balance custom work requests with standardized offerings to manage resource allocation
- Continue refining the contract model based on market feedback and internal capabilities
