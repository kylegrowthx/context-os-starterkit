## Summary

Kyle Gaudreau and Jason Gong walked Brian Magida through GrowthX's proposed content strategy for Biologica's September launch, addressing his concerns about concrete deliverables during a 6-week strategy sprint. They discussed how to balance quality expert review with speed, leveraging Biologica's internal dossiers for fact-checking, and monitoring how Biologica appears in LLM responses as an emerging traffic source. ## Context

Biologica is a women's health supplement brand preparing for a late September launch with a narrow focus on women's hormonal health and natural compounds for specific life stages. Unlike broad supplement brands like AG1, Biologica positions itself around clinically-backed dosages for specific cohorts—e.g., addressing perimenopause symptoms like brain fog, mood swings, and sleep disruption rather than positioning as a catch-all wellness product. The company is preparing substantiation dossiers (36-page regulatory documents with source materials) for each product to ensure claims are medically defensible.

This meeting came after an initial sales conversation where Brian Magida flagged concerns about GrowthX's timeline: they wanted to know what concrete deliverables would come from a 6-8 week strategy sprint. Kyle leads GrowthX's sales side (recently transitioned from delivery) and was catching up after vacation, while Jason Gong leads execution and brings deep expertise in content strategy, editorial voice, and AI visibility optimization. The team proposed a 6-week strategy phase to establish brand voice, validate content opportunities, build review workflows, and produce initial pieces before moving into execution—but Brian wanted more visibility into what he'd receive upfront.
