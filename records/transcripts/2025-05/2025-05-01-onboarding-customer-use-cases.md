## Summary

Kyle Gaudreau led an onboarding session for new GrowthX team members, walking them through the company's service offerings and customer examples. GrowthX currently delivers three core work streams—editorial (50-100k words/month), programmatic SEO (templated, location or industry-based scaling), and link building—with editorial serving as the foundation for all client engagements. Kyle highlighted examples from existing customers like Yurko (use case pages), Galileo (podcast episode expansion into blog content), and Ramp (per-diem location pages) to illustrate how GrowthX customizes approaches for each client's unique needs and competitive position.

---

## Context

GrowthX is an early-stage B2B content marketing services company founded around the principle of establishing content as the foundation for customer success. Kyle Gaudreau, VP of Customer Growth, shared that he was GrowthX's first customer before joining as an employee and leading both the sales and customer services sides. This session served to onboard a cohort of new hires into the company's service model, work streams, and philosophy of driving attributable revenue—not just traffic. The audience included content managers, implementation specialists, and other operational roles who will be supporting client deliverables.

---

## Relevance

- **To GrowthX Delivery:** Editorial content (longer-form, not constrained to blogs) is the foundation for all engagements; programmatic SEO requires careful template design and cross-functional collaboration; link building is shifting from AI-powered outbound email to partnership-focused approaches due to recent email domain handling challenges and post-Google algorithm changes that penalize broad-topic websites and link farms.
- **To GrowthX Business Development:** Lead magnet/gated asset creation is an early opportunity to expand beyond core content work; potential future offerings include interview-first content (case studies, customer stories) and distribution/repurposing workflows (e.g., webinar content optimization); all customers currently require either editorial, programmatic, or both—no exceptions.
- **To GrowthX Sales & Positioning:** Content strategy informs how GrowthX helps customers think beyond keyword-driven blog content to creative angles and custom site sections; customization and thoughtful portfolio work (like Galileo's podcast integration) are key client differentiators and excitement drivers.

## Key Topics

### GrowthX's Strategic Focus

- Initially focused on content to establish a foundation for customer engagements
- Content strategy informs distribution channels (SEO, social, email)
- Emphasis on driving attributable revenue, not just traffic
- Exploring lead magnet creation (e.g., gated assets) to enhance value proposition

### Current Work Streams

- Editorial: 50-100k words/mo (7-15 articles/week), not limited to traditional blog posts
- Programmatic SEO: Templated approach for scaling content across variants (e.g., Ramp's per diem pages)
- Link building: Crucial for improving domain authority, exploring partnership-focused approaches

### Editorial Content Strategies

- Flexible approach beyond standard blogs (e.g., Yurko's use case pages)
- Creative content repurposing (e.g., Galileo's podcast episode expansions)
- Emphasis on understanding client needs and creating unique value propositions

### Programmatic SEO Insights

- Ideal for industry-specific or location-based content at scale
- Requires careful template design and cross-functional collaboration
- Becoming more common, but processes still being refined

### Link Building Challenges

- Moving away from AI-powered outbound email outreach due to complexities
- Exploring partnership-focused approaches and inter-client link sharing
- Caution against low-quality link farms and broad-topic websites post-Google algorithm updates

### Potential Future Work Streams

- Interview-first content (case studies, customer stories)
- Content distribution and repurposing (e.g., webinar content optimization)
- Outbound strategies deprioritized due to recent challenges in email domain handling

## Action Items

- **GrowthX Team:** Refine processes and workflows for programmatic SEO to increase efficiency and operational maturity
- **GrowthX Team:** Develop legitimate, high-quality partnerships for link building; continue vetting partnership approaches to avoid low-quality link farms and broad-topic websites post-Google algorithm changes
- **GrowthX Team:** Explore and validate potential future work streams including interview-first content (case studies, customer stories) and distribution/repurposing workflows for webinar and other content assets
- **All delivery teams:** Continue to customize and innovate content approaches for individual client needs, looking beyond keyword-driven blogs to creative angles and unique site sections
