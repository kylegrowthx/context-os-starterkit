## Summary

GrowthX introduced 14 new team members across content, operations, and leadership roles spanning Nigeria, Brazil, Croatia, South Africa, Portugal, and the US. Marcel Santilli presented a major shift in GrowthX's engagement model: replacing traditional long-term contracts with an 8-week "Strategy Sprint" — a $20,000 paid scoping phase that delivers ICP validation, editorial roadmaps, competitor analysis, voice guides, and benchmark content before either party commits to a 12-month execution phase. The model addresses recurring problems: misalignment between sales promises and delivery, over-focus on word count instead of outcomes, inconsistent quality expectations across clients, and the challenge of assigning new customers to newly hired teams. GrowthX is also pausing programmatic SEO sales until platform readiness (June 16 target).

## Context

This is GrowthX's bi-weekly all-hands meeting, held during a period of rapid expansion. The company has hired 14 new team members in recent weeks and is navigating the operational challenges of scaling delivery while maintaining consistent service quality. Over the past several months, GrowthX has experienced misalignment between what sales promised clients and what delivery could realistically provide — leading to churn, inconsistent content quality, and frustrated teams managing conflicting expectations. Bridget McGillivray ran the meeting as host; Marcel Santilli presented the strategic solution to these problems. The meeting functions both as company culture (introducing new hires) and strategic realignment (rolling out a new client engagement framework that is intended to reduce friction and improve outcomes).

---

## Relevance

**To GrowthX Delivery:**
- Strategy Sprint model establishes a gatekeeping function for new customer engagements, preventing poor-fit clients from entering the delivery pipeline and reducing churn.
- New specialized sprint team (Marcel, dedicated engineer, managing editor, content manager) removes context-switching in onboarding; pods receive customers with finalized strategy documents, not raw proposals.
- Standardized deliverables in scoping phase (ICP validation, editorial roadmap, competitor brief, voice/style guides, 3-asset benchmark content) create reusable templates and consistent handoff quality.
- Pause on programmatic SEO sales eliminates capacity pressure on engineering until internal platform is production-ready (June 16 onboarding deadline).
- Retroactive application of Strategy Sprint deliverables to existing customers improves alignment and reduces mid-engagement friction.

**To GrowthX Business Development:**
- $20,000 Strategy Sprint fee (potentially $30,000 for complex deals like Augment Code) generates upfront revenue during scoping and shifts risk from GrowthX to mutual evaluation.
- Mutual opt-out clause filters unrealistic expectations early; if client doesn't continue to Phase 2, GrowthX doesn't waste 12 months on poor fit.
- 12-month execution term with upfront payment preference improves cash flow and contract stability.
- About 8 proposals currently in pipeline ready to close and kickoff first Strategy Sprints; early learnings will refine the model.

**To CheckThat:**
- Voice and style guides created during Strategy Sprints are "AI-ready" — designed for use in prompting ChatGPT or Claude, directly relevant to CheckThat's visibility positioning.
- Strategy Sprint positioning emphasizes that clients can try platform-only paths after scoping; creates conversion funnel for CheckThat as a lower-cost alternative to ongoing services.

  - Inconsistent content quality expectations across clients
  - No clear process for GrowthX to opt out of poor-fit engagements
  - Challenges with new team members handling new accounts simultaneously

### Strategy Sprint Approach

  - 6-8 week paid ($20k) scoping period before long-term engagement
  - Deliverables: ICP/persona validation, editorial roadmap, competitor analysis, voice/style guides, benchmark content
  - Mutual opt-out option at end of sprint
  - Specialized team led by Marcel to handle strategy sprints
  - Aims to provide value even if client doesn't continue long-term

### Post-Strategy Sprint Execution

  - 12-month term with upfront payment preferred
  - Standardized "packages" (small, medium, large) for editorial work
  - Flexible scope for non-material changes within agreed framework
  - Improved handoff to execution teams with clear strategy and expectations

### Platform Development

  - Internal platform onboarding target: June 16th
  - Pausing programmatic SEO sales until platform readiness
