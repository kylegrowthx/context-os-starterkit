## Overview

- LLM-generated content needs improvement in human readability and narrative structure
- Current workflows (e.g., AirOps) have limitations in fact-checking, linking, and tone calibration
- Transition to sprint model will lead to more customized, client-specific workflows and polishing instructions
- Need for better fact-checking processes, especially for clients like InterVest dealing with health-related topics

---

## Key Topics

### Content Quality and Readability

  - LLMs tend to rely on "bullet point sandwiches" and single-sentence paragraphs
  - Need for varied paragraph structures and narrative flow to improve readability
  - Importance of critical thinking in expanding thin sections (e.g., weather information for travel content)
  - Moving away from word count as a metric; focus on "as long as it needs to be and then slightly shorter"

### Workflow and Tool Usage

  - AirOps used for some clients (InterVest, HomeBase, Tribe) but has limitations in research, structuring, and linking
  - ChatGPT preferred for certain clients (e.g., Seat) due to faster calibration and familiarity with client needs
  - Assignment brief and feedback column in content grids have been helpful improvements
  - Need for custom workflows and polishing instructions per client, to be developed during sprint phases

### Client-Specific Challenges

  - Seat: Moving from short-form, scannable content to longer-form, more digestible content
  - InterVest: Programmatic content generation requires significant post-editing (about 1 hour per piece)
  - Reddit: Ongoing issues with multiple rounds of manual edits, potentially exceeding contract terms

### Future Improvements

  - Develop fact-checking workflows, especially for health-related content (e.g., InterVest)
  - Improve linking capabilities in content generation tools
  - Retcon improvements for existing clients based on new client onboarding processes
  - Design transition process for handing off clients from sprint teams to regular pods
