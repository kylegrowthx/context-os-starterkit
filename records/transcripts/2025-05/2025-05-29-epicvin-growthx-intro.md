## Summary

Kyle Gaudreau introduced GrowthX's AI-powered content creation platform to EpicVIN, a multi-vertical car services company (vehicle history reports, auction sales, auction brokerage) that experienced a significant traffic drop after the August 2024 Google update. GrowthX pitched an 8-week strategy sprint ($20K) followed by ongoing content production at $15K/month (range: $9K–$30K), emphasizing their hybrid AI-human approach, expert team (former TechCrunch editor, Fanatics SEO leader), and focus on content quality over automation for automation's sake. Alex Listopad raised concerns about AI content exclusion from search results and the lack of early-exit options in the 12-month commitment, so both teams agreed to a follow-up call to review case studies, competitive analysis, attribution methodology, and AI visibility opportunities.

---

## Context

EpicVIN is a Miami-based company operating multiple car-related services: vehicle history reports (direct competitor to Carfax and AutoCheck), live auction sales for corporate auctions (partnered with insurance auto auctions), and vehicle classified listings. The company added a massive vehicle classifieds section to their site just before the August 2024 Google core update, which introduced millions of new keywords and product pages. Since the update, they've experienced a sustained organic traffic decline that their internal SEO team has been unable to diagnose or reverse. This is the first exploratory call with GrowthX, initiated because EpicVIN saw case studies of content marketing wins on GrowthX's marketing materials and wants to understand whether a similar content strategy could help recover and grow their traffic. The relationship is new—this is positioning and discovery.
