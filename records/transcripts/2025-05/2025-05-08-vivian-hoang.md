## Key Topics

### Webflow's Organizational SEO Landscape

Webflow operates SEO across multiple distinct surface areas: the main marketing site (product pages, resources library, blog, enterprise pages), University (for existing users), Help Center (documentation), and Marketplace (templates, Made in Webflow showcase, integrations, and libraries). Vivian's primary goal is bringing in new business customers, so she focuses on the main marketing site, templates, and Marketplace. University and Help Center are managed separately with their own goals (retention and support).

### Primary Target Personas

Webflow has historically targeted two main personas: service providers (freelancers and agencies building client sites on Webflow) and in-house teams (marketers, designers, developers building their company's marketing site). Developers are a more ambiguous segment—they can be marketing team members building sites, but also users extending Webflow via custom code or building apps. The developer track that GrowthX is pursuing is broader: developers who might use Webflow as a headless CMS alongside other platforms like Contentful or Strapi.

### Developer Content Strategy

Content targeting developers breaks into two buckets: top-of-funnel awareness content (e.g., "best headless CMSs," server-side rendering deep dives) and product-specific guides (e.g., "how to use Webflow APIs"). The first should live on the blog to reach non-Webflow users; the second belongs in documentation. Short-term approach is to publish all developer content on the existing Webflow blog for speed (avoiding design and domain approval delays). Long-term, Webflow may move developer content to a dedicated Webflow.dev domain, but that initiative isn't yet approved internally. Key risk: cannibalization between webflow.com and webflow.dev.

### Integrations Content Strategy

Integrations content targets top-of-funnel non-Webflow users searching for "how do I integrate [tool] with [platform]"—not just Webflow users. GrowthX should explore organic keywords like "Stripe CMS integration" where Webflow could be discovered as an alternative. Strategy is to publish immediately on university.webflow.com/integrations while pursuing design approval for a permanent dedicated integrations experience. This decouples content launch from design workflows.

### AI Search Optimization (AEO)

Webflow isn't currently measuring AI visibility—Vivian attempted to build an internal dashboard but found it impractical because AI model outputs vary based on model version, user login state, and conversation history. GrowthX's AEO dashboard will use third-party vendor data (likely Scrunch) to provide directional metrics on key prompts. Measurement should be strategic and selective since vendors charge heavily by prompt volume. Key opportunity: getting Webflow cited in headless CMS conversations alongside Contentful and Strapi (currently it doesn't appear). Strategy emphasizes earned media distribution (Reddit, YouTube, LinkedIn) over on-site citations, since AI models actively filter out self-promotional branded content.

### Cross-Functional Collaboration and Approval Processes

Webflow's SEO strategy requires heavy cross-functional alignment. When launching new initiatives (like integrations pages), Vivian pulls in product, PMM, and marketing stakeholders. Design resources are limited (3-4 internal designers servicing all of marketing), so new page designs require design review and roadmap sequencing. Vivian acts as the SEO domain expert, facilitating these conversations and flagging SEO concerns. Current constraint: the company is in heavy build mode for a conference in September, limiting available resources for new design work.
