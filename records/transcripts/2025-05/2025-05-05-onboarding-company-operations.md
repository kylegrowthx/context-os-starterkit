### Company Structure and Organization

Bridget explained GrowthX's two-org model that was recently implemented (about a month prior). The **Build org** (prefix B/) includes non-client-facing functions: operations, engineering, sales, and growth teams. Dig is joining Jason's growth function within Build. The **Deliver org** (prefix D/) is client-facing and produces customer deliverables and outcomes — Mariana is joining the Deliver org under Matthew. The Slack reorganization with B/ and D/ prefixes helps clarify which org owns which channels. Bridget noted that the two orgs interact significantly because the Deliver team provides feedback to engineering about what needs to be built.

### Onboarding Day 1: Account Setup

Mariana had already completed most of Day 1 during the morning: setting up Notion account, Linear access, Slack, and other company tools. Bridget offered to send a formal checklist to ensure nothing critical was missed. Witt is the go-to person if anyone hits tool access issues. Mariana should also grab time on Matthew's calendar for an intro call to discuss urgent projects and how to divide responsibilities.

### Onboarding Week 1: Core Materials and Key Meetings

Both Dig and Mariana have scheduled sessions during Week 1: a business overview session with Marcel (described as a 2-hour workshop covering what GrowthX does, why it exists, and why customers value it — new hires consistently report this as the most helpful material), a customer overview session with Kyle (sales lead) on Friday, and an engineering/CTO intro with Alice (who is supporting Daniel, the co-founder and CTO). There are also handbook documents to review. Bridget noted that some materials may be redundant and she's planning to audit them. Both new hires should take notes on the onboarding process and provide feedback on what's working, what's confusing, and what could be improved.

### Culture and Expectations

Bridget emphasized that while Alice and Woods (experienced EAs) can explain team norms, Dig and Mariana — both highly experienced — should feel empowered to bring their own ideas and suggest improvements. The company is changing rapidly and values fresh perspectives. She stressed balance: learn how things are done, but don't assume that's how they should be done.
