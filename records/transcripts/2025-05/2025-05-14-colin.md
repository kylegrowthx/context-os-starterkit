## Key Topics

### Scope Negotiation: From Execution to Strategy

Colin pushed back on GrowthX being positioned as a pure content execution vendor. He wants GrowthX to function as strategic advisors on site architecture, content hierarchy, and SEO approach. Jason acknowledged the tension: the initial SOW was vague on what "strategy" meant, and he's been in a holding pattern trying to publish content while the structural questions go unresolved. Agreement: GrowthX should draft a high-level site architecture recommendation and lead a strategy session with Colin, Jason, and Vivian before scaling content to thousands of pages.

### Current Documentation Problems

Webflow's developer docs are fragmented and failing at self-service onboarding. Integration pages live in the university (designed for agencies, not developers), are orphaned from the main developer docs, and have no connecting funnel (e.g., "Do you need a CRM? Payment processing? Here's how to integrate"). Documentation lacks structure around audience personas (app developers vs. agency developers vs. enterprise integrators). The platform team created a narrow persona around app developers, but the docs need to serve a much broader set of integrators.

### Content Strategy and SEO Approach

Colin wants to move away from programmatically generating 6,000 pages without strategy ("dead repository that is just there to keyword stuff"). He advocates for a pillar-page strategy: define core pillars (e.g., "Integrations"), support them with guides, recipes, and implementation help, and establish clear audience pathways. Jason noted pillar strategy is high-effort but works for competitive topics. Colin is the only person in the room who understands SEO strategy (everyone else is tactical), so GrowthX needs to come prepared with a framework to explain structure and approach.

### AI/LLM Visibility and Modern SEO

Colin introduced AI visibility as a strategic priority. How does Webflow show up in Perplexity, ChatGPT, Google AI Overviews, and LLM training data? Are there mechanisms like llm.txt or on-page metadata that help? Jason will research this, noting it's adjacent to SEO but will diverge soon as LLMs learn to rank beyond Google's top 10. This is future-proofing for competitive developer search.

### Organizational Alignment and Ownership

Clear ownership has been missing. GrowthX didn't know who owned what (Vivian wasn't even at earlier calls). Colin is now the clear owner of developer platform strategy, with Curot (previous owner) in transition. Vivian owns SEO. Karat is distributed across many priorities and won't be the day-to-day approver. This clarifies who GrowthX should be talking to and about what.

### Webflow's Developer Platform as Strategic Imperative

The platform team was created 2 years ago to serve app developers and achieved its initial goals. Now at a scaling inflection, it's being repositioned as a first-class competitive advantage. Colin sees differentiation opportunity against competitors like Shopify and Webstudio, especially as the pattern of building shifts toward code-based platforms and away from low-code designers. This is a multi-year, high-value engagement if structured right.
