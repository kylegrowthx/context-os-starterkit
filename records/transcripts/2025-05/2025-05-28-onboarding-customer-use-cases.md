## Summary

Kyle Gaudreau, GrowthX's VP of Sales, walked new team members through GrowthX's current work streams and customer use cases, emphasizing the strategic shift toward editorial content as the foundation for all engagements. He explained why GrowthX moved from 90-day pilots to strategy sprints, how the company is pausing new programmatic content sales to improve processes, and outlined emerging opportunities around AI SEO optimization—where he positioned GrowthX's jobs-to-be-done framework and high-quality content creation as the foundation for visibility in both traditional search and AI-powered engines like Perplexity.

---

## Context

GrowthX is a B2B content marketing services firm that helps growth-focused companies improve organic visibility and drive revenue through content strategy. This was an internal onboarding session for new team members (many noted as notetakers joining the company). Kyle Gaudreau, who was previously the first customer at GrowthX before joining as Head of Client Services and then transitioning to lead sales, delivered a presentation on how GrowthX thinks about customer engagements and the services it offers. The meeting happened a week later than originally scheduled and occurred as GrowthX was in the midst of significant strategic transitions in how it approaches customer onboarding and structures engagements—moving from quick 90-day pilots to more deliberate strategy sprints that establish clear roadmaps and alignment before delivery begins.

---

## Relevance

**To GrowthX Delivery:**
- Strategy sprints are now the entry point for all editorial engagements; teams should expect to invest time upfront in alignment and roadmap definition before 12-month engagements move into delivery phase
- Editorial content at 8-15 articles per week is the baseline work stream; pricing and team structure is being built around this foundation
- Programmatic content is paused for new business due to process gaps; existing programmatic clients need better workflows for template creation, CMS integration, and quality consistency
- Link building service exists for low domain authority sites and requires partnership development with Jason Gong
- Interview-first long-form content offering is in beta and not yet sold to new customers, but team members formerly from Verbatim may have input on how to integrate it

**To CheckThat:**
- AI SEO is now the top question from prospects and customers—sales calls consistently surface concerns about Google's declining market share and the need to optimize for AI search engines (Perplexity, ChatGPT, etc.)
- GrowthX is partnering with Scrunch to track AI search engine rankings and create visibility data; this tracking capability could complement CheckThat positioning
- Current thesis: Jobs-to-be-done framework and high-quality content still rank well in both Google and AI engines; the example of Ramp's office furniture expense category page ranking #1 in both demonstrates this
- Opportunity to develop a comprehensive "AI SEO optimization" work stream that goes beyond tracking to include prompt optimization, content structure, and schema decisions

**To GrowthX Business Development:**
- New engagement model (strategy sprints) should reduce misalignment issues that occurred during 90-day pilots; this is a positive signal for customer satisfaction and renewal risk
- Homebase (a current customer) is still a customer despite being Kyle's previous employer; signals strong product-market fit in scaling companies that hit paid search cap
- Customers are increasingly asking about diversifying digital presence beyond organic search (YouTube, reviews, social media); Gong's podcast repurposing example shows compound content distribution potential
- Editorial-first positioning and publisher mindset differentiate GrowthX in a market where competitors may position as content factories or tactical SEO services

## Overview

- GrowthX is focusing on editorial content as the foundation, starting engagements with strategy sprints to ensure alignment and clear roadmaps
- The company is adapting its approach to meet changing market demands, including concerns about AI search engines impacting traditional SEO
- GrowthX aims to help customers diversify their digital presence and create content that performs well across various platforms, including AI-powered search

---

## Key Topics

### GrowthX's Evolution and Current Focus

  - Shifted from offering both editorial and programmatic content to prioritizing editorial
  - Now starts engagements with strategy sprints instead of 90-day pilots
  - Focus on helping customers "operate like publishers" with a strong content foundation
  - Aim to accelerate customers' organic growth strategies and eventually drive revenue

### Work Streams and Offerings

  - Editorial content: Primary focus, most mature work stream
  - Programmatic content: Currently paused for new sales, needs process improvements
  - Link building: Offered for low domain authority sites, partnerships being developed
  - Interview-first long-form content: New offering in beta testing phase
  - Potential future work streams being explored (e.g., AI SEO optimization)

### AI SEO and Changing Search Landscape

  - Increasing customer inquiries about optimizing for AI search engines
  - GrowthX partnering with Scrunch to track AI search engine rankings
  - Current strategy: Focus on jobs-to-be-done framework and creating high-quality content
  - Example: Ramp's content ranking well in both Google and AI-powered search (Perplexity)

### Content Strategy and Distribution

  - Emphasis on diversifying digital presence (YouTube, reviews, social media)
  - Example: Gong's podcast repurposing workflow for extended content distribution
  - Importance of creating content that can be repurposed across multiple channels
