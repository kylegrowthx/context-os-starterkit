## Summary

GrowthX and DataGrid aligned on a customer discovery research plan to validate DataGrid's existing use cases and develop a go-to-market positioning strategy. Rather than continuing to frame DataGrid as construction-focused software, the team decided to highlight the underlying role-based and workflow-focused value—how DataGrid automates high-volume, low-complexity tasks for specific personas. ## Key Topics

### DataGrid's Current Positioning and Success

  - DataGrid presents itself as an "agentic AI for all your data" solution
  - The company has booked $1 million in ARR in the last 110 days
  - Customers are primarily from construction, but interest is growing in other industries
  - The product focuses on automating high-volume, low-complexity tasks across various workflows

### Customer Use Cases and Deployment Stages

  - Many customers are in different stages of deployment:
      - Some have been using the platform for 5-6 months and are preparing for enterprise deployment
      - Others are expanding usage after initial testing
      - New customers are still in the early stages of implementation
  - Example use cases include:
      - Automating receipt processing and data entry (500 times daily)
      - Streamlining estimation processes in construction projects
      - Enhancing sales workflows across industries

### Market Positioning Challenges

  - The team is cautious about narrowing focus solely on construction:
      - Construction is seen as a fragmented sector rather than a unified industry
      - Previous construction-focused tech companies have struggled to scale
  - There's a preference for a role-based or workflow-based approach rather than industry-specific marketing
  - The goal is to maintain broad appeal while leveraging current success in construction

### Customer Discovery Process

  - The team will compile a list of 15-20 interviewees, prioritizing:
      - Existing DataGrid customers who purchased within the last 8 months
      - Happy, non-churned users
      - Super users and ideal customers
      - Those who found DataGrid independently (not through warm relationships)
  - Interviews will focus on understanding specific problems solved and the impact on users' roles
  - The process aims to identify common themes that can inform positioning across industries

### Emerging Role: AI Agent Architect

  - DataGrid is observing a new role emerging within customer organizations
  - This role involves deploying and managing AI agents across the enterprise
  - The company plans to highlight this trend through webinars and customer stories
