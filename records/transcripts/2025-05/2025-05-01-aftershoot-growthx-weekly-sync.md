### Content Ideas and Resources

AfterShoot's Facebook community is a primary source for identifying topic gaps and audience needs—posts show photographers asking about importing photos, cloud storage, business operations, and finance questions. Shoot.edit.com is a key reference site showing the type of educational content that resonates with target photographers. GrowthX can mine AfterShoot's 12-13 existing podcast episodes and YouTube video library for repurposing into blog posts, matching content to high-intent keywords. Jakub flagged the opportunity to identify multiple articles per topic (e.g., product-focused "how-tos" vs. concept-focused "examples") to avoid cannibalization and maximize SERP coverage.

### SEO and Keyword Research

GrowthX will conduct keyword research based on today's targeting criteria (wedding/portrait, business/pricing, client acquisition) to build a prioritized keyword list for content development. Strategy emphasizes top-of-funnel, educational content that positions AfterShoot as a resource for photographers rather than product-driven how-tos. The pose-tips series presents a strong opportunity for interlinking—creating 20-40 interconnected articles on pose variations drives authority and keeps readers within the content cluster, improving topical ranking.

### Content Refresh Strategy

Content refresh work was paused to prioritize the new content production workflow, but Jakub will message Rasheed to confirm whether refreshes should be reactivated. Team sees potential to rebalance workload between new content creation and existing content improvements, especially since top-of-funnel refresh work typically requires less approval overhead than new content.

### Enhancing Content Quality

Identified opportunity to add photographer credibility and authenticity to articles by requesting short tips, quotes, or case examples from the photographers already reviewing content. Could add "pro tip" sections with photographer names and portfolio links to increase shareability on social media and foster photographer community engagement. Monalisa suggested either requesting tips upfront (with a plan to supplement later if not received in time) or converting existing podcast and YouTube content into authored tips. This approach turns reviewers into contributors without significantly slowing approval cycles.
