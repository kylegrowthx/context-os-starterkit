## Key Topics

### Brainstorming Brand Descriptors

  - Used a timed exercise to generate a large quantity of potential brand descriptors
  - Encouraged both aspirational and contrasting words to stimulate idea generation
  - Captured ideas on virtual sticky notes in a shared Figma workspace

### Categorizing and Refining Descriptors

  - Sorted descriptors into "yes," "no," and "maybe" categories
  - Further categorized "yes" descriptors into presentation, tone, and personality buckets
  - Discussed and debated the merits of different descriptors within each category
  - Aimed to select the most impactful and representative words for the brand

### Key Brand Attributes

  - Presentation: Simple, bold, refined, rhythmic, premium, experimental
  - Tone: Raw, real, confident, expert, energizing, provoking
  - Personality: Curious, responsible, brave, adaptable, warm, mysterious

### Balancing Brand Qualities

  - Discussed the need to balance seemingly contradictory qualities (e.g., expert but experimental)
  - Aimed for a mix of attributes that convey professionalism and innovation
  - Considered how different attributes might apply to various aspects of the business (agency, community, content)

### Brand Positioning Considerations

  - Debated the merits of terms like "exclusive" vs "valuable" vs "premium"
  - Discussed how to position the brand as high-quality without being exclusionary
  - Considered how to convey expertise while still maintaining an experimental, cutting-edge image
