## Context

Diligent is a PE-backed SaaS compliance and risk management platform with a compliance/governance focus that now competes in the risk/audit space. Abi Ravindra leads content/marketing at Diligent and is driving a board-mandated push to leverage AI and automation for SEO and AI visibility—a major strategic shift for the company. Diligent is losing organic traffic in the US market while growing internationally, and 30-40% of inbound pipeline comes from organic search; they're currently compensating with increased ad spend. The company has a 7-person internal content team focused on blogs and papers, an in-house CMS with significant publishing friction (pages take 1-2 weeks to build vs their target of 24 hours), and has been working with another agency on backlinks to pages they don't track. They're also running a parallel web rebuild project with a third agency to improve the CMS architecture. GrowthX is being brought in to rebuild content strategy and scale top-of-funnel content for governance and risk/audit—two strategic product verticals—while the CMS rebuild completes.

## Key Topics

### Current Content Challenges

  - Diligent's 7-person content team produces blogs and papers but lacks a clear content strategy, information architecture, or funnel structure
  - Losing organic search traffic in US market while growing internationally; relying heavily on paid ads to compensate
  - CMS architecture bottleneck: 1-2 week turnaround to build a page instead of target 24 hours, limiting ability to scale content production
  - Previous agency sent backlinks to pages Diligent didn't select, creating unpredictable SEO signals and wasted spend
  - No visibility into engagement metrics for existing content (blogs, papers, pages)

### Revised Engagement Scope

  - Diligent wants to start with reduced scope (not full 100k words), focusing exclusively on two product verticals: governance funnel and risk/audit top-of-funnel content
  - Remove custom imagery generation and CMS integration from SOW; Diligent's brand team handles imagery, and LastRev (web vendor) is rebuilding CMS to fix turnaround bottleneck by end of June
  - 12-month engagement term starting end of May, with lower initial monthly spend to demonstrate growth before increasing budget (PE-backed structure requires showing ROI first)
  - GrowthX to meet with Diligent content team (including UK-based content head) before finalizing scope; audit existing content and align on what's being produced, frequency, and pages in system
  - If internal team and GrowthX lanes are clear, then rework SOW and chunk Q2/Q3/Q4 into focused quarterly goals around two verticals
  - Typical 100k words = ~15 articles/week; 50k words = 5-8 articles/week. Abi wants to "start slow, move fast" rather than overpromise and underdeliver

### GrowthX Content OS and Service Model

  - **Team structure:** Managing Director (20+ years SEO/content experience) sets strategy; Managing Editors are main client contact (Slack, Notion, weekly agendas); Content Managers (specialized writers) handle hands-on workflows; Editors polish final output
  - **Human-in-the-loop process:** Not just AI-to-publish. Multiple checkpoints—strategy alignment, feedback loops, editing revisions for tone/voice/structure—to ensure consistent quality over time
  - **Content opportunity identification (Airtable-based):** Personas → jobs to be done → research questions → competitive gap analysis → topical opportunity clusters. Example: In a few weeks with Abnormal Security, identified ~1,300 content opportunities, clustered by keyword themes, ranked by priority
  - **Dual-track content:** Net new content (driven by strategy and opportunity analysis) + content refresh/enhancement (low-hanging fruit SEO wins by auditing existing pages, refresh strategies, keyword expansion)
