## Summary

GrowthX explored building internal link building capabilities with Rob Mitchell, a specialist who's been doing white hat link building for 11 years at Red Ventures and as a consultant. The team discussed moving beyond paying $750-1000 per link to brokers, and instead building in-house using white hat strategies with overseas teams (estimated $150-200 per link). Jason, Dave, and Rob aligned on the future of SEO: links account for ~25% of Google's ranking factors, but brand mentions on Reddit and other platforms are becoming increasingly important as AI reshapes search.

## Context

GrowthX is adding link building services to their portfolio. Today they have ~40 clients, about 10 of whom request link building. Currently, GrowthX outsources this work to paid link brokers, spending $750-1000 per link. Rob Mitchell is an external contact who Dave knew from Red Ventures; Dave connected him to Jason as a potential advisor or contractor to help GrowthX build a better, in-house link building operation. Rob has 11 years of experience doing white hat link building (acquiring links through content distribution and outreach, not purchasing them) at Red Ventures and as a side consultant. He uses a team in the Philippines for harvesting contact lists and QA, plus tools like Pitchbox for email outreach. Rob shared his model, costs, and conversion rates so GrowthX can decide whether to hire him part-time, bring on someone like Neil Photo full-time, or build the capability internally.
