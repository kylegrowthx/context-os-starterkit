## Summary

Jon Darbyshire, CEO of SmartSuite (recently crossed $4M ARR), explored a potential partnership with Marcel Santilli at GrowthX Labs to build an embedded content and SEO program. Marcel outlined GrowthX's approach: hiring journalists instead of marketers, using AI-powered workflows to scale content production, and embedding a dedicated team (managing director, editor, SEO strategist, writers) into the client's operations. The service starts with a $20K, 60-day strategy phase to align on content pillars, personas, and voice; then transitions to ongoing work streams starting at $9K/month (approximately 25 articles) and typically averaging $15K/month depending on scope and additional services like content refresh or case studies.

## Context

SmartSuite is a no-code automation platform that recently crossed $4M ARR with entirely organic growth and a product-led growth (PLG) model. Jon Darbyshire, the CEO, is actively building the company's marketing function for the first time—95% of leads currently come through the website, and the sales team is only three people managing high-touch enterprise deals. The company is well-positioned in the low-code/automation space (competing with products like Softer and others) and is now shifting focus from SMB/PLG toward mid-market and enterprise segments.

GrowthX Labs, founded by Marcel Santilli (former CMO at ScaleAI, ServiceSight, and HashiCorp) with co-founder Daniel Chia (former product/engineering lead at IFTTT), specializes in embedded content marketing through a journalist-first model combined with AI-powered workflows. The team has recently closed Series A funding (announced Monday of this call, May 19, 2025) and has successfully completed projects with high-profile SaaS companies like Abnormal Security, Ramp, Galileo AI, and others. Marcel positioned this as a potential good fit because Jon is actively seeking execution partners rather than traditional agencies, and SmartSuite's market position and growth trajectory align well with GrowthX's approach of building publication-like content at scale.
