## Summary

Kyle Gaudreau introduced GrowthX's AI-powered content creation service to Ashish Aggarwal (founder, AppBroda), a bootstrapped adtech company planning to launch a new MarTech product for user acquisition teams. GrowthX's offering—starting at $9K/month with 12-month engagements—did not align with AppBroda's current stage: pre-product market fit, limited resources, and a focus on outbound sales rather than SEO. Ashish agreed to revisit GrowthX when content becomes a strategic priority for the new product vertical.

---

## Context

AppBroda is a 4-year-old bootstrapped adtech company founded by Ashish Aggarwal (based in Delhi) and operating lean with ~20 people. The primary business serves mobile gaming publishers (Zynga, Voodoo) with ad-serving technology, generating high-value contracts ($20K-$300K ACV) with roughly 60 clients out of a potential market of 400-500 companies concentrated in Europe (40%), China (30%), and the US/Southeast Asia. The company is now diversifying into MarTech—building a new product to help user acquisition teams (UA managers and creative operations) optimize workflows and reduce manual overhead by replacing fragmented tools (Google Drive, Slack, WhatsApp comments) with a unified platform. AppBroda discovered GrowthX on the Lean AI leaderboard and reached out because Ashish recognizes that this new product vertical (targeting ~20,000 potential customers at lower ACV) will require a different GTM strategy: one that shifts from pure founder-led outreach to include content and thought leadership. This is a pre-product market fit stage for the new vertical, and the company is bootstrapped, making significant content investment risky before proving market fit.
