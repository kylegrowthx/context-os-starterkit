## Key Topics

### Content Audit Overview

  - 20-30% of content feels good, substantial portion needs improvement
  - No reason to panic, but definite need for enhancement
  - Exercise aimed to take temperature of current content output

### Client-Specific Content Challenges (AfterShoot)

  - Very hands-on with content, making frequent edits
  - Prefer storytelling style, sometimes bordering on "corny"
  - Slow feedback loop affecting content production speed
  - 32,000 words written in a month, but publication process is slow

### Structural Content Issues

  - Skeletal structure with single sentences, H3s, and bullets needs addressing
  - Aim for more narrative flow and fleshed-out sections
  - Watch for repetitive patterns like "not the worst thing, it's the best thing"
  - Consider converting tables to graphics for better visual appeal

### Content Hallucination Risks

  - Example: Hub and Spoke article contained irrelevant logistics information
  - Need for careful review, especially with unique or new product cases
  - Recommendation to refresh and republish affected content

### Workflow Improvements

  - New sprint group for new clients to ensure better calibration
  - Custom workflows and instructions for each client
  - Matthew personally editing calibration content with directors and MEs
  - David's client-specific polishing instructions praised as a model for future use

### Client Communication Strategies

  - Suggest dedicated client call to discuss workflow and publishing speed
  - Be upfront about balancing speed and quality, offering to revisit and improve content
  - Emphasize the refresh process built into SoWs for continuous improvement
