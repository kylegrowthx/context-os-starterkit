## Context

Rachael Tiow leads a new growth function at Okta, building what she calls an "internal growth lab and abstraction layer" to test AI-powered tools, vet data quality, and validate business signals before rolling them out to the broader sales and marketing teams. This is Rachael's second scaling effort — she previously built outbound and ABM capabilities at OddZero before Okta acquired it. Now that OddZero and Okta are operating as separate products again, Okta's outbound motion has atrophied, and Rachael is tasked with rebuilding it from first principles. Okta's security-sensitive environment (public company, identity and security focus) makes it difficult to integrate third-party AI tools directly into their systems, so the growth lab operates independently, testing approaches in a controlled environment before escalating to the broader teams. GrowthX's custom workflow capabilities — their ability to build infrastructure-heavy processes like distributed research, vector DB retrieval, and complex enrichment pipelines — are a strategic fit for Rachael's team, which wants expert-guided processes rather than autonomous agents that require heavy maintenance.
