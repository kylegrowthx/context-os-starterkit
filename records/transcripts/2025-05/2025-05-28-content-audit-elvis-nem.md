## Summary

Matthew conducted a content quality audit with Nemanja and Elvis, highlighting issues with LLM-generated content—overly templated headlines with colons, anemic introductions, and choppy narrative structure—and identifying that current AirOps workflows have critical gaps in research, briefs, and polishing instructions that force content managers to work outside the system. The team is implementing a sprint team approach for new client onboarding (2-3 weeks with high-level staff including Marcel and Ada) to build custom, steady-state workflows before handing off to delivery pods, with a complete migration off AirOps to a new internal platform by mid-June.

---

## Context

Matthew is conducting a company-wide content quality audit by meeting with individual team members to understand both quality standards and systemic workflow issues. The audit is not meant to critique individuals but to identify where workflows and processes break down, so the team can build better systems. Nemanja has been running his own custom workflow using Claude and Perplexity for the past two months because AirOps (the current content workflow platform) has become a bottleneck—the outputs are poor and require an extra hour of manual work to finish. Elvis joined in January and quickly identified similar issues with AirOps onboarding and feedback loops. The broader context is GrowthX's transition from AirOps to a new internal platform by mid-June, coupled with a new operational model using sprint teams for client onboarding that will build custom workflows tailored to each client's specific needs before handing the client off to steady-state delivery pods.

---

## Relevance

**To GrowthX Delivery:**
- Content quality suffers from LLM patterns (templated colons in headlines, swimming metaphors, choppy short sentences) that require systematic polishing instructions to fix
- Current AirOps workflows are missing critical steps: research depth, brief quality, and polishing instructions that address narrative structure
- Nemanja's successful custom workflow (Claude + Perplexity, 20-60 min per blog, 35 blogs/week) should be deconstructed and codified into new platform workflows for other CMs
- Table of contents with clickable navigation is not currently generated by workflows but is needed for SEO (e.g., Exec client has it built into their blog design)
- Each client needs custom workflows built from modular blocks, not one-size-fits-all templates from six months ago

**To GrowthX Business Development:**
- New sprint team approach (2-3 week onboarding with senior staff) will allow faster, better-quality client launches and smoother handoff to pods
- Client handoff process from sprint teams to steady-state pods is still TBD and needs design before first sprint team launches (within 8-9 weeks)
- Current practice of signing SOWs without sprint team input is causing pod overload (e.g., pods asked to build templated pages with embedded API calls without engineer, designer, or PM support)
- Sprint teams will deliver production-ready architecture and documentation before handing off, reducing steady-state pod friction
