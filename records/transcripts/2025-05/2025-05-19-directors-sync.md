## Summary

Marcel Santilli led the directors through a strategic restructuring of GrowthX's client onboarding and delivery model, announcing a shift from 3-month "pilot periods" to accelerated 6-8 week "strategy sprints" designed to deliver high-impact value upfront and set clearer mutual expectations. The team discussed forming a dedicated SWAT team of top performers to lead these strategy sprints, plus broader changes to content production processes including centralizing roles (researchers, publishers, editors), implementing stricter quality control with director review of calibration articles, and moving to full-time employees instead of freelancers. Key constraint: the move off Airtable/Airops to a custom platform by June 16th will unlock better programmatic SEO capabilities, so the team is focusing near-term efforts on editorial/publishing services where they're already strong, while scoping and resetting expectations on custom/programmatic work.

---

## Context

GrowthX is a B2B services and software company that has grown rapidly (multiple kickoffs per week since January 2025) but struggled with onboarding consistency and customer satisfaction. The service side involves helping brands become effective publishers/content creators, and is notoriously challenging — requiring depth, quality, and alignment that many teams struggle to deliver. This meeting brought together the newly formed Director team (Matthew Panzarino as Chief Content Officer, plus multiple new Associate Directors including Andi Bailey who joined on this day) to address systemic issues with the rapid growth model, client expectations, and the need for more structured, specialist-driven delivery.

---

## Relevance

**To GrowthX Delivery:**
- Strategy sprint model (6-8 week paid scoping period instead of 3-month pilot) reduces churn and aligns expectations—clients can opt out mutually, and GrowthX can opt out of poor-fit accounts
- SWAT team approach (dedicated top performers + managing editors) for strategy sprints improves consistency and quality—enables learning what it takes to do onboarding well at scale
- Shift from freelancers to full-time employees for security/compliance; focus on 80%+ "publish-ready" content before client review raises delivery bar
- Directors become primary client-facing contacts for new onboarding and strategy work; managing editors/content managers pulled from stable accounts to seed new ones

**To GrowthX Business Development & Account Health:**
- $20-30k strategy sprint packages create a lower-friction entry point for prospects and a proven assessment/scoping motion similar to consulting firms
- Editorial/publishing services are current strength; programmatic/technical SEO being deprioritized near-term due to platform constraints (moving off Airtable by June 16)
- Custom/technical opportunities (e.g., Augment Code, Bolt) treated differently—require heavy engineering input and look more like McKinsey-style consulting
- Goal is strong, consistent delivery and customer delight for 9-15k/month average contract value, not chasing larger deals or upsells

**To Platform/Product:**
- Custom platform launch by June 16 is critical—unlocks programmatic SEO capabilities, better workflows, quality control, and self-serve SaaS option for strategy sprint outputs
- Platform enables new business model: clients can buy self-serve access to strategy sprint work and run it themselves, vs. recurring GrowthX work streams

## Key Topics

### New Client Onboarding Process

  - Replacing 3-month "pilot period" with 6-8 week "strategy sprint"
  - Goal is to deliver high value upfront and set clear expectations
  - Will involve dedicated SWAT team to ensure quality
  - Allows mutual opt-out if not a good fit
  - Focuses on editorial/publishing services initially, less on programmatic SEO

### Content Production Changes

  - Centralizing production vs. delivery roles
  - Creating specialized positions like researchers and publishers
  - Implementing stricter quality control, including director review of calibration articles
  - Moving away from freelancers to full-time employees
  - Aiming for 80%+ of content to be "publish-ready" before client review

### Team Structure and Roles

  - Forming SWAT team of top performers for strategy sprints and challenging accounts
  - Directors should be primary client-facing contacts, especially for new accounts
  - More specialized roles like dedicated publishers to be added
  - Shifting away from having CMs/MEs interact directly with clients until ready

### Platform/Technology Updates

  - Moving off Airtable/Airops to custom platform by June 16th
  - New platform will enable better workflows, quality control, and programmatic SEO capabilities
  - Engineering team making rapid progress on new features

### Programmatic SEO Challenges

  - Currently limited capacity for programmatic/technical SEO work
  - Need to review existing programmatic client commitments
  - May need to reset expectations or shift focus for some accounts
  - New platform will improve programmatic capabilities in \~4 weeks
