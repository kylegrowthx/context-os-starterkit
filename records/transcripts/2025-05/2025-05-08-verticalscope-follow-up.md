## Summary

GrowthX and VerticalScope explored a potential partnership to accelerate content production and user engagement across VerticalScope's 400+ forum properties, particularly focusing on automotive sites. Chris Goodridge outlined VerticalScope's current tactics: reviving dormant threads with AI-generated questions, translating high-volume content into six languages using Google Vertex AI, and manually seeding new communities to simulate early activity. Kyle Gaudreau identified parallels with GrowthX's existing workflow methodologies around cohort analysis and iterative content optimization, but raised concerns about customization requirements and whether the forum-specific tactics fit GrowthX's core capabilities. The team agreed to explore starting with one focused property as a proof-of-concept to measure impact on engagement and SEO metrics before scaling.

---

## Context

VerticalScope is a leading digital publisher operating 400+ niche community forums across verticals like automotive, home services, and lifestyle. With ~$70M in online revenue and $30M EBITDA (2022), the company monetizes through programmatic advertising, subscriptions, and affiliate commerce, supported by a 65+ million-person email database. The core challenge: forum post volume is declining over time as user behavior shifts, and the company depends heavily on organic search traffic from Google. This follow-up meeting builds on earlier discussions between Kyle Gaudreau and Chris Goodridge about potential collaboration. Chris brought Bruno Santilli into the conversation to explore whether GrowthX's content workflows and AI capabilities could help VerticalScope scale its community seeding and thread revival processes, historically done manually by small teams.

### Challenges and Opportunities

  - Declining post volume over time across properties
  - Heavy dependence on organic search traffic from Google
  - Need to diversify traffic sources beyond search
  - Changing user preferences, particularly towards video content
  - Opportunity to curate content across entire verticals (e.g., motorcycle content across 400 sites)
