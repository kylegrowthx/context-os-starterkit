## Key Topics

### Eon's Starting Point

Jenn joined from Webflow (Josh Grant was her boss) to build Eon's growth function from scratch. Eon has raised significant capital and is disrupting AWS/Azure/GCP with clean, queryable live datasets that support AI use cases. The growth infrastructure is nonexistent: no content, no SEO, ~60 random pages, zero organic or LLM traffic. She immediately hired Summit Chase (Rex) for SEM and paid social, and submitted a scope for an SEO/AEO partner. ABM is a priority because SDRs and reps are already engaged.

### GrowthX Agency + Platform Model

Marcel explained GrowthX as an agency-plus-platform: SEO, AEO, content, and engineering delivered as a service. He walked through case studies — Lovable (rescued from two bad SEO agencies creating garbage content), Brex (site refactoring), Ramp (vendors catalog, per diem pages, expense categories), Webflow (integration and dev pages). Data Grid: started with a two-page site, built domain authority with GrowthX, acquired by Procore.

### CheckThat Demo

Marcel showed Eon's workspace inside CheckThat:
- 120 prompts already collecting data across Eon's category for ~90 days
- 32,000 AI-generated responses already accumulated
- Eon showed up in only **one** prompt: "what is the most reliable AWS backup solution" — nowhere else
- Workspace is free (14-day look-back on the free tier); Marcel will send invite
- Data can't be backdated — the longer you wait, the more historical context you lose

### Competitive and Perception Analysis

Marcel built a full competitor map including Veeam, Commvault, Rubrik, Cohesity. He reviewed every analyst and peer review platform (Gartner, G2, TrustRadius, etc.) to show where competitors appear and where Eon is absent. He introduced a two-axis framework:
- **Reputation**: community peer reviews + analyst presence
- **AI Perception**: how AI engines qualitatively rank a brand within buyer-relevant attributes

Eon sits in "hidden gem" tier — high potential, no established presence. Marcel advised against Product Hunt (prosumer audience, not enterprise buyers). G2 and TrustRadius were flagged as quick wins for reputation building.

### Category Expansion

Jenn confirmed Eon's core is backup and next-gen data storage, but they'll expand into data lake, data warehouse, and data governance over time. She's still getting up to speed on the space (came from Webflow). Marcel noted the current CheckThat categorization under "Backup and DR / Cloud & Infra" and said additional buying categories can be added as Eon's positioning evolves.
