### Company vision and strategy

- AI = biggest shift in buyer behavior since the Internet; every company impacted.
- Three audiences: buyers, AI agents influencing buyers, training bots.
- Brands want growth and leverage; website is the main driver—from “important” to “existential”; job is 100x harder (more pages, faster decay, manage/create/optimize at scale).
- Impossible without AI as force multiplier; promised land = AI handles production/optimization/learning, humans guide strategy/quality/judgment.
- Four layers: infrastructure, data, platform, services, distribution. Community (AI-led growth), Check That (visibility data + lead gen), OS + service, Output AI (agentic framework).
- Decoupling in the next month: platform vs. services; not service-only—platform with optional managed service.
- 2026: $34M ARR core, $4M ARR Check That; strong margins and burn multiple; own destiny, minimal dilution.
- Three priorities: (1) platform as OS for website-driven growth, (2) best dataset on AI brand perception, (3) Output AI as best framework for agentic workflows.

### Churn and retention

- Three Q1 churns: Surge ($2.7M, much of it non-core work), Okta (sales enablement vs. core), Superhuman (merged with Grammarly; they chose Air Ops for in-house control).
- Andi: some clients see GrowthX as transactional (content only); need to personify “compounding growth engine,” show engineering/tech behind outcomes, right decision-makers bought in; Atlas workflows and agentic quality issues hurt trust; acquisitions are a red flag until environment is clear.
- Marcel: platform stickiness and compounding learning reduce churn; decoupling lets clients feel in control and see value; “five Google Docs a week” = diminishing value; “compounding growth engine they can’t do without” = long-term retention.
- Already in motion: strategy sprints, more engineering in pods, North Star metrics, EM communication.

### Pipeline and upsells

- Tyler: 10 deals in 30 days—Talon 1 (Sephora, Adidas, Live Nation, $135M Series B), Daylight AI ($33M A), Fieldwire (Hilti >$300M), Witness AI ($58M B), Spendesk (unicorn, Paris, >$300M), Assort Health ($76M B), JAMA (acquired >$1B), Giga ($61M A, DoorDash, Parloa, Cresta), Elementum (ex-Smartsheet/Conga CMO), etc. St. Jude’s still in play.

- Lovable: 5 → 25 guides/week (then 10/week); 10 templates/week; Atlas workflow, Will, Nico’s publishing bot; George’s strategy sprint; possible integrations pages next.

### Platform and OS

- Output AI = framework powering execution (e.g. 25 guides/week for Lovable); learning/reprioritization “physically impossible” without platform; OS enables all three jobs.
