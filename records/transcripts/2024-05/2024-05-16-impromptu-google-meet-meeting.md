## Overview

- OpenAI's multimodal AI demo raised expectations, but Deep Gram can still showcase unique value for enterprise use cases
- Video should focus on practical applications and being the fastest path to production, not making grandiose claims
- Ground the narrative in humanity - how voice interfaces give us more time for meaningful experiences

---

## Key Topics

### Positioning Against OpenAI/Google

  - Acknowledge their progress positively - it's advancing the market we want to enable
  - Don't try to directly compete on consumer use cases they demoed
  - Highlight Deep Gram's strengths: cost, speed, enterprise-readiness, customizability

### Target Audience

  - CTOs/technical decision makers evaluating voice AI for production use cases
  - Prospective customers and investors, but avoid pure brand-building exercise

### Narrative Approach

  - Use storytelling framework: change in world, winners/losers, obstacles, promised land
  - Ground narrative in humanity - how voice interfaces enhance rather than replace human experience
  - Showcase practical use cases where voice AI solves real needs (e.g. understaffed call centers, healthcare, accessibility)
  - Inspire builders to create solutions, not just entertain
