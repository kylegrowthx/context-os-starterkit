## Key Topics

### Content Performance Metrics

  - Search Console data shows strong performance for GrowthX-created articles (14% CTR vs 3% benchmark for "best beaches" articles)
  - Specific win: "things to do in Puerto Vallarta in March" has generated 14 clicks in only a few days despite only 20 monthly search volume baseline
  - Team tracking clicks, impressions, CTR for GrowthX-created content (will expand to monitor all blog posts for refresh opportunities)
  - Additional metrics to watch: time-on-page (indicates content quality/engagement) and user behavior across the funnel (impression → click → tour page visit → purchase)
  - Agreement to monitor overall correlation: 20% increase in impressions/clicks should correspond with 20% revenue growth to validate strategy effectiveness

### Revenue Tracking Challenges

  - Analytics purchase data has discrepancies but is useful for identifying directional trends and high-performing articles
  - Complex attribution problem: users rarely convert directly from article click. Common journey: blog article → additional research articles → tour page → purchase decision (multiple touchpoints)
  - Decision: Track overall site-level metrics (impressions, clicks, revenue) monthly rather than attributing revenue to specific articles
  - Google Analytics changing default attribution model to data-driven on April 3, removing per-article attribution complexity
  - Isabel Luna to confirm purchase data accuracy with finance team and provide screenshots of discrepancies for analysis

### Banner Implementation Strategy

  - Current approach: Individual banners added to specific articles (e.g., one banner added in November to lower-traffic test article)
  - Goal: Centralized, scalable system using Sanity CMS components to replace manual per-article edits
  - Technical design: Create a reusable "banner block" component in Sanity; add snippet code to articles by category (e.g., all "adventures" posts reference same banner component)
  - Benefits: Change banner creative once in central component; all articles using that category automatically update without touching individual posts
  - Measurement: Include UTM parameter tracking so banners clicked can be attributed in Analytics; component dashboard shows banner impressions and CTR
  - Initial test: Deploy to top 10 high-traffic articles across the site (previous test with 12 page views in 28 days was too small sample)

### Keyword Cannibalization Concerns

  - Developer flagged risk: blog articles may be ranking above tour pages for commercial intent keywords (e.g., "ATV tours", "whale watching tours")
  - Evidence: Tour pages (120+ total) are performing well for some keywords but may be blocked by blog competition for others
  - Two approaches discussed:
    1. (Isabel's approach) Search Console: identify top 50-70% of traffic-driving keywords, then check if blog or tour page ranks first for each
    2. (Mariana's approach) Audit all tour pages for primary/secondary keywords, ensure they're optimized with content and FAQ sections
  - Mariana's additional observation: Some older articles (not GrowthX-written) already convert well; strategy must account for legacy content before making edits
  - Next step: Mariana to do Search Console analysis of top traffic keywords, then work with tech team to optimize tour pages for keywords they should rank for

### Content Review and Publishing Status

  - Articles ready for review and publishing (high search volume, good conversion signals):
      - "Best Cancun beaches for every type of traveler" (assigned to Hector for review) — has already started converting on Cabo property
      - "Best Puerto Vallarta beaches" — related to high-performing "things to do" series

  - "Drinking age in Mexico" article: Mariana to shorten/edit (currently 1,600 words, too long) and resend for review. Despite non-obvious travel intent, team believes it has potential for bookings during trip planning research
  - Broader strategy: Mariana being selective about which keywords to target (avoiding "curiosities" like "Mayan ruins facts" that attract browsers, not bookers). Testing intent-based approach.
  - Future pipeline: April and May "things to do in [location]" articles to be prepared and sent for review, building on success of Puerto Vallarta article
