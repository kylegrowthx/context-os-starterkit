## Summary

GrowthX and Webstacks reviewed SEO performance (traffic steady despite Google core update rolling through early April), then pivoted focus from volume-based content production to curating the existing content ecosystem. ## Context

Webstacks is a web design and development agency working with GrowthX on a content-driven SEO and conversion strategy. This is an ongoing managed engagement where GrowthX handles content production, strategy, and conversion optimization while Webstacks manages execution and technical implementation. Aida Knežević (GrowthX) leads the content and conversion strategy; Jesse Schor (Webstacks CEO) owns the broader business context and pipeline goals; Devon Wood (Webstacks) owns content publishing and technical SEO; Jakub Rudnik (new GrowthX data analyst on the account, week 3) is responsible for conversion analysis and experiment design.

The meeting comes three months into a content strategy focused on design and development keywords, where traffic has grown but pipeline conversion remains inconsistent. Webstacks is an enterprise-sized agency with high-ACV deals ($500k+), so volume doesn't drive revenue—consistency and lead quality do. The team is shifting from "more content" to "better targeting and internal structure" and exploring how to turn blog traffic into capability page visits and qualified conversations.

## Key Topics

### SEO Performance Update

  - Google core update rolling out, expected to complete by early April
  - Slight increase in clicks, but no dramatic changes yet
  - Internal linking improvements made to address orphan pages
  - Some blogs (10-15) have lost significant traffic over time, not due to algorithm changes

### Content Strategy Shift

  - Focus on curating existing content ecosystem rather than producing high volumes of new content
  - Emphasis on creating embedded pillars on capabilities pages
  - Goal: Connect high-traffic blog content to pipeline-generating pages (capabilities, solutions, use cases)
  - Experiment with pushing traffic from lower-funnel blog content to capabilities pages

### Conversion Optimization

  - Current email nurture program is basic and not particularly strong
  - Most bottom-funnel conversions happen within single sessions
  - Short-term experiment: Add more targeted conversion panels (e.g., "Book a Call") to bottom-funnel content
  - Prioritize learning about user journeys and quality of traffic over raw lead volume

### New Thought Leadership Content

  - Short-form blog posts sharing insights from stakeholder interviews (e.g., CTO of Gong, CPO of Drata)
  - Not SEO-optimized, but meant to showcase Webstacks' perspective and expertise
  - Can be incorporated into longer-form blog content and used for social media posts
