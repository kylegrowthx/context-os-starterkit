## Key Topics

### Current SEO Performance

  - Weekly clicks hovering around 7,500, not breaking out of this range
  - Impressions on upward trajectory, likely not hit by recent algorithm updates
  - Focus on changing trajectory to accelerate traffic and attract right type of visitors

### Backlink Strategy

  - Current backlink profile significantly underperforming; competitors have substantially more backlinks to their articles
  - Attention has proprietary domain redirects which create self-referencing issues; immediate tactic is leveraging GrowthX's client network (30-40 clients) to build initial backlinks
  - Tactics discussed for link acquisition:
      - VC/incubator link exchanges: outreach to companies backed by the same VCs or within same accelerator programs
      - Non-competitive marketing/sales niche outreach: companies in adjacent spaces rather than direct competitors
      - Broken link building: identify target blogs with broken links, offer higher-quality replacement content
      - Data-driven journalism: leverage unique Attention data (sales call data, product insights) to publish research pieces
      - Thought leadership: company-level perspectives on industry trends, syndicated through press releases and outreach
      - Nurturing sequences: incoming visitors from link building can be captured into Attention's existing outbound nurturing workflows

### Outbound Email Optimization

  - Current stats: \~60% open rate, 30 emails per mailbox, 180-210 emails per domain daily
  - Recommendations:
      - Reduce to 90-100 emails per domain (max 3 per domain)
      - Split across multiple workspaces to avoid restrictions
      - Continue inbox placement tasks for better deliverability

### Internal Keyword Strategy

  - Critical gap identified: main site pages lack clear keyword focus, preventing Google from understanding topical authority and core offering
  - Page title analysis revealed generic descriptions instead of keyword-targeted titles (current: "AI agents that learn from your best sales conversations" vs. target: "AI sales agent")
  - 50% of Attention's traffic comes from blog; blog is struggling to rank because Google lacks clear signal about what the main domain represents
  - Key areas for improvement:
      - Optimize all page titles with primary keywords (e.g., "Generalize Insights" page needs keyword-focused title)
      - Refine main site content to include semantic keywords and related terms
      - Align internal pages with blog topical authority so Google understands company's core domain expertise
      - Expected ranking impact: proper topical authority signals should improve article rankings across the blog portfolio
      - Timeline: expect traction within 3 months of implementation

### Programmatic Content Ideas

  - **Competitor comparison pages**: "Attention vs. [competitor]" — show pros/cons and differentiation
  - **Feature-specific pages**: Deep dives into individual Attention capabilities
  - **Industry and use case pages**: "How to improve sales calls by industry" (vertical-specific content targeting decision-makers)
  - **Integration opportunity pages**: Pages highlighting how Attention integrates with specific tools (with awareness of competitive implications)
  - **Buying guides and "best of" lists**: For supporting buyer research phase
  - **Sales script templates by industry**: High-value, repeatable content (e.g., "Cold call scripts for X industry" or "Sales email templates for X industry")
  - **Decision-maker persona focus**: All content targets VPs of Sales, RevOps, and leadership with purchasing power (20K+ ACV), not individual contributors
  - **Repeatable execution model**: Kyle emphasized value of high-repeatability templates that enable quick production and let GrowthX test what drives traffic and domain authority capture
  - **Top priorities**: Kyle recommended sales scripts and industry use cases as strongest bets; Stewart interested in industry-specific content; all formats under consideration pending discovery of what drives traction

### Technical SEO Improvements

  - **Missing article schema**: Blog posts currently lack article schema markup, which Google uses to understand article context and boost rankings
  - **Missing author markup**: Author information currently appears only in an image, not as visible page content or schema; proper implementation requires moving author off image and adding Author schema
  - **Webflow implementation**: Stewart to investigate whether Webflow supports article schema and author schema implementation (likely yes given Webflow's sophistication)
  - **Potential approach**: GrowthX exploring ability to instrument author schema via Airtable integration into Webflow, though manual implementation may be necessary initially
  - **Impact**: Proper schema markup will improve blog post rankings and context signals to Google
