## Overview

- GrowthX currently uses Mercury, offering 4.05-4.4% yield (minus 25bps fee); JPM's proposed yields are lower but emphasize stability and long-term partnership

## Key Topics

### Current Banking Situation

  - GrowthX currently uses Mercury for banking
  - Earning 4.05-4.4% yield (minus 25bps management fee)
  - Easy access to funds, but concerns about long-term viability as company grows

### JPM Liquidity Proposals

  - Option 1: $1M in operating account, $11M in savings at 3.6% yield
  - Option 2: $1.5M in operating account, $10.5M in 3-month CD at 3.95% yield
  - Potential hybrid approach discussed, combining elements of both options

### JPM Value Proposition

  - Fortress balance sheet for stability during market turbulence
  - Scalable solutions as GrowthX grows (e.g., transition to Access platform)
  - Dedicated support team and relationship managers
  - Connections to VC ecosystem and capital raising expertise
  - Integrated bill payment solution (Cash Flow 360) at no additional cost

### Risk and Access Considerations

  - FDIC insurance limited to $250k, but JPM emphasizes overall bank stability
  - CD option restricts access to funds for 3 months; early withdrawal incurs fees
  - Savings account option provides more flexibility but at lower yield

### GrowthX's Concerns

  - Lower yield compared to current Mercury offering
  - Potential complexity in managing CDs and rollovers
  - Desire for easy access to funds for potential opportunities (e.g., acquisitions)
