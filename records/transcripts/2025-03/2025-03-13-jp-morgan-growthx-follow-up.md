## Summary

Marcel and the GrowthX finance team (Bridget McGillivray, Ericksson Abad) met with JP Morgan's commercial and private banking teams to explore treasury and liquidity management options. JP Morgan presented two primary structures: a flexible operating account + high-yield savings (3.6% yield), or a higher-yield option combining operating account + 3-month CDs (3.95% yield). While GrowthX appreciated JPM's fortress balance sheet and long-term partnership potential, Marcel expressed concern that the yields underperform GrowthX's current Mercury offering (4.05-4.4%) and committed to benchmarking against other major banks before deciding.

---

## Context

GrowthX is a B2B content marketing and AI visibility services company currently managing ~$12M in liquid reserves through Mercury, earning 4.05-4.4% yield (after 25bps management fee). The company is venture-backed and planning for eventual institutional-grade banking infrastructure as it grows. This meeting was a follow-up to an earlier discussion with JP Morgan's disruptive commerce team, focused on transitioning GrowthX from fintech-focused Mercury to an established tier-one bank offering both personal wealth services for Marcel (private banking) and institutional treasury/liquidity solutions for the company. JP Morgan's team emphasized their fortress balance sheet, VC relationships, and ability to scale with GrowthX as it grows, referencing the SVB collapse as a stability argument.

## Overview

- JPM presented two primary liquidity structures: Option 1 ($1M operating + $11M savings at 3.6% yield for maximum flexibility), Option 2 ($1.5M operating + $10.5M in 3-month CDs at 3.95% yield for maximized returns)
- GrowthX currently banks with Mercury (4.05-4.4% yield after 25bps fee); JPM yields are lower on surface but offer fortress balance sheet, JPM flagged Mercury's partnership transition risk (moving from Evolve Bank)
- JPM emphasized dedicated treasury team, long-term partnership, private banking for Marcel, VC ecosystem access, and integrated bill payment (Cash Flow 360) at no additional cost
- Marcel pushed back: yield gap is material on $12M AUM, CD lockup adds complexity, and easy fund access is critical for potential acquisition opportunities. Committed to benchmarking against other major banks before deciding

---

## Key Topics

### Current Banking Situation

  - GrowthX currently uses Mercury for banking
  - Earning 4.05-4.4% yield (minus 25bps management fee)
  - Easy access to funds, but concerns about long-term viability as company grows

### JPM Liquidity Proposals

  - Option 1: $1M in operating account, $11M in savings at 3.6% yield
  - Option 2: $1.5M in operating account, $10.5M in 3-month CD at 3.95% yield
  - Potential hybrid approach discussed, combining elements of both options

### JPM Value Proposition

  - Fortress balance sheet for stability during market turbulence
  - Scalable solutions as GrowthX grows (e.g., transition to Access platform)
  - Dedicated support team and relationship managers
  - Connections to VC ecosystem and capital raising expertise
  - Integrated bill payment solution (Cash Flow 360) at no additional cost

### Risk and Access Considerations

  - FDIC insurance on single bank limited to $250k standard limit; JPM clarified that services spreading deposits across multiple institutions create different risks (depositor doesn't know counterparty banks)
  - CD option restricts access to funds for 3 months; early withdrawal incurs 1-3% prepayment breakage fee
  - Savings/MMDA option allows 6 transfers per month from savings to operating account for flexibility without penalty
  - Mercury partnership transition risk: Mercury moving from Evolve Bank partnership as historic partner, creating uncertainty about future rates and fund location
  - SVB crisis context: JPM emphasized their fortress balance sheet; during SVB/First Republic failures, JPM captured approximately 50% of deposit inflows, demonstrating stability

### GrowthX's Concerns and Next Steps

  - Yield gap is material: Mercury net yield (3.8-4.15% after 25bps fee) on $12M AUM significantly outperforms JPM options (3.6-3.95%), creating 20-55bps spread
  - CD complexity: quarterly rollover decisions and maturity management add operational overhead; hybrid approach discussed (mixing operating, MMDA, laddered CDs) but not pre-packaged
  - Acquisition scenario risk: locked capital in CDs inaccessible without 1-3% penalty; critical for growth-stage company with M&A optionality
  - Committed next steps: regroup internally to benchmark JPM against other major banks (Wells Fargo, Citibank, Bank of America), compare yields after fees, and decide on banking direction
