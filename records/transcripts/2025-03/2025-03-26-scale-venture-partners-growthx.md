## Summary

Scale Venture Partners partner Max Abram conducted an exploratory call with Marcel Santilli to understand GrowthX's business model ahead of a potential Series B round. Marcel outlined GrowthX's $12M Series A close, 74% Q4 gross margin, and AI-powered content marketing platform that automates 80% of work through expert managing directors. However, the conversation ended without a clear path forward—Marcel expressed that Scale VP's check size ($40M max) and information-sharing approach didn't align with GrowthX's Series B positioning and ongoing discussions with larger tier-one investors.

---

## Context

Scale Venture Partners is a 25-year-old business software specialist with a focus on leading AI investments. Max Abram specializes in automation and marketing technology, having been an early investor in Jasper (AI content writing) and more recently in Bland (video generation) and Vappy (voice bots). This was an introductory call to evaluate GrowthX as a potential Series B investment. Marcel Santilli, GrowthX's founder, was interested in explaining the business but made clear early that GrowthX was in the middle of Series B fundraising conversations with larger firms (Iconiq, Raylock, Bessemer) and wanted to ensure mutual fit before sharing deep operational details.

## Key Topics

### GrowthX's Business Model and Technology

  - AI agent builds workflows as code with runtime, observability, and intervention layers
  - Learning layer in development to improve from interactions
  - Services delivered through industry experts (e.g., former TechCrunch editor-in-chief)
  - Managing directors serve as product developers, client-facing roles, and oversight
  - Near-zero customer acquisition costs due to reputation-based sales model
  - Workflows handle 80% of work, constantly adapting and improving

### Product Offerings and Revenue Streams

  - Main offerings: Content Workstream, SEO, and Custom solutions
  - \~80% of revenue from Content and SEO services
  - Example workflow: content strategy, creation, optimization, and ongoing management
  - Replacing traditional agency spend ($2/word) with more efficient, AI-driven processes

### Market Positioning and Competitive Advantage

  - Targeting $520B/year content marketing and services market
  - Displacing agency spend and augmenting internal teams
  - Value proposition: higher quality, faster output, and cost-efficiency
  - Case studies: Ramp (increased output), Abnormal Security ($3.2M content syndication budget)

### Growth Strategy and Funding

  - Controlled growth approach: $2M to $20M in revenue vs. rapid $2M to $50M
  - Focusing on building strong foundation and data collection
  - Preparing for Series B round by end of year
  - High interest from top-tier VCs (Iconiq, Raylock, Bessemer)

### Scale VP's Investment Approach

  - 25-year-old firm focusing on business software
  - Check sizes: $5M to $40M, leading rounds and taking board seats
  - Recent investments in AI: Jasper (content writing), Bland (video generation), Vappy (voice bots)
