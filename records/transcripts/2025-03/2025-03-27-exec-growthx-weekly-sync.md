## Context

This is a weekly strategic alignment meeting between GrowthX's content and marketing team and Sean Linehan from Exec, a B2B SaaS company building AI-powered sales tools including role-play and prospecting capabilities. Exec operates under enterprise contracts with existing customers, which restricts data usage for content and marketing. GrowthX is exploring how to build a content marketing engine for Exec's product—targeting sales leaders and reps with valuable, problem-focused content that introduces Exec's brand as a solution. Kyle Gaudreau brings experience from Homebase's content strategy (including the "pink cloud content" philosophy), and the team is trying to find angles where Exec can rank for high-intent search terms without relying on proprietary customer data.

## Key Topics

### Content Strategy for Sales Objections

  - Identified \~20 relevant topics related to sales objections and GPT applications
  - Plan to create content incorporating GPT functionality within the page
  - Add CTAs around the GPT element to drive sign-ups for AI role-play product
  - Aim: Create value-add content that ranks well and introduces Exec's brand to ICP

### Compensation Calculator Tool

  - Sean built a comprehensive sales compensation simulator
  - Features: Salary, commission, quota ramp, guarantees, and accelerators
  - Potential uses:
      - Content marketing around sales comp planning
      - SEO targeting "sales comp calculator" keyword
      - Demonstrating ROI of effective sales onboarding

### Content Marketing Opportunities

  - Explore "pink cloud content" approach (Kyle's framing from Homebase): highly relevant to the audience but not directly about the product
  - Consider creating roundup pieces for AI prospecting tools to capture search intent and surface Exec's brand
  - Investigate programmatic variants of the compensation calculator for different cities with region-specific salary benchmarks
  - Add subtle, useful content within the calculator interface (e.g., subheadings, tooltips, or hidden content to avoid cluttering the UI)

### Data Utilization and Constraints

  - Unable to use customer data for content due to enterprise contract restrictions
  - Exploring alternative data sources for potential stat-based blog posts
  - Consider scraping public data (e.g., government websites) for city-specific salary information
