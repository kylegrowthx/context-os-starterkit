## Key Topics

### Content Production Progress

Prateek confirmed 44 articles published on track; full weekday content bucket covered. Out of 25 articles in week three, Daniel helped identify high-conversion, enterprise-specific topics (vs. generic high-volume keywords). Focus is on quality audience match: "even two visitors who are the right visitors is better than high volume." Next: run internal linking retrospective after all articles published to boost indexing without waiting for new content.

### Blog Optimization: Mobile-First Lead Magnet Problem

Current design has lead magnets only on right sidebar—fine for desktop but below the fold on mobile, where 70% of Goodcall's traffic lives. Prateek proposed dynamic in-content insertion: drop lead magnets after every 5–10 paragraphs. Goodcall using free 14-day AI agent trial as the conversion funnel; Bob emphasized direct signup (no SDR motion required currently). Concern: how to track which magnet variant / article drives signup.

### Blog Design and Author Attribution

Blog design is "plain vanilla and boring." Daniel offered existing CTA + left-content layout template for rapid redesign. Currently all articles authored by Daniel (because he's been the only one pushing to Webflow); clarified this can change. Template vs. non-template question: articles intentionally un-templated to avoid Google penalization, but this blocks dynamic content insertion.

### CMS vs. Static Webflow Debate

Bob and Daniel showed example of existing CMS work: "Business Productivity" section uses collections with embedded CTAs, internal linking, and unified tracking. For blog: Goodcall's tech team was advised not to use Webflow CMS, opting for individual static pages instead. Now both CTAs and internal linking are broken at scale. Bob flagged this as a recommendation decision that may need reversal; Daniel has a blog CMS template ready. Prateek to check with Goodcall tech team on feasibility.

### Indexing Risk and Site Architecture Changes

All 44 articles just published; concern about losing index gains if URL structure changes during CMS migration. Prateek plans to check indexing status before any architecture changes. Internal linking requires CMS to solve properly; without it, can't dynamically generate "related articles" sections like the visa client example (tag-based category pages).
