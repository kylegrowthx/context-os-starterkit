## Key Topics

### Website Security and SEO Impact

- Pixis website suffered two security attacks: first attempted admin login brute-force, second injected fake metadata (Chinese, Japanese, Korean product info) that Google Search Console picked up
- Previous contractor transitions (person on notice in India) contributed to sluggish incident response; internal IT team now owns website DevOps
- Evan is re-indexing content with Google Search Console and cleaning up old blog posts with zero impressions; rate limiting slows recovery
- SEO impact: "LLM for marketing" keyword rankings dropped temporarily post-attack; team focused on SEO fundamentals (meta titles, slugs, focus keywords)

### SEO Performance Tracking

- New Looker report live for Pixis, pulling real-time Google Search Console data; grouped by custom weeks (Wed-Tue to match meeting cadence)
- Strong keyword performance: "LLM for marketing" showing 20 searches/month with 55% CTR; other keywords with decent CTR include "AI use cases in e-commerce" and "low-code AI"
- GA4 access still pending (property not visible in account list despite being on other accounts); once resolved, report will include LLM referral traffic (perplexity.ai, ChatGPT, other models)
- Looker report includes week-over-week comparison capability; team exploring data box integration for executive-level reporting

### Content Strategy and Optimization

- GrowthX updating meta titles, slugs, and focus keywords for old blog content; many lack clear focus keywords or proper optimization
- Internal/external link ratio being rebalanced to ~1:1 (Wikipedia-style); many old posts had 8-10 internal links with 0-1 external
- 74 articles in "considering" phase for future content pipeline; new account manager hired to help with editing and stats articles
- Glossary pages (10-15/week) publishing gradually to avoid triggering Google's spam filters; focus on fundamentals before pushing for conversion attribution

### AI and AEO (AI Engine Optimization)

- Exploration of triprofound.com ($2k/month): crawls 10-100 queries per input term across all major AI models, shows AI search rankings and source articles for link outreach intelligence
- Early learnings from triprofound: link-building strategy should focus on older/established publishers (10+ year old links work best with AI engines) due to safety preferences vs Google's ranking approach
- LLM traffic to Pixis already measurable: sales rep noted prospect came through "AI prompts for marketing" article; tools like Koala, Sift, Factors tracking this
- GrowthX considering in-house AEO tracking and potentially offering AEO as a service to clients; Jacob's arrival (ex-G2 SEO lead) supports this strategic pivot

### Team Updates and Collaboration

- Jacob joined GrowthX as managing director this week with deep SEO and SaaS marketing background from G2; will support Pixis strategy and bring fresh perspective on AEO, link building, and content fundamentals
- Figma template editing friction: Jimena (designer) invited aida@growthxlabs.com to project but assets locked read-only; can download but can't edit text; Evan needs bulk featured image variants
- Canva solution: Evan will set up Pixis Canva account and give Aida access; more suitable for rapid design iteration than Figma's constraints
- Account manager recently onboarded for additional content editing support
