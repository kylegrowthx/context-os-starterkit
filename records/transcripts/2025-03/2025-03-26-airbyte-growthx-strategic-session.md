## Summary

GrowthX presented Airbyte with a comprehensive content strategy roadmap based on keyword gap analysis across competitor sites, identifying seven content clusters (data integration, analytics, pipelines, warehousing, APIs, governance, quality) with over 200-volume opportunities. The team aligned on a 50/50 split between content refreshes and new pieces, targeting 10 articles per week at 1,500 words average (50,000 word/month cap), starting with 2 calibration articles to establish tone and voice before scaling. Priority refresh areas identified include data engineering resources and "how to sync" categories, where recent layout changes have impacted rankings and non-branded traffic is declining due to search market shifts.

## Context

Airbyte is a leading data integration platform helping enterprises move data between sources and destinations. This was a strategic planning session between GrowthX (content and SEO agency) and Airbyte's product marketing team to define Airbyte's organic content strategy for 2025. The engagement began with GrowthX conducting keyword gap analysis against Airbyte's competitors (including Talend, Airbyte's positioning, and others), using SEO research to identify content opportunities Airbyte isn't currently capturing. This session marked a transition from research into execution planning: defining how many articles per week, what types of refreshes versus new content, and how to measure performance. The Airbyte team (Mario, Nithin, Tanmay) needed alignment on workload and resourcing before kickoff, while the GrowthX team (David, Kyle, Jakub) was positioned to guide the execution workflow and measure success.

## Key Topics

### Content Strategy Overview

  - Keyword gap analysis conducted using competitor data
  - Content OS created with keyword opportunities grouped into logical clusters
  - Clusters include: data integration, analytics, pipelines, warehousing, APIs, governance, and quality
  - Initial focus on refreshing existing content while also creating some new pieces
  - Cautious approach to capitalize on current rankings before expanding

### Content Refresh Process

  - Identify refresh opportunities (e.g., articles ranking 2-5 page range for relevant keywords)
  - Three refresh types: wholesale rewrite, expansion-only, or hybrid approach
  - Use SEO testing tool to track performance of refreshed content cohorts
  - Balance between new content and refreshes (aim for 50/50 split)

### Priority Areas and Insights

  - Data engineering resources and "how to sync" categories identified as key areas for refreshes
  - Recent layout changes in "how to sync" category impacted rankings
  - Non-branded traffic declining due to search trends and Google's market share shift
  - Opportunity to expand on A to B connector pages and related use cases

### Content Production and Workflow

  - Start with 2-3 articles per week, ramping up to 5-10 as process is refined
  - Target average of 1,500 words per article
  - 50,000 word monthly limit
  - Use SEO testing tool and Google Search Console for performance tracking

### Competitive Content Strategies

  - Potential to expand on comparison articles and listicles
  - Consider creating more alternatives content (e.g., "Airbyte vs. X" articles)
  - Evaluate lower-funnel content opportunities (e.g., pricing comparisons)
