## Context

GrowthX is a 14-week-old AI-powered content and knowledge work services company founded by Marcel Santilli and Daniel, with Karim (CTO from Ramp) joining as investor, board member, and co-founder during the Series A. The company was built on Marcel's experience as a CMO at multiple companies and his observation that enterprise brands with massive marketing budgets—IBM, Twitter, Amazon—could become the biggest authority in their space through owned content and research but choose not to, due to the friction of scaling expert knowledge work. ### Product & Architecture

- **Workflows-as-code model**: AI coding agent builds expert-architected workflows in code (not low-code/UI), auto-documents them, and executes complex multi-step processes in minutes vs. weeks
- **Three-layer infrastructure**: Task hub (orchestration), AI workflow engine (execution, already built and working at scale), and learning engine (extracts expertise data from outcomes and expert feedback to fine-tune task-specific models)
- **Expertise extraction**: Every engagement generates data—expert reviews of outputs, workflow iterations, changes made by clients—which feeds back into model training, positioning GrowthX to offer platform/API access later
- **Outcome-first positioning**: Delivers actual results (content published, research completed, hiring done) rather than just software to operate, reducing customer risk and decision friction
- **Versatility**: Initially built for content (topic research, outline generation, content enrichment, repurposing, SEO, link-building campaigns), but the architecture also powers hiring workflows (resume parsing, interview scoring, candidate disqualification)

### Customer Base & Traction

- **Current customers**: Ramp (perennials, expense category pages, ad campaigns, domain authority content), Reddit (research and ad-copy workflows), Webflow, Stack Blitz (programmatic landing pages + pre-prompted app generation), Yellow AI, Sentinel One, and others
- **Investor-customers**: Five current clients also invested in the Series A, signaling product-market fit
- **ICP**: Mid to late-stage tech companies ($100M+) that have raised Series A/B, are scaling quickly, and need to expand content/organic channels or have already proven out one growth channel and want to scale it
- **Non-ICP**: Early-stage companies with no PMF or growth experience that haven't tried to grow themselves; lack the urgency and conviction to commit
- **Verticals**: E-commerce, travel, healthcare, fintech, AI tools, developer tools, security
- **Deal structure**: Minimum $108K/year; most proposals $144K–$480K/year depending on scope; can scale with additional work streams
- **Pipeline velocity**: 60+ deals in pipeline; currently executing 2 launches per week (scaling to 3 by late April); nearly booked through April (Webflow April 14, Ramp April 21)

### Team & Organization

- **Leadership**: Marcel Santilli (CEO, ex-CMO, ex-HashiCorp), Daniel (CTO, co-founder), Karim (board member and investor from Ramp, also CTO there)
- **Recent hires**: Dave (from Fanatics/Ramp, head of SEO & content, building e-commerce pod); Jacob (from G2, built content infrastructure); Kirkland (from ClickUp, built AI internal tooling); Matthew (ex-editor-in-chief at TechCrunch); Felipe (early Airbnb, early Stripe); Glenn (engineer #6 at HashiCorp, helped build Terraform)
- **Organization structure**: Managing directors (with business development, sales, product, and client-facing responsibilities; modeled on legal firm partners with carry) oversee lean, specialized pods underneath
- **Hiring velocity**: 20 people hired in two weeks; hiring internationally to access top talent; cash-heavy offers and 25% exercise cost on preferred shares (employee-friendly)

### Financial Model & Economics

- **Current state**: $6.6M ARR as of March 12, 2025 (14 weeks post-incorporation); profitable with capital in bank; raised $12M Series A from Madrona Venture Group
- **Margins**: 74% in Q4 2024; intentionally dipping to 54% in H1 2025 (managing directors' costs booked to delivery to prove scalability); recovering to 61% by year-end, 71%+ by 2026
- **Projection**: $22–23M ARR by year-end if close rate remains constant (two deals per week); does NOT assume any marketing investment or platform/API consumption revenue
- **Valuation strategy**: Deliberately kept Series A valuation lower and exercise costs low to benefit early employees; planning Series B later in 2025 to "optimize valuation"
- **Investor selection**: Chose Madrona/Karim in Series A for trust and operational alignment (Marcel had worked with Karim for two years) over bidding war

### Go-to-Market & Positioning

- **Value proposition**: Addresses the gap between self-serve software (requires hiring full team, still needs services) and traditional agencies (expensive, slow, no scalability infrastructure). GrowthX bundles expert talent + automation + scalability at 10–20 cents per word.
- **Customer adoption speed**: Companies quickly understand the value because it's positioned as a "work stream" solving a bundle of tasks (e.g., "fractional CMO + SEO head + editor-in-chief + production") rather than a specific job. Closes deals fast because customers see immediate outcome delivery vs. tool adoption friction.
- **Vertical specialization**: Building vertical-focused pods (e.g., Dave's e-commerce pod) and considering small acquisitions of niche agencies at low EBITDA multiples to accelerate distribution and build deep expertise per vertical
- **Platform vision**: Currently selling managed services; exploring platform/API access with a few customers (e.g., Yellow AI wants to use platform directly, not services). Offering workflow and API access later in roadmap but being thoughtful about timing.

### Founder Thesis & Philosophy

- **Content is king**: Content is not low-value; it is "everything we do." It's the embodiment of knowledge work, applies across functions, and has a $500B annual spend
- **Expert + AI + Scale**: Experts don't scale; pure AI is unreliable; but experts architecting workflows + AI executing + learning layer refining creates something new. This is different from hiring freelancers or agencies.
- **Infrastructure for scale**: Learning from HashiCorp and Terraform: knowledge work needs the equivalent of "infrastructure-as-code"—workflows-as-code—to scale without accumulating team bloat
- **Services done right**: Don't hide services; own it. High-margin, outcome-driven services backed by IP and learning. Models like Palantir (services but called something else) work, but better to be transparent
- **Transparent about traction**: Will be visible on market (approaching Series B) but building trust relationships with investors (like Bessemer) over months rather than rushing funding rounds
