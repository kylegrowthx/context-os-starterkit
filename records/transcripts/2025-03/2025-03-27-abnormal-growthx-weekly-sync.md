## Summary

GrowthX and Abnormal Security reviewed two calibration articles to refine content voice and strategy, with plans to ramp to five new articles weekly starting the following week. The team discussed a major domain migration from abnormalsecurity.com to abnormal.ai on April 16th, identifying the need for SEO mitigation recommendations to handle expected volatility in the first six days post-migration. A key strategic shift emerged around AI-focused content: shifting away from email deliverability topics (inspired by MailTrap) toward behavioral AI and human vulnerability angles, with potential for a major content pillar around how humans are the weakest link in security. The team also clarified image handling processes and agreed to use hidden blog functionality for 50-75% of new content to balance SEO-driven articles with high-value public-facing thought leadership.

## Context

Abnormal Security is a cybersecurity company focused on AI-powered email and identity threat detection. GrowthX is providing content marketing services to support Abnormal's SEO strategy as part of an ongoing engagement. This was a weekly sync meeting where GrowthX presented initial calibration articles for review and feedback. Abnormal is in a major platform transition—not only launching new content through GrowthX, but also rebranding/migrating their main domain from abnormalsecurity.com to abnormal.ai on April 16th, requiring parallel efforts to keep SEO momentum during the transition. Jade Hill is managing the content strategy from Abnormal's side and emphasized the tension between publishing SEO-focused content and protecting Abnormal's brand perception (e.g., not publishing about spam or email deliverability topics on the main blog). Jason Gong, a new GrowthX hire with a security background and YC startup experience, was introduced in this meeting as the managing director overseeing Sydney Go's team on the Abnormal account.
