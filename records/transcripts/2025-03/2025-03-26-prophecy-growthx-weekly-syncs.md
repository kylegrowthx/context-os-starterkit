### Google Algorithm Updates and Domain Authority

Google has been running algorithm updates for three weeks, expected to conclude end of week. Despite this, Prophecy's content shows no negative impact, which is encouraging. The algorithm changes focus on domain knowledge—whether a company is authoritatively discussing topics they should be discussing (e.g., Prophecy shouldn't discuss expense management). Since Prophecy's content is well-aligned with their domain, the updates should not negatively affect rankings.

### Analytics and Measurement Refinement

Vivek set up a basic GA4 exploration to understand how many people land on articles and take significant actions (demo requests, content downloads, form fills). He's checking with Evan on whether Fathom's default significant action definitions align with Prophecy's needs and if any modifications are needed. The team acknowledged that new tracking definitions start going forward and don't retroactively process old data. Matt and Kyle discussed the need to move beyond correlation (we published and got interest) toward causation through multi-touch analysis, potentially using Qualified data to identify ICP engagement on pages. Kyle noted that meaningful conversion attribution typically takes 4+ months of data and advocated for looking at first-touch and last-touch patterns to understand the buyer journey across tools.

### Strategic Content Direction and Positioning Shift

Matt outlined a significant pivot in Prophecy's content strategy: moving from focus on pure data engineering and fine points toward data preparation in cloud contexts. The core narrative is that data engineering requires substantial resources and time; data analysts working independently can cause problems; Prophecy is the platform that brings both together with self-service capabilities and governance. This shift requires rebalancing content to reach not just data engineers but also data analysts, scientists, and business users. However, the team clarified that while educational content should touch all stakeholders, conversion-focused content should still primarily target platform teams and data architecture roles—the people who make purchasing decisions. Matt will share positioning reference points and industry influencers Matt and Kyle should surface.
