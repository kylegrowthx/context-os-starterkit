## Summary

GrowthX kicked off a comprehensive content and SEO engagement with Airbyte, a data infrastructure platform targeting enterprise customers (2000+ employees, $5-10B+ revenue) and aiming for $21M ARR by year-end (up from $9M). The team aligned on Airbyte's strategic pivot toward AI-first, self-hosted solutions with emphasis on first-party data for model training, and agreed that GrowthX will take over content production from their current agency while collaborating on keyword research and strategy. Key focus areas include shifting to bottom-of-funnel (BOFU) content, addressing keyword cannibalization, leveraging organic content assets (CEO podcasts, webinars, customer calls), and establishing weekly syncs with measurement tied to qualified pipeline (stage 2 opportunities) and AI search traffic growth.

---

## Context

Airbyte is an open-source data integration platform experiencing rapid growth—from $2M to $9M ARR in roughly one year, almost entirely organic. The company is now targeting enterprise transformation: relocating from cloud-centric to on-premise, self-hosted deployment models, with the strategic goal of enabling large enterprises (Disney, Adobe, major financial institutions) to own their data infrastructure while leveraging Airbyte's 500+ connectors for AI model training without vendor lock-in. Mario Moscatiello (Head of Growth, early investor, and recent full-time joiner) and Tanmay Sarkar (Head of SEO/Content/Paid Media) are driving the engagement. This is GrowthX's first true Friday kickoff and represents a significant shift in how Airbyte wants to scale: from organic-only growth to a structured content engine that can support their enterprise go-to-market while protecting brand terms and capturing emerging AI search traffic.

## Key Topics

### Airbyte's Business Context and Goals

  - Experienced rapid growth: $2M to $9M ARR in about a year, mostly organic
  - Targeting 3X growth to $21M ARR by year-end (50% enterprise, 50% self-serve)
  - Shifting from cloud-based to on-premise, self-hosted enterprise solutions
  - Positioning as an AI-first data infrastructure provider, enabling first-party data usage for AI model training

### Target Audience and Messaging

  - Primary: Data engineers, AI engineers, and their leadership
  - Secondary: System integrators (e.g., Deloitte)
  - Key selling points: Open-source, data sovereignty, vendor lock-in prevention
  - Challenge: Shifting perception from point-to-point ETL to core AI infrastructure provider

### Content Strategy and Challenges

  - Current volume: 30-50 articles/month, manually written by technical experts
  - Shift towards more bottom-of-funnel (BOFU) content in recent months
  - Issues with keyword cannibalization and topic overlap
  - Need for higher-quality, technical content that resonates with the audience
  - Opportunity to leverage CEO's podcast, customer calls, and weekly demo webinars for content

### SEO and Performance Measurement

  - Recently implemented HubSpot for attribution
  - Focus on pipeline influence and branded organic traffic growth
  - Desire for more sophisticated measurement of content impact on conversions

### GrowthX Engagement Scope

  - Taking over content production from current agency
  - Collaborating on keyword research and content strategy
  - Potential for writer support for more technical or human-touch pieces
  - Focus on content refresh, consolidation, and enrichment of existing materials
  - Exploring new content formats (e.g., podcast summaries, webinar recaps)
