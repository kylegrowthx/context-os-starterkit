### Process Improvement Example: Outgo Inbound Automation

Guy's primary accomplishment at Outgo demonstrates his end-to-end process design thinking:

**Challenge:** Outgo had economics problems with outbound-heavy sales team trying to close small-deal customers from a long tail of leads. CAC was too high. When Guy joined, there was no operational structure; sales team was manually creating deals and contacts.

**Solution:** Designed and built an end-to-end automated inbound machine:
  - Interest form on website (initially HubSpot embedded form, switched to native Webflow form)
  - **Lead routing:** Automated routing based on deal size into different sequences
  - **12-step task sequence in HubSpot:** Guides leads through interest form submission to final activation, using automated emails and manual sales team touchpoints based on deal size
  - **Slack integration:** When deals hit underwriting stage, a Slack notification thread is spawned so risk and sales teams can discuss approvals/rejections synchronously (reduces back-and-forth delays)
  - **Webhook automation:** Integrated with internal "Ops tool" (internal product managing risk and underwriting). Deal stages automatically progress in HubSpot as customers move through the application funnel—no manual sales involvement needed
  - **Result:** Reduced friction through the funnel, improved conversion rates, and made sales team more productive by automating tedious data entry and status updates

**Key insight:** Guy emphasized that Outgo operates in a complex domain (credit/lending with intensive application funnel), but automation still dramatically improved speed and efficiency

  - **Lead magnets and conversion elements:** New service offering to help clients generate demand

  - **Implementation complexity:** These services introduce operational complexity (need dedicated resources, new skillsets, client success requirements). Kyle mentioned potentially dedicating half an engineer's time to one client use case to test model before scaling
  - **Strategic framing:** Kyle is intentional about keeping editorial and programmatic SEO as the "base layer" while exploring new offerings—lots of runway within SEO alone, so expansion should complement (not replace) core offering. Goal: diversify revenue streams, strengthen moat, provide more value to clients
