## Key Topics

### Content Strategy and Knowledge Hub Structure

Innerwell and GrowthX decided to use the existing blog structure for knowledge hub content rather than creating a separate section, optimizing for both speed and SEO. The blog will include table of contents with internal links to improve user navigation and inform SEO. Content will feature "medically reviewed by" attribution but doctors won't be listed as primary authors. The team is considering 2-3 consistent authors for different content categories to balance SEO authority with medical credibility. Target is 10-15 medically reviewed articles per week, with an April cadence of 3 knowledge hub articles ready by the end of the first week, then ramping to 5 articles/week for the last two weeks of April.

### Location Pages and Services Architecture

The team will draft all 23 location pages by the first week of April, each focused on services offered in that specific location. Location pages will feature auto-populating clinician information to reduce manual updates. The structure allows Innerwell to highlight new or underutilized clinicians through pinning or featured placement. Innerwell flagged that EMDR and psychiatric services were missing from initial AI-generated templates, requiring workflow adjustments. For states where Innerwell doesn't yet operate, GrowthX recommends creating "coming soon" pages with lead capture (email waitlist) to build interest before launch. The next phase in mid-April will launch service+location pages (e.g., "Therapy in San Francisco"), which will link from the broader state pages.

### Link Acquisition Through Original Data

GrowthX proposed creating stats pages on mental health topics, leveraging Innerwell's proprietary patient outcome data to generate unique, link-worthy content. Innerwell shared key statistics: 87% of patients report improvement within 4 weeks, 69% report depression reduction after 10 weeks, and 16% report anxiety reduction after 10 weeks. These data points are rare in publicly available research on ketamine-assisted and mean therapy, making them valuable for backlinking. The team discussed patient surveys as an additional data source to explore questions like "What percentage of your patients tried psychedelics before mean therapy?" GrowthX will brainstorm specific data points to collect and help amplify findings across the web to drive referral traffic.
