## Key Topics

### Content Performance Update

- Last week showed slight increase despite Google core update still rolling out; full impact will be visible in 2-3 weeks.
- Early reports from LinkedIn show more AI overviews appearing in search results, which has caused some sites to drop rankings.
- High-engagement content analysis revealed common patterns. Successful topics include practical/resourceful content (templates like "15 Dental Point Reminder Templates," "Top Issues in Dentistry") and authoritative content ("What Dental Assistants Are Not Allowed to Do").
- Structural elements driving engagement: introductions that are direct and to-the-point, descriptive subheadings focused on reader pain points (e.g., "Why Is X Important" rather than "Importance of X"), numbered lists, and strategic bold formatting (especially useful for AI extraction from bullet lists).

### LLM Search Impact

- Best SEO practices and page 1 rankings appear to be the primary driver of LLM visibility; Jakub noted from Softer experience that featured snippets are often pulled directly by different LLMs and Google snippets (though these evolve).
- Beyond page 1 rankings, Jakub observed that broad SERP coverage helps — appearing on own branded pages AND on competitor comparison pages (e.g., "Best CRM" pages, Salesforce lists on others' sites).
- Emerging signals for LLM ranking (partially validated): brand mentions, schema markup (helps LLMs extract structured data), and reviews (strong review presence signals reputation/trustworthiness).
- Kyle emphasized caution about overhyped "LLM hacks" on LinkedIn; the core strategy remains: understand user intent, create valuable content, optimize for traditional SEO, and these fundamentals will drive LLM visibility across platforms.

### Teero's Growth Progress

- Now in week 11 of publishing (started early January); Teero is Aida's first client with this kind of exponential growth trajectory.
- Reached ~200 daily sessions, which is ahead of typical pace for domain authority and industry volume; usually takes longer to hit this threshold.
- Growth is not linear; SEO typically shows lumpy growth with distinct inflection points. Kyle expects ~1 major inflection point per quarter given typical program dynamics; Teero's current momentum is strong.

### Conversion Optimization

- CTA and panel implementation is in progress with Frank (Teero). Aida followed up as she wants this live to convert blog traffic into leads; Nate confirmed it's on Frank's list for this week with likely completion next week.
- HubSpot CRM access: Nate will grant GrowthX team access via team email so Aida and team can track leads and see conversion reports from blog traffic. This closes the loop between content and lead generation.

### Content Strategy Focus Areas

**1. AI Content for Teero's Second Product:** Nate wants to establish authority before the product launch. Content should cover AI's impact on dental practice management workflows, tools for practice managers, etc.

**2. W2 Employment Model Positioning (Priority):** Teero will be the largest employer of dental hygienists in the US by end of year — a massive differentiator that competitors don't have. However, Teero currently ranks nowhere for W2-related searches despite competitors ranking. Nate spoke to a hygienist today who found competitor info via Perplexity but not Teero.

Multiple content angles identified:
- Compliance (some states legally require W2 over 1099)
- Tax simplification (no hassle filing for hygienists)
- Insurance and benefits (employer-provided health coverage, 401k)
- Positioning as "Why You Should Only Accept W2 Employment" (per Dental Hygienist Association guidance)

Competitor research: 2-3 smaller W2 competitors rank and speak to this topic; GrowthX will audit their approach and create multiple articles from different stakeholder angles (hygienist perspective, practice manager perspective).
