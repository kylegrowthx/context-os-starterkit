## Summary

Aftershoot and GrowthX reviewed content quality feedback on the first two published articles, focusing on making copy more actionable and less formal, while defining the content strategy for Q1 and Q2. The team aligned on a phased approach: Phase 1 (now through Q2) targets core professional photographers with articles on culling, editing, retouching, and business growth, while Phase 2 launches with a free raw editing tool in Q2/Q3 that will unlock hundreds of new article topics. ## Context

Aftershoot is a photo editing and culling software company that GrowthX is providing B2B content marketing services to. The content strategy centers on serving professional photographers looking to scale their businesses and improve their editing workflows. This was a weekly check-in to review initial article performance and align on content production processes before scaling volume. The conversation reflects early-stage calibration on voice, sourcing workflows, and strategy constraints from Aftershoot's upcoming product roadmap.

## Relevance

**To GrowthX Delivery:**
- Content feedback revealed formality/jargon issues that require rewriting sections for clarity (especially technical topics like presets vs. looks)
- Successful content needs 2-3 rounds of iteration; first drafts are on-point structurally but need simplification and deeper explanations
- Photography audience expects actionable advice ("how to use golden hour" not "when golden hour occurs")

- Volume potential: hundreds of articles per month once free tool launches; currently constrained by Phase 1 strategy
- Risk: Lightroom/Photoshop content historically backfired—Aftershoot's own guides positioned them as competitors to their own product, internal and external feedback was clear to stop

**To Aftershoot Account Health:**
- Team is engaged, responsive, and collaborative on iteration and process design
- Clear alignment on content pillars and avoiding feature-comparison content that cannibalizes their product
- Proactive about workflow (image sourcing, expert quotes, feedback loops) rather than reactive complaints

## Key Topics

### Content Feedback and Refinement

  - Articles generally on-point, but some sections too formal/jargony
  - Need for simpler language and more in-depth explanations with examples
  - Make content more actionable, focusing on practical application for photographers
  - Add photographer quotes to increase authenticity and expertise

### Image Sourcing Workflow

  - Combination of GrowthX sourcing obvious images and Aftershoot team sourcing specific ones
  - Flag image needs during writing process to parallelize sourcing efforts
  - Option to publish without images initially and update later to avoid delays

### Content Focus and Strategy

  - Concentrate on culling, editing, retouching, and professional business growth topics
  - Avoid Lightroom/Photoshop content due to potential customer confusion
  - Prepare for expansion into broader topics with upcoming free raw editing tool launch (Q2/Q3)

### Scaling Content Production

  - Implement system for gathering photographer quotes (e.g., Google Forms, help a B2B writer)
  - Use existing content clusters and high-converting articles as guides for new topics
  - Plan for increased content volume (potentially hundreds per month) after new tool launch
