- GrowthX uses Mercury's Choice Financial banking partner and is unaffected by Mercury's transition away from Evolve Bank to Column
- GrowthX's FDIC insurance coverage extends to $50M through sweep networks (not the $5M stated on Mercury's website)

## Key Topics

### Yield Comparison: Mercury vs JPMorgan Chase

  - JPM US Treasury Fund yield: 4.1%
  - Mercury's net yield after management fee: 4.03%
  - Difference: \~8 basis points (\~$10,000 annually for GrowthX's current balance)
  - Mercury's management fee non-negotiable, but offers easier transfers and operations

### Banking Partners and FDIC Insurance

  - GrowthX uses Choice Financial (Mercury partner), unaffected by Evolve Bank transition
  - Mercury diversifies banking partnerships for stability (Choice, Column, others)
  - FDIC insurance up to $50M through sweep networks (previously thought to be $5M)
  - Funds over $250k spread across multiple banks in $250k increments

### Management Fees and Deposit Tiers

  - Current tier (10-20M): 25 basis points management fee
  - If balance drops below 10M: Fee increases to 35 basis points
  - Fees automatically adjust based on balance fluctuations
  - No transfer fees for withdrawals from Mercury

### Additional Services and Comparisons

  - GrowthX plans to use RAMP for corporate credit cards due to more robust features
  - JPM offers white-labeled bill.com integration; Mercury has its own bill pay product ($35/month max)
  - Morgan Stanley partnership available for larger deposits (\>$50M), with 8 basis point fee
