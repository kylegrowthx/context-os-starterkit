## Overview

- StackBlitz is interested in partnering with GrowthX to rapidly expand their content and traffic, aiming to outpace competitors like Replit
- GrowthX proposed a two-pronged approach: template pages for long-tail keywords and potential fully-functional starter kits
- StackBlitz has valuable prompt data that can be leveraged for content strategy and categorization

## Key Topics

### Current StackBlitz Priorities

  - Eric (StackBlitz CEO) is focused on paid ads
  - Limited bandwidth for content creation internally
  - Desire to outsource growth efforts to an agency

### GrowthX's Proposed Strategy

  - Create template pages for long-tail keywords and app categories
  - Develop starter kits and showcase apps for users to fork
  - Leverage StackBlitz's existing prompt data for content ideation
  - Use AI and clustering to categorize and process user prompts
  - Scale rapidly to increase indexable pages (currently \~60) to thousands

### Data and Technical Collaboration

  - StackBlitz can provide a dump of \~10,000 initial prompts for analysis
  - GrowthX has clustering capabilities to identify trends and categories
  - StackBlitz offers an API to trigger builds from prompts
  - Potential to integrate template pages into StackBlitz's user registration flow

### Partnership Benefits

  - GrowthX aims to act as StackBlitz's "growth team as a service"
  - Focus on speed and aggressive growth to outpace competitors
  - Potential for case studies and public success stories
  - Accelerate time-to-value for StackBlitz users with targeted templates

### Competitive Landscape

  - StackBlitz wants to surpass Replit in traffic metrics
  - Current competition on unique users and ARR, but not on page views
  - Opportunity to create network effects and leverage proprietary data
