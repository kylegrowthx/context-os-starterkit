## Overview

- GrowthX aims to scale services through a "Services as Software" model, focusing on making the services team more efficient without burnout
- The product development approach centers on creating workflows and tools to automate and streamline the content creation process
- A multi-layered product architecture is being built, including workflow orchestration, content OS, and potential micro-apps for specific tasks
- The engineering team is developing a framework to rapidly create and iterate on workflows, with an emphasis on prompt engineering and API integration

---

## Key Topics

### Business Model and Team Approach

  - GrowthX is pursuing a "Services as Software" thesis to scale services efficiently
  - The services team acts as the product, with the goal of making them more efficient without burnout
  - Focus on creating a white-glove offering where GrowthX learns and does the work for clients
  - Engineering team growth is prioritized to support isolated, parallel development of multiple apps

### Product Architecture and Development

  - Current process grouped into strategy, production, and publishing phases
  - Developing a multi-layered product architecture:
      - Workflow orchestration layer (replacing AirOps)
      - Content OS (custom web UI for GrowthX-specific needs)
      - Potential micro-apps for focused tasks (e.g., image generation, translation)
  - Emphasis on breaking down tasks into smaller, more manageable workflows
  - Use of a custom framework for rapid workflow creation and iteration

### Engineering Workflow and Tools

  - Demonstrated a workflow creation process using plain text input
  - Automatic generation of workflow steps, prompts, and documentation
  - Integration with AI-assisted coding tools (e.g., Cursor) for efficient development
  - Focus on making programmers as productive as possible, then extending efficiency to the client team

### Future Plans and Phases

1.  Optimize agency operations and automate boring tasks
2.  Potentially offer access to clients or teams wanting to run internally
3.  Apply the same mindset to other areas (e.g., recruiting)
