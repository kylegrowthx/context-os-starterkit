## Context

Bubble is a no-code platform that has engaged GrowthX for content marketing services, including AI-powered featured image generation for their blog. This meeting brought together Eve Spears (Bubble's Design/Brand lead) with Aida Knežević and Katia Pembayun from GrowthX to review initial AI-generated image concepts. The meeting occurred as Bubble is undergoing a brand evolution toward positioning as a "powerful, trustworthy" solution rather than a playful, approachable platform, requiring visual assets to reinforce that positioning. GrowthX's role is to generate blog-featured images using AI pipelines that align with Bubble's brand guidelines and visual style.

## Overview

- **Refine Brand Tone:** Shift from a "playful" feel (e.g., storybook rockets) to a "serious, trustworthy" one, reflecting Bubble's brand evolution.
- **Adopt Key Visuals:** Incorporate "glass" textures and a secondary color palette to add visual interest and avoid a monotonous "sea of blue and purple."
- **Improve Technical Quality:** Increase image saturation and contrast to prevent a "washed out" look and ensure visual impact.
- **Streamline Workflow:** Use Slack for communication and provide multiple image options per draft for faster, more collaborative reviews.

---

## Key Topics

### Brand Alignment & Tone

  - **Problem:** Initial AI images felt "playful" and "weird," misaligning with Bubble's brand goal of being perceived as "powerful" and "trustworthy."
  - **Guidelines:**
      - **Avoid:** Storybook illustrations (e.g., rockets), text overlays (due to cropping issues on the blog), and overused AI tropes (e.g., magic wands).
      - **Embrace:** "Glass" textures (translucent white) and a secondary color palette to add visual interest beyond the primary blue/purple.
  - **Process:** GrowthX will provide multiple image options per draft for review. Bubble will stage content in the CMS to vary publication dates and avoid a sudden, jarring change in blog aesthetics.

### Technical & Aesthetic Feedback

  - **Problem:** Some images were "washed out" with poor contrast, lacking visual impact.
  - **Solution:** Increase saturation and contrast in the AI generation pipeline to create more visually interesting, non-monochromatic images.
  - **Tooling:** The current pipeline uses GPT for image generation, which may limit creative control. A new Figma brand guidelines page is in development and will be shared with GrowthX to inform future designs.
