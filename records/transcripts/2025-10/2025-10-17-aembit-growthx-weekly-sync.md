## Context

Aembit is a cybersecurity company focused on secure identity and access management, particularly around identity federation and authorization workflows. GrowthX has been executing a content marketing engagement for Aembit, producing blog articles and glossary pages to drive organic traffic and establish thought leadership on topics like MCP (Model Context Protocol), agentic AI security, and identity management.

In earlier feedback, Aembit had flagged that the content was over-focused on identity federation as a standalone topic, and some claims about Aembit's capabilities weren't verifiable against their public documentation. This meeting reviewed the process improvements implemented to address those concerns and discussed performance metrics and next priorities for the content program.

## Key Topics

### Content Strategy Correction

  - **Problem:** The previous workflow produced content with two issues:
      - An over-focus on identity federation.
      - False or unverified claims about Aembit's capabilities.
  - **Root Cause:** The source artifacts (writing guidelines, product docs) were overly focused on identity.
  - **Solution:** Two new artifacts now guide content creation:
    1.  **Writing Instructions:** Simpler guidelines with tone examples and specific conventions (e.g., no em dashes, MISI framework compliance).
    2.  **Aembit POV Document:** A consolidated source of truth on Aembit's security stance, built from past webinar articles.
  - **Validation Step:** The workflow now searches `aembit.io` for every Aembit-specific claim. If a claim is not found on the live site, it is removed from the draft.
  - **Alternative:** Kyle suggested an alternative using GrowthX's "Atlas" knowledge base to feed the engine with a curated set of source documents.
