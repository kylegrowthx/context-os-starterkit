## Summary

GrowthX and Goodwin Law aligned on a direct pushback strategy against Navan's DPA requirement, concluding that GrowthX's current services do not involve personal data processing and therefore do not require a DPA — only work with publicly available information and client-provided marketing documents. Jonathan Ng from Goodwin will join a Friday call with Navan's security and procurement teams to make this argument, while GrowthX will prepare a list of comparable enterprise clients (Webflow, SentinelOne, Okta, Udemy) who don't require DPAs. For Q1 product launch, GrowthX will work with Goodwin to develop boilerplate DPA, privacy policy, and Terms of Service templates.

---

## Context

Navan, an enterprise travel and expense management platform, has made a Data Processing Agreement (DPA) a blocking requirement for their Master Service Agreement with GrowthX. This is the first time GrowthX has encountered a client insisting on a DPA. GrowthX brought in Jonathan Ng, a privacy attorney from Goodwin Law, to evaluate whether a DPA is legally necessary. Goodwin's assessment: GrowthX is not a "processor" under privacy law because it handles only public information (websites) and client-provided marketing documents (brand guidelines, personas) — never customer data, employee data, or personally identifiable information. The urgency is moderate: Navan wants to complete the deal by early November, and procurement/security are the blocking stakeholders.

## Key Topics

### Navan DPA Negotiation

  - Navan is requiring a DPA, which is blocking the MSA.
  - **GrowthX's current services do not require a DPA:**
      - Work uses public info (websites) or client-provided marketing docs (brand guidelines, personas).
      - No access to client customer data or PII.
      - GrowthX has successfully pushed back on DPAs with other enterprise clients (e.g., Webflow, Okta).
  - **Goodwin's assessment (John Ng):**
      - The DPA is "ludicrous" for current services; GrowthX is not a "processor."
      - Navan's conservative stance likely stems from CCPA updates that include B2B data.
      - Access to Navan's CMS or Google Analytics does not make GrowthX a processor, as Navan remains the controller of its own systems.
  - **Decision:** Shift from redlining to direct pushback.
      - **Rationale:** Redlining an irrelevant DPA is inefficient and strategically weak, as it reveals GrowthX cannot meet its terms. It's better to negotiate a relevant DPA later for the future product.

### Future Product Privacy & Legal Needs

  - GrowthX's product launch (Q1) will require a boilerplate DPA, privacy policy, and Terms of Service (ToS).
  - **Product context:**
      - B2B SaaS with a potential self-serve funnel.
      - May process business contact info, device fingerprinting (IP, browser), and passwords.
  - **Legal strategy for the product:**
      - Position GrowthX as a "processor" only, not a "controller."
      - Clarify in the privacy policy that services are for business use and governed by the DPA.
  - GrowthX will provide Goodwin with a product walkthrough and roadmap to inform document creation.
