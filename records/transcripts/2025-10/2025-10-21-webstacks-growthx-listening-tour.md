## Summary

Marcel conducted a listening tour with Webstacks CEO Jesse Schor to assess renewal viability on their current 3-month contract. While Webstacks is highly satisfied with GrowthX's quality (8–9/10) and performance (8–9/10), they perceive output as limited (6/10) due to a recent shift to word-count-based scope. Jesse emphasized that Webstacks values GrowthX's strategic guidance over execution, noting that the core differentiator is GrowthX's cross-client insights—not operational delivery, which could be sourced cheaper elsewhere. To convert to an annual contract, GrowthX must provide a clear 6–12 month strategic roadmap that enables Webstacks' leadership to justify the investment.

---

## Context

Webstacks is a web development and design agency led by CEO Jesse Schor and co-founder Nikon. GrowthX has been working with Webstacks for several months on content marketing and SEO strategy, currently under a 3-month rolling contract with a dedicated team including a director, strategist (Jacob), writer (Rachel), and account manager (Jessalyn). The engagement has been highly successful—Webstacks has seen significant increases in organic traffic and LLM search visibility, which correlates with GrowthX's content work. However, the relationship is at an inflection point: Webstacks' internal leadership is questioning whether to continue at higher investment levels, and GrowthX needs to move from a transactional (monthly renewal) to a committed (annual) contract structure to justify maintaining a senior team on a below-market engagement value.

**To GrowthX Delivery:**

## Overview

- **Roadmap Dependency:** The 6-week "AI for web management" sprint proved that clear roadmaps drive both execution quality and internal stakeholder buy-in. Webstacks needs a 6–12 month strategic roadmap to justify to leadership (Nikon, Sean) an annual commitment and potential expansion.
- **Renewal Path:** Jesse is open to annual renewal contingent on GrowthX providing a strategic roadmap; Marcel offered to coordinate a Bay Area process walkthrough and Warriors game outing to deepen the relationship.

## Key Topics

### GrowthX's Internal Scorecard

Marcel introduced GrowthX's internal scorecard to frame the feedback discussion.

  - **Quality:** 8–9/10
  - **Output:** 6/10
  - **Performance:** 8–9/10
  - **Relationship:** 8–9/10

### The Output Gap & Strategic Value

  - **Output Constraint:** The 6/10 output score is tied to scope (word-count basis), not quality. Jesse acknowledged that some of the constraint is on Webstacks' side as well.
  - **Value Perception:** Webstacks values GrowthX for strategic guidance, not execution.
      - **Execution:** Could be outsourced cheaper via contractors (Upwork), and execution quality would eventually match.
      - **Strategy:** GrowthX's cross-client experience and insights are the key differentiator justifying the price premium over commodity contractors.
  - **Attribution Challenge & LLM Success:** Direct attribution to conversions is hard, but Webstacks sees clear month-over-month growth in LLM search traffic and organic search. The "AI for web management" content series in particular has driven tangible new traffic spikes.
  - **Jacob's Strategic Role:** Jesse clarified that Jacob's value isn't in day-to-day execution but in connecting dots—idea generation, helping tell the story to leadership, and defining "what's next." If Jacob spent even 1 hour/month or was a Slack resource for Jesse's questions, Webstacks would expand services immediately.

### The Renewal Path

  - **GrowthX's Goal:** Convert the current 3-month contract to an annual agreement to justify the high-value team (director, strategist, writer, PM) on a lower-than-average contract.
  - **Webstacks' Condition:** An annual contract is contingent on GrowthX providing a strategic roadmap.
      - **Why:** A roadmap enables Webstacks to tell a compelling story to leadership (Nikon, Sean) to justify the investment.
      - **Example:** The "AI for web management" content series was successful because it had a clear 6-week plan, allowing for targeted execution and performance tracking.
