## Summary

Rapyd and GrowthX aligned on a strategy to eliminate a 39-article review backlog by improving reviewer notifications: tagging users directly in Airtable's "Rapyd Internal Reviewer" column instead of mentioning them in comments, which will trigger email notifications with direct links. The teams also discussed a tiered reviewer-assignment system by technical complexity and addressed a critical content quality issue where articles were being rejected for false technical comparisons (like framing PayFac and Marketplace as competing models rather than complementary approaches)—GrowthX will test a reframe that preserves SEO intent while maintaining technical accuracy, and Rapyd will map reviewers to topic clusters to enable future automation.

## Context

Rapyd is a fintech platform specializing in payments infrastructure (APIs, payment processing, seller onboarding, and compliance). GrowthX is producing a comprehensive B2B content program for Rapyd's website—an engagement worth $200k+ annually. This weekly sync is part of the ongoing delivery workflow: Vivek Shankar and Mara Leighton (GrowthX) are working with Mark Stiltner, Nicolette Mychajluk, and Vanessa Laymon Johnson (Rapyd) to manage the content production pipeline, resolve technical accuracy issues, and streamline the review process.

The core problem is operational: GrowthX has written ~39 articles in various stages (draft, in-review, on-hold), but the review bottleneck is slowing production. Reviewers at Rapyd aren't receiving timely notifications, which creates delays. Additionally, articles are being rejected at review stage for technical inaccuracies that suggest a disconnect between GrowthX's SEO-driven content strategy and Rapyd's need for precise, credible explanations of complex payment infrastructure.

## Key Topics

### Content Review Bottleneck

  - **Problem:** A 39-article backlog exists, driven by slow reviews. The goal is to clear \~15 articles/week to catch up.
  - **Root Cause:** Reviewers are not receiving notifications, as they are only mentioned in Airtable comments instead of being tagged as users.
  - **New Workflow:**
      - **Action:** Tag reviewers in the `Rapyd Internal Reviewer` column.
      - **Rationale:** This triggers an email notification with a direct link to the content, improving visibility and reducing manual checks.
      - **Reinforcement:** GrowthX will also tag reviewers in the external Slack channel to provide an additional reminder.

### Defining Reviewer Assignments

  - **Challenge:** Rapyd's team prefers Monday.com, creating resistance to using Airtable for review tracking.
  - **Proposed Solution:** Assign reviewers by topic cluster, allowing GrowthX to tag them automatically.
  - **Reviewer Tiers (by technical complexity):**
      - **Low:** Lizzie, Coral
      - **Mid:** Vanessa, Nicolette
      - **High:** Solutions Team (for infrastructure, API, and regulatory topics)

### Content Quality & Accuracy

  - **Problem:** Articles are being rejected ("Hold" status) for flawed comparisons that damage credibility.
  - **Example: "PayFac vs. Marketplace" Article**
      - **Flawed Premise:** The article treated PayFac and Marketplace as competing models.
      - **Technical Reality:** A Marketplace can *become* a PayFac to simplify seller onboarding and capture transaction fees. They are complementary, not mutually exclusive.
      - **SEO Rationale:** The "vs." structure was used to match competitor articles (e.g., Stripe) and satisfy Google's algorithm.
  - **New Approach:**
      - **Test Case:** The "PayFac vs. Marketplace" article will be rewritten to balance SEO needs with technical accuracy.
      - **Strategy:** Frame the section as "PayFacs and the Marketplace Model," explaining the symbiotic relationship to build trust.
  - **Other Rejection Patterns:**
      - **Terminology Confusion:** Mixing up "settlements" (money to merchant) and "payouts" (merchant sending money).
      - **Redundant Comparisons:** Treating "merchant processing" and "payment processing" as distinct concepts when they are interchangeable terms.
