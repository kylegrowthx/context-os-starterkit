## Key Topics

### Campaign Performance and Optimization

Current email performance (1 positive reply per 170 leads) significantly outperforms industry benchmarks (1 per 400). The discussion centered on scaling volume versus changing the reach strategy. Analysis of recent campaigns shows most positive replies come from steps 1 and 2 of the sequence; step 3 captures zero responses on the recent Webflow campaign. This lets Understory add more leads to the sequence and cast a wider net, which was the original goal when they first engaged to focus on nurture rather than just event invites.

### Surge Seattle Event List Building (Early November)

Target profile: AI researchers, ML engineers, product builders working at hyperscaler companies (OpenAI, Anthropic, Microsoft, Google) and hot startups in the Seattle area. Geographic focus: 30-40 mile radius from downtown Seattle. Timeline: event is early November with no date set yet; they need list ready in 1.5-2 weeks to allow time for client review iterations and import prep.

### GrowthX Happy Hour (October 23rd)

Location: GrowthX office (Bay Area). Target: 50 attendees, 65 RSVP target. Composition: 10% existing contacts/customers (5-10 people), 90% new prospects (40-60 people). Prospect criteria: Bay Area-based, have engaged with GrowthX content or community in some way but have never attended a live event. Naufal proposed developing a scoring system for existing contacts to prioritize senior-level attendees who've engaged but never come to live events—these are highest-intent prospects. Mark confirmed the list can be ready by Monday for Tuesday send-out. They'll start with warm/previous engager list first, then resort to cold list as backup if needed.
