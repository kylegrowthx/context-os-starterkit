## Summary

Tyler Pavlas (GrowthX founding AE) and Rachel Kim (interim head of marketing at Mutiny) explored a potential partnership to help Mutiny compete in AI search while maintaining its position as a definitive ABM content resource. Rachel explained Mutiny's challenge: declining website traffic due to LLM adoption, a narrow target audience (~100 companies in North America), and questions about whether their current 4-6 blogs per week on ABM topics can reach a sufficiently large search volume. Tyler presented GrowthX's two-phase approach—strategy sprint followed by daily publishing execution—and shared case studies (Town for accuracy in specialized verticals; Augment Code's 100+ guides generating significant traffic; Ramp's programmatic vendor database). Rachel committed to a follow-up meeting with Marcel to understand whether GrowthX's methods could uncover enough content opportunities to justify the investment given Mutiny's narrow niche.

## Context

Mutiny is an ABM (Account-Based Marketing) platform with a small but highly specialized target market. Rachel Kim joined as interim head of marketing in December 2024 and has since invested heavily in SEO and content strategy—partnering with Impact Partners for Reddit visibility and Omniscient for SEO briefs, while managing an internal freelance writing team. The company recently shifted focus to becoming the definitive source for ABM-specific content, but faces headwinds from increasing LLM usage reducing website traffic and the fundamental challenge of a limited addressable market. Tyler Pavlas, GrowthX's founding account executive, reached out to explore whether GrowthX's content strategy and execution capabilities could help Mutiny navigate both the technical (Contentful CMS bottlenecks) and strategic (content saturation in a narrow niche) barriers to growth. This was a discovery call to assess fit before involving Marcel (GrowthX CEO) in deeper opportunity analysis.

## Key Topics

### Mutiny's Current Content Strategy

  - Invested in SEO and blog content in the last 2-3 months
  - Partnering with Impact Partners for Reddit exposure and Omniscient for SEO strategy
  - Publishing 4-6 blogs per week with internal freelance writers
  - Facing limitations in search term diversity for their niche ABM audience

### GrowthX.ai's Approach

  - Two-phase process: Strategy sprint followed by execution
  - AI workflows combined with expert oversight for content creation
  - Capability to handle end-to-end strategy, daily publishing, and CMS management
  - Focus on creating credible, comprehensive, and current content

### Content Scaling and Quality

  - GrowthX.ai emphasizes scaling quality content to improve AI search visibility
  - Mutiny's concern: limited pool of relevant search terms in the ABM space
  - GrowthX.ai suggests exploring broader content types (guides, programmatic SEO) to maximize value

### Case Studies and Examples

  - Town: Blog built for accuracy in accounting/tax advice
  - Augment Code: 100+ guides created, significant traffic increase
  - Ramp: Programmatic vendor database and tool comparison pages

### Technical Considerations

  - GrowthX.ai is CMS agnostic, comfortable with headless CMS like Contentful
  - Offers creation of cover images and other visual elements
