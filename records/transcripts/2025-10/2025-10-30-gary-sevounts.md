## Context

ZipSec is a Series A security platform founded to productize the MSP (Managed Service Provider) model for SMBs. The company automates security compliance, manages third-party tool deployments (CrowdStrike, SentinelOne, Jamf), and ensures continuous compliance—a critical gap that traditional MSPs and vCISOs struggle to address between engagements. Founded in spring 2025, ZipSec is in mid-single-digit millions ARR and targeting 5-8x growth in the next 12 months. Gary Sevounts is the founder, and Dave Katz recently joined as VP Marketing (literally one week prior to this call). The company has moved beyond founder-led sales but still lacks a scalable inbound motion—their current spend is only $10k/month on LinkedIn with nascent outbound efforts. Jason Gong and Tyler Pavlas from GrowthX were brought in to explore whether content and AI-powered workflows could build the awareness and pipeline infrastructure ZipSec needs to hit their growth targets.

## Key Topics

### ZipSec's Growth Challenge

  - **Product:** An AI-driven platform productizing the MSP model for SMBs. It automates security compliance, manages licenses (CrowdStrike, SentinelOne), and provides continuous compliance.
  - **ICPs:**
      - **"Partner of none":** Companies without IT/security staff.
      - **"Department of one":** Small security teams (1–3 people).
      - **Security Advisory Firms:** vCISOs and consultants who use ZipSec to provide continuous compliance between engagements, protecting their brand.
  - **Business Context:**
      - **Stage:** Series A (raised spring 2025).
      - **Revenue:** Mid-single-digit millions, targeting 5x–8x growth in 12 months.
      - **ASP:** $20–30k, making it a sales-assisted product requiring a high-quality inbound pipeline.
  - **Primary Goal:** Replace the unscalable founder-led sales motion with a high-quality inbound engine.
      - **Channel Focus:** Inbound (web, ChatGPT) and a partner program with vCISOs.
      - **Messaging Need:** Condense the complex value prop into a clear, compelling pitch for each ICP.

### GrowthX's AI-Powered Content Engine

  - **Core Concept:** Build a "content factory" (systems) for scalable, high-frequency content, not just one-off pieces.
  - **Process:**
      - **Phase 1 (8-week Strategy Sprint):**
          - **Cost:** $30k (Series A rate).
          - **Deliverables:** In-depth artifacts (ICP personas, SEO/GEO analysis) to inform AI workflows.
          - **Output:** \~10 published articles to demonstrate quality.
      - **Phase 2 (Ongoing Partnership):**
          - **Cost:** $15k/month (Series A rate).
          - **Output:** Daily publishing to maintain recency, which is critical for AI search.
  - **Methodology:**
      - **Hybrid AI/Human Workflow:** An "assembly line" of AI agents (research, writing, tone) with human oversight (outlines, final review).
      - **Domain Experts:** Used for highly technical content (e.g., Webflow's dev docs).
  - **Content Types:** Best for scalable, high-frequency needs like glossaries, guides, and programmatic content. Not ideal for low-volume, hand-crafted assets like case studies.

### ROI & Success Metrics

  - **ZipSec's Requirement:** Concrete ROI data is needed to justify the investment to leadership.
      - **Example:** "$100k spent → $3M attributed revenue."
      - **Rationale:** The investment must be a "no-brainer," not a speculative expense.
  - **GrowthX's Response:**
      - **Timeline:** 2–3 months for initial signals (signups, leads); \~6 months for full impact.
      - **Case Study (Discern):** A company with a similar $20k ASP saw a 3x increase in organic traffic and 25–28% monthly revenue growth coinciding with the GrowthX launch.
      - **Case Study (Augment Code):** Drives 150–200 signups/week and ranks for high-intent LLM prompts (e.g., "best AI coding tool").
