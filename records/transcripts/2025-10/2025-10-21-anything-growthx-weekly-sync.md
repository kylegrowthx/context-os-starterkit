## Key Topics

### Problem: Generic Content & Inconsistent Tone

  - The first "calibration" article was too generic and lacked specific examples, despite conflicting feedback from multiple reviewers (Becerra, Callie).
  - The brand voice was inconsistent, remaining too "marketing-focused" instead of adopting the desired "talk like a builder" tone.
  - This issue stemmed from using outdated writing guidelines that did not incorporate Dhruv's previous Notion comments.

### Solution: New Workflow & Artifacts

  - **Mandatory Outline Review:**
      - **Why:** To prevent generic drafts by ensuring early alignment on structure and content.
      - **Process:** GrowthX generates an outline using SEMrush API data (for ranking) and Perplexity (for search intent), then sends it to the client for review.
      - **Impact:** Adds a 1–2 day delay to the next three articles.
  - **Updated Artifacts:**
      - **Writing Guidelines:** GrowthX will first apply Dhruv's existing Notion comments, then notify him for a final review.
      - **Product Use Cases Doc:** A new document for the client to provide specific examples (e.g., customer stories) to enrich articles. This will be integrated into the outline review step.

### SEO Strategy: Word Count vs. Quality

  - **Client Concern:** Balancing SEO-driven word count with concise, high-quality writing.
  - **GrowthX Position:**
      - **Minimum:** Aim for \~1,200 words to cover subtopics and fulfill search intent.
      - **Priority:** Avoid fluff. A 3,000-word article is acceptable if well-structured (e.g., bullets, tables) to maintain readability.
      - **Human Experience:** For long articles, add a "Key Takeaways" or TLDR section at the top.
