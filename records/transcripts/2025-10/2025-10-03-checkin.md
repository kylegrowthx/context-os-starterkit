## Summary

Renato, Giel, and Nol reviewed two CheckThat branding concepts: a radar-inspired "C" logo and an abstract circular form. The team strongly favored the radar concept for its uniqueness and versatility, with plans to implement it as CheckThat's primary logo by the following week. Giel presented website mockups showing how the new branding could integrate animated elements, while Nol demonstrated how the design could extend into broader visual identity and marketing materials.

## Context

This is a branding review for CheckThat, GrowthX's AI visibility index software product. Renato Valdes Olmos from GrowthX is leading the CheckThat initiative, working with design partners Giel Cobben and Nol Cobben from Shuttle (an external design studio). The meeting took place to finalize the visual identity for CheckThat's rebrand—a critical step ahead of product launch and team rollout. The team had developed two distinct logo concepts and wanted to align on direction before implementation work began over the following weekend.

---

## Key Topics

### Radar Logo Concept

  - Primary design features a "C" shape resembling a radar symbol
  - Unique and recognizable, not found in competitor logos
  - Combines well with sans-serif font, suggesting tech without being too rigid
  - Versatile for various applications and scales

### Visual Identity Elements

  - Color palette: Primary green with supporting grays
  - Additional functional colors (red, yellow) for variety
  - Potential for abstract visual elements derived from the radar concept
  - Stackable search-like fields and layered visuals to represent AI functionality

### Website Design Proposals

  - Simplified, ChatGPT-inspired layout
  - Radar visual incorporated into header design
  - Animated elements suggested (e.g., rotating radar, pulsing circles)
  - Flat visuals preferred over 3D for clarity and text readability

### Alternative Abstract Logo

  - Simplified circular form offering more abstraction
  - Potential interpretations: bullseye, search icon, or general target
  - Provides flexibility for brand expansion and various applications

### Color Variations

  - Demonstrated how changing the primary color (e.g., to blue) affects overall brand feel
  - Offers easy customization options for different use cases or team preferences
