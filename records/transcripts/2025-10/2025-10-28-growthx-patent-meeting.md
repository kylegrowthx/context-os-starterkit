## Summary

GrowthX met with patent attorney Sanj Dutta to evaluate patenting output.ai, a new open-source AI workflow framework launching in mid-December. The team decided to move forward with a provisional patent filing (~$7,500 + $130 USPTO fee) to create prior art and prevent competitors from blocking the technology, noting that while M&A value is limited for open-source tech, patenting preserves the option to pivot to a freemium model later. Filing can happen before or after launch—pre-launch secures international rights, but a 1-year US grace period allows post-launch filing if needed.

---

## Context

GrowthX is preparing to launch output.ai, an open-source AI workflow framework with a plain-text language for defining workflows and a custom trace file format for execution tracking. This represents a new product line building on GrowthX's expertise in AI and automation, with plans to offer premium features and visualization tools as paid adjacent products. Bridget McGillivray (board member/executive) and Daniel Lopes (product lead) engaged Sanj Dutta, a patent attorney at Goodwin Law, to advise on the strategic and tactical approach to patenting the framework before launch. The meeting was motivated by Bridget's prior experience patenting the connector builder at AirByte and uncertainty about whether patenting makes sense for truly open-source technology.

## Relevance

**To GrowthX Business Development:**
- output.ai represents a new product line diversifying beyond B2B content marketing. Patent filing protects the core innovation (plain-text workflow language + trace file combination) and preserves optionality for freemium monetization.
- Filing timeline is critical: provisional patent costs ~$7,500 + $130 USD before mid-December launch to secure international rights, or ~February 2026 to use 1-year US grace period (sacrificing non-US patentability).
- Trademark strategy for "output.ai" is straightforward since the name is descriptive—GrowthX can use it freely but cannot prevent others from doing so. Brand strength will depend on market adoption and secondary meaning over time (like Google's evolution).

**To Competitive Defense:**
- Patent filing creates prior art preventing competitors from blocking GrowthX's core workflow innovation. This is the primary defense goal, not M&A valuation (which is minimal for open-source tech).
- The combination of plain-text workflow language, custom trace file format, and integration with open-source components is the patentable element, not individual components. Attorney recommends filing broad provisional patent covering entire stack.
- If a competitor develops improvements (e.g., better visualization tools) and patches the framework, they could theoretically patent their additions—this is a risk but manageable with good prior art from GrowthX's disclosure.

## Overview

- **Patent for Defensibility, Not Revenue:** The primary goal is to create prior art, preventing competitors from patenting the core technology and blocking GrowthX. A patent's M\&A value is limited if the tech is open-source.
- **Patent the Whole Stack:** File a provisional patent covering the entire system, as the unique combination of components (e.g., plain-text workflow language, custom trace file) is the key innovation.
- **File Before Launch (Ideally):** Filing before the mid-December launch secures international rights. Post-launch filing is viable due to a 1-year US grace period, but sacrifices foreign patentability.
- **Phased Cost:** The provisional patent costs \~$7,500 (attorney fees) + \~$130 (USPTO fee). Converting to a non-provisional application within 12 months costs an additional \~$20,000 (attorney fees) + \~$1,000 (USPTO fee).

## Key Topics

### Patent Strategy for an Open-Source Product

  - **Core Question:** Is patenting valuable for a product that will be open-sourced?
  - **Sanj's Rationale:**
      - **Defensive Value:** Creates prior art, preventing competitors from patenting the tech and blocking GrowthX.
      - **Future Options:** Preserves the option to monetize premium features or pivot to a freemium model later.
      - **Irreversible Decision:** Once released, the tech is dedicated to the public and cannot be patented.
  - **M\&A Value:** A patent's M\&A value is low if the technology is already free, as the market value is effectively zero.

### Patent Scope & Filing Process

  - **Product Overview:** An AI workflow framework with a unique plain-text language for defining workflows.
  - **Key Innovations:**
      - Plain-text language for defining workflows.
      - Custom "trace file" format for storing execution data.
      - Unique combination of existing open-source components.
  - **Filing Approach:** File a broad provisional patent covering the entire stack.
      - **Rationale:** The innovation lies in the unique combination of components, not just individual parts.
      - **Timing:** A prior art search is deferred to the non-provisional conversion stage to save initial cost.

### Timing & Cost

  - **Launch Timeline:** Mid-December.
  - **Filing Timeline:**
      - **Ideal:** File provisional patent before launch to secure international rights.
      - **Viable:** File after launch using the 1-year US grace period. This sacrifices foreign patentability but still creates US prior art.
      - **Process:** Requires \~1 month from receiving documentation.
  - **Cost Breakdown:**
      - **Provisional Patent:** \~$7,500 (attorney fees) + \~$130 (USPTO fee).
      - **Non-Provisional Conversion:** \~$20,000 (attorney fees) + \~$1,000 (USPTO fee).

### Trademark Strategy

  - **Product Name:** "output.ai."
  - **Trademarkability:** The name is descriptive, making it difficult to register as a trademark initially.
  - **Implication:** GrowthX can use the name but cannot prevent others from doing so.
  - **Future Potential:** Registration may be possible if the name acquires secondary meaning (e.g., "Google" for search).
