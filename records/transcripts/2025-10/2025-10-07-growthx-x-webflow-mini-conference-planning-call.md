## Key Topics

### Event Format and Venue

  - Two competing formats discussed: (1) 3-4 hour morning session at GrowthX office with content/panels followed by dinner, vs. (2) purely dinner/networking format at upscale venue like Lazy Bear
  - GrowthX office (turnkey, low/no rental costs, recording capabilities) vs. upscale restaurants (Lazy Bear, Shack 15, Above the Rim at Chase Center—higher cost ~$25-30k booking + F&B but better brand experience)
  - Team consensus: Priority on turnkey execution and simplicity over venue grandeur; prefer to avoid complex logistics that distract from content and audience recruitment
  - Breakfast or lunch included; timeline spans morning preferred over afternoon (better attendee focus and attention)
  - Key insight from Marcel: previous Lazy Bear event attracted Meta CTO, Figma CEO, Airtable CEO due to venue appeal; venue can help recruit high-caliber guests

### Budget and Logistics

  - Upscale venues (Above the Rim, Shack 15) cost ~$25-30k booking fee + F&B = ~$100k all-in for larger event
  - GrowthX office option: near-zero venue cost, some minor rentals if capacity >50, highly cost-effective
  - Venue options explored: NASDAQ SF (now closed), Convene (Union Square, ~$50-75k range), Shack 15, Above the Rim, Lazy Bear
  - Team agreed: cost-consciousness + turnkey simplicity more valuable than premium venue branding
  - Budget owner: Marissa Kraines (Webflow) to finalize with leadership; GrowthX willing to split costs

### Content and Speakers

  - Focus on establishing thought leadership in AEO/GEO and AI for marketing
  - Both GrowthX and Webflow to contribute speakers and panel participants
  - Content to be recorded and distributed post-event for wider reach

### Target Audience and Invitations

  - Size debate: settled on 35-50 people (small enough for high guest density and quality, large enough for meaningful scale)
  - Target profile: CMOs and VPs of Marketing from hot startups (Brex, Ramp, Base 10, Sign Up) and some Fortune 500 tech companies
  - GrowthX pool: ~30+ local SF marketing leaders from hot startups; note that many hot AI companies don't have CMOs, so VPs of Marketing is primary target
  - Webflow pool: Similar hot startup focus; overlap expected between GrowthX and Webflow networks (mutual customers)
  - GrowthX also runs monthly CMO breakfasts and has track record recruiting for large events (110-person HubSpot event)
  - Both companies to compile invite lists (~25-30 per side) and coordinate for deduplication/coordination
  - Time constraint: All recruitment must happen within 4 weeks (1 month timeline deemed tight for demand generation)
  - Reason to keep size small: team concerned about generating demand for 50+ in such short timeframe; smaller intimate event more feasible and higher quality

### Content, Speakers, and Programming

  - Panels and talks from both GrowthX and Webflow experts on future of web, AEO/GEO, AI impact on marketing
  - Guy Yalif (Webflow) offered two approaches: (1) "Genuine discussion" format with meaningful content/panels, or (2) pure networking dinner (different value but also valid)
  - Guy's preference: Morning sessions better than afternoon (attendees have more focus)
  - All sessions to be recorded and distributed post-event for extended reach beyond in-person attendees
  - Mock-up design shared; Figma prototype for further comment/iteration

### Website and Promotion

  - Webflow team (Carly Botelho, Marissa Kraines) to build event website using Webflow platform (dog-fooding Webflow product)
  - Simple landing page targeting Friday, Oct 10 deadline for initial announcement (tells people "when and where")
  - Post-event content amplification strategy to be discussed later
