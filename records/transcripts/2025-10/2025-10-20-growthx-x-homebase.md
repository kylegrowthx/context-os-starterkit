## Context

Homebase is a GrowthX client where Marcel Santilli has a long-standing relationship — John (Homebase leadership) and Marcel go back years. The engagement covers SEO and GEO content programs under an ongoing contract with ~10 months remaining. Over the past four to five months, Homebase has undergone a strategic shift toward building and owning internal AI infrastructure — a directive from leadership (John and Ray). This move is driven by a desire to maximize work product from their available budget while maintaining full ownership of workflows and outputs.

The friction point is process overhead. Kerry's team reports that GrowthX's engagement requires more internal management, revision cycles, and post-work than their own internally built systems (currently using Aerofs). After a recent migration to a new system, the team had to re-coach content quality back to prior levels. The core complaint: feedback feels applied directly to workflows without "AI engineering" translation, leading to drafts that don't address the actual intent and slow progress. Additionally, performance reporting has never been delivered. This conversation is about finding a path forward that honors both the relationship and Homebase's business needs.

## Key Topics

### Homebase's Strategy to Bring Programs In-House

  - Homebase is moving SEO and GEO content programs in-house as a strategic priority.
  - **Strategic Drivers:**
      - **Internal AI Ownership:** Leadership (John, Ray) has mandated building and owning internal AI infrastructure — not outsourcing it.
      - **Overhead Reduction:** GrowthX's current process requires extensive internal management, multiple revision cycles, and post-delivery edits — heavier than Homebase's own internally-built systems.
      - **Budget Maximization:** Homebase wants to deploy available dollars on work product and ownership, not on revision cycles and external iteration.
      - **Transition in Progress:** Kerry has already moved the team from "human content agency" to "AI-enabled contractor base" working closely in-house, with Aerofs as the core platform.

### Critical Feedback on GrowthX Engagement

  - **Process Overhead:** The GrowthX model is heavier than Homebase's internal systems. Kerry's team prefers building and running their own Aerofs flows, editing during production, and owning the output — rather than managing external iteration cycles.
  - **Migration Brittleness:** The shift to Becerra required extensive re-coaching to restore prior content quality. Knowledge loss during platform transitions is a significant risk.
  - **Feedback Loop Breakdown — The Core Problem:** When Homebase gives feedback, it feels like it's being applied directly to the workflow without "AI engineering" interpretation. The subsequent drafts don't address the actual intent. Kerry noted: "It feels like you kind of have proprietary software, and then you have humans taking our feedback, going directly into the product. What we're missing is the AI engineer translating that." This creates slow, sticky, heavy engagements.
  - **Personal Context (Positive History):** Kerry loved working with Jess (prior GrowthX account manager) — she was phenomenal and got things done. Kerry also valued direct working sessions with Marcel where feedback got translated into real creative breakthroughs. That's the skillset and dynamic Homebase is not seeing in the current engagement.
  - **Reporting Gap:** Performance reporting has never been delivered — a recurring conversation between Kerry and Marcel but unresolved.

### GrowthX's Path Forward: Two Options + Exit Terms

  - Marcel presented two paths to retain the relationship while addressing Homebase's core needs.
  - **Path 1: "Bake-Off" Strategy Sprint**
      - **Concept:** Re-onboard Homebase as a "new client" with a fully dedicated team using GrowthX's proven new onboarding process (successfully used with Lovable, Ramp, Webflow — high-profile clients seeing significant growth).
      - **Terms:** Short sprint with explicit quality goals. If outputs and quality aren't significantly better than Homebase's alternatives by sprint end, the contract ends early with no hard feelings.
      - **Rationale:** Fresh team, proven process, clear success metrics, clean exit clause.
  - **Path 2: Platform Pilot with Direct Workflow Ownership**
      - **Concept:** Pause all billing. Provide Homebase early access to GrowthX's upcoming platform (launching in weeks) so they can build and own workflows directly.
      - **Platform Advantages:**
          - **Code-Based Workflows:** Built using the open-source `output.ai` framework (launching very soon) — not locked into proprietary software.
          - **Full Ownership & Exportability:** Unlike Aerofs (where knowledge bases and workflows are Aerofs-native), `output.ai` workflows are exportable and can be deployed anywhere.
          - **API Integration:** Workflows exposed via API for seamless n8n orchestration or custom tooling.
          - **Advanced Capabilities:** Agentic research with confidence scoring, programmatic infographics, site-wide monitoring, direct workflow edition.
      - **Why This Matters:** Kerry can "manipulate all the different pieces" directly — no intermediary, no "AI engineer translation" needed. Homebase owns the code, the logic, the output.
  - **Contract Resolution (Both Paths Include Exit Options)**
      - **Option A (Clean Break):** Pay 50% cancellation fee on remaining ~10 months to terminate early. (Marcel noted this is a GrowthX first, offered because of the founder relationship and long history.)
      - **Option B (Redeployment):** Keep the budget, redirect it toward building advanced custom workflows specifically for Homebase to own, run, and export. Either path lets Homebase "keep the door cracked" on the GrowthX relationship while moving forward on their own terms.
