George Haikal, Erik O'Brien, and Nicholas Smarrelli discussed N-able's content bottlenecks (slow approvals, competing priorities across 1,900 employees) and outlined next steps: deep-dive meetings with N-able's PMM, customer success, and brand teams; GrowthX to develop writing guidelines, audience personas, and a topic cluster content strategy; and a demo of GrowthX's Atlas content generation platform in the following week.

## Context

N-able is a 1,900-person global IT management and cyber resiliency software company undergoing a strategic repositioning. Historically focused on MSP-oriented solutions (80% of revenue from managed service providers), N-able is pivoting to serve mid-market enterprises (MME) with an emphasis on cyber security, particularly through its AdLumen MDR (managed detection and response) product. The company operates three main product lines: N-Central (RMM/UEM), Cove (data protection), and AdLumen (MDR).

This kickoff meeting represents the start of a strategic content partnership between GrowthX and N-able to support this market transition. N-able recognized that their current marketing is heavily skewed toward bottom-funnel demand generation and needs significant top-funnel awareness content to establish thought leadership and educate the market on cyber resiliency solutions. N-able's internal content approval processes and competing organizational priorities have become significant bottlenecks, making external content expertise and streamlined delivery critical to their go-to-market success.

## Key Topics

### Company Transition and Growth Focus

  - Shifting from IT management to cyber resiliency/security positioning
  - Three main products: N-Central (RMM/UEM), Cove (Data Protection), AdLumen (MDR)
  - Primary growth focus on MDR product and mid-market enterprise (MME) segment
  - Current revenue split: 80% MSPs, 20% MME; aiming to grow MME significantly

### Target Audience and ICP

  - MSPs: 1-10 employees, 11-50 employees, 50+ employees (focus on 11-50 and 50+)
  - MME: Internal IT teams, 250+ employees (financial, manufacturing, healthcare, education)
  - Resellers: Important channel, especially in Europe

### Current Marketing Efforts

  - 80% of leads from "core MGen": paid search, organic, web direct
  - Paid social (LinkedIn) working well, spending \~$2.5M annually
  - Enterprise team focuses on in-person events for 11-50 and 50+ MSP segments
  - Top-performing content: SOC report, threat report, backup guides, free scripts

### Challenges and Bottlenecks

  - Approval processes often too long with "too many cooks in the kitchen"
  - Competing priorities across large organization (1,900 employees globally)
  - Limited scalability in content creation and distribution
  - Lack of effective nurture campaigns for moving leads down the funnel

### Competitors and Differentiation

  - RMM/UEM: Ninja1, Atera, Kaseya, ConnectWise
  - Data Protection: Veeam, Acronis, Axcient
  - MDR: Huntress, Arctic Wolf, Red Canary (600+ providers in market)
  - N-able differentiation: Cloud-first approach, local data centers, "head nerds" for customer support

### Desired Outcomes and Metrics

  - Increased ICP lead generation and web traffic
  - Improved domain authority and AI search visibility
  - Consistent presence in top 3 vendor shortlists
  - Overall bookings growth, especially for MDR product
