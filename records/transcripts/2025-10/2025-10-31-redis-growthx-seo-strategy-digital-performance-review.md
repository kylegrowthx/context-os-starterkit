## Context

Redis is a critical infrastructure company with a strong engineering audience (developers, architects) and product-focused buyers. Organic search drives 80% of their product signups and 50% of hand raisers overall, making SEO existential to their lead gen strategy. However, Redis's internal SEO and content production process is severely bottlenecked by a 2-3 month approval cycle involving product marketing, brand, and web teams, limiting output to just 4 articles per quarter. This came to a head in October 2025 when Fung-Lin Wu (Head of Product/Growth) and Alexis Ruiz-Pedregon (Content/SEO Lead) brought in GrowthX to overhaul the strategy. The timing is critical: organic traffic has been steadily declining since April, conversion rates are down 30% QoQ, and Redis's glossary page—formerly a major traffic driver—has lost half its traffic. Meanwhile, ChatGPT and other LLM referral channels represent a new, high-intent source of traffic that needs strategic focus alongside traditional organic search.

## Key Topics

### SEO Performance & Challenges

- **Organic Traffic Decline:** Steady decline since April 2025, with 5% QoQ session loss.
- **Conversion Rate Drop:** Organic conversion rate down 30% QoQ; overall all conversion rates are dropping despite channel-specific variations.
- **Glossary Page Crisis:** Traffic cut in half over 18 months (20k → 10k sessions/month). Root cause: glossary is pure top-of-funnel (definitions, "what is X" content) with very low intent; drives volume but no conversions.
- **ChatGPT/LLM Traffic:** Up 250% QoQ and drives the highest conversion rate of all channels (80-90% engagement vs. 50% typical). This is becoming Redis's strongest referral source.
- **Funnel Imbalance:** No systematic top-of-funnel (ToFu), middle-of-funnel (MoFu), and bottom-of-funnel (BoFu) breakdown; unclear whether content is 90% ToFu or evenly distributed.

### Content Strategy & Process

- **Current Process Bottleneck:** 2–3 month approval cycle (Product Marketing → Brand → Web Team) limits output to ~4 articles/quarter, creating backlog. Root cause: low PMM bandwidth; most in-progress content is stuck in PMM review.
- **GrowthX Takes Over:** Fully own all SEO content production (net new, refreshes, and competitive analyses). Goal: Redis teams become completely hands-off after onboarding. GrowthX has Sanity CMS write access and can launch independently.
- **Scaling Target:** From 4 articles/quarter to ~5 pieces/week (~200-250 articles/year). Mix will be 3 net new + 2 refreshes per week, prioritized by audience personas and strategic goals.
- **Content Topics & Strengths:** High-performing content includes technical deep-dives ("AI agent memory," "fuzzy matching"), data-driven competitor analyses, and benchmark reports. Redis was not tracking AI keywords until recently but wants to build AI infrastructure authority. Glossary page will remain Redis's responsibility (freelance writer) unless contract clarification determines otherwise.
- **Tone & Brand Protection:** Redis is protective of tonal voice; Erik meeting with Allison (Brand) Monday for training. Writing guidelines will be the most volatile artifact during onboarding; brand review will not be required after calibration.

### Implementation Plan

- **Next Week (Topic Clusters & Keywords):** Erik will present topic clusters to Fung-Lin & Alexis for approval, establishing broad content areas to build subject authority. Concurrent keyword gap analysis will identify competitor keywords Redis doesn't rank for, informing targeted keyword list.
- **Keyword Tracking:** Redis currently tracks 1,300 keywords in Ahrefs with ~200 open slots. New high-priority target keywords will be added; older irrelevant keywords can be replaced or removed. GrowthX will check for duplicates against existing Ahrefs data.
- **Content Management:** Airtable will serve as the "Content OS"—replacing Redis's internal spreadsheet. Workflow: competitive analysis → topic/blog assignments → approval → production → publishing, all tracked in Airtable.
- **Reporting & Analysis:** GrowthX will build a 7-page Looker dashboard pulling from GA4 and Google Search Console to track performance by topic cluster, LLM referral traffic, and landing page impact. This enables weekly tracking of what's working vs. what's not.
- **Onboarding & Access:** Erik already has GSC and GA4 access. Remaining blockers: contract clarification on glossary scope, ICP/customer insights brief from Fung-Lin (owner unclear after PMM transition), and Redis for AI FY26 deck (docs not in shared drive).
