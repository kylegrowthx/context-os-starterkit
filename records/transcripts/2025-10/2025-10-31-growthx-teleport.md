## Summary

Teleport (CMO Diana Jovin, VP Marketing Doug Busk, Manager of Web & Design Travis Swientek) met with GrowthX's Jason Gong and Tyler Pavlas to explore scaling Teleport's B2B content production and SEO/GEO strategy to reach C-suite audiences (CSOs, CTOs) alongside their existing engineering-focused content. GrowthX proposed a two-phase engagement: a $30k–$40k strategy sprint to define content roadmap, personas, and brand voice, followed by a $15k/month execution phase targeting 5 articles per month using AI workflows with human editorial review. The team aligned on structuring content across separate domains (premium blog for thought leadership, `/learn` hub for SEO-focused guides) to maintain brand authority while scaling top-of-funnel discovery, with a flexible opt-out option after Phase 1.

---

## Context

Teleport is a network security and identity access management platform that has built a strong presence among infrastructure engineers and security teams. The company currently dominates in this persona but is expanding upmarket to influence C-level buyers (CSOs, CTOs, CISOs) who care about risk mitigation, operational efficiency, and compliance/audit advantages. This requires a content strategy shift: moving from purely technical, engineer-focused content to broader narratives about business value, governance, and organizational impact.

Doug Busk framed this as "making the funnel wider and higher"—not just routing more engineer traffic, but also attracting and converting decision-makers. The current content mix is uneven: competitive comparison pages and product content perform well, the Teleport Academy (glossary/resource hub) is developing, and the blog is underutilized, historically focused on earned media and LinkedIn content. Teleport has experienced hiring and wants to accelerate content production without adding large headcount. This is a growth partnership opportunity for GrowthX.

## Key Topics

### Teleport's Content Challenges & Goals

  - **Primary Goal:** Expand audience reach from core engineering/security personas to the C-suite.
      - **Why:** To tell stories about Teleport's value in efficiency, risk mitigation, and audit advantages.
  - **Key Pain Points (per Travis):**
      - **Production Bottleneck:** Content creation is slow due to extensive writing, editing, and review cycles.
      - **Unreliable Analytics:** Current tools (e.g., Semrush's AI plugin) are insufficient for measuring GEO impact and proving ROI.
  - **Current Content Strategy:**
      - **High-Performing:** Competitive "takeout" pages and product pages.
      - **Developing:** Teleport Academy (a glossary and resource hub).
      - **Underutilized:** The blog, which has focused on earned media and LinkedIn posts.
  - **Future Direction:** A multi-channel "atomized content" strategy (video, social) to maximize content reuse.

### GrowthX's "Content Factory" Model

  - **Core Approach:** Build scalable content "factories" using AI workflows and human editors, rather than just staffing writers.
  - **Strategy Sprint (Phase 1):**
      - **Cost:** $30k–$40k (based on company stage).
      - **Deliverables:**
          - **Artifacts:** Detailed personas, brand voice guides, and a knowledge base to provide context for AI.
          - **Analysis:** Keyword research (volume, difficulty, intent) and competitor analysis.
          - **Roadmap:** A topic cluster map and content plan.
  - **Execution Phase (Phase 2):**
      - **Cost:** $15k/month for one workstream (e.g., editorial, programmatic).
      - **Target:** 5 articles/month, with a goal of daily publishing.
      - **Process:** AI-driven workflows (research, outline, draft) with human editors at every step to ensure accuracy, especially for technical content.
      - **Opt-Out:** Teleport can cancel after the strategy sprint if the quality or fit isn't right.

### Analytics & Content Structure

  - **Performance Tracking:**
      - GrowthX builds custom Looker dashboards to ingest data from Search Console, Amplitude, etc.
      - **Goal:** Use performance signals to iterate on the content machine and improve results.
  - **Content Structure Recommendations:**
      - GrowthX advises separating content types to maintain brand trust.
      - **Blog:** Reserved for high-tier thought leadership and company announcements.
      - **Resource Hub:** A `/learn` or `/resources` path for SEO-focused guides and how-tos.
      - **Why:** To prevent top-of-funnel content from diluting the blog's authority with a C-suite audience.
