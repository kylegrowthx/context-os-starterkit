## Key Topics

### David Myriel's Background and Expertise

  - 10 years of technical writing experience, originally from economics/finance background; grew up in Southern Ontario and attended Ottawa University
  - At Quadrant (vector database): grew from initial team member to head of DevRel with 7-person team; developed expertise in content repurposing (podcast → blog → docs) and optimizing visibility in LLM outputs
  - At LanceDB (2025): led both growth and marketing; worked on positioning vector search for production use (product and content strategy); left when company pivoted toward data lake focus instead of vector search
  - Currently running independent consultancy from Europe, working with e-commerce and enterprise clients building agentic applications; also advising on GTM for 2–3 "very popular" framework/tool companies (using vector databases, building agents)

### GrowthX's Content Creation Approach

  - Founded by Marcel (ex-CMO at Scale, which scaled a marketplace with strategic content positioning and community association)
  - Two-pronged focus: (1) Traditional SEO and content optimization for LLM citations; (2) Expanding into DevRel-tier content for developer/AI space clients
  - Content distribution: podcast repurposing (e.g., Surge founder Edwin's podcast episodes → 500–1000-word blog posts), Reddit engagement (both transparent participation and paid community management), link building (outreach, author trades, prompt-aware backlinking)
  - Emerging focus: "Generative optimization" — tracking which prompts clients care about, seeing what LLMs cite, reverse-engineering content structure and keyword inclusion to get into those LLM outputs (structure, markdown formatting, FAQ sections, table of contents)
  - Clients include Surge AI (data labeling, post-training), Webflow (integration guides), and Base 10 (AI-focused fund with technical bar for content)

### Surge AI Collaboration Model

  - Trial approach: Jason sends David brief topic ideas + links to Surge founder Edwin's podcast episodes; David writes a 500–1000-word draft blog post; team evaluates quality and alignment
  - Content focus: posts that go deeper than soundbites on benchmarking ("how gameable benchmarks are"), post-training, data labeling, and AI model performance — the "why" and "path forward," not just a hot take
  - Repurposing strategy: turn Edwin's podcast moments into longer-form content that can be referenced internally and distributed on Surge's blog for both developer/community reach and LLM citation visibility
  - Engagement structure: Jason and David agree to start small; if successful, can scale to retainer model covering Surge and potentially expand to other GrowthX clients

### Content Optimization for AI/LLM Visibility and Learning from the Wild West

  - David shared his experience at Quadrant: started on page 4 of Google, not mentioned in GPT/Claude outputs; brute-forced way in by outsourcing content to Medium/Dev.to blogs with consistent messaging (similar approach worked for LanceDB with production-readiness positioning)
  - Jason confirmed GrowthX is using similar "Wild West" tactics: build tools to track specific prompts + what gets cited → see the pattern → optimize content structure, copy, and format to get included in future LLM outputs
  - No one fully knows the rules yet, but common patterns emerging: content structure matters (FAQs, headings, markdown), specific keywords matter (if all citations have "2025" and "best AI tool," add those), Reddit and discussions shape LLM training data (not always visible in citations)
  - Both agree the biggest skill is adaptability — industry is changing too fast for playbooks; teams need to stay nimble, track results, and test new methods (Reddit, link building, technical SEO, product marketing)
  - GrowthX is trying to productize/automate this process using AI agents to handle parts of content creation that don't require expert judgment
