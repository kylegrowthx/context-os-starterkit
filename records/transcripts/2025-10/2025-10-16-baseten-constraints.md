## Summary

GrowthX discussed critical challenges with Baseten's initial content outline, which Baseten's head of marketing rejected as worse than "college student with Google search" quality—revealing a mismatch between AI-generated content standards and Baseten's need for technical accuracy with sources less than 3-6 months old. The team proposed three paths forward: (1) refining the AI pipeline with expert review and recency constraints, (2) shifting to high-level, glossary-style educational content, or (3) building a separate website or seeding content on platforms like Reddit to reduce scrutiny. Immediate next steps include Daniel evaluating the Content OS and testing the agentic pipeline on 10 topics, Andi drafting a one-pager of options for Baseten's head of marketing, and Jason getting pricing and expertise from an AI/vector database specialist who could review content part-time.

---

## Context

Baseten is a GrowthX delivery client in the AI infrastructure space—they operate in a fast-moving, highly technical domain where accuracy and currency are paramount. The engagement started with Baseten approving a set of content topics and working with Marcel on a kickoff call, but when GrowthX delivered the initial outline generated by an AI pipeline and validated by Perplexity, Baseten's head of marketing rejected it outright, saying it looked worse than something a college student with Google Search could produce. This wasn't a disagreement on topics—Baseten had signed off on the direction and Ada's gap analysis—but on execution quality. The rejection signals both a skill gap in GrowthX's current approach to highly technical AI content and a potential account risk, especially given precedent with Unstructured (another client who churned after losing confidence in content quality). The core tension: Baseten is trying to be cutting-edge and factually accurate in an industry where developments shift every 3-6 months, but GrowthX's standard AI pipeline can't keep pace with that velocity or depth without human expert review.

## Key Topics

### Content Accuracy and Recency Challenges

  - Baseten requires content less than 3-6 months old due to rapid industry changes
  - Initial AI-generated outline deemed worse than "college student with Google search"
  - Perplexity and initial pipeline incorrectly validated the outline's accuracy
  - Difficulty in producing super-accurate technical content highlighted

### AI Pipeline Improvements

  - Current pipeline not agentic; new agentic pipeline now in place
  - Proposal to add constraints, restriction courses, and expert review to the pipeline
  - Option to pass recency parameters (e.g., only content from September forward)
  - Exploration of AI watch directory and other data sources for up-to-date information

### Content Strategy Adjustments

  - Shift towards high-level, educational, "glossary-style" content
  - Focus on decision processes rather than specific model comparisons
  - Explore creating a separate website or subdomain for less scrutinized content
  - Consider seeding content on platforms like Reddit for AI mentions

### Human Expert Involvement

  - Necessity of involving domain experts for review and validation
  - Challenges in finding affordable, knowledgeable reviewers (e.g., $2000/month part-time)
  - Potential candidate identified who has written for vector database companies

### Client Expectations and Relationship

  - Baseten approved topics but rejected the execution
  - Need to align on content creation process and quality checks
  - Importance of showing the pipeline and human-in-the-loop processes to build confidence
  - Possibility of price renegotiation based on increased complexity
