## Summary

Surge AI met with GrowthX Labs to explore organic growth for recruitment, moving beyond reliance on paid ads. Marcel presented GrowthX's content-driven strategy: research audience-specific questions, leverage Surge's strong domain authority (ranked #1 for "data annotation" with 55 pages), and scale content through agentic workflows paired with human editors. The team aligned that website content expansion should take priority over video/UGC, and that recruitment success should be measured by quality and targeting (the right people for specific cohorts) rather than volume. Surge AI will provide detailed audience context (target cohorts, priorities, multilingual segments, CAC considerations), and GrowthX Labs will deliver an initial content strategy with volume estimates by early the following week.

---

## Context

Surge AI is a data annotation and AI training platform that recruits experts globally to label, evaluate, and improve AI models. The company has built a strong position in the market with approximately 55 pages of web content and dominates traffic for the core keyword "data annotation," ranking #1 and generating 96% of their organic traffic from that single term. However, their acquisition strategy has relied almost entirely on paid advertising, limiting growth in organic channels. In recent months, Surge has expanded beyond native English speakers to include multilingual cohorts and STEM experts, creating more complex and varied recruitment needs across geographies and skillsets.

Marcel Santilli is the founder of GrowthX Labs, a content marketing services firm specializing in B2B organic growth through research-driven content strategies and agentic content production workflows. GrowthX has proven success scaling content for companies like Tiro and works with companies across multiple verticals (code editors, security, workplace management, SMB SaaS). This meeting represented GrowthX pitching their services to Surge AI's leadership — Akash Gupta (on Surge's side), Nancy Flournoy (leading recruitment strategy), and Jason Sexton (operations/scaling) — to explore a content-driven recruitment acquisition model.

## Decisions & Commitments

- **Priority: Website content first** — Both GrowthX and Surge agreed that building out Surge's website content library should come before investing in video, YouTube, or UGC. With only 55 pages, there is substantial low-hanging fruit to capture.
- **Quality over volume** — Recruitment success will be measured by targeting the right people for specific cohorts (multilingual groups, STEM experts, high-need/high-CAC segments) rather than maximizing overall recruit volume.
- **Deep dive on audience data** — Surge will provide detailed context on target audiences, including cohort definitions, recruitment priorities, and nuances across languages and skill levels.

- **Initial strategy by early next week** — GrowthX Labs will conduct research and deliver an initial content strategy proposal including volume estimates, topic clusters, and recommended starting cohorts.

## Key Topics

### Current Recruitment Landscape

  - Surge AI dominates traffic but relies mainly on paid ads
  - Competitors (e.g., Mercor) use referral programs effectively
  - Aligner improved creative quality; Handshake and Mercor gaining organic attention
  - Surge expanded beyond native English speakers to multilingual and STEM experts
  - Surge doesn't use agencies for recruitment (unlike Scale AI)

### GrowthX Labs' Approach to Organic Growth

  - Research audience-specific questions and become the best answer programmatically
  - Leverage Surge's existing domain authority (ranks \#1 for "data annotation")
  - Create topic clusters and content types (e.g., how-tos, comparisons) based on audience research
  - Use agentic workflows and human editors to scale high-quality content production
  - Example: Tiro's success in ranking for specific salary-related keywords

### Content Strategy and Scaling

  - Surge has only 55 pages; 96% of traffic from "data annotation" keyword
  - Opportunity to expand content significantly (e.g., Handshake has 45,000 pages, 1,000 blog posts)
  - GrowthX's platform includes dashboards, custom knowledge bases, and detailed workflows
  - Focus on creating content that can be cited in AI-powered search results (e.g., ChatGPT, Claude)
  - Leverage dataannotation.tech as a perceived third-party site for credibility

### Video Content Considerations

  - YouTube is the second-largest search engine
  - Videos increasingly cited in search results
  - Priority should be on building out website content before expanding to video

### Recruitment Goals and Priorities

  - Focus on efficient recruitment targeting the right people, not just volume
  - Consider prioritizing specific multilingual cohorts or high-need/high-CAC segments
  - Balance between quantity and quality of recruits
