## Key Topics

### Kira's v2.0 Launch & Programmatic SEO Vision

  - **Context:** Kira is launching v2.0 in early January, shifting from a sales-led (district-first) to a PLG (teacher-first) model.
  - **Product:** A generative platform for interactive educational resources, including:
      - Interactive videos with AI-generated questions and chat.
      - Full, multi-component lessons (video, chat, assessments).
      - Chat environments with goal-based progression.
  - **Key Differentiator:** Kira creates interactive "lessons," not static "lesson plans" like competitors (e.g., School.ai, Magic School).
  - **Vision:** Use the product to generate a massive, public library of resources for SEO.
      - **Lesson Library:** Create a unique page for each of the \~10,000 standards (subject, grade, state).
      - **Interactive Videos:** Generate pages for thousands of educational YouTube videos.
      - **Quizzes:** Build and publish large-scale exams.

### GrowthX's Recommended Strategy: Editorial First

  - **Rationale:** Kira's low domain authority (\~1.8k backlinks) makes a large-scale programmatic launch risky.
  - **The Risk:** A large launch could fail to get indexed or rank, wasting significant time and resources.
  - **The Solution:** A two-phase approach.
    1.  **Editorial Content First:**
          - Build domain authority and topical relevance.
          - Generate quick wins with high-intent topics (e.g., "6th grade social studies standards, Alabama").
          - Validate keyword clusters and user signals before committing to a large-scale build.
    2.  **Programmatic SEO Second:**
          - Once authority is established, build custom templates and workflows.
          - Start with small batches (10-20 pages) to test indexing, impressions, and CTR.
          - Scale only after seeing strong positive signals.
  - **Benefit:** Editorial content can link to the interactive programmatic pages, creating a powerful, interconnected content strategy.

### Logistics & Process

  - **Website Platform:** Kira's site is on Framer.
      - **GrowthX:** Not a blocker. For thousands of pages, a database (e.g., Supabase) is more scalable than a CMS like Framer or Webflow.
  - **GrowthX Process:**
      - **Lead Time:** \~6 weeks to kickoff.
      - **Kickoff Phase:** 8 weeks (or 10 for holiday periods).
          - **Goal:** Unblock all dependencies and get to publishing quickly to gather performance signals.
      - **Execution Phase:** Ongoing growth based on data-driven optimization.
