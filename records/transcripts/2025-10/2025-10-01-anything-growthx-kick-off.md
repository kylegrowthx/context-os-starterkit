GrowthX and Anything held their kick-off meeting to chart a content strategy focused on SEO and AI-powered content to accelerate ARR growth from the current $3-4M run rate. ## Context

Anything is an AI app builder that recently pivoted to focus on entrepreneurs wanting to build production apps and make money online. Founded four years ago, the company iterated through code generation and a dev marketplace before embracing LLMs in 2023. ## Key Topics

### Anything's Product and Market Position

  - Recently pivoted to an AI app builder, launched in August 2023
  - Revenue grew to \~$2M in first 2 weeks, now at $3-4M run rate
  - Differentiators:
      - Reliable AI agent (compared favorably to dev-focused tools)
      - Strong mobile app building capabilities
      - High-quality AI-generated designs
      - End-to-end infrastructure built-in (no need for external services)
  - Targeting entrepreneurs wanting to make money online with AI apps
  - Pricing tiers from $50 to $2000/month

### User Demographics and Behavior

  - \~40% identify as entrepreneurs or want to launch new products/services
  - "Tech-adjacent builders" - not full-time devs but familiar with app development
  - Job roles include marketers, designers, product managers, IT/ops
  - User journey often starts with internal tools, evolves to monetization
  - Most sticky users: those building real products/services with revenue potential
  - Conversion often happens within first 3 hours of usage

### Current Marketing and Growth Channels

  - \~5K daily sign-ups across channels
  - Channel breakdown:
      - Search: 20%
      - Word of mouth: 30%
      - AI-related: 12%
      - Social (YouTube, Instagram, TikTok, Twitter): remainder
  - Focus areas:
    1.  Product launches
    2.  Social UGC (TikTok, LinkedIn, X, YouTube, Reddit)
    3.  AI/SEO content (new focus with GrowthX)
  - Potential future channels: partnerships, agency partner programs

### Retention Challenges and Opportunities

  - Main churn reasons:
    1.  Expense (especially for less serious users)
    2.  Reliability issues (top product focus to improve)
    3.  Users forgetting to use the product regularly
  - Opportunity: Create content as triggers to re-engage users
  - Plan to add more background processes and potentially charge for hosting

### Content Strategy and Quality Bar

  - CEO recently rewrote blog content to improve quality
  - Aiming for a tone similar to OpenAI, Stripe, and Paul Graham's writing
  - Want content that appeals to both customers and potential hires/investors
  - Goal: Be known as the go-to platform for making money online with AI apps

### Metrics and Goals

  - Primary metric: ARR growth
  - Secondary goals:
      - Driving high-converting traffic
      - Improving retention through helpful content
      - Building brand awareness to attract users and potential hires
