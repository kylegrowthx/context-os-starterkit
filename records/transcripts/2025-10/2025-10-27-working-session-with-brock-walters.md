## Key Topics

### Strategic Narrative: Cross-Platform MDM

  - The document's "Apple-first" framing is outdated and must be removed.
  - **Rationale:** Competitors (Kanji, Jamf) are now adding Windows/Android support, copying Fleet's cross-platform strategy.
      - This eliminates Fleet's previous differentiator and makes a platform-agnostic narrative essential for future competition.
  - The new narrative must position Fleet as an "Apple-first MDM" that is also a leading cross-platform solution.

### Persona Refinement: Champion vs. Buyer

  - The document's personas are too generic and must reflect the complex Champion-Buyer dynamic.
  - **Insight:** A champion's influence varies widely. A champion with 100% decision-making power can still be overruled by a manager's non-product concerns (e.g., contract consolidation, budget).
  - **Content Goal:** Equip champions to influence buyers.
      - **Buyer Challenges:** Navigating CFO pressure to consolidate tools (e.g., favoring "free" Microsoft Intune due to complex licensing) and justifying spend.
      - **Champion Challenges:** Overcoming user resistance to new tools (a "Stockholm Syndrome" effect) and educating management on Fleet's value.

### Product Positioning: Explicitly an MDM

  - The document must explicitly state that Fleet is an MDM.
  - **Rationale:** To counter the competitor-driven narrative that Fleet is *only* an OS Query manager.
  - **Language Edits:**
      - Remove phrases like "built on OS Query" → reinforces the "only OS Query" narrative.
      - Remove jargon: "data instrumentation," "bring your own threat intelligence."
      - Replace vague terms: "asset tracking" → "hardware/software inventory."
      - Correct inaccuracies: "FedRAMP" → Fleet is not FedRAMP certified.
