## Overview

- **Define a Single ICP:** Focus all content on one Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) to build a loyal audience and trigger YouTube's "tranche" algorithm, which rewards consistent viewership.
- **Adopt a "Title-First" Process:** Reverse the current workflow. Start with a compelling title/thumbnail, then script the hook and CTA to maximize Click-Through Rate (CTR) and 30-second retention.
- **Create a Separate Podcast Channel:** Move conversational podcasts to a dedicated channel. This prevents them from diluting the main channel's focus and failing the algorithm's "tranche" test.
- **Use Competitor Analysis for Topics:** Find video topics by analyzing competitor channels for videos that significantly outperform their channel average (e.g., 5-10x more views).

## Key Topics

### The Problem: Inconsistent Content & Audience Confusion

  - The AI-led Growth channel's varied content (SaaS builds, SEO, GEO) attracts different viewers for each video.
  - This inconsistency confuses the algorithm, which optimizes for audience watch time (CTR + retention).
  - **Result:** Videos fail the "tranche" test. The algorithm shows a new video to the most loyal viewers; if they don't engage, the video is not promoted to a wider audience.

### The Solution: A "Title-First" Production Process

  - Caleb's process, which grew his channel from 5k subscribers in 15 months to rapid growth in 7, reverses the current workflow.
  - **Process Steps:**
    1.  **Title:** Create a compelling, "mystery box" title that hooks viewers without giving away the ending.
    2.  **Thumbnail:** Design the thumbnail to complement the title and maximize CTR.
    3.  **Hook:** Script the first 30 seconds word-for-word to immediately confirm the video's topic and set up a content loop (e.g., a challenge).
    4.  **Outline:** Build a skeleton outline for the main content.
    5.  **CTA:** Script a word-for-word outro that directs viewers to a specific, relevant video.
  - **Rationale:** This structure ensures high CTR and 30-second retention, signaling to YouTube that the video is valuable.

### Platform-Specific Content Strategy

  - **YouTube:** Best for deep-dive, scripted videos. Caleb's "Young Timmy" ICP is a male interested in AI for SEO.
  - **Shorts/Reels/TikTok:** Requires native content designed for short-form viewing. Repurposing long-form content fails because it's not scripted for the format.
  - **Email/Facebook:** Audiences are similar and respond well to posts that perform on either platform.
