## Key Topics

### Strategic Pivot: From Listings to "Ruth"

  - Vendr's new AI negotiation agent, "Ruth," launches tomorrow, shifting the partnership's focus.
  - **Old Goal:** Drive traffic to product listing pages → generate price estimates.
  - **New Goal:** Drive traffic to `vendr.com` → convert users into engaged "Ruth" users.
  - **Why:** "Ruth" provides richer purchase intent data (e.g., product scope, user count) than the old price calculator.
  - **Monetization:** "Ruth" is free at launch but will have paid tiers, making user acquisition a top priority.

### Content Strategy: Editorial-First Approach

  - Vendr's 12,000 product listing pages have shallow content, with only \~200 updated.
  - **GrowthX's Recommendation:** Start with an editorial-first strategy, as it provides faster signals and avoids the high upfront cost of programmatically updating all pages.
  - **Rationale:**
      - **LLM Citations:** LLMs (e.g., ChatGPT) favor listicles and guides, which are easier to get cited than deep product pages.
      - **Traffic Source:** Vendr's blog and buyer guides already have the most traffic and backlinks.
  - **Proposed Plan:**
    1.  Create net-new editorial content (listicles, comparisons, guides) to capture traffic and LLM citations.
    2.  Use this content to cross-link to key product listing pages, building topical authority.
    3.  Postpone the programmatic update of all 12k pages until the editorial strategy is validated.

### Scope & Term Length

  - **Vendr's Request:** A 6-month term for the execution phase.
  - **Rationale:** The 6-month term allows Vendr to quickly validate the new strategy's impact on "Ruth" conversions.
  - **GrowthX's Standard:** A 12-month term for execution, preceded by an 8-week strategy sprint.
  - **Secondary Focus:** Develop a marketing strategy for the `agent.vendr.com` domain, including content and potentially paid ads.
