### SEO/GEO Strategy Insights

  - GEO is an extension of SEO, not a replacement; what ranks on Google should rank on LLMs, but LLMs have stricter requirements.
  - LLMs are more sensitive to technical structure: JavaScript is invisible to LLM crawlers (vs. Google's partial JS rendering), page speed must be <0.03 seconds (vs. Google's 0.3s target).
  - LLMs allocate ~1000 tokens per page crawl; if content sits deep in page structure, additional tokens are consumed, reducing likelihood of citation.
  - Success metrics for GEO: (1) brand mentions in LLM responses, (2) citations/links included, (3) sentiment (positive vs. negative mentions).
  - Citation depends on content structure (headers, subheaders answering the query directly), not ranking position; page rank doesn't guarantee citation in LLM results.

### AI and LLM Discussion

  - AI will augment (not replace) human roles; allows scaling (1 person managing 100 clients instead of 10), increases efficiency, creates new jobs; anyone not using AI will lack competitive advantage.
  - LLM citation selection is query-dependent, not ranking-dependent; ChatGPT with Bing integration may not behave like Bing itself; crawling pattern differs from Google.
  - ChatGPT Path (Chrome plugin) reveals LLM web search results and citation sources; can expose 100+ crawled links per query for research and strategy adjustment.
  - Persona-based prompt tracking: Define client personas/buyer types, generate LLM-aligned prompts for each persona using LLMs themselves, track weekly/monthly performance, measure mentions/citations/sentiment across personas.
  - Prompt wording matters; ChatGPT queries are sentences (not 2-3 keywords like Google); LLMs understand user context/profile (age, income, buying intent, caretaker status), enabling behavioral targeting at scale.

### GrowthX Platform and Service Model

  - GrowthX is building a platform for knowledge work (internal tool + potential future product); currently uses agency model where customers see services, not the platform directly.
  - Platform architecture: workflow builder (similar to Clay/Airtable but optimized for content); research step pulls from web/private repos; content generation step with LLM; human review gates at various points; high-touch for technical clients, more programmatic for scalable work.
  - GrowthX customers: 60+ B2B startup/tech clients, mostly pre-IPO, some approaching public markets; mostly US-based; custom integrations with clients' existing tools (GSC, GA, etc.).
  - Analytics/visibility challenges: Currently using Scrunch for AI visibility tracking; this is described as immature; building out native analytics is part of the role scope.
  - Go-to-market philosophy: Charging for human service allows flexibility if/when AI replaces parts of work; GrowthX can maintain revenue per client by increasing efficiency rather than reducing headcount.

### Company Vision and Future Roadmap

  - Ultimate goal: Build a platform that other companies could use (SaaS model), while currently using it internally as an agency.
  - Portfolio includes: (1) Core agency (largest revenue), (2) AI-led Growth community (AI education at ailledgrowth.com), (3) Future product launch planned.
  - Exploring applying the AI-agency model to other domains (e.g., recruiting with AI workflows), but cautious about overextension.
  - ~70 employees: 20 engineering/design/product, 5-6 go-to-market contractors, 40-50 client services.
  - Async-first collaboration model; core hours 9am-1pm Pacific; employees across US and Europe.
