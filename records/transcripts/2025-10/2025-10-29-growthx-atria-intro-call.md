## Key Topics

### Atria's Content & SEO Challenge

  - **Context:** Atria's current content output (2–3 articles/week) is unsustainable for its new high-ACV, ABM strategy, which requires a scalable partner.
  - **Goal:** Use programmatic SEO as a "supporting motion" to build credibility and capture long-tail search traffic, freeing internal resources for high-touch thought leadership.
  - **Concerns:**
      - Skepticism about programmatic SEO's ability to drive high-quality leads.
      - Fear of Google penalizing low-quality, AI-generated content.
  - **Current State:**
      - **CMS:** Framer.
      - **Blog:** Content is written but not yet live due to a bottleneck in the CMS publishing process.

### GrowthX's Solution: Scalable Editorial

  - **Model:** A "tech-enabled agency" (not a pure software company) that scales quality editorial content.
      - **Platform:** Proprietary internal platform, built after migrating from AirOps.
      - **Process:** Combines AI workflows with expert human oversight.
      - **Capacity:** Limited by human resources, leading to the EOY booking.
  - **Engagement Structure:**
      - **Phase 1 (8-week Strategy Sprint):** Builds a deep ICP profile (fears, motivations, pain points) to create a personalized content roadmap.
          - **Outcome:** A 6-month content plan and live articles by week 3–4.
          - **Off-Ramp:** Atria can exit after Phase 1 with a full content roadmap.
      - **Phase 2 (Growth Execution):** GrowthX handles end-to-end execution, including:
          - CMS publishing (Framer).
          - LLM visibility monitoring.
          - Performance tracking to conversions.
  - **Programmatic SEO:** A secondary option requiring a specific, data-driven use case.
      - **Examples:** Ramp's vendor database or per diem calculator.
      - **Atria's Ideas:** Competitor comparisons, product feature pages, keyword clusters (e.g., "creator analytics reporting").
