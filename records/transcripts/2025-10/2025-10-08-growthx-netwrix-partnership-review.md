## Summary

GrowthX and Netwrix held a partnership review to evaluate fit and address concerns from their initial conversation six months prior. Netwrix is launching a redesigned website next week and faces a critical challenge: scaling high-quality, technical content for decision-maker personas (CIOs) while avoiding vanity metrics like high traffic from non-converting, generic topics (e.g., PowerShell commands). GrowthX presented its workflow-as-code engine that creates deeply researched, persona-specific content at scale, integrated with product marketing insights. The key question for both parties: Is there sufficient mutual value and integration potential to move forward?

---

## Context

Netwrix is a data security and compliance platform that inherited a significant organic traffic decline (~1.5M visitors lost in the prior year). John Knightly and James Levin lead strategy and growth initiatives at Netwrix, while Mariel Grinstein heads a lean content marketing team that recently lost two people due to international relocation and is pivoting from 18 product-focused messages to six core solution areas. Marcel Santilli and Tyler Pavlas represent GrowthX, a content marketing services company ($200k+/year engagements) with a proprietary AI workflow that creates technical guides and content at scale for companies like Abnormal Security, SentinelOne, and Clerk. The Netwrix team had evaluated GrowthX six months prior and now wants to dig deeper into whether GrowthX can integrate seamlessly with their existing product marketing processes and help them create conversion-focused content for decision makers—a painful problem neither their current in-house team nor their external SEO agency can solve at the required depth and speed.
