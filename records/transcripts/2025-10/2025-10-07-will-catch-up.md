## Summary

Will Ruzvidzo, a new Managing Editor at GrowthX, met with Aida Knežević to understand the company's evolution from 18 people in December 2022 to its current post-Series A structure with Managing Directors, Managing Editors, and Content Managers. Aida outlined how the Strategy Sprint team conducts 8-week client onbodings with 10-15 initial blog posts before clients transition to execution teams, and explained that ME work is approximately 60% editing and 40% strategy collaboration. ## Key Topics

### Company Evolution and Structure

  - Dec '22: 18 people, first MEs hired to improve content quality
  - Post-Series A: Added Managing Directors (MDs) for strategic leadership
  - Current structure: Strategy Sprint team (8-week onboarding) → Execution teams (ongoing content delivery)
  - Roles: Managing Directors (MDs), Managing Editors (MEs), Content Managers (CMs)

### Strategy Sprint Process

  - 8-week client onboarding period
  - Develop blog content strategy (typically topic cluster approach)
  - Create 10-15 initial blog posts
  - Calibrate voice, tone, and setup
  - Client decides to continue or not; GrowthX.ai also evaluates fit

### Managing Editor Responsibilities

  - Editing (≈60% of workload)
  - Collaborate on strategy development with Managing Directors
  - Keyword research and topic ideation
  - Client call participation and feedback implementation (starting week 3-4 of client engagement)
  - Content Manager mentoring and skill gap support
  - Content calendar management and performance tracking

### Tips for Success at GrowthX.ai

  - Proactively identify and solve problems (e.g., Vivek's Looker dashboard)
  - Maintain high content quality standards
  - Exercise agency in suggesting improvements and solutions
  - Customize workflows with CMs to fit your style
  - Develop strong client relationships as a trusted advisor
  - Communicate issues, especially with content production workflows

### Company Culture and Growth

  - Fast-paced startup environment
  - Continuous improvement in tools and processes (e.g., Aerofs → Atlas)
  - Leadership trusts MEs to execute and innovate on content strategies
  - Company culture still developing beyond work focus
