## Key Topics

### GrowthX's Offering and Approach

Marcel outlined GrowthX's core model: a managed service combining an AI-powered platform with human expertise, specifically a "forward deploy strategist" embedded in the client's team as an extension of their marketing operations. The platform is built around three key capabilities: (1) context engineering, using document ingestion and semantic understanding to build comprehensive knowledge graphs about a client's products, audiences, and competitive landscape; (2) agentic workflows that handle research planning, content drafting, fact-checking, code validation, internal linking, and cover image generation; and (3) continuous content optimization monitoring tracked through dashboards showing organic search and AI visibility metrics. GrowthX targets continuous content refreshing (ideal cadence: refresh all 1,800 pages every three months) combined with gap-filling for new search opportunities. The fact-checking workflow is particularly sophisticated: it breaks content into chunks, extracts claims, formulates validation questions, conducts deep research, and flags inaccuracies with evidence. Marcel emphasized that this human-in-the-loop, agentic approach produces "really high-quality content at scale" that he believes stands apart from single-prompt LLM tools that require heavy human babysitting.

### BitSight's Current Content Situation and Motivation

BitSight operates a substantial content catalog of approximately 1,800 pages covering security research, industry guidance, and product documentation. Catherine and Michael noted that BitSight has strong internal content capability: their security research team continuously produces original research, SMEs contribute, and their overall SEO performance is solid. However, their key frustration is that while their buyers are actively using LLMs to research security solutions, BitSight's pages are underrepresented in AI-generated answers. Catherine emphasized the challenge: "We know that behavior, even though we're not getting a lot of traffic to our website from LLMs, our qualitative research shows that our buyers and their teams are using the heck out of them, right? So it's like, we got to be in there." Michael highlighted another driver: LLM models and their citation behaviors are changing rapidly (ChatGPT 5 behaves differently from ChatGPT 4; different models favor different sources and content types), and BitSight needs expert guidance to keep up. BitSight's primary need is optimization and gap-filling (not new content creation), plus prompt engineering expertise.

### GrowthX's Track Record and Proof Points

Marcel cited a customer portfolio of 60+ companies, many in cybersecurity (Abnormal Security, Sentinel-1, Okta, Promfu, Obsidian, Auth0) and other verticals (Ramp, Zapier, Brex, Navan, Reddit, Webflow, Bubble, Udemy, Diligent). For Augment Code, GrowthX created over 120 technical guides in four months, achieving 24x traffic growth and 10x AI visibility on priority keywords. For Ramp (which has a five-person content team and over 1,800 existing pages), GrowthX delivered 900+ pages in one year, resulting in significant brand visibility increase. Marcel also shared personal background: he spent ~10 years in cybersecurity, launched securityintelligence.com at IBM, and later ran techbeacon.com (1,000+ contributors). This history attracted security companies to GrowthX. Weekly strategy refinement meetings with clients allow GrowthX to iterate and adapt its approach based on performance.

### The LLM Visibility Challenge and Content Optimization Philosophy

Marcel explained the shift in search behavior driven by LLMs. Traditional search optimization focuses on ranking for specific queries (e.g., "best security platform"). But with LLMs, context is richer and citation patterns are wider: "what are the sources cited in one single answer? Sometimes it's six or seven of the pieces we've created for them...sometimes 200 sources." This means success requires both volume and quality—the long tail of questions is longer because LLMs can field nuanced questions people would once ask sales teams. Marcel's philosophy: "it's no longer a volume or quality game. You have to do both. And that has to come from a deeper understanding of your audience and also your company, your product, right?" He noted that unlike SEO optimization (which might be a relatively simple backlink or keyword play), AI visibility optimization requires continuous content management because "your catalog of content is your catalog now, you know? Amazon spends over a billion dollars a year in improving their catalog of items."

### Pricing and Service Model Uncertainty

Michael asked directly: "How does this factor into what I'm assuming is your pricing model in terms of you're not going to do 1,800 pages for us over the course of the next month. So what is like the volume that we would expect between net new versus optimization?" Marcel acknowledged that strategy varies by customer: Abnormal Security receives new blog posts, articles, guides, and a refreshed glossary; Sentinel-1 gets a vulnerability database plus cybersecurity 101 content. Most customers have GrowthX publishing daily (net new or reworked content). However, the exact model for BitSight—which doesn't need heavy content creation but does need optimization—wasn't clearly defined in the call. Catherine acknowledged she needed to "get my head wrapped around it in terms of what that value proposition would be for BitSight." Marcel reiterated that optimization is fundamentally about managing content freshness and credibility at scale, which requires humans in the loop and isn't decoupled from content work. The team agreed to continue the conversation with a focus on BitSight's specific optimization gaps and potential pricing for a customized engagement.
