## Key Topics

### Go-to-Market Strategy: Express Motion Dominates Near Term

Cody explained that Express Motion ($40K package) remains the primary focus, with Professional (PLG motion) launching December. Prophecy's channel strategy is tiered by opportunity and feasibility: enterprise cold outreach and trade shows are must-do activities; AEO/GEO on product pages is S-tier; traditional SEO content ranks lower. Raj wants to optimize Express and Express-adjacent pages for high-intent searches, not create broad top-funnel blog content. Kyle nodded that this AEO/GEO strategy works well — showing up in consideration stage with a fast buying process (Express's strength).

### Founder-Driven Skepticism: SEO is Slow, Capital is Tight

Cody was direct: Raj thinks SEO is slow, hasn't delivered ROI, and doesn't align with startup needs. With B-round capital partially deployed and potential Series C funding on horizon, Raj prioritizes channels delivering revenue in the next 6 months. Kyle acknowledged this is a real founder dynamic — he's seen companies wait too long and regret it — but the tradeoff is real: immediate wins vs. long-term organic authority. Cody agrees in principle but notes that Prophecy is investing in PLG and lower-friction buying, which should drive organic channel ROI higher than traditional enterprise-only sales.

### Content Strategy Pivot: Ungated, Question-Driven Resources

Instead of gated lead magnets, Prophecy is shifting to ungated, guide-length content answering analyst questions in data prep and AI. This content serves multiple functions: AEO/GEO on product pages, founder brand vehicles, social repurposing, and community education. Cody plans benchmarks, reports, and white papers — all ungated, all searchable. This satisfies Raj's bar: deep, founder-worthy content that'd impress other data-tech founders. Kyle showed Augment Code examples of technical guides performing in top AEO results and driving 2x traffic.

### Advanced Research Methods: Reddit Scraping and Persona Mapping

Kyle pitched Reddit scraping as a research method: identify subreddits where personas engage, scrape questions and discussion patterns, rank by engagement, surface high-intent topics. Cody said this is a skillset they lack internally — they're good at execution but not discovery research. Kyle positioned this as high-leverage: answers questions for AEO/GEO, seeds founder brand experiments, informs PLG community strategy. Cody was intrigued; GrowthX can "check off multiple boxes" for them.

### Website Launch Friday: Technical and Messaging Overhaul

New site goes live Friday, driven by design and messaging not SEO. Cody flagged core web vitals issues (known for 3 years). Kyle offered GrowthX's experience (Galea migration Nov 2025) as a model. Friday launch is a pivot moment: low-hanging AEO/GEO optimization on product pages, technical fixes post-launch, and positioning for Raj's high-impact expectations.

### Kickoff Next Week: Show and Tell, Not Just Ask

Kyle is blending GrowthX's usual kickoff (ask and learn) with show and tell — demonstrating past wins. Kyle wants time with Marcel (CEO) to show authority and logos (Augment Code, Lovable, Zapier just signed). Cody endorsed this: "You guys are killing it right now. Flashing logos at Raj works." The meeting also served as Cody advocating internally to Raj's team by sharing Prophecy's strategic context.
