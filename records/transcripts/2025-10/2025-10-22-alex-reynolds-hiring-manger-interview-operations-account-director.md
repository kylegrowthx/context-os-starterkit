## Key Topics

### GrowthX's Agency & SaaS Model

  - **Core Business:** Organic content agency (blogs, newsletters, social).
  - **Internal Platform:** An AI workflow tool, built in-house after outgrowing AirOps, that will eventually become a client-facing SaaS product.
  - **Pod Structure:**
      - **Account Director (This Role):** High-level strategist and relationship builder.
      - **Account Strategist:** Day-to-day PM, goal tracking, contractual alignment.
      - **Managing Editor / Content Manager:** Generates content in the platform.
  - **Reporting:** The Account Director manages 2–3 pods, each with an Account Strategist and a content creator.

### Alex's Experience at Graphite

  - **Current Role:** Account Director managing 4 clients and overseeing an 8-client pod.
  - **Primary Focus:** High-level strategy and relationship building, with renewals as the main KPI.
  - **Strategic Approach:**
      - **"90/5 Rule":** Focus on the 5% of work that drives impact, not just best practices.
      - **Prioritization:** Based on client needs (renewals, new kickoffs, C-suite visibility), not just account size.
  - **Team Management:**
      - **Direct Reports (4):** Focus on career growth and development.
      - **Pod Capacity:** Directors meet monthly to plan staffing; leadership meets with sales to align on hiring.
      - **Bandwidth Target:** Keep team at \~80% capacity to create a buffer for urgent work.

### Strategic Leadership & Cross-Functional Alignment

  - **AEO (AI Engine Optimization) Success:**
      - Led AEO strategy for Webflow, driving a 40% increase in non-brand signups from ChatGPT since January.
      - Provides a strong foundation for advising clients on new AI models like Atlas.
  - **Resolving Cross-Functional Conflict:**
      - **Problem:** A major disconnect between the flexible Account team and the rigid Editorial team (e.g., fixed 1,500-word article limit).
      - **Solution:**
          - Trained the pod on positive collaboration and end-to-end ownership.
          - Met with Editorial leadership to align on processes.
          - Implemented a new editorial kickoff to flag project nuances early.
      - **Result:** A more collaborative workflow, with junior staff now empowered to resolve issues.

### Management Philosophy & Lessons Learned

  - **Biggest Mistake:** Overloading the team with work, which unintentionally reduced quality and increased Alex's own workload for corrections.
  - **Lesson Learned:** Director decisions have a strong trickle-down effect. Now intentionally manages scope by pushing back on clients with a clear "yes, but here's the impact on the roadmap" framework.
