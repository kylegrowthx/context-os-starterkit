## Key Topics

### Conversion.ai's Challenge

  - **Problem:** Over-reliance on cold outbound leads, which are harder to close than inbound. Conversion.ai had strong inbound from Series A launch but hasn't kick-started inbound channel since.
  - **Goal:** Build an inbound channel via content marketing, but lacks the internal bandwidth. Kyle is the only person handling growth informally, and actively hiring for growth/content roles.
  - **Requirement:** A "white-glove" partner to act as an interim growth/content lead for 3-6 months, not a low-cost point solution. Kyle rejected smaller vendors with point solutions after finding them weren't right for his needs.
  - **Content Priorities:**
      - **Editorial:** High-quality blog content for SEO and topical authority (80% of value is idea, outline, collateral; 20% is writing).
      - **Programmatic:** Landing pages, lead magnets for blog, use-case specific landing pages, and sales collateral for mid-funnel deal support.
      - **Technical SEO:** Interlinking, meta-data, backlink profile acquisition, and competitor analysis.

### GrowthX's Proposed Solution

  - **Service Model:** Editorial-first foundation (3-6 months minimum) before expanding to programmatic work. Tyler explained that custom programmatic work requires heavy human time that doesn't scale.
  - **Process:** End-to-end service (strategy → content roadmap → publishing) powered by internal platform with AI workflows and human experts (MD per client, Managing Editor, Content Manager). Every step has experts in the loop. Agentic workflows run content through quality loops until threshold is reached.
  - **Competitive Advantages:**
      - Strategic artifacts created in Airtable (ICP personas, writing guidelines, company context, proofreader checklists, approved sources).
      - AI research tools (Exa, Tavli) combined with client resources (decks, calls) fed into content creation.
      - Product roadmap: Client platform access coming Q1-Q2 2026; currently using Google Docs for reviews/approvals.
      - Daily publishing at scale without sacrificing quality.
  - **Pricing:**
      - **Strategy Sprint:** $30k for 8 weeks.
          - **Deliverables:** SEO analysis, content roadmap, and ≥10 published articles.
          - **Start:** Week 3-4, content creation begins during sprint.
          - **Rationale:** Protects GrowthX's 8-week investment while giving client opt-out window last week.
      - **Ongoing:** $15k/month for daily publishing (comparable to startup rates).
  - **Workflow & Integration:**
      - **Inputs:** Client resources (decks, sales calls) and deep research (Exa, Tavli) feed AI-powered workflows.
      - **Outputs:** Includes internal/external linking, cover images, infographics, and direct CMS publishing (Webflow — GrowthX prefers access to manage publishing themselves).
      - **Programmatic Templates:** GrowthX builds custom templates on case-by-case basis; can leverage client template prep or GrowthX design engineers depending on logo priority.

### Market & Product Context

  - **AEO as Strategic Priority:** Tyler emphasized AI search as "massive opportunity" and top-of-mind for every CMO. GrowthX uses AugmentCode guides and Ramp case studies to demonstrate success with AI visibility.
  - **Case Studies Mentioned:** Town (full blog build), Ramp (vendor directory, alternative comparisons, spend data integration), AugmentCode (technical guides positioning against GitHub Copilot and Cursor).
  - **Sales Tool Opportunity:** Kyle sees mid-funnel blog content as sales enablement tool to help close technical questions (e.g., Salesforce sync robustness). Tyler counters that case studies with metrics are stronger on sales side than technical blog posts.

### The Scheduling Conflict

  - **GrowthX's Availability:** Booked through EOY 2025; first kickoff is late Jan 2026. Tyler: "highest demand I've ever seen in my career" — no paid outreach, working with Understory to drive event traffic.
  - **Conversion.ai's Urgency:** The 2–3 month delay conflicts with leadership pressure to accelerate growth. Kyle is facing internal pressure to "put the pedal to the gas" immediately.
  - **Deal Risk:** Kyle explicitly flagged that waiting 2-3 months "might be tough" and he needs to "think about it a little" with the team. This suggests the timeline could be a deal-killer.
