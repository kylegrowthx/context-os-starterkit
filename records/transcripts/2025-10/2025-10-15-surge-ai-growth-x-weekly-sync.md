## Summary

GrowthX and Surge AI aligned on a strategic shift in their partnership, pivoting away from general content creation toward three core areas: data annotation work (starting immediately), video content mining from existing podcasts and paused footage, and event execution in New York and Seattle. The teams agreed video clips would be the highest-value near-term output, as Surge AI lacks bandwidth for content repurposing. They also planned to adjust meeting cadence—moving the main sync to bi-weekly while maintaining dedicated work stream calls for data annotation, events, and leaderboard work.

---

## Context

Surge AI is an AI infrastructure company that GrowthX has been partnering with on multiple fronts since mid-2025. The relationship spans content marketing strategy, product feature development (particularly a differentiated leaderboard), and event marketing. This sync represents a recalibration after 3-4 weeks of exploration revealed that Surge AI's in-house content creation is their preference for highly technical or specialized topics, and that their real constraint is not content ideas but execution bandwidth—particularly around video and events. Nick (Surge AI's lead on this partnership) wanted clarity on where GrowthX could add measurable value, given that collaboration on general blog content has felt marginal. The conversation shows pragmatic alignment on focus areas rather than friction.
