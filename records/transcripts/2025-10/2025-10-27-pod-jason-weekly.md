## Summary

The team discussed the strategic pivot from volume-driven to quality-focused content production: shifting Augment from 30 new articles/week to refreshing ~100 underperforming articles to recover declining search traffic. They also explored a capacity-based upsell strategy for Webflow to accelerate engagement, decided to transition the weekly pod sync to monthly meetings, and outlined hiring plans for external domain expert reviewers across Webflow, Augment, and Data Annotation content tracks.

## Context

This is a weekly operations sync for the Pod (product/content delivery team) focused on three major client accounts: Augment (an AI code completion tool), Webflow (a no-code web builder), and Surge/Data Annotation (interview prep platform). The team is shifting from weekly cross-account syncs to account-specific cadences, moving this meeting to monthly strategic vision-setting.

Augment is GrowthX's longest-running engagement with strong content production volume (300+ articles in a few months), but search traffic is declining due to quality issues—prompting a strategic pivot to refresh-focused work. Webflow is in an upsell moment with internal disorganization but high client satisfaction, requiring both clearer engagement framing and technical domain expertise to unblock use-case guides. Both engagements highlight a recurring theme: the team needs external domain expert reviewers to accelerate production and ensure technical accuracy, which is a key hiring priority.
