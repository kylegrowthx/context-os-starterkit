## Overview

- **Content is misaligned:** Current content is too technical and focuses on internal processes (e.g., regulations), not customer problems (e.g., authorization rates), creating a review bottleneck and liability risks.
- **New priority is PayFacs/ISOs:** After successfully building the "High-Opportunity" (high-risk) merchant channel, the focus shifts to partners (PayFacs/ISOs) who drive the majority of transaction volume.
- **Messaging must be nuanced:** Content must avoid disparaging partners (e.g., don't over-promote being a "directly licensed acquirer" to partners who are not) and use indirect language for high-risk clients (e.g., "iGaming" vs. "gambling").
- **Key triggers for PayFacs/ISOs:** Partners seek Rapyd to improve authorization rates (a direct path to revenue), add omni-channel capabilities (Europe only), or gain redundancy after an outage. ISOs specifically need fast merchant onboarding.

## Key Topics

### Problem: Content Misalignment & Review Bottleneck

  - Current content is too deep and speaks to Rapyd's internal processes, not the customer's needs.
  - **Example:** A "Peru Payments Guide" over-indexed on regulations, FX, and taxation—topics that create liability and are irrelevant to merchants.
  - This approach creates a review bottleneck, as only Mark and one other SME can perform final checks, slowing publication.
  - **Goal:** Shift to an audience-first approach, answering high-impact questions directly and avoiding overly technical or liability-prone topics.

### Solution: Refined Customer Personas

  - The previous persona document was too granular; the strategy will now focus on three core buckets.
  - **1. Payment Facilitators (PayFacs) & ISOs (Independent Sales Organizations)**
      - **Definition:** Partners who integrate payments into their own platforms (e.g., Wix, Shopify, QuickBooks).
      - **Role:** Handle merchant onboarding (PayFacs) or just sales/support (ISOs), while Rapyd provides the backend infrastructure.
      - **Titles:** Director-level or above in Payments, E-commerce, Partnerships, BizDev, or Product.
      - **Primary Triggers:**
          - **Better Authorization Rates:** A direct path to revenue by rescuing lost sales.
              - **Rapyd's Edge:** 91–95% auth rate for low-risk clients.
          - **Omni-channel Expansion:** Adding POS capabilities for physical locations (Europe only).
          - **Redundancy:** Seeking a backup provider after an outage or to optimize auth rates.
          - **ISO-Specific:** Slow merchant onboarding.
              - **Rapyd's Edge:** 88% of low-risk merchants onboard in \<1 hr; 95% of onboarded merchants in \<48 hrs.
  - **2. Enterprise Merchants**
      - **Definition:** Large firms (e.g., Uber, Amazon) that manage their own payments infrastructure.
      - **Motivation:** Gain control, visibility, and avoid partner markups.
  - **3. High-Opportunity Industries**
      - **Definition:** Industries considered "high-risk" by others (e.g., iGaming, adult, CBD, crypto, travel).
      - **Motivation:** These clients need a provider and will do the work to integrate directly.
      - **Messaging Challenge:** Must use indirect language (e.g., "iGaming") to avoid being perceived as a "shady" high-risk specialist.
      - **Key Message:** Quick onboarding, high auth rates, and long-term partnership.

### Goal: Reinforce Brand Perception

  - The objective is not to change perception, but to reinforce Rapyd's current image as an innovative, hands-on disruptor.
  - **Key Attributes to Reinforce:** Innovative, hands-on, fast, disruptive.
