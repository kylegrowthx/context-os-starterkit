## Key Topics

### Ramp Upsell Opportunity

Ramp, an existing GrowthX customer on a legacy $9k/quarter contract, wants to create a website for their cashflow desk YouTube channel. The company has 15+ videos and wants to build SEO authority around cashflow content. The challenge: Marcel previously pitched a full website development scope (including domain setup, design, and web development), but GrowthX prefers not to do end-to-end web dev work.

Key unknowns: exact scope (editorial content creation vs. full site build), which resources GrowthX would use, cost structure, and who the decision-maker is now that growth director Luke (the original champion) has left. Victoria, the SEO specialist, is junior and may not have approval authority.

Decision: Tyler will use tomorrow's qualification call to understand Ramp's exact needs, clarify whether they can hire their own web dev agency while GrowthX focuses on editorial, and determine phase one vs. phase two costs. Start date likely December 1-8, possibly sooner if existing MSA speeds approval. Must post findings in DealDesk with resource requirements before committing.

### Zapier Engagement

Zapier faces market pressure from N8N and wants to optimize their template ecosystem for SEO. GrowthX has identified thousands of existing Zapier templates that can be repurposed and optimized, plus opportunities for net new template creation.

Kirkland (GrowthX) has been heavily involved, validated the technical approach, and is confident in execution feasibility. The engagement can start with template repurposing as phase one, with new template creation as a follow-on opportunity. The scope is well-defined and manageable. Team consensus: proceed with engagement; this is a good, well-scoped opportunity.

### Reed AI Consideration

The CEO of Reed AI (formerly CEO of Quantcast and Foursquare, now venture advisor at Madrona) reached out directly to Marcel requesting a termination for convenience clause in the contract.

Decision: Decline. GrowthX does not offer term for convenience options. Tyler will politely decline using the standard rejection response.

### Plus AI Opportunity

Plus AI CEO (venture partner at Madrona) was met by Marcel at a recent event in Seattle and immediately requested a scoping call with Tyler. The ask: programmatic template creation to make their presentation product more SEO-crawlable and reduce friction in template editing.

The complication: Plus AI doesn't need editorial content—they need custom programmatic workflows to rebuild their product offering. This is high complexity and capital-intensive. GrowthX currently has five editorial clients in pipeline willing to sign for standard scope.

Key insight from discussion: Tyler doesn't have a clear framework for scoping programmatic-only (non-editorial) use cases. George suggested a potential play: audit Plus AI's template editorial coverage and propose expanding template variants by audience/use case, plus refreshing descriptions—but this is still a stretch.

Decision: Post detailed requirements in DealDesk, discuss with Marcel in 1:1 to determine if this relationship justifies the effort. Likely defer to 2026 given bandwidth constraints.

### Process Improvements Needed

The team identified several process gaps:

1. **Scoping complex deals:** Tyler lacks confidence scoping programmatic use cases. Solution: Involve technical expert (like Kirkland) in early scoping calls to assess feasibility and build client confidence before committing.

2. **Confidence building with prospects:** Consider offering sample content refreshes or SEO audits as proof-of-concept to help prospects visualize GrowthX's value and reduce procurement friction.

3. **Custom work decision framework:** GrowthX is trying to avoid upselling custom work, yet keeps getting pulled into it (Ramp, Plus AI). Need clearer criteria for when custom work makes sense vs. when to decline or defer.
