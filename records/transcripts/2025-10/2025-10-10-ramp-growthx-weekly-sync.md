## Context

Victoria Naef leads growth marketing at Ramp and has been working with GrowthX for nearly a year on content strategy, primarily around vendor directory pages and editorial content. The engagement has been highly customized at $9K/month with three-month renewals—well below GrowthX's standard rates for new clients. The challenge: Victoria sees this as an experimental project with limited budget, but GrowthX's proposal reflects custom development and design work that normally costs $40K upfront plus $30K/month. Meanwhile, the core engagement (vendor pages and net-new content) has been shrinking as needs shift and team capacity changes.

## Key Topics

### Cash Flow Desk Project Overview

- Ramp's goal: Build an owned affiliate channel to generate external mentions; this is a separate revenue/visibility stream from current work
- Content plan: 3-5 articles per week aligned with their 3-videos/week production pace
- Simple site structure initially (just blog + topics), with expansion potential as project scales
- Domain strategy: Prefer purchasing existing domain with search equity to accelerate indexing vs. starting from zero; timeline 3-6 months to see significant results either way
- Future vision: If Cash Flow Desk succeeds, Ramp plans to create multiple similar owned affiliate sites (Cash Flow Desk is first of planned ecosystem)

### GrowthX Proposal and Pricing

- Standard GrowthX rates: $30K strategy sprint + $15K/month for 5 editorial articles/week (no custom dev)
- Cash Flow Desk proposal: $40K strategy sprint + $30K/month to cover editorial (3-5 articles/week) + web development + design + site infrastructure + video integration workflows
- Additional $15K/month covers custom development, design, and ability to build out site sections over time (not just blog)
- Context: Ramp currently pays $9K/month for ~25 articles/week (20 programmatic + 5 editorial) on custom contract with 3-month renewals—far below market rate
- Ramp's concern: This budget is "more than expected" for an experimental project and doesn't feel scalable; pushed for MVP pricing

### Scope and Budget Alignment

- Victoria wants to define a true MVP: what's the minimum viable product to test whether Cash Flow Desk + video + site mentions actually move visibility metrics?
- Ramp's scaling vision: Prove the concept with one site, then if it works, multiply the engagement (Cash Flow Desk + 3X more or 5X more similar sites)
- Budget uncertainty: Victoria doesn't have a specific number but knows $40K + $30K/month is "way more than expected"; wants to find a price point that works for experimental validation
- Decision process: Victoria and George (Ramp leadership) make final call based on budget fit and ROI potential; Tyler emphasized need for Marcel-George alignment before moving forward
- Ramp's caveat: If GrowthX builds the infrastructure, Ramp expects long-term commitment from GrowthX to manage it (not bringing in-house)

### Current GrowthX-Ramp Engagement (Status & Uncertainty)

- Net-new content: Paused months ago when Abigail went on maternity leave; no new articles being produced
- Vendor directory pages: Main current work, but scope is shifting due to design changes and new data. Matt (Ramp) may no longer need support with refresh work.
- Engagement context: Originally had multiple team members working with GrowthX; now just Victoria and possibly Matt. Luke Tubinis (original contact) is no longer at Ramp.
- Scope trend: Overall work has been decreasing; unclear what the next phase looks like
- Next sync topic: Mara and team will clarify current needs, vendor page status, and potential scope adjustments next week to firm up what ongoing work actually looks like
