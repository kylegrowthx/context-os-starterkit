## Summary

Marcel Santilli presented GrowthX's strategic vision to onboarding team members, positioning the company's shift from a pure services model to a tech-enabled platform (Atlas/ContentOS) combining AI with expert human delivery. The company is operating at $13M+ run rate with 70% gross margins across 60 customers averaging $216K/year, backed by a recent $2M HubSpot investment. Core focus remains on building a "system of work" for marketing that codifies AI workflows, tracks activities against outcomes, manages client context, and continuously improves through context engineering, with initial customer-facing features launching within the next month alongside CheckThat.ai for AI visibility tracking.

---

## Context

This was an internal all-hands onboarding meeting for new GrowthX employees joining the company's delivery and build teams. The meeting served as both strategic alignment and welcome for recent hires, addressing common questions about the company's direction and why leadership is investing in platform development rather than scaling services. Marcel Santilli, co-founder and CEO, walked through the company's positioning as an AI-enabled services firm transitioning toward platform ownership, explaining the market opportunity (32,000+ content agencies and 300,000+ freelancers in the US) and the rational for selective service delivery. The timing is significant: the meeting occurred shortly after HubSpot's $2M investment closed and as the team prepares to launch CheckThat.ai and customer-facing features of Atlas—reflecting both growth capital and strategic execution pressure.

## Overview

- GrowthX is evolving from a service-based model to a tech-enabled platform (Atlas/ContentOS) that combines AI and human expertise for marketing outcomes
- The company is at $13M+ run rate with 70% gross margins, serving 60 customers at an average of $216K/year
- GrowthX recently secured a $2M investment from HubSpot and is launching new products like CheckThat.ai for AI visibility tracking
- The long-term vision is to build a "system of work" for marketing that codifies workflows, measures interventions, and continuously improves through context engineering

---

## Key Topics

### Company Overview and Market Opportunity

  - GrowthX is targeting a massive market: 32,000 content agencies in the US, 300,000+ freelancers in content/SEO services
  - Potential $3.2-4B annual run rate if reaching similar market penetration as SEMrush (25,000 customers)
  - Addressing pain points: rising customer acquisition costs, demand for AI visibility, talent shortages, and complex marketing tools

### Platform Development: Atlas/ContentOS

  - Internal "system of work" platform combining AI workflows and human expertise
  - Features include:
      - Activity tracking to correlate actions with outcomes
      - Context management for client knowledge
      - Deep researcher using AI (AXA, Proplaxity) to build a knowledge graph
      - Agentic workflows as code for scalable execution
      - Fact-checking and content improvement capabilities
      - Automated image and infographic generation

### Strategic Direction

  - Shifting from 100% service-based revenue to a mix of high-end services and platform offerings
  - Expanding from content/organic growth focus to potentially cover more marketing jobs-to-be-done
  - Gradually moving from internal use to customer-operated platform
  - Launching standalone products like CheckThat.ai for AI visibility tracking

### Team and Roles

  - Company structure: build team and deliver team working closely together
  - Forward-deployed strategists (experts) will remain important, but roles may evolve as the platform develops
  - Focus on being selective with service clients to drive platform innovation

### Funding and Growth

  - Recently closed $2M investment from HubSpot
  - Running capital-efficiently while proving AI+human expert model
  - Building in public: sharing progress and some financials for transparency
