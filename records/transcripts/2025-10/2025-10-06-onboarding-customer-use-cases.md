## Summary

## Relevance

### To GrowthX Delivery
- **Strategy sprints model is working**: 8-week sprint structure improved retention dramatically — only one opt-out since May launch, vs. higher churn with the old 90-day pilot model. This is now the standard playbook for new customers.
- **Editorial content is the primary foundation**: Customers that start with editorial content (blog articles, guides) are the best fit and generate the highest success rates. This is where GrowthX lays the foundation for strategy, discovery, and process repeatability.
- **Programmatic content requires timing and selectivity**: Programmatic (templated, multi-variant content) works well when there's clear search volume, low competition, and bad user experiences in existing results. Not all customers need it; only introduce it after proving editorial success.
- **Sales and delivery handoff matured**: Kyle recently transitioned back to delivery after doing temporary sales work, indicating the team now has a dedicated AE. This separation of concerns improves onboarding focus.

### To GrowthX Business Development
- **Strong retention outlook**: After pivoting to strategy sprints in May 2025, the company is "feeling good and optimistic" about renewals. Several early customers (Homebase, Ramp) remain active and paying.
- **Clear ICP alignment**: Venture-backed B2B companies with 14-month average CMO tenure, over-reliance on paid search, and budgets of $200k+/year. This profile is well-understood and consistently delivers results.
- **Upsell opportunity in programmatic**: Once editorial traction is proven, programmatic projects (like Ramp's per diem pages) create expansion revenue. Augment Code is a success story for volume and speed.

### To CheckThat
- **AI Answers mentioned as distribution channel**: Kyle referenced "AI Answers" as an organic channel customers can diversify into alongside SEO and organic social. This signals relevance for CheckThat's AI visibility product in customer expansion conversations.

---

## Key Topics

### Customer Context and Pain Points

  - Many customers are venture-backed companies under pressure to grow quickly
  - Often over-reliant on paid channels (e.g. paid search), seeking to diversify to organic
  - Building organic engines is time-consuming, costly, and challenging to coordinate
  - Average CMO tenure of 14 months creates urgency to show results

### GrowthX.ai's Value Proposition

  - Accelerates organic growth through AI-powered content creation
  - Significantly reduces costs and time to market compared to traditional methods
  - Provides end-to-end support: strategy, execution, measurement, and iteration
  - Helps brands move faster and show results within tight timeframes

### Content Types and Approach

  - Editorial content: Longer-form articles/blogs, requiring deeper editorial process
  - Programmatic content: Template-based, scalable content targeting multiple variants
  - Focus on understanding customer ICP, motivations, and creating valuable answers
  - Rigorous process for strategy, calibration, and continuous improvement

### Business Evolution and Retention

  - Shifted from short 3-month contracts to 12-month agreements with 90-day pilots
  - Recently introduced 8-week "strategy sprints" to improve alignment and retention
  - Retention has improved significantly since implementing strategy sprints
  - Only one opt-out from new model; generally positive outlook on renewals

### Programmatic Content Example

  - Created per diem rate pages for Ramp, targeting high-volume search terms
  - Leveraged AI to scale content creation across multiple state/city variants
  - Balanced templated approach with unique, valuable content for users
