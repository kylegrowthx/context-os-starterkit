## Summary

Hyperbrowser's CEO Shri Sukhani met with GrowthX's Marcel Santilli and Tyler Pavlas to explore a content marketing partnership. Hyperbrowser is building a developer tool for browser automation but has minimal organic content (a failed 11-day effort months ago), while competitors like Bright Data and Oxylabs dominate with hundreds of pages. GrowthX outlined its standard offering: an $30k 8-week strategy sprint followed by $15k/month for daily publishing, with realistic expectations of 9–12 months to significant traffic given Hyperbrowser's low domain authority (~437 referring domains, comparable to DataGrid). The team discussed domain authority comparisons (Lovable at 50k+ domains, DataGrid requiring 4-5 months for results) and agreed February 2026 is the earliest GrowthX can start, though they're planning a lower-cost strategy-only package for earlier-stage companies.

---

## Context

Hyperbrowser is a developer tool for browser automation serving two distinct audiences: enterprise buyers (Directors of Product, Engineering Execs) and AI agent builders (solo developers, founders). The company faces a marketing challenge — they need to compete with large incumbents (Bright Data, Oxylabs) that have hundreds of pages of content, but they must maintain a high-quality, non-"SEO farm" tone to appeal to technical audiences who would be turned off by thin or over-optimized content. This is Shri's first outreach to GrowthX, introduced by Hyperbrowser investor Arnav. The call explored whether GrowthX's full-service content partnership model makes sense for Hyperbrowser's stage (10 months old, early revenue, booked through 2025 with limited marketing bandwidth).

## Key Topics

### Hyperbrowser's Content Challenge

  - **Goal:** Build a robust content library to compete with large incumbents like Bright Data and Oxylabs.
  - **Dual Audience:**
      - **Enterprise Buyers:** Directors of Product, Engineering Execs.
      - **AI Agent Builders:** Solo developers, founders.
  - **Tone Requirement:** Content must be high-quality and avoid an "SEO farm" feel to appeal to the technical AI builder audience.
  - **Current State:** Minimal content, with one prior 11-day effort.

### GrowthX's Proposed Solution

  - **Service:** End-to-end editorial partnership using AI workflows with human oversight.
  - **Process:**
    1.  **8-Week Strategy Sprint ($30k):** Deep research on ICP, competitors, and topics to create context artifacts for AI workflows.
    2.  **Growth Execution ($15k/mo):** Daily publishing/refreshing of content (blogs, guides) based on the strategy.
  - **Timeline:** 9–12 months for new domains to see significant traffic.
  - **New Offering:** A strategy-only package, launching soon, for a lower-cost entry point.

### Domain Authority & Performance Expectations

  - **Hyperbrowser's Domain Authority:** \~437 referring domains.
  - **Performance Comparison:**
      - **DataGrid (Similar Authority):** Required 4–5 months for initial results.
      - **Lovable (High Authority):** Saw immediate impact due to 50k+ referring domains.
  - **Goal:** Capture 10% of Bright Data's non-branded organic traffic (\~90k/mo), targeting \~10k clicks/mo by EOY 1.
