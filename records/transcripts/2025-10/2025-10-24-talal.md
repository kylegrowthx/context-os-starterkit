## Overview

- **Standardization is the top priority.** Inconsistent SEO processes (e.g., strategy creation, keyword research) are a major quality risk, as best practices are not shared.
- **Scrunch data is a "firehose" without a playbook.** The tool generates high-volume, unstructured data (e.g., 600+ ChatGPT prompts) with no standard process for action or tracking impact.
- **Looker reporting is a major bottleneck.** Manual data blending is required due to platform-level aggregation differences (GA4 vs. GSC), and ad-hoc client requests cause significant delays.
- **Talal's strategy is to centralize experiments.** He will build a database of internal LLM/AEO tests to identify and scale effective workflows, avoiding redundant effort.

## Key Topics

### Problem: Inconsistent SEO Processes

  - **Core Issue:** No standardized, company-wide process for foundational SEO tasks.
  - **Example:** Content audits are performed differently by each team, preventing the surfacing and adoption of best practices.
  - **Impact:** Inconsistent strategy quality. Some MEs use only basic SEMrush exports, while others are unfamiliar with advanced techniques like Jobs-to-be-Done (JTBD) frameworks.

### Problem: Unactionable Scrunch Data

  - **Challenge:** Scrunch provides a high volume of data without a clear playbook for action or impact measurement.
  - **Example:** A report showed a client's competitors appearing in ChatGPT for 600+ prompts where the client did not.
      - **Action Taken:** Mapped prompts to existing articles and used them in a content refresh workflow.
      - **Missing:** A method to track if the refresh actually improved ChatGPT visibility for those specific queries.
  - **Process Gap:** The prompt creation process needs standardization.
      - **Issue:** A method that worked for one client (Strapi) produced a bloated, unactionable list of 1,500 queries for others (Rapid, Galileo, AMP).
      - **Need:** A clear definition of "granular but not too granular" for prompt selection.

### Problem: Looker Reporting Bottlenecks

  - **Primary Bottleneck:** Manual data blending is required to combine GA4 and GSC data.
      - **Cause:** Incompatible data aggregation levels between the two platforms.
      - **Impact:** A time-consuming, manual process for MEs.
      - **Solution:** Awaits a company-wide data warehouse project.
  - **Secondary Bottleneck:** Ad-hoc client customization requests.
      - **Cause:** Clients have varied KPIs, leading to complex, one-off dashboard builds.
      - **Impact:** Each request can take a week to process, delaying other work.

### Proposed Solution: Centralize & Standardize

  - **Talal's Role:** A horizontal role focused on standardizing and leveling up processes across the company.
  - **Initial Focus:** Improve foundational SEO workflows (content audits, keyword research, clustering) before tackling advanced AEO.
  - **Proposed Solution for AEO:**
      - Build a central database of all internal LLM/AEO experiments.
      - Systematically test promising workflows across multiple clients.
      - Talal will own these experiments, with MEs providing support.
  - **Proposed Solution for Reporting:**
      - Front-load dashboard creation into a new 2-week pre-sprint process.
      - **Rationale:** This captures all client KPI requirements upfront, enabling a specialized resource to build the dashboard before the main engagement starts.
