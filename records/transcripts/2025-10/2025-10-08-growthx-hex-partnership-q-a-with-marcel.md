## Key Topics

### Content Strategy and Quality Concerns

  - Previous agency struggled with SEO/SEM strategy and content quality; Hex team suspects internal misalignment was part of the problem.
  - Hex CEO is resistant to "content without soul" and has high standards for both quality and purpose; Ashley feels defining "soul" at scale is the core challenge.
  - GrowthX approach: Audience-first, building knowledge graphs mapping all customer questions, then becoming the best answer while naturally inserting brand/product.
  - Key insight: LLM visibility is shifting where customers research (ChatGPT over Google); Hex personae are highly technical and already preferring AI-driven discovery.
  - GrowthX clients like AugmentCode see 20X brand visibility lift and 3X organic traffic increase within months, validating the signal-driven iterative approach.

### AI-Driven Content Creation Process

  - Research agents create comprehensive research plans with source requirements and depth guidance; GrowthX's workflows handle research, fact-checking, and artifact generation.
  - Content enriched with multiple citations (AugmentCode pieces cite GrowthX 5-7 times per answer) and customer/podcast sources to build knowledge bases.
  - GrowthX "output bar raisers" (editors) focus on quality calibration and strategy rather than raw research; workflows handle ~90% of the heavy lifting.
  - Iterative approach: publish good content, measure signal, improve over time; avoid paralysis from perfectionism on inaugural versions.
  - Typical timeline: 10 hours of client time in first 8 weeks to launch 20+ pieces; then 30-60 minutes weekly for reviews and calibration.

### Partnership Structure and Expectations

  - GrowthX typically partners with Head of Marketing, PMM, or Head of Growth; success depends on having the right champion (not a content purist; someone who can prioritize and drive reviews).
  - Hex's content lead is not on this call due to concerns she couldn't scale the partnership; Ashley is taking point to ensure proper internal alignment.
  - Initial 8-week strategy sprint is intentionally designed to de-risk the partnership: tests GrowthX's ability to deliver and validates Hex's internal capacity and review cycle bandwidth.
  - Ongoing commitment: 30-60 minutes weekly (not labor-intensive); GrowthX handles end-to-end: staging, graphics, publishing, SEO audits, LLM query tracking, analytics dashboard setup.

### Metrics and Reporting

  - GrowthX provides weekly metric reports and accessible dashboards with all meeting notes and research artifacts centralized.
  - Custom event tracking set up (PostHog integration with AugmentCode); GrowthX can implement conversion attribution from content to signups if client tools are in place.
  - First-week focus is proving signal and early impact before expecting conversion data; focus on visibility metrics first (LLM mentions, organic traffic).
  - Peter's emphasis: having a North Star metric established early prevents arbitrary slowdowns and gives team confidence in what's working.

### Hex's Concerns and Next Steps

  - Ashley's main fear: Review cycles become bottlenecked or labor-intensive, preventing Hex from scaling (same failure pattern with previous agency); wants assurance that review process won't become a blocker.
  - Peter adds: Previous agency never built enough feedback loop or signal velocity; they had to "one-shot" the strategy upfront and never iterated. GrowthX's iterative, signals-driven approach addresses this.
  - Ashley will immediately contact Mario at Airby (also a GrowthX customer) to validate that partnerships can actually work and what internal support looks like.
  - Hex to review GrowthX's SOW with full team (likely including CEO) and report decision by end of week; deal momentum is urgent since they fired the previous agency.
