### Internal Linking Optimization

  - **Problem:** Internal linking is a major time sink (\~45 mins/article) because Atlas's suggestions are inaccurate, requiring manual verification.
  - **Solution:** Use Google's `site:domain.com "phrase"` operator.
      - **Process:**
        1.  Identify a potential anchor phrase in the article.
        2.  Search Google using the operator to find relevant pages on the site.
        3.  Link directly to the best result.
      - **Result:** This method is faster (\<15 mins) and more accurate, eliminating the need for corrections.

### Finding Content Refresh Opportunities

  - **Goal:** Identify opportunities to improve existing content, including landing pages.
  - **Method 1: SEMrush Organic Research**
      - **Process:**
        1.  Enter the domain (e.g., `smith.ai`).
        2.  Apply advanced filters:
              - Exclude branded keywords.
              - Filter by keyword (e.g., "virtual receptionist").
              - Filter by position (\#4–15) to find pages on the cusp of page one.
        3.  Analyze search intent for each keyword to determine if it's a good fit for the page.
  - **Method 2: Google Site Search**
      - **Process:**
        1.  Use `site:domain.com "phrase"` to find all pages containing a target phrase.
        2.  Filter by URL folder (e.g., `site:smith.ai/blog "virtual receptionist"`) to target specific content types.
        3.  Paste the resulting URLs into SEMrush to analyze their keywords and positions.
