## Summary

Jason and Gloria met with Dan Ahmadi (Upside) to debrief on Upside's successful "Future of Marketing SF" event—a $50K, 110-person B2B CMO mixer held at The Battery penthouse. Dan shared a detailed playbook covering event structure (lightning talks + 1-hour CMO panel + organic networking dinner), venue selection, attendee curation (130 registered, high selectivity for target market), budget breakdown ($30K venue/food, $10K AV, $2K miscellaneous), and post-event engagement tactics (on-site photography, shared photo drive, LinkedIn encouragement). Jason and Gloria are planning a smaller, similar event with Webflow focused on AEO/GEO topics, and took away actionable recommendations on timing, speaker selection, attendee management strategy, and logistics.

---

## Context

Dan Ahmadi is VP of Marketing at Upside, a venture-backed B2B SaaS company in the marketing platform space. Upside recently executed a high-profile marketing event in partnership with their investor XYZ (a VC firm), along with co-sponsors Graphite and Sequel SQL. The event was designed to build brand awareness among B2B CMOs—a key audience for Upside's product. Jason Gong and Gloria Willis (GrowthX's new events lead) approached Dan to learn from Upside's execution, since GrowthX is planning its own B2B event focused on AI-enabled content marketing topics like AEO and GEO, likely in partnership with Webflow.

- Format: Lightning talks (5 min each, 20 min total for non-pitchy tech presentations) + 1-hour CMO panel discussion + organic networking dinner with multiple food stations
- Main draws: Five high-profile CMO panelists + exclusive venue location; both were critical to event selling out quickly with a waitlist
- Success factors: Selective attendee curation (rejected 50% of signups for target market fit), heavy pre-event communication confirming attendance, weekday evening timing that aligns with executives' schedules
- Post-event engagement: On-site photographer, shared Google Drive with all photos distributed immediately post-event, encouraged LinkedIn posts (10+ attendees posted); led to extended reach but some confusion about sponsor attribution in social posts

## Key Topics

### Event Structure and Timing

- 4:30 PM doors open, 5:45 PM start time (15-minute buffer encouraged early arrivals)
- 20-minute lightning talks from portfolio companies, followed by 1-hour panel discussion
- Dinner and networking until 10 PM close; 10-15 people stayed until close, indicating deep engagement
- Weekday evening (Tuesday-Thursday, 4-7 PM) ideal for busy B2B executives; easier to "duck out" from end-of-day commitments than morning events
- Total content time: ~1.5 hours, allowing 2+ hours for organic networking and informal conversations

### Venue and Logistics

- Held at The Battery penthouse (exclusive, hard-to-access SF location); venue itself was a major draw and easy promotional hook
- Theater-style seating for talks/panel (packed, with ~5% standing room acceptable), later rearranged for networking
- Multiple food stations (kebab, pizza, pasta) instead of sit-down dinner; allows attendees to grab food and move freely around seating areas with fireplaces
- AV setup: 3-4 video camera operators, 1 livestream operator, confidence monitor for panelists, music piped through venue
- Cost breakdown: Venue/food ($30K+), AV ($10K+), badges and swag (~$2K); XYZ VC handled most logistics coordination

### Attendee Management

- 130 registered, 110 attended (85% conversion rate; higher than typical event attendance)
- Invite-only, free event; limited public LinkedIn promotion but primarily word-of-mouth and curated invitations
- Used Luma for registration; set artificially low initial cap to quickly create a waitlist, which improved perceived exclusivity
- Careful curation strategy: Initial acceptance to seed attendance, then increasingly selective based on title/level/market fit; last 20 seats required target-market alignment across all 4 sponsors
- Frequent pre-event communication: Thank you + registration confirmation → 1-week-before check-in (reconfirm attendance, mention waitlist pressure) → final details and directions
- Attendee list on Luma hidden until final week, then opened to confirmed attendees only
- Dan manually reviewed unknown signups to approve/deflect to livestream option

### Content and Speakers

- 5 CMO panelists moderated by 1 moderator (typically the format is 3 CMOs + 1 VP Marketing + 1 finance/rev ops, but this event ended up with 5 CMOs after everyone said yes to invitations)
- Lightning talks: 5-minute presentations from XYZ portfolio companies, required to be informative/educational rather than pitchy, showcasing cool new technology
- Panel discussion: 1-hour moderated discussion on relevant industry topics; high-profile speakers were the main draw and accounted for rapid sellout
- Planned roundtables with moderators and assigned topics were prepared but scrapped last-minute; attendees were so engaged in organic conversations during dinner that facilitating structured roundtables felt forced
- Moderators for scrapped roundtables had to be managed diplomatically (they'd prepared to present)

### Promotion and Engagement

- Easy to promote: Dan described it as "the easiest event I've ever promoted" compared to his years doing DemandGen events; filled up fast with a waitlist; credits this to either the high-profile CMO speakers or The Battery venue (or both)
- On-site photographer captured event; photos uploaded to shared Google Drive and distributed immediately post-event
- Strategy encouraged LinkedIn engagement: Shared photo links with attendee names/LinkedIn profiles so people could tag and connect with each other
- Result: 10+ attendees posted about the event on LinkedIn, extending reach organically
- Lesson: Sponsor attribution was weak in social posts; only 2 of 4 sponsors were typically tagged; could improve with clearer pre-event messaging about co-sponsors

### Sponsorship and Costs

- Total cost: $50,000 ($40K venue/AV/logistics + $10K food; some sources mention $30K+ venue/food, $10K+ AV)
- Primary sponsors: Upside ($10K), Graphite ($10K), XYZ venture capital ($30K); XYZ handled most operational/logistics work
- Additional sponsorship: Sequel SQL contributed $5K
- Cost per attendee: $450 all-in ($50K ÷ 110 attendees)
- Biggest expense drivers: Venue rental (Battery penthouse not cheap), catering for 110+ people, and professional AV setup (3-4 camera ops, livestream, confidence monitor, sound)

---

## Action Items

- Consider using an exclusive venue like The Battery for the Webflow event
- Plan for a weekday evening (Tuesday-Thursday, 4-7 PM) timeframe
- Curate a high-profile speaker lineup to drive registrations
- Implement strategic attendee management (waitlist, careful curation)
- Arrange on-site photography and encourage post-event social sharing
- Explore Lyft vouchers as a transportation option for attendees
