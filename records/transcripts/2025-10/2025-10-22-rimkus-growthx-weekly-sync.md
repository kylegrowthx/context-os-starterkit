## Key Topics

### Content Review Process

  - **Problem:** Rimkus missed review notifications for 4 articles because the automated status update was triggered by simply opening the Google Doc.
  - **Solution:** Rimkus will now add a "done reviewing" comment in each Google Doc to confirm completion. Aida will reset the status of the 4 missed articles to re-trigger notifications.

### Content Categorization

  - **Decision:** New content will be published as "Articles" (e.g., `rimkus.com/articles`), not "Blog Posts."
  - **Rationale:** This aligns with Rimkus's existing structure for technical resources and avoids potential SEO issues from using a new subfolder.

      - **Significance:** Fast indexing is a strong positive signal, indicating Google perceives the content as high-quality and relevant.
  - **Performance Tracking:**
      - Impressions and clicks should appear in the Looker dashboard next week.
      - GrowthX is resolving an AWS-related error preventing Google Search Console data from loading into the dashboard.

### Content Pipeline & Quality

  - **Review Batch \#2:** 5 articles are with Rimkus for review.
  - **Review Batch \#3:** 5 articles are with GrowthX's editor and will be sent to Rimkus by tomorrow.
  - **Writing Guidelines:** Updated based on Rimkus feedback (e.g., no second-person address). Rimkus should continue to flag any issues for continuous improvement.

### Image Strategy

  - **AI-Generated Images:** GrowthX is exploring AI-generated images to create unique visuals.
      - **Initial Challenge:** AI-generated humans looked "Play-Doh-like."
      - **New Direction:** Focus on abstract or distant architectural/construction imagery, which is easier for AI to generate realistically and aligns with Rimkus's preference to avoid human photos.
  - **Rimkus-Provided Images:** Rimkus can provide high-impact, real-world photos for specific articles (e.g., NYC facade inspections), but the internal approval process is slow.

### Existing Content Audit & Pruning

  - **Audit Status:** The audit of all existing articles is due next week.
  - **Pruning Strategy:** GrowthX will recommend removing outdated sales enablement content (e.g., regional campaigns) that is factually incorrect and has no SEO value.
      - **Rationale:** This improves overall site quality signals for search engines, making it easier for new, valuable content to rank.

  - **Proposal:** Aida will provide a Notion document by Friday with:
      - A summary of Phase 1 results.
      - A proposal for Phase 2, focusing on pruning and refreshing existing content.
