  - **Tavrn's Plan:**
      - Bring Google-related marketing in-house, which will not affect the GrowthX content partnership.
      - Accelerate content reviews to maintain publishing momentum.

### Publishing Cadence & Review Process

  - **Goal:** Maintain a consistent publishing cadence to send positive signals to Google and improve rankings.
  - **New Review Workflow:**
      - **Case-by-case auto-publish:** For non-sensitive content (e.g., case studies), articles will auto-publish on Friday if no feedback is received by then.

  - **Process Improvement:** Matthew will work to deliver articles earlier in the week (e.g., Friday before) to give Kellie more review time.

  - **RJLP Case Study:** Removed at Tavrn's request.
      - **Reason:** The featured eDiscovery product is one of Tavrn's least mature.
      - **Action:** Matthew will remove the case study section from the blog post, which can then be published.
  - **Product Information:** The initiative to gather robust product stats is paused.
      - **Reason:** Tavrn's product team is at capacity, and current ad copy lacks the integrity for public use.
      - **Action:** GrowthX can use any available stats for hooks, but a full data analysis project is needed before a robust library can be built.
  - **Content Diversification:** The strategy will shift from a sole focus on "Retrieval."
      - **New Focus:** Incorporate "Demands" and "Chronologies" content.
      - **Rationale:**
          - Capture higher search volume (e.g., "chronologies" has more queries than "retrieval").
          - Improve internal linking structure.
