## Key Topics

### GTM Model & Process Refinement

  - **Problem:** Outreach campaigns have messaged known contacts and competitors (e.g., AirOps), risking the partnership.
  - **Cause:** Unclear contact ownership and a lack of a clean blocklist.
  - **Solution:** A new GTM model with clear responsibilities:
      - **GrowthX:** Owns "white glove" relationships (customers, funnel contacts, engaged event attendees).
      - **Understory:** Owns cold outreach to a clean, segmented list.
  - **Process Change:** All campaigns must be documented in Notion for pre-approval to ensure alignment.

### Outreach Performance & Strategy

  - **Webflow Webinar Campaign Results:**
      - **LinkedIn (HeyReach):** >20% reply rate (41 replies from 184 messages sent). Mark noted 50–60% of these are interested replies, with the remainder being inquiries.
      - **Cold Email (Instantly):** 1 reply per 262 leads (12 interested replies from 3,000 newly contacted people out of 6,000 emails sent). Performance is within KPI for mass email but underperforms typical Understory benchmarks of 1 in 400–500.
  - **Strategic Pivot:**
      - **LinkedIn:** Continue for event registrations; add more sender accounts to increase volume. Target is 1 positive reply for every ~46 people reached.
      - **Cold Email:** Pause for high-ACV agency sales (GrowthX is booked through EOY and can't accept more business at current capacity).
          - **Future Use:** Test the motion for a new, lower-ACV product launching in ~2 weeks, where cold email and outreach make more sense operationally.

### New Outreach Playbook

  - **Goal:** Proactively mine competitor activity for leads.
  - **Tactics:**
      - Scrape competitor LinkedIn followers (e.g., Profound, Airhouse).
      - Scrape engagement from competitor LinkedIn posts.
      - Mine attendee lists from competitor events.
  - **Copy:** Understory will create a tone guide based on GrowthX's revised copy to enable independent campaign execution.
