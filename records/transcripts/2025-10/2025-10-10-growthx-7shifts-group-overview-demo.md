## Summary

Justin Holmes, newly hired VP of Content at 7Shifts, explored potential collaboration with GrowthX to address the company's lack of AI-driven content creation. 7Shifts has a 4-person content team and a $50M ARR restaurant software business transitioning from high-growth to profitable growth, but the internal team lacks AI capabilities and they're terminating an underperforming $7.5k/month SEO agency. Tyler Pavlas pitched GrowthX's combined strategy-plus-execution model ($15k/month), but Justin preferred an 8-week strategy sprint to train the internal team — a deal structure GrowthX doesn't typically offer. The team agreed that GrowthX's workshops could serve as an interim learning resource, and Justin committed to reassessing team restructure decisions by end of October, at which point a full engagement might make financial sense if budget flexibility opens up.

## Context

7Shifts is a point-solution software for restaurant management, serving low-ACV customers and operating at $50M ARR. The company transitioned from aggressive growth (previously targeting 60%+ YoY growth) to profitable steady growth. Justin Holmes recently joined as VP of Content, taking over from Libby (who is leaving end of October). He inherited a 4-person content team that has not adopted any AI workflows, despite market momentum. The company's current SEO partner is contracted through March 2026 but underperforming. 7Shifts recently completed an ICP refresh, which Justin sees as groundwork that would reduce onboarding time for a content strategy partner. The CEO is aware of GrowthX, having learned about the company through a Homebase reference and directed the team to explore them; GrowthX previously met with Libby in February.
