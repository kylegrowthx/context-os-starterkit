## Summary

GrowthX and Surge AI's leadership discussed how to reposition Surge's brand to align with customer expectations and competitive dynamics in AI. Surge is currently perceived as a "cagey underdog" with high-quality output but potentially difficult to work with — a mature, selective professor rather than a hungry, obsessed startup. The team explored four positioning pillars (data refinement and quality, trust and partnership, research collaboration, and continuous improvement) and considered repositioning Surge as a "post-training research lab" rather than a data vendor, while developing concrete language to communicate their end-to-end quality control processes without revealing sensitive proprietary methods.

---

## Context

Surge HQ is a data and quality company serving AI labs (their primary customers). GrowthX has been brought in as a strategic consultant to help Surge refine its brand positioning and narrative. This workshop brought together Surge's leadership (Edwin Chen, Scott Heiner, Nick) and GrowthX's Marcel Santilli and Renato Valdes Olmos to analyze how Surge is currently perceived by customers and prospective clients, and to develop positioning that better communicates Surge's competitive advantages.

The core tension: Surge has built genuinely high-quality technology and processes, but customers often perceive them as secretive, selective, and somewhat arrogant — which damages larger deals. The team needs to articulate what makes Surge special (their obsession with quality, their research partnerships, their measurement rigor) while maintaining operational confidentiality around proprietary methods.

## Key Topics

### Current Brand Perception

  - Viewed as "cagey underdog" with high-quality output but potentially difficult to work with
  - Some customers perceive Surge as looking down on them or being overly selective
  - Compared to a "mature-ish professor" rather than a "scrappy, hungry" startup
  - Lack of institutional presence may hinder larger deals ($100M+)

### Customer Priorities and Competition

  - Customers (AI labs) want to be perceived as "obsessed," fast-moving, and aggressive about scaling
  - OpenAI and Anthropic seen as current market leaders
  - LMSys leaderboard and Twitter sentiment are key, though imperfect, measures of success
  - Product experience (UI, speed, formatting) significantly impacts preference, even over model quality

### Surge's Unique Value Proposition

  - High-quality data and technology, but struggling to articulate specifics without revealing trade secrets
  - Strong collaborative relationships with researchers, often preferred over internal teams
  - Robust internal quality control processes (e.g., review thresholds, AI usage detection)
  - Challenge in balancing transparency about processes with maintaining operational flexibility

### Potential Positioning Pillars

  - Data refinement and quality (technology, operations, workforce)
  - Trust and partnership with customers
  - Research collaboration and expertise
  - Measurement and continuous improvement (signals, feedback loop)

### Future Vision and Aspirations

  - Desire to see AI unlock new capabilities (e.g., long-form creative writing, running $100M companies)
  - Potential to position Surge as leaders in agent-based AI data and quality measurement
