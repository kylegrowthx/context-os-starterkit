## Key Topics

### RoomBlocks Context and Goals

- Separate product from Engine.com targeting B2C group travel (weddings, sports teams, church groups) where consumer mindset and content expectations differ sharply from business travel
- Engine maintains partnerships with hotels offering negotiated group rates; RoomBlocks enables them to serve both public and business group travel without confusing consumers with business-focused messaging
- Currently 100% paid traffic; goal is to build organic search engine as primary growth lever
- Strong conversion rates on existing group landing pages suggest high ROI potential for organic traffic
- Need to navigate hotel partnership constraints: cannot build pages around specific hotel brands (e.g., "Embassy Suites in Charleston") but can target category-level searches (e.g., "hotel room blocks in Charleston")

### Content and Experience Strategy

- Start with wedding-focused content hub, scalable to other verticals (sports teams, corporate retreats, church groups)
- Blend of editorial content (topical hubs, best practices, planning guides) and programmatic content (comparisons, hotel listings, calculators, checklists)
- Custom recommendation engine that lets users describe their event and receive tailored suggestions for venue types, planning considerations, budget implications
- Leverage Engine's proprietary data: hotels with meeting spaces, pricing benchmarks, capacity options
- Page structure: marketing-owned editorial and hub pages as entry points, then handoff to product team's booking experience
- New domain: "RoomBlocks by Engine" or "powered by Engine" to signal separation from Engine.com

### Approach to New Domains and Content Scaling

- Plant seeds early with editorial content before full launch; GrowthX cited examples of new domains (Sotero, DataGrid) that saw strong growth by starting editorial early
- Watch for quality signals before aggressive scaling; GrowthX uses signals-first approach with monitoring and iteration vs. bulk volume
- Avoid pitfall of launching 1,000 pages on a brand new domain at once (raises Google's suspicion); layer in programmatic content after editorial foundation proves signals
- GrowthX can build initial MVP experience end-to-end (brand guidelines, homepage, content hubs) to prove concept in 2-4 weeks, then hand off to Engine team

### GrowthX Case Studies Referenced

- **Ramp vendor catalog:** Programmatic vendor pages (Airtable, Smartsheet, etc.) with depth via categories, comparisons, alternatives; editorial + custom workflows pulling proprietary data
- **Steadily landlord hub:** Topical hub model applicable to wedding planning, event planning, sports teams
- **Town platform:** High-quality, factual content from curated sources (IRS docs for taxes); signals-first approach applicable to event planning verticals
- **Interwell expansion:** Similar hub model (ketamine-assisted therapy) with editorial foundation before layering programmatic
- **The Adventures Group:** Travel site with city-specific content hubs (Cancun, Cabo, etc.); slice-and-dice approach around experiences, seasons, demographics

### Strategy Sprint Proposal

- 4-8 week engagement to scope, prove, and build initial content approach
- Hybrid editorial + programmatic model with focus on quality signals early, then controlled scaling of volume
- Output: scoped content roadmap, MVP experience, and proof of concept to build internal Engine buy-in for continued investment
- GrowthX can accelerate zero-to-one; not positioning as long-term dev/design partner
