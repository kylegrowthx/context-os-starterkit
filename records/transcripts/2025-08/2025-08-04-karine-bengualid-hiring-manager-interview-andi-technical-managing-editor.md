## Context

Andi Bailey leads customer operations at GrowthX and is hiring for a Technical Managing Editor who will work within GrowthX's pod-based delivery system. Each pod comprises three roles: Content Manager (runs daily workflows), Technical Managing Editor (editorial oversight and technical coordination), and Director/Strategic Account Manager (long-term client strategy). The Technical Managing Editor is responsible for both project management with clients and technical feedback to the engineering team on workflow improvements. Since GrowthX's proprietary AI platform is only seven weeks old and still being refined, the role requires someone who can identify process bottlenecks, express specific improvement needs, and work with engineering to iterate quickly.

Karine Bengualid is a freelance content strategist and marketer based in Moncton, New Brunswick. She started as a marketing generalist, then specialized in B2B SaaS and e-commerce content marketing. She has managed between one and seven clients simultaneously, wearing multiple hats including strategy, editing, writing, and project management. Her experience includes working with diverse PM tools (Airtable, ClickUp, Monday, Trello, Asana) and AI tools (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, DeepSeek, Unbounce Smart Copy), though she emphasizes using AI as a research aid and brainstorming partner rather than relying on direct output.

## Key Topics

### The Technical Managing Editor Role

- Dual responsibility: project management/client coordination AND technical coordination with engineering
- Works within a pod: Content Manager (daily workflow execution), Technical Managing Editor, and Director/Strategic Account Manager (long-term strategy)
- Scale: managing 3-5 clients generating 50+ content pieces weekly through AI workflows
- Key responsibilities: editorial oversight, identifying workflow gaps, requesting platform improvements via Linear tickets, ensuring high-quality briefs feed into workflows
- Context is critical; GrowthX's platform design uses "human-in-the-loop" processes where improving artifact quality upstream can solve downstream output problems
- AI-assisted work should complete in under one hour given efficient workflows

### Karine's Content Marketing Background

- Started as marketing generalist, specialized in B2B SaaS and e-commerce content after moving west
- Freelanced across full spectrum: strategy, writing, editing, content management, client coordination
- Managed 1-7 clients simultaneously, using disciplined project management (Airtable, ClickUp, Monday, Trello, Asana) to track deadlines, prioritize by urgency, and maintain visibility
- Built marketing strategy into her content work; previous manager-level experience in marketing informs content decisions

### AI Integration: Research-First, Synthesis-Focused

- Uses AI for ideation and brainstorming: headlines, subheads, topics, keyword angles
- Method: research independently first, then prompt AI with research findings and keywords to get multiple angle suggestions
- Critical distinction: never uses direct AI output; instead synthesizes ideas across multiple AI suggestions and combines with own research
- Applies same approach to tool selection: ChatGPT for brainstorming, Claude, Gemini, DeepSeek for variety, Unbounce Smart Copy as needed
- Also uses ClearScope, Ahrefs, SimilarWeb, Hotjar, Grammarly, Hemingway, Google Analytics for research and optimization

### Case Study: Talkspace B2B Blog Launch

- Talkspace is an online mental health service provider; wanted to launch B2B blog targeting HR teams (distinct from existing B2C blog)
- Karine's role: coordinate across internal teams (B2B marketing manager, account managers, design group, counselor SMEs) and develop go-to-market strategy
- Process: conducted multiple meetings with stakeholders to identify pain points and content needs, then synthesized into content strategy with specific article types and positioning
- Pitched strategy to stakeholders for approval, then executed content creation (independent research + SME interviews to gather information)
- Result: blog generated ~$70,000 per month in traction; sales collateral performed well, helping account teams
- Demonstrates ability to manage complex stakeholder coordination and translate business needs into content strategy
