## Key Topics

### Budget Constraints and Negotiation

Nick opened with $10K/month as Relay's maximum available budget, citing Series B cash constraints. He's juggling four budget buckets: full-time employees, contractors, media spend, and software. Tyler held firm on $12K/month as GrowthX's minimum ongoing rate, noting it's the floor across all their recent contracts. Rather than compromise on service scope, Tyler offered a structural win: an 8-week, $20K fixed-fee strategy sprint lets Relay evaluate whether GrowthX's content approach works before committing to the higher ongoing rate. Nick was skeptical but open—ultimately agreeing to escalate to his CFO in Boston for the additional $2K/month approval.

### Timeline and Start Date

The original plan was a September 22 kickoff. Nick, eager to get started, asked if GrowthX could accelerate to September 2 (right after Labor Day). Tyler confirmed there's one open slot on the calendar, contingent on Relay signing quickly—GrowthX is in a race against another prospect who was supposed to sign by end of day August 22. If Relay signs before that other prospect, they get the September 2 slot; otherwise, the September 22 date stands.

### Success Criteria and Expectations

Tyler articulated GrowthX's definition of success: collaborative artifact creation, publishing content by the midway point of the strategy sprint (week 4-5), operating with a fast feedback loop via Slack, and access to Relay's experts and internal documentation. These aren't just nice-to-haves—they're the gate to Phase 2. If Relay can't or won't collaborate effectively, or if there's no published content by mid-sprint, GrowthX would be "very wary" of extending beyond the strategy sprint. Nick appreciated the candor and acknowledged the alignment: he also needs to see results (rankings, published content) before justifying the higher spend to his CFO.

### Contract Structure and Pricing

The deal breaks down as follows: $20K fixed for the 8-week strategy sprint (effectively $10K/month at the 2-month mark), then $12K/month for Phase 2 (ongoing growth execution). This was originally quoted at $15K/month, so GrowthX is giving a $3K/month concession on the ongoing phase—betting that strong execution during the sprint will justify the renewal. Tyler committed to updating the contract to reflect the $12K/month ongoing rate before end of meeting.
