## Summary

GrowthX and Webstacks realigned on their AI content strategy, pivoting from a 60-70 article plan to a leaner 24-piece structure with 6 core pillars and 18 sub-pillars. The team compressed the timeline from year-end to end of September/early October by focusing on higher-level scaffolding pieces first, with the flexibility to add deeper how-to content later as specific topics prove popular. Jesse is drafting a thought leadership piece on strategy importance in web projects to provide context-setting across all sections.

## Context

Webstacks is a web design and development agency building tools and content focused on AI-enhanced web projects. GrowthX is managing their content marketing strategy and execution. This sync is part of Webstacks' regular weekly cadence with their GrowthX delivery team. The group is refining their approach mid-project after recognizing that the original plan to publish 60-70 articles through year-end was overly ambitious and would lock them into outdated playbooks in a rapidly evolving AI landscape.

The goal is to create evergreen, flexible content that establishes foundational thinking and decision-making frameworks rather than specific tooling or how-to playbooks. Webstacks wants to leverage their internal experience—client case studies, team recordings, and direct examples—to differentiate their content while GrowthX provides the structure, SEO strategy, and publishing cadence management.
