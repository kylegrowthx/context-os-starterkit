## Key Topics

### Happy Hour Event at Southern View

- **Venue choice:** Southern View selected for large screen, open layout, and ability to support interactive content
- **Interactive model comparisons:** Eval decks (benchmark comparisons of latest open-source and commercial AI models) displayed on screens; allows audience to see performance differences and discuss implications
- **Labeling party mechanics:** Contentious AI response examples printed or projected; attendees vote with stickers on preferred response; facilitators then call people up to advocate for their choice ("who wants to debate me?"). Purpose is to build intuition about subtle AI differences and generate organic conversation
- **Food:** Full dinner chosen to ensure attendees can eat well and stay longer; Nick's strong preference ("my pet peeve is pizza at after-work events")
- **Swag:** Prize wheel with varied prize tiers — high-value items (e.g., Steam Deck, $300-400 range at 1x frequency), medium items (AI tool credits, premium SF items like Dandelion Chocolates), low-value items (filler)
- **Structured conversation:** Combination of open networking + light structure (conversation starters, table topics submitted in advance) to accommodate "awkward nerds" and get vibes started without feeling forced

### Dinner Event at Quince (12 attendees)

- **Purpose:** High-touch relationship building with key industry champions who can become long-term advocates and diffuse Surge across their organizations when they move to new companies
- **Experience:** Brief welcome, conversation starters, possibly some labeling party content; emphasis on intimate conversation over structured programming
- **Vendor management issues from prior event:** Previous Quince dinner ran 4-5 hours (they promised 3), narrow room layout made cocktail hour awkward, staff interrupted conversations, some attendees escaped without getting swag bags. All need to be tightened this round.
- **Private room constraints:** Need space that accommodates cocktail hour + dining (not a long narrow table); bar setup helps fill space and prevent "dead" feeling if room is large
- **Staff protocol:** Brief them on waiting for genuine conversation pauses, not comedic pauses; no interruptions for "how's everything" check-ins
- **Check drop:** Never happens in front of clients; all payments handled behind the scenes

### Invitation Strategy and Audience

- **List composition:** Blending researchers and builders; asked GrowthX to identify "builder" personas from GrowthX network to balance Surge's researcher-heavy list
- **Name recognition for happy hour:** Some GrowthX-connected people (e.g., founder of Olama) invited to happy hour for credibility/anchor function
- **Email alias for outreach:** Invitations for people without pre-existing relationship with Surge team sent from Nick's email alias to improve open rates and response; coordinate with Sully on email forwarding logistics
- **Attribution and measurement:** GrowthX provides list of who attended; Surge tracks which attendees later appear in their funnel or become advocates who bring new business
