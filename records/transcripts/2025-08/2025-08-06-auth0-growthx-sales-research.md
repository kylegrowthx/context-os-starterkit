## Key Topics

### Current SDR Workflow

SDRs at Auth0 manage a split funnel: inbound leads (QLs from contact forms, gated content, webinars, trade shows) and outbound prospecting. Both are focused on enterprise accounts in the 1,000-5,000 employee range. Day-to-day, they review QL reports each morning, then split time between new business outbound and existing customer expansion (e.g., selling Auth0 retail product to companies that already use Okta). For outbound, they do account mapping, identify buying teams, and coordinate with Account Executives (AEs) who own territory and strategy. Research involves examining company 10Ks, social posts, LinkedIn profiles, and using internal tools (Gemini for synthesis, ZoomInfo for account filtering, Sixth Sense for intent signals). Tiffany noted that Sixth Sense is "funky" and unreliable as a signal source. The AE relationship is critical—SDRs lean on AEs to validate who to target and which accounts are worth deep research.

### Pain Points and Challenges

Manual research is by far the most time-consuming and frustrating part of the SDR role. While there is a wealth of available knowledge about accounts and industries, SDRs struggle to find and synthesize the right data quickly. Information overload is a constant problem: too many resources, too many tools, unclear which data is most relevant. Current tools don't reliably tell SDRs which accounts are worth focus or when to reach out. ZoomInfo can surface account "focus areas" (e.g., whether they're focused on identity, mobile, specific verticals), but it requires manual filtering. SDRs don't have a consolidated view of past interactions with prospects or existing customers—this context often lives in individual AE heads or scattered across Salesforce. Without better automation, each SDR spends hours doing research that similar teams have already done.

### GrowthX AI-Powered Solution

GrowthX had already built custom artifacts for Auth0's retail segment: a general retail persona document (fears, motivations, how to win trust), niched versions for four verticals (e-commerce, travel/hospitality, wholesale B2B, grocery/food/beverage), sales writing guidelines and value propositions, call scripts, and objection handling frameworks. They fed all of this into a "call brief" template to show what a single SDR-facing document could look like—using Jersey Mike's (a GrowthX customer) as a mock prospect. The V1 call brief included prospect background, current tech stack, pain points, "why now" rationale, discovery questions, value propositions, objection handling, and a call prep checklist. The intent was to show how a workflow could auto-generate this level of detail for any account an SDR targets or any inbound lead they receive.

### Feedback and Refinement Needs

Tiffany's core feedback was that the information is valuable but overwhelming. SDRs won't use a 20+ page guide; they need concise, context-specific documents. She suggested several refinements:

- **For cold calling outbound:** Create a short, focused guide with the key objections they'll face and how to handle them (1-2 pages max).
- **For email sequences:** Embed discovery questions and pain-point references directly into email templates so SDRs can customize without needing to read a separate discovery guide.
- **For target accounts:** The comprehensive brief works well for A/B accounts that are strategically important, but not for generic C-list prospecting.
- **Integration with Salesforce:** If an account was contacted 6 months ago and there's a record, pull that conversation history into the brief so SDRs know what was already discussed.
- **Customer success stories and competitor intelligence:** Tie in Auth0's existing customers in the same vertical (e.g., "Here's how Starbucks uses Auth0 for X") and competitor positioning to show SDRs what a strong value prop sounds like.
- **Stage criteria alignment:** Auth0 has stage 1 and 2 goals for SDRs (likely qualification and meeting setting); the content should be packaged to match those specific goals, not generic discovery.

Next steps include Tiffany connecting GrowthX with one or two experienced Auth0 SDRs for a deeper tactical discussion, plus her report back from the Auth0 offsite on H2 priorities for the team.
