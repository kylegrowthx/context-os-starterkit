## Key Topics

### Content Strategy and Workflow

- GrowthX created seed list of authoritative sources (50% journalistic/niche publications, 50% institutional resources) to guide internal linking
- Atlas tool houses three canonical artifacts: company context (understanding core customers, competencies, jobs to be done), buyer personas, and writing guidelines
- Writing guidelines are updated weekly by analyzing all client feedback—changes are tracked with diffs to show how client comments drive improvements
- Custom pipelines created for different content types: standard article pipeline (outline → research → draft → fact-check → internal linking → SEO meta), and specialized pipelines can be built for projects like white papers
- Full-draft approach (versus outline-first) targets reducing review cycles by up to 75%; typical clients leave 6 comments per full draft versus Metronome's 30-40 per outline
- Looker dashboard cohorts traffic and metrics by content pillar (glossaries, white papers, monetization strategy content) with separate views for GrowthX content versus overall site performance

### Pricing Index Project

- Now top priority for Metronome, shifting bandwidth from ongoing thought leadership work
- Still in scoping phase with Marcel; requires design, engineering, and content work—not just content
- Team plans 5-10 website pilot before scaling; goals and resource allocation to be discussed with Chris
- Glossary work will continue, but other editorial content buckets (monetization strategy, enterprise architecture, build-vs-buy framework) deprioritized until pricing index is scoped

### GEO (Google-Extended Optimization) Tactics

- Reddit identified as #1 referrer across all LLMs—significantly outperforming individual websites
- Matthew shared experiment with another client (architecture firm): generated questions/answers based on authoritative sources and seeded Reddit with content matching community tone; ranked LLM results appeared within 24 hours
- GrowthX proposed similar Reddit content generation for Metronome, leveraging the seed source list and writing guidelines to maintain authentic community voice
- Profound.ai GEO tools can break down to article-level granularity for competitor benchmarking, suggesting GrowthX should explore similar capabilities or tools

### Content Review Process

- Current process: outline review (many comments—30-40 per piece), then article draft review
- Matthew benchmarked against two other clients: typical full-draft reviews receive ~6 comments versus outline-first cycles requiring multiple rounds
- Proposed change: deliver full drafts in next cluster to test if tightened pipeline reduces Metronome's review burden; pipeline improvements include fact-checking and internal linking steps
- Goal: systematic reduction in review time while maintaining quality, with improvements tracked back to client feedback in writing guidelines

### Future Content Direction

- Editorial content strategy document outlines three optional content buckets: (1) monetization strategy/AI era business models, (2) enterprise architecture/scale topics, (3) build-vs-buy decision framework
- Glossary content will continue regardless of bucket chosen; other clusters deprioritized pending pricing index scope and Metronome's bandwidth
- Metronome marketing team (Chang, Maggie, Megan) will discuss priorities and next steps in tomorrow's marketing meeting; Megan will share the editorial strategy doc in Slack beforehand
- Matthew offered custom pipeline creation for any new major editorial projects (e.g., white papers), with client feedback incorporated into both writing guidelines and project-specific artifacts
