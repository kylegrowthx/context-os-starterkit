## Key Topics

### Content Production Challenges

- Currently publishing ~2-3 posts/week, falling short of the 5/week target. Only 20 posts published since mid-July (7 weeks = ~2.8/week actual).
- Root cause this week: Internal and external linking failures — team was creating links to non-existent pages, which slowed production while debugging.
- Post length considerations: Ehtisham defended long-form posts (5,000+ words with 30+ headings) as beneficial for organic search traffic, though not suitable for newsletter/social distribution. Keeping mix of 1500-2000 word posts to test what data reveals.
- Publishing strategy: Team has been backdating posts (publishing 3 in one day but dating them across consecutive dates) to maintain appearance of 1/day cadence. This is creating confusion on actual publication velocity.
- Buffer strategy: Agreed to build 6-7 post buffer (to eventually 8-10) so that feedback delays and production hiccups don't derail the 5/week commitment.

### Blog Performance and Organic Traffic

- Traffic analysis window: July 30 to August 26 (organic sources only, via GA4).
- Top performers: FIIR PTA compliance guide (26 organic views), startup tax credits savings guide (21 organic views). Both are foundational, searchable topics for Town's core audience.
- Breadth of traffic: All published articles are now generating at least some organic views and clicks — indicates good keyword coverage and search visibility.
- Growth trajectory: Expect organic traffic to accelerate as the seed announcement traffic (paid/PR spike) subsides and organic search momentum builds. Early signals are positive.

### Operational Improvements and Process Changes

- Communication transparency: Pier emphasized importance of proactive communication when blockers arise (e.g., "We hit an issue, need 48 hours to fix the pipeline"). GrowthX team committed to reporting delays early rather than letting schedules slip silently.
- Link strategy pivot: Kyle's directive — prioritize getting content into the queue first, add/fix links retroactively. Don't let linking issues block publishing. This removes a production bottleneck.
- LLM.txt integration: Kyle to follow up with Darrell on LLM.txt status and next steps. This is pending and not yet implemented.
- Shadow blog exploration: Pier mentioned potential "shadow blog" concept as an idea to evaluate — status unclear, no decision made yet.

### Future Marketing Strategy and Direct/Demand Planning

- Strategic horizon: Town is formulating hypotheses for 6-9 month direct and demand strategy, with an eye toward the coming tax season and an 18-month outlook.
- Approach: Pier framing it as testing hypotheses this year to inform 18-24 month roadmap. Distinguishing between big partner channels (higher certainty), as-needed partner channels (variable delivery), and direct channels (testing phase).
- Kyle's role: As a recently onboarded hire with Homebase background, Kyle brings expertise in scaling customer acquisition and content-driven growth. He's positioned to advise on execution and help connect dots between GrowthX's capabilities and Town's goals.
- Next session: Separate 45-minute meeting scheduled to go deeper. Pier to come prepared with 2 versions of the world (scenario planning) for discussion. Focus: ensure blog operations are solid before expanding GrowthX's involvement in broader strategy.
