### Workshop and Community Strategy Discussion

- Current state: workshops are rare, 2-hour format is "too long for learning," inconsistent frequency creates perception of scarcity (good) but also confusion ("do they do it regularly?")
- Sara's recommendation: tiered offerings
  - **Awareness tier (free, 30-45 min):** Quick intro, tease, drive to community
  - **Commitment tier (paid cohorts, ~1 hour):** Deeper dive, hands-on, "squeeze the brain," includes breaks
  - **Premium tier (half-day private):** Full immersion, programmed, repeatable multiple times/year
- Workshop sizing: Selective audience for premium (account-focused, champions vs. agencies), open for awareness
- Frequency and consistency matter as much as format—current "every 6 months" perception undermines demand

### Content Strategy and LinkedIn Positioning

- Pillar content: playbooks and AI prompt frameworks Jason shared in webinars (e.g., "how to structure documents for AI," example artifacts fed to models)
- Current problem: these valuable assets get buried in 2-hour workshops, then disappear
- Sara's suggestion: treat playbooks as reusable, standalone content library (potentially sellable as part of the community or as products)
- LinkedIn role: **Tease, seed, repurpose** content from community/playbooks, NOT create original content for LinkedIn
- Cadence: 2-3 times per week showing clips, snippets, "full access in community" calls-to-action
- Leverage Sara's audience: She can cross-promote to her newsletter/followers, bringing new people into the funnel

### Competitive Positioning and Flywheel

- GrowthX's moat: **They do the work they teach.** Content isn't theoretical—it's how they deliver for clients
- Flywheel: Community → awareness → demand gen signals → sales motion (RevOps picks up high-intent prospects, e.g., "VP of Marketing at [company] attended workshop, saw traffic decline" → outreach)
- GrowthX service entry point: $15k+/month, so audience-building isn't direct sales but long-term pipeline + credibility
- Newsletter + community + workshops create "one touch becomes a series" effect, keeping audience warm
