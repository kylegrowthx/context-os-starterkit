## Summary

Tyler Pavlas and Steve Krenzel negotiated the final terms of GrowthX's partnership with Logic, resolving a key tension around contract length and risk allocation. After intense discussion about Steve's concerns that a 12-month SOW was one-sided with no termination clause, Tyler secured Marcel's approval for a 6-month commitment at GrowthX's lowest tier of $9K/month. Both parties agreed to proceed, with an 8-week strategy sprint (including 7 weeks of publishing) followed by a growth execution phase, pending final legal review and signing on August 8th.

## Context

Logic is a seed-stage B2B SaaS company planning to raise Series A early next year. Steve Krenzel is evaluating GrowthX's content marketing services as a major investment in growth before that fundraising round. The previous discussion had revealed a sticking point: Logic's standard legal practice would never accept a 12-month SOW with no termination clause, which GrowthX typically requires. This call was Tyler's high-stakes negotiation to find middle ground without setting a precedent that would undermine GrowthX's model. Steve came prepared, referencing his experience at Brex and other ventures, and insisted on standard protections like termination-for-convenience clauses or early-exit penalties. The conversation captured a moment where GrowthX's rigid stance bent slightly to capture a deal with a founder they believed had real upside potential.
