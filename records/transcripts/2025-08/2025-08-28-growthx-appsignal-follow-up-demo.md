## Key Topics

### Strategy Sprint & Context Engineering

Tyler walked Mike through the 8-week strategy sprint approach that establishes company context, personas, brand voice, writing guidelines, and identifies high-opportunity topic clusters. The sprint outputs include detailed artifacts: elevator pitch, ideal customer profiles, verticals, personas, and industry-specific context. These are portable deliverables that remain AppSignal's property if the engagement ends. The entire approach is built on ingesting existing AppSignal documentation and recordings to calibrate the context.

### Content Creation Workflows & Orchestration

The platform orchestrates content through multiple validation stages: brief creation, deep research, article drafting, and fact-checking. Each step contains hundreds of sub-steps built in code. For example, the brief step fetches organic search results, analyzes competitor content, and uses that to generate a detailed brief. The research step generates research questions and validates answers against third-party sources. The fact-checking layer splits articles into passages, validates accuracy with confidence intervals, and flags passages needing rewrites with evidence for why. Editors approve or reject rewrites rather than blindly accepting AI output. By week 4-5 of a strategy sprint (like Airbyte), clients are already publishing 30+ articles.

### Flexible Client Involvement & Expert Integration

Clients control their involvement level—fully hands-on oversight or completely autonomous publishing, or anywhere in between. Tyler showed how GrowthX built custom workflows for Biologica (founded by former Allbirds CEO in the women's supplement space) where the chief medical officer's expertise was synthetically embedded in the workflow through interviews and artifact creation, eliminating manual review steps while maintaining oversight. This same approach can work for AppSignal's in-house programming language experts.

### LLM Visibility & AI Search Performance

GrowthX tracks how content shows up in LLM outputs (e.g., ChatGPT prompts). Tyler showed the Augment Code article, published July 29, which started appearing in ChatGPT within days and now appears as a top citation in AI search results for queries like "AI enterprise coding tools." This demonstrates GrowthX's focus on AEO (AI engine optimization) alongside traditional SEO. Customers see quick wins in both organic search and AI visibility.

### AppSignal's Current Economics & Build vs. Buy

AppSignal currently spends $4-6k/month on external subject-matter experts who write all blog content except product announcements. Mike acknowledged internal debate on whether to build a similar workflow in-house vs. buy from GrowthX, but emphasized limited internal capacity makes buying more attractive. The value exchange (quality + speed + expertise orchestration) nets out favorably vs. building, but the company needs to justify the decision to leadership and private equity ownership. AI is a major strategic initiative at the ownership level, which supports investment in content infrastructure.
