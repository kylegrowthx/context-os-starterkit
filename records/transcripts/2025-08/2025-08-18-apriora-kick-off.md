## Summary

GrowthX and Alex AI (formerly Apriora) aligned on a strategic content marketing engagement aimed at driving direct employer leads and building brand equity for a recently rebranded AI recruiting platform. Alex AI is shifting its customer mix from 90% staffing firms to 40-60% direct employers within 12 months, with an aggressive timeline: first content piece by week 3, daily publishing cadence by week 4. The team discussed Alex AI's product strengths (workflow automation, end-to-end solution, enterprise support), competitive positioning against HireVue and others, and a high-intent content strategy focusing on keywords like "AI recruiter" in both Google and ChatGPT organic results.

---

## Context

Alex AI is a Series A-stage recruiting automation startup that automates 80-90% of recruiter tasks (email outreach, texting, voicemails, scheduling, resume screening) for both staffing firms and direct employers. The company recently rebranded from Apriora to Alex AI, recognizing that the original name was difficult to pronounce and that customers were already calling the product "Alex." This first engagement call with GrowthX followed the brand transition and preceded a new website launch (launching within ~2 weeks with updated branding emphasizing natural, trustworthy, grounded aesthetics). Alex AI has 20 employees total, including 5 engineers and a small marketing team, and is primarily relying on outbound sales and events to drive leads. Only 1-2% of their SQLs currently come from inbound channels—a major gap given their product's market potential and the crowded competitive landscape (HireVue, Eightfold, Paradox, Bullhorn). GrowthX was brought in as their content marketing partner to address this gap and help Alex AI become a top search result for recruiter-focused keywords across Google and ChatGPT.

## Key Topics

### Company Overview and Product

  - Alex AI (formerly Apriora) offers an AI recruiting assistant
  - Product automates 80-90% of recruiter tasks (emailing, texting, voicemails, scheduling, resume screens)
  - Differentiators: workflow automation, end-to-end solution, strong enterprise support

### Target Audience and ICP

  - Staffing firms: 10+ recruiters, VP/Director of Operations, CEO, COO
  - Direct employers: Fortune 500/1000, CHROs, VPs of Talent, Directors of Talent Acquisition
  - Geographic focus: Primarily US, some presence in North America and Europe

### Current Marketing Efforts

  - Main channels: conferences, outbound (email, cold calling), word-of-mouth
  - Limited inbound marketing; \~1-2% of SQLs from inbound
  - Team: 20 people total, 5 engineers, 2 handling marketing, 1 interim SDR lead, 2 SDRs

### Competitive Landscape

  - Direct competitors: HireVue (one-way interviewing)
  - Adjacent competitors: Eightfold, Paradox
  - Incumbents offering similar features: Bullhorn Amplify, Bullhorn Sense

### Growth Goals and Challenges

  - Increase unqualified leads (MQLs) to build brand equity
  - Shift from 90-10 staffing firms vs. direct employers to 40-60 split
  - Become top search result for "AI recruiter" in both Google and ChatGPT
  - Increase inbound SQLs from \~1-2% to 50% of total leads

### Website and Branding

  - Recent rebrand from Apriora to Alex AI
  - New website launch in \~2 weeks with updated branding (more natural, physical elements)
  - Using Webflow CMS for website management

### Content Strategy

  - Focus on high-intent, easier-to-rank content initially
  - Build up to more comprehensive coverage as domain authority increases
  - Potential for programmatic plays (e.g., interview guides for specific companies)
