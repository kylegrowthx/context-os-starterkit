## Overview

- GrowthX AI is scoping a potential weekly newsletter focused on AI enablement for Udemy Business (UB)
- Udemy's lifecycle marketing is segmented by audience (B2B learners, admins, consumers, instructors)
- Email systems are fragmented (Braze, transactional, prospects) with strict sending limits (1/day, 3/week max)
- Content strategy needs to balance gated/ungated experiences and avoid duplicating existing efforts

---

## Key Topics

### GrowthX AI's Proposed Newsletter Project

  - Weekly cadence focused on AI enablement, thought leadership, education
  - Aims to drive up Average Selling Price (ASP) for Udemy Business
  - Early stages: refining metrics, audience, and content strategy
  - Challenges: working within Udemy's existing systems vs. using GrowthX's preferred platform (Beehive)

### Udemy's Current Email Landscape

  - Lifecycle teams recently restructured by audience segments
  - Existing quarterly newsletter for admins and learners
  - Email platforms:
      - Braze: Main system for lifecycle emails
      - Unnamed system: Transactional emails (product-owned)
      - Third system: Prospect emails
  - Strict sending limits: 1 email/day, 3 emails/week max per user
  - Challenge: Potential noise from transactional emails outside Braze limits

### B2B Learner Lifecycle Marketing

  - Focuses on existing enterprise subscribers (UB, UB Pro, TeamPlan)
  - Key campaign types:
      - Onboarding
      - Retention (post-onboarding)
      - Feature adoption (e.g., AI features like role play, AI assistant)
      - Collaborative efforts with integrated marketing (e.g., webinar promotions)
  - Sensitive to over-communication, risk of org-wide unsubscribes

### Content Accessibility and Upselling

  - Most B2B learners have full access to UB catalog
  - Some specialized subscriptions (e.g., "AI starter pack") with limited course access
  - UB vs. UB Pro feature differences create upselling opportunities (e.g., Labs feature)
  - Challenge: Limited ungated content for potential newsletter subscribers not yet in Udemy ecosystem
