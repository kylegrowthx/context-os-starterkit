## Summary

Kyle Gaudreau (GrowthX AI) and Michelle Wu (Udemy) discussed a potential weekly AI-enablement newsletter to drive Average Selling Price (ASP) growth for Udemy Business, currently in the strategy sprint phase. Michelle provided critical context on Udemy's lifecycle email infrastructure: Braze as the primary platform with strict limits (1 email/day, max 3/week per user), fragmented transactional systems, and a quarterly newsletter currently covering both admins and learners. Kyle also learned about Udemy's B2B learner focus (UB, UB Pro, TeamPlan), content gating challenges, and the need to coordinate with admin and consumer teams to avoid duplication and identify ungated content opportunities for the newsletter.

## Key Topics

### GrowthX AI's Proposed Newsletter Project

  - Weekly cadence focused on AI enablement, thought leadership, and education—differentiated from Udemy's current quarterly newsletter
  - Primary goal: drive up Average Selling Price (ASP) for Udemy Business subscribers
  - Current phase: strategy sprint (week 1.5) to validate assumptions, identify content gaps, and scope execution approach
  - Challenges: Beehive (GrowthX's preferred platform) not viable for Udemy due to enterprise scale and public company restrictions; need to work within Udemy's existing Braze or alternative systems

### Udemy's Current Email Landscape

  - Lifecycle teams recently restructured by audience segments
  - Existing quarterly newsletter for admins and learners
  - Email platforms:
      - Braze: Main system for lifecycle emails
      - Unnamed system: Transactional emails (product-owned)
      - Third system: Prospect emails
  - Strict sending limits: 1 email/day, 3 emails/week max per user
  - Challenge: Potential noise from transactional emails outside Braze limits

### B2B Learner Lifecycle Marketing

  - Focuses on existing enterprise subscribers (UB, UB Pro, TeamPlan)
  - Key campaign types:
      - Onboarding
      - Retention (post-onboarding)
      - Feature adoption (e.g., AI features like role play, AI assistant)
      - Collaborative efforts with integrated marketing (e.g., webinar promotions)
  - Sensitive to over-communication, risk of org-wide unsubscribes

### Content Accessibility and Upselling

  - Most B2B learners have full access to UB catalog
  - Some specialized subscriptions (e.g., "AI starter pack") with limited course access
  - UB vs. UB Pro feature differences create upselling opportunities (e.g., Labs feature)
  - Challenge: Limited ungated content for potential newsletter subscribers not yet in Udemy ecosystem
