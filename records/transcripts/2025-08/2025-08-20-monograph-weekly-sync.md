- Matthew proposing Q&A-style Reddit content on Monograph company account (not Robert's personal account, which Monograph should manage separately)
- Motivation: Reddit is Monograph's #1 LLM referrer; Q&A posts on fintech/payments topics related to CheckThat tracking prompts show early success, surfacing in ChatGPT/Perplexity outputs days after publication

- Implementation challenges: Reddit has aggressive bot protection (like Facebook); likely publishing manually rather than programmatically to avoid bans
- Tone adjustment: Reddit content requires different tone/voice than company blog; specs and pipeline to incorporate this distinction
- Cadence: Weekly posts planned to maintain natural, human-like presence without triggering bot detection (avoiding bulk dumps of 100+ posts)
- Timeline: Matthew targeting preliminary spec doc + rough pipeline draft for next week; Gary interested in fleshing out details before departure so successor can execute
- Success case: Matthew has early wins with other clients using this approach

### Newsletter Transition

- Robert inquired about GrowthX taking over Monograph's newsletter production (currently managed by Gary DeBerry, who is leaving)
- Monograph's current setup: HubSpot for lead scoring, marketing automation, form fields; integrated with Chili Piper (scheduling/routing) and internal app
- Key requirement: Any solution must maintain compatibility with existing HubSpot infrastructure and integrations (cannot break lead scoring pipeline or form capture)
- GrowthX reference case: Augment client where GrowthX produces newsletter via Beehive (which integrates with HubSpot API); also expanded to dedicated landing pages for indexing and lead magnets
- Monograph interest: Would like similar landing page strategy for their newsletter (expanded surface area for SEO)
- Matthew's investigation: Checking Beehive HubSpot compatibility and gathering detailed scope of Augment newsletter engagement (copy, images, frequency, landing page build)
- Complexity: Taking over existing newsletter differs from creating new one; requires knowledge transfer and potential HubSpot workflow cleanup before expansion

### Content Strategy Refinements

- Gary flagged a topic proposal: "ACH vs. EFT — Understanding Key Differences" (deep fintech content)
- Strategic debate: Generic glossary content (pure TOFU/top-of-funnel traffic play) vs. contextual content clusters (conversion-focused)
- Matthew's assessment: Generic "What is EFT?" drives traffic but low-intent searchers; unlikely to convert. Glossary format is valid for authority signaling but low ROI on conversions
- Better approach: Contextual angle, e.g., "Should architects accept ACH payments?" embeds same keywords (ACH, EFT) but targets intent-qualified decision-makers (architects/CFOs)
- Execution: Build query that identifies what A&E managers actually care about (payment methods, compliance, processing costs) and cluster content around those decision contexts rather than definitions
- Next steps: Monograph to review flagged content in Airtable; Matthew to unflag items by tomorrow so team can clear out irrelevant ones
