## Key Topics

### Market Opportunity and the AI Content Shift

- Content services is a $500B annual market globally, with over 8,000 agencies in the US alone offering content services
- Fundamental market shift: 15-70% of search traffic is moving from traditional web search to AI conversational interfaces (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude)
- First sales impressions and brand discovery increasingly happen within AI systems, not on websites—affecting how companies approach visibility and positioning
- Salesforce parallel: the market for Salesforce services (consulting, setup, admin) is 50 times larger than Salesforce's market cap annually, suggesting massive opportunity in services-led software models
- Services business can extract far more value per customer ($200K/year per customer) compared to pure SaaS (which might charge $200-300/month)

### Business Model: Services + Platform Hybrid

- GrowthX's approach: start with services to validate product ideas and build process expertise, then build platforms to streamline or productize that work
- Services allows extraction of 70% gross margins (software-like economics) while building the common technology core
- Palantir-inspired model: come in as a strategic advisor, execute services work with platform support, leave the platform behind
- The "content OS" is both an internal tool for services delivery and a potentially productizable platform (like the fact-checker tool being developed)
- Not limiting to single business: could eventually have multiple business units built on the same technological core (like Tiny's portfolio model)

### Growth and Financials

- Bootstrapped to $10M in revenue in less than a year with 70% gross margins
- Raised $12M earlier in 2025, with 50+ investors actively interested in participating
- Revenue targets: $15M by year-end 2025, $45M in 2026
- Profitable before fundraising and has control of the company (only one investor, Daniel and Marcel retain significant ownership)
- Recently closed largest deal to date; not demand-constrained

### Product Development Approach

- Building MVPs for internal use first, validating with customers, then deciding what to productize or open-source
- Rapid iteration cycles: example given of swapping out research APIs (Perplexity for exa.ai) over a weekend based on testing results
- Focus on immediate product-market fit: everything built has already been "sold" as a service outcome first
- Technology components being built: AI workflow builder, coding agents, human-in-the-loop orchestration, learn engine, visibility/auditing layer
- Leveraging AI-native teams and rapid deployment capabilities as a competitive advantage

### Company Culture and Work Environment

- Remote-first with quarterly off-sites in San Francisco for team building and planning
- Emphasis on building new things and shipping to real users, not maintaining legacy systems
- Three core company principles: (1) Do the best work of our lives, (2) Inspire each other and the world through how we build and behave, (3) Accomplish more than we thought possible—continuous improvement mindset
- Fast-paced, minimal bureaucracy environment—contrasts sharply with enterprises (Harvest example) that use extensive feature flags and approval processes
- Recently held a workshop that attracted 1,000 signups with 500 attendees; strong external reputation attracting talent and inspiring others to start their own ventures

### Future Plans and Funding

- Raising $30-50M in Series A round to fund acceleration across seven engineering development "lanes"
- Currently understaffed relative to opportunity: approximately two people per lane (including principal engineers, design engineers, and EPD team members)
- Investing in marketing and sales functions for the first time (just hired first Account Executive two weeks prior to this call)
- Expanding engineering team significantly based on scope of platform development ahead
- Maintaining fiscal conservatism ("no backup plan" mentality) while being "all in" on the market opportunity
