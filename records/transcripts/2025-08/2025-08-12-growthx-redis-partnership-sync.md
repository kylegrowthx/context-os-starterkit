## Summary

Kyle Gaudreau from GrowthX negotiated partnership terms with Andy Varshneya from Redis, offering reduced pricing of $30k for an 8-week strategy sprint and $25k/month for execution (down from initial $40k/$30k), addressing Andy's concerns about the $330k total cost relative to Redis's marketing budget. Andy's core issue wasn't just price but ROI clarity—he needed to understand whether turning off digital ad spend for a major content push would deliver better results than maintaining the current marketing mix. GrowthX committed to delivering ROI projections and quarterly milestone scenarios by Friday, plus connecting Andy with customer references, while Redis will begin the NDA/MSA process; a realistic procurement timeline is 3 weeks, not the original August 25 kickoff date.

## Context

Redis is a major open-source database and caching platform company, and GrowthX (a B2B content marketing services firm) had been in conversations with Megan (Redis's VP of Marketing, implied) about a content strategy partnership. This call was specifically a contract negotiation with Andy Varshneya, who handles procurement and partnership evaluation at Redis. GrowthX framed the engagement as two phases: an 8-week strategy sprint ($30k) focused on building operational muscle, content quality standards, personas, and context engineering; followed by ongoing execution ($25k/month) targeting ~10 content pieces per week. Andy was cautious about the total cost (~$330k/year) and wanted proof of concept and ROI clarity before committing. The meeting occurred mid-August as Redis's procurement and approval processes moved at institutional pace. GrowthX (Kyle Gaudreau leading, with Tyler Pavlas managing logistics) had CEO approval for the reduced pricing and emphasized this was a services engagement, not SaaS, to help Redis understand the cost structure.
