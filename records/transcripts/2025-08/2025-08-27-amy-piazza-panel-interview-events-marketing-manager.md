## Summary

GrowthX conducted a panel interview with Amy Robertson Piazza for the Events Marketing Manager role. Amy presented a comprehensive 6-month events strategy covering CMO breakfasts, executive dinners, happy hours, and a potential user conference, drawing on her experience at Anthropic, Andreessen Horowitz, and production agencies. ## Context

Amy Robertson Piazza is an experienced events professional with 5 years in the industry. She recently managed internal events at Anthropic (team offsites, hackathons, company-wide initiatives) and spent 3 years at Andreessen Horowitz executing high-level executive dinners, LP summits, and Growth Fund programs. GrowthX has built a strong events program organically from day one: founder Marcel Santilli ran virtual workshops that generated the first customers, then evolved into CMO breakfasts and executive dinners. The company now wants to mature this program and define repeatable processes as the business scales. Jason Gong leads growth at GrowthX and has been involved in events strategy alongside Marcel. This interview brought together the full team to evaluate Amy as the dedicated person to own and scale the events function.

## Key Topics

### Amy's Background and Experience

- 5 years in events industry, most recently at Anthropic on internal events team
- 3 years at Andreessen Horowitz working on Growth Fund events, executive dinners, LP summits
- Early career in production at agencies like George P. Johnson and Creative Edge
- Experience spans internal events, executive-level programs, and large-scale conferences

### GrowthX Current Event Strategy

- Four main event types: CMO breakfasts (1-2 months), executive dinners (every couple months), joint happy hours, goal of user conference
- Amy presented 6-month event calendar incorporating these events
- Discussion on expanding audience and network through events, especially for breakfasts

### Event Execution Process

- Pre-event: Stakeholder management, resource builds, venue/vendor coordination, budget tracking
- Day-of: On-site management, vendor coordination, host support, content capture
- Post-event: Thank yous, feedback collection, performance analysis, stakeholder debrief

### Event Ideas and Recommendations

- Executive Dinners: Intimate roundtables with facilitated discussions and thought leadership
- CMO Breakfasts: Scalable networking opportunity, potentially monthly "breakfast club" format
- Joint Happy Hours: 2-hour post-conference events to build reputation and industry engagement
- User Conference: 6-hour format with sessions and networking, focus on education/innovation

### Measuring Event Success

- Post-event surveys for direct feedback
- Attendance rates and audience quality metrics
- Engagement on social media and with event content
- Network growth and new attendee acquisition
- Lead generation or deal conversion (to be defined with team)
- Stakeholder feedback and debrief sessions

### Budget and ROI Considerations

- Discussion on balancing event scale/production with ROI
- Exploring ways to create valuable experiences without excessive costs
- Importance of defining clear goals and metrics for each event type

### Long-Term Events Strategy

- Building repeatable processes and templates to increase efficiency over time
- Potential to add new event types like client retreats as program evolves
- Focus on creating awareness and "halo effect" for GrowthX brand
- Balancing relationship-building with measurable business impact
