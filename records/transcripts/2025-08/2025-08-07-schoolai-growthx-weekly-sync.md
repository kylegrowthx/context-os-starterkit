## Context

SchoolAI is an AI-powered educational platform helping teachers and administrators leverage AI in classrooms. GrowthX is running programmatic SEO for SchoolAI, targeting teacher and admin personas by creating templated content around high-quality spaces within the platform. This sync is part of a weekly cadence between SchoolAI's product and marketing leadership (Nikki Muncey) and GrowthX's SEO and content delivery team (Jenn Peters and Megan Dickey) to track content strategy execution, platform updates, and content performance. The team is shifting from manual, high-touch content creation to an automated, volume-based approach—a significant operational change requiring alignment on priorities, review processes, and technical dependencies (particularly SchoolAI 2.0 feature deprecation and space taxonomy changes).

---

## Relevance

**To GrowthX Delivery:**
- Programmatic SEO implementation requires lightweight review process and tooling (Framer categories, templated blog structure) to accommodate high-volume publishing (~300 articles per space at 2 posts/day cadence).
- Client platform changes (SchoolAI 2.0) create content debt: out-of-box spaces (Sidekick, Bell Ringer) may be deprecated or renamed, requiring retroactive content audits to remove obsolete feature references.
- Template-driven content strategy (subject + grade level + teacher benefit) is working at scale—standardized structure will reduce variation and enable faster review turnaround.

**To GrowthX Business Development:**
- SchoolAI account is actively expanding SEO investment and operational scope, moving from experimental to production-scale content delivery (from single-digit articles to 300+ per space).
- Client is planning major product restructuring (2.0) with ongoing feature integration; clarity on deprecation timeline and naming changes will be critical for content roadmap planning.
- Meta description updates (20 completed, monitoring before expansion) show willingness to test and iterate on SEO tactics—signals good fit for performance-driven, experimental engagement model.

**To Content & SEO Strategy:**
- First-party insights on what spaces teachers and admins actually search for (via programmatic content pillars) will feed into broader AEO and visibility research for education vertical.
- Granular content structure (subject + grade + benefit) is a replicable template for other verticals and could inform CheckThat research on intent-driven content patterns.
