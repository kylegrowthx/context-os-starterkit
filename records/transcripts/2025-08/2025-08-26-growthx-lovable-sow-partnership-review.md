## Key Topics

### Partnership Scope and Deliverables

Marcel outlined GrowthX's plan to ship a catalog (prompt library, template library, or programmatic editorial) in 8 weeks, designed to help Lovable users discover and leverage AI-native components at scale. The key insight: an e-commerce catalog model (like Amazon's de-duped, curated product catalog) applied to templates and UI components. Users can search by multiple angles (dark mode + portfolio, React stack, etc.), and Lovable becomes the discoverability layer that competitors like Base44 lack. GrowthX is also building starter kits with proper scaffolding, rules, and design systems that import from Figma—an approach validated internally and with customers like Augment Code.

### Exclusivity and Contract Terms

Ceci requested exclusivity from GrowthX for V0, Bolt, Replit, and Base44—the four direct low-code/no-code competitors that could dilute Lovable's positioning. Marcel agreed to time-bound, specific exclusivity in the SOW (not blanket exclusivity). Marcel noted GrowthX's largest customer pays $2.3M/year without exclusivity, and he emphasized keeping pricing fair—70% gross margin is important as GrowthX fundraises. Ceci wasn't focused on price negotiation, only ensuring value and exclusivity. They agreed to not worry about minor cost variations and move forward on shared value.

### Search Discovery and Long-Tail Strategy

Marcel showed research on 1,800+ subcategories (law firm, production company, food blogger, hotel website, etc.) and long-tail keyword strategy similar to Wix's distribution dominance. The problem: Lovable's current search is nearly useless (hard to find anything). The opportunity: curate a high-quality catalog with editorial rigor, then map and SEO-optimize for long-tail keywords. This feeds organic traffic the same way e-commerce companies spend billions optimizing their catalogs. Ceci validated this aligns with enterprise demand and Lovable's organic traction.

### Prompt Engineering and Rules Systems

Marcel shared examples of how GrowthX builds AI-native rules and prompts—analyzing tech stacks (React, Next.js, TypeScript, Postgres) and researching best practices, then generating principles for type safety, testing, performance, caching, security. Ceci noted that designing rules and design systems is a huge enterprise ask. Marcel's insight: templates and prompt libraries need to be powerful enough to be customizable (users learn by adjusting prompts), but also simple enough for non-technical users.

### Team Alignment and Internal Constraints

Ceci is managing internal restructuring at Lovable with multiple rogue projects happening simultaneously. She finds out her role (likely head of product marketing vs. other role) Friday, which determines her sign-off timeline. If she doesn't get the marketing head role, Elena takes full ownership. Marcel is similarly constrained—booked to end of September but willing to pull a top design engineer to Lovable-only for this partnership if GrowthX's co-founder/CTO agrees. Both emphasized speed and autonomy as non-negotiable.

### Market Competitive Context

Base44 is growing fast (nearly $1M/week ARR) and executing well on go-to-market; Wix has distribution and cash but is seen as less innovative. Lovable's advantage is speed and product momentum. Marcel suggested leveraging agencies and freelancer ecosystems (Dribbble) as a distribution channel—if agencies can highlight projects influenced by Lovable, they get deal flow. This mirrors HubSpot's partner ecosystem success. The goal is to make Lovable the platform that gives agencies and freelancers distribution, not a threat.

### Backend and Enterprise Integration

Ceci raised that enterprises want CMS and backend options. They're making Supabase seamless (no extra clicks to set up), and the next question is which backends to support—HubSpot, Notion, headless CMSs, etc. This is a product discovery exercise for the sprint.
