## Key Topics

### Pro Referral Page Updates

  - Current headline "Easy as one, two, three: we use AI to help our live staff" needs revision to reflect both AI receptionist and virtual receptionist products
  - Remove mentions of chat and outbound calling services (outbound will return early next year, but focus now is on two core products)
  - Clarify "done-for-you" vs. DIY: emphasize white-glove service, onboarding, and integration support — not just self-service signups
  - Replace generic "easy" messaging with Smith.ai value props: "results-driven partner," "AI and human as teammates," "high quality," "done-for-you," "scalable"
  - Incorporate "Expert Onboarding" component from homepage ("we set you up for success, we design, we build, we integrate") to replace vague onboarding language
  - Simplify signup flow: embed Calendly directly on referral page instead of routing through separate booking page; reduce friction for existing referral partners
  - Include pricing section for transparency on AI receptionist offering

### Partner Page Revisions

  - Partner page currently lacks technical depth and partner-specific information
  - Include interactive demo of partner account dashboard and control capabilities (model on Novatic demo from AI receptionist page) — partners want to know what tools they get access to and what their clients see
  - Clarify three partner models: reselling (buy at volume, resell to clients), referring (send leads, earn commission), controlling (manage call handling for clients)
  - Highlight partner benefits tied to each model and impact on their clients (e.g., franchise companies buying for rollout across locations, agencies integrating with existing Google Ads workflows)
  - Incorporate FAQ addressing partner concerns: What tools do I get? Can I customize? How much control do I have?
  - Add trust elements: testimonials, success metrics (e.g., "4,000+ clients," "20 million+ calls handled"), partner use cases

### Content Strategy and Homepage Alignment

  - Smith.ai recently revamped homepage; most landing pages should inherit modules rather than reinvent
  - Homepage messaging pillars: "AI and human as teammates" (not rivals), white-glove support, done-for-you approach, scalable results
  - Landing pages should use homepage-sourced components (expert onboarding, industry comparisons, testimonials, trust blocks) to reduce redundancy
  - Focus on two main products (AI receptionist, virtual receptionist); deprioritize chat and outbound calling from most pages (features page can keep these, but not main navigation)
  - Experimenting with LLMS.txt files for SEO; planning to ask Julian to share Smith.ai's LLMS.txt in GrowthX Slack for feedback
  - Plan monthly SEO and backlink performance reporting (Jo Kaminska will own this) including ScrunchMetrics insights, LLMS.txt impact, and backlink updates

### Team Updates

  - Background: strong expertise in SEO and content strategy; previously on GrowthX strategy sprint team
  - Responsibility: overseeing Smith.ai account and other accounts; leading monthly performance reporting
  - Attributes: highly organized, proactive, well-versed in SEO and strategy
