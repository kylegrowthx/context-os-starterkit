## Key Topics

### Company Overview

  - Deepgram is a foundational AI company focused on building voice models for voice AI
  - Research and engineering-led approach, productizing advanced models for enterprise use
  - Targeting untapped potential of voice data in enterprises (customer calls, sales calls, meetings)

### Speech-to-Text Capabilities

  - Nova model family: Nova 2 (base model) and Nova 3 (premium, advanced model)
  - Nova 3 key features:
      - Designed for noisy environments (e.g., call centers, drive-thrus)
      - Enhanced numerical and industry-specific terminology recognition
      - Real-time multilingual support and PII redaction
      - Flexible self-service customization (100 terms at runtime)
  - Industry-tuned and custom models available (e.g., healthcare, FSI)
  - Expanding language support for Nova 3 (European languages added)

### Text-to-Speech Capabilities

  - Competing with Eleven Labs, but focused on enterprise use cases rather than creative
  - 40+ voices with localized accents and distinct voice profiles
  - Strengths in domain-specific accuracy, handling formatted terms
  - Cost-effective for high-volume, real-time enterprise scenarios
  - Multilingual support coming in Q4 2023

### Speech-to-Speech (Voice Agent API)

  - Unified API handling speech-to-text, LLM integration, and text-to-speech
  - Manages complex orchestration (turn-taking, interruptions, conversation logic)
  - Features include prompt updating, voice switching, and function calling
  - Offers cloud, self-hosted, and dedicated deployment options
  - Launched for production use cases in June 2023

### Enterprise Focus

  - Tailored for mission-critical applications requiring high accuracy, low latency, scalability
  - Security and compliance certifications for regulated industries
  - Flexible deployment options (cloud, on-prem, dedicated instance)
  - Partnerships with contact center and technology partners for turnkey solutions

### Market Positioning

  - 90% of current revenue from speech-to-text solutions
  - Competing with cloud providers (AWS, Azure) and specialized players (Assembly AI, Rev.ai)
  - Differentiation through customization, industry-tuned models, and enterprise-focused features
