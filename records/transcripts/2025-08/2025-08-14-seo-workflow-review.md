## Summary

GrowthX and SchoolAI aligned on critical content workflow improvements to make SEO articles more valuable and accessible to educators. The team identified four core issues: avoiding overly technical AI explanations in favor of pedagogy and best practices, improving scannability through better headers and strategic placement of key takeaways at the top of articles, reducing word counts to 900-1100 words (following Edutopia's educator-friendly format), and removing language that frames teachers as martyrs or superheroes in favor of positioning them as professionals. Jenn Peters will integrate these insights into GrowthX's content workflow artifacts, and Addy will implement key takeaway placement immediately on new articles.

## Context

SchoolAI is an EdTech platform designed to help educators integrate AI into classroom workflows while maintaining pedagogical rigor and best practices. Their CEO tasked Maureen Young, a former instructional technology coach and current head coach at SchoolAI, with reviewing GrowthX's SEO content to ensure it resonates with educators and can be vetted by SchoolAI's team of coaches (rather than a single individual) in about 15 minutes per article. This review revealed gaps between GrowthX's content workflow and what educators actually need: content that connects to classroom realities, avoids overly technical AI theory, is highly scannable, and respects teachers as professionals rather than saviors. The meeting brought together Nikki Muncey (SchoolAI's business lead), Maureen Young (SchoolAI's education strategist), Jenn Peters (GrowthX's content director), and Megan (GrowthX's SEO specialist) to align on specific workflow changes needed to bridge these gaps.

---

## Key Topics

### Content Focus and Tone

  - Lean into best teaching practices, pedagogy, and "why" behind AI tools
  - Avoid over-indexing on technical AI explanations (e.g. how LLMs work)
  - Strike balance between formal tone and accessibility (pre-K through higher ed)
  - Frame teachers as professionals, not martyrs/superheroes ("making school awesome every day")

### Readability and Structure

  - Improve scannability with descriptive H2/H3 headers (not generic)
  - Use bullet points, numbered sections for visual flow
  - Limit text under headers to \~2 paragraphs max
  - Consider "read more" expandable sections to avoid overwhelming readers
  - Move key takeaways to top of articles (currently at bottom)

### Word Count and SEO

  - Target 900-1100 words for shorter pieces (including key takeaways)
  - Balance SEO needs with educator time constraints
  - Maintain content pyramids (broad topics -\> granular supporting pieces)

### Educator-Friendly Resources

  - Create AI terminology glossary page (link terms in articles)
  - Emulate Edutopia's educator-friendly format (short, chunked content)
