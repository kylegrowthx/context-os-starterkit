## Summary

Marcel Santilli (GrowthX) and Linus Talacko (getden.io) explored potential synergies between their platforms — getden.io's no-code agent builder for PLG teams and GrowthX's AI-powered content services business ($11M revenue, Series B stage). Both founders discussed the market's shift toward vertical specialization ("10x better at one thing") rather than horizontal AI platforms, and the challenge of delivering reliable, production-ready agents beyond the quick time-to-value window. They concluded with openness to future collaboration or joining forces as complementary solutions.

---

## Context

Linus Talacko is the founder of getden.io, a no-code workflow automation platform that provides natural language access to N8N-like capabilities for non-technical users. The company recently closed a $5.5M seed round from General Catalyst post-YC and has built 200,000+ agents on its platform, with 80+ tool integrations via MCP. This meeting was initiated by Marcel Santilli after learning about getden.io from a customer (Homebase's CEO John), demonstrating how GrowthX uses customer recommendations to evaluate potential solutions or partnership opportunities.

GrowthX is an AI-powered B2B services company generating $11M in revenue with average deal sizes of $200k, currently raising Series B. Marcel and his team are exploring opportunities to scale delivery beyond manual services, including more self-serve options and internal workflow automation. The conversation reflects both founders' shared challenge: building tools that move beyond quick wins ("time to first value") to deliver sustained, reliable value to non-technical team members.

## Key Topics

### getden.io's Product Overview

  - Natural language version of N8N for non-technical users
  - Previously included chat, docs, and agents (now streamlined)
  - 80+ tool integrations through MCP
  - 200,000+ agents built on the platform
  - Raised $5.5M seed from General Catalyst post-YC

### growthx.ai's Business Model

  - AI-powered agency with both internal tools and openness to external solutions
  - $11M in revenue, raising Series B
  - Average deal size: $200,000
  - Specializes in content creation with expert interventions
  - Exploring more self-serve options

### Market Challenges and Strategies

  - Time-to-value vs. depth of utility trade-off
  - Horizontal vs. vertical focus debate
  - Need for specificity: "Harvey AI for something"
  - Labs (e.g., OpenAI) eating into horizontal plays
  - Importance of having "10x better" value proposition

### Use Cases and Customer Adoption

  - getden.io: 50% personal use (calendar, emails), 50% business (mainly PLG efficiency)
  - growthx.ai: Deep research, content generation, customer-specific projects
  - Challenge: Making agents truly useful and reliable for non-technical team members

### Technical Insights and Agent Reliability

  - Company context is critical for agent effectiveness — agents without company context don't understand business goals or constraints
  - GrowthX's current manual workflow: pull transcripts, gather metrics, create artifacts in cloud projects, process through prompts, copy-paste results to Slack — error-prone and inconsistent
  - The "time-to-value vs. utility" problem: easy to build 200,000 agents, hard to make them reliably deliver production-ready output every run
  - MCP (Model Context Protocol) enables 80+ integrations for data aggregation, but orchestration logic still requires manual human oversight
  - Potential use of getden.io to automate GrowthX's internal customer update workflows
