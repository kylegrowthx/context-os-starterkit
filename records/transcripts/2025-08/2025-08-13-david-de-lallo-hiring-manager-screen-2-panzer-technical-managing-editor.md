## Summary

Matthew Panzarino (Chief Content Officer at GrowthX) interviewed David DeLallo, a 15-year content veteran with expertise in editorial strategy, AI, and financial services, for the Technical Managing Editor (TME) role. The discussion centered on GrowthX's content atomization approach — breaking down content into reusable components and linking workflows to scale production — and the TME's core responsibility: identifying editorial quality issues and articulating why content works or fails so the team can improve both the humans and the systems producing it. David shared his background running content programs at McKinsey (AI editor for 6 years), IBM, and his own freelance practice, emphasizing his comfort spanning diverse content types and his ability to mentor content creators. Matthew outlined a typical week: Monday is content generation and review for three managed accounts, then the rest of the week is strategy, client calls, workflow improvements, and identifying upsell opportunities — a mix designed to prevent the "blog factory" trap while keeping the role intellectually engaging.

## Key Topics

### GrowthX's Approach to Content Creation

  - Focuses on atomizing content and leveraging technology
  - Uses workflows and pipelines to automate and scale content production
  - Aims to create high-quality, client-specific content efficiently
  - Constantly iterates on processes to improve output and reduce manual effort

### Role Responsibilities

  - Review and manage weekly deliverables
  - Interact with content managers to resolve issues and improve processes
  - Conduct client meetings and manage expectations
  - Identify and articulate workflow improvements
  - Perform quality control on content outputs
  - Contribute to product development and feature ideation

### Typical Workday

  - Review scope of deliverables for the week
  - Check progress with content managers
  - Review and edit content
  - Attend client meetings (avg. 1 per week per client)
  - Troubleshoot workflow issues
  - Collaborate on process improvements
  - Engage in content strategy and new product development

### Content Types and Distribution

  - Editorial content (blog posts, articles): \~65%
  - Programmatic SEO projects: \~15%
  - Newsletters and social media content: \~10%
  - Experimental/ancillary projects: \~10%
  - Product development input: \~10% of time

### Workflow and Pipeline Management

  - Use tools like Airtable, Notion, and various AI platforms
  - Create and optimize workflows for content creation and editing
  - Build pipelines to link multiple workflows for complex projects
  - Identify areas for automation and process improvement
