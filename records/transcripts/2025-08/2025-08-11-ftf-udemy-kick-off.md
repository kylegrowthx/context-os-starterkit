## Summary

Udemy and GrowthX held a strategic kickoff to align on a transformative partnership focused on positioning Udemy as a thought leader in AI and skills-driven learning. Udemy seeks to shift from a course-centric to skills-based narrative, with targeted depth in AI topics like cybersecurity and AI, to better serve L&D leaders and career-focused consumers (career starters, pivoters, advancers). GrowthX will leverage its AI-powered workflow engine to help build skills mapping, curated content, and authority-building through newsletters and audience-specific messaging. Next steps include deep-dive sessions starting the following week, skills spreadsheet enrichment, and calibration of GrowthX's content strategy artifacts with Udemy's teams.

---

## Context

Udemy is a B2B and consumer online learning platform with a catalog of 250,000 courses spanning tech, business, creative, and other domains. The company operates two main segments: Udemy Business (UB), targeting enterprise L&D leaders, and consumer DTC, targeting career-focused individuals (80% of learners). Udemy has 17,000 B2B customers, significantly outpacing Coursera's 1,000. The company's core competitive advantage is content freshness—particularly critical in fast-evolving domains like AI—and the breadth of its instructor network.

The meeting was convened by Genefa Murphy (VP/Product Marketing for Consumer & Instructor Segment) and included subject-matter leaders: Veronika Benkeser (curation, skills mapping, trials), Krista Chism (SEO, domain strategy), Glenn Lehrman (communications, brand), and Ava Plasse (content innovation, YouTube Shorts strategy). GrowthX brought Marcel Santilli (founder/CEO) and Kyle Gaudreau (Head of Customer Growth), alongside delivery and engineering team members. The kickoff framed Udemy's strategic challenge: the company possesses premium content and data, but currently packages and presents it as a commodity (courses). Udemy wants to reposition as a skills and thought-leadership authority, particularly in AI and emerging tech, to command pricing power and customer loyalty with enterprise clients and career-advancing consumers.

## Key Topics

### Udemy's Current Challenges and Goals

  - Shift focus from individual courses to comprehensive skills development
  - Improve discovery and showcase 250,000 course catalog more effectively
  - Create more touchpoints before purchase to build first-party data
  - Better leverage and showcase instructor authority
  - Develop a stronger narrative around skills of the future, especially in AI

### Target Audience and Competitors

  - B2B: Enterprise customers, focusing on L\&D leaders and tech leaders
  - Consumer: Career starters, pivoters, and advancers (80% of learners)
  - Main competitors: ChatGPT and other AI providers, YouTube, LinkedIn Learning, Pluralsight, Skillsoft, Coursera
  - Udemy has 17,000 B2B customers compared to Coursera's 1,000

### GrowthX's AI-Powered Solutions

  - Workflow engine for content generation, fact-checking, and deep research
  - Custom knowledge bases and integration with existing data
  - Ability to create highly curated newsletters with 41% open rates and 10% click-through rates
  - Generation of comparison pages and vendor catalogs, applicable to skills mapping
  - Creation of detailed writing guidelines and expert-in-the-loop content review processes

### Potential Areas of Collaboration

  - Develop a skills graph and leaderboard
  - Create a weekly curated newsletter to become the "single point of entry" for skills information
  - Enhance Udemy's catalog with AI-enriched descriptions and categorizations
  - Showcase instructors more prominently and leverage their authority
  - Build confidence with L\&D leaders by creating comprehensive, industry-leading programs

### Udemy's Unique Selling Points

  - Freshness of content, especially crucial in rapidly evolving fields like AI
  - Breadth and depth of course offerings across various roles and skills
  - Ability to upskill across multiple roles within an organization
  - Strong tech-focused content, particularly in areas like AI and cybersecurity
