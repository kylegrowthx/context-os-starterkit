## Key Topics

### Content Tone and Style

- Redone blog post ("Maximizing Percentage of Policy Limits") received approval from Jon
- Tone shift successful: changed from informal/enthusiastic to professional, neutral, authoritative (Investopedia-like)
- Jon emphasized: neutral, professional, educational without being patronizing — "speaks to lawyers as lawyers who already know this, but offers additional insight"
- Minor refinement: reduce passive voice usage in future drafts
- Jon appreciated the readability, conciseness, and appropriate level of detail

### Content Strategy Refinement

- Jon wants Tavrn positioned as a true content/news engine for lawyers, not a sales channel
- Reject sales-adjacent listicle topics ("3 reasons for document automation," "Why CRM is great") — these feel promotional and are too similar to direct advertising
- Focus on educational and news-like content: legal precedents, settlement data, case studies, industry news that lawyers actually care about
- Example approved topics: Cambridge case law eligibility precedent, Starbucks drive-through lawsuit (life-altering injury case)
- Content should be fact-based and help lawyers understand legal trends and implications, then organically lead them to discover Tavrn through the value of the resource

### AI Content Approach

- Jon emphasized the need to manage buyer expectations: current market overestimates AI capabilities, leading to disappointment when AI underperforms
- Parallel insight: users are more forgiving of human mistakes than AI mistakes — "people who use a chatbot are less forgiving than if a human made the mistake"
- Frame AI as a competitive advantage, not a threat: "AI won't take your job, but people who know how to use AI will"
- Draw parallels with historical tech precedents: Excel adoption created competitive advantage, ATMs didn't eliminate bank tellers but made them more productive, Westlaw became a skill badge of honor
- Position AI adoption as a skill that gives individual lawyers and law firms competitive edge: first-movers learn the tool and pull ahead
- Content angle: "Law students will be the first AI-native lawyers in the world" — AI adoption is an emerging skill set like Bloomberg in finance or Salesforce in sales
- AI content should emphasize tools, benefits, and realistic expectations — not hype

### Blog Post Ideas and Approved Topics

- **Approved case law:** Cambridge case precedent (eligibility), Starbucks drive-through lawsuit (life-altering injury)
- **Rejected topics:** sales-oriented listicles like "3 Reasons for Document Automation"; Kemp Lejeune settlement; "Outsource Medical Chronology" — Jon feels these are too industry-insider/sales-adjacent and give the site "the ick"
- **Data-driven topics (Jon's favorite):** settlement trends, jury decisions, Pennsylvania statute of limitations, tax rates by state — Jon loves fact-based content with sourced, verifiable data
- **Programmatic content idea:** Jon fantasized about scraping government websites and packaging data (like a state-by-state tax map or legal limit database) and making interactive, visually beautiful maps so lawyers can search and find info (e.g., "Is a chair considered furniture?" maps to legal interpretation databases)
- **AI adoption strategies:** frame around how law firms can use AI as a competitive tool; parallels with Excel, ATMs, Westlaw
- **Bottom-of-funnel content:** Jo suggested creating content that discusses problems and solutions (like document automation) without ever mentioning Tavrn — pure educational value

### Content Review Process

- Content will be reviewed jointly by Jon and Pedro to ensure consistent feedback and learning loop
- Committed to weekly 1-hour dedicated meeting to review content, discuss feedback on coverage direction, and suggest additions or refinements
- GrowthX will send content to Jon, Pedro, and Victor for review before publishing
- Feedback style will be directional: "cover this," "cover that," or "it would be cool to add [x]"
- GrowthX can always add more depth, context, and talking points (especially using AI assistance) — Jon prefers depth over brevity for comprehensive coverage
