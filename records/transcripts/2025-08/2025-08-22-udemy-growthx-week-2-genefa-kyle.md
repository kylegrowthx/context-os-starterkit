## Context

This is week 2 of GrowthX's engagement with Udemy Business (UB), the B2B e-learning platform for enterprises. Genefa Murphy is CMO at Udemy; Kyle Gaudreau is running the GrowthX project. The previous week included a kickoff call and a deep-dive session between Kyle and Veronica (a key Udemy stakeholder on learning content), where Kyle identified several strategic questions about persona targeting and content gaps. This follow-up meeting focused on narrowing strategy: aligning on whether to target traditional L&D buyers or the executives who actually drive organizational transformation decisions, and deciding between two paths—improving the underperforming business.udemy.com/resources page first, or launching an MVP newsletter to drive perception shift and subscriber engagement. Udemy's resources page currently generates minimal organic traffic relative to brand size, a concern Kyle flagged. The conversation surfaced a critical asset: Udemy has ~300 VIP instructors and is formalizing instructor partnerships for content repurposing, which significantly expands the available content pool for both newsletter and other initiatives.

  - Shifting focus from L\&D leaders to line-of-business executives (CIO, CTO, heads of development)
  - L\&D often acts as "order takers" rather than strategic leaders in many organizations
  - Goal: Target decision-makers who are thinking about team optimization and transformation

  - Mapping skills to content using Udemy's skills tree
  - Customers want to know foundational skills and "jobs to be done" for specific roles
  - Opportunity to create content around building end-to-end learning experiences
  - Highlight multimodal approach and human-in-the-loop advantage over pure LLM solutions

### Instructor Content Utilization

  - List of \~300 VIP instructors available
  - Many instructors open to content repurposing for marketing
  - New team member starting to formalize instructor partnerships for content creation

### Newsletter vs. SEO Strategy

  - Current resources page (business.udemy/resources) shows low SEO value
  - Two potential approaches discussed:
    1.  Improve SEO first, then add newsletter

  - Leaning towards starting with newsletter for perception shift and transformative impact

  - Explore repurposing existing gated content (webinars, ebooks) into ungated formats
  - Consider hosting new content on a subdomain
  - Leverage course transcripts and other available materials
  - GrowthX to create content filling identified gaps
