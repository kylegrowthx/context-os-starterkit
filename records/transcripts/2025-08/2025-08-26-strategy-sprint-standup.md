## Summary

GrowthX's delivery team held a strategy sprint standup to review progress across eight active client engagements (Understory, Udemy, Clerk, Tavern, Outreach, Sentinel One, Okta, and Diligent). The team identified three critical bottlenecks: content approval delays blocking Clerk and Understory, unclear deliverables and stakeholder alignment challenges at Udemy, and design-to-pipeline capability misalignment at Sentinel One. Positive momentum came from Okta, where the client approved the first 20 documentation pieces with no revisions. The team committed to unblocking approval workflows, refining stakeholder engagement strategies, and ensuring design feasibility before client presentations.

---

## Context

This was an internal GrowthX delivery team standup, one of the recurring strategy sprint meetings where the delivery leads review active engagements, identify blockers, and coordinate next steps. The team includes both account managers (like Andi Bailey and Aida Knezevic) and content/technical leads (Matthew Alves-Hill, Sydney Arin Go, Simran Sethi). The meeting focused on eight simultaneous client projects ranging from early-stage content strategy (Tavern, Udemy) to mid-project production challenges (Clerk, Understory, Sentinel One) to established projects with good momentum (Okta, Diligent). Each project faces different maturity challenges: some clients haven't approved initial assets, others lack clear direction on deliverables, and some have design or workflow gaps that could slow production. The standup is a tactical checkpoint to surface these issues early and assign owners to unblock them before the next sync.
