## Summary

Kyle Gaudreau (GrowthX) and Dan Kaplan (Aembit) discussed persistent challenges in capturing Aembit's unique technical voice and perspective in GrowthX-produced content. Dan emphasized the disconnect between GrowthX's output and content written by Aembit's engineering team, particularly around complex topics like non-human identities versus credentials. Kyle proposed exploring alternative content sources — recording engineer demos, all-hands meetings, and internal discussions — combined with social listening tools (Brand24) to identify audience interests, while also improving direct communication on rejected topics and feedback loops.

---

## Context

Aembit is a security infrastructure company focused on non-human identities (the application, service account, and the credentials that allow them to interact). GrowthX has been working with Aembit on content marketing to improve their blog rankings and visibility in a competitive market. Dan Kaplan manages day-to-day content operations at Aembit, while Apoorva oversees strategic direction and budgeting. Kyle Gaudreau, who recently joined GrowthX, is getting up to speed on the account and trying to understand why GrowthX's content hasn't matched the depth and authority of Aembit's engineering team's internal posts. The core tension is that Aembit's most successful content comes from their engineers, but scaling that approach is difficult due to time constraints and the unique perspective engineers bring to complex technical topics.

## Key Topics

### Content Creation Challenges

- Difficulty in replicating the depth and perspective of engineer-produced content
- Industry-specific terminology (e.g., non-human identities vs. credentials) causing confusion
- Content often lacks Aembit's inherent perspective and voice
- Previous attempts with technical contractors yielded unsatisfactory results

### Potential Solutions

- Leverage internal resources:
  - Record engineer demos and all-hands meetings
  - Use these recordings to extract deeper technical insights and unique perspectives
- Explore adjacent topics:
  - Identify issues of interest to the target audience beyond direct product promotion
  - Use social listening tools (e.g., Brand24) to inform content strategy
- Improve communication:
  - More direct feedback on rejected topics
  - Increase proactive communication between GrowthX and Aembit teams

### Content Strategy Evolution

- Shifted focus from "workload identities" to "non-human identities" based on industry trends
- Now aiming to differentiate Aembit's approach within the non-human identity space
- Balancing between owning the topic and exploring adjacent areas of interest to the audience

### Audience-Centric Approach

- Goal: Create content that makes life easier for the target audience
- Focus on solving challenges without overtly promoting Aembit's solutions
- Identify and address topics that resonate with DevOps and security professionals dealing with non-human identities
