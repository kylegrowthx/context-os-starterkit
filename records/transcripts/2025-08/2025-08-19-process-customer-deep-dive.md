## Key Topics

### Customer Acquisition Process

  - Inbound-focused strategy, no outbound sales motion
  - Two primary contact types: researchers/engineers (preferred) or AI data ops teams
  - Word-of-mouth driven, with recent increase from public appearances
  - Initial calls focus on understanding customer needs and showcasing Surge's expertise
  - Quality demonstration through conversation or data samples is crucial

### Contracting and Pricing

  - Negotiation primarily on standard terms (indemnity, liability, IP rights)
  - Pricing often higher than competitors, causing initial surprise
  - Non-negotiable pricing model based on hourly rates
  - Aim for initial projects worth at least a couple million dollars

### Model Evaluation and Insights

  - Comprehensive "eval" process comparing customer's model to competitors (e.g., ChatGPT)
  - Uses diverse, real-world prompts across various domains
  - Produces detailed insights deck (70+ slides) highlighting strengths/weaknesses
  - Takes about 1.5 weeks to complete (prompt creation, evaluation, deck preparation)
  - Builds trust and informs data recommendations

### Ongoing Customer Relationship

  - Organic process after initial project, driven by customer needs
  - Periodic re-evaluations to track progress and identify new areas for improvement
  - Flexible approach to project scoping and funding (e.g., open POs for large amounts)

### Challenges and Friction Points

  - Ongoing price sensitivity with each new project quote
  - Requests to work on customer platforms, compromising quality control
  - Difficulty in communicating the value of Surge's proprietary platform
