## Key Topics

### Homebase's AI Strategy

  - Shifting from software that helps do work to software that does work for customers
  - Launched first small-scale AI product, now expanding to all product teams
  - Focused on three areas: product features, software development, and internal productivity
  - Goal: close gap with potential future AI-native competitors

### Challenges in AI Adoption

  - Difficulty in mandating AI use across teams
  - Need for cultural shift and reducing activation energy for AI adoption
  - Balancing central resources/training with team-level ownership of AI initiatives
  - Overcoming "deer in headlights" effect and cold start problem for employees

### Lessons from Successful AI Initiatives

  - G-gram example: 100+ automations created, employees building their own tools
  - Importance of showcasing results (e.g., organic traffic growth) to inspire adoption
  - Value of having champions (e.g., CTO, RevOps) and curious early adopters
  - CEO mandate and consistent messaging about AI importance

### AI Implementation Approaches

  - Focus on changing mindsets rather than immediate ROI
  - Start with highly motivated individuals/teams, then expand
  - Use hackathons and workshops to generate excitement
  - Encourage public sharing of AI wins to inspire others

### Tools and Platforms

  - Caution against over-reliance on SaaS AI tools that may become outdated quickly
  - Preference for flexible, customizable solutions over black-box offerings
  - Consideration for specialized tools in areas like knowledge retrieval (e.g., Glean)
