## Overview

- Duplicate pages with "webflow.firework.com" subdomain are being indexed, causing SEO concerns
- Two immediate actions: 1) Update robots.txt to stop crawling of webflow subdomain pages 2) Implement 301 redirects from webflow subdomain to main domain
- Complexity arises due to the current nginx proxy setup during transition phase
- CMS access and publishing process on Webflow explained to the content team

---

## Key Topics

### SEO Issue: Duplicate Pages

  - Webflow migration creating duplicate pages with "webflow.firework.com" subdomain
  - \~7-8 pages currently indexed by Google on the webflow subdomain
  - Concern: Duplicate content, potential loss of ranking history

### Proposed Solutions

1.  Update robots.txt:
    
      - Prevent crawling of webflow.firework.com pages
      - Cameron to implement ASAP

2.  301 Redirects:
    
      - Redirect webflow.firework.com pages to corresponding firework.com URLs
      - Challenges due to nginx proxy setup
      - Options discussed:
        a. JavaScript redirect (window.location.replace)
        b. Webflow's built-in redirect functionality with regex
      - Concerns about search engine interpretation of JavaScript redirects

3.  Canonical URLs:
    
      - Mentioned but noted as not a directive, may be ignored by Google

### Technical Setup Explanation

  - Current setup uses nginx proxy
  - Fetches content from both Webflow and WordPress during transition
  - Goal: Eventually transition fully to Webflow and remove WordPress/nginx proxy

### Webflow CMS Access and Publishing

  - Process for accessing Webflow CMS demonstrated
  - Login URL: firework.com?edit
  - Navigation of Webflow interface for creating/editing blog posts shown
