## Overview

- AirOps introduced Co-pilot, a powerful AI assistant for workflow comprehension and troubleshooting
- New home and workspace organization launched, featuring improved navigation and folder structure
- GXL's training approach combines AirOps Academy, webinars, and hands-on practice with simple workflows
- Permissions and sharing improvements are on AirOps' roadmap, with GXL emphasizing need for granular access control

---

## Key Topics

### Co-pilot Feature Demo

  - AI-powered assistant for building and navigating workflows
  - Provides context-aware explanations of workflow functions
  - Assists in troubleshooting by identifying and fixing errors in code steps
  - Future capabilities to include generating new workflow steps based on user descriptions

### New Home and Workspace Organization

  - Improved navigation with tabs for grids and workflows
  - Enhanced information display: icons, version info, total runs, last edited details
  - Introduced folder structure for better organization, replacing tags
  - Toggle option available to switch between new and old interfaces

### Training and Education Feedback

  - GXL's approach:
      - Utilizes AirOps Academy and webinars
      - Assigns new team members to build simple workflows
      - Places newcomers in supporting account manager roles
  - Team found AirOps training "quite good" and appreciated weekly check-ins
  - AirOps to include demo videos for each product update in the future

### Challenges and Improvements

  - Occasional error messages in workflow execution (frequency and cause unclear)
  - Co-pilot suggested as a tool for understanding and resolving errors
  - GXL team generally finds AirOps intuitive and reliable

### Feature Requests: Permissions and Sharing

  - GXL needs more granular access control:
      - Full access for full-time employees
      - Limited access for freelancers (e.g., view/edit grids only, no workflow access)
  - AirOps acknowledges the request, noting it's on their roadmap
  - Collaboration features (like content approval process) being considered
