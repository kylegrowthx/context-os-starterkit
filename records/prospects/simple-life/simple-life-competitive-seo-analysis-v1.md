## Executive Summary

Simple.Life already ranks better than most of its direct IF competitors in organic search. With 17,800 keywords and 29,156 monthly organic traffic, Simple outperforms Zero (10,556), Fastic (18,966), and Lasta (13,518) — the three closest direct rivals. That's the good news.

The bad news: 72% of Simple's organic traffic goes to the homepage. Strip out branded search, and Simple's blog content drives roughly 8,200 monthly visits from ~50 indexed pages — a content efficiency of ~164 visits per page. That's decent for a young app site, but it's 10x below what the best content-first sites achieve in this space.

**Three things stand out:**

1. **The "does X break a fast" content cluster is Simple's strongest organic play — and it's barely tapped.** Simple already ranks #1 for "does heavy cream break a fast" (390 vol) and "does ag1 break a fast" (480 vol). Zero, Fastic, and Autumn Elle Nutrition collectively prove there are 50+ variations of this question, each driving 100-1,000+ monthly visits. Simple has ~15 pages in this cluster. There should be 40+.

2. **Calculator and tool pages are the highest-ROI content type in the entire landscape.** Ruled.me's keto calculator alone drives 8,299 monthly visits (15.5% of their total traffic). Nutrisense's blood sugar chart page drives 41,018 visits (26% of their total). Simple has zero calculator or tool pages. An IF schedule calculator, a fasting zone tracker, or a calorie deficit calculator could each become top-5 traffic drivers overnight.

3. **The content sites demolishing Simple's direct competitors are doing it with food lists, recipes, and diet guides — not fasting content.** Diet Doctor (135K traffic), Perfect Keto (99K traffic), and Ruled.me (53K traffic) all outperform Simple by 2-5x using food/recipe/diet-plan content. Simple's highest-traffic blog post is already "keto diet meal plan for beginners" at 789 visits. This cluster has 10-50x more room to grow.

**Quantified opportunity:** Based on competitor benchmarks, a focused content program of 80-100 new pages across the six highest-performing clusters could add 25,000-45,000 monthly organic visits within 12 months — roughly doubling Simple's current organic traffic.

### Key Observations

**Simple beats all direct IF competitors in organic search.** Among apps built specifically around intermittent fasting (Zero, Fastic, BodyFast, DoFasting, Lasta, Unimeal), Simple has the highest organic traffic at 29,156. Fastic is the closest at 18,966. This is a meaningful lead — but it's driven almost entirely by branded search, not content.

**The real competition is content-first sites, not apps.** Diet Doctor (135K), Perfect Keto (99K), Dr. Berg (89K), Ruled.me (53K), and Bulletproof (52K) all generate 2-5x Simple's traffic through content programs, not app downloads. These sites capture the informational and consideration-stage searches that eventually lead users to apps like Simple.

**Lose It! and SnapCalorie are the SEO outliers.** Lose It! (440K traffic from 368K keywords) and SnapCalorie (187K from 681K keywords) have massive organic footprints — but driven by programmatic content at scale (food nutrition pages), not editorial blog content. This is a fundamentally different strategy (more on this in Part 4).

**Simple has the highest traffic value per visit among direct IF competitors.** At $1.86/visit, Simple's traffic is worth more than Fastic ($0.37), Zero ($0.26), or Lasta ($2.84). This reflects the commercial intent of branded search — people searching for "simple weight loss app" are ready to buy.

**Lasta is the most interesting direct competitor to watch.** With 16,859 keywords and $38,445 traffic value but only 13,518 traffic, Lasta ranks for a lot of keywords at low positions. Their content efficiency is improving rapidly — they're investing heavily in IF content similar to Simple's.

## Appendix C: Methodology

**Data source:** SEMRush API via MCP, US database, February 2026.

**Reports used:**
- `domain_rank` — Domain-level overview metrics (rank, keywords, traffic, traffic value, backlinks)
- `domain_organic_unique` — Top pages by organic traffic (URL, keyword count, traffic, traffic %)
- `domain_organic` — Full keyword list with positions, volumes, KD, CPC (for Simple.life deep audit)

**Domains analyzed:** 28 competitors across 4 categories (direct IF apps, adjacent nutrition/weight loss apps, IF/fasting expert sites, content/media sites under 1M traffic). 2 domains returned no US data (reversehealth.com, eatingacademy.com — replaced with peterattiamd.com).

**Traffic estimation model methodology:**
- Conservative scenario benchmarks against autumnellenutrition.com (~118 traffic per content page)
- Moderate scenario benchmarks against zerolongevity.com for fasting content (~388 per page) and median competitor efficiency for food/tool content
- Aggressive scenario benchmarks against fastic.com top content (~700 per page) and ruled.me tool pages (5,000-8,000)
- Timeline projections assume typical SEO indexation velocity: new pages begin ranking within 4-8 weeks, reach stable positions within 3-6 months

**Frameworks applied:**
- Content clustering methodology (cross-competitor top page analysis, grouped by content type and topic)
- Per-page efficiency benchmarking (traffic / content pages, normalized by domain authority tier)
- Keyword tier analysis (page 1 / striking distance / poor positioning)
- Competitive gap analysis (Simple vs. direct IF median vs. content site median)

**Limitations:**
- SEMRush estimated traffic is directional, not precise. Actual traffic from Google Analytics would likely differ.
- No Google Search Console data available — cannot assess actual impressions, CTR, or engagement.
- No backlink profile analysis was performed. Would require separate SEMRush backlink report.
- No technical/Core Web Vitals audit was performed.
- AEO visibility was not directly tested.
- App-dominant competitors (BodyFast, Fastic, Unimeal) may have larger audiences than website traffic suggests — most of their engagement happens in-app.
