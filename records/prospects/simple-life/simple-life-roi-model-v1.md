## Executive Summary

Simple.Life's blog currently drives approximately **59 purchases/month** from 8,650 organic visits through the download → purchase funnel. The goal: **10x that to ~590 purchases/month** from incremental traffic our content program generates.

**The path gets there in 12-18 months across three channels:**

| Channel | Month 12 Visits | Month 12 Purchases | Month 18 Visits | Month 18 Purchases |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Incremental organic search | +35,000 | 239 | +48,000 | 328 |
| AEO referral traffic (from zero) | +8,000 | 109 | +18,000 | 246 |
| Brand lift from incremental awareness | +6,000 | 94 | +14,500 | 226 |
| **Total incremental** | **+49,000** | **442** | **+80,500** | **800** |
| **Multiple vs. current (59/mo)** | | **8.5x** | | **14.6x** |

The moderate scenario reaches **8.5x at month 12** and **crosses the 10x threshold at month 14**. The aggressive scenario (higher content volume + faster AEO ramp) reaches 10x by month 11-12.

**The AEO unlock is the swing factor.** Simple currently has **zero AI referral traffic** because the site was blocking AI indexation. Removing that block and optimizing content for AI citations creates an entirely new channel that converts at 2-3x the rate of organic search. This single change — going from blocked to optimized — could be worth 150-250 incremental purchases/month by month 12.

**The investment comparison:** At ~$30K/month engagement cost, the month-12 projection of 442 incremental purchases equals ~$68 per content-attributed purchase — dropping to $38 by month 18. Compare that to Meta ads where CPA is rising against a $1M/month budget. The organic channel compounds (each month gets cheaper per purchase), while the paid channel is doing the opposite.

## Part 2: The Funnel Model — Conversion Rates by Channel

Not all traffic converts equally. The three channels in our model have different conversion characteristics.

### Channel conversion rates

| Channel | Visit-to-Download | Download-to-Purchase | Effective Visit-to-Purchase | Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Organic search (blog content)** | 17.5% | 3.9% | **0.68%** | Stated rates. Content traffic has lower intent than branded search. |
| **AEO referral traffic** | 35% | 3.9% | **1.37%** | AI pre-qualifies intent — users arrive because an AI recommended Simple in response to a specific question. Research shows AI referrals convert at 2-5x organic. Conservative 2x on download rate. |
| **Brand search (lift)** | 40% | 3.9% | **1.56%** | Brand searchers are the highest-intent traffic. They specifically searched for Simple. The site-wide 44% download rate is mostly brand traffic. Applying slight discount for new brand-aware visitors. |
| **Quiz-originated** | 17.5% | 1.5% | **0.26%** | Lower purchase rate but 2-3x higher LTV ($140-210). If quiz traffic increases, model the higher LTV separately. |

### Why AEO converts higher

When someone asks ChatGPT "what's the best intermittent fasting app?" and it recommends Simple, that visitor arrives with:
- Pre-qualified intent (they're looking for an IF app)
- Third-party endorsement (the AI recommended it, not an ad)
- Context (they know what Simple does before they click)

Research across B2B shows AI referral traffic converts at 14.2% vs. 2.8% for organic (5x). For a consumer app the multiple is more conservative (2x on download rate), but the directional advantage is the same: AI-referred visitors are warmer than organic search visitors.

### Revenue per visit by channel

| Channel | Visit-to-Purchase | LTV | Revenue per visit |
|---|---|---|---|
| Organic search | 0.68% | $70 | $0.48 |
| AEO referral | 1.37% | $70 | $0.96 |
| Brand search | 1.56% | $70 | $1.09 |
| Quiz-driven (any channel) | 0.26% | $175 (2.5x) | $0.46 |

Brand search traffic is worth **2.3x** more per visit than organic search. AEO referral traffic is worth **2.0x** more. This is why the three-channel model matters — it's not just about traffic volume, it's about the quality and conversion premium of each channel.

### Model B: AEO Referral Traffic (Starting from Zero)

This is the biggest unlock. Simple was **blocking AI indexation**. Removing that block opens an entirely new channel.

**The opportunity:**

The intermittent fasting space is one of the most heavily queried categories in AI. People constantly ask:
- "What's the best intermittent fasting app?"
- "Does [food] break a fast?"
- "What intermittent fasting schedule should I do?"
- "Is intermittent fasting safe for [demographic]?"

Reddit's r/intermittentfasting (2M+ members) is the most-cited community source in this space, and Reddit accounts for 40.1% of all AI citations. Simple's content — if properly optimized and unblocked — will compete for these citations.

**AEO ramp assumptions:**

| Phase | Timeline | What Happens | Monthly AI Referral Visits |
|---|---|---|---|
| Unblock + enable | Month 1 | Remove AI blocking, submit sitemaps, enable crawling | 0 (indexing) |

| Mature channel | Month 13-18 | AI adoption continues growing. Simple's citation rate and traffic compound. | 12,000-25,000 |

**Conservative vs. the market:** The broader market is growing AI referral traffic at 527% YoY. We're projecting much more conservatively (going from 0 to ~8,000-12,000 in 12 months) because Simple is starting from a complete block state. But the upside is massive — they have zero competition for their own content in AI right now.

**AEO-to-purchases formula:**

```
Monthly AI purchases = AI referral visits x 35% download rate x 3.9% purchase rate
```

At 8,000 AI visits/month (month 12): 8,000 × 0.35 × 0.039 = **109 purchases/month**

### Model C: Brand Lift from Incremental Awareness

Every incremental visitor — whether from search or AI — has a probability of becoming a future brand searcher. Brand search traffic converts at the highest rate.

**How it works:**

1. Content drives 43,000 new incremental visitors in month 8 (search + AI combined)
2. A percentage of those visitors become aware of Simple
3. Some fraction search for "simple fasting app" or "simple.life" later (1-3 month delay)
4. Those brand searches convert at 1.56% visit-to-purchase (the highest rate)

**Brand lift assumptions:**

| Variable | Value | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| % of incremental visitors who become brand-aware | 20% | Conservative. Many are just reading a "does X break a fast" article. But they see the brand. |
| % of brand-aware who eventually brand-search | 15% | Of those who become aware, 15% will search for Simple within 3 months |
| Effective brand lift rate | 3% of incremental traffic → brand searches | 20% × 15% = 3% |
| Delay | 2 months average | Brand recall typically lags 1-3 months |

**Additional AEO brand amplification:** When AI engines mention Simple in response to "best intermittent fasting app" — even without a click — that's brand exposure. With 89% of health-conscious consumers using AI assistants, every AI citation is a brand impression. This amplifies the brand lift rate beyond what organic search alone delivers.

Brand search volume also correlates with AI visibility (0.334 coefficient per Evertune research). More brand searches → higher AI visibility → more citations → more brand exposure. Self-reinforcing loop.

## Part 7: What Makes This Work — The Key Levers

### Lever 1: Calculator/tool pages (highest single-page ROI)

From the competitive analysis: Ruled.me's keto calculator alone drives 8,299 visits/month (15.5% of their total traffic). Simple has zero tool pages.

Four tool pages could add 10,000 visits/month — more than the entire current blog. These are the highest-priority content items because:
- Single pages driving massive traffic
- High engagement (people spend time using calculators)
- Natural app download CTAs ("Get personalized results in the Simple app")
- AI citation magnets (tools get cited heavily in AI responses)

### Lever 2: Unblocking AI indexation (zero → something)

This is the single highest-leverage technical change. Simple is currently invisible to every AI engine. In a category where people constantly ask AI about fasting, diets, and apps, Simple doesn't exist.

Removing the block and optimizing for AI citations creates an entirely new channel with 2x conversion rates. The competitive analysis shows Simple already has authoritative content in the "does X break a fast" cluster — content that AI engines would readily cite if they could access it.

### Lever 3: "Does X break a fast" expansion (proven pattern)

Simple already ranks #1 for "does heavy cream break a fast" and "does AG1 break a fast." The pattern is proven, KD is under 20, and there are 20+ untapped variants. This is the lowest-risk, fastest-to-rank content cluster.

Each page is also an ideal AI citation target — these are exactly the questions people ask AI engines.

### Lever 4: Refresh program (improving the baseline)

120 existing pages are actively driving results. A systematic refresh program (updating content, adding schema, cross-linking to new content) could lift the existing baseline by 30%+ over 12 months. That's ~2,600 additional monthly visits from zero new pages.

### Lever 5: Cross-linking density

As the content library grows from ~120 pages to 200+, the interlinking opportunity multiplies. Every new page can link to existing content, and every refresh can link to new content. This improves:
- Crawl depth and indexation speed
- Topical authority signals
- User engagement (lower bounce rate, more pages per session)
- AI model understanding of Simple's content graph

**Highest-sensitivity variables:** Visit-to-download rate and content volume have the largest impact. These are also the most controllable — we control content production, and we can influence download conversion through better CTAs, page design, and offer matching.

### What could accelerate the model

1. **Quiz funnel optimization:** If we can route more traffic through the quiz (1.5% purchase but 2-3x LTV), revenue per visit increases significantly even if purchase count stays flat.
2. **Seasonal surge:** January is peak for health/fitness. If the content program is ramped by December, the January surge could deliver an outsized month.
3. **AEO growing faster than projected:** We're projecting conservatively (0 to 8K in 12 months). If AI adoption in health/wellness accelerates (likely — 527% YoY market growth), the AI channel could be 2x our projection.
4. **Reddit strategy:** With r/intermittentfasting at 2M+ members and Reddit accounting for 40.1% of AI citations, an active Reddit presence amplifies both direct traffic and AI visibility.
5. **Third-party citations:** Getting Simple cited on review sites (G2 for apps, listicle articles, expert blogs) drives both direct referral traffic and increases AI citation rates.

### What could slow the model

1. **Technical SEO issues:** If the site has crawl issues, Core Web Vitals problems, or indexation delays beyond what's expected.
2. **Content quality misses:** If content doesn't match user intent or isn't differentiated from competitors, per-page performance will underperform projections.
3. **Google algorithm volatility:** Major algorithm updates could shift rankings. Mitigated by building genuine topical authority across multiple clusters.
4. **Lower-than-expected conversion rates:** If the 15-20% visit-to-download rate is optimistic for content traffic (it might be closer to 10-12% for purely informational content), the purchase projections would need to come down ~30%.
5. **Competitive response:** If Fastic, Zero, or Lasta aggressively invest in content during the same period, per-page performance may be lower than competitor benchmarks suggest.

## Appendix: The Math Behind the Model

### Organic search purchase calculation

```
Monthly organic purchases = New pages × Visits/page × Maturation % × Visit-to-download × Download-to-purchase
```

Example (Month 6, Cluster 1 — "break a fast" pages, 25 pages published by month 4):
- 25 pages × 120 visits/page × 100% matured × 17.5% download × 3.9% purchase = 20 purchases

### AEO purchase calculation

```
Monthly AEO purchases = AI referral visits × AI download rate × Download-to-purchase
```

Example (Month 12):
- 8,000 × 35% × 3.9% = 109 purchases

### Brand lift purchase calculation

```
Monthly brand purchases = (Previous month total incremental visits × 3% brand conversion rate)
                          × Brand download rate × Download-to-purchase
```

Example (Month 12, based on month 10 traffic of 45,700):
- 45,700 × 3% × 40% × 3.9% = 21 purchases from brand lift

Note: brand lift is cumulative — each month's new brand-aware visitors stack on previous months'. The brand lift number in the projection table reflects the cumulative effect, not just the marginal month.

### Total model

```
Total monthly purchases = Existing baseline (59)
                        + Organic search purchases (new pages × conversion)
                        + Organic search purchases (refreshed pages × lift × conversion)
                        + AEO purchases (AI referrals × higher conversion)
                        + Brand lift purchases (cumulative awareness × brand conversion)
```
