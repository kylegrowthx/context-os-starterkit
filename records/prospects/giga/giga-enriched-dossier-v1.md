## What Giga Actually Does

### Core Product
Multi-modal AI agent platform that deploys agents across **voice, chat, email, text** with built-in analytics, QA, and insights modules. The core offering is an end-to-end agent lifecycle: deploy, analyze, optimize across any channel.

### Planned Add-Ons (not yet formalized)
- **Browser agent** product
- **Voice navigation** product
- **Compliance** product (not yet launched or publicly discussed)

### Strategic Vision — Agent Infrastructure Layer
Morgan's key narrative: Giga is building the **infrastructure layer between LLM models and applications**. The AI stack goes: Energy → Chips → Cloud → Models → **[Agent Infrastructure]** → Apps.

This layer provides:
- **Agent Ops** (like DevOps but for agents) — visibility into agent behavior, token usage, model performance, modality performance, GPU consumption
- **Governance & observability** — role-based access, data controls, executive-level dashboards
- **Model orchestration** — agnostic across any model provider (OpenAI, Anthropic, 11Labs, etc.), optimize which model for which use case
- **Model optimization** — LoRA (Low-Rank Adaptation), inference optimization, post-training patching to specialize models for enterprise needs
- **Efficiency across the full stack** — agents need to be efficient across models, cloud, and GPUs; without this, "the whole system just locks up" as scale increases

**Morgan's pitch**: "Everyone's doing wrong is they're just saying let's get agents live, but they're not looking at efficiency across the whole stack."

### Why This Matters (Morgan's framing)
- As enterprises deploy more agents, compute demands will spike — Anthropic already shut off some heavy users
- Models need continuous adaptation via inference/LoRA — not just initial training
- Without infrastructure visibility, companies have "no real idea what's happening with agent swarms"
- Companies like Cresta and Parloa "just die off — they just become a CX company, no one cares"

## Sales Intelligence

### Win Factors
1. **Time to value** — deploy in weeks vs. 2-6+ months for competitors; "a month is a year in this space"
2. **Voice channel excellence** — started as voice-first company; hardest channel to get right; strong on latency
3. **Low latency** — driven by predictive RAG and AI-written policies
4. **Low hallucination rate** — aspirational target <0.1% (current LLMs at 3-4%); critical for regulated industries
5. **Complex use case strength** — reportedly "really strong when it comes to complex use cases" vs. simple transactional ones
6. **Undefeated record** — sales team hasn't lost a single deal where they had a seat at the table

3. **Commercial inflexibility** — Kyle says he can fix this

5. **The Sierra safety play** — "I won't lose my job if I go with Sierra" mentality from CTOs
