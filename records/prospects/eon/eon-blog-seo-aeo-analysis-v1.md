## Executive Summary

Eon's blog is in its infancy from an organic search perspective. With 102 ranking keywords driving an estimated 26 monthly visits across 24 indexed pages, the blog currently accounts for roughly 5% of total domain organic traffic (~515/month). The homepage dominates at 80%.

This isn't surprising for a company founded in January 2024. What matters is the foundation being laid — and where the compounding opportunities are.

**Three things stand out:**
1. Eon already owns page-1 positions for its category-defining term ("cbpm") and several multi-cloud backup keywords. These are the seeds of topical authority.
2. The biggest traffic opportunity is hiding in plain sight: the AWS S3 backup page ranks for 16 keywords including "s3 backup" (480 monthly searches) but sits at positions 42-59. Moving that page to page 1 alone would multiply blog traffic.
3. Eon's blog has almost zero AEO readiness. In a market where 90% of B2B buyers use generative AI during evaluation, that's a gap that matters more than any individual keyword ranking.

## Part 3: Applying the SEO Ranking Factors Framework

Using the evidence-based priority stack from the GrowthX SEO ranking factors guide, here's how Eon's blog performs against what Google actually measures.

### Tier 1: Foundation (Must Have)

**Content relevance and depth — Score: 2/5 (Below average)**

Text relevance appears in 90.6% of top-10 results (Semrush). Eon's pages that rank well (ransomware-package-overview, multi-cloud-backups-basics) likely match intent, but 15 of 24 indexed pages generate zero traffic. That's a signal of thin or misaligned content. The AWS S3 backup page ranks for 16 keywords but can't crack the top 30 for any of them — depth is almost certainly the issue.

**E-E-A-T signals — Score: Unknown (needs manual audit)**

Key questions:
- Do blog posts have named authors with credentials in cloud infrastructure/backup?
- Are there case studies with specific customer data (e.g., SoFi)?
- Does content demonstrate firsthand experience with the technology?
- Are claims supported by original data or testing?

Google's December 2025 Core Update gave 23% gains to sites demonstrating experience and expertise. For a company defining a new category (CBPM), E-E-A-T signals are especially important — Eon needs to demonstrate they're not just marketing a concept but have deep technical authority.

**Technical health — Not assessed in this report**

Requires PageSpeed Insights, Core Web Vitals data, and crawl analysis. Pages with LCP under 1.0s rank 7.5 positions higher on average. Given Eon is a cloud-native company, technical performance should be strong, but it needs verification.

### Tier 2: Amplifiers

**Topical authority — Score: 2/5 (Early stage)**

Google's leaked API confirms siteFocusScore and siteRadius — they literally measure how concentrated a site's content is around core topics. Eon's blog has the beginning of topical clusters but hasn't committed to the architecture.

Current cluster seeds:
- **Multi-cloud backup** (3 pages): multi-cloud-backups-basics, multi-cloud-backup-resilience, five-ways-to-improve-cloud-backup-strategy
- **Ransomware/security** (3 pages): ransomware-package-overview, cloud-ransomware-guide, aws-cloud-ransomware
- **AWS services** (4 pages): aws-s3-backup, amazon-redshift-backup, amazon-eks-backup, amazon-dynamodb-backup, deployed-aws
- **CBPM/posture management** (1 page): cloud-backup-posture-management

The evidence is strong: content grouped into clusters drives ~30% more organic traffic and holds rankings 2.5x longer (HireGrowth, 2025). HubSpot saw a 43% traffic increase from implementing topic clusters. Pages with high topical authority gain traffic 57% faster (Graphite, 2024).

**Eon's opportunity:** The multi-cloud-backups-basics page already ranks positions 2-4 for its core cluster. That's proof the approach works. But there's no pillar page architecture, no systematic internal linking, and the cluster doesn't go deep enough to signal comprehensive expertise.

**Backlinks — Not assessed in this report**

Would require Ahrefs/Semrush backlink analysis. However, the site's overall authority is clearly low given the domain age (founded Jan 2024). PageRank measures "distance from a known good source" (confirmed under oath). Eon's Gartner Cool Vendor recognition and Series D coverage from TechCrunch/etc. should generate some quality editorial links, but blog-specific backlink building is likely minimal.

**User satisfaction signals — Cannot assess without GSC data**

NavBoost uses 13 months of rolling click data tracking "goodClicks," "badClicks," and "lastLongestClicks." Without Google Search Console access, we can't evaluate CTR performance or engagement patterns for Eon's blog content.

### Tier 3: Competitive Edge

**Brand signals — Score: 3/5 (Growing fast)**

Branded search is a strong trust signal. Eon's homepage captures 160 keywords and 413 monthly visits — most of that is likely branded traffic driven by $500M in funding, $4B valuation, and significant press coverage. CRN, Gartner, and tech press all amplify the brand.

The gap: brand strength hasn't transferred to the blog. The blog doesn't leverage the company's brand momentum to build content authority.

**Schema and structured data — Unknown (needs audit)**

Rich results drive 82% higher CTR. Schema markup is increasingly important for AI citation. Would need to crawl the blog to assess.

**Content freshness — Score: 2/5**

85% of AI Overview citations were published in the last two years. Eon's blog content is all recent (the company is <2 years old), but the "2025-state-cloud-backup" page is already dated — it references "2025" when we're in Feb 2026.

## Part 5: AEO Readiness Assessment

This is where the real strategic gap lives. Applying the AEO buyer evaluation playbook.

### Why This Matters for Eon Right Now

- 90% of B2B buyers use generative AI during evaluation (Forrester)
- 95% of the time, the winning vendor is already on the buyer's Day One shortlist (6sense)
- Traffic from AI platforms grew 527% YoY in 2025
- Pages cited in AI Overviews earn 35% more organic clicks than uncited competitors

Eon competes against Veeam, Rubrik, Commvault, Cohesity, and Druva. Every one of those competitors has massive content libraries, years of review data on G2/TrustRadius, extensive third-party coverage, and the brand signals that AI engines prioritize when generating recommendations.

When a buyer prompts "best cloud backup for enterprise" or "Rubrik vs alternatives," Eon needs to show up. Right now, it almost certainly doesn't.

### The Evaluation-Stage Prompts Eon Should Be Tracking

Using the six prompt categories from the buyer evaluation playbook:

**1. Direct Comparison Prompts:**
- "Eon vs Rubrik"
- "Eon vs Veeam for cloud backup"
- "Eon vs Commvault for multi-cloud"
- "Eon vs Druva for AWS backup"
- "Rubrik vs Veeam vs Eon"

**2. Alternatives-To Prompts:**
- "Alternatives to Veeam for cloud-native backup"
- "Alternatives to Rubrik for multi-cloud"
- "Best Commvault alternatives for cloud backup"
- "Cloud-native alternative to Cohesity"

**3. Category + Use Case Prompts:**
- "Best cloud backup for enterprise AWS"
- "Best cloud-native backup platform for multi-cloud"
- "Best backup solution for ransomware protection"
- "Best cloud backup with data lake capabilities"
- "Best agentless cloud backup for large enterprises"

**4. Review & Reputation Prompts:**
- "Eon backup reviews"
- "Is Eon good for enterprise cloud backup?"
- "Eon cloud backup pros and cons"

**5. Pricing & Cost Prompts:**
- "Eon backup pricing"
- "Cloud backup cost comparison Eon vs Veeam"
- "Most cost-effective enterprise cloud backup"

**6. Feature & Integration Prompts:**
- "Does Eon support Azure and GCP backup?"
- "Which cloud backup has the best ransomware protection?"
- "Cloud backup with data lake export capabilities"
- "Best CBPM platform"

### Content Gaps for AEO

Using the Content Gap Loop from the buyer evaluation playbook:

| Gap | Content Action |
|---|---|
| Not appearing for "X vs Y" comparisons | Eon has /compare/eon-vs-commvault but needs pages for every major competitor: vs Rubrik, vs Veeam, vs Druva, vs Cohesity, vs AWS Backup |
| Not appearing for "alternatives to [competitor]" | Create content explicitly positioning Eon as the cloud-native alternative to each legacy vendor |
| Not appearing for "best cloud backup for [use case]" | Industry-specific landing pages: healthcare (HIPAA), financial services (SOC2), SaaS companies |
| Limited review/third-party presence | TrustRadius, Capterra, PeerSpot profiles are missing. G2 profile is thin. These directly feed AI responses |
| No structured comparison data | Comparison tables with schema markup that AI engines can extract and cite |

### AEO Content Formatting Requirements

Per the ranking factors guide, semantic completeness (r=0.87) and multi-modal content (r=0.92) are the strongest AI citation signals. Eon's blog content needs:

1. **Answer-first formatting.** Lead with the core claim in the first 40-60 words. 44% of AI citations come from the first 30% of content.
2. **Headers as questions.** Rewrite H2s to match how buyers actually prompt AI (e.g., "How does CBPM differ from traditional backup management?" not "CBPM Overview").
3. **Standalone sections.** AI extracts passages, not full pages. Each section should make sense if pulled out of context.
4. **Structured data.** Comparison tables, pros/cons lists, FAQ blocks. Implement schema markup (Product, FAQ, Comparison, Review). Structured data drives 300% higher AI comprehension.
5. **Multi-modal content.** Architecture diagrams, product screenshots, video demos. Pages combining text + images + video + structured data see 156% higher AI Overview selection rates.

Content refresh + depth expansion on existing pages.

- [ ] **Update "2025 State of Cloud Backup" to 2026.** Content with a stale year in the title loses freshness signals.

**Strategic investments (new content):**

- [ ] **Build the CBPM pillar page.** Eon is defining this category. The current page (6 keywords, 5 traffic) is a thin seed. Create the definitive 3,000+ word guide. Target: "cbpm" (210 vol), "cloud backup posture management," "autonomous backup management," "cloud backup security" (170 vol).
- [ ] **Build proper topic cluster architecture** for all five clusters identified in Part 4. Internal linking between cluster pages and pillar pages.
- [ ] **Create competitor comparison pages.** Eon vs Rubrik, Eon vs Veeam, Eon vs Druva, Eon vs Cohesity. These serve both SEO and AEO — comparison content is the most common evaluation-stage prompt type.
- [ ] **Create "alternatives to" content.** "Best Veeam alternatives for cloud-native backup," "Rubrik alternatives," etc. These are high-intent, high-conversion prompts in AI search.

### Layer 3: Authority (Compound Over Time)

- [ ] **Build third-party review presence.** Eon has no TrustRadius profile, no Capterra profile, no PeerSpot profile. G2 profile is thin. 85% of brand mentions in AI come from third-party pages. These directly feed AI responses.
- [ ] **Pursue editorial backlinks.** Leverage Gartner Cool Vendor recognition, Series D coverage, and CBPM category creation narrative for earned media links from cloud/enterprise tech publications.
- [ ] **Publish customer case studies.** SoFi is a notable reference. Named customers with quantified ROI provide both E-E-A-T signals and AI-citable content.

### Layer 4: AI Visibility (Emerging, Monitor and Invest)

- [ ] **Implement AEO prompt tracking.** Build a 50-prompt library using the six categories above. Run weekly against ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, Claude. Measure inclusion rate, share of voice, and sentiment.
- [ ] **Restructure existing content for AI extraction.** Answer-first formatting, question-based headings, standalone sections, schema markup.
- [ ] **Create FAQ schema pages** for high-value evaluation queries. "Does Eon support Azure?" "What is CBPM?" "How does Eon compare to Veeam?" FAQ schema drives 300% higher AI comprehension.
- [ ] **Monitor Wikipedia/Wikidata presence.** 22% of LLM training data comes from Wikipedia. Eon may be notable enough for a Wikipedia entry given its $4B valuation and Gartner recognition.
