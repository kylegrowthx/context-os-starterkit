## 1. Company Overview: Eon.io

**Eon** is a cloud-native, autonomous backup platform that converts static cloud snapshots into live, queryable data lakes. The company introduced the concept of **Cloud Backup Posture Management (CBPM)** — a new category it is defining — which automatically discovers, classifies, and enforces backup policies across AWS, Azure, and Google Cloud environments.

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Founded** | January 2024 |
| **HQ** | New York, NY (Israeli founding team) |
| **Total Funding** | $500M ($20M Seed → $30M Series A → $77M Series B → $70M Series C → $300M Series D) |
| **Valuation** | $4 billion (Dec 2025) |
| **Investors** | Sequoia Capital, Lightspeed Venture Partners, Greenoaks, BOND, Elad Gil / Gil Capital |
| **Revenue** | Tripled YoY; undisclosed exact figure |
| **Customers** | "A few tens" of large enterprises including SoFi |
| **Leadership** | Ofir Ehrlich (CEO), Gonen Stein (President), Ron Kimchi (CTO), Avi Biton (CFO), Moshe Milman (CSO) |
| **Key Differentiators** | Agentless CBPM, global search across all backups, granular file/record restore, backup data → AI-ready data lake (Parquet, Delta Lake, Iceberg), ransomware anomaly detection, multi-cloud (AWS/Azure/GCP) |

### Eon's Core Capabilities
- **Autonomous Resource Discovery & Classification** — Scans cloud environments, maps infrastructure, classifies resources without manual tagging
- **Self-Driving Posture Management** — Automatically assigns backup policies and retention periods
- **Global Search** — Unified search across all backup data, all clouds, all providers
- **Granular Restore** — Individual file, table, or record-level restoration without full environment recovery
- **New Storage Tier** — Compression + deduplication; claims 30–50% cost savings over native cloud snapshots
- **Ransomware Protection** — Anomaly detection, clean version hold, guided rollback
- **Open Data Formats** — Export backup data as Parquet, Delta Lake, Iceberg for analytics/AI

## 2. Market Categories & Buying Categories

### Primary Market
Eon operates in the **Cloud Backup & Data Protection** market.

**Market Size:**
- Cloud Backup Market: **$7.13B in 2026**, growing at **24.84% CAGR** to $21.62B by 2031 (Mordor Intelligence)
- Broader Data Protection Market: **$79.6B in 2025**, projected $159.9B by 2033 at 9.1% CAGR
- Fortune Business Insights estimates the cloud backup market reaching **$51.57B by 2034**

### Key Market Dynamics

**Drivers:**
- Massive enterprise data growth (120 ZB created in 2024)
- Ransomware/cyber threat acceleration ($4.88M avg breach cost per IBM)
- Regulatory compliance demands (GDPR, HIPAA, CCPA)
- Multi-cloud adoption requiring unified protection
- AI/ML workloads requiring data accessibility from backups
- Shift from on-prem to BaaS (Gartner predicts 85% of large enterprises will use BaaS by 2029)

**Market Shifts:**
- Cyber resilience becoming "table stakes" — backup ≠ just recovery anymore
- AI-driven automation in backup operations
- Convergence of backup, security, and data management
- Cloud-native / SaaS-first architectures displacing legacy appliance models
- Open data formats and backup-to-data-lake pipelines emerging

## 4. Perception Score Methodology

Based on prior analysis framework, each competitor is scored on **15 dimensions** using a **1–10 scale** (10 = best in market). Scores are directional, synthesized from analyst reports, G2/TrustRadius reviews, Reddit/community sentiment, Forrester/Gartner positioning, funding trajectory, and market reputation.

**Dimensions:**
1. **Trust / Reliability** — Track record, uptime, data integrity reputation
2. **Innovation / Forward-Thinking** — Product velocity, category creation, R&D investment
3. **Ease of Use** — UI/UX quality, setup simplicity, learning curve
4. **Value for Money** — TCO, pricing transparency, ROI
5. **Customer Support Quality** — Review scores, response times, escalation effectiveness
6. **Security / Compliance** — Certifications, ransomware protection, zero-trust features
7. **Scalability** — Multi-cloud, enterprise-scale, global deployments
8. **Integration Capability** — Ecosystem breadth, API quality, third-party connectors
9. **Industry Expertise / Domain Knowledge** — Vertical specialization, use-case depth
10. **Thought Leadership** — Content quality, analyst engagement, conference presence
11. **Product Quality / Performance** — Backup speed, restore speed, reliability
12. **Speed / Time to Value** — Deployment time, onboarding, first-value milestones
13. **Transparency** — Pricing clarity, roadmap visibility, honest marketing
14. **Customer-Centricity** — NPS signals, product-led growth, responsiveness to feedback
15. **Modern / Contemporary vs Legacy** — Architecture modernity, cloud-native design, tech debt

### Composite Score Calculation
**Composite = Average of all 15 dimensions** (unweighted). Scores reflect market perception, not absolute product capability.

## 9. Market Category Emerging Category: Cloud Backup Posture Management (CBPM)

Eon is attempting to define **CBPM** as a net-new market category, mirroring the playbook of:
- **CSPM** (Cloud Security Posture Management) — pioneered by Palo Alto / Prisma Cloud, now a Gartner-recognized category
- **DSPM** (Data Security Posture Management) — pioneered by Cyera, Normalyze, BigID; recognized by Gartner in 2023

**CBPM Core Tenets:**
1. Automatic discovery of cloud resources requiring backup
2. Classification of resources by business criticality and compliance requirements
3. Policy-driven backup assignment (not manual tag-based)
4. Continuous drift detection and remediation
5. Posture scoring and compliance reporting

**Analyst Recognition Status:** Not yet formally recognized by Gartner, Forrester, or IDC as a distinct category. However, Gartner's 2025 MQ analysis emphasizes automated posture management and compliance-driven backup as growing evaluation criteria — suggesting CBPM concepts are gaining traction even if the label hasn't been formalized.
