## The Opportunity

Spendesk's organic traffic is declining. That's the headline. But the real problem is bigger than an SEO trend line.

Your buyers are changing how they discover and evaluate spend management software. CFOs are asking LLMs for shortlisted recommendations before handing research to their finance managers. If Spendesk isn't surfacing in those responses, you never enter the consideration set.

Three things make this the right moment to act:

1. **Your competitors are US-focused.** Ramp and Brex dominate total search volume, but their content is built for American audiences (IRS mileage rates, EIN numbers, ACH guides). The European competitors (Moss, Payhawk, Pleo) have thin content footprints. Nobody owns UK/European spend management content.

2. **Your content pace can't keep up.** At 1-2 articles per week, you can't reverse the traffic decline or compete with rivals publishing at 5-10x that volume. This engagement scales you to 5 articles per week through our pipeline.

3. **Your brand awareness is an asset, but it's eroding.** Years of strong marketing built real recognition. Without a content engine feeding it, that awareness fades. Content is how you stay top of mind, in search results and in LLM responses.

**Phase one** focuses on UK/English content. French localisation is planned for phase two, with 1-2 experimental French pieces during the first eight weeks to start training the pipeline.

## Who We're Writing For

### The Buying Committee

Most Spendesk deals (70-80%) involve three roles:

| Role | What They Do | What They Need |
|------|-------------|----------------|
| **Finance Manager / Controller** (Champion) | Discovers the solution, evaluates it, advocates internally. Lives in invoices, reconciliation, receipt-chasing, and month-end close. | Proof that things can be faster and more accurate without sacrificing control. |
| **Head of Finance / VP Finance** (Validator) | One level below CFO. Faces resource constraints and compliance pressure. | Confidence that the platform handles both speed and accuracy. |
| **CFO** (Economic Buyer) | Signs off. Increasingly uses LLMs for quick vendor summaries instead of doing deep research. | Clean tech stack integration. Seamless finance operations. |

### The Daily Users

| Role | Why They Matter |
|------|----------------|
| **Accountant / Bookkeeper** | Highest-touch platform user. Handles reconciliation, month-end, ongoing expense management. Their satisfaction drives word-of-mouth and G2 reviews. |
| **Budget-Owning Department Lead** | Not finance. Accountable for team budgets. Wants speed, mobile approvals, real-time visibility. Spendesk's highest NPS scores come from these users. |

### What Triggers a Purchase

Three moments push companies from "we have a problem" to "we need this now":

**"We're losing control."** The company wants visibility into spend without creating friction that slows the business. They can't keep manually approving every purchase, but they don't trust giving everyone cards with zero traceability.

**"We've outgrown manual."** What worked at 30 employees breaks at 100+. Month-end close stretches to a week. Data is scattered across spreadsheets and disconnected tools. Even a single penny off means staying late to find it.

**"There has to be a better way."** Accumulated frustration hits a tipping point. One too many painful month-end closes, plus noise about AI and automation, triggers a search. Spendesk's existing brand awareness means prospects have usually already heard of you.

## What Makes Spendesk Different

Five things came through clearly in our research:

1. **One connected ecosystem.** Everything related to spend management on one screen. Not a collection of point solutions stitched together.
2. **Genuinely easy to use.** The UI is clean and intuitive. Highest NPS scores come from non-finance users. That's rare.
3. **Control without bottlenecks.** Finance sets parameters and trusts the system. Decentralised spending, centralised visibility.
4. **Fast to deploy.** Quick implementation compared to heavier enterprise platforms.
5. **Finance becomes the enabler.** Procurement goes from 8 weeks to under 1. Finance stops being the team that slows everything down.

**The AI angle:** Spendesk has built strong AI brand association. The technology is machine learning (OCR, smart categorisation, pre-fill, duplicate detection), and customers perceive these features as AI. Content should lean into automation and intelligent efficiency without overclaiming.

### The Gap

The two largest players (Ramp, Brex) are entirely US-focused. They dominate on American tax rules, mileage rates, and banking infrastructure that doesn't apply to Spendesk's audience.

The European competitors (Moss, Pleo, Payhawk) have relevant audiences but thin content. Moss has 158 URLs. Pleo has 209. Neither covers multi-entity operations, procurement, or AP automation with any depth.

**Nobody owns the UK/European spend management content space.** That's the gap. Spendesk can build the definitive content hub for European finance teams.

## Content Strategy: 6 Topic Clusters

We built these clusters by cross-referencing three inputs:

1. **Competitor gaps** (what nobody owns in the European mid-market)
2. **Spendesk's product capabilities** (the 7-in-1 ecosystem, multi-entity design, AI-powered automation)
3. **The purchase triggers** from our kickoff (loss of control, month-end chaos, finance as bottleneck)

Each cluster targets the people Spendesk needs to reach (CFOs, Finance Managers, Controllers, Budget Owners) at the moments they're most likely to search.

---

**Why this is #1:** This is Spendesk's core story. The tension between wanting control and wanting to empower employees is the exact problem your platform solves. Only 48K in total search volume across all seven competitors. Nobody has built meaningful content here.

**The angle:** How modern finance teams balance control with autonomy. Not just corporate card programmes (what competitors cover), but the full ecosystem: approval workflows, spend policies, real-time dashboards, budget delegation. Every piece connects to the emotional win: finance teams don't want to be the bottleneck.

**Cluster 1 is first** because it's where Spendesk has the strongest right to win and the least competition. It also establishes the editorial foundation that all other clusters build on. **Cluster 4 drives the most traffic** but converts indirectly. It's a brand trust play that builds daily touchpoints with accountants and bookkeepers. Content here should funnel readers toward Clusters 1-3 via internal linking.

## What Spendesk Replaces

For content positioning, Spendesk typically replaces one of two scenarios:

**Scenario 1: Manual processes and spreadsheets.** Shared corporate cards with no traceability. Paper expense reports. Manual data entry. Spreadsheet reconciliation. Finance chasing receipts (recovering as little as 10% before automation). Procurement taking 8+ weeks.

**Scenario 2: Fragmented point solutions.** One tool for expenses, another for cards, another for invoices. Data stitched together manually at month-end. Reconciliation nightmares and data silos.

The pattern: companies have some way of managing individual spending components, but nothing connecting the full lifecycle from spend request through approval, payment, and accounting reconciliation.
