PREPARING FOR ORTHOPAEDIC SURGERY:
----------------------------------

At Premier Orthopaedics, thorough preparation is key to ensuring a successful orthopaedic surgery and smooth recovery. Here’s what you need to know leading up to your surgery:

### Pre-Surgery Preparation:

**Follow Pre-Surgery Instructions:** Adhere strictly to any pre-surgery instructions provided by your surgeon or healthcare team. This may include guidelines on fasting (no eating or drinking) for a specified period before surgery, typically starting at midnight(12 am) the night before your procedure.

**Medication Restrictions:** Follow your surgeon’s instructions regarding medication restrictions. Certain medications may need to be temporarily stopped before surgery to reduce the risk of bleeding or other complications. Below is a list to use as a guide. Make sure to inform your surgeon of all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking.

*   Nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs)
*   Blood thinners
*   Fish oil,
*   Bariatric medicine 

**Arrange Transportation and Support:** Plan for transportation to and from the hospital or surgical center on the day of your surgery. Arrange for a responsible adult to accompany you and provide support, especially if you will be receiving sedation or anesthesia.

**Prepare Your Home:** Make arrangements at home to ensure a comfortable and safe recovery environment. Stock up on groceries, household essentials, and any necessary medical supplies or equipment you may need during your recovery period.

**Communicate with Your Healthcare Team:** Keep open lines of communication with your surgeon and healthcare team. Inform them of any changes in your health status, including new symptoms or illnesses, leading up to your surgery.

24 HOURS BEFORE ORTHOPAEDIC SURGERY:
------------------------------------

As your surgery date approaches, here are some important steps to take 24 hours before your procedure:

**Finalize Preparations:** Double-check your pre-surgery instructions and ensure you understand and are following all guidelines provided by your surgeon. Confirm the time of your surgery and any pre-operative appointments or tests.

**Complete Pre-Surgery Checklist:** Review and complete any pre-surgery paperwork or consent forms required by the hospital or surgical center. Bring any necessary documents, identification, and insurance information with you on the day of surgery.

**Confirm Transportation and Support:** Confirm transportation arrangements for the day of surgery and ensure that your designated support person is available to accompany you. Arrange for someone to stay with you for the first 24 hours after surgery, as you may still be recovering from anesthesia and unable to drive.

**Prepare Your Personal Items:** Pack a small bag with essential personal items for your hospital stay, including comfortable clothing, toiletries, and any comfort items or entertainment devices to help pass the time during recovery.

**Follow Fasting Guidelines:** Follow the fasting guidelines provided by your surgeon or healthcare team. Typically, you will be instructed not to eat or drink anything after midnight on the night before your surgery. This includes food, beverages, chewing gum, and mints.

**Avoid Alcohol and Smoking:** Refrain from consuming alcohol or smoking cigarettes for at least 24 hours before your surgery. These substances can interfere with anesthesia and the healing process.

By following these instructions leading up to your orthopaedic surgery, you can help ensure a smooth and successful procedure and optimize your chances for a swift and effective recovery. If you have any questions or concerns, don’t hesitate to reach out to your surgeon or healthcare team for guidance and support.

---------------------

Following orthopaedic surgery, patients may experience:

*   Mild to moderate pain at the surgical site
*   Swelling and stiffness in the affected area
*   Limited mobility and range of motion
*   Temporary numbness or tingling sensations
*   General fatigue and discomfort

Our recovery process includes the following steps:

**Lifestyle Guidance:** Our team provides guidance on activities and lifestyle choices to aid in the recovery process, including proper nutrition and activity modifications.
