FAQ
===

Premier Orthopaedics & Sports Medicine offers cutting-edge care and personalized treatment plans for sports injuries, arthritis, and chronic pain. We prioritize conservative treatments whenever possible but employ advanced surgical techniques when needed. Known for empathy and genuine care, we ensure a welcoming atmosphere for all patients.

### General Orthopaedic Surgery

[What are the most common reasons for seeing an orthopaedic surgeon?](https://acespremortho.com/spine-care-faq/)

Common reasons include chronic joint pain, sports injuries, fractures, arthritis, and back pain.

[What types of surgeries do orthopaedic surgeons perform?](https://acespremortho.com/spine-care-faq/)

They perform a range of surgeries such as joint replacements, arthroscopy, spinal surgery, and repair of fractures and torn ligaments.

[How long is the recovery period after a joint replacement surgery?](https://acespremortho.com/spine-care-faq/)

Recovery typically takes several months, with most patients resuming normal activities within 3 to 6 months. Full recovery may take up to a year.

### Arthritis and Joint Disorders

[What are the treatment options for osteoarthritis?](https://acespremortho.com/spine-care-faq/)

Treatments include lifestyle modifications, physical therapy, medications, injections, and in severe cases, joint replacement surgery.

[How does rheumatoid arthritis differ from osteoarthritis?](https://acespremortho.com/spine-care-faq/)

Rheumatoid arthritis is an autoimmune disorder causing inflammation in the joints, whereas osteoarthritis is a degenerative joint disease caused by wear and tear.

[Can lifestyle changes help manage arthritis symptoms?](https://acespremortho.com/spine-care-faq/)

Yes, maintaining a healthy weight, regular exercise, a balanced diet, and avoiding joint overuse can help manage symptoms.

### Spine Disorders

[What are common spine conditions that orthopaedic surgeons treat?](https://acespremortho.com/spine-care-faq/)

Conditions include herniated discs, spinal stenosis, scoliosis, and degenerative disc disease.

[How is a herniated disc treated?](https://acespremortho.com/spine-care-faq/)

Treatments range from physical therapy, medications, and epidural injections to minimally invasive surgery if conservative methods fail.

[What is spinal stenosis, and how is it treated?](https://acespremortho.com/spine-care-faq/)

Spinal stenosis is the narrowing of the spinal canal, causing nerve compression. Treatments include physical therapy, injections, and surgical decompression.

### Hand & Foot Conditions

[What are common hand conditions treated by orthopaedic surgeons?](https://acespremortho.com/spine-care-faq/)

Conditions include carpal tunnel syndrome, trigger finger, and fractures.

[How is carpal tunnel syndrome treated?](https://acespremortho.com/spine-care-faq/)

Treatments include wrist splinting, anti-inflammatory medications, corticosteroid injections, and surgery in severe cases.

[What are common foot and ankle conditions?](https://acespremortho.com/spine-care-faq/)

Conditions include plantar fasciitis, Achilles tendonitis, and ankle sprains.

[How is plantar fasciitis managed?](https://acespremortho.com/spine-care-faq/)

Management includes stretching exercises, orthotic devices, anti-inflammatory medications, and in severe cases, surgery.

### Pain Management

[What are common pain management techniques used in orthopaedics?](https://acespremortho.com/spine-care-faq/)

Techniques include medication, physical therapy, injections, nerve blocks, and minimally invasive surgery.

[How effective are corticosteroid injections for joint pain?](https://acespremortho.com/spine-care-faq/)

Corticosteroid injections can provide significant pain relief and reduce inflammation, but the effect is usually temporary.

[What role does physical therapy play in pain management?](https://acespremortho.com/spine-care-faq/)

Physical therapy helps improve mobility, strength, and function, reducing pain through targeted exercises and modalities.

[Are there non-surgical options for managing chronic back pain?](https://acespremortho.com/spine-care-faq/)

Yes, options include physical therapy, medications, spinal injections, chiropractic care, and lifestyle modifications.

### Sports Injuries

[What are the most common sports injuries treated by orthopaedic surgeons?](https://acespremortho.com/spine-care-faq/)

Common sports injuries include ACL tears, meniscus tears, rotator cuff injuries, and stress fractures.

[How are ACL tears treated?](https://acespremortho.com/spine-care-faq/)

Treatment can involve physical therapy, bracing, and often surgical reconstruction to restore knee stability.

[What role does physical therapy play in recovering from an orthopaedic injury?](https://acespremortho.com/spine-care-faq/)

Physical therapy helps restore function, improve strength and flexibility, and reduce pain and inflammation.

### Conclusion

Orthopaedic surgery and pain management encompass a wide range of conditions and treatments. Understanding the scope of practice, common conditions, and treatment options is crucial for effective patient care and management.

[What conditions can be treated with spine surgery?](https://acespremortho.com/spine-care-faq/)

Spine surgery is often recommended for conditions such as herniated discs, spinal stenosis, degenerative disc disease, spinal fractures, and certain deformities. It is crucial to consult with a specialist to determine the most suitable treatment for your specific condition.

[What is minimally invasive spine surgery?](https://acespremortho.com/spine-care-faq/)

Minimally invasive spine surgery involves using small incisions and specialized instruments to access and treat spinal issues. This approach typically results in less pain, reduced scarring, and faster recovery compared to traditional open surgery.

[How do I know if I need spine surgery?](https://acespremortho.com/spine-care-faq/)

Your healthcare provider will assess your condition through a thorough examination, medical history review, and diagnostic tests. Non-surgical options are often explored first, but surgery may be recommended if conservative treatments prove ineffective in alleviating your symptoms.

[What is the recovery time for spine surgery?](https://acespremortho.com/spine-care-faq/)

Recovery times vary based on the type of surgery and individual factors. Minimally invasive procedures generally have shorter recovery periods compared to open surgery. Your surgeon will provide personalized postoperative instructions to guide you through the recovery process.

[Are there risks associated with spine surgery?](https://acespremortho.com/spine-care-faq/)

As with any surgical procedure, spine surgery carries some risks. These may include infection, bleeding, or complications related to anesthesia. Your surgeon will discuss potential risks and benefits with you before the procedure to help you make an informed decision.

[How do I prepare for spine surgery?](https://acespremortho.com/spine-care-faq/)

Preparing for spine surgery involves medical evaluations, discussions with your surgeon about expectations, and following any preoperative instructions provided. These may include stopping certain medications, fasting before surgery, and making necessary arrangements for postoperative care.

[How long does it take to see the results of spine surgery?](https://acespremortho.com/spine-care-faq/)

The timeline for experiencing the full benefits of spine surgery varies. While some patients may notice improvements shortly after surgery, others may require more time for the healing process. Your surgeon will provide guidance on what to expect during your recovery period.
