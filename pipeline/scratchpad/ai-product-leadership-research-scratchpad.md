1. **Fault-Tolerant UIs** (Gustav Soderstrom) — Design UI to match ML performance; show multiple options when hit rate is low
2. **Three-Part Utility Equation** (Mike Krieger) — Intelligence + Context/Memory + Applications/UI
3. **"Can AI Do That?" Framework** (Paul Adams) — Map product problems to AI capabilities
4. **Shape Up for AI** (Ryan Singer) — Appetites over deadlines, fixed time variable scope

6. **Pre-Mortems** (Shreyas Doshi) — Tigers, Paper Tigers, Elephants
7. **LNO Framework** (Shreyas Doshi) — Leverage, Neutral, Overhead tasks

## Key Skills Identified (Ranked by Frequency)

1. **Understanding AI deeply** — Not delegating to engineering (mentioned by 12/18 guests)
2. **Comfort with uncertainty** — Non-deterministic outputs, probabilistic thinking (10/18)
3. **Context engineering** — Providing right context to AI systems (8/18)
4. **Clear goal/outcome definition** — Precise specs for AI agents (8/18)
5. **Systems thinking** — Workflows and decision loops, not features (7/18)
6. **Using AI products daily** — Building intuition through practice (7/18)
7. **Writing well** — Highest leverage skill for product leaders (6/18)
8. **Evaluation design** — Building and running evals, quality assessment (5/18)
9. **Architectural thinking** — Problem definition over implementation (5/18)
10. **Cross-functional fluency** — Product + engineering + design + research (5/18)

## Patterns Observed

**Consensus:**
- AI is a fundamental shift, not incremental improvement
- Product leaders must understand AI deeply, not just delegate
- Integration beats bolting-on (embed AI across teams, don't create separate AI teams)
- Context > model choice for product quality
- Smaller teams, more ownership in AI era
- Use AI products daily to build intuition

**Debate:**
- How fast will AI replace PM tasks vs augment them
- Feature teams vs empowered teams in AI context
- When to build custom models vs use APIs
- How much technical depth product leaders need
