## Full Description

_For the event/registration page. ~400 words._

### The volume vs quality debate is over. You need both.

Most companies pick a side. Publish a lot and hope something sticks. Or agonize over every page and publish five a month.

Both approaches have a ceiling.

**Volume is speed to signal.** Publish 5 pages a month and you get 60 experiments per year to learn what works. Your influence on search engines and AI is limited to the surface area you create. Fewer pages = less data, slower learning, capped growth.

**Quality means too many things. So we broke it down.** 7 layers: context, strategy, research, planning, drafting, post-processing, and publishing. Each layer predicts whether your page becomes the best answer for a specific person with a specific intent. Miss one and the whole thing falls apart.

**The fastest-growing companies do both.** Our most successful engagements like Lovable share one pattern: speed to signal, iterate fast, double down on what's working, expand as you gain confidence.

Backed by:

- **2,000+ pages** we publish and update every week for customers
- A study on **600K+ AI responses** and their citations
- **2M+ AI responses** we collect and analyze monthly through CheckThat
- Results from our own properties and customer sites

### What you'll walk away with

- The real relationship between volume and quality (and why choosing one kills the other)
- A framework for decomposing "quality" into 7 measurable layers
- How to think about alignment — communicating the right thing the right way for your brand, consistently
- Lessons from scaling content for companies growing fast in search and AI visibility
- How to use signals from AI engines (not just Google) to guide what you publish and update

### Who this is for

Marketing leaders, content teams, and founders who want to grow their presence in search and AI. Whether you're publishing 5 pages a month or 500, you'll leave with a framework for doing both without burning out your team.
