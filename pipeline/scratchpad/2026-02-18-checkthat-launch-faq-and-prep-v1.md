## Part 2: FAQ

### Product & Positioning

**What problem does CheckThat solve?**

B2B buyers stopped Googling. They ask AI. "What's the best expense management tool for a 200-person finance team?" — that's a real query, and the AI gives a real answer. If your brand isn't in that answer, you're invisible to a growing share of your market.

CheckThat tells you whether you're showing up, how you're showing up, and what to do about it.

**How is this different from SEO tools?**

SEO tracks your ranking on search engines. CheckThat tracks your presence in AI-generated answers across five engines: ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, Gemini, and one more. These are fundamentally different surfaces. You can rank #1 on Google and be completely absent from ChatGPT's recommendations.

**What makes CheckThat different from competitors like Profound, Scrunch, or Peec AI?**

Three things:

First, the open index. Competitors put everything behind a paywall. You pay, then start from an empty dashboard. CheckThat gives you access to 172 B2B categories and 5,800+ brands before you spend a dollar. You see the landscape first, then decide if you need deeper tracking.

Second, buyer intent intelligence. Most tools just track whether your brand gets mentioned. CheckThat maps the actual questions buyers ask and shows you how AI answers them. That's the difference between knowing "we got mentioned" and knowing "when someone asks for the best tool in our category, here's exactly what AI says about us vs. our competitors."

Third, no cold start. We've already tracked 2.6M+ AI responses. When you sign up, you get historical data immediately. Competitors make you wait weeks.

**Why did we build this?**

GrowthX does content marketing for B2B companies. We saw the shift happening: AI answers are replacing search results for B2B buying decisions. Our clients kept asking "how do we show up in ChatGPT?" and nobody had good data. So we built the data layer ourselves.

**What AI engines does CheckThat track?**

Five: ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, Gemini, and one additional engine. We probe each engine with the same prompts and compare how they answer.

**What does "open index" mean?**

It means the category-level data is public. Anyone can see how AI engines answer buyer questions across 172 B2B software categories without signing up. No paywall, no login required. The open index is what makes the flywheel work: public data drives organic traffic, traffic drives signups, signups feed more data back into the index.

**What are the key signals CheckThat measures?**

Four signals:

- **RECALL**: Are you mentioned in the AI's answer?
- **SENTIMENT**: How are you described? (Positive, neutral, negative?)
- **ALIGNMENT**: What claims does the AI associate with your brand?
- **LIFT**: Are you moving up or down over time?

Most competitors only measure RECALL. That's like measuring SEO by checking if Google indexed your page — technically useful, wildly incomplete.

### Pricing & Packaging

**What are the pricing tiers?**

Three tiers:

- **Free ($0/mo)**: 50 custom prompts, 14 days of historical data, 5 AI engines, access to 100,000+ shared prompts, weekly probing, unlimited users, full company research database.
- **Pro ($249/mo)**: 500 custom prompts, 60 days of historical data, near-daily probing, export capabilities, competitor benchmarking, dedicated Slack support.
- **Business ($1,500/mo)**: 2,000 custom prompts, full historical archive, near-daily probing, dedicated AEO strategist, managed prompt strategy, custom reports, ongoing audits, dedicated engagement manager.

**Is Free included in current plans, or is it an add-on?**

Free is standalone. Anyone can sign up. It's not bundled with GrowthX services — it's its own product with its own value.

Pro and Business are separate paid tiers. For existing GrowthX services clients, see the "Existing Customers" section below for how to handle conversations about bundling or discounts.

**Is pricing the same for new and existing customers?**

Yes, list pricing is the same. But the conversation differs:

- **New customers** get standard pricing.
- **Existing GrowthX services clients** may have bundling or loyalty incentives. Check with Bridget or Ella before offering anything off-list. The principle is: protect the relationship on enterprise accounts already paying a lot, look for upsell on accounts with room to grow.

**How does pricing differ by account type?**

Think about it in four buckets:

1. **Enterprise accounts already paying a lot**: Protect the relationship. Don't nickel-and-dime them. Consider including Pro as a value-add to retain the account.
2. **Enterprise accounts with upsell potential**: Lead with Business tier. Position the AEO strategist as the differentiator.
3. **SMB / growth-stage companies**: Pro is the sweet spot. Emphasize the 10x prompt volume over Free and the near-daily probing cadence.
4. **Free/trial users**: Let them experience the open index. The conversion trigger is when they want to track their own brand with custom prompts and get historical data.

**What's the contract structure?**

- **Free**: No contract.
- **Pro**: Month-to-month or annual (annual preferred for retention).
- **Business**: 18-month preferred. This locks in cash flow and retention. Discuss with Bridget for contract specifics.

**What triggers an upsell from Free to Pro?**

The customer wants more than 50 custom prompts, wants more than 14 days of history, or wants near-daily probing instead of weekly. The natural trigger: they've been using Free, they've seen their category data, and now they want to go deeper on their own brand.

**What triggers an upsell from Pro to Business?**

The customer wants a managed service. They don't just want data, they want someone to build their prompt strategy, interpret results, and run ongoing audits. The 2,000 prompt ceiling and dedicated strategist are the differentiators.

**When should we discount?**

Rarely. The Free tier is already more generous than what competitors charge $99+ for. If you're in a competitive deal, talk to Bridget before discounting. The preferred play: add value (more prompts, longer history, faster probing) rather than cut price.

**When should we offer it for free?**

Two scenarios: (1) strategic accounts where CheckThat data strengthens the GrowthX services relationship, and (2) potential case study partners who'll go public with results. Both need Marcel or Bridget approval.

### Support & Operations

**Does support differ across tiers?**

Yes.

- **Free**: Self-serve. In-product help, community, documentation.
- **Pro**: Dedicated Slack support with response SLA.
- **Business**: Dedicated engagement manager + AEO strategist. White-glove support.

**Who handles support post-launch?**

- **Product bugs / technical issues**: Route to Daniel and Stevie.
- **Billing and pricing questions**: Route to Bridget or Ella.
- **Strategy and "how do I use this" questions**: Route to Andi (CS) or Jason (for sales prospects).
- **Escalations**: Route to Marcel.

**What's the escalation path?**

Tier 1 (Support/CS) → Tier 2 (Daniel for technical, Bridget for billing, Jason for sales) → Tier 3 (Marcel for anything unresolved or high-stakes).

Document every escalation. If you're unsure who owns it, ask in the launch war room channel.

**How do I demo CheckThat?**

Demo scripts are owned by Jason. If you don't have yours yet, ask him directly. The demo flow should hit three beats:

1. Show the open index. "Here's what AI engines say about your category right now — for free."
2. Show brand tracking. "Here's what happens when you add your brand: historical data, competitor benchmarks, custom prompts."
3. Show the insight layer. "Here's where you stand, here's what's working, here's what to do next."

**What if a customer reports a bug on launch day?**

Log it immediately. Include: what they did, what they expected, what happened, and screenshots if possible. Route to Daniel and Stevie. If it's blocking the customer's workflow, escalate to the war room.

**What changed in my team's daily work?**

If you're on CS or Support: you now need to know CheckThat well enough to answer basic product questions, walk customers through the dashboard, and explain the difference between free, Pro, and Business. Training materials and walkthroughs are available — if you haven't done them, do them before launch.

If you're on Sales: CheckThat is a new product to sell and a new upsell path for existing accounts. Know the pricing, know the demo, know the triggers.

### Competitive & Market

**Who are the main competitors?**

Four primary:

- **Profound** ($399/mo, 200 prompts, no free tier, raised $58M)
- **Scrunch** ($700/mo, 700 prompts, no free tier, raised $19M)
- **Peec AI** (~$220/mo, 100 prompts, trial only, raised $29M)
- **Athena HQ** (~$295/mo, custom prompts, no free tier)

**Why do we win?**

We're open, they're closed. We give buyer intent intelligence, they give mention tracking. We have historical data from day one, they make you start from scratch. And our Free tier gives more value than what most of them charge for.

The moat is the data. Six months of proprietary AI response data across 172 categories can't be backfilled by a competitor launching today.

**What if a prospect is already using a competitor?**

Don't trash the competitor. Instead: "You already know this matters — that's smart. What we've found is that most tools answer 'are we mentioned?' but not 'what should we do about it?' Show them the open index for their category. Let the data do the talking.

**What is AEO?**

Answer Engine Optimization. It's the practice of influencing how AI engines answer questions relevant to your brand. Think of it as SEO's successor for the AI era. SEO optimized for search rankings. AEO optimizes for AI recommendations.

### Existing GrowthX Customers

**How does CheckThat relate to GrowthX services?**

They're complementary. GrowthX services builds the content that improves your brand's presence. CheckThat measures whether that presence is translating into AI visibility. Together: strategy + measurement.

**Should we offer CheckThat to all existing services clients?**

Yes, at minimum introduce it. Every GrowthX services client should know CheckThat exists and see their category data. Whether they pay for Pro or Business depends on their needs and account dynamics. Talk to Ella or Andi about the right approach for each account.

**Can we bundle CheckThat with services engagements?**

Potentially. But don't freelance — bundling decisions go through Bridget and Marcel. The default: CheckThat is priced independently. Bundling is a strategic lever, not a standard offer.

## Part 5: Success Metrics

### 90-Day Checkpoint (the four questions that matter)

1. Can we get 30k monthly visitors organically?
2. Are we converting at least 5% of Free to Pro?
3. Have we closed at least 10 Business deals?
4. Is Pro churn under 10%?

If all four are yes: we have a business. Double down.
If any are no: adjust fast. Cut costs, change pricing, rethink the model.

### Launch Day/Week Metrics

- System uptime and performance
- New signups (Free, Pro, Business)
- Support ticket volume and resolution time
- Sales pipeline created
- Website traffic and conversion rate
- Social/PR reach and engagement
