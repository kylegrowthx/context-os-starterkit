## Executive Summary

Unusual has carved out a differentiated position with "AI Relations"—treating AI models as influencers rather than search engines. It's clever but narrow. CheckThat's opportunity is to own a bigger position: **the open AI visibility index for B2B**. Don't fight on Unusual's turf. Define a different game.

---

| Dimension | Unusual | CheckThat | Implication |
|-----------|---------|-----------|-------------|
| **Philosophy** | AI as influencers (PR paradigm) | AI as decision layer (buyer intent) | Both valid, different frames |
| **Data model** | Closed, proprietary | Open index, shared | CheckThat has moat potential |

## What Unusual Gets Right

### 1. Fresh Framing
"AI Relations" is smart positioning. It:
- Distances from crowded "AEO/GEO" terminology
- Creates a new mental model (influencers vs search engines)
- Resonates with PR/comms buyers
- Sounds premium and strategic

### 2. Qualitative Focus
Their "enterprise readiness score" (18/100 → 54/100) is a compelling metric. It answers a different question than citation counts: **How does AI perceive my brand?** not just **Does AI mention my brand?**

### 3. Case Study Execution
Reducto story is well-crafted:
- Specific problem: "Described as niche startup tool"
- Specific result: 11x citations, "enterprise platform" perception
- Time frame: 3 months
- Social proof: $108M raised, A16Z-backed

### 4. End-to-End Positioning
They do analysis → content creation → monitoring. Full service. This reduces buyer complexity but also creates a black box.

---

## Where Unusual Is Vulnerable

### 1. Closed Data Model
Every client's research stays private. No network effects. No compounding moat. CheckThat's open index is structurally different and compounds over time.

### 2. Cold Start Problem
Despite their positioning, you still start with "brain probes" that they run for you. It's better than competitors but not fundamentally different. You don't see your category before you pay.

### 3. Narrow Buyer
"AI Relations" speaks to comms/PR buyers. Misses the CMO/demand gen buyer who thinks in terms of pipeline and visibility. CheckThat's "buyer intent" framing captures a bigger audience.

### 4. Premium Positioning Limits Scale
No public pricing + seed stage = they're going enterprise/high-touch. This leaves the mid-market and self-serve motion wide open.

### 5. No Historical Data Moat
They just started tracking. CheckThat's 2.6M+ responses and daily tracking since inception creates irreplaceable historical data.

## CheckThat's Unique Advantages

### 1. The Open Index (Category-Defining)
This is the Crunchbase/G2 play. No one else is building a public, shared AI visibility index. This creates:
- SEO traffic (people searching for AI visibility by category)
- Trust (transparency before paywall)
- Network effects (more data → better benchmarks → more users)

**In the video:** Lead with this. "We made it all public. No signup required."

### 2. No Cold Start
172 categories mapped. 5,800+ brands researched. Prompts already built and tracked. Historical data backfilled.

**In the video:** Contrast with competitors. "They hand you an empty dashboard. We did the heavy lifting."

### 3. Buyer Intent Layer
Not just "does AI mention you" but "what questions are buyers asking and how does AI answer." This is more actionable than perception scores.

**In the video:** "We mapped how buyers discover and evaluate B2B software in AI search."

### 4. Freemium + Transparent Pricing
You see value before you pay. This is fundamentally different from every competitor including Unusual.

**In the video:** "The best part? It's free. You only pay if you want more."

### 5. B2B Software Focus
Unusual goes broad. CheckThat goes deep. Focus = better product for the core buyer.

## Positioning Recommendations

### Don't Say
- "AI Relations" (that's their term)
- "We track how AI perceives you" (their frame)
- "AEO tool" (commodity positioning)
- "Dashboard for AI visibility" (every competitor says this)

### Do Say
- **"The open AI visibility index for B2B"** (category-defining)
- **"Buyer intent intelligence meets AEO"** (unique combination)
- **"No cold start"** (pain point every buyer has felt)
- **"We did the heavy lifting"** (contrast to competitors)
- **"See your category before you pay"** (transparency differentiator)

## Video Script Recommendations

### Hook (First 10 seconds)
**Current transcript:** Multiple takes of intro, gets to the point slowly.

**Recommendation:** Start with the problem, not the intro.

> "Your first sales call now happens inside an AI answer. If your brand isn't mentioned—or isn't mentioned correctly—you don't get a shot. That's the shift."

### The Problem Section
**Current transcript:** Good content but buried. "Empty dashboard" insight is strong.

**Recommendation:** Lead with what competitors get wrong.

> "There are 170+ AEO tools. They all work the same way. Pay upfront. Guess which prompts matter. Get a dashboard with no context. Figure it out yourself. We think that's wrong."

### The Solution Section
**Current transcript:** "Buyer intent intelligence meets AEO" appears multiple times. Good.

**Recommendation:** Structure around three things you built, not features.

> "We took a different approach:
> 1. We mapped every B2B software category.
> 2. We researched the buyer questions that actually matter.
> 3. We started tracking daily across every AI engine.
> 
> Then we made it all open and shared. No cold start. You plug in and get answers."

### The Differentiation Section
**Current transcript:** Competitive framing is there but scattered.

**Recommendation:** Make the contrast explicit.

> "Other tools give you an empty dashboard and say good luck. We give you your category already mapped, your competitors already researched, buyer questions already tracked, and historical data already there. You don't start from zero."

### The CTA
**Current transcript:** "It's free" is strong but buried at minute 14.

**Recommendation:** Lead with free, explain the model simply.

> "The open index is free. You see your category, your competitors, how AI answers buyer questions—no signup required. You only pay if you want more historical data or custom prompts on top of our shared library. That's it."

## The Bottom Line

Unusual is a well-positioned competitor with smart messaging. But they're playing a different game—"AI Relations" for perception management.

CheckThat's opportunity is bigger: **become the source of truth for AI visibility in B2B.**

The open index is the moat. No cold start is the hook. Buyer intent is the differentiation.

Don't fight on their turf. Own yours.
