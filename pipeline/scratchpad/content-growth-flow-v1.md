## Pipeline Overview

```
INPUT → WORK → OUTPUT → DESTINATION → OUTCOME → METRICS → TARGET
```

---

## 1. INPUT
The system starts with foundational inputs:
- **Context** – Understanding the current situation, goals, and constraints
- **Knowledge Base (KB)** – Existing information, research, and reference materials

## 2. WORK
The execution layer where strategy meets action:
- **Opportunities** – Identifying areas for content creation or optimization
- **Execution** – Actually producing the work

## 3. Human in the Loop (HIL)
Quality control mechanisms to ensure output quality:
- **Failure Mode** – Identifying what can go wrong
- **Calibrating** – Adjusting parameters and approach
- **Polishing** – Final refinement before publication

## 4. OUTPUT
The tangible deliverables:
- **Pages** – The primary output unit
  - *Create* – New content
  - *Update* – Revisions to existing content
  - *Delete* – Removing outdated/irrelevant content

## 5. DESTINATION
Where content lives and how it's structured:
- **Website** – The primary destination
  - *Code* – Technical implementation
  - *Content & Assets* – The actual material (text, images, videos)
- **Knowledge Graph / Brand** – Structured data representing brand authority and topic relationships

## 6. OUTCOME
The primary goal of the system:
- **Organic Growth (Compounding)** – Traffic and authority that builds on itself over time

## 7. METRICS
How we measure success:
- **Conversion** – Actions taken by visitors
- **Traffic / Engagement** – Volume and depth of interactions
- **Visibility** – Search rankings, impressions, brand awareness

## 8. TARGET AUDIENCE
Who this system serves:
- **Buyer** – End customers making purchase decisions
- **Agents** – Intermediaries, partners, or AI agents discovering content
- **Training** – Educational use cases, internal or external

---

## Key Insight: The "Why?" Loop

The diagram shows a feedback loop from the target audience back to the Knowledge Graph. This represents the continuous cycle of:
1. Understanding *why* audiences engage
2. Feeding insights back into the brand's knowledge structure
3. Informing future content creation

This creates the **compounding effect** central to organic growth strategy.
