# AI Voice Agents Research Scratchpad

## Topic
How AI Voice Agents Actually Work

## Learner Profile
- **Role:** Non-technical person (business leader, curious professional)
- **Goal:** Understand from first principles how voice agents work, what the pieces are, and where the hard problems live
- **Context:** AI voice agents are everywhere (customer service, assistants, sales). Marcel wants a deep, clear understanding without needing an engineering degree.

## Audience Analysis
| Question | Answer |
|----------|--------|
| WHO is learning this? | Non-technical business professionals who encounter voice AI in their work |
| WHAT do they care about? | How it actually works (not marketing fluff), where it breaks, what's real vs hype |
| WHY do they need this? | To make informed decisions, evaluate vendors, understand capabilities and limits |
| WHAT sources would they trust? | Academic papers (explained simply), engineering blogs from major companies, respected tech journalists, practitioner talks |
| WHAT should be excluded? | Vendor marketing materials (unless citing architecture), overly academic notation |

## Research Categories
1. Foundations: What is a voice agent, history, core pipeline
2. Speech Recognition (ASR/STT): How machines hear
3. Natural Language Understanding + LLMs: How machines understand
4. Dialog Management: How machines decide what to say
5. Text-to-Speech (TTS): How machines speak
6. Real-time Infrastructure: Latency, streaming, orchestration
7. Challenges & Failure Modes: Turn-taking, accents, noise, hallucination
8. Key Players & Platforms: Who's building what
9. Real-World Examples: Deployments that worked (and didn't)
10. Emerging Trends: Multimodal, emotion, embodied agents
