## Frameworks Discovered

### Positioning Frameworks

#### 1. April Dunford's Positioning Canvas (Obviously Awesome)
**The 5 Components:**
- **Competitive Alternatives**: What would customers use if you didn't exist?
- **Unique Attributes**: What features/capabilities only you have?
- **Value**: What value do those attributes enable? (So what?)
- **Target Customer Characteristics**: Who cares most about that value?
- **Market Category**: What context makes your value obvious?

**Key Insight**: "Positioning is the act of deliberately defining how you're the best at something that a well-defined set of customers cares a lot about."

**Source**: Obviously Awesome book; Lenny's Podcast transcript

#### 2. April Dunford's Sales Pitch Framework (Sales Pitch book)
**Setup Phase:**
1. **Insight** - Share a perspective on the market the customer hasn't considered
2. **Alternative Approaches** - Show how others solve this problem (and their limitations)
3. **Perfect World** - Describe what the ideal solution would look like

**Follow-Through Phase:**
4. **Differentiated Value** - Show how you deliver the perfect world
5. **Proof** - Evidence that you can deliver (case studies, data)
6. **Objections** - Proactively address concerns
7. **The Ask** - Clear next step

**Key Insight**: "Don't lead with features. Lead with insight that changes how they see the problem."

**Source**: Lenny's Podcast - April Dunford episode

#### 3. Category Design Framework (Play Bigger)
**Components:**
- **Point of View (POV)**: Your unique perspective on the problem
- **Category Name**: What you call this new space
- **Category Blueprint**: How the category works
- **Lightning Strikes**: Big moments to establish category leadership

**Source**: Play Bigger; Christopher Lochhead; Category Pirates newsletter

#### 4. Al Ries & Jack Trout Positioning Principles
- Own a word in the prospect's mind
- Be first or create a new category to be first in
- Positioning is about perception, not reality
- Narrow focus beats broad claims

**Source**: "Positioning: The Battle for Your Mind"

#### 5. Geoffrey Moore's Positioning Statement Template
"For [target customer] who [statement of need], [product name] is a [product category] that [key benefit]. Unlike [competitors], our product [primary differentiation]."

**Source**: "Crossing the Chasm"

### Messaging Frameworks

#### 1. Emily Kramer's Fuel + Engine Framework
**Fuel (Demand Generation):**
- Content marketing
- Paid acquisition
- Events
- PR
- Partnerships

**Engine (Conversion + Retention):**
- Website/product experience
- Sales process
- Onboarding
- Customer success
- Expansion

**Key Insight**: "You need both. Too much fuel without engine = leaky bucket. Great engine without fuel = slow growth."

**Source**: Lenny's Podcast - Emily Kramer episode; MKT1

#### 2. Emily Kramer's Three Marketing Archetypes
1. **Content Marketing** - Top-of-funnel, thought leadership, SEO
2. **Growth Marketing** - Acquisition, conversion, experimentation
3. **Product Marketing** - Positioning, messaging, launches, enablement

**Key Insight**: "Hire pie-shaped marketers" - people with broad base + deep expertise in one area.

**Source**: Lenny's Podcast - Emily Kramer episode

#### 3. StoryBrand Framework (Donald Miller)
1. A **Character** (customer) has a **Problem**
2. And meets a **Guide** (your company) with **Empathy** and **Authority**
3. Who gives them a **Plan**
4. And calls them to **Action**
5. That results in **Success** or helps them avoid **Failure**

**Source**: "Building a StoryBrand"

#### 4. Problem-Agitation-Solution (PAS)
- **Problem**: Identify the pain
- **Agitation**: Make it vivid, emotional
- **Solution**: Present your answer

**Source**: Classic copywriting; Eugene Schwartz influence

#### 5. Value Proposition Canvas (Strategyzer)
**Customer Profile:**
- Jobs to be done
- Pains
- Gains

**Value Map:**
- Products/services
- Pain relievers
- Gain creators

**Source**: "Value Proposition Design" - Osterwalder & Pigneur

#### 6. Andy Raskin's Strategic Narrative
1. Name a big, relevant change in the world
2. Show there will be winners and losers
3. Tease the promised land
4. Introduce features as "magic gifts" for the journey
5. Present proof you can deliver

**Source**: "The Greatest Sales Deck I've Ever Seen" article

### GTM Frameworks

#### 1. Elena Verna's PLG + Sales Hybrid Model
**Growth Motions:**
- **PLG (Product-Led Growth)**: User self-serves, product drives acquisition
- **Sales-Led Growth**: Rep-driven, enterprise focus
- **Marketing-Led Growth**: Content/demand gen drives pipeline

**Key Insight**: "Most successful B2B companies combine PLG + sales. It's not either/or."

**Metrics by Motion:**
- PLG: Activation rate, time-to-value, PQLs
- Sales: SQL conversion, deal velocity, ACV
- Marketing: MQLs, CAC, attribution

**Source**: Lenny's Podcast - Elena Verna episodes (4 total)

#### 2. Brian Balfour's Four Fits Framework
1. **Market-Product Fit**: Does the market want the product?
2. **Product-Channel Fit**: Does the product suit the acquisition channel?
3. **Channel-Model Fit**: Does the channel match the business model?
4. **Model-Market Fit**: Does the model work for this market?

**Key Insight**: "You can't just optimize one fit. All four must align."

**Source**: Lenny's Podcast - Brian Balfour episode; Reforge

#### 3. Casey Winters' Growth Strategy Framework
**Three Levers:**
1. **Acquisition**: How do you get new users?
2. **Retention**: How do you keep them?
3. **Monetization**: How do you extract value?

**Key Insight**: "Retention is the most important. Without it, acquisition is filling a leaky bucket."

**Source**: Lenny's Podcast - Casey Winters episode

#### 4. Bowling Pin Strategy (Geoffrey Moore)
- Target one niche segment first (the head pin)
- Win that segment completely
- Use references to expand to adjacent segments
- Knock down pins one by one

**Source**: "Crossing the Chasm"

#### 5. Sean Ellis' Growth Hacking Process
1. **Ideation**: Generate growth ideas
2. **Prioritization**: ICE scoring (Impact, Confidence, Ease)
3. **Testing**: Run experiments
4. **Analysis**: Learn and iterate

**"Must-have" Test**: If 40%+ of users say they'd be "very disappointed" without your product, you have PMF.

**Source**: Lenny's Podcast - Sean Ellis episode; "Hacking Growth"

### Pricing Frameworks

#### 1. Madhavan Ramanujam's Willingness-to-Pay Framework
**Four Types of Products by WTP:**
1. **Feature Shock**: Too many features, low WTP - simplify
2. **Minivation**: Small innovation, matches WTP - proceed
3. **Hidden Gem**: High potential, under-priced - raise prices
4. **Undead**: No WTP, won't work - kill it

**Key Insight**: "Have the pricing conversation BEFORE you build."

**Source**: Lenny's Podcast - Madhavan Ramanujam episode; "Monetizing Innovation"

#### 2. Kyle Poyar's PLG Pricing Principles
- **Value metric alignment**: Charge based on value delivered
- **Free tier strategy**: Enough to show value, not so much they never pay
- **Expansion revenue**: Build in natural upgrade triggers
- **Transparent pricing**: Reduce friction in buying process

**Source**: OpenView blog; PLG benchmarks

### Launch Frameworks

#### 1. Nikita Bier's Viral Launch Playbook
- **Constraint**: Launch in one school/location first
- **Density**: Need critical mass in that location
- **Social proof**: Make usage visible to others
- **Timing**: Launch when attention is high (back to school, etc.)
- **Urgency**: Limited availability drives action

**Source**: Lenny's Podcast - Nikita Bier episode

#### 2. Product Hunt Launch Framework
- Pre-launch community building
- Launch day coordination
- Post-launch momentum

#### 3. Jeff Walker's Product Launch Formula
- Pre-prelaunch (build anticipation)
- Prelaunch (value + relationship building)
- Launch (open cart)
- Post-launch (delivery + testimonials)

### Competitive Intelligence Frameworks

#### 1. Klue's Compete to Win Framework
- **Collect**: Gather intel from all sources
- **Curate**: Synthesize into actionable insights
- **Consume**: Distribute to sales/marketing teams
- **Close**: Use in deals to win

#### 2. Win/Loss Analysis Framework
- Interview won and lost deals
- Identify patterns in competitive situations
- Update battlecards and positioning
- Feed back to product

#### 3. SWOT for Competitors
- Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats
- Do for each major competitor
- Update quarterly

### Sales Enablement Frameworks

#### 1. MEDDIC/MEDDPICC
- **M**etrics: What's the business impact?
- **E**conomic Buyer: Who has budget authority?
- **D**ecision Criteria: How will they decide?
- **D**ecision Process: What's the buying process?
- **P**aper Process: Procurement requirements?
- **I**dentify Pain: What's the core problem?
- **C**hampion: Who's your internal advocate?
- **C**ompetition: Who else are they considering?

#### 2. Challenger Sale Framework
- **Teach**: Lead with insight
- **Tailor**: Customize message to stakeholder
- **Take Control**: Guide the buying process

**Source**: "The Challenger Sale"

#### 3. SPIN Selling
- **S**ituation questions
- **P**roblem questions
- **I**mplication questions
- **N**eed-payoff questions

**Source**: "SPIN Selling" - Neil Rackham

## Mental Models

### Positioning Mental Models

1. **"Positioning is a team sport"** - Requires alignment across product, marketing, sales, leadership. Can't be done in isolation.

2. **"Your positioning lives in the customer's mind, not your pitch deck"** - It's about perception, not what you say about yourself.

3. **"Best product doesn't always win; best positioned does"** - April Dunford's core insight.

4. **"Category creation vs. category entry"** - Christopher Lochhead: Creating a new category is 6-10x more valuable than entering an existing one.

5. **"Positioning is strategy, not messaging"** - It's a strategic choice about where to compete, not just words.

### Growth Mental Models

6. **"Retention is the most important metric"** - Casey Winters: All other growth levers multiply off retention.

7. **"Growth loops > funnels"** - Brian Balfour: Sustainable growth comes from compounding loops, not linear funnels.

8. **"The best growth comes from the product"** - Elena Verna: PLG compounds because the product does the selling.

9. **"Four fits must align"** - Brian Balfour: Market, product, channel, and model must all fit together.

10. **"Distribution is king"** - Having a great product with no distribution is worse than mediocre product with great distribution.

### Pricing Mental Models

11. **"Price before you build"** - Madhavan Ramanujam: Validate willingness to pay early.

12. **"Price is a feature"** - Pricing communicates value and segments customers.

13. **"Value-based pricing > cost-plus pricing"** - Price based on value delivered, not cost to produce.

### Launch Mental Models

14. **"Density before breadth"** - Nikita Bier: Win one location completely before expanding.

15. **"Launches are moments, not events"** - The best launches create ongoing momentum, not one-time spikes.

### Messaging Mental Models

16. **"Clarity beats cleverness"** - Simple, clear messaging outperforms creative but confusing.

17. **"Lead with insight, not features"** - Andy Raskin/April Dunford: Change how they see the problem first.

18. **"The customer is the hero, not your product"** - StoryBrand: Position your product as the guide.

### Competitive Mental Models

19. **"Competition is in the mind"** - Al Ries: You're competing for mental real estate.

20. **"Know your competitive alternative"** - April Dunford: Understand what customers would do if you didn't exist.

## Key Questions

### Questions Great PMMs Ask

**About Positioning:**
- What would our customers do if we didn't exist? (Competitive alternatives)
- What can we do that no one else can?
- Why do customers choose us over alternatives?
- What's the context that makes our value obvious?
- Are we competing in the right category?

**About Customers:**
- Who are our best customers? What do they have in common?
- Why do customers stay? Why do they churn?
- What "job" are customers hiring our product to do?
- What does success look like for our customers?
- What objections do they raise and why?

**About Messaging:**
- Does our messaging pass the "so what?" test?
- Can a prospect explain what we do after 30 seconds on our homepage?
- Are we leading with insight or features?
- Is our message differentiated from competitors?

**About Go-to-Market:**
- What's our primary growth motion (PLG, sales-led, marketing-led)?
- Do our four fits align (market, product, channel, model)?
- Where are we losing prospects in the journey?
- What's our path from initial value to expansion?

**About Competition:**
- Why do we lose deals? To whom?
- What do competitors say about us?
- What's our unfair advantage?
- Where can we not compete?

### Interview Questions for PMM Roles

**Strategic:**
- Walk me through how you would position a new product in a crowded market.
- How do you validate messaging with customers?
- Describe a launch you led. What worked? What didn't?
- How do you prioritize what PMM works on?

**Execution:**
- How do you build a battlecard?
- What makes great sales enablement content?
- How do you measure PMM success?
- Walk me through your process for a launch.

**Collaboration:**
- How do you work with product on roadmap?
- How do you handle disagreements with sales on messaging?
- How do you get buy-in for new positioning?

### General PMM Principles
1. Positioning is foundational - get it right first.
2. Talk to customers constantly - sales calls, win/loss, user research.
3. Messaging should be tested, not declared.
4. Sales enablement is about making sellers successful, not impressive.
5. Competitive intelligence is ongoing, not a one-time project.
6. Launches are marketing moments - plan them as campaigns.
7. Metrics matter - PMM should own pipeline influence and win rates.

## Examples & Case Studies

### Positioning Examples

**Slack: From "Chat for Teams" to "Where Work Happens"**
- Started competing against email, chat tools
- Repositioned as productivity platform
- Changed category from communication to work coordination

**Figma: "Design tool" to "Collaborative interface design"**
- Differentiated from Adobe/Sketch on collaboration
- Made multiplayer the core value prop
- Won because of positioning, not features

**Notion: From "Note-taking app" to "All-in-one workspace"**
- Expanded category beyond notes
- Positioned against the proliferation of tools
- "One tool to replace them all"

**Superhuman: "Email client" to "Fastest email experience ever"**
- Didn't compete on features
- Positioned on speed (specific, ownable)
- Premium positioning enabled premium price

### Launch Examples

**Gas App (Nikita Bier)**
- Launched in one school at a time
- Created density before expanding
- Used exclusivity ("your school isn't open yet") as growth lever
- Sold to Discord for $50M+

**Superhuman Launch**
- Extensive waitlist to build anticipation
- Onboarding calls for every user
- Referral-only growth initially
- Premium experience matched premium price

### Pricing Examples

**Slack Free Tier**
- Free up to 10k messages searchable
- Showed full value, but limited history
- Natural upgrade trigger when teams hit the limit

**HubSpot Freemium**
- Free CRM as wedge
- Upsell to marketing/sales/service hubs
- Land and expand model

### Sales Enablement Examples

**Salesforce Competitive Intel**
- Real-time battlecards in CRM
- Win/loss analysis fed back to product
- Sales certification on competitive positioning

## Patterns Observed

### What Great PMMs Have in Common
1. **Customer obsession**: They talk to customers constantly - sales calls, win/loss, interviews
2. **Strategic thinking**: They see positioning as strategy, not just messaging
3. **Cross-functional influence**: They work across product, sales, marketing
4. **Data-informed**: They track metrics - pipeline influence, win rates, competitive wins
5. **Strong writing**: Messaging excellence requires excellent writing

### Common PMM Mistakes
1. **Positioning too broad**: Trying to appeal to everyone
2. **Feature-led messaging**: Leading with what, not why
3. **Ignoring competition**: Positioning in a vacuum
4. **Launch-and-done**: Not iterating based on market feedback
5. **Sales enablement as content dump**: Creating content no one uses
6. **No measurement**: Not tracking PMM's impact

### Areas of Expert Agreement
- Positioning is foundational - must be right before everything else
- Clarity beats cleverness in messaging
- Customer research is essential and ongoing
- Competitive intelligence is a continuous process
- Cross-functional collaboration is critical
- Launch is a moment to create, not just announce

### Areas of Expert Disagreement
- **Category creation vs. category entry**: Lochhead says always create; Dunford says it depends
- **Personas vs. JTBD**: Some swear by personas; others prefer jobs-to-be-done
- **PLG vs. sales-led**: Verna says combine; some are pure PLG advocates
- **PMM reporting structure**: Some say under marketing; others under product

### Emerging Trends
1. **AI in PMM**: Using AI for competitive intel, content creation, personalization
2. **PLG everywhere**: Even enterprise companies adding self-serve options
3. **Community-led growth**: Building communities as go-to-market lever
4. **Product-led sales**: PLG combined with sales assist
5. **Messaging validation at scale**: Tools like Wynter for rapid testing
