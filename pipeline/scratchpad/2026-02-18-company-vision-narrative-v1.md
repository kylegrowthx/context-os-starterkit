## The Biggest Shift Since the Internet

AI is changing how buyers make decisions. This is the biggest single shift in buyer behavior since the Internet. Every company on the planet is affected.

It's not just about buyers getting more information. The mechanism of discovery itself is changing. When someone researches a product or vendor today, they're increasingly interacting with AI agents that form opinions on their behalf and fetch information to support those opinions. Brands now have three audiences they need to reach, not one:

1. **Buyers** — getting harder to reach and competing with more noise than ever
2. **AI agents** — acting on behalf of buyers, forming recommendations before a human even asks
3. **Training bots** — crawling your site continuously, learning what your brand stands for, and encoding that into future model behavior

If you build a company around an inevitable shift, that's a good place to start. This is that shift.

## What Every Company Wants

Strip it back and every company wants the same two things: growth and leverage. They want to grow faster and do it with less. More output, fewer people, lower cost per result.

That hasn't changed. What's changed is what you have to do to get there.

## Why the Website Became Existential

The website is the biggest single driver of growth for B2B companies right now. Not the most important channel. The most strategic asset. And the job of managing it just got a hundred times harder.

Here's why:

The website influences buyers who come through your front door. It's where AI agents fetch information about you. It's the one thing you can actually control — you can change every page on your site tomorrow, immediately. Most transactions still happen there. And if you do it right, it compounds.

B2B companies have treated websites like a publishing channel for years. E-commerce figured this out long ago. Amazon doesn't see pages as pages — it sees a catalog of assets. Billions of dollars have been poured into enriching, de-duplicating, and optimizing that catalog because it drives discoverability, conversion, and loyalty. The Amazon flywheel is built on product pages.

B2B is catching up fast, but the demands are accelerating faster.

The number of pages required to be visible is exploding. An AI search result doesn't return 10 blue links — it pulls from 20 to 200 to 500 sources. To show up in those answers, you need more surface area. More pages, more content, covering more of the long tail than was ever necessary before.

And those pages are decaying faster. AI systems are always looking for fresh, high-quality sources. The half-life of a stale page is getting shorter. Leave a page alone and it fades. The website went from important to existential, and the job of keeping it healthy is no longer something a small team can manage manually.

---

## Three Jobs. One System.

Every website — regardless of company size, category, or complexity — comes down to the same three jobs:

**Manage pages.** Keep them organized, current, healthy, and performing. This means knowing what's working, what's not, and what needs attention before it decays.

**Create pages.** Expand the surface area of influence for buyers, AI agents, and training data. More answers for more questions. More citations. More signals teaching the models what your brand stands for.

**Optimize pages.** Every page, all the time. Fresh, relevant, high quality, converting. Not once. Not on a schedule. Continuously.

Right now, most companies can't do any of these three things well. CMSs are a mess. Analytics are broken. There's no one with both the strategic clarity to know what to build and the operational capacity to build it at scale. They're swimming in AI noise and don't know what's actually driving results.

---

## Why AI Isn't Optional

There is absolutely no way to do manage, create, and optimize at scale without AI as a force multiplier.

The long tail is getting longer. The pace of decay is accelerating. The number of pages required to compete is growing faster than any team can produce manually. This is already true for our best customers. Lovable went from 5 guides per week to 25. That's only possible with an AI-powered workflow. Without it, you either don't publish or you sacrifice quality.

The promised land isn't robots replacing people. It's a better division of labor: AI handles production, optimization, and learning at scale. Humans guide strategy, quality, and judgment where it matters. The system gets smarter with every page, every week. Growth compounds across all three audiences — buyers, agents, and bots.

## What We Built and Why

GrowthX has four layers working together:

**Infrastructure.** The operational primitives that make scale possible. Output AI — our agentic workflow framework — powers execution across every piece of work we do. It's already running behind the scenes for every client. We're open-sourcing it.

**Data.** CheckThat. The first AI visibility index for B2B companies — measuring how AI engines see, mention, and recommend your brand. This is some of the most important data available right now because AI agents are now the audience that matters most before a human buyer even gets involved. CheckThat is both a product and a lead-generation engine for everything else we do.

**Platform.** The OS — formerly Atlas, now being completely rewritten. Shipping early March. It's the operating system for website-driven growth: manage, create, optimize, and continuously learn and improve. When it's in a client's hands, they feel the compounding. When it's not, they see five Google Docs a week and wonder why they're paying.

**Services.** The strategy and forcing function layer. Some companies need all of it — full managed service, a team, a pod, an engagement manager keeping them honest. Others need less. The platform now decouples from the service, which means we can serve both. Platform-only or platform with managed service. The spectrum opens up.

And above all of it: the community. AI-led growth. Demand and education at scale. That's the distribution layer that feeds everything else.

## 2026

Three strategic priorities:

1. **Make the platform the OS for website-driven growth.** The platform should enable manage, create, optimize for any company — regardless of how much service they need on top of it. That's the product. That's the moat.

2. **Build the best dataset on how AI sees brands.** AI agents are a new audience and we have more data on how they perceive B2B companies than anyone. That data compounds. It informs the platform. It informs every piece of client work. And it's going to matter more, not less, over time.

3. **Make Output AI the best framework for developers building agentic workflows.** The infrastructure we built to power our own work is genuinely good. Opening it to the developer community extends our reach and builds a different kind of defensibility.

The financial targets: $34M ARR on core services and platform. $4M ARR on CheckThat. ~3x growth from where we are now.

We're not burning money to get there. We're building infrastructure, data, platform, and community while staying efficient on burn. The goal is to own our own destiny — not give the company away to VCs to fund growth we could fund ourselves if we execute.

## The Short Version

The world changed. Buyers use AI. AI uses your website. The website went from a channel to an existential asset, and managing it at the pace and scale required is impossible without AI.

We built four layers to solve it: services to learn, infrastructure to scale, data to see what's happening, and a community to own the category.

The promised land: AI handling production and optimization autonomously, humans guiding strategy and quality where it counts, and growth that compounds across all three audiences — buyers, agents, and bots.

That's why we exist.
