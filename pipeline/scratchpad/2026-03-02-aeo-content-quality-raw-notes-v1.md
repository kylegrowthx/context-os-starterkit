# AEO Content Quality Factors - Raw Research Notes
**Date:** March 2, 2026
**Research Period:** 60+ searches across AI visibility categories

---

## KEY PATTERNS & OBSERVATIONS

### Pattern 1: The Authority Paradox
- Traditional authority metrics (Domain Authority, PageRank) have COLLAPSED in importance (r=0.18 down from 0.23)
- Yet 96% of AI Overview citations come from sources with strong E-E-A-T signals
- **Insight:** Being authoritative (genuine expertise/credentials) matters more than appearing authoritative (backlinks)
- Third-party verification (reviews, press coverage, media mentions) matters MORE than owned content
- Brands 6.5x more likely to be cited through third-party sources than their own domains

### Pattern 2: The Freshness Obsession
- Content freshness is 25.7% more important in AI than traditional SEO
- ChatGPT shows strongest recency bias: 76.4% of cited pages updated in last 30 days
- 50% of Perplexity citations from 2025 alone (half the citations from content published this year)
- Content decay begins 2-3 days post-publication for Perplexity
- BUT: "Last updated" dates alone don't help—AI evaluates whether updates changed substance

### Pattern 3: Structure > Length
- 53.4% of AI citations go to pages under 1,000 words
- No correlation between word count and AI citation likelihood
- Information DENSITY matters: ~40% higher density in cited content
- 100-150 words per section is sweet spot (boosts citations to 4.7)
- Q&A format shows +89% to +221% (with schema) citation lift

### Pattern 4: E-E-A-T Is The New Gatekeeper
- 96% of AI Overview citations have verified E-E-A-T signals
- E-E-A-T verification became 27% stricter than 2024
- Real-time fact verification: 0.89 correlation with AI inclusion
- Medical authority (Mayo Clinic, Cleveland Clinic, WebMD) maintains top-25 rankings
- Author credentials in bylines/bios significantly boost AI extractability

### Pattern 5: The Multi-Modal Multiplier Effect
- Text-only content: baseline
- Text + images/video/structured data: 156% higher selection rates
- Full multimodal + schema integration: 317% MORE citations
- YouTube now surpassing Reddit as citation source (39.2% vs 20.3% social share)
- Video transcripts + descriptions + community posts = far more extractable than fragmented text

### Pattern 6: The Training Data Signal
- Content appears in AI answers not just from current search, but from LLM training data
- Multiple sources repeating same information across credible formats = stronger training signal
- Wikipedia is "default knowledge layer" (cited 1 in 6 conversations)
- Original research + proprietary data outperforms opinion content
- Consistent author/brand info across web (LinkedIn, podcasts, profiles) builds recognition in training data

### Pattern 7: The Third-Party Trust Amplifier
- 85% of brand mentions originate from third-party pages, not brand's own domain
- Blog posts (only 28.7% of citations) show STRONGEST correlation with AI recommendations
- Press releases from GlobeNewswire/PR Newswire/Business Wire: 95% of earned media citations
- G2/Capterra/Trustpilot reviews: 84-88% of review platform citations
- User-generated content (reviews, testimonials) = trust signal for AI systems

### Pattern 8: Extraction Preference Signals
- Clear H2/H3 hierarchical structure: 40% more likely to be cited
- Bullet points and numbered lists: preferred over paragraph text
- HTML tables: AI extracts easily (vs JavaScript-heavy interactive elements)
- FAQ schema: +28% citation increase, highest probability among schema types
- Breadcrumb schema + semantic URLs: 11.4% more citations

### Pattern 9: The Inconsistency Problem
- Less than 1 in 100 chance ChatGPT gives identical brand list in 2 consecutive queries
- Only 30% of brands stay visible from one AI answer to next
- Fewer than 1 in 5 brands achieve both frequent mentions AND consistent citations
- Brands with both signals: 40% more likely to resurface in consecutive responses
- **Implication:** Consistency requires building presence across multiple platforms, not just one

### Pattern 10: Platform-Specific Nuances
- **Google AI Overviews:** 60% of searches show overviews; 85% citations from last 2 years; 44% from 2025 alone; behaves most like traditional search
- **ChatGPT:** Strongest recency bias; 76.4% updated in 30 days; cites Wikipedia heavily; values conversational + comprehensive content
- **Perplexity:** 50% of citations from 2025; Reddit dominated (until recently); YouTube now leading; 2-3 day content decay
- **Claude:** Limited direct citation data, but values nuanced analysis; emerging platform, 164.82M monthly visits (Aug 2025)
- **Bing Copilot:** Emphasizes real-time freshness, authority, clear structure; integrated with Bing index

### Pattern 11: The Backlink Paradox in AI Search
- Backlinks show 0.37 correlation with AI citations (vs 0.41 for organic keywords)
- Sites with 99.5% citations from top 10 rankings, yet 47% now come from below position #5
- Brand search volume: 0.334 correlation (STRONGEST predictor of AI citations, stronger than backlinks)
- Referring domain count still matters for crawling frequency (affects training data inclusion)
- Link velocity: steady organic growth signals naturalness; spikes trigger manipulation flags

### Pattern 12: The Page Speed Nuance
- Speed impact modest (+27% max) vs content quality (+200-300%)
- Page with FCP <0.4s: 6.7 citations; >1.13s: 2.1 citations (3x difference)
- BUT: Pages with slightly higher INP (more interactive/complex) get cited more
- Super-fast pages = sometimes overly simple, not seen as authoritative
- **Rule:** Baseline requirement, not competitive advantage

### Pattern 13: Semantic Understanding Layer
- AI systems read for comprehension, NOT keyword matching
- Entity relationships matter more than keywords
- Pages with 15+ recognized entities: 4.8× higher selection probability
- Knowledge graphs: LLMs integrated with graphs achieve 300% higher accuracy
- Structured data (schema) makes entities machine-readable

### Pattern 14: The Featured Snippet Transition
- Featured snippets dropped 64% (15.41% to 5.53%) between Jan-June 2025
- Strong correlation decline (0.9) between snippets and AI Overviews
- AI Overviews replacing featured snippets, serving similar function
- BUT: Structure that earned snippets still matters for AI extraction
- Position Zero now more about AI Overviews citation slot than traditional snippet

### Pattern 15: Distribution Channel Authority
- 95%+ of earned media citations from independent outlets
- Press release distribution: GlobeNewswire, PR Newswire, Business Wire dominate
- Posting only on own site = minimal AI visibility for press releases
- Newswire archives meet AI standards for authority/verifiability
- Structured, predictable layouts = easier for LLM interpretation

### Pattern 16: The Sentiment & Recommendation Inconsistency
- Sentiment score: tone AI uses when describing your brand (can be negative)
- Frequent mentions but poor sentiment = weak recommendations
- Inconsistent recommendations due to: model variations, data freshness, context-dependent weighting
- Build presence across platforms for consistency signals

### Pattern 17: Proprietary Data as Moat
- Proprietary data > public data > opinion content (for AI citations)
- Original research: unique signal AI systems prioritize
- First-party insights: traceable, credible sources preferred
- Enterprise proprietary data: competitive edge in generative AI

### Pattern 18: The Q&A/Intent Matching Layer
- AI systems match user intent, not keywords
- Q&A formats map perfectly to how AI constructs responses
- Intent detection: bridge between user questions and intelligent retrieval
- Multi-intent queries handled via query expansion ("fan-out")
- Semantic embeddings enable matching without exact keyword phrases

### Pattern 19: Mobile-First Reality
- 53.68% Claude traffic from mobile (vs 46.32% desktop)
- Mobile-first indexing: AI crawls mobile version regardless of where buyers browse
- Responsive design (CSS-based): preferred by AI systems
- Mobile version broken/different content = invisible to AI despite desktop visibility
- 83% B2B desktop traffic, yet 48% buying with AI (mobile gap is real)

### Pattern 20: Wikipedia's Unique Position
- Nearly half of ChatGPT citations point to Wikipedia
- Wikipedia: single largest training data source for every major LLM
- Treated as "neutral authority" despite being one source among many
- Citation network effect: single Wikipedia mention cascades across platforms
- Model collapse without continually updated human-created knowledge

### Pattern 21: The Academic & Scientific Authority Signal
- Scientific papers: highest credibility signal for claims
- Scite's Smart Citations: 1.6B+ citations across 280M+ sources
- Elicit: 99.4% accuracy in extracting data points from academic papers
- Health/YMYL content: scientific citations critical for AI credibility
- Peer review as training signal: AI systems recognize quality markers

### Pattern 22: Review Platform Dominance
- G2 ecosystem (G2, Capterra, Software Advice): 84% review platform citations
- Gartner Peer Insights, TrustRadius: fill remaining slots
- 92% of consumers read reviews before purchase
- 73% Trustpilot boost to consumer trust; 71% purchase likelihood increase
- Review platform citations: critical AI evaluation stage for software buyers

### Pattern 23: The Accessibility-to-Extraction Connection
- WCAG compliance: helps AI parse and extract content
- OCR + NLP + layout analysis: intelligent document processing
- Screen reader compatibility: accessibility = extractability
- AI prioritizes machine-readable structures (both for humans with disabilities AND for AI itself)
- Semantic HTML: serves both accessibility and AI extraction

### Pattern 24: Search Intensity & Citation Velocity
- Impression volume up 49% YoY, but CTR down 30%
- "Traffic flip" projected 2028: AI platforms > traditional search
- AI visitors convert 4.4x higher than traditional search (pre-qualified)
- Citation velocity: frequency + recency + authority of mentions matters
- Share of voice metrics updated in 2025 to reflect prompt volume

### Pattern 25: The Media Coverage Multiplier
- Earned media dominance for AI visibility
- News coverage signals: reaches readers AND informs AI answers
- Press mentions = double duty (human + machine perception)
- Third-party endorsement: stronger than any owned-channel claim
- Media relationships = training data coverage

## SURPRISING FINDINGS

1. **YouTube overtook Reddit as top citation source** - transcript + metadata + structure matter more than engagement
2. **Domain Authority nearly useless for AI** (r=0.18) - authority is now about credentials, not links
3. **Featured snippets dying** (-64% in 6 months) - AI Overviews replaced them entirely
4. **Mobile matters despite desktop dominance** - 48% of B2B buyers research on mobile with AI tools
5. **Brand search volume > backlinks** - 0.334 vs 0.37 correlation; brand building = AI visibility
6. **No word length correlation** - 53.4% of AI citations from pages <1000 words
7. **Freshness starts decaying in 2-3 days** (Perplexity) - not just seasonal/quarterly
8. **Third-party mentions >> brand mentions** - 6.5x more likely cited through others
9. **Wikipedia treated as default knowledge layer** - but only for what Wikipedia covers
10. **AI citations highly inconsistent** - <1 in 100 chance of identical brand list across runs

---

## CONTRADICTIONS & NUANCES

- **Speed matters but not much** (+27% vs content +200-300%), yet crawl frequency depends on it
- **Backlinks correlate less with AI citations (0.37)** than traditional rankings, yet referring domains still matter for crawling
- **Long-form content earns more citations** (2,300+ words = 5.1 vs <500 = 4.1) BUT no correlation between word count and selection
- **Featured snippets declining** while structure that earned them still needed for AI extraction
- **Domain Authority useless** but overall site authority (E-E-A-T) critical
- **PageRank still used internally** but traditional DA metric worthless for AI predictions
- **Proprietary data preferred** yet Wikipedia training data still primary for LLMs
- **Recency critical** yet evergreen content (yearly refresh) still valuable if substance changes

## EMERGING SIGNAL CLUSTERS

### Cluster 1: Credibility & Trust (Highest Weight in AI)
- E-E-A-T signals: experience, expertise, authority, trustworthiness
- Author credentials & bylines
- Third-party validation (reviews, press, media)
- Verifiable facts & sources
- Scientific citations for YMYL

### Cluster 2: Extractability & Structure (High Weight)
- Semantic HTML hierarchy (H2, H3)
- Q&A format + FAQ schema
- Bullet points, numbered lists
- HTML tables vs JavaScript widgets
- Clear passage-level answers (100-150 word sections)

### Cluster 3: Freshness & Activity (High Weight)
- Publication/update recency (30-90 days critical)
- Substance changes (not cosmetic updates)
- Active author presence (social, podcasts, profiles)
- Consistent brand mentions across platforms

### Cluster 4: Knowledge Architecture (Medium-High Weight)
- Topic clustering & topical authority
- Entity relationships & semantic clarity
- Knowledge graph integration
- Related content linking
- Breadcrumb + semantic URL structure

### Cluster 5: Distribution & Authority Signals (Medium Weight)
- Earned media coverage
- Named press release distributors (GlobeNewswire, PR Newswire, Business Wire)
- Third-party platforms (reviews, G2, Wikipedia, forums)
- Brand search volume & mentions
- Backlink velocity (steady growth)

### Cluster 6: Format & Presentation (Medium Weight)
- Multimodal content (text + image + video + data)
- Mobile optimization & responsive design
- Core Web Vitals baseline (then diminishing returns)
- Schema markup (especially FAQ, Article, Product)
- Accessibility compliance (WCAG)

### Cluster 7: Content Specificity (Medium Weight)
- Original research & proprietary data
- Unique insights vs rehashed information
- Primary sources over aggregation
- Case studies & frameworks
- Implementation examples

## PLATFORM DIFFERENCES (Critical for Strategy)

**Google AI Overviews:**
- Cites from top 10 most; 99.5% of sources ranking
- Most like traditional search (behaves conservatively)
- 85% citations from last 2 years
- Semantic completeness scores: 8.5+/10 = 4.2× more likely

**ChatGPT (Search/SearchGPT):**
- Strongest recency bias (76.4% updated in 30 days)
- Cites Wikipedia heavily (nearly 1 in 6 answers)
- Values conversational + comprehensive content
- Answer capsules + original data most citeable
- FCP <0.4s gets 6.7 citations vs >1.13s = 2.1

**Perplexity:**
- Highest freshness requirement (2-3 day decay)
- 50% citations from 2025 alone
- Domain authority matters (~15% of algorithm)
- Direct, unambiguous answers preferred
- YouTube now dominant source

**Claude:**
- Values nuanced, multi-perspective analysis
- Ad-free model (no sponsored links)
- Augmentation mode (user iteration) = 52% of conversations
- Limited direct research on citation patterns yet

**Bing Copilot:**
- Real-time retrieval emphasis
- Authority + freshness + structure critical
- Integrated with Bing index
- More prominent citations showing publisher content

## RECOMMENDATIONS FOR CHECKSTHAT PRODUCT

1. **Build citation tracking across ALL platforms** - AI patterns differ significantly
2. **Score E-E-A-T signals** - easier to measure than raw backlinks, more predictive
3. **Track third-party mentions separately** - more valuable than brand's own content
4. **Monitor freshness decay rates** - platform-specific (2-3 days Perplexity vs 30+ days ChatGPT)
5. **Measure multimodal score** - combination of formats matters
6. **Track semantic completeness** - extractability is core AEO signal
7. **Include author credibility scoring** - bylines/bios increasingly critical
8. **Monitor consistency metrics** - citation stability across repeated queries
9. **Benchmark platform differences** - one size doesn't fit all
10. **Track earning velocity** - speed of citation growth as signal
