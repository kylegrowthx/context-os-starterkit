## TL;DR

- **Pre-Kickoff (Day -14 to Day 0):** Research, relationship-building, and preparation. Don't skip this.
- **Sprint (Weeks 1-8):** Discovery → Strategy → Calibration → Production → Decision → Scale or Wrap.
- **Handoff:** Standardized package that sets the next team up for success.
- **Goal:** Reduce surprises. Improve conversion confidence by Week 6.

---

## Part 1: Pre-Kickoff (Day -14 to Day 0)

Pre-kickoff is where sprints are won or lost. Show up to kickoff ultra-prepared and you instantly earn trust.

### The Pre-Kickoff Gate

Nothing moves forward until this checklist is complete:

| Day | EM Task | ME Task | Deliverable |
|-----|---------|---------|-------------|
| Day -14 | Receive handoff from Sales | — | Handoff notes reviewed |
| Day -13 | Send first Slack message + intro | — | Client contacted |
| Day -12 | Run research pipeline | Get briefed by EM | Research started |
| Day -10 | Share research insight with client | — | Research artifact shared |
| Day -7 | Send strategic question | — | Question sent |
| Day -5 | Send kickoff agenda | — | Agenda confirmed |
| Day -3 | Final research review | Review research | Ready for kickoff |

**Health Signals (Pre-Kickoff):**

| Signal | Green | Red |
|--------|-------|-----|
| Tech SEO audit | Everything looks solid | Major technical issues |
| CheckThat setup | Ticket submitted, account provisioned | Not started |
| Looker dashboard | Ticket submitted | Not started |
| Research pipeline | Complete, insights shared | Incomplete or not run |
| Client communication | Responsive, engaged | Silent, delayed responses |

### What You Need from Sales

1. **Handoff notes** — Key priorities, stakeholder preferences, immediate deliverables expected
2. **Call recordings** — Watch at least the discovery and close calls
3. **Contract details** — Scope, timeline, specific commitments made

### What You Build Before Kickoff

**Internal Prep Doc (Week 00 Research Overview)**
Organize findings under:
- Company Background
- Product/Service
- Audience & Goals
- Competitors
- Brand Voice

**For each category, add a "What Stands Out" bullet list** — the insights that will make you sound prepared.

### Research Deep Dives

| Research Area | What to Capture |
|---------------|-----------------|
| Website & Collateral | USPs emphasized, main audience, tone |
| Product Hands-On | Sign up for trial, watch demos, read G2/Capterra reviews |
| Persona & ICP | Job roles, challenges, buying journey, 2-3 pain points per persona |
| Competitors | Top 3-5 direct competitors, content gaps, differentiators |
| Domain/Industry | Publications, influencers, 15-20 keyword brainstorm |
| Existing Content | Audit latest 5-10 posts, identify quick wins |

### Client Checklist (Pre-Kickoff)

Send to client by Day -7:

- [ ] Share existing materials (brand decks, style guides, product sheets, competitor intel)
- [ ] Provide access to analytics, prior content docs, CMS
- [ ] Confirm kickoff meeting date/time
- [ ] Clarify stakeholders (who should be in Slack and Notion)
- [ ] Highlight immediate priorities (product launches, urgent topics)

### Expected Behaviors (Pre-Kickoff)

**EM should:**
- Proactively reach out, don't wait for client
- Share value before asking for anything (research insight on Day -10)
- Over-communicate in first week (Golden Rule: client shouldn't go 2 days without hearing from us)

**Client should:**
- Respond within 48 hours to requests
- Provide access to systems before kickoff
- Confirm key stakeholders

**If client is silent for 5+ days pre-kickoff → escalate to Director.**

## Part 2: The Sprint (Weeks 1-8)

### Week 1: Kickoff and Learn Quickly

**Mission:** Rapidly absorb everything about the client's business, audience, and competitive landscape.

**Risk We're Targeting:** Building content for the wrong audience or solving the wrong problems.

**Success Criteria:**
- Complete business context documented
- Key stakeholders aligned
- Content gaps identified
- No major unknowns remaining

**Deliverables:**
1. Access to Systems: GSC, GA, CMS access and walkthrough
2. "What We Heard" document sent <24 hours after kickoff
3. All key artifacts generated and enriched
4. Deep Dives Scheduled:
   - Product Deep Dive (1hr)
   - Expert Deep Dive (1hr)
   - Customer/User Deep Dive (1hr)
5. Recurring weekly meeting scheduled
6. Comprehensive follow-up with semi-shaped lanes

**Health Signals (Week 1-2):**

| Signal | Green | Red |
|--------|-------|-----|
| Artifacts | Approved, enriched with feedback | No response, major revisions |
| Content Strategy | Approved, clear direction | Client pushback, scope change |
| Calibration Article | Delivered | Not started |
| Relationship | Enthusiastic, positive, responsive | Distant, transactional |
| Stakeholder | Ownership clear, decision-maker engaged | Unclear who approves |
| Access | All systems provided | Missing key access |

**Triggers (Any of these = escalate):**
- Key decision-maker skips kickoff
- No feedback on artifacts after 5+ days
- Contradictory inputs from stakeholders
- Unknown approval chain
- No product deep-dive scheduled by end of Week 2

---

### Week 2: Set the Foundation, Align on Direction

**Mission:** Establish the strategic framework that guides all future content decisions.

**Risk We're Targeting:** Scattered content efforts that don't build toward meaningful business outcomes.

**Deliverables:**
1. Sign-off on all key artifacts:
   - Writing Guidelines
   - Company Context
   - Audience Personas
   - Direct and Audience Competitors
   - Calibration Guide
   - Examples of Good
   - Product Specific Artifact (Feature Matrix)
2. Finish all deep-dives
3. First weekly check-in meeting
4. Sample publication/article sent
5. ContentOS fully fleshed out with topical clusters and recommended assignments

**Week 2 Health Check (The Week 2 Alarm):**

| Checkpoint | Status |
|------------|--------|
| Calibration article approved? | ☐ |
| Product deep dive completed? | ☐ |
| Response time < 1 week? | ☐ |
| All approvers identified? | ☐ |
| CMS access confirmed? | ☐ |
| Strategy feedback received? | ☐ |

**Scoring:**
- 1 item unchecked = GREEN (monitor, proceed)
- 2-3 items unchecked = YELLOW (escalate to Director)
- 4+ items unchecked = RED (Director reaches out directly to client)

---

### Weeks 3-4: Set Priorities, Define Great, Remove Publishing Blocks

**Mission:** Establish what excellent content looks like and eliminate all friction from creation to publication.

**Risk We're Targeting:** Producing content that doesn't meet quality standards, or content getting stuck in approval cycles.

**Health Signals (Weeks 3-4):**

| Signal | Green | Red |
|--------|-------|-----|
| ContentOS | Approved | Still debating topics |
| Calibration Article | Approved, quality solid | 3+ revision rounds, vague feedback |
| Production | Ramping up to first 3 articles | Blocked, not producing |
| Publishing | Article in CMS (staging) | No publishing path |
| Decision Maker | Involved, engaged | "Word babysitter" behavior |
| Review Process | Clearly identified, working | Unknown, blocked |
| Early Results | Showed to client | Not shared |
| Client Updates | Regular, proactive | Silence |

**Deliverables:**
1. Looker Dashboard set up
2. POD assignment decided and aligned
3. Hand-off brief for director and POD created

**The Calibration Test:**
- 1-2 revision rounds = Normal calibration
- 3+ revision rounds with no convergence = Calibration FAILURE → Stop. Escalate.

**If client says "it doesn't feel right" without specifics:**
→ Push for examples: "Can you show me what 'right' looks like?"
→ If still vague after round 3, trigger Quality Bar Alarm

---

### Weeks 5-6: Build Rhythm, Start Measuring, Decide on Continuation

**Mission:** Establish sustainable production cadence and evaluate progress against goals.

**Risk We're Targeting:** Unsustainable production, publishing without learning, or continuing an approach that isn't working.

**Health Signals (Weeks 5-6):**

| Signal | Green | Red |
|--------|-------|-----|
| Content Production | Running smoothly | Stalled, blocked |
| Indexation | Getting indexed | Not indexed |
| Performance | Capturing impressions and clicks | No visibility |
| Feedback Loop | Client feedback fed back into pipeline | Feedback ignored |
| Quality Trend | Improving over time | Declining or stagnant |

**Deliverables:**
1. LLM tracking set up (Looker + Scrunch)
2. ME, CM, and Lead aligned with new director
3. Performance review and ROI analysis
4. Clear decision on engagement continuation

**Week 6 is the Circuit Breaker:**
- If working → Ramp up
- If not working → Pivot or wind down
- No ambiguity allowed

---

### Weeks 7-8: Scale Up or Wrap Up

**Mission:** Scale successful approaches OR finalize engagement with complete documentation.

**If Ramping Up:**
- Expand content production
- Implement advanced distribution strategies
- Optimize high-performing pieces
- Begin conversion funnel development

**If Wrapping Up:**
- Deliver comprehensive playbook
- Complete final performance analysis
- Transfer all assets and knowledge
- Provide 90-day strategic roadmap

## Part 3: Handoff After Conversion

The post-sprint handoff package must be standardized. Sloppy handoffs kill accounts.

### The Handoff Package (Required)

| Section | What to Include |
|---------|-----------------|
| **What Was Promised** | SOW commitments, sales promises, client expectations |
| **What Was Delivered** | Actual outputs, deviations from plan, why |
| **The Strategy** | Content pillars, audience, positioning, why this strategy |
| **Pipeline State** | Current production queue, calibration status, known limitations |
| **Open Risks** | Stakeholder concerns, quality issues, relationship flags |
| **Open Tickets** | Technical issues, access problems, outstanding requests |
| **First 2 Weeks** | Exactly what the next team must do immediately |

### Handoff Meeting Agenda

1. **Overview** (5 min) — Client summary, key stakeholders, relationship health
2. **Strategy Review** (10 min) — What we learned, why this direction
3. **Pipeline Walkthrough** (15 min) — Live tour of artifacts, ContentOS, active assignments
4. **Risks & Flags** (10 min) — What to watch, known concerns, escalation history
5. **Questions** (10 min) — New team asks, EM answers

### What the Next Team Must Do (First 2 Weeks)

1. Introduce themselves to client
2. Review all artifacts and pipeline state
3. Confirm production rhythm is maintaining
4. Schedule QBR prep meeting
5. Address any open risks from handoff

## Customer Communication Templates

### Welcome Message (Pre-Kickoff)

```
Hi [Name],

Welcome! I'm [EM Name], your Engagement Manager for this sprint. I'll be your main point of contact throughout our engagement.

Over the next 8 weeks, we'll work together to build a content engine that drives [specific goal from SOW].

Before we kick off, I've started researching your space and have some initial questions. I'll send those over in the next few days.

For now, could you share:
- Brand guidelines or style docs
- Access to your CMS and analytics
- Any key stakeholders who should be involved

Looking forward to working together.

[EM Name]
```

## Appendix: The Two-Track System

**Shaping Track:** Each week we analyze performance data and stakeholder feedback to shape the following week's approach. This happens in parallel with execution.

**Building Track:** Each week we execute the shaped work while gathering signals for the shaping track. Clear separation prevents analysis paralysis.

**Week 6 Decision Point:** The two-track system culminates in a clear decision based on measurable progress toward conversion goals.
