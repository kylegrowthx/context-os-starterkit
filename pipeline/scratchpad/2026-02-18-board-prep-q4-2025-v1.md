## 1. Customer Conversion & Churn Learnings

### The Headline Numbers

- **Total churned ARR (Q4 2025 - Q1 2026):** $3.61M
- **Surge AI alone:** $2.76M (77% of all churn)
- **Average customer tenure at churn:** 8 months
- **Q1 2026 renewal rate (excluding Surge):** Projected 70-80%

### The Surge Post-Mortem

**What happened:**
- Started with $120K Strategy Sprint (Jun 2025)
- Expanded to $2.76M Growth Execution (Aug 2025) — our largest contract ever
- Churned Jan 14, 2026 — 6-month customer
- **No churn reason recorded in HubSpot** — critical documentation gap

**What we know about Surge's profile:**
- They appeared to check all ICP boxes: B2B tech, well-funded, growing, content investment
- $2.76M ACV suggests they had budget authority and believed in content
- Short tenure (6 months) suggests something broke during Growth Execution, not Strategy Sprint (they expanded after sprint)

**What we need to understand:**
1. Was this a champion problem? Did the buyer leave or lose influence?
2. Was this a delivery problem? Did we fail to prove value at scale?
3. Was this a fit problem? Did we misqualify despite apparent ICP match?
4. Was this a timing problem? Budget cuts, strategy pivot, M&A?

**Key question for board:** Surge's churn ($2.76M) is bigger than most companies' entire ARR. What ICP refinement or qualification process changes would have flagged this risk earlier?

### Broader Churn Patterns

| Churn Driver | Example | ARR Lost | Preventable? |
|--------------|---------|----------|--------------|
| **Cost/value perception** | SchoolAI ($216K) — felt they could replace us with cheaper agency | $216K | Maybe — earlier ROI proof, tighter value delivery |
| **New decision-maker** | Superhuman ($144K) — new VP chose different direction | $144K | Hard — champion risk is structural |
| **Service delivery** | Exec ($144K) — poor backlinking performance, reporting issues | $144K | Yes — QA and delivery rigor |
| **ROI timing** | Biologica ($72K) — survival mode, needed immediate returns | $72K | Partially — better expectation setting |
| **Channel shift** | Rapyd ($108K) — moved budget to events | $108K | No — strategic reallocation |

### ICP Lessons Learned

**Our ICP says $10M+ raised, growing, single decision-maker, fast approvals.**

What we're seeing:
1. **Champion risk is underweighted.** Superhuman's new VP killed the engagement. Champion turnover should be a tracked risk factor.
2. **"Early-stage" isn't just about funding.** Biologica was funded but in survival mode. Growth trajectory matters more than cash on hand.
3. **Expansion velocity ≠ retention signal.** Surge expanded 23x in 2 months, then churned 4 months later. Fast expansion can mask underlying fit issues.

**Proposed ICP refinements for discussion:**
- Add "champion stability" factor (tenure, org chart, influence level)
- Weight growth trajectory heavier (20%+ headcount growth, not just recent raise)
- Red flag: Rapid expansion within first 90 days (signals over-commitment vs. calibrated growth)

### Process Gaps Identified

1. **Documentation:** 3 of 8 churned accounts have no churn reason recorded (Surge, Teero, Okta)
2. **Renewal management:** SchoolAI — "we didn't get ahead of this renewal and assumed it would be fine"
3. **Champion tracking:** No systematic detection of new decision-makers entering accounts

## 2. Market Positioning: AEO/GEO Category

### The Challenge

> "One of my portfolio companies asked ChatGPT 'What are the best tools to use for AEO/GEO' and we didn't show up."

This raises two questions:
1. Are we being seen in the same category as Profound, Scrunch, Peec?
2. Do we *want* to be seen in that category?

### How the Market Sees AEO/GEO

The "AEO tools" category is crowded (163+ vendors) and increasingly commoditized:

| Player | Positioning | Funding | Core Approach |
|--------|-------------|---------|---------------|
| **Profound** | Enterprise AEO platform | $58M | Closed dashboard, charge per prompt |
| **Scrunch** | AI brand visibility | $19M | 500+ brands, tiered pricing |
| **Peec AI** | Multi-language AEO | $29M | Auto-prompts, daily probing |
| **Athena HQ** | AI search intelligence | YC-backed | Ex-Google team, technical credibility |

**What they all have in common:**
- "Paywall first, value later"
- Brand-focused, market-blind (you see your dashboard, not the category)
- Data dump without strategy

### Our Counter-Position

We're explicitly **not** building AEO dashboard #164. Our strategy:

> "We're building the **open AI visibility index for B2B** — not another closed AEO dashboard."

**The Crunchbase/G2/SimilarWeb playbook:**
- Make data open → Rank for searches → Traffic brings data → More data improves rankings → Repeat

**Key differentiator:** Historical data moat. Once we've tracked a category for 6 months, competitors can't backfill that history.

### Should We Want to Be in the AEO Category?

**Arguments for being seen as AEO:**
- That's where buyers are looking right now
- Easier sales motion ("We're like Profound but better/cheaper")
- CheckThat naturally fits this category

**Arguments against being seen as AEO:**
- Category is crowded and commoditizing
- AI providers may offer intent data directly (platform risk)
- We're actually selling something bigger (content operations, not just monitoring)

### Recommended Positioning

**Two-level positioning:**

1. **CheckThat** — Position as AEO category (freemium, $200/mo max)
   - This is our marketing budget, not our core product
   - Counter-position against expensive enterprise tools
   - Plant flag in the category to generate leads

2. **GrowthX/ContentOS** — Position as "AI Growth System for Websites"
   - Bigger vision than AEO monitoring
   - "The complete AI system for website growth"
   - This is where the $200K+ contracts live

**The messaging hierarchy:**
- CheckThat = "How do you show up in AI?" (awareness)
- ContentOS = "How do you win in AI?" (action)
- GrowthX = "The partner that helps you compound" (relationship)

### Why We Don't Show Up in AI Answers

Likely reasons:
1. **CheckThat just launched** (first week of February) — no signal yet
2. **We haven't optimized for AEO queries ourselves** — ironic but fixable
3. **Our organic content strategy is still ramping** — need brand pages, answer pages, category pages ranking

**Immediate actions:**
- Ensure CheckThat site is optimized for "AEO tools," "GEO tools," "AI visibility" queries
- Build answer pages for "best AEO tools" with us prominently featured
- Track our own AI visibility in the category we claim to own

## 3. Near-Term Product Leverage & Bottlenecks

### The Constraint

**Current state:** 2 kickoffs/week = ~74 kickoffs/year (2025 actual)
**FY26 target:** 140 kickoffs/year = ~2.7 kickoffs/week average, exiting at 4/week

At 80% conversion and $204K ACV, the difference between 2 and 4 kickoffs/week is:
- 2/week: ~$13M new ARR
- 4/week: ~$23M new ARR

**Kickoff capacity is the single biggest lever for revenue growth.**

### What Constrains Kickoffs?

| Constraint | What's Happening | Leverage Potential |
|------------|------------------|-------------------|
| **Sales pipeline** | 33% win rate, 6-week cycle, only 1 AE until recently | Hired Nigel (2nd AE), need at least 1 more in Q1 |
| **Sales booking** | Currently booking 4-6 weeks out with 1 AE | 2nd AE increases capacity, but need pipeline to fill |
| **Strategy Sprint capacity** | Team can run X sprints in parallel | Unclear if team is at max or has headroom |
| **ContentOS onboarding** | Each sprint requires platform setup, training | Opportunity for automation and templates |
| **Delivery pod capacity** | Need pods ready to absorb converted customers | Team grew 80→96, but diminishing returns noted |

### Where Product Can Create Leverage

**The Q1 TLDR from board deck:**
> "2026 is about building capacity through leverage, not headcount."

**Priority leverage opportunities:**

1. **ContentOS "Closed Loop"**
   - Currently building to improve scalability and stickiness
   - Goal: Reduce time-to-value in Strategy Sprints
   - Metric: Days from kickoff to first published content

2. **Strategy Sprint Templating**
   - Standardize sprint deliverables and workflows
   - AI-assisted strategy generation (using brand context)
   - Metric: Hours of strategist time per sprint

3. **Onboarding Automation**
   - Reduce forward-deployed engineer time per account
   - Self-serve CMS integration where possible
   - Metric: Setup hours per new customer

4. **Delivery Automation**
   - AI workflows for content production (Output.ai)
   - Reduce editor touch per piece
   - Metric: Editor hours per published asset

### Proposed "Product Leverage" Metrics

For board accountability, suggest tracking:

| Metric | Current | Q1 Target | Q2 Target |
|--------|---------|-----------|-----------|
| **Kickoffs/week capacity** | 2 | 3 | 4 |
| **Sprint conversion rate** | 70% | 80% | 80% |
| **Days to first content** | ? | -20% | -40% |
| **Setup hours per customer** | ? | -15% | -30% |
| **Editor hours per asset** | ? | -10% | -25% |

## 4. Product Leverage Accountability Metrics

### Current Sensitivity Analysis Summary

From fiscal plan, the four variables that matter:
1. **Sprint capacity** (140 target)
2. **Conversion rate** (80% target)
3. **ACV** ($204K target)
4. **Renewal rate** (80% target)

**Key insight:** Sprint capacity × conversion has more leverage than ACV × renewal. Operational throughput matters more than pricing or retention in FY26.

### Proposed Accountability Framework

**Tier 1: Leading Indicators (Weekly)**
These predict whether we'll hit capacity and conversion goals.

| Metric | Definition | Why It Matters |
|--------|------------|----------------|
| **Pipeline coverage** | Open deals ÷ sprint slots next 8 weeks | Can we fill capacity? |
| **Sprint NPS (weekly)** | Customer satisfaction during sprint | Predicts conversion |
| **Content velocity** | Assets approved ÷ assets planned | Leading signal of sprint health |

**Tier 2: Efficiency Metrics (Monthly)**
These measure whether product is creating leverage.

| Metric | Definition | Why It Matters |
|--------|------------|----------------|
| **ARR per FTE** | Total ARR ÷ total headcount | Are we getting more efficient? |
| **Revenue per Delivery FTE** | ARR ÷ Delivery team headcount | Delivery leverage specifically |
| **Sprint cost** | Total sprint cost ÷ sprints completed | Cost efficiency of conversion motion |
| **Setup hours/customer** | Total setup hours ÷ new customers | Onboarding leverage |

**Tier 3: Outcome Metrics (Quarterly)**
These confirm the strategy is working.

| Metric | Definition | Target |
|--------|------------|--------|
| **Sprint conversion** | Sprints converted ÷ sprints completed | 80% |
| **Kickoff capacity** | Max kickoffs we could handle | 4/week by Q2 end |
| **Gross margin** | Revenue - COGS | >75% by year end |

### ARR per Headcount Analysis

**Current state (Q4 2025):**
- $12.1M ARR
- 96 employees
- **ARR/HC = $126K**

**FY26 target:**
- $33.8M ARR
- ~134 employees (96 + 38 planned)
- **ARR/HC target = $252K** (2x improvement)

This 2x improvement in ARR/HC is only possible through product leverage. Pure headcount scaling would require ~270 people at current productivity.

| Category | Annual Spend |
|----------|--------------|

| SEO tools (SEMrush, Ahrefs) | $2-10K |

| Technical SEO audits | $5K |
| CMS & web development | $50-150K |
| Project management overhead | $10-30K |
| **Total** | **$300-500K** |

**Our current pricing:**
- Strategy Sprint: $20K (8 weeks)
- Growth Execution: $15-18K/mo → $180-216K/year
- **Total year one:** ~$200-240K

**The gap:** We're capturing 40-80% of their total spend, but there's room to expand.

### Current Product Pricing

| Product | Price | Model | Target |
|---------|-------|-------|--------|
| **CheckThat** | $0-$200/mo | Freemium PLG | Lead gen, data moat |
| **ContentOS (Self-Operated)** | ~$2K/mo | Platform subscription | Teams running own content |
| **ContentOS (GrowthX-Operated)** | $10K+/mo | Full-service | Current core business |
| **AI Led Growth** | $50/mo | Community | Demand gen, talent pipeline |

### CheckThat Pricing Deep Dive

**Proposed tiers (from pricing doc):**

| Tier | Price | Prompts | Probe Frequency | Margin |
|------|-------|---------|-----------------|--------|
| **Free** | $0 | 50 custom + 100K shared | Weekly | -$6/mo |
| **Pro** | $249/mo | 500 | Near daily | 61% |
| **Business** | $1,500/mo | 2,000 | Near daily | 68% |

**Strategic intent:**
- Free = growth engine (more generous than competitors)
- Pro = self-serve, credit card, filters for right customers
- Business = sales-led, 18-month contracts, managed service

**vs. Competitors:**

| | CheckThat Pro | Profound | Scrunch | Peec AI |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Price | $249/mo | $399/mo | $700/mo | €199/mo |
| Prompts | 500 | 200 | 700 | 100 |
| Free tier | Yes (50) | No | No | Trial only |

### Packaging Questions for Discussion

1. **Should CheckThat Business convert to ContentOS?**
   - Currently $1,500/mo for managed prompts
   - vs. ContentOS Self-Operated at $2K/mo
   - Risk of cannibalization vs. smooth upgrade path?

2. **How do we price the bundle?**
   - CheckThat included with ContentOS?
   - Discount for using both?
   - Or keep fully separate?

3. **Where does ExpertLayer fit?**
   - Marketplace take-rate model
   - Integrated with ContentOS
   - Adds to customer value without raising price

4. **Should we have enterprise tiers?**
   - Current ACV ~$200K is already "enterprise"
   - But no formal enterprise pricing or features
   - Security, compliance, SLA differentiation?

### The Platform Gap Opportunity

The $300-500K annual spend insight suggests:

**Current capture:** ~$200K (content production + strategy)
**Potential capture:** Additional value from:
- AI visibility monitoring (CheckThat) — +$3-18K/year
- Expert marketplace (ExpertLayer) — variable
- Advanced workflows (Output.ai) — TBD
- CMS/web services — opportunity area

**Question:** Do we want to expand into more of the website stack (vertical integration) or stay focused on content (horizontal excellence)?
