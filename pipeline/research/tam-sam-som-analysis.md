## 1. Market Context: The AI Transformation of B2B Marketing & Engineering

### The Macro Picture

Global AI spending is forecast to reach **$2.52 trillion in 2026**, a 44% increase year-over-year (Gartner, January 2026). Enterprise AI adoption has reached **78% of organizations** using AI in at least one business function (McKinsey, 2025), up from 55% just one year prior. By 2026, **40% of enterprise applications** will integrate task-specific AI agents, up from less than 5% in 2025 (Gartner).

For B2B marketing specifically: **91% of B2B marketers** now use content marketing, **83% of B2B marketing decision-makers** expect marketing investments to grow in 2026 (Forrester), and **94% of CMOs** plan to increase AEO/GEO investments in 2026 (Conductor). Average B2B marketing spend stands at **8–10% of revenue** in 2025, with early-stage SaaS companies dedicating **20–30%**.

### The Three Converging Shifts

1. **Content creation is being automated by AI** — 60% of marketing leaders decreased agency spend due to AI in 2025 (Typeface research). The "service-as-software" model is replacing traditional agencies.

2. **Search is fragmenting toward AI** — Gartner predicts 25% of organic search traffic will shift to AI chatbots by 2026. Zero-click searches already make up 65% of all Google searches. Google AI Overviews appear in 30%+ of queries.

3. **AI agents are eating software** — Goldman Sachs estimates agents may account for 60%+ of the software market by 2030. Companies need infrastructure to build, deploy, and manage AI workflows.

GrowthX's ecosystem sits at the intersection of all three.

## 2. Market 1: GrowthX (AI Content Marketing Services + ContentOS Platform)

### Product Overview

GrowthX provides AI-powered content marketing services to B2B tech companies through Strategy Sprints (2-week, $15K) converting to 12-month contracts ($200K+ ACV). ContentOS is the proprietary platform that powers delivery. The service model combines AI automation with human experts — a "service-as-software" approach delivering 70%+ gross margins at scale.

### 2.1 Total Addressable Market (TAM): $107B

The TAM for GrowthX spans two overlapping markets:

**A) Content Marketing Services Market**

The global content marketing market is estimated at **$525–$576 billion in 2025**, growing at **13.5% CAGR** to ~$990 billion by 2030 (Mordor Intelligence, Business Research Insights). However, these broad figures include all content spend globally. The more relevant frame is the **outsourced/managed content services** slice.

The outsourced digital marketing services market — including agencies, managed services, and platforms — represents approximately **$107 billion in 2025** (Statista projection for content marketing revenue, confirmed by Research Dive's $107.5B estimate for 2026). This is the appropriate TAM: the money companies spend on external partners to plan, create, and distribute content.

**B) AI Content Creation Platform Market**

The AI-powered content creation market adds a platform layer, estimated at **$3.5–$19.6 billion in 2025** (varying by scope) and growing at **18–32% CAGR** to $7–$80 billion by 2030. The ContentOS platform participates in this market but is currently bundled with services.

**TAM = $107B** (outsourced content marketing services, 2025)

### 2.2 Serviceable Addressable Market (SAM): $12.8B

The SAM narrows to GrowthX's actual target: **B2B tech companies with $10M+ raised or 500+ employees, DA 40+, already investing in content marketing, with budget for $200K+/year managed content services.**

**Bottom-up calculation:**

- There are approximately **22,100 funded SaaS companies** in the US alone (Tracxn), with ~14,000 being Series A+ funded. Globally, the figure is higher.
- Filtering for GrowthX's ICP ($10M+ raised, B2B tech, 500+ employees or well-funded): estimated **~8,000–12,000 companies** in North America and Europe.
- Average content marketing spend for these companies: **$100K–$300K/year** on outsourced content (based on $5K–$10K/month benchmark from industry data, scaled up for the ICP's company size).
- SAM = ~10,000 target accounts × ~$200K average annual spend = **~$2.0B** for managed services alone.
- Including the broader set of B2B companies spending $50K–$200K on content: ~64,000 companies × $200K average = **$12.8B**.

**SAM = $12.8B** (B2B tech companies willing to spend $50K+/year on managed content marketing)

### 2.3 Serviceable Obtainable Market (SOM): $270M by 2028

**Growth-rate SOM (3x annual compounding):**

- GrowthX services end 2026: ~$29.6M ARR (fiscal plan: $33.8M total minus $4.2M CheckThat)
- End 2027: $29.6M × 3x = ~$89M
- End 2028: $89M × 3x = ~$267M
- Rounded: **~$270M ARR by end 2028**

**Validation:** This implies ~1,350 active accounts at ~$200K average ACV, representing **~2.1% market share** of the $12.8B SAM. The growth-and-valuation doc projects $122.5M total company ARR by H2-2027, consistent with 3x+ compounding. Harvey AI reached $100M+ ARR in ~3 years on a similar service-as-software model. For comparison, SEMrush serves ~117,000 customers at $3,500 average = $455M ARR — GrowthX targets fewer customers at 57x higher ACVs.

**SOM = $270M** (3-year, 3x annual growth, ~1,350 accounts, 2.1% SAM penetration)

## 3. Market 2: CheckThat (AI Visibility / AEO Platform)

### Product Overview

CheckThat is an open AI visibility index for B2B, designed as a freemium PLG product. Free tier provides visibility scores; paid tiers ($249/mo and $1,500/mo) add monitoring, recommendations, and competitor benchmarking. CheckThat is the top-of-funnel acquisition engine for the GrowthX ecosystem, targeting $4.2M ARR in 2026.

### 3.1 Total Addressable Market (TAM): $75B

CheckThat operates at the intersection of SEO tools and the emerging AEO/GEO category.

**A) SEO Software & Services Market**

The global SEO services market is estimated at **$75–$85 billion in 2025** (Grand View Research, MarkNtel, Mordor Intelligence), growing at **12–13.5% CAGR** to **$149–$172 billion by 2030**. This includes both software tools and services.

The SEO software-only segment is estimated at **$33–$37 billion in 2024–2025** (360iResearch), growing to **$72 billion by 2030**.

**B) Emerging AEO/GEO Category**

There is no standalone AEO market size report yet, as the category barely existed before 2024. However, the signals are powerful: **$200M+ in disclosed VC funding** across 24+ AEO platforms by end of 2025, with **97% of CMOs reporting positive impact** from AEO/GEO and **94% planning to increase investments** in 2026 (Conductor).

The AEO category will likely be carved out of — and eventually expand — the existing SEO tools market.

**TAM = $75B** (SEO services + software market, which is transitioning to include AEO/GEO, 2025)

### 3.2 Serviceable Addressable Market (SAM): $4.2B

The SAM narrows to companies actively investing in or evaluating AI search visibility tools.

**Top-down calculation:**
- SEMrush has ~117,000 paying customers, Ahrefs has ~50,000 — together representing ~167,000 companies already paying for SEO tools.
- Early AEO adoption: 56% of CMOs made significant AEO/GEO investment in 2025 (Conductor), suggesting ~50% of current SEO tool users will add AEO tools within 2 years.
- Target market: ~250,000 companies that currently use SEO tools or will adopt AEO tools.
- Average spend on AI visibility tooling: ~$3,000–$18,000/year (based on CheckThat's $249–$1,500/mo pricing and competitor pricing from Profound, Scrunch, Peec).
- SAM = 250,000 companies × ~$8,400 average midpoint spend × ~50% adoption rate in 3 years = **~$1.05B** (bottom-up, conservative)

**Top-down validation (yields higher estimate):**
- SEMrush's $455M ARR serves a fraction of the SEO tools market
- The AEO tools market is additive to SEO (not replacement)
- AEO/GEO tools will represent 5–8% of the $75B SEO market within 3 years = **$3.75–$6.0B**
- Mid-point of bottom-up ($1.05B) and top-down ($4.9B) = ~$3.0B

**SAM = $3.0B** (B2B companies investing in AI visibility monitoring/optimization; blended bottom-up and top-down estimate)

> *Note: Bottom-up and top-down methods diverge significantly here, reflecting the uncertainty of sizing a nascent category. The bottom-up is conservative (assumes only current SEO tool users); the top-down captures the broader shift. We use the blended mid-point.*

### 3.3 Serviceable Obtainable Market (SOM): $51M by 2028

**Growth-rate SOM (3.5x annual compounding — PLG products grow faster):**

- CheckThat end 2026: $4.2M ARR (fiscal plan)
- End 2027: $4.2M × 3.5x = ~$14.7M
- End 2028: $14.7M × 3.5x = ~$51.5M
- Rounded: **~$51M ARR by end 2028**

**Validation:** This implies ~12,000 paying customers at ~$350/mo average, representing ~1.7% of the $3.0B SAM. The 3.5x growth rate is conservative for a PLG SaaS product in a hypergrowth category — Peec AI hit $4M ARR in just 10 months, and the open index flywheel (free data → SEO → signups → paid conversion) compounds faster than traditional SaaS.

**SOM = $51M** (3-year, 3.5x annual growth, PLG-driven, ~12,000 paying accounts)

The long-term vision includes an open-source agent framework and a workflow marketplace. Today, it serves as technical credibility and a services revenue stream; tomorrow, it's a platform play.

### 4.1 Total Addressable Market (TAM): $52.6B

Output.ai's TAM spans AI engineering services and the AI agents/workflow automation market.

**A) AI Agents Market**

The AI agents market is estimated at **$7.8–$8.0 billion in 2025**, projected to reach **$42.7–$52.6 billion by 2030** at a **41–46% CAGR** (MarketsandMarkets, Grand View Research, BCC Research). This is one of the fastest-growing segments in all of tech.

**B) AI Consulting/Engineering Services Market**

The AI engineering market was valued at **$10.6 billion in 2024**, expected to reach **$32.9 billion by 2030** at **20.8% CAGR** (Virtue Market Research). The broader AI consulting services market is estimated at **$11–$22 billion in 2025**, growing at **26–36% CAGR**.

**C) Workflow Automation Market**

The workflow automation market is valued at **$23.8 billion in 2025**, forecast to reach **$37–$79 billion by 2030** (depending on scope), at **10–22% CAGR** (Mordor Intelligence, Research and Markets).

**TAM = $52.6B** (AI agents market by 2030, the primary market for Output.ai's platform vision)

### 4.2 Serviceable Addressable Market (SAM): $3.9B

The SAM narrows to companies that need external help building AI agents and workflows — specifically mid-market and enterprise companies that want custom AI solutions but lack internal ML/AI engineering teams.

**Bottom-up calculation:**

## 6. Market Timing Analysis

### Why Now

1. **AI search inflection point (2025–2026):** ChatGPT went from 300M to 800M weekly users in under a year. AI Overviews went from 6.5% to 30%+ of Google queries. The shift is happening faster than SEO emerged in the 2000s.

2. **Service-as-software window:** The 2–3 year window where AI is good enough to augment but not replace human expertise is exactly when service-as-software companies can build dominant positions. Harvey AI ($3B valuation) and Mercor ($2B valuation) prove this model works.

3. **Enterprise AI agent adoption:** 40% of enterprise apps will have AI agents by end of 2026 (Gartner). Companies need help building this — Output.ai is well-timed.

4. **Budget expansion:** 83% of B2B marketing leaders increasing budgets in 2026. Brand awareness + content marketing is the #1 investment priority (16% of spend).

## 7. Key Risks & Considerations

### Market Risks
- **AI tool commoditization:** As AI writing/content tools improve, the "services" portion of GrowthX could face pricing pressure. Mitigation: strategy and expertise are harder to commoditize than content production.
- **AEO market consolidation:** 160+ vendors will collapse to 5–10 meaningful players within 2 years. CheckThat must establish position before consolidation.
- **Platform risk:** Google, Microsoft, or OpenAI could build native AEO/visibility tools that commoditize the monitoring layer.

### Execution Risks
- **Services scaling:** Growing from $12M to $245M in services requires maintaining quality while scaling the expert-in-the-loop model.
- **Capital efficiency:** Competitors like Profound ($58.5M), Peec ($29M), and n8n ($253M) are well-funded. GrowthX must be capital-efficient or raise accordingly.
- **Three-product complexity:** Running three products simultaneously risks spreading resources thin. Sequencing and focus are critical.

### Upside Scenarios
- **CheckThat viral adoption:** If the free index achieves network effects, it could become the "Google PageRank" for AI visibility, creating a defensible data moat.
- **Output.ai framework traction:** If the open-source framework catches developer adoption (like LangChain's 118K stars), it creates a massive distribution advantage.
- **Ecosystem lock-in:** Companies using all three products become deeply embedded — similar to how HubSpot's CRM + Marketing + Sales suite drives 106% NRR.

## 8. Methodology & Source Notes

### Approach

- **TAM:** Top-down from industry analyst reports (Grand View Research, MarketsandMarkets, Mordor Intelligence, Gartner, Forrester), triangulated across 3+ sources per market.
- **SAM:** Bottom-up from ICP criteria × addressable company counts × average spend, validated against top-down market share analysis.
- **SOM:** Capacity-based projections from internal fiscal plans, validated against comparable company growth trajectories and market penetration benchmarks.

### Source Confidence Notes

- **High confidence:** Content marketing market sizing (multiple consistent sources at $500B+), AI agents market ($8B in 2025, 46% CAGR), SEO market ($75B+), enterprise AI adoption rates.
- **Medium confidence:** AEO-specific market sizing (category too new for dedicated reports), SOM projections (dependent on execution), competitor revenue figures (often estimates from Latka/Sacra).
- **Lower confidence:** Output.ai platform revenue projections (earliest stage, most uncertain), cross-sell uplift estimate (based on analogies, not proven data).

- McKinsey: Enterprise AI adoption survey (78% adoption), AI agent experimentation (62%)
- Mordor Intelligence: Content marketing market ($525B, 13.5% CAGR), SEO market
