# Marcel Santilli: Personal LinkedIn Style Guide

> Your LinkedIn voice is an extension of how you actually think and talk. This guide captures what makes your communication distinctive and translates it into repeatable patterns for LinkedIn.

---

## Why People Engage (The Psychology)

Understanding this shapes everything else. People engage when content:

| Trigger | Why It Works | Your Natural Fit |
|---------|--------------|------------------|
| **Creates an open loop** | Brain needs to close incomplete information | Your math hooks ("At $100M with 80% GRR...") create loops |
| **Pattern interruption** | Unexpected claims break the scroll | Your first principles challenges violate expectations |
| **Invites identity expression** | People signal who they are by engaging | Your peer-level questions let founders demonstrate expertise |
| **Validates what they suspect** | Makes them feel smart for agreeing | Your "the conventional wisdom is wrong" posts do this |
| **Triggers emotion** | Inspiration, frustration, vindication | Your vulnerability and radical transparency create connection |

**Your edge:** You combine evidence (numbers, data) with vulnerability (failures, struggles). That's rare. Most creators do one or the other.

---

## Your Core Voice

Write like a **practitioner teaching a peer**. Not a guru dispensing wisdom. Not a vendor selling services. A founder who's done the work sharing what he's learned with others doing the same work.

**Three qualities that define your voice:**

| Quality | What It Means | Example |
|---------|---------------|---------|
| **Evidence-backed** | Never claim without proof | "Our burn multiple is 0.33. That's how we sleep at night." |
| **Systems-focused** | Everything connects to everything | "Retention isn't just a metric. It affects fiscal planning, which affects fundraising, which affects survival." |
| **Radically transparent** | Share what others hide | "I've been fired multiple times as CMO. I considered myself pretty good. That's the job." |

---

## The 210-Character Rule

LinkedIn truncates posts at 210-220 characters before "...see more." Your hook lives or dies in this window.

**Every hook must:**
- Stop the scroll instantly
- Create enough intrigue to warrant a click
- Stand alone as a complete thought
- Promise value worth the reader's time

**Test before posting:** If your hook doesn't work in isolation, rewrite it.

## Your Post Structure

Based on how you think and communicate:

### The Marcel Structure

```
1. HOOK: Specific number, observation, or admission (1 line)

2. CONTEXT: Why this matters / where it came from (2-3 lines)

3. BREAKDOWN: First principles decomposition (list or short paragraphs)

4. SYNTHESIS: What it all connects to (1-2 lines)

5. INVITATION: Question that invites peer-level discussion
```

### Example Post in Your Voice

```
We went from 9x-ing ARR to 2x-ing ARR.

Here's what that taught me about the difference between momentum and sustainability:

Momentum feels like winning. 
Sustainability is actually winning.

Three things we changed:

1. Stopped optimizing for bookings. Started optimizing for retention.

2. Built systems for value delivery, not just sales velocity.

3. Accepted that slower growth with healthy unit economics beats fast growth that's underwater.

Our burn multiple is now 0.33. Two of the last three months were cash flow positive.

Not as exciting as hypergrowth. But I sleep better.

What's a metric you stopped chasing?
```

### When Teaching
- Use numbers to make points concrete
- Break down the system, not just the symptom
- Give credit to sources ("Ryan Singer told me...")

### When Sharing Struggles
- Be matter-of-fact, not dramatic
- Connect to the lesson, not the emotion
- End with what you'd do differently

### When Challenging Ideas
- Lead with evidence, not opinion
- Acknowledge the conventional view before challenging
- Offer an alternative framework, not just criticism

## Format Preferences

Based on your style:

### Text Posts (Your Primary Format)
- Lead with one punchy line
- Use line breaks for readability
- Short paragraphs (2-3 sentences max)
- End with a genuine question

### Avoid
- Excessive emojis (you use them sparingly, if at all)
- Bold/italic formatting overuse
- Generic engagement questions ("Thoughts?")
- External links in post body (put in comments)

### Carousels (More Strategic Than "Occasional")

Carousels get 6.60% engagement—highest of all formats. Use them for:
- Teaching a framework step-by-step
- Showing before/after or progression
- When a concept needs visual decomposition

**Structure that works:**
- Slide 1: Bold hook (2-3 words max as headline)
- Slides 2-4: Build context (one concept per slide)
- Slides 5-7: Deliver the value (examples, proof points)
- Slides 8-9: Actionable takeaway
- Slide 10: CTA ("Follow for more" or question)

**Technical specs:** 1200x1500px (vertical), large readable text, 5-10 slides optimal.

## Comment Strategy (Growth Lever, Not Courtesy)

Commenting strategically can grow your audience faster than posting alone. The algorithm treats comment activity as a signal of expertise.

### The Perfect Comment Formula

1. **Specific compliment** — What specifically resonated
2. **Add your insight** — Related experience or perspective
3. **End with a question** — Keeps conversation going

**Example in your voice:**
> "This matches what I saw at Scale and Deepgram—the companies that cracked organic growth treated it like infrastructure, not marketing. One thing I'd add: the 1% rule applies here too. Top pages compound, the rest decay. Have you seen companies actually act on that insight, or do they keep spreading resources evenly?"

### Building Thread Conversations

Multi-turn conversations are exponentially more valuable than single comments.

**How to create them:**
- End comments with genuine questions
- Reference and build on previous comments
- Tag relevant people who might add perspective
- Make your comment section worth reading

### Daily Comment Practice

Comment thoughtfully on 5 posts daily from people in your target audience. Not generic "Great post!"—add real value. This drives more followers than posting alone.

## The Algorithm: What Actually Matters

### The Golden Hour (Critical)

LinkedIn tests every post with a small segment of your audience in the first 60-90 minutes. High engagement velocity = broad distribution. Low = only ~5% ever see it.

**Your protocol:**
- Post only when you can be present for the next 90 minutes
- Reply to every comment within the first hour (not courtesy—algorithm signal)
- Ask follow-up questions in replies to create thread conversations
- 10+ comments in the first hour typically predicts 1,000+ views

### Engagement Hierarchy

Design content to invite the highest-value engagement:

1. **Comments with depth** — Multi-sentence responses (highest value)
2. **Thread conversations** — Back-and-forth in comments (exponentially valuable)
3. **Shares with commentary** — Reshares that add perspective
4. **Saves** — High-value content worth returning to
5. **Reactions** — Lowest value but still signals engagement

**One thoughtful comment is worth 10 likes.** Design for conversation.

### Posting Rhythm

**Frequency:** 3-5 posts/week optimal
- 1x/week = baseline performance
- 5x/week = 78% higher engagement than irregular posters
- Consistency beats virality

**Best times:**
- Tuesday: 10 AM (secondary: 6-8 AM, 12 PM)
- Wednesday: 9 AM (secondary: 8 AM, 12 PM)
- Thursday: 10 AM (secondary: 8 AM, 12 PM)
- Overall sweet spot: **9 AM - 2 PM, Tuesday-Thursday**

### What the Algorithm Deprioritizes

- **Engagement bait** — "Comment YES if you agree!" gets flagged
- **External links in post body** — Put links in first comment instead
- **Overly promotional content** — Selling triggers penalties
- **Coordinated engagement** — Pods are increasingly detectable
