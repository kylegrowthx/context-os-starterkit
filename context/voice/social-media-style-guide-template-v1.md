## Platform-Specific Rules

### LinkedIn

**Hook window:** LinkedIn truncates posts at 210-220 characters before "...see more." Your hook lives or dies in this window.

**Every hook must:**
- Stop the scroll instantly
- Create enough intrigue to warrant a click
- Stand alone as a complete thought
- Promise value worth the reader's time

**Format preferences:**
- Lead with one punchy line
- Use line breaks for readability
- Short paragraphs (1-2 sentences max)
- End with a genuine question
- No external links in post body (put in comments)

**Best posting times:** [FILL IN: When your audience is most active. Default guidance: Tuesday-Thursday, 9 AM-2 PM in your primary timezone.]

**Frequency:** [FILL IN: Target posts per week. Reference: 3-5/week is optimal for most B2B audiences.]

### X / Twitter

**Hook window:** First line must work in isolation. Threads should have a strong opener that makes people want to read the full thread.

**Format preferences:**
- Shorter sentences than LinkedIn
- Threads for longer ideas (number the tweets or use natural breaks)
- Quote tweets for commentary on industry trends
- Retweet with commentary rather than plain retweets

## Algorithm Awareness

### The Golden Hour

Most social platforms test posts with a small segment of your audience in the first 60-90 minutes. High engagement velocity = broad distribution.

**Your protocol:**
- Post only when you can be present for the next 90 minutes
- Reply to every comment within the first hour
- Ask follow-up questions in replies to create thread conversations

### Engagement Hierarchy

Design content to invite the highest-value engagement:

1. **Comments with depth** — Multi-sentence responses (highest value)
2. **Thread conversations** — Back-and-forth in comments (exponentially valuable)
3. **Shares with commentary** — Reshares that add perspective
4. **Saves** — High-value content worth returning to
5. **Reactions** — Lowest value but still signals engagement

**One thoughtful comment is worth 10 likes.** Design for conversation.
