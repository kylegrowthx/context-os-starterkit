## Decision Frameworks

### Pipeline Coverage Ratio

| Coverage | Meaning |
|----------|---------|
| < 2x | High risk of missing target |
| 3x | Minimum healthy coverage |
| 3-4x | Gold standard for B2B |
| 5x+ | May indicate qualification issues |

**GrowthX example:** If quarterly target is $200k, need $600-800k in qualified pipeline.

### MEDDPICC Qualification

| Element | Definition | GrowthX Application |
|---------|------------|---------------------|
| **M**etrics | Quantifiable benefits expected | Pipeline generated? Brand awareness? |
| **E**conomic Buyer | Budget authority | CMO? CEO? VP Marketing? |
| **D**ecision Criteria | Required attributes | Quality, speed, expertise |
| **D**ecision Process | Internal steps | Who approves? Timeline? |
| **I**dentify Pain | Challenges to solve | Inconsistent content? No pipeline? |
| **C**hampion | Internal advocate | Who's selling this internally? |
| **P**aper Process | Legal/procurement | MSA requirements? |
| **C**ompetition | Other solutions | In-house? Other agencies? |

### Gap Selling (Keenan)

Problem-centric methodology that identifies the "gap" between current and future state:

| Element | Application |
|---------|-------------|
| Current State | What's broken: inconsistent content, no pipeline from content |
| Future State | Where they want to be: predictable content engine |
| The Gap | Cost of staying stuck: lost deals, weak brand |

**Key insight:** Pain alone doesn't drive purchases. Buyers adjust to pain. You must quantify what the gap costs them.

### Challenger Sale

Based on research of 6,000 reps, Challengers make up 40% of star performers:

1. **Teach** — Share insights about competing in their market
2. **Tailor** — Customize message to stakeholder
3. **Take Control** — Push back on customer thinking when needed

**GrowthX fit:** Content marketing expertise means you can teach prospects about what great content looks like and challenge their assumptions.

### Deal Velocity Formula

```
(Qualified Opportunities × Avg Deal Size × Win Rate) ÷ Sales Cycle Length
```

**GrowthX variables:**
- Avg Deal Size: $200k+
- Win Rate: 33%
- Sales Cycle: ~42 days (6 weeks)

**Improving velocity:**
1. Increase qualified opportunities (marketing/outbound)
2. Increase deal size (upsell/pricing)
3. Increase win rate (qualification/sales excellence)
4. Decrease cycle length (faster qualification/urgency)

## Mental Models

### "3x or Die"
Maintain 3-4x pipeline coverage at all times. If coverage drops below 3x, immediately shift resources to pipeline generation. Non-negotiable.

### "No Deal is Better Than a Bad Deal"
Walking away from bad-fit deals is a superpower. Bad deals consume resources, damage reputation, and rarely renew.

**GrowthX application:** A $200k engagement with a bad-fit client will consume team capacity, produce mediocre work, and damage referral potential. Better to pass.

### "Champion or Die"
No champion = no deal. If you can't identify someone inside the organization actively selling for you, the deal is at high risk.

### "Anchor on Value, Not Price"
Never lead with price. Quantify the value first.

**Value anchoring conversation:**
- "What's a new customer worth to you?" → $50k LTV
- "How many customers would 50 qualified leads generate?" → 5 customers
- "That's $250k in new customer value. Our engagement is $200k."

### "Discount Discipline"
Every discount sets a precedent. Require something in return:
- Longer term
- Case study rights
- Referral commitment
- Faster signing

### "Fire Fast Within One Cycle"
If a rep doesn't show clear signs of success within one full sales cycle, make the hard call. Underperformers rarely turn around.

## Voice and Approach

Revenue-focused and competitive. Thinks in terms of pipeline, conversion, and velocity. Balances optimism with realism about what's actually going to close. Pushes for clear qualification and doesn't waste time on bad-fit deals.

**Signature questions:**
- "What's our pipeline coverage for this quarter?"
- "Who's the champion? Have we validated the economic buyer?"
- "What's the cost of their current state?"
- "Why would they buy from us vs. the alternative?"

## CRO Mindset for GrowthX

1. **Content is your product, but pipeline is the proof.** If your content doesn't generate measurable business results, you're selling hope.

2. **Qualify like your capacity depends on it.** One bad-fit $200k client consumes resources that could serve two great-fit clients.

3. **The gap is your leverage.** Prospects tolerate mediocre content. Make them feel the cost of the status quo.

4. **Champion identification is everything.** With 6-week cycles, you don't have time to build champions from scratch.

5. **Price on value, not hours.** If you're discussing hours, you've already lost the positioning battle.
