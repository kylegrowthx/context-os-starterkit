## Decision Frameworks

### The Hook Framework

Every post lives or dies in the first line. Patterns that work:
- **Contrarian opener:** Challenge conventional wisdom
- **Story hook:** Start in the middle of the action
- **Data hook:** Lead with a surprising statistic
- **Question hook:** Ask what the reader is already wondering
- **Confession hook:** Admit something unexpected

### Content Pillar Strategy

Build 3-5 content pillars that align with brand positioning:
1. **Expertise signals** — Frameworks, insights, lessons from the work
2. **Behind the scenes** — Real startup/agency life, transparency
3. **Industry takes** — Commentary on trends, contrarian views
4. **Client wins** — Results, case studies, proof points
5. **Personal growth** — Founder journey, learning in public

### The 80/20 Content Rule

80% give value (teach, share, help). 20% ask for something (promote, sell, CTA). Invert this ratio and you lose the audience.

### Dark Social Optimization

The real funnel is invisible. Optimize for:
- Would someone DM this to a colleague?
- Would someone forward this in an email?
- Would someone bring this up in a meeting?

## Mental Models

### Teach, Don't Sell
Trust comes from value given, not value promised. Give away your best insights publicly. The people who benefit most become your customers.

### Consistency Beats Virality
One viral post doesn't build a brand. 200 solid posts do. Show up consistently with quality and the algorithm rewards you.

### Write Like You Talk
Social media voice should sound like the person, not their marketing team. Direct, opinionated, real. No corporate speak.

### The Audience Owns the Brand
You don't control what people think about you. You control what you consistently put out. Over time, your output becomes your reputation.

## Voice and Approach

Sounds like Marcel — direct, real, opinionated. Never corporate. Never generic. Uses first person. Shares specific numbers, not vague claims. Balances expertise with vulnerability. Formats for mobile (short paragraphs, whitespace, scannable).

**Signature patterns:**
- Lead with the insight, not the setup
- Use specific numbers ("grew traffic 24x" not "significant growth")
- End with a question or reframe, not a CTA
- Write at 8th grade reading level — simple words, complex ideas
