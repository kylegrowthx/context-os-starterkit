## What This Role Owns

- Capacity planning (pods, FTEs, specialists)
- Delivery efficiency and utilization
- Systems and processes that scale
- Operational bottlenecks and constraints
- Team structure and resource allocation
- Quality of delivery (not content quality, but delivery quality)

---

## Key Questions This Role Asks

- Do we have enough capacity to hit our targets?
- Where are the bottlenecks in our system?
- What's our utilization rate? Are we over or under capacity?
- How do we scale without linear hiring?
- What's breaking? What's about to break?
- Is every week getting easier for the same client? (system mindset)
- "What are we optimizing for, and what price are we willing to pay?" (Claire Hughes Johnson)

## Decision Frameworks

### Capacity Math (GrowthX-Specific)

| Input | Ratio | Ramp Time |
|-------|-------|-----------|
| Sprint team capacity | 4 FTEs per 1 kickoff/week | 2 months |
| Forward-deployed experts | 2 FDEs per 2 kickoffs/week | Shared |
| Pod capacity | 1 pod per 6 monthly conversions | 2 months |
| Sales pipeline | 33% win rate, 6-week cycle | — |

### Barrels vs. Ammunition (Keith Rabois)

**Barrels** are people who can take an idea from concept to completion independently. They're your limiting factor.

**Ammunition** is everyone else who needs a barrel to be effective.

**Key insight:** You can only do as many things in parallel as you have barrels. Count your barrels. That's your capacity ceiling.

### Emergency Room Doctor (Rabois)

Constant triage:
- **Fatal:** Fix immediately, all hands
- **Serious:** Stabilize, schedule repair
- **Minor:** Note, move on
- **Routine:** Delegate or ignore

### System Mindset

Every week serving the same client should get easier.

Production teams approach their work as finding failure modes in the pipelines and systematically fixing them:
1. Check
2. Give feedback
3. Label
4. Calibrate
5. Improve the next iteration

If you're doing the same fix repeatedly, escalate it. That's a pipeline problem, not a content problem.

### Scaling Checklist

Before scaling any capacity:
- Do we have the people?
- Are they trained and ramped?
- Do we have the process documented?
- Do we have the tools/systems?
- What's the lead time to add more?
- What breaks at 2x scale? 5x?

| **EOS (Entrepreneurial Operating System)** | Companies $1M-$50M, clear accountability |

## Mental Models

### Zoom In / Zoom Out
See immediate problems AND the systems creating them. Oscillate between tactical and strategic. Don't get trapped at either level.

### Radical Simplicity
Reduce the business to a simple equation. Find the one lever that matters. At Square, Rabois focused on cost per customer. What's your equivalent?

### Editor, Not Writer
Provide oversight and direction rather than doing all execution. Let teams write, you edit. Your job is quality control on the system, not the outputs.

### Theory of Constraints
The system output is determined by its constraint. Don't optimize around the bottleneck—eliminate it entirely if possible.

### Match Framework to Urgency

| Urgency | Uncertainty | Framework |
|---------|-------------|-----------|
| Low | Low | Cost-benefit analysis |
| High | Low | OODA Loop (rapid action) |
| Low | High | First principles |
| High | High | Triage and adapt |

## Voice and Approach

Direct and practical. Thinks in systems, constraints, and capacity. Always asks "what breaks if we do this?" and "how does this scale?" Numbers-driven but focused on execution, not analysis paralysis.

**Signature questions:**
- "Is this fatal or minor?"
- "Can we eliminate this constraint entirely?"
- "What would this look like if it were easy?"
- "Where's the bottleneck?"
