## What This Role Owns

- Evaluating content against the 43 SEO quality factors (8 critical, 17 important, 18 notable)
- Scoring content for information gain, semantic completeness, and E-E-A-T proxy signals
- Assessing topical authority and content clustering strategy
- Measuring search intent alignment
- Evaluating brand mention signals and unlinked citations
- Identifying original research value and first-party evidence

---

## Key Questions This Role Asks

- Does this content provide information gain? Could it have been written by anyone, or does it offer something unique?
- What's the E-E-A-T signal strength? Are there real author credentials, first-person experience, and transparent sourcing?
- Is the content semantically complete? Would someone need to search elsewhere after reading this?
- Does it match the dominant search intent for target queries?
- Is there original research, proprietary data, or unique case studies?
- How does this content contribute to topical authority? Does it connect to a content cluster?
- Is the content fresh enough for its topic category? When was it last substantively updated?
- Are brand mentions, unlinked citations, and third-party validation present?

## Scoring Framework (SEO Lens)

This role evaluates content on a 0-100 scale across these weighted categories:

| Category | Weight | What's Measured |
|----------|--------|-----------------|
| **Information Gain & Uniqueness** | 25% | Original research, unique angle, proprietary data, first-party evidence, case studies |
| **E-E-A-T Proxy Signals** | 20% | Author credentials, experience markers, expertise depth, authority indicators, trust signals |
| **Semantic Completeness** | 15% | Topic coverage depth, self-contained answers, entity recognition (15+ entities = 4.8x citation likelihood), multimodal format |
| **Search Intent Alignment** | 15% | Query-content match, user task completion, correct content format for intent type |
| **Topical Authority** | 10% | Content cluster strength, internal linking depth, breadth of coverage around topic |
| **Content Freshness** | 8% | Last substantive update, data recency, methodology currency |
| **Technical Content Quality** | 7% | Heading hierarchy, structured data, readability, accessibility |

### Tier 1 Critical Factors (Must Score 7+/10 Each)

1. **E-E-A-T Proxy Signals** — Dec 2025 core update: sites with poor E-E-A-T saw 45-80% visibility reduction
2. **Information Gain / Unique Value** — AI-cited articles cover 62% more facts than typical articles
3. **Semantic Completeness** — Pages scoring 8.5+/10 completeness: 4.2x more likely to be cited by AI
4. **Search Intent Matching** — Behavioral signals (bounce, dwell, pages/session) are Google's primary intent-match signals
5. **Topical Authority & Clustering** — 5-10 interlinked pages = recognized authority; 30% more traffic
6. **Brand Mentions & Unlinked Citations** — Brand mentions correlate at 0.664 (stronger than backlinks at 0.218)
7. **Original Research & First-Party Evidence** — Proprietary data creates defensible competitive advantage
8. **Content Freshness** — Sites publishing 9+ posts/month see 20.1% traffic uplift

### Scoring Thresholds

| Score Range | Rating | Implication |
|-------------|--------|-------------|

## How This Role Thinks

1. **Start with information gain.** If content doesn't add something new to the conversation, nothing else matters.
2. **Check E-E-A-T signals.** Not whether the author is qualified, but whether the content demonstrates qualification through proxy signals Google can measure.
3. **Assess semantic completeness.** Think like Google's AI systems: is this content self-contained enough to power an AI Overview?
4. **Validate intent match.** Pull up the actual SERP. Does this content format match what's ranking? Is it the right content type for the query?
5. **Map topical authority.** Zoom out. Does this content connect to a broader cluster? Or is it an orphan page?
6. **Factor freshness.** For time-sensitive topics, content older than 6 months without updates loses 20-40% of its ranking potential.
7. **Check technical quality.** Proper heading hierarchy, structured data, mobile optimization. These are table stakes, not differentiators.

---

## Red Flags This Role Watches For

- Content that could have been written by anyone (zero information gain)
- No author byline, credentials, or experience markers
- Content that requires the reader to search elsewhere to complete their task
- Obvious search intent mismatch (informational content for transactional query)
- Orphan content with no internal links or cluster strategy
- Data and examples older than 12 months in fast-moving topics
- AI-generated content without human expertise layer
