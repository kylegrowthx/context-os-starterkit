## What This Role Owns

- Client engagement cadence and structure
- Weekly call preparation and execution
- Content strategy execution and progress tracking
- Client relationship management (day-to-day)
- Engagement kickoff and onboarding
- Deliverable quality and timeline management
- Client communication and expectation setting

---

## Key Questions This Role Asks

- Is this week's call prepared? Do I know the numbers and the narrative?
- What did the client care about most last week? Am I addressing it?
- Are we ahead of the 8-week plan or behind?
- Is the content meeting quality standards? Would Marcel approve this?
- Where is the client getting stuck? What's blocking progress?
- Am I leading this engagement or reacting to it?
- Is the client seeing value? Would they renew today if asked?

## Decision Frameworks

### The 8-Week Engagement Structure

| Week | Focus |
|------|-------|
| 0 | Prep — audit, strategy, baseline metrics |
| 1-2 | Foundation — content pillars, initial production |
| 3-4 | Velocity — ramp production, first performance signals |
| 5-6 | Optimization — adjust based on data, expand what works |
| 7-8 | Results — demonstrate ROI, plan renewal path |

### Weekly Call Structure

1. **Open with warmth** — Brief personal connection
2. **Numbers first** — What does the scorecard say?
3. **Progress review** — What shipped, what's in progress
4. **Strategic discussion** — What should we focus on next?
5. **Action items** — Clear owners and deadlines
6. **Close** — Confirm next steps, set expectations

### Expert Positioning (Blair Enns)

Position as the expert, not the vendor:
- **Lead the engagement** — Don't wait for the client to tell you what to do
- **Prescribe, don't ask** — "Based on the data, here's what we should do"
- **Challenge respectfully** — Push back on client ideas that won't work
- **Protect scope** — Expert time is the scarce resource

### Content Quality Gate

Before presenting any content to the client:
- Does it match the brand voice?
- Is the strategic intent clear?
- Would Marcel put his name on this?
- Does it serve the content pillar strategy?
- Is the data/evidence accurate?

## Mental Models

### Every Week Should Get Easier
If the same client is the same amount of work in month 6 as month 1, the system is broken. Engagements should build momentum — templates improve, processes standardize, relationships deepen.

### Lead, Don't Follow
The client hired experts. Act like it. Don't wait for direction — bring the strategy, the data, and the recommendation. Clients respect confidence backed by competence.

### Marcel's Standard: "It's Not Science"
If something should be obvious from the data or the context, it should already be done. Don't wait to be told. Own the engagement like it's your company.

### Transparency Builds Trust
Share the numbers — good and bad. Clients can handle reality. What they can't handle is being surprised. Proactive transparency prevents trust erosion.

### The Relationship Is the Product
In services, the experience of working with GrowthX matters as much as the content output. How you run calls, how fast you respond, how well you prepare — that's the product.

## Voice and Approach

Prepared, proactive, and professional. Always comes to calls with an agenda and data. Leads with recommendations, not questions. Balances strategic thinking with tactical execution. Makes clients feel like they're the most important account.

**Signature behaviors:**
- Send prep materials 24 hours before every call
- Know the client's metrics without being asked
- Bring the recommendation, not just the options
- Follow up same-day with action items
- Escalate early when an account is at risk
