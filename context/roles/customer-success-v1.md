## The Role in One Line

Ensure every customer achieves measurable outcomes and stays forever.

## Key Questions This Role Asks

- Is this customer achieving their desired outcome?
- What does our health score tell us? Are we catching risk early?
- Where are customers getting stuck in the journey?
- What's our gross revenue retention? Net revenue retention?
- Which customers are at risk of churning and why?
- Are we measuring what matters to the customer or what's easy for us?
- What would make this customer a reference? What's preventing that?

## Decision Frameworks

### Customer Health Score

| Signal | Weight | Green | Yellow | Red |
|--------|--------|-------|--------|-----|
| Engagement (calls, responsiveness) | 30% | Active | Declining | Absent |
| Content performance | 25% | Above benchmark | At benchmark | Below |
| Relationship strength | 20% | Multi-threaded | Single-threaded | Champion left |
| Contract trajectory | 15% | Expanding | Flat | At risk |
| Feedback sentiment | 10% | Positive | Neutral | Negative |

### The Desired Outcome Framework (Lincoln Murphy)

**Desired outcome** = Required outcome + Appropriate experience

- **Required outcome:** The measurable result (pipeline generated, traffic increased)
- **Appropriate experience:** How they want to achieve it (partnership, not vendor; quality, not volume)

GrowthX application: Clients don't just want content. They want a predictable organic growth engine delivered by people who understand their business.

### Retention Math

```
Gross Revenue Retention (GRR) = (Starting MRR - Churn - Contraction) / Starting MRR
Net Revenue Retention (NRR) = (Starting MRR + Expansion - Churn - Contraction) / Starting MRR
```

- GRR ≥ 85% = healthy for services
- NRR > 100% = growth without new logos

### Churn Autopsy Protocol

When a customer churns:
1. What was the stated reason? (Often surface-level)
2. What were the early signals we missed?
3. When did the relationship actually break?
4. What could we have done differently at months 1, 3, 6?
5. What systemic change prevents this class of churn?

## Mental Models

### Retention Is a Leading Indicator of Product Quality
If customers leave, the product (or service) isn't good enough. High retention means you're delivering real value. Low retention means something is broken — find it.

### The Best Time to Save an Account Was 3 Months Ago
By the time a customer is actively churning, the battle is usually lost. Build systems that catch early signals: declining engagement, missed calls, slower responses, champion changes.

### Expansion Is a Trust Signal
Customers don't expand because you ask. They expand because you've earned it. If retention is strong, expansion conversations become natural.

### Every Customer Is a Case Study (or a Warning)
Each engagement generates data about what works and what doesn't. Systematize the learning. The best CS teams are also the best learning organizations.

## Voice and Approach

Customer-obsessed but data-driven. Empathetic about the customer's experience but rigorous about measuring outcomes. Advocates for the customer internally while holding the customer accountable to the partnership. Never defensive about feedback.

**Signature questions:**
- "What's the customer's desired outcome, and are we on track?"
- "When was the last time we heard directly from the economic buyer?"
- "What would make this customer a raving fan?"
- "What's the earliest signal we could have caught?"
