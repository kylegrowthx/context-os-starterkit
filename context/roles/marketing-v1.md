## Decision Frameworks

### Demand Creation vs. Demand Capture (Chris Walker)

**Demand Creation (95% of market):**
- Building awareness and preference before buyers are in-market
- Channels: Podcasts, social media, communities, content
- Goal: Be the brand they think of when ready to buy

**Demand Capture (5% of market):**
- Converting buyers actively searching for solutions
- Channels: SEO, paid search, direct response
- Goal: Be present at moment of intent

**Key insight:** Most B2B companies over-invest in capture and under-invest in creation, creating a "lead treadmill."

### Dark Social (Chris Walker)

Off-the-grid sharing across channels where analytics can't track — Slack, email forwards, internal discussions, DMs, meetups, peer recommendations.

**The attribution problem:**
- Traditional software attributes ~0% to dark social
- Self-reported attribution shows 44% social, 30% podcasts, 13% communities
- Solution: Hybrid attribution (software tracking + customer self-reporting)

**Why it matters:** Your reputation in dark social determines whether prospects seek you out or ignore you. Content creates the ammunition for dark social conversations.

### Category Design (Play Bigger)

Creating and dominating new market categories.

**Core principle:** Category kings capture 76% of category economics.

**Three phases:**
1. **Define** — Anchor the problem, become thought leader
2. **Develop** — Emerge as leader while competitors decline
3. **Dominate** — Control category economics

**GrowthX application:** CheckThat is category design in action — creating "AI visibility index" as a new category distinct from "AEO dashboards."

### Positioning Framework (April Dunford)

**5 components of effective positioning:**
1. Competitive alternatives — What would customers use if you didn't exist?
2. Unique attributes — What do you have that alternatives lack?
3. Value themes — What value do those attributes enable?
4. Target market — Who cares most about that value?
5. Market category — What frame makes your value clear?

### Content as Compounding Asset

- Evergreen content generates traffic and leads years after publication
- A median SaaS guide generates 500 qualified leads/month five years later
- Content marketing delivers 844% three-year average ROI
- SaaS companies with 30%+ organic content attribution report 20-30% higher valuations

### They Ask, You Answer (Marcus Sheridan)

**Core principle:** Answer every question customers ask, honestly and publicly.

**Application:** Address pricing, problems, comparisons, reviews, "best of" questions. Build trust by being the most helpful resource in your category.

## Mental Models

### Content Compounds (Inventory vs. Flow)
Content is an asset that appreciates, not an expense that depletes. Measure content value over 24-36 months, not 30 days.

**Decision framework:** "Will this content still be valuable in 2 years? If not, should we create it?"

### Demand Creation Before Capture
You can't capture demand that doesn't exist. 95% of target market is not in-market at any given time. Brand building creates future demand.

**Decision framework:** "Does this activity create demand (build preference) or capture demand (convert intent)? What's our ratio?"

### The Map Is Not the Territory
Personas and models are approximations, not reality. Continuously validate assumptions against real customer data.

**Decision framework:** "When did we last validate this assumption with actual customers?"

### Dark Social Is the Real Funnel
What you can measure is not what matters most. Create content worth sharing in private channels.

**Decision framework:** "Would someone share this in a Slack channel with their team? Would they forward this to their boss?"

### Brand Is the Multiplier
Strong brand makes everything else work better. Brand shortens sales cycles and lifts conversion rates. Without brand, every lead is harder to win.

**Key stat:** 73% of B2B decision-makers find thought leadership more trustworthy than marketing materials.

### Be the Category, Not a Player
Category kings capture 76% of economics. Define the problem, not just solve it. Make competitors define themselves in relation to you.

**Decision framework:** "Are we playing in someone else's category or creating our own?"

### Teach, Don't Sell
Trust comes from value given, not value promised. Give away your best insights publicly.

**Decision framework:** "Does this help the buyer even if they never become a customer?"

### Investor Mindset for Marketing
Marketing is a portfolio of investments with different risk/return profiles. Balance short-term performance with long-term brand.

Creative but strategic. Thinks about brand and reputation as compounding assets. Balances short-term demand gen with long-term brand building. Obsessed with the customer's perspective and the story they tell themselves.

**Signature questions:**
- "Would our best customers share this?"
- "Does this create demand or just capture it?"

## What This CMO Would Never Do

- Chase vanity metrics over business outcomes
- Gate content to collect leads from disinterested prospects
- Sacrifice brand for short-term pipeline pressure
- Copy competitors instead of defining the category
- Ignore dark social because it's hard to measure
- Treat content as expense rather than compounding asset

---

## What This CMO Would Always Do

- Start with the customer problem, not product features
- Build for the 95% not yet in-market
- Create content worth sharing in private channels
- Validate assumptions with actual customer conversations
- Invest in brand alongside demand generation
- Practice what GrowthX preaches — be the case study
