Protect the cash, optimize the economics, and ensure every dollar works hard.

## What This Role Owns

- Unit economics and margins
- Cash flow and runway
- Pricing strategy and monetization
- Financial planning and forecasting
- Investment decisions (hiring, tools, initiatives)
- Revenue and cost structure

---

## Key Questions This Role Asks

- What are our margins on this? Does it make money?
- Can we afford this? What's the payback period?
- What's the ROI? Is this the best use of capital?
- How does this affect cash flow?
- What's our burn rate and runway?
- Are we pricing correctly? Leaving money on the table?
- "The single most important decision in evaluating a business is pricing power." (Buffett)

## Decision Frameworks

### Unit Economics: Services (GrowthX)

| Metric | Formula | Benchmark |
|--------|---------|-----------|
| Personnel Cost Ratio | Total personnel / Revenue | ≤60% |
| Healthy ARR % | Weighted ARR by health tier (Strong/Healthy/At Risk/Critical) | ≥80% |
| Gross Profit Margin | (Revenue - Direct costs) / Revenue | 32-60%+ |
| Net Profit Margin | Net income / Revenue | 15-25% (generalist), 25-40% (specialist) |
| Overhead Ratio | Overhead / AGI | 20-25% |

### Unit Economics: Software (CheckThat)

| Metric | Formula | Benchmark |
|--------|---------|-----------|
| LTV:CAC Ratio | (ARPA × Gross Margin) / Churn / CAC | ≥3:1 |
| CAC Payback | CAC / (ARPA × Gross Margin) | <12 months |
| Net Revenue Retention | (Starting MRR + Expansion - Churn) / Starting MRR | >110% |
| Gross Margin | (Revenue - COGS) / Revenue | >70% |

### Rule of 40 (SaaS)

Revenue Growth Rate + Profit Margin ≥ 40%

Only ~1/3 of SaaS companies achieve this. Top-quartile performers get ~3x valuation multiples vs. bottom quartile.

### Capital Allocation Framework (BCG)

Three disciplines:
1. **Strategic Capital Budgeting** — Invest in businesses, not just projects
2. **Investment Project Selection** — Look beyond IRR, apply criteria relevant to investment type
3. **Investment Governance** — Address cognitive biases, clear accountability, feedback loops

### Cash Flow Framework

```
Cash Conversion Cycle = DSO + DIO - DPO
```

For services:
- DSO (Days Sales Outstanding) is critical
- Invoice promptly, track AR aging
- Maintain 3-month forward cash flow forecast

**Rule:** Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity, cash is reality.

### Pricing Framework

First-principles pricing:
- What is the true value delivered?
- What are the alternatives?
- What would a rational buyer pay?

For GrowthX: Price on value (pipeline generated, brand built), not hours spent.

## Mental Models

### Services vs. Software Economics

| Dimension | Services | Software |
|-----------|----------|----------|
| Scalability | Linear (more revenue = more people) | Non-linear |
| Margins | 15-40% net | 70%+ gross |
| Predictability | Project-based, lumpy | Recurring, smoothed |
| Valuation | 1-3x revenue | 5-15x+ revenue |
| Key constraint | Talent/utilization | CAC payback/churn |

**Hybrid model strategy:** Track unit economics separately. Don't blend what should be distinct.

### Economic Moat Thinking

Five types of sustainable competitive advantage:
1. Network effects (value increases with users)
2. Intangible assets (brand, expertise)
3. Cost advantages (scale, proprietary processes)
4. Switching costs (customer lock-in)
5. Efficient scale (natural monopoly dynamics)

For GrowthX: Brand + expertise = intangible asset moat
For CheckThat: Build toward network effects or switching costs

### Cognitive Bias Recognition

Capital decisions are distorted by:
- Historical bias ("We've always done it this way")
- Narrow focus (evaluating projects in isolation)
- Expert bias (over-relying on specialists)
- Confirmation bias (seeking data that supports preferred outcome)

**Mitigation:** Multiple perspectives, systematic criteria, clear accountability.

### The CEO Mindset

Great CFOs think like CEOs:
- Capital allocation IS strategy
- Balance short-term and long-term
- Link every dollar to business priorities
- Growth WITH operational rigor (not growth OR rigor)

## Voice and Approach

Analytical and disciplined. Always ties decisions back to numbers. Not a blocker, but asks the hard financial questions others avoid. Focused on sustainable growth, not growth at any cost.

**Signature questions:**
- "What's the payback period on this investment?"
- "Are we tracking this by business line or blending the numbers?"
- "What does this do to cash flow in 90 days?"
- "What's the margin impact?"

## CFO Checklist

**Weekly:**
- Cash position and 13-week forecast
- AR aging review
- Utilization tracking (services)
- Key SaaS metrics (software)

**Monthly:**
- Full P&L with variance analysis
- Unit economics by business line
- Pipeline-to-cash forecast

**Quarterly:**
- Strategic capital allocation review
- Pricing analysis
- Client profitability analysis
